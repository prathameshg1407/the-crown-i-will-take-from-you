Chapter 270

Moonlight and
Vows

"Wherever you are, you are my most precious family, and your home is Valdina."

It was the grandest chapel in the central temple—a cathedral of light and ancient stone.

The vaulted ceiling soared impossibly high, its gilded ribs catching the morning sun like threads of molten gold. Vast stained-glass windows transformed ordinary daylight into something sacred, spilling jewel-toned radiance across marble floors polished by centuries of reverent footsteps.

Down the center aisle, a runner of cream-colored silk had been unfurled with meticulous care—the path that would carry the bride to her future.

"I never imagined I would witness a wedding here in my lifetime."

The whisper carried through the pews, hushed with awe.

"His Holiness himself declared this the only fitting venue—a union ordained by prophecy deserves nothing less."

Every guest in attendance held a title of noble rank or higher. Yet even among such distinguished company, envy moved through the congregation like a subtle perfume.

And all of it flowed toward the bride—the woman at the center of the rumors that had set the continent ablaze.

"Have you heard what the First Prince of Katzen sent to Valdina as a betrothal gift?"

"And the dowry from the Valdinian Emperor? They say His Majesty would move mountains for his only sister..."

Some are blessed with distinguished lineage. Others are graced with exceptional fortune. But precious few possess both.

"And now a wedding in this sacred basilica, with the Holy Father's own blessing!"

If merely stepping foot within these hallowed walls was considered an honor by the most powerful figures present, then how extraordinarily blessed must the bride be—to walk its length as a bride, before the eyes of God and empire alike?

"Her Highness carries honors that others cannot earn in ten lifetimes. And her husband..."

The women's gazes drifted, almost involuntarily, toward the altar where Cesare stood.

His ceremonial robes fell without a single crease across a broad chest. The diagonal epaulettes marking Katzen's royal house rested on shoulders wide enough to bear the weight of kingdoms. He was always striking—but today, polished and purposeful, he radiated an almost overwhelming presence.

"How does the First Prince appear so... composed?"

A faint smile had softened his usually austere features, and the assembled guests realized anew just how devastatingly handsome he truly was.

Reputation aside—the countless dark rumors that trailed him like shadows—none could deny he was a man utterly pleasing to behold.

"So this is how the First Prince of Katzen goes to his bride. I confess, I was deeply curious who would finally stand beside such a man."

"Hmph. If I had known it would come to this, I would have thrown myself before the oracle as a sacrifice. What extraordinary luck she possesses."

Regret and envy spilled from painted lips.

"Well, Princess Molicia never ventured into Death Canyon, did she? The evil dragon would have devoured me before I could draw a second breath."

"..."

But the wiser among them understood. There is no such thing as fortune without cost. Luck of this magnitude could only be won by enduring trials several times more severe.

"Hm. The First Prince's side is spoken for now... but surely there are other opportunities?"

"Precisely what I was thinking."

As if choreographed, the assembly's attention pivoted toward the Valdinian delegation seated in the bride's section.

The noblewomen craned their necks, searching. The young Emperor of Valdina had yet to appear.

"The seat beside the First Prince is claimed forever now..."

Precious few members of continental royalty remained unmarried. The Emperor of Valdina—with his empire's wealth, no rival claimants to his throne, no inconvenient relatives to manage, and that immaculate, almost sacred bearing—represented the final prize.

"What does he favor? What sort of woman captures his interest?"

"Who can say? The man refuses every overture. My own attempts have struck nothing but ice and stone."

Those who had already tested Valdina's defenses sighed in collective frustration.

"But surely there must be some opening? Some weakness in the wall? I intend to approach His Majesty during the communion. Surely he must be heartsore, watching his only sister depart..."

"Hmph! The brilliant Emperor of Valdina, oblivious to a woman's designs? Unlikely."

"Are you calling me obvious?"

A peculiar tension hummed among the continental royalty—the subtle, vicious energy of hunters circling the last great quarry.

As this was a sacred ceremony held in the Holy Land and presided over by the Pope himself, the guests had anticipated the attendance of Emperor Perdiccas II.

"...Empress Xi?"

But unexpectedly, it was Empress Xi alone who arrived to represent the Katzen imperial family.

She entered with a face carved from marble—solemn, unreadable. Her manners were flawless, her gown magnificent in its severe elegance.

"Wait—Empress Xi? Isn't she the mother of the Third Prince?"

None among the guests were ignorant of Katzen's brutal succession wars—the vicious conflicts that churned beneath the empire's glittering surface.

The Third Prince and the First Prince were, in all but name, sworn enemies.

"That is hardly the countenance of someone attending a wedding. I would sooner believe she came to preside over a funeral."

The Empress's expression never shifted. She spoke to none of the guests save the attendants who trailed her like shadows.

On this auspicious day, the groom's only imperial representative was a silent adversary—someone who would gladly see him destroyed.

The message was unmistakable: Emperor Perdiccas II was deeply displeased with this marriage.

Yet Perdiccas II had miscalculated.

Too many greater luminaries had gathered this day for his petty display of disapproval to cast any shadow.

Beyond the monarchs present for the Continental Congress...

"The head of Facade has arrived! And look—the legendary mercenaries!"

"By the gods, is that the Lord of the Magic Tower?"

"All rise—pay your respects to His Holiness!"

In this constellation of stars, the Empress's cold presence was utterly eclipsed.

Peleus knew nothing of the fierce undercurrents swirling inside the basilica, nor of the continental royalty's predatory scheming.

Even if he had known, he would not have cared.

"Dea, truly... you are beautiful."

He could not tear his gaze from his sister, resplendent in her gown of pure white.

"When... when did you grow so?"

The little girl who had once barely reached his fingertips now stood before him as a bride.

"Could you not have grown a little more slowly? This brother would have been content to wait."

Peleus reached out, gently stroking Medea's hair. Their silver locks caught the light identically—soft as moonlight, luminous as memory.

He swallowed against the tightness in his throat. The time had truly come to let his little sister go.

"Dea."

Before the great doors of the basilica, Peleus paused.

"Yes?"

"Never forget this. Wherever you are, you are my most precious family—and your home is Valdina. It is your land, and you may return to it whenever you wish."

Medea went still.

"I have wanted to tell you this for so long. I am grateful it is not too late."

"..."

Medea turned her face away. If she did not bite her lip, she feared her eyes would redden with tears.

"Peleus, tell me something."

Her voice emerged thin, strained. Peleus turned to face her fully.

The warmth reflected in his blue eyes made Medea's heart ache, and she asked the question that had haunted her for so long:

"Was I... was I ever a burden to you?"

" Just as before—she had struggled desperately not to become a weight on Peleus's shoulders, not to disappoint him as he bore the crushing duties of empire. "

And now she feared—perhaps she had failed again.

Peleus's eyes widened briefly in surprise. Then his expression settled into something resolute and tender.

"Not once."

"..."

"You have never been a burden to me, Dea. Never."

"...I am glad."

Medea swallowed hard against the emotion threatening to overwhelm her. Peleus smiled—a faint, gentle thing.

As if his little sister, forever worrying about him, was both endearing and heartbreaking.

"Dea. You may not believe me, but..."

The Emperor's face—usually as remote and cold as moonlight fracturing on water—softened into something almost vulnerable.

"I have always been proud of you. No matter what you look like, no matter what path you choose—this will never change."

"I do not blame you for anything. Live, Dea. Simply live—that is all I ask."

Tears welled in Medea's eyes, unbidden.

"Lies."

It could not have been like this before. It could not have been like this back then.

"I would be happy—truly happy—to see you free to walk your own path."

"I must fulfill my duty as King of Valdina, as guardian of our ancestors' legacy. But you, Medea..."

Peleus lifted his hand and gently stroked his sister's cheek.

"You are free."

"My dearest sister. My Dea."

The choir's voices rose, pure and crystalline, filling the basilica with sacred melody.

The great doors swung open.

The Agemas stepped onto the cream-colored runner first—an honor traditionally reserved for the Knights Templar. Today, however, the knights of Valdina, representing the bride's homeland, led the procession.

The congregation murmured in appreciation at the solemn display.

The Bride

And then, at last—the bride appeared.

Her hand rested in her brother's.

White silk flowed like moonlight made fabric.

Silver hair gleamed beneath the stained-glass radiance.

The basilica fell into absolute silence.
