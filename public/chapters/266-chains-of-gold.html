Chapter 266

Chains of
Gold

"The most binding gifts are those wrapped in velvet."

Terence was not merely a follower of the First Prince—he was a trusted confidant who had returned to Marquis Ipoleto's service specifically to oversee the delivery of these gifts.

Gallo, as head of the Facade, had long been acquainted with Cesare. Thus, to avoid revealing any prior connection to the First Prince, only Terence could serve as the appropriate envoy.

"Without question. His Highness regards this marriage with the utmost seriousness."

"..."

"This is not merely a political arrangement. His Highness hopes that this modest gesture of sincerity will convey his intention to become true family to Valdina."

Terence withdrew something that resembled a thick leather-bound volume.

The cover was black leather, its corners trimmed in gold.

"His Highness has instructed that this be delivered separately to Her Highness the Princess before the wedding."

When he opened the first page, layer upon layer of certificates appeared—each one nestled in velvet, bound together with meticulous care.

"This is..."

Sissair found himself momentarily speechless.

It was a collection of deeds—estates, authority, and assets that the First Prince intended to transfer entirely to his bride.

With each turn of the page, seals and wax stamps clinked softly against one another.

Property Transfers

'Guild seats in the Romagna fiefdoms... warhorse ranches in the Principality of Benapro... diamond mines in Alphanon...'

All of it—transferred to the bride?

Since when had the First Prince of Katzen become so generous?

Terence added:

"Oh, and as you'll see at the end—there is also the deed to the Golden Rose Garden. It lies on the outskirts of the capital, so unlike the other properties, Her Highness will be able to visit at her leisure."

"..."

Romagna. Benapro. Alphanon.

The names were unmistakable—fiefdoms and conquests under the First Prince's direct control.

Sissair's brow furrowed.

When the First Prince had been a man marked for death, he had understood why the Emperor could never quite succeed in crushing his power.

'He's held the purse strings all along.'

"Her Highness the Princess is leaving her homeland behind. His Highness wishes to ensure she will not feel isolated in a foreign land."

Terence allowed one eye to close in a subtle wink.

"However, I trust a minister of your brilliance will understand precisely why this cannot be conveyed to Her Highness through official channels."

The velvet-wrapped deeds had passed through multiple owners before finally being transferred to Princess Medea—a complex trail designed to obscure their origin.

Sissair was silent for a long moment before casting a cold glance through his spectacles.

"...You reveal the source of His Highness's hidden treasury so brazenly to us?"

The private wealth of the First Prince—assets unknown even to his father, Emperor Perdiccas II?

Earlier

Terence paused at Sissair's question, removing his glasses to rub his weary face.

'I didn't particularly want to reveal that either.'

The memory surfaced unbidden—Gallo's voice, incredulous:

"Are you certain you want to surrender all of this? What happens if the marriage ends after three years, as agreed?"

Before Gallo could finish, Cesare's sharp voice had cut through.

"That depends entirely on the circumstances."

"But Her Highness was quite clear about the terms—"

Hadn't she explicitly stated it was a three-year contract?

Gallo had tilted his head in confusion. Terence had stared at his friend, wondering if perhaps he'd struck his head during the descent from Locke.

Then came Cesare's unwavering voice:

"When Dea becomes Crown Princess, I will make her the mistress of Romagna. Begin preparations now so the transfer can proceed without delay."

The guild seats were merely a foundation to minimize internal resistance. But Romagna was the First Prince's own fiefdom—the land where he had been born and raised.

Setting aside the audacity of treating the Crown Prince's throne—not yet confirmed—as a foregone conclusion...

"She needn't feel bound to honor the contract."

Don't be deceived by the beautiful wrapping.

This was his calculated design to bind Medea to him permanently.

"Wait—does Her Highness the Princess share this understanding?"

"Did it sound as though I was soliciting your opinion?"

Gallo's mouth fell open. Terence could only shake his head.

Present

"Marquis?"

Terence snapped back to attention at the sound of Sissair's voice. He replaced his glasses.

"We are to become family. What cause is there for concern? The First Prince has proven himself exceedingly devoted to those who have captured his heart."

'Ah... Your Highness the Princess. My sincerest apologies—you've been ensnared by something quite dangerous.'

He offered a silent apology to Medea in his heart.

He ought to warn her, he knew. But if Cesare discovered such a betrayal, Terence might find himself cast into some forsaken abyss for the remainder of his days.

"Still, I never expected him to change so profoundly."

A faint smile touched Terence's lips.

Perhaps it was the countless storms they had weathered together over the years.

His friend Cesare, when it came to matters of the heart, had always been so cold and detached that one might stab him and draw not a single drop of blood.

No attachments. No regrets over severed bonds.

'So you were human after all, Cesare. Beyond mere survival, something has finally taken hold of you.'

But now—watching him offer his most treasured possessions like a child desperate to win someone's favor—there was a strange comfort in witnessing such earnest longing.

"All that remains is for you to convey His Highness's sentiments to Her Highness."

"..."

When no response came, Terence turned to study Sissair more closely.

A slender frame. A complexion that spoke of exhaustion.

Though they had only just met, Terence noted a certain kinship between them.

'Gallo mentioned he seemed carved from stone, but he's not nearly as impassive as reported.'

Despite his composed exterior, the man was clearly struggling—caught in a tempest of conflicting emotions that flickered just beneath the surface.

"Your Excellency. Is the missing Sir D'Angel now with Her Highness the Princess?"

Ever since word of Medea's oracle had reached him, Sissair had not slept a single peaceful night.

"Your Excellency! Word from His Majesty! Her Highness the Princess will soon return to Valdina!"

The months he had spent protecting her and safeguarding Valdina in the aftermath of the Continental Congress now seemed like distant memories.

The announcement of Medea's betrothal had struck like a thunderbolt.

'Why did I never consider this possibility?'

Even with his formidable intellect, the inheritance of the oracle in this particular form—through Medea's marriage—had been wholly unforeseen.

His rational mind accepted the inevitability of her choice. His heart, however, refused to follow.

Before he could quiet the chaos within, the First Prince's carriages had arrived in a relentless procession.

'The betrothal of Cesare Katzen and Her Royal Highness the Princess.'

The image of the Princess—still young, still innocent—overlaid with the shadow of Katzen's arrogant and dangerous First Prince made his heart plummet.

'Not yet. Too soon. If only Her Highness were a little older... if only a little more time had passed...'

In that moment, Sissair understood.

He understood why his intellect had so stubbornly refused to accept the news of Medea's engagement.

'You fool. You've forgotten your place as a servant.'

The realization washed over him like ice water poured from above.

Sissair released a bitter, self-deprecating chuckle and shook his head.

Behind his spectacles, his gaze fell upon the stiff certificates once more.

"..."

What all three documents shared in common: they represented the unofficial financial channels of the First Prince.

If these fell into Medea's hands, the First Prince's own servants would have no choice but to bow before her.

Not merely nominal courtesy from a royal spouse—but genuine, tangible power.

"Then Her Highness will be able to exercise her will in Katzen as freely as she does in Valdina."

The realization pierced him, drawing a short, incredulous laugh.

'The ostentatious gifts sent earlier were merely bait—a distraction to conceal these deeds.'

Even the First Prince could not openly present his bride with weapons capable of severing his own limbs.

"Yes... I am grateful for His Highness the First Prince's gracious consideration."

Despite his fearsome reputation, the First Prince of Katzen had demonstrated an extraordinary commitment to this engagement.

Just as Peleus understood the root cause of his own reluctance regarding Medea's match, so too did Sissair now see a path to protecting her within Katzen.

These deeds would form the foundation of Medea's independent influence in a foreign land.

The certificates before him seemed to strike his cheek—a silent rebuke, reminding him never to forget his duty.

"Rest assured, I shall convey His Highness the First Prince's intentions to both His Majesty and Her Royal Highness with the gravity they deserve."

Sissair's expression grew more severe than before.

"Given such generosity, Valdina would naturally wish to offer a fitting response. However, please understand that adequate preparations will require time."

At Sissair's words, Terence waved his hand dismissively.

"No, no. His Highness desires nothing in return. He has, however, made one earnest request—that all arrangements proceed with haste, so the ceremony may be conducted smoothly and Her Highness properly attended to in her preparations."

The words were politely phrased, but the meaning was unmistakable.

He wished to advance the wedding date before anyone had time to prepare alternatives.

'I didn't want to be the one to say this...'

Even Terence's face flushed as he delivered the message.

To shower them with extravagant gifts and then demand the wedding be expedited—who could deliver such a brazen request with a straight face?

"...That matter lies beyond my authority. However, I shall convey it to His Majesty."

Mercifully, no insults followed. Sissair's reply was perfectly neutral.

But Terence did not know.

Beneath the table, hidden within his sleeve, Sissair's fingernails dug deep into his palms.

The tender ache in his heart—still unnamed, still unacknowledged—sank silently into darkness before he could grasp its meaning.

The Imperial Palace of Valdina

The formidable inventory of gifts dispatched by First Prince Cesare was soon brought before Peleus.

"Your Majesty, observe the luster of these horses! If they truly hail from the Principality of Benapro, they must rank among the finest steeds in the entire empire!"

Human nature tends toward seeing only what it wishes to see.

"And these consecrated weapons would prove invaluable for suppressing demonic incursions—"

D'Angel, who had been enthusiastically perusing the lengthy inventory that seemed to stretch to the floor, suddenly sensed a chill emanating from beside him.

He fell silent.

Peleus's eyes had narrowed, fixing D'Angel with a glacial stare.

"...My apologies, Your Majesty."

The young Emperor's gaze was as cold and cutting as a winter wind.
