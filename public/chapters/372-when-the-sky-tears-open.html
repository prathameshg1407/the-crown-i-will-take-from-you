Chapter 38

When the Sky
Tears Open

"In the face of obsession, even reality itself can be sacrificed."

Cesare stared at the figure that emerged from beneath Paul's torn flesh.

"A little worm that escaped from the Golden Tower."

His voice was cold, clinical—the tone of someone cataloging vermin.

Before he could say more, his body was yanked roughly backward.

He turned toward the owner of that familiar warmth, and there was Medea—pale as daybreak, eyes wide with fear.

"Dea."

Before he could answer, her small hand flew to his neck, fingers pressing against his pulse.

She pressed her white hand to the nape of his neck where his heartbeat hammered frantically, needing to feel that he was alive.

"Dea, it's all right. I'm alive."

Cesare read the terror in her eyes and pulled her into his arms, wrapping her slender shoulders protectively.

"I thought—I thought you were dying again!"

Medea struck his chest with her fist, the blow carrying all her frustrated fear.

"Just a little surprise."

It wasn't the first time he'd suddenly intervened in a dangerous situation. But this time felt different. Heavier.

"An unbreakable oath! What if something had gone wrong in the middle?!"

Even Medea felt a chill run down her spine.

She would never gamble so recklessly—staking everything on a single moment.

Cesare smiled faintly, accepting her gentle violence without complaint. He stroked her temple with tender fingers.

"I'm sorry I startled you, Dea. But I would do it again—a thousand times over—if it meant protecting you."

Silence.

"So you shouldn't leave. Unless you plan on killing your husband."

Medea looked up at him, overcome with emotion.

Then another piercing scream tore through the hall.

Everyone's gaze turned toward the sound.

"It hurts! Save me! Jason, help me!"

Jason froze at the sound of his name escaping Rachel's lips.

"Jason! You promised! Our plan can't fail like this!"

A grand plan. They promised each other?

At those words, the last fragile hope in people's hearts crumbled to dust.

All eyes turned to Jason.

With that gentle face, that noble bearing—he was working with the forces of darkness all along?

The Saintess was revealed to be a fraud.

Even the Vicar Paul and the countless priests of the Holy Nation were impostors.

People's minds went blank. Then an even more terrifying question arose.

"What happened to the real Holy Nation?!"

"Something terrible must have befallen the Pope!"

"Has the goddess abandoned this continent? How can black magicians run wild like this?!"

People were beyond shocked. They felt fear.

What could possibly be happening on this continent that the central temple—the very pinnacle of faith—had been infiltrated by those who worshiped the primordial darkness?

Rachel shouted desperately at the silent Jason.

"Do you think you'll be safe if I die?!"

It was a vicious threat, laced with venom.

"...You idiot."

Jason's expression twisted with contempt.

From the very beginning, Rachel's plan had been nothing more than a sideshow to him.

All he needed—all he had ever needed—was to get his hands on Medea.

But now the tables had turned.

Jason raised his head and glared at Cesare standing on the altar.

How could he have known that man would make an unbreakable, eternal oath before the Emperor himself?

If Cesare was insane enough to risk everything, then Jason had no choice but to match that madness.

'Medea, watch closely. See how far I'll go for you.'

"If I collapse like this, you'll never get that woman you want!"

Rachel suddenly rushed toward Jason and clung to him, begging for help.

Now that all the warlocks had fallen, she realized instinctively that this man was the only thread she could grasp.

"I understand. Now shut up."

Splash!

Jason doused Rachel with something, his expression filled with cold contempt.

A blue liquid, glimmering in the dim light.

Rachel thought it was holy water that would heal her burning scars.

But—

"Wait—what is this?! What did you just spray on me?!"

It wasn't holy water at all.

It was a mark—a stigma identifying the first victim for the ritual to come.

Black smoke enveloped her instantly.

Rachel flailed, still unable to grasp what was happening to her.

CRACK!

From Rachel's feet to the base of the goddess statue, the cathedral floor split in a jagged line.

"No... no way..."

Fear and rage flooded Rachel's eyes.

Dark smoke coiled around her entire body.

She recognized this—the same sign that appeared when black magic consumed its offering and began the ritual.

"How—how could you use me as a sacrifice?!"

Jason looked down at Rachel as she writhed, engulfed in black smoke.

"Originally, it should have been Cesare. Unfortunate, but there's nothing to be done about it."

Then, in the blink of an eye, the magic circle erupted through the crack and swallowed Rachel whole.

People stood frozen, unable to move at the shocking sight.

The Cathedral Falls

BOOOOM!

The pillars of the cathedral shook violently in unison.

The essence of black magic, having devoured its sacrifice, surged upward from beneath the earth in writhing layers.

Through the widening cracks, an endless stream of maggots, worms, and all manner of crawling things poured forth.

"Ugh—!"

At the same moment, black hands erupted from the fissures and seized the ankles and calves of everyone inside the cathedral.

"My legs—I can't move!"

"Let go! Let go! We have to get out of here!"

Chaos.

Jason watched as the black magic completed its preparations.

His one and only wish would soon come true.

He bit his lip until he tasted copper.

"Medea... yes. I see now. The one you chose is different from me."

Was it the thrill of this ancient magic succeeding that filled his mouth now?

Or was it the bitter taste of defeat—knowing she had chosen someone else in her past life?

He couldn't tell.

But even if it was the latter, he would never admit it.

'If your choice is not me, then I have no choice but to correct it—over and over—until it becomes me.'

The sky began to distort before Jason's eyes.

It was the moment when black magic rewound time itself, sacrificing everything inside the cathedral.

Medea heard Rachel's scream pierce the air.

When she turned her head, she saw Jason pushing Rachel forward as the first sacrifice to activate the black magic that would turn back time.

"Jason... your actions remain the same, even when your victims change."

Medea muttered softly.

That selfishness—that willingness to betray anyone for his own sake.

In this life, Rachel was merely the scapegoat for that betrayal.

How ironic. Two people who had been married in a past life, now locked in a cycle of mutual betrayal.

CRASH!

With a deafening roar, the ceiling of the cathedral was torn off.

Stones and dust rained down among the chaos.

A gust of icy wind howled into the hall, striking Medea's cheek like a slap.

Black smoke swirled upward from the center of the magic circle beneath the goddess statue.

Suddenly, a giant hand rose from the depths.

"Medea!"

The black hand seized her—she who stood closest to the statue of the goddess—and lifted her into the air in one swift motion.

Clutching her tightly, it surged toward Jason.

It happened in the blink of an eye.

Jason stretched out his arms toward Medea as she was carried closer.

"Medea, my wife, my love, my companion—we're finally together."

Madness flashed in his red eyes.

The cathedral in chaos, the screaming crowd—none of it registered. He saw only Medea.

"If I were given another chance, I could do better than anyone else! This time, I wouldn't make a single mistake."

It was a desperate, heart-wrenching delusion—the belief that going back to the past would solve everything, that he could reclaim all he had lost.

And black magic was the perfect vessel for such malice and desire.

"Medea, let's go back together. Now."

The black hand was about to lower her into Jason's waiting arms—

SLASH!

A blue flash split the smoke.

Cesare's aura severed the black hand in one clean stroke.

At the same instant, his strong arms caught Medea as she fell, snatching her from the void.

As soon as he set her down in a safe place, Cesare surged straight toward Jason.

Hissss—

The black hand regenerated from the severed stump, reaching once more for Medea.

"Cesare, no matter how much you struggle, you can't separate Medea and me! She and I are destined to be together!"

Before Jason could finish speaking, a shadow fell across his face.

"If you covet another man's wife, you deserve to die."

Cesare's blade descended, filled with nothing but chilling, murderous intent.

CLANG! CLANG! CLANG!

Jason barely managed to block it with his sword, the force reverberating through his bones.

"You have the Emperor's power—so what? Do you really think you can stop this?"

If you can stop me, go ahead and try.

Jason's lips twisted into a mocking smile.

Right then—

"THE SKY—!"

"THE SKY IS TEARING APART!"

He heard people screaming in terror.

Above them, reality itself began to fracture.
