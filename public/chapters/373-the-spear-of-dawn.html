Chapter 39

The Spear
of Dawn

"When darkness threatens to consume all, the goddess's champion rises with light incarnate."

Through the blown-open ceiling of the cathedral, they could see a massive vortex consuming the sky.

A dark, primordial color spread across the blue heavens like spilled ink, twisting and churning as though sculpting reality itself.

"It's a temporal fracture! Time has been forcibly reversed—space-time itself is distorting!"

Terence's voice cracked as he shouted, teeth clenched in horror.

Between the torn rifts, a kaleidoscope of twisted colors flickered—fragments of broken time and shattered space.

"Wait—if the fabric tears like this, then the demons from the cracks will—"

The sight of those otherworldly colors momentarily stole everyone's breath.

"AAAAHHHHH!"

Massive crimson eyes flashed through the cracks.

SHRIIIIIEK!

A monstrous bird spread its colossal wings and descended through the rift, blotting out what remained of the sky.

"Run! Get out of the way! RUN!"

Demons began to rain from the sky like a plague of locusts.

The cathedral transformed instantly into pandemonium.

"It's too late! The magic has already begun!"

Jason's voice rang out amid the chaos, drunk with manic glee.

"The Golden Rule's clock is turning! Now neither you, Cesare, nor anyone else can stop it!"

Time itself—the past drawing ever closer—is on my side now, Jason Katzen!

Cesare regarded the scene with ice-cold eyes, then spoke with chilling mockery.

"This isn't the first time you've been a fool, Jason. But this time, I don't need to keep you alive."

"You—!"

"Every time you stick your neck out, you waste it on such useless things."

CLANG! CLANG!

Their swords clashed with savage intensity.

Jason fought with everything he had against Cesare's black aura, but all he could do was tremble and barely hold his ground against the overwhelming disparity in skill.

"What confidence could you possibly have with such pitiful technique, Jason?"

Jason gasped for breath as he desperately parried Cesare's relentless strikes.

Even pouring every ounce of experience from his past life into the duel, Jason couldn't overcome him.

But Jason was no longer anxious.

He knew he would be the victor of this final confrontation.

Peleus sprinted toward Medea as she touched down on solid ground.

Meanwhile, the black hands—manifestations of Jason's obsession—continued their relentless advance.

"Protect Dea!"

"Yes, Your Majesty!"

The Agemas knights severed the grasping black hands as fast as they could regenerate.

Then Gallo's terrified cry pierced the air.

"Sir Neriel—your body!"

Medea spun around.

Neriel's form was dissolving. The lines of her armor rippled and flickered, colors bleeding in and out of existence.

"What in the world—?!"

"Be careful! This could be black magic!"

One by one, the bodies of the Agemas knights began to fade the same way.

And then—

"Your Majesty!"

Peleus.

Medea's gaze snapped to Cesare.

His form, too, was becoming unstable.

'Things that never existed in this timeline are being erased.'

Medea understood in an instant.

"Don't let your guard down! Dea's protection is our top priority!"

Peleus barked orders even as his own body flickered in and out of existence.

But—

"Damn it, there are too many!"

Monsters poured from the sky like a cascading waterfall of nightmares.

"These are primordial monsters—the ones that existed before the goddess banished the darkness!"

Terence fumbled desperately for his magic stones, hands trembling.

Everyone's hands shook at the sight of the darkness that exceeded their worst nightmares.

Meanwhile, the rift in the sky crept slowly, inexorably closer to the ground.

"Peleus."

Medea raised her head, assessing the rift's progression with calculating eyes.

"Give me time. You and the Agemas—hold them back."

"Dea, it's too dangerous. You stay here. I'll—"

Peleus grabbed her arm.

But Medea shook her head—firmly, yet calmly.

"From this moment forward, this is my fight."

Divine Manifestation

Medea drew her sword.

Pure white light shimmered along the blade's razor edge.

It was an aura unlike anything Peleus had ever witnessed—the power far clearer, sharper, more divine than any knight's technique.

"Please. Do me this favor."

Medea turned and rushed forward without waiting for a response.

Peleus reached out to stop her, then froze mid-motion.

As his sister ran forward to save everything, he could have sworn he saw white wings unfurling around her.

SHRIIIIIEK!

The clash of swords echoed with an eerie, ear-piercing screech.

Even in this hellscape where reality fractured and primordial horrors poured forth, Jason and Cesare's blades remained locked in their deadly dance.

But the outcome was already decided.

CRASH!

As though delivering the final blow, Cesare's black aura surged forward.

Slash!

Jason's shoulder split open.

But then—

Cesare hesitated.

The air around his hand rippled and twisted like heat haze.

"Didn't I tell you?! You can never beat me!"

Jason's laughter rang out as the intense aura Cesare had been wielding gradually lost its strength.

"So that's what the clock hands meant."

Cesare looked up at the sky.

The violent vortex spun counterclockwise, unwinding time itself.

"You died long ago, Cesare. How can a dead man defeat the living?"

Not missing the moment his strength faltered, Jason aimed for Cesare's shoulder.

Splash!

Hot blood arced through the air.

"Yes, Cesare Katzen—do you understand now? You should already be a skeleton rotting in the ground!"

Jason shouted in triumph, addressing not only Cesare but Medea as well.

"Look, Dea! Look!"

Jason pointed skyward.

The afterimage distorted as reality twisted—and the imperial crown flickered above his head, appearing and disappearing in stuttering intervals.

Finally!

Jason was certain.

Everything would fall into place.

Doesn't this crown—growing heavier on my head—prove it? I am the chosen victor, the great Emperor of Katzen, the triumphant hero of this story!

Cesare, too, stared at the Emperor's crown solidifying above Jason's head.

'So after I died, that bastard really did become Emperor of Katzen.'

Hearing about it and seeing it with his own eyes were two vastly different things.

'He drove Dea to her death wearing that crown.'

Rage boiled within him like molten steel in a furnace.

He felt as though he were experiencing the humiliation Medea had endured with his entire being.

"What a waste—a worthless mongrel taking over an empty house and making a racket."

His jaw hardened with grim determination.

"Then enjoy the glory of being slain by the dead. It'll make quite the spectacle for your final moments."

His black aura struck Jason with savage fury.

It was punishment delivered on Medea's behalf.

"You bastard!"

Jason was stunned that such power remained even as the black magic progressed.

But soon, Cesare's aura began to lose its color. Time was forcibly binding him, pulling him toward oblivion.

Splash!

Seizing the moment of weakness, Jason's blade drove deep into Cesare's shoulder.

Cesare's hot blood scattered through the air.

Damn!

When Jason's sword—cruel intent blazing in his eyes—aimed for Cesare's heart—

CLANG!

Another blade intercepted it.

"Jason."

Medea twisted her sword, deflecting Jason's strike, then pushed Cesare back to safety before facing Jason herself.

Jason swallowed his frustration at the failed killing blow and twisted his lips into a smile.

"Medea, now is the time."

Triumph spread across his face like poison.

"Come back with me."

Silence.

"Let's return to the beginning. To the place where we were happy."

"No."

Medea raised her head.

Her green eyes held no fear. No hesitation.

"I have waited for this moment, Jason. The moment when I would crush your last, desperate hope."

"...What?"

The instant Medea finished speaking—

"NOW!"

The mercenaries of the Facade rushed toward the five black pillars encircling the magic circle.

THUNK!

Daggers embedded themselves deep into each pillar.

The Goddess's Will

At the same moment, Theo began chanting the sacred incantation.

"In the name of the goddess, I declare: The evil darkness that defies the providence of this world—split asunder and vanish into nothingness."

White light erupted from the five daggers.

Five rays of radiant power flowed through the magic circle like purifying fire, burning away the corruption.

Crack... crack... crack...

A splintering sound rang out from the center beneath the goddess statue.

The sharp, intricate lines of the black magic circle began to fracture and split apart.

Jason's body staggered mid-swing.

"What—what is—?!"

His eyes went wide with horror. The massive magic circle beneath his feet was shattering.

Then—

Urk!

Jason coughed up crimson blood.

The dark power of the ritual flowed backward, turning against him.

The black hands reaching for Medea convulsed and froze mid-grasp.

The screams and wingbeats of the descending monsters grew weaker.

"No... this can't be..."

Jason stared in disbelief.

"NO! NO!"

He screamed.

In that moment, Jason felt exactly what Rachel had experienced mere minutes before—rising to the pinnacle of joy only to plummet into the abyss.

"Remember, Jason? It was the goddess's power that extinguished the primordial darkness in the first place."

What?

Medea's voice cut through his crumbling consciousness.

"There is a fragment of my pendant—imbued with divine power—embedded in each of their blades."

Jason's face contorted in rage and despair.

He tried to lunge forward, but a white beam of light blocked his path like a divine spear.

"So tell me, Jason—where does this power I wield come from?"

Go ahead. Guess.

Medea raised her arms.

FLASH!

Another beam of light erupted from the tip of her sword in a brilliant lance.

SHRIIIIIEK! ROAAAAAR!

The white beam split the air and pierced straight through the hearts of the beasts.

Creatures that had seized people with their talons—

Titans that had trampled the streets—

Gray smoke rose from each pierced spot as the darkness evaporated.

The Goddess's Champion

The beam of light that destroyed them looked like a spear of pure dawn.

It was almighty power incarnate—

The force that could banish and obliterate all darkness with a single strike.

End Chapter 39
