Chapter 236

The Lovers'
Crimson Requiem

"In the end, even the most ruthless hunters become prey."

Which came first—the dagger plunging into his chest, or the blood welling from his lips?

Samon staggered backward, his legs failing him. Marieu twisted her wrist with practiced precision and wrenched the blade free. Blood erupted in a violent arc, spattering across marble and silk alike.

She struck again.

"Were you planning to abandon me again? Cast me aside for that precious Saintess of yours?"

Marieu's lips curved into something between a smile and a snarl.

"No, Samon. Never again. You will never abandon me again."

She shook her head slowly, almost tenderly, as her arm rose and fell without mercy.

His body had gone rigid. His limbs refused to obey.

All the rage Marieu had swallowed for years—every humiliation, every discarded promise—she poured it all into the unmoving form of the man who had treated her like refuse.

"Let us die together, my darling. Like the legendary lovers of old—together on the same day, at the same hour."

"Ugh... hah... hah..."

Drenched in his own blood, Samon could barely draw breath, let alone form words.

The promising young Duke Claudio. Bearer of royal blood. A man whose ambition had seemed limitless, whose talent had made him the envy of the empire.

Now he was being torn apart by the one person he had dismissed as utterly beneath him—nothing more than dust on the sole of his boot.

A pitifully shabby end for such grand aspirations.

"Just like your mother. A beautiful finale, don't you think?"

Marieu's voice dripped with venomous satisfaction.

"More than fitting for a beast like you."

"Hah... hah... hah...!"

Samon could only clutch at his ruined chest, each ragged breath more desperate than the last. The wounds were so numerous, so deep, that his continued consciousness seemed a cruel miracle.

Then Marieu raised her dagger one final time.

"I will apologize to our child. Sincerely. With you by my side."

For the briefest moment, something flickered in her eyes—something almost human beneath the madness.

And then—

Marieu crumpled into the spreading pool of crimson.

"AAAAAAAH—!"

A scream—raw and primal—echoed through the vaulted ceiling.

But most of the onlookers could not even breathe. They stood frozen before the carnage, minds unable to process the brutality they had just witnessed.

"Move aside! Out of the way!"

Priests shouldered through the paralyzed crowd, their urgency cutting through the shock.

As the spectators retreated, the full horror became visible—two bodies lying in a spreading lake of blood.

"She has no breath left."

The priest who examined Marieu shook his head grimly.

"This one still lives!"

By some twisted grace, Samon Claudio clung to life.

But barely.

"Quickly—a physician! No, there's no time! His condition is far too critical...!"

Blood bubbled from his lips with every gasping breath. He seemed on the verge of drowning in his own fluids. Even divine intervention appeared inadequate.

"High Priest! High Priest Paul—!"

"I'm already doing everything I can!"

Paul's voice cracked with strain.

"The rest of you—stop gawking and help me!"

He would not allow these wretched deaths to desecrate the sacred memorial ceremony.

"Damn it all. Too many wounds, and the poison has bonded with his blood—purification simply will not take hold!"

The attending priests' power was insufficient. In truth, the "abilities" Rachel's followers claimed to possess were hollow pretense—Paul alone bore the burden of genuine effort.

"Oh dear. This is quite troublesome."

A clear, composed voice cut through the chaos. Medea.

"Saintess, I beseech you—lend us your aid."

Her gaze fixed upon Rachel with unsettling calm.

"Surely the holy power that vanquished those ghouls can pull Samon back from death's threshold."

She paused, letting the words settle.

"Setting aside the particulars of the situation... should we not prevent this sacred occasion from being marred by such misfortune?"

Particulars.

The Princess's choice of words carried unmistakable weight.

Only moments ago, the dying lover had screamed accusations for all to hear—that Samon had been intimate with the Saintess herself.

Rachel's carefully maintained composure fractured, almost imperceptibly.

She knows. Is that what she's implying?

That Samon was mine?

That the maid meant to frame Medea ended up killing him instead...?

Wariness and suspicion coiled together in Rachel's mind.

If Medea knew about my arrangement with Samon...

Then those words just now—they're a snare designed to trap me.

Rachel felt the walls closing in. Every path forward seemed to lead to ruin.

"Ugh—!"

While Rachel wrestled with her impossible choices, Samon's breath ceased.

"My lord... he is gone."

"Ah—!"

High Priest Paul collapsed where he knelt, energy utterly depleted, sweat streaming down his ashen face.

"What a tragedy."

Medea released a sigh of apparent regret.

"Had the Saintess acted just a moment sooner... Samon might yet have lived."

Silence.

"Ah, well."

Her voice softened to something almost philosophical.

"Perhaps this was simply the fate the goddess ordained for him."

Rachel turned away without a word.

Samon lay motionless, mouth agape, eyes fixed and staring. In those lifeless orbs, the final expression remained frozen: utter shock at his lover's betrayal.

Samon had always looked down upon Marieu—her humble origins, her modest station.

He had discarded her without a second thought whenever she became inconvenient, then drawn her back without remorse the moment he found her useful again.

To him, she had been nothing more than a disposable tool. Something to be used, enjoyed, and thrown away at will. He had offered his affection as though it were charity, bestowed upon one unworthy of receiving it.

Never—not once—had he imagined that his end would come at the hands of one he had so thoroughly taken for granted.

"A most unfortunate incident has disrupted our sacred proceedings. We have no choice but to conclude today's memorial service."

"Yes, indeed... this year's ceremony has come to its end, it seems."

"We were so hopeful when the Saintess arrived... who could have foreseen such horror?"

Murmurs rippled through the crowd as people exchanged uneasy glances. None wished to linger at the site of such carnage.

Soon the central corridor fell silent, emptied of spectators. Only the priests remained, grimly collecting the bodies and scrubbing at the evidence.

"We shall take custody of Samon's body. Under no circumstances is it to be surrendered to the temple."

"Understood, Your Highness. I shall see to it personally."

Neril nodded in agreement. With Rachel still within the temple's walls, handing over Samon's remains would be tantamount to giving her ammunition for whatever scheme she might devise next.

The corridor floor remained stained deep crimson—a visceral testament to the violence that had unfolded. The priests worked frantically to erase the evidence, but the blood had already seeped into the crevices between the tiles, stubbornly resisting their efforts.

Medea watched as Marieu's body was borne away on a stretcher.

"Your Highness. Marieu has returned the antidote. She indicated... it would no longer be necessary."

When Marieu had conspired with the Chief Maid to frame Medea, her life had been spared in exchange for a periodic supply of the antidote—insurance against the slow poison coursing through certain veins.

I knew even then. That she intended to end everything alongside Samon.

Having completed her vengeance, Marieu had closed her eyes and surrendered to stillness. Her pale features, so often shadowed by bitterness and despair, now appeared strangely at peace.

She looked free—as though she had finally shed the weight of every dark emotion that had haunted her.

When all of this is over... will I wear that same expression? Will I know that kind of freedom?

"Don't look, Dea."

A cool hand covered her eyes, gently blocking the grim tableau.

"They have merely met the ends they earned."

"Peleus!"

Medea spun around.

Her brother stood before her—finally returned from the central trade route, his conflict with Katzen resolved at last.

"You've grown thin."

Worry creased his features, but Medea didn't hesitate. She stepped forward and wrapped her arms around him.

The armor was cold against her cheek. Yet his embrace—filled with longing and fierce protectiveness—radiated a warmth that pierced straight to her heart.

The western terminus of the El Amuz Strait.

A solitary figure stood at the precipice, where savage winds and driving rain conspired against anything foolish enough to remain upright.

The hem of her tattered gown whipped violently in the howling gale. Her pink hair, saturated by the relentless downpour, clung to her skull like a second skin.

Between the ghostly flutter of sodden fabric and the tempest that raged around her, the scene possessed an almost spectral quality.

"Hmmm... hmm hmm..."

The woman humming softly into the storm was Birna Claudio.
