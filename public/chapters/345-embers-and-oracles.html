Chapter 345

Embers
and Oracles

"When one fire dies, another kindles in the ashes."

Fury blazed in Jason's crimson eyes.

"So you think he's different from me?"

"Yes."

Medea's answer came without hesitation.

"It's an insult to even compare you to him."

"He's exactly like me. The moment he sits on that throne, he won't be your man anymore. He'll change. Just as I did."

"Just admit it, Jason. You were a coward from the very beginning. Human scum masquerading as a hero."

Medea looked down at him with utter indifference.

"He's different."

Jason felt his sanity fray under the weight of jealousy.

Her infinite trust in Cesare gnawed at his mind like acid.

Those eyes—once blind with devotion to him—now saw someone else entirely.

It was mine. That love was my share.

Even now, he wanted to hunt down the man somewhere within this villa and tear him apart for stealing what belonged to Jason alone.

"Your Highness, I've been looking for you. What's—Grand Duke Castullo?"

The Eastern Empress appeared in the corridor.

Her eyes widened at the sight of Jason standing so close to Medea, his expression twisted with barely restrained violence.

"You'll come back to me eventually."

Jason's whisper was venomous.

"You'll realize soon enough—he's no different."

He turned sharply and strode away, movements stiff with fury.

"Are you all right? Why did the Grand Duke look so... unhinged?"

The Eastern Empress had only ever seen Jason meek and obedient before Perdiccas II.

Is this who he truly is?

She shuddered, unsettled by the madness she'd glimpsed in those blood-red eyes.

"Where are your escorts? You must keep them close at all times. If you continue like this, he'll do something reckless."

Medea nodded at the Eastern Empress's warning and returned to her seat.

Neril approached swiftly.

Though Medea appeared calm, worry flickered in the knight's eyes.

"Your Highness, are you unharmed?"

"Everything went exactly as I anticipated."

Medea had deliberately left Neril behind—creating an opportunity for Jason to approach.

"He murdered the Western Empress."

Medea's gaze dropped to the scabbed wound on the back of Jason's hand—evidence she'd glimpsed during their confrontation.

"And he's the one who freed Rachel."

Jason's mocking prophecy of Cesare's betrayal had confirmed it.

"I wondered how Rachel escaped the Black Floor."

As the Temple Knights had proven with their deaths, the Black Floor's security was formidable.

"Only insiders know the location of the Holy Prison. Outsiders have no way of finding it."

Medea had wondered who could have reached Rachel through such impenetrable defenses.

"Jason could. He knows the Central Temple inside and out."

In her past life, he'd been the Saintess's companion.

Just as Rachel had risen to become Empress of Katzen, Jason had served as the Holy Kingdom's closest ally.

"There were traces of Castullo at the warlock's stronghold."

Hearing this, Cesare had also begun piecing together Jason's involvement.

"Gallo reported that a few months ago, Castullo's carriage was spotted traveling to and from the Holy Kingdom's border."

Gallo had apologized profusely, explaining that he'd dismissed it as the Grand Duke gathering intelligence—snooping because he'd lost his foothold in Katzen.

Medea nodded slowly.

Jason. Rachel.

The two who had destroyed her in her past life now revealed themselves once more—resilient as weeds, thriving no matter how many times she cut them down.

In the end, they've joined hands. It seems the bond forged in that previous lifetime is unbreakable.

"If they won't learn without experiencing it firsthand, then they'll repeat the same mistakes."

Medea's voice was cold.

"Fools."

A sigh escaped her lips.

"Then I have no choice but to let them suffer the consequences themselves."

◆◆◆

Outside the villa, a carriage bearing the Castullo coat of arms waited.

Jason climbed inside, his expression thunderous.

But he was not alone.

"The Western Empress's death is still under investigation, yet you parade around like this? Are you trying to get us caught?"

The warlock Rachel had sent glared at him with irritation.

"Have you forgotten what we're trying to accomplish? If your recklessness exposes our plan, we're all finished!"

Jason's hand shot out, seizing the warlock by the collar and slamming him against the carriage wall.

"I haven't forgotten. But neither should you—who gave you the means to survive?"

His crimson eyes glowed with eerie intensity.

The warlock swallowed, suddenly reminded that Katzen's entire royal family was dangerously unstable.

"I-I remember. How could I forget?"

"We need to accelerate the plan. If we delay any longer and even a single Templar escapes, our involvement will be exposed."

The warlock pressed his lips together nervously.

How does he know about the Black Floor's inner workings? Only someone with inside knowledge could...

"There's one more element to add to the ritual."

A cruel light flickered across Jason's face.

"If they refuse the oracle, let the gods punish them."

With this divine proclamation, everything would shift in their favor.

Medea, no matter how clever you are, you won't be able to handle this.

His eyes burned with savage triumph.

"Yes... that could work."

The warlock nodded slowly.

Divine punishment would make the plan far more effective.

However, invoking such power against her would require a substantial sacrifice.

Fortunately, the corpses of slain Temple Knights lay piled in the Black Floor like cordwood.

They had wielded pure holy power—their bodies had not decayed.

"The day will come soon."

The warlock rubbed his temples and vanished like smoke.

Left alone, Jason stared through the carriage window at the distant palace.

You believe in him, don't you, Medea? You claim Cesare is different?

"Foolish woman. You've changed, yet nothing has truly changed at all."

Once again, she placed her faith in the man who claimed to be her husband.

"Let's see if he betrays your precious trust."

Jason's smile was venomous.

"I'll let you witness firsthand just how fragile your faith truly is."

◆◆◆

At the Leniard estate, deep in the night, ministers and nobles loyal to the First Prince gathered in secret.

"We begin preparations for the coronation."

"But we haven't received the Emperor's approval yet. Is this wise?"

Someone asked cautiously.

"The time for seeking permission has long passed."

Marquis Leniard—Cesare's maternal grandfather—spoke with cold authority.

His eyes gleamed like a predator waiting for the perfect moment to strike.

"His Majesty's health deteriorates daily. The shock of the Western Empress's death has left him barely functional. We all witnessed how he couldn't even oversee her funeral properly."

It was a convenient justification—framing the Emperor's callousness as weakness.

"Yes. The empire needs a new master."

"And that master can only be His Highness the First Prince."

Whispers of agreement rippled through the assembled nobles.

"The time has come."

Marquis Leniard nodded slowly.

"Make haste with the preparations."

None of them noticed the shadow pressed against the wall—a spy who had overheard every word and now melted into the darkness like smoke.

◆◆◆

In the Imperial Palace, the Commander of the Palace Knights knelt before the Emperor.

"What? They're already preparing for the coronation?"

Perdiccas II sat propped against his bedpost, eyes snapping open.

"Your Majesty, they gathered at the Marquis Leniard's estate—"

When the full report reached him, fury erupted.

"They treat me like a damned scarecrow! I am still Emperor! I am still—*cough!*"

His tirade dissolved into violent coughing.

An attendant hurried forward with tea.

As the liquid slid down his throat, a bitter laugh escaped Perdiccas II.

He realized, with crushing clarity, that he could no longer even sustain his own rage.

"Now that they've crushed Juan's faction, they have no reason to wait. Of course."

With the Third Prince's supporters obliterated, all power had shifted to Cesare.

Perdiccas II felt a deep, unfamiliar helplessness settle over him.

Like a slab of meat in a butcher's stall. No one even glances my way anymore.

It was a strange, humiliating sensation.

"They once trembled before my very presence..."

Now, even news of the First Princess's pregnancy brought anxiety rather than joy.

What if my son seizes the throne before his wife even bears my grandchild?

"I cannot let this stand. I must regain control..."

But his eldest son's power had grown too formidable to oppose.

Remembering the Western Empress's pitiful end, Perdiccas II no longer dared aim for Cesare's life.

"How... what should I do? Will the heavens let me meet such a wretched end?"

He clutched his chest.

But fortune—or fate—had not entirely abandoned him.

"Your Majesty! A messenger from the Holy Kingdom has arrived!"

Within days, news arrived that would overturn everything.

"A new oracle has been issued."

The embers buried beneath cold ash suddenly flared to life, and their heat would soon shake the entire continent.
