Chapter 161

The Stolen
Fate

"To claim another's destiny, one must take everything—even their death."

"Remember, Rachel. You must claim everything and make it yours. Leave that woman no chance of survival, and never repeat your mother's foolish mistakes."

Her mother's final words echoed in her mind—those skeletal fingers gripping her wrist with desperate strength, the fevered urgency in her voice bleeding into the present.

"Remember. You must never forget what I've taught you."

Rachel's mother had been a priestess of the Shadeia tribe.

The Primordial Covenant

In the ancient days, when the goddess first descended upon the primordial continent, she required a vessel—a representative to convey her divine will to mortals. She chose a single human, fed her blood to this chosen one, and shared with her the omnipotent gifts of prophecy and healing.

That human's name was Shadeia.

The Saintess venerated by the Holy Kingdom was merely a pale imitation, a figure crafted in Shadeia's sacred image.

After helping the goddess banish the primordial darkness and establish a haven for humanity, Shadeia closed her eyes for the final time. But her bloodline endured, passing the mantle of divine agency from generation to generation.

The world called these representatives shamans.

Though the goddess's blood had grown thin across millennia—its once-absolute omnipotence faded to a whisper—the Shadeia lineage persevered.

The Revelation

When Medea's mother, Selene, came of age, a revelation descended upon the reigning priestess:

"Venus lies shrouded in the fog of calamity. Only when the darkness bearing misfortune is driven away shall the full light illuminate the world."

On the prophesied night—when Venus's celestial path crossed with the Light Saints—the priestess's daughter gave birth to a child and perished in the same breath.

The tribe named the infant Selene and proclaimed her the Child of Prophecy.

Selene was attended by a handmaiden named Lilith—an orphan plucked from the desert wastes by the aging priestess.

"My granddaughter and you share the same age and birth date. Surely this is fate, ordained by the goddess herself."

Lilith remained at Selene's side as a devoted servant and trusted confidante. Selene, in turn, treated her as family.

But a single question gnawed at Lilith's heart.

I was born in the city of light as well. Why am I not the child of prophecy?

Same age. Same birth date. The only difference between them was blood.

The question festered into longing. The longing swelled into obsession—an insatiable hunger that could not be contained.

The Corruption

"Is it truly possible to defy prophecy?"

"Ah, the handmaiden does not yet comprehend the omnipotence of black magic. Why do you think the goddess was so determined to banish the darkness? Because its power is so vast that even the divine tremble before it."

And so Lilith sought out the warlocks and touched the forbidden—the taboos that should never be broken.

"Place your trust in us. We shall make you the priestess of Shadeia."

Her course was set.

If prophecy would not choose her, she would seize it with her own hands.

"Lady Selene! Oh, what have you done? What should we do?"

Lilith's tears were a masterwork of deception. She had tricked Selene into bearing the mark of black magic, then denounced her before the entire tribe.

"Black magic! Selene, how could you commit such sacrilege?"

The scheme worked flawlessly. Selene's qualifications as priestess were revoked, and she was cast out from her people.

"The true Child of Prophecy would never touch black magic. Perhaps we misinterpreted the revelation?"

"They say the handmaiden shares the same birth time as Selene. Could it be that the prophecy speaks of her instead?"

In this manner, Lilith ascended to become the priestess of Shadeia.

But was this the goddess's punishment for a tribe that failed to recognize her true representative?

A cascade of catastrophes descended upon the Shadeia people.

Demonic hordes ravaged their settlements. Floods and tempests swept away the survivors. Those who endured fell to the persecution of the Holy Kingdom.

Lilith—the priestess who had usurped Selene's place—foresaw none of it.

The tribe was annihilated. Only in their final moments did they understand.

"The priestess is false! That is why the goddess has forsaken us!"

Fearing discovery, Lilith abandoned her dying people and fled to the warlocks who had first corrupted her.

And there, in the shadows, she learned the truth.

She had stolen nothing from Selene's fate.

"Have you heard? Selene has become Queen of Valdina."

"What?"

The revelation was devastating.

Her master—beloved by that accursed goddess—had been driven from the tribe, yet instead of descending into ruin, she had risen higher. Nobler. Happier.

While Lilith skulked through damp caverns, trailing after warlocks like a stray dog.

This cannot be right. I stole her destiny. Why does she still bask in the light while I remain trapped in this endless darkness?

"I should have killed her then..."

At last, Lilith understood her fatal error.

The reason the goddess's power refused to flow through her, despite possessing the orbit that should have belonged to Selene—

"I should have taken her life! Only then would Selene's fate have become truly mine!"

She had been so fixated on claiming the title of priestess that she had merely sent Selene away. A mistake that would haunt her forever.

Soon after, Selene was slain by an enemy's blade.

But Lilith felt no joy.

Because she had not taken that life herself, she could no longer interfere with Selene's fate. The thread of destiny had slipped through her fingers.

"Do not despair. You still have a chance. Your daughter can shoulder this karmic burden—the knot you failed to untangle."

This was Rachel's inheritance: the stolen destiny her mother had never been able to claim.

" Selene and Lilith.
And their daughters—Medea and Rachel.

In this struggle where one must take or be taken, coexistence was never possible. "

"Rachel, never forget. To claim her orbit as your own, you must strip away everything: her family, her affections, her achievements. Everything."

Lilith's voice dropped to a venomous whisper.

"Even her death."

Until her dying breath, Lilith had prayed for her daughter's success. She could not bear for Rachel to repeat her own fatal mistakes.

The sins of the mother become the mission of the daughter.

"Don't worry, Mother. I haven't forgotten."

Rachel murmured the words to her reflection, watching herself in the mirror's cold surface.

Hssss...

The round pendant at her throat flared with sudden heat. Rachel's fingers moved to open it, revealing where the sacred image of the goddess should have been.

It had turned completely black.

"Has the King arrived yet?"

A harsh, metallic voice emanated from within the pendant.

"Not yet. But the Agema knights are nearly secured."

Rachel answered with practiced ease, as though such conversations were routine.

"I've implanted the marks. Once the conditioning stabilizes, they will move at your command."

The "divine blessing" she had so graciously bestowed upon the knights was, in truth, a brand of black magic.

"Do not spread the marks too liberally. Conserve your strength for the primary target."

Despite her success with the Agema, the voice betrayed no satisfaction. Instead, it urged restraint—a reminder not to squander her borrowed power.

The conditioning wrought through black magic was potent but ephemeral. Without periodic reinforcement, the hold would weaken and eventually shatter.

This was why Rachel had remained close to Peleus and his knights since arriving in Valdina. The work required constant maintenance.

"The Knights Templar will arrive soon. Surely you don't intend to leave empty-handed? Do you have any idea what sacrifices were required to orchestrate this opportunity?"

Rachel's composure finally cracked.

"I understand that! But what am I supposed to do when nothing goes according to plan?"

Peleus, King of Valdina, possessed a will of maddening resilience.

Even D'Angelo, captain of the royal guard, had nearly succumbed to her influence. But the "Holy Slayer" himself offered black magic no foothold—no weakness to exploit, no darkness to consume.

His barriers stood as unyielding and frigid as ice, never thinning no matter how she probed.

Even though Rachel had saved his life.

Why? This should not be possible...

Never before had her salvation failed to create an opening. Never before had her kindness gone unrewarded.

Worse still, since arriving in Valdina, Peleus had refused every offer of purification—blocking even her attempts to plant a mark.

His defenses were absolute.

And Rachel was running out of time.
