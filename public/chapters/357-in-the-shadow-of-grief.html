Chapter 357

In the Shadow of Grief

"Some wounds cannot be healed—only witnessed and held."

Medea entered the Imperial Palace.

Perhaps it was merely her imagination, but a faint metallic scent of blood lingered in the air.

"We greet Your Highness, the First Princess."

An imperial attendant approached the entrance and bowed deeply toward Medea.

The reverence in that greeting exceeded what was customary for a princess—it carried the deference reserved for an empress.

"Your Highness!"

Other palace servants followed suit, bowing low. Some even prostrated themselves on the grass.

Gone was the mixture of wariness and pity she'd encountered mere hours ago.

"Your Highness."

Terence emerged quickly upon hearing of Medea's arrival.

"You must have heard the general situation from Gallo. Allow me to escort you."

He guided Medea forward, naturally positioning himself to block the servants' curious gazes.

"Cesare."

Medea turned her head, searching.

All traces of chaos had been erased. The frantic activity she'd expected was nowhere evident.

"The Emperor's death has been announced as accidental. The Marquis of Eratosthenes will receive a state funeral as well."

Terence rubbed his chin anxiously.

"Cesare's condition isn't optimal. From the moment he managed to awaken... since entering the Imperial Palace, the marks of divine punishment seem to be continuously manifesting on his body. I don't believe he showed it openly, but..."

Terence had noticed blood seeping from the backs of Cesare's hands even as he'd departed his confrontation with the Emperor.

"Then he simply vanished. Where could he have gone in that state?"

A strange tremor colored Terence's voice.

With Cesare gone, Terence alone remained to manage the crisis. He couldn't abandon his post recklessly.

"The Emperor's death will be announced publicly soon. The principal vassals of the First Prince's faction, including Marquis Leniard, will attend."

Before attention could focus too intensely on the circumstances, Perdiccas II's true cause of death had to remain concealed.

Even from Leniard, the cornerstone of the First Prince's faction.

Yet the person who should have been at the forefront of these proceedings had disappeared entirely.

"Continue managing things here, Terence. I'll search for Cesare."

Medea looked skyward. The sunset stained the heavens crimson as spilled blood.

She thought she knew where to find him.

Medea stood before the late Empress's palace.

The structure had been sealed following Empress Eleanor's tragic death.

It had become a place where none dared trespass—a location eventually forgotten in the collective memory.

The outer walls, once adorned with ivory, now wore a coat of dark green moss that filled every crack and crevice.

Medea stepped onto the faded marble pathway.

Weeds had colonized the grounds surrounding the palace.

Perhaps that explained why the structure appeared so desolate in the gathering dusk.

She ventured inside.

The deeper she walked, the more evident it became that despite its age, the interior remained largely unchanged.

Someone's hand had been maintaining it constantly. The overgrown bushes outside served as camouflage.

Medea paused mid-stride in the hallway.

A chamber containing a portrait of an elegant woman with beautiful yet somehow austere eyes.

There, she found her husband, leaning heavily against the wall beneath that image.

"Cesare."

There was no time for relief at discovering him.

Finding that perpetually straight back now bowed and compressed, Medea froze.

He appeared unfamiliar, hunched in a shadowed corner where the sunset's light couldn't reach.

The proud man who always stood bathed in brilliant golden radiance now cowered in darkness as though afraid of illumination.

Click. Click.

A quiet sound, reminiscent of rustling leaves, echoed through the space.

He was manipulating the wooden toy mechanically, but touching him felt as though it might send him tumbling into an uncontrollable abyss.

Until Medea positioned herself directly behind him, he remained motionless.

As if blocking out all external stimuli, focusing every fragment of his attention on the object in his hands.

"Cesare."

Medea reached toward that desolate back.

His hand ceased its fidgeting with the toy. His fist clenched tight.

The warmth on his shoulder made Cesare raise his head.

The murderous intent that had been about to surge toward the intruder who'd disturbed his solitude evaporated instantly upon seeing Medea.

His unfocused eyes captured the green gaze looking down at him.

He confirmed each detail meticulously, as though imprinting everything: her delicate features, flushed cheeks, even the white breath escaping from the corners of her mouth.

He reached out without conscious thought.

"Dea."

A thin voice. Thick arms wrapped around her waist.

Medea found herself pulled into his embrace instantly.

Cesare pressed his forehead against the nape of her neck, exhaling as though releasing breath he'd been holding too long.

Unlike his grandfather's body, now cooling in death, Medea's living warmth pulled Cesare from his hazy dissociation.

"Now, the seat where you will reign is clean."

This moment is real.

"I'm sorry... for everything."

Then—

A sharp intake of breath, almost a gasp.

Medea found herself momentarily speechless.

Her shoulder grew hot and wet.

He resembled a child who'd never learned to cry properly...

The desperate embrace. The buried face. The trembling shoulders.

Like someone who'd never been taught how to release the grief coursing through their entire being.

Medea glanced down at the toy Cesare had been handling and understood the complete story.

The Marquis of Eratosthenes eliminated the Emperor... for us.

For Cesare and Medea. For them.

The old man who'd stood silently beside Perdiccas II during the forced oracle had chosen to shield his grandson from the coming storm with his branch-like body.

"Cesare..."

Medea couldn't bear it any longer. She embraced him by the neck.

And concealed his face within her arms.

Like beasts licking each other's wounds, she absorbed the grief he couldn't express.

Cesare's lips moved.

"...Grandfather..."

He murmured in a voice made hoarse by suppressed emotion.

And realized.

"Grandfather..."

From the moment he'd understood that his very existence represented shame and wounding to Eratosthenes and Eleanor, he'd been unable to voice that title.

Only now, when breath had fled that frail body, could Cesare finally speak it aloud.

"Grandfather... goodbye..."

He wished he'd called him that earlier. That he hadn't cared about propriety and had simply said it.

But where was there time to wait for the perfect moment?

Cesare couldn't finish the words.

Medea stroked his back continuously throughout.

She hoped her clumsy comfort might prevent his silent tears from becoming audible sobs.

For now, she simply held everything she wanted to ask, burying it in this shared moment of grief.

Katzen Castle, Streets of Persepolis

As the sun descended toward the horizon, a somber tolling spread through the streets.

Dong... Dong...

The echo resonated for a prolonged moment.

"What's that sound?"

Merchants preparing to close their stalls looked up in confusion.

"Those are the bells from the Imperial Palace, aren't they? Strange. They only toll on ceremonial days or festivals."

People exchanged puzzled glances, about to return to their activities when—

Broooom... Broooom...

The ceremonial trumpets sounded.

Even passersby mid-stride halted in their tracks.

"Three times..."

Those of Katzen remembered this sound.

Decades ago, when an emperor had passed, this sound had resounded across earth and sky.

"His Majesty is dead!"

"The Emperor has died...!"

Cries of shock and hushed whispers erupted throughout the streets.

By the time Marquis Leniard entered the Imperial Palace, everything had been restored to its customary splendor—save for the faint scent of blood that lingered.

"Terence!"

Though his face maintained the dignity of an aged nobleman, joy he couldn't entirely conceal sparkled in his eyes.

It seems God hasn't abandoned this country after all.

Both concerns that had weighed on him had been resolved simultaneously.

"Where is His Highness? Where is the First Prince?"

It wasn't enough that First Prince Cesare—who'd been hovering at death's threshold—had awakened. Even Perdiccas II, the greatest obstacle to succession, had died.

"Your Excellency, Marquis Leniard. The palace gates were sealed, yet news traveled remarkably swiftly."

Terence's observation carried a subtle edge.

It was a mild rebuke—noting that despite having informants planted within the Imperial Palace, the Marquis hadn't yet properly grasped the truth surrounding the Emperor's death.

However, the overly excited Marquis failed to catch the implication.

"Is this the time for such leisurely attitudes?! Why aren't you preparing for the coronation?"

Perdiccas II had died without designating a Crown Prince.

This meant the throne remained vacant—and that the other imperial children harbored no viable claims.

"We cannot delay even a single day due to the funeral! His Majesty's—or rather, the late Emperor's funeral will draw attendees from across the Empire. But before that occurs, the coronation ceremony must be completed. His Highness Cesare must wear the imperial crown first!"

End of Chapter 257
