Chapter 299

The White
Glove

"Some lessons are best taught with a silk-covered hand."

Had she been an innocent young bride, she might have wept and fled the room in shame.

But Medea stood perfectly still, her expression unchanged—as though the insult had been directed at someone else entirely. Not a flicker of distress crossed her features.

That insufferable creature. She drove my Angelique into exile, yet she doesn't so much as blink at this humiliation.

The Eastern Empress smiled, her voice sweet as springtime.

"Is something the matter, Crown Princess? Why do you still stand while everyone else has found their seats?"

The words were gentle, but their meaning was unmistakable—she alone remained displaced while the rest of the gathering had settled comfortably.

From this moment forward, I will make you suffer. One indignity at a time.

But the seating arrangement was merely the opening salvo.

The crimson flowers adorning Medea's designated table were a rare foreign variant, forced into bloom through alchemical means. Their petals released a subtle hallucinogen—after half an hour of exposure, the victim's mind would cloud, their vision distorting reality.

And when the worm hidden in her refreshments emerges while she's in that state...

The instinctive recoil from an insect would appear as though she were throwing away her food in disgust. And those refreshments had been carefully chosen.

The sweets prepared for that foreign girl contain date palms—a fruit His Majesty treasured during his childhood.

Few knew this secret. In his youth, Perdiccas II had been crushed by the previous Emperor, expelled from the Holy City to live in abject poverty. The dates that nomadic merchants occasionally gifted the wretched prince had been his only comfort.

What happens when a hallucinating girl discards the memory of the Emperor's most shameful days?

Perdiccas II's madness could ignite over far less.

Even with the Emperor as her guardian—can that protection withstand His Majesty's own fury?

The corners of the Eastern Empress's mouth curved upward.

Cesare shields her like an iron fortress... but such opportunities rarely come twice. I must strike now, shatter her foundation completely.

As much as her golden daughter Angelique suffered, this foreign usurper deserved to suffer tenfold.

The Eastern Empress's schemes unfolded perfectly in her mind—until familiar faces appeared beside the Crown Princess.

"Beaumont? Evelyn...?"

She turned sharply.

These were her own confidantes. Women who had stood at her side moments ago. Women who had helped her prepare every detail of today's trap.

Clarice... why are you...?

Countess Beaumont—childhood friend of the Eastern Empress—curtsied deeply before the Crown Princess.

"The sunlight is rather intense today. Perhaps it's my age, but my back has begun to ache terribly. If Your Highness would permit, I should very much like to take a seat at your table."

She turned to Lady Evelyn.

"Don't you agree, Lady Evelyn?"

"Indeed. If we shift slightly, there should be room for... oh dear!"

Lady Evelyn's sleeve caught the tablecloth—or so it appeared. The vase toppled, water cascading across the surface.

The crimson flowers tumbled into the spreading pool, their petals instantly ruined, mixing with scattered leaves in a sodden mess.

"Oh my, what a disaster. This will need to be cleaned at once."

The Eastern Empress watched, trembling, as the second trap was swept away by servants summoned to manage the spill.

Her head turned—just in time to glimpse Dean Clarice discretely carrying a plate of date pastries toward the kitchens.

Impossible. Why would they...

Not strangers. Not enemies. Her own people—the very women who had helped construct today's scheme—now dismantled it piece by piece.

In an instant, they had betrayed her.

The Eastern Empress felt as though someone had struck her skull from behind.

"I appreciate your kindness, but if you all join me here, what will Her Majesty the Eastern Empress do?"

The Crown Princess's voice carried across the hushed greenhouse—calm, almost concerned, even as the gathering buzzed with confusion at the entourage's inexplicable behavior.

That creature...

The harmless serenity in those silver eyes cut deeper than any blade.

"We would ask the Crown Princess to take our places beside Her Majesty. On our behalf."

The women who made this proposal could not meet their mistress's eyes. Their gazes remained fixed on the ground, clouded with fear and guilt.

"Then let us do so."

Watching the Crown Princess approach with that faint, knowing smile, the Eastern Empress finally understood.

That cunning witch has claimed my people.

✦ ✦ ✦

When the Eastern Empress's own entourage intervened on the Crown Princess's behalf, the anticipated confrontation dissolved into anticlimactic quiet.

The gathered nobles hid their disappointment behind polite expressions—but none felt the sting more keenly than Princess Strauss.

All that bold talk, and yet the Empress couldn't even manage this much? If it were me, I would have slapped her face myself.

Her pretty lips twisted into a pout.

"How gracious of Her Majesty the Eastern Empress to relinquish her seat so generously. And how... convenient... that the Crown Princess arrives to claim it as though she had been waiting all along."

Princess Strauss's eyes widened with feigned innocence.

"I understand Your Highness lost her mother at a very young age. Perhaps Her Majesty hoped this gesture might ease your longing—even for a moment."

The words were honey-sweet.

The barb they concealed was razor-sharp—a direct strike at Medea's mother, the wandering dancer whose humble origins remained a favored target of courtly whispers.

Breathless silence descended upon the greenhouse.

Glances darted between Princess Strauss and the Crown Princess, anticipation crackling like lightning before a storm.

The Northern King does treasure his daughter, doesn't he?

The nobles watched with barely concealed hunger, eager to witness the Crown Princess's response to such blatant provocation.

Princess Strauss had seized the arrow the Eastern Empress had fumbled and aimed it with deadly precision. This was far more entertaining than any dance performance.

Medea surveyed the room.

No one spoke. No one intervened. But she could feel their anticipation pressing against her like a physical weight.

Princess Strauss stood before her, triumph already gleaming in those almond-colored eyes.

What will you do now?

This was a jungle.

Even predators who were not hungry would attack the moment they sensed weakness. She needed to show these circling hyenas exactly what she was made of.

Medea's spine straightened almost imperceptibly.

At her side, the ever-attentive Saya produced a pair of pristine white gloves.

The gathering watched in hushed silence as the Crown Princess drew them on, finger by deliberate finger.

The air grew heavy—the oppressive stillness before a tempest breaks.

Sensing the weight of the moment but misreading its meaning, Princess Strauss pressed forward.

"Oh my... perhaps Your Highness might grace us with a demonstration? Show us whether your mother's talents have been passed down to—"

CRACK.

The sharp sound cut through the greenhouse like a whip.

Princess Strauss's hand flew to her cheek, eyes wide with shock.

"You... you just..."

The Crown Princess had struck her across the face.

Not with a bare hand in vulgar fury—but with silk-gloved fingers, lending the blow an air of aristocratic disdain. This was not emotional outrage. It was calculated retribution.

"Is there a problem?"

Medea's voice was utterly tranquil.

Princess Strauss glared at her with murderous fury.

"My father is the Northern King! You cannot—"

CRACK.

A second blow landed on the opposite cheek.

"Dear me. It seems the Princess's ignorance of imperial etiquette reflects upon the Northern King himself."

Medea flexed her white-gloved wrist with languid grace—a gesture of elegant contempt.

"When a title is granted to those unequipped to bear it, such unfortunate scenes become inevitable. I suppose I must instruct you personally."

The words carried a subtle but unmistakable mockery—not merely of the Princess, but of the current Northern King, who had assumed his position only three years prior under... controversial circumstances.

Princess Strauss's face burned crimson. Not only had she been struck—twice—but her father had been publicly ridiculed.

"Even with the Emperor as your guardian, I remain a noblewoman! You have no right to punish me privately! You will pay for this!"

Her voice shook with rage.

Medea laughed softly.

"Ah. It seems everyone keeps forgetting who I am."

She turned slowly, her gaze sweeping across the assembled nobles—one face at a time.

"Beyond His Majesty's guardianship, I possess immunity. The privilege to be absolved of any transgression committed on Katzen soil."

A collective shudder rippled through the gathering.

Immunity?

The Emperor's personal pardon—unrestricted, unchallenged, extending even to treason itself. It was as though she carried an extra life in her pocket.

"Good heavens..."

Whispers erupted through the greenhouse.

How desperately did the Emperor wish to appease Valdina that he granted such a privilege?

In their haste to resolve that crisis, none had considered the long-term implications of Perdiccas II's generosity.

"I had intended to preserve such protections for matters of genuine consequence."

Medea's voice remained perfectly measured.

"But I find my mind has changed."

A small dessert fork appeared in her hand.

With delicate precision, she pierced a worm that had been crawling across the tabletop.

The insect twitched once. Twice. Then went still.

Every noble present held their breath.

The Crown Princess lifted her gaze to Princess Strauss. Those silver eyes were terrifyingly calm—and all the more chilling for it.

"It seems I shall have no difficulty being absolved for crushing a writhing little vermin beneath my heel."

She smiled.

"Wouldn't you agree, Princess?"
