Chapter 275

The Mirror's
Truth

"No mask survives the light of revelation—and every truth demands its reckoning."

Under the crushing weight of accusation pressing against her neck, Rachel's mind raced frantically.

How can I redirect their attention? How can I deceive them?

But Rachel failed to realize one crucial truth: Medea had no intention of offering her any path to escape.

"Your Holiness, I believe the answer lies within the Covenant Cup—the very vessel this woman has touched."

At Medea's subtle gesture, Rachel's attendant stepped forward.

Before the Pope's watchful gaze, the priest shed his familiar cream-colored robes. Beneath them gleamed the flat steel of paladin armor.

It was Theo.

Rachel stood frozen, understanding flooding through her at last.

So that's why my brainwashing failed to bind Medea.

"How... when did you...?"

Had he been hiding at her side all along? Then where was the original warlock she had assigned?

How had this knight slipped free of her mental chains?

But the moment Medea lifted the Covenant Cup, Rachel realized that none of her questions mattered anymore.

"The senior high priests of this Holy Kingdom know well that the Covenant Cup bears indelible marks whenever the wicked touch its sacred surface."

Medea raised the goblet high, turning it so that all might witness.

"The stain of Primordial Darkness."

Pope Benedict examined the interior of the silver cup. Where once the metal had gleamed bright, now it was blackened—corrupted.

His voice fell into the chamber like a stone dropped into still water.

"Black magic..."

The priests who had been holding their breath released it in groans of dismay. The only beings who wielded Primordial Darkness were warlocks—those who had forsaken all sacred law to embrace the forbidden.

"Very well—let us say there are traces upon the cup."

Rachel broke the suffocating silence, forcing strength into her voice.

"But where is your proof that I am responsible?"

Her words trembled despite her efforts to steady them. The harder she fought for control, the more a discordant metallic edge crept into her tone.

"If such evil power truly emanated from me, how could I have remained within this sacred temple for months on end? Do you honestly believe that possible?"

The truth reveals itself

Rachel's madness, barely concealed beneath a veneer of righteous indignation, made Medea decide to wait no longer.

Shrrrrrrk.

An outstretched hand seized the curtain and pulled.

The heavy fabric cascaded to the floor, revealing the mirror that had been hidden behind it.

Rachel's reflection gazed back at her from the pristine glass.

"Ahh..."

"Dear Goddess—look!"

Screams of horror erupted from the assembled priests.

Rachel turned slowly toward the mirror, dread coiling in her stomach.

What she saw there shattered what remained of her composure.

The True Face

A jagged fissure of pure darkness split her chest like a wound in reality itself.

But what drew every horrified gaze was her right hand—the hand that had always appeared pristine and pale.

The mirror's sacred power to reflect essence had torn through the camouflage of black magic like gossamer.

Rachel's right hand was black and twisted, resembling a branch left to rot for centuries. The flesh had cracked and split. The knuckles—barely recognizable as human—pulsed with dark energy.

"That... that is..."

"Wickedness incarnate! The mark of Darkness itself!"

Even those priests who had never awakened their divine sight could feel it now—the eerie, unnatural corruption radiating from her ruined hand.

"Black magic!"

Rachel's true self stood exposed in the mirror that revealed essence.

And in that moment, the terrible truth was laid bare: the Saintess whom the temple had awaited for centuries was, in reality, a warlock of the darkest order.

As Rachel felt the collective divine power of the Pope and High Priests sharpen against her like drawn swords, her face contorted into something demonic.

Is this truly how it ends? So abruptly? So mercilessly?

Her violet eyes swept frantically across the Chamber of Judgment, counting her enemies.

Only the Pope and the prelates stand within. If I am to be captured regardless...

She would summon her warlock apprentices from outside to silence every witness.

"Kill them. Every last one of them."

Rachel issued the command to her servants waiting beyond the chamber doors.

Silence.

"Did you not hear me? Kill them all!"

Still nothing.

Whoosh!

A dagger sliced past Rachel's cheek, close enough to draw blood. She froze.

Thud.

Something landed at her feet.

"Looking for these?"

Cesare leaned against the doorframe, a languid smile playing across his lips.

He held up a fistful of bloodied daggers—the weapons of her acolytes. Her warlocks.

"They possessed far too much talent to waste their deaths here."

Rachel's vision flickered, darkness encroaching at the edges.

What rushed toward her now was not the glorious victory she had so long anticipated—

—but utter, irrevocable ruin.

"NO! I've done nothing wrong! That woman deceived me! They're all conspiring—all of them are lying!"

Medea approached the shrieking Saintess with measured steps.

"No matter what schemes you employ, the only deceiver here is you."

She observed the image of Rachel staring back at her with naked hatred—despair, bewilderment, confusion, and malice all warring in those wide violet eyes.

"Seize her!"

Rachel lunged toward Medea, fingers curved like claws—but the temple knights intercepted her with brutal efficiency.

The scene that unfolded was the perfect inverse of another lifetime.

The beast caught in the trap was now the Saintess.

The hunter who had sprung it was Medea.

Divine Revelation

Medea allowed herself a moment of dark satisfaction—it would be a lie to deny the base desire to witness this spectacle in full.

And in that moment, as Medea's reflection happened to fall upon the Mirror of Essence—

The mirror erupted with platinum light.

Simultaneously, the necklace around Medea's throat came undone of its own accord.

It floated into the air, drinking in the brilliant radiance like a vessel finally filled.

"No... no, no, no..."

Rachel's voice emerged as a broken moan.

She knew instinctively what this meant.

This is the moment. The true Saintess of this Holy Land—of the entire continent—is being revealed at last.

"That necklace... it bears the seal of Shadeia..."

Pope Benedict's voice trembled with recognition.

As head of the Orthodox Faith, he knew that the original divine representative had been the shrine maiden of Shadeia.

The Pope staggered. Then his knees buckled, and he sank to the floor.

"She is the true Vessel. This woman is the Holy Maiden!"

Those priests who had awakened their divine sight felt the surge of Medea's power as it burst forth like a dam finally broken.

One by one, they dropped to their knees, pressing their foreheads to the cold stone floor.

"What... why is this..."

Bewildered, Medea retreated a step, uncertain how to respond.

"The foul black magic blinded us to the truth! We failed to recognize the genuine Vessel walking among us!"

"She is the true representative of the Goddess!"

"No... it cannot be..."

Most stricken of all was High Priest Paul.

His face had gone deathly pale, as though every drop of blood had fled his body.

He thrashed against the truth even as it crushed him.

He could not—would not—accept the image before him: Medea bathed in sacred light within the mirror, while Rachel remained shrouded in the instruments of darkness.

"It's a lie... this cannot be..."

He pressed his forehead to the floor and wept.

The divine representative he had awaited for so long—the savior destined to deliver the continent from catastrophe—had been a warlock all along.

This was not merely betrayal. It was a desecration of the very divinity he had devoted his life to serving.

"Remove this woman from my sight."

Pope Benedict's voice emerged cold and absolute.

"Confine her in the Tower of Sinners."

Unlike the other High Priests—who reeled in shock at the truth the holy relics had revealed—no astonishment or confusion marked his aged features.

Perhaps I sensed the truth of this moment from the very beginning.

Looking upon Rachel's rotted hand reflected in the mirror, the Pope swallowed a heavy sigh.

Medea had approached him weeks ago, under the guise of wedding preparations.

"If Your Holiness also harbors doubts regarding Saintess Rachel, I would ask for your cooperation in this matter. This concerns not merely the Central Temple, but the welfare of the entire continent."

The specially constructed Chamber of Judgment. The relics positioned to reveal truth. Every preparation for Rachel's trial had been carried out in secrecy under the Pope's own direction.

"Black magic..."

The unease and discomfort he had felt in every encounter with the Saintess was now explained.

The Pope's gaze fell upon Theo, who had shed his priest's robes to stand revealed as Medea's ally.

In all this vast Central Temple, only a single paladin saw through the deception.

He swallowed another sigh.

The Central Temple had strayed far from what it once was. The power of the Goddess had faded, and those who claimed to worship her had been tainted by corruption and worldly desire.

A witness in the shadows

In the corridor beyond, Jason sensed approaching footsteps and pressed himself behind a pillar.

"I AM THE SAINTESS! These accusations are beneath me! I am innocent! This is a trap—you are all being deceived!"

Rachel's shrieks echoed through the passageway as the temple knights dragged her away. Her face was a mask of fury and madness, thick as poison.

The gentle, gracious Saintess the world had known was nowhere to be found.

The Weight of Truth

Jason bit his lip without realizing it.

Shock held him paralyzed.

Standing outside the Chamber of Judgment, he could scarcely believe the truth he had witnessed.

Saintess Rachel was a warlock.

Then... even back then?

The second wife he had chosen after abandoning Medea and their children.

Had he truly been ensorcelled by a warlock? Had he forsaken his family and committed unforgivable sins while under her spell?

Jason gasped for breath.

Cold seeped into his bones as though his very skeleton had turned to ice.
