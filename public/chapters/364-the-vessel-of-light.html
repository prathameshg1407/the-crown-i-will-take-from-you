Chapter 264

The Vessel of
Light

"If the Goddess is absent, I will bear Her burden."

Medea's voice was calm—unnervingly so.

No one dared speak. The silence pressed against them like a physical weight.

"There is no God. We have no hope..."

"I do not care."

What would fleeing accomplish?

Neither fate nor her enemies would grant her peace. They would pursue her as she retreated, driving their blades ever deeper with each step she took backward.

When did I begin placing my hopes in the divine?

The life restored to her, the grace the Goddess had bestowed—that alone had been enough. Starting from nothing, she had clawed back every precious thing that had been taken from her.

"Just as we averted Valdina's destruction, this is no different."

"I will not surrender."

Medea spread her hands before her.

Her fingers rose to the golden chain at her throat—a necklace the Elder of Shadeia had entrusted to her long ago. A sacred token, passed through generations of priestesses.

Instinct whispered that now was the moment to use it.

"If no relic exists that can neutralize this darkness... then I shall become that relic myself."

CRACK—!

The wing-shaped pendant—its heart a flawless crystal—shattered against the base of the Goddess's statue.

"Your Highness—!"

The three men lurched forward in alarm, but Medea raised her other hand to halt them.

"You know what my blood can do. It purifies corruption. It will simply require time."

The shattered fragments bit into her palm. Crimson drops fell from her fingertips onto the cold marble of the sculpture.

Her gaze swept to Theo, whose face had gone ashen.

Slowly, Medea lifted her eyes.

The statue of the Goddess gazed down upon them all—Her expression one of eternal mercy, yet somehow distant. Indifferent. As though the great evil coiling at their feet held no concern for Her.

"I will not flee."

Medea's green eyes blazed as she addressed the stone face above her.

Sacred Vow

If this is the trial destiny has laid before me, I will meet it with pride.

If the Goddess is absent, I will shoulder Her burden.

If darkness rises to smother the light and devour this world—

I will gladly shed my blood and flesh to become the barrier in its path.

"I will protect what is mine. I will surrender it to no one."

Until the last pulse fades from my veins, I will never yield.

The resolve forged through two lifetimes blazed within her like an eternal flame.

The floor began to tremble.

KROOOOM—!

An overwhelming force shook the cathedral to its foundations. An invisible power—formless, wordless, beyond comprehension—swept through the nave like a divine tempest.

The three men dropped to their knees without conscious thought, overwhelmed by the sheer magnitude of what they witnessed.

This cannot be real. What I am seeing—

FAAAA—!

A flash of radiance split the darkness.

"Ah...!"

Theo stared, transfixed.

From the space between Medea's shoulder blades, vast wings of pure white light unfurled, wrapping around her like a divine embrace. Golden brilliance spread outward from where she stood, shattering their vision once more.

None among them understood.

The Goddess who had borrowed the darkness of the Beginning.

The meaning of Venus—bearer of the brightest light in all the heavens.

At this very moment, it was being realized anew... in Medea.

✦ ✦ ✦

Dong... Dong...

The funeral bells tolled with solemn weight.

Black banners rippled in the wind as mourners stood in respectful silence. The joint funeral of Emperor Perdiccas II and the scholar Eratosthenes proceeded with restrained dignity.

The magnificent ceremony the late Emperor had envisioned during his lifetime would never come to pass.

Even the white chrysanthemums laid upon the coffins told a story—those heaped upon Eratosthenes's bier far outnumbered those gracing the Emperor's. Courtiers who had offered only perfunctory obeisance at the imperial coffin took care to place their flowers before the scholar's remains.

They mourned, in truth, the loss of a brilliant mind and pillar of the nation.

Had Perdiccas II witnessed this stark disparity, he would have risen from his coffin to vomit blood in rage.

The long funeral procession concluded at last, and those who had paid their respects began to disperse.

Yet no one spoke of the dead.

All attention had turned to the next Emperor.

"Has the First Prince still not awakened?"

Today, neither the First Prince nor his wife had appeared at the ceremony.

"Impossible. On the day His Majesty passed, the First Prince stood and took command of the situation himself. His absence today must be due to coronation preparations... unless he has other intentions."

The nobles murmured in hushed tones, eyes darting through the thinning crowd.

"The Count of Montere held a clandestine meeting with ministers of the Third Prince's faction outside the city not long ago. I assumed he had been thoroughly humbled after the Western Empress's fall, but it seems his ambition remains undimmed."

The nobles aligned with the First Prince cast sharp glances toward Count Montere and his allies.

"They say the Fifth Prince, banished to the eastern provinces, has begun recruiting supporters."

"Ha! Is that all? Did you hear that Admiral Albain—the Sixth Princess's uncle—has been gathering his fleet?"

The ministers of the First Prince's faction maintained composure on the surface, but beneath it, anxiety churned.

"Your Highness must don the crown without delay. If we hesitate, we may find ourselves embroiled in another brutal succession war."

"A coronation alone will resolve nothing. Circumstances are far graver now than three years past. Then, at least, we were not shackled by religion!"

The second oracle was the crux of their troubles.

"How can the continent recognize an Emperor who defies the will of the Goddess? To secure the throne, the oracle must be accepted. We cannot hand our enemies justification to drag His Highness from power."

As the younger ministers' voices rose in sharp debate, an elder statesman raised a placating hand.

"I understand your concerns, but do not let anxiety cloud your judgment. Did not the late Perdiccas II himself acknowledge the oracle?"

"It appears His Highness has reached a decision. Marquis Leniard—is it not so?"

Every eye turned to the leader of the First Prince's faction.

"You dispatched the ceremonial robes to the Saintess, did you not? She will be crowned as a member of the Katzen royal family. Were those the First Prince's orders?"

Silence was his only answer.

The ministers knew that coronation robes had indeed been sent to the Saintess. The First Prince, who had dismissed the oracle without a second thought, had apparently changed his mind.

Though privately relieved, they could not suppress their unease.

"Or... was it Her Highness the Princess's decision?"

They interpreted the Marquis's silence in their own way.

"Come now, speak plainly."

"...The coronation will proceed as scheduled."

At that moment, one of Marquis Leniard's escorts approached and bowed low.

"The delegation from the Holy Kingdom has arrived."

Though the man kept his voice low, he did not entirely conceal his displeasure. Those nearby heard every word.

The Marquis's weathered hand seemed to brush the signet ring upon his finger before he nodded.

"Very well. Prepare the carriage."

"Yes, Your Excellency."

The ministers exchanged meaningful glances. Leniard himself—pillar of the First Prince's faction—was personally welcoming the Saintess and her entourage.

"So it is true, Your Excellency? The Saintess will become His Highness's consort?"

"Has the First Princess given her consent?"

"Remember your duties, and guard your tongues."

Marquis Leniard offered only a warning against rash action.

"All proceeds according to Her Highness's will. We follow where she leads."

The ministers swallowed their sighs. A heavy silence descended as they regarded one another—none certain whether the "Highness" Leniard spoke of was Cesare... or Medea.

"Then it truly will proceed as promised."

The Katzen nobles who had been eavesdropping murmured among themselves as they watched the Marquis depart to greet the Holy Kingdom's delegation.

"Even the First Prince cannot resist. This is not merely the Crown—no matter how precious a youthful romance may be, how can he forsake such power?"

"A pity, nonetheless. How must the First Princess feel, watching her own husband welcome another woman into his arms? And with a child growing in her womb..."

It was then that the sound reached them.

Thundering hooves—heavy and urgent—shook the very ground beneath their feet. Even the candles flickering in the funeral hall trembled at the force of the approach.

DOOM—DOOM—DOOM—
