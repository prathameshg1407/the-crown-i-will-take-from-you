Chapter 339

The Crumbling
West

"A crown means nothing when the throne beneath it turns to ash."

Though Perdiccas II had sired many children, not a single one had yet given him a grandchild.

The news of his first heir—arriving amid such turmoil—quelled his fury in an instant.

Cesare and Medea... what extraordinary offspring might those two produce?

His mind whirred with calculation, arriving at a conclusion before his next breath. Even if his other children eventually bore grandchildren, none would compare to the progeny of those two.

Cesare had long slipped beyond his grasp. But if he could raise the child under his own hand—

I will not fail this time.

Moreover, with the child as leverage, neither Cesare nor Medea would dare move against him.

The tangled knots of succession, the festering inferiority that had plagued him for decades, the empire's uncertain future—all of it suddenly seemed to have an answer.

A sinister smile curved across the lips of Perdiccas II.

"A great-grandchild? This is a sign that my bloodline remains vital! The Goddess bestows new light upon Katzen!"

His voice rang with genuine pleasure—the first warmth anyone had heard from him in months.

"I humbly beseech Your Majesty to bless this child and grant him your protection."

Medea lowered her eyes with practiced modesty.

"Protect our child, Your Majesty. Punish the wretches who dared attempt to render Your Majesty's bloodline a fatherless bastard."

Beside her, Cesare's expression had turned to stone. His hand drifted toward his blade—but Medea's fingers found his sleeve first, a gentle tug that stilled him.

"If you kill the Western Empress now, you'll never find the mastermind behind her."

Her whisper was barely audible.

"The story ends with her taking all the blame alone. That's precisely what those lurking in the shadows desire."

Cesare's jaw tightened, but his hand fell away from the hilt.

Medea turned her face from the Emperor and slowly wiped her eyes. Her lips trembled—a portrait of maternal anguish.

The Crown Princess's eyes glistened with unshed tears, reddened at the rims.

Once, right before me, she glared with defiance and fire...

Perdiccas II studied her. This was not the woman he remembered. This was simply a mother protecting her child—vulnerable, pleading, bowing her head before him for help.

She had even silenced her husband.

A far greater prize than Juan has fallen into my hands.

"Who dares threaten my bloodline? Step forward at once! Speak the truth plainly, or face the consequences!"

As if attempting to rebuild a relationship already in ruins, Perdiccas II suddenly played the role of doting grandfather, his staff striking the marble floor with each furious word.

"You wretched creatures! If you do not confess, I shall tear your limbs asunder and ensure you never again see the light of day!"

The gathered nobles gasped, shrinking back as he swung his staff like a weapon.

Only the Western Empress remained in the empty space before the throne.

"Western Empress. Have you nothing to say?"

Her shoulders trembled. Cold sweat beaded at her temples. She looked like a mouse cornered with no escape.

"Your Majesty, I—I—"

She never imagined this. That sly Valdinian woman played the imperial heir card. I thought I could simply keep ignoring her!

The Western Empress bit her lip and locked eyes with the First Princess.

How much does she know? Surely she hasn't obtained actual evidence?

Anxious glances darted among the vassals. Within their midst, the Western Empress caught sight of a red hairpin—and the moment she recognized that color, words rose to her lips.

"Your Majesty... Everything was—"

But her tongue seized. No sound emerged. She wanted to speak of Jason Castulo—the foolish, wicked architect who had led her to this precipice.

Only phlegm choked her throat.

"Gah—kaah—"

The moment she forced her voice, searing pain lanced through her spine, buckling her knees.

What did you do to me, you bastard?

Was it her imagination, or did the corners of Jason's mouth twitch? Silently, he shaped a single word with his lips.

Quiet.

His index finger rose to his lips and fell away in an instant.

"Mother! Please—tell Father it wasn't you!"

Juan rushed forward, dropping to his knees beside the fallen Western Empress. Her hair hung in disarray, her hands shook, and her eyes swam with terror and confusion.

Yet still her mouth remained sealed.

"Mother, please..."

The Emperor approached, his shadow falling over them like a shroud.

Thud.

His staff struck Juan's shoulder—not a gentle touch, but a shove. Clearing an obstacle from his path.

Juan staggered backward.

"Look upon this wretched creature. Not a single member of my bloodline shall remain in your treacherous hands."

The Emperor gazed down at the Western Empress. Not a trace of warmth lingered in those cold eyes.

"Execute her."

The words fell like a blade—cold, resolute, final.

"Father!"

"She dared target my son. There is no reason to uphold the dignity of royalty for such filth. Allowing her to live only invites greater calamity."

The fact that this woman had shared half a lifetime with him had already faded from his mind.

Then Medea stepped forward.

"Your Majesty, the Western Empress is the biological mother of the Third Prince and a member of the imperial family. We cannot disregard public sentiment entirely."

The nobles of Juan's faction could not conceal their astonishment. Now that the Western Empress's crimes had been exposed, no one had anticipated that the First Princess—of all people—would plead for her life.

"...Cesare. Do you concur?"

Perdiccas II's gaze shifted to his son.

Cesare glanced indifferently at the Western Empress, then seized his wife's wrist with quiet possessiveness.

"My wife's will is my will."

The hall, which had held its breath, erupted in whispered shock.

What manner of man is the First Prince? A cold-blooded creature who never spared those who sought his life—yet he buried that grudge at his wife's single word?

It was a moment that made everyone present realize anew what the First Princess meant to him.

"...Very well. Confine the Western Empress to her palace. She shall not be released until she confesses the full truth."

"Father, please forgive her. Please... spare my mother."

At Juan's desperate plea, Perdiccas II raised his hand, silencing him.

Then he added, his voice colder still:

"Nothing shall pass in or out. No food. No medicine. Nothing."

The West Palace of Katzen

The Western Empress regained consciousness several days later.

And she shuddered at the cold reality that greeted her.

She had not yet recovered from the catastrophe that had unfolded just before Juan was to be named Crown Prince.

"I was one step away... just one step from the summit!"

The West Palace was now too wretched to bear the name. Every trace of splendor had vanished. Furniture lay shattered across the floors. Every item of value had been confiscated, leaving only the muddy boot prints of soldiers ground into the carpets she had once cherished.

Her loyal attendants—those who had served her faithfully for years—had been dragged away, subjected to humiliation.

All of this... all of this humiliation is merely the Emperor venting his rage to appease Medea.

The Western Empress gritted her teeth until they ached.

There was no warmth here now. No comfortable chair. Only a single elderly maid—brought from her family's estate long ago—remained by her side.

"That candelabra was a gift from Her Majesty's father! It does not belong to the imperial treasury!"

The old maid wept bitterly, trying to wrest a torch from a sturdy knight's grip.

"If you take that as well, how is our mistress meant to survive? Have you no humanity at all?"

"Forgive me. It is His Majesty's command. You should be grateful for his merciful decision not to cast you into the tower."

The commander repeated the words mechanically, like a puppet reading from a script.

But this is a place no one visits. No one cares.

The tower would be better. At least there, I would not have to watch my former glory crumble to dust before my eyes.

The Western Empress surveyed the ruins of her palace—the shattered vases, the bare walls, the silence where servants once bustled.

"How pitiful I have become..."

Her voice was hollow. A whisper to no one.
