"Some return from the dead; others merely wait to take their place."

Gallo's expression grew grave as he continued his report.

"Master, if the Third Prince is formally named Crown Prince, we can expect an immediate offensive against our faction. The Privy Council has already launched an imperial investigation into Marquis Leonard's affairs, and orders have been issued to withdraw all Façade operatives from the capital."

"Without me present, what threat could they possibly pose?"

Cesare's voice carried its characteristic edge of contempt.

Indeed, the Western Empress was sharpening her blade with singular purpose—determined to repay every slight and indignity she had endured during her imprisonment.

"Your Highness, what would you have us do? We await your command."

Having delivered his full report, Terrence turned not to Cesare but to Medea.

The gesture was deliberate—a mark of respect that acknowledged her authority as equal to, if not exceeding, her husband's in matters of strategy.

Cesare, too, looked to her. His hand moved to cover hers, warm and steady.

The touch was a silent promise: whatever she decided, he would support.

Medea let her gaze wander across the humble cottage.

The warmth of the fireplace still suffused every corner. Beyond the frosted windows, snow continued to fall in heavy drifts, yet inside, all was peaceful and still.

When they returned to Katzen, these quiet days would become nothing more than memories—mornings and evenings spent together like an ordinary couple, answering to no one but themselves.

And yet, Medea had never intended to remain here forever.

"We must return."

Her voice was soft but carried the weight of absolute certainty.

The Farewell

Parting Words

"Leaving so soon? Oh, my heart can scarcely bear it!"

"We owe you more than we can ever repay. Your kindness will not be forgotten."

When Mr. and Mrs. Jenkins returned to find the young couple preparing to depart, their disappointment was palpable.

Mrs. Jenkins, in particular, clung to Medea's hand as though reluctant to let go.

"I feel as though I've barely had time to look after you properly. Your face still shows signs of exhaustion."

In truth, Mrs. Jenkins had watched the young wife transform during her stay. The woman who had arrived pale and trembling, having collapsed after ensuring her husband's safety, had gradually recovered her color and composure.

Now, a gentle pink touched her cheeks, and the anxious, hunted look had faded from her green eyes.

"My husband and I shall remain here always. Promise you'll visit whenever the thought crosses your mind."

Mr. Jenkins nodded in agreement, and Cesare responded by extending a folded envelope of fine parchment.

"My wife and I reside in the capital. Should you ever find yourselves traveling that way, please do not hesitate to call upon us."

"The capital? Goodness, that's a terribly long and difficult journey from here..."

The elderly couple had retreated to this remote glacier specifically to escape the politics and dangers of court life under the Northern Empress. The capital felt like another world entirely.

"Do be careful out there. I've heard the power struggles among the nobility grow more vicious by the day. Though I'm certain a capable young man like yourself will manage..."

Mr. Jenkins trailed off, suddenly feeling foolish for offering unsolicited advice to someone who clearly needed none.

"Still—promise me you'll be cautious. If a blade is too rigid, it will shatter. For your wife's sake if nothing else, you mustn't allow yourself to be wounded again."

He looked at Cesare with genuine concern, hoping this confident young man would survive the bloody machinations of imperial politics.

Cesare regarded him in silence.

There was something strange about receiving such earnest worry from a middle-aged man—someone roughly his own father's age, yet so unlike the Emperor in every way that mattered.

"Do you understand? When you wish to live a long life, follow your wife's counsel unconditionally. That's the wisdom I've gathered from forty years of marriage—even when you think you're right, defer to her judgment. You'll never go hungry that way."

"Oh, stop it. Anyone listening would think you've never given me a moment's grief."

Mrs. Jenkins rolled her eyes as her husband clapped Cesare on the shoulder with paternal familiarity.

"I understand. I will remember."

Cesare inclined his head with uncharacteristic obedience.

From the yard came the eager whinny of horses—two magnificent steeds, one black as midnight and one white as fresh snow, pawing impatiently at the frozen ground.

Cesare and Medea mounted their respective horses with practiced grace. Their retinue—those who had come to find them—offered brief, respectful bows to the elderly couple.

"Take care of yourselves..."

Mr. Jenkins watched them go with a heavy heart. He had witnessed the fall of the Northern Emperor firsthand, and he worried that this proud young man might charge recklessly toward those who wished him harm.

He raised his hand in farewell, holding it aloft until the riders vanished from sight. Only then did the couple turn back toward their home.

"How empty it feels already, with them gone..."

All that remained in the cottage was silence—and a golden envelope resting upon the wooden table.

Feeling inexplicably melancholy, Mr. Jenkins picked up the letter with a heavy sigh.

"Darling? What's wrong? You've gone pale as a ghost."

Her husband had frozen mid-motion, staring at the contents of the envelope as though it had transformed into a venomous serpent.

"F-First... the First Prince. I... I... that means..."

The parchment within bore the unmistakable imperial seal, stamped with the personal sigil of the First Prince himself.

It was a formal pass, designating Mr. Jenkins as a "Distinguished Guest" with unrestricted access to the imperial villa whenever he chose to visit.

I clapped the First Prince on the shoulder. The Reaper of the Battlefield. The man who has never lost a war.

I lectured him about marriage.

"The First Prince..."

"For heaven's sake, why are you clutching your chest instead of the letter? Speak sense!"

Mrs. Jenkins snatched the envelope from her husband's trembling fingers as he lowered himself shakily into the nearest chair, his face an alarming shade of blue.

Meanwhile

The Imperial Palace

Within the halls of the Katzen Imperial Palace, Perdiccas II surveyed the mountain of documents accumulating upon his desk.

In Cesare's absence, administrative matters from across the empire had begun to pile up at an alarming rate. Each day brought fresh complications, fresh crises demanding immediate attention.

The growing disorder made the Emperor's expression sour.

Only now, faced with this bureaucratic avalanche, did he truly comprehend how much his despised eldest son had been handling.

"Empress. What is the status of the funeral arrangements?"

"...The Imperial Guard proceeds with preparations as we speak, Your Majesty."

The Western Empress chose her words with care, watching the Emperor's face for any shift in mood.

"However, the First Princess has not yet returned. Would it not be... premature?"

"Do you not see the documents choking my desk? We must restore order with all possible haste. This is no time for such idle concerns."

Yet opinions regarding Cesare's fate remained divided.

The First Princess, who had ventured onto the glacier in search of her husband, had likewise vanished without trace.

When the pillar trembles, the entire edifice shakes. And so it was with Katzen—servants, nobles, and ministers alike found themselves caught in the uncertainty, their loyalties wavering.

"Should we not at least await the First Princess's return before discussing funeral rites?"

"Are you suggesting we further destabilize the succession in the midst of such chaos?"

"A fair point. But tell me—has the First Princess truly vanished? Or has she merely seized this opportunity to flee to Valdina under cover of confusion?"

More than a month had passed since news of Cesare's disappearance first reached the capital.

Not a single encouraging report had found its way to the palace. Instead, rumors multiplied like winter frost—whispers of the First Prince freezing to death on the glacier, of avalanches claiming both husband and wife.

Now that word had spread of the First Princess's silence as well, more and more voices spoke of the royal couple as already dead.

Even among the imperial vassals, consensus proved elusive.

"Can it truly be? Are they both gone?"

Those who had placed their faith in the First Prince—who had believed he would lead Katzen to unprecedented glory—now found their certainty crumbling.

"If even His Highness, whom the goddess herself seemed to favor, could meet such a pointless end... is Katzen truly cursed beyond redemption?"

"Worse still—with rumors of the First Prince's death spreading beyond our borders, every nation we've conquered is preparing for war. His presence alone kept them cowed. Without him..."

"They've been sharpening their blades for years, waiting for exactly this moment. Were it not for the First Prince, they would already have invaded to reclaim their lost territories."

"Alas... what becomes of us now? Has the goddess truly abandoned this empire?"

Whispers spread through every corner of the realm: the tragic end of the First Prince and his wife might herald the collapse of Katzen itself.

Citizens and nobles alike grew increasingly anxious.

Perdiccas II could feel the fear rippling through his empire—a mounting dread that threatened to unravel everything.

This is my empire...

Bitter fury churned in his stomach.

To realize that his own presence inspired less confidence than that of his presumably dead son—it was an indignity beyond bearing.

Yet regardless of personal grievance, he was still Emperor. In the face of his son's death, he must act for the good of the realm.

"Ensure the funeral is conducted with the utmost grandeur. No expense is to be spared."

"As Your Majesty commands."

The more magnificently he honored his deceased eldest son, the more his own reputation for fairness and righteousness would be enhanced.

Perdiccas II's gaze drifted to a corner of the chamber, where Juan stood in silent attendance, head bowed in perfect submission.

The Emperor rose from his throne and crossed the room to stand before the Third Prince. His faded eyes moved restlessly, calculating, assessing.

At last, he spoke.

"Juan."

The young man kept his head lowered, not daring to meet his father's gaze.

"Can you bear the weight of the Crown Prince's mantle?"

Juan's head snapped up, shock written plainly across his features.

But comprehension followed swiftly.

This was not a genuine offer of succession—not a recognition of worth or ability. It was merely another piece moved across the board, a desperate attempt to stabilize an empire shaking at its foundations.
