Chapter 310

The Counter-Trap

"The hunter becomes the hunted. The snare springs backwards."

Every eye in the ballroom fixed upon the First Princess.

The face that moments ago had appeared serene and composed no longer seemed quite so lovely.

"Your Highness, explain yourself."

The Imperial Commander spoke with undisguised triumph.

Medea responded slowly, deliberately.

"It's true that I instructed my guard to remove the sword and dispose of it."

She admits it!

The Princess confessed!

Sharp intakes of breath rippled throughout the assembled nobility.

"However, my intentions were entirely innocent. I swear before the Goddess that I have never done anything to bring shame upon Katzen."

Despite countless watchful, suspicious gazes, the First Princess addressed the Emperor with remarkable composure.

"There exists a reason I couldn't easily disclose—as a member of the Katzen imperial family. If Your Majesty permits, I shall explain everything privately."

"You confess with your own mouth? Valdina's spy!"

Perdiccas II cut off his daughter-in-law mid-sentence. His eyes gleamed sharp as freshly forged steel.

"Father."

The First Prince stepped forward.

"How can you render judgment after hearing only one side?"

"Have you become so blinded by your wife that you can no longer distinguish heaven from earth?"

The Emperor's fury escalated beyond reason.

"Are you Katzen's First Prince or Valdina's? Will you sacrifice this empire at your wife's altar and grovel before foreign powers?"

The Western Empress added her voice to the accusations.

"Princess, forgive me, but attempting to escape this situation through eloquent wordplay will not suffice. You were accepted into the Katzen imperial family, yet you've betrayed that trust by conspiring with your homeland... This constitutes treason."

Cold compassion filled her narrowed eyes.

"His Majesty's immunity may preserve your life. But you cannot remain within this empire any longer."

The Emperor's expression hardened further at the Empress's pronouncement.

"These insufferable Valdinian siblings dared strike me from behind—abusing the very privileges I granted them!"

Observing her husband's mounting rage, the Western Empress suppressed a breathless laugh of satisfaction.

Then, as though genuinely sorrowful, she turned toward the First Prince.

"Cesare, you were careless. I cannot believe people could be so deceived..."

She didn't forget to plant another dagger in her political enemy's ribs.

"When you allow the wrong person into your household, it brings complete ruination. The imperial family proves no exception to this rule."

While maintaining gracious dignity befitting an imperial matron, she managed to simultaneously rebuke her supposedly short-sighted stepson and his treacherous wife.

Medea's lips curved upward slightly.

"You condemn my homeland when nothing has yet been proven. What if your accusations prove false, Your Majesty?"

"First Princess, I already stated this situation cannot be resolved through clever rhetoric."

The Western Empress's voice remained kind—as though soothing an immature child—yet chilling accusation threaded through every word.

"..."

Medea regarded the Western Empress with unsettling intensity.

"First Princess? Why do you stare so?"

'Has she realized this was my doing? Impossible... Even if she suspects, it's far too late.'

Despite the momentary unease twisting through her chest, the Western Empress maintained her composed smile.

Meanwhile, Medea released a short sigh.

"You know... I've grown quite accustomed to watching good intentions twisted into malice."

Her voice emerged very slowly, very deliberately.

"If that's truly what you desire... I suppose there's nothing to be done."

She delivered the cryptic statement as though aimed directly at the Western Empress, then turned her attention to the blade the Imperial Commander had presented as evidence.

"It's true my knight recently acquired this sword. However..."

'She confesses to the sword but denies the correspondence?'

The First Prince and Valdina's young Chancellor, understanding the implication, moved simultaneously.

Each retrieved the parchment nearest to them from where they'd scattered across the floor.

"Interesting."

Though both executed identical gestures, their resulting expressions differed dramatically.

Unlike the First Prince, whose smooth features unexpectedly hardened, Valdina's Minister's lips quirked with barely suppressed amusement.

"Secret correspondence from Knight Menil of the Agemas to his Valdinian homeland."

Detecting the faint mockery threading through Sissair's tone, observers wondered how audaciously proud Valdina—now elevated to imperial status—would behave even when caught red-handed.

"Examine it yourself, Your Majesty, Emperor of Katzen."

Sissair attempted to hand the parchment to Perdiccas II.

The Emperor snorted dismissively and waved him away.

"That won't be necessary. What more do I need to know? Valdina may claim imperial status now, but your mentality remains that of a provincial backwater."

"Then surely you won't object if I read this aloud here."

The Chief Chamberlain froze at that statement. Something felt... wrong.

"If that guard truly served as Valdina's spy, neither the First Prince, his wife, nor this minister would remain so utterly composed."

Before the Emperor could deliver another reprimand, the attendant stepped forward swiftly and collected the parchment.

"...!"

His weathered hand unfolded the small note and stopped abruptly. His wrinkled face drained of all color.

"Your... Your Majesty. This... I believe you must see this personally."

With grave deliberation, the Chief Chamberlain extended it toward the Emperor.

As Perdiccas II read, his complexion grew progressively redder. His philtrum and eyebrows began trembling violently.

The reaction suggested near-manic fury—though the gathered nobles couldn't comprehend why.

'What could the correspondence possibly contain?'

'Why is he reacting like that?'

The Western Empress frowned as well.

'The parchment should contain Katzen's classified intelligence and strategic vulnerabilities. I personally selected content guaranteed to enrage the Emperor, but this reaction seems excessive even so...'

Meanwhile, the Emperor glared at the First Princess with burning intensity. After a prolonged, weighted silence, he spoke.

"...You possess considerable foresight."

Whenever his anger boiled over like an overheated kettle, he always pivoted to sudden, unexpected compliments. The assembly grew confused.

'The Emperor's capriciousness strikes again. There's no predicting which direction he'll bounce next.'

The Western Empress bit the inside of her lip hard enough to taste copper.

"Your Majesty, this constitutes treason! I implore you to demonstrate your dignity as Katzen's supreme ruler by not permitting such capital crimes to pass unpunished."

Third Prince Juan, sensing an excellent opportunity to cripple the First Prince's ascendant faction, supported his mother's position.

"The First Princess is my sister-in-law, but this concerns Katzen's honor! Father, please reconsider—"

"SILENCE!"

CRASH!

The teacup hurled by the Emperor shattered at the Western Empress's feet. A sharp fragment struck and bounced away.

"Empress! If you command the Inner Palace, you should know how to embrace and protect this family! Why do you lead the charge in creating scandals instead?"

His fierce voice reverberated through the stunned ballroom.

"Return to your palace immediately and confine yourself there. Do not emerge until I grant permission."

At the Emperor's own birthday celebration—in the presence of distinguished guests, ministers, and foreign envoys—the head of the Inner Palace had just been placed under house arrest.

It amounted to a public slap across the Western Empress's face.

'Why...?'

This humiliation bordered on outright insult. Both the Western Empress and her son's faces hardened like stone masks.

She couldn't comprehend why the Emperor's fury had suddenly redirected toward her.

'How did everything go so catastrophically wrong?'

'What could have possibly wounded his pride this time—'

At that moment, the Western Empress's gaze fell upon the parchment scattered at her feet alongside the shattered teacup.

Instantly, ice water seemed to pour down her spine.

"M-Mother..."

The Third Prince called out cautiously, but she didn't appear to hear her son's voice.

With trembling fingers, the Western Empress slowly retrieved the parchment.

Dear Alfie,

'Why... How...'

As the Western Empress read the unfolded letter, blood slowly drained from her features.

Her fingertips trembled visibly.

The familiar handwriting appeared unmistakably on the parchment:

Katzen feels like a silent prison. Are you well after abandoning me? You don't even care about this heart bound by shadows, growing old and sick, rotting day by day, do you?

'Why does correspondence written in MY handwriting appear on this parchment?'

She squeezed her eyes shut, but the incriminating words remained unchanged when she looked again.

'Bridget—!'

The Western Empress's head snapped up.

She searched desperately for her chief lady-in-waiting—the one she'd personally instructed to plant the fabricated correspondence in that sword.

Her eyes darted frantically across the ballroom.

But Bridget was nowhere to be found.

Nowhere in that bright, bustling celebration could she locate any trace of Bridget—who typically moved like her mistress's shadow, her hands and feet.

As cold realization swept down the Western Empress's spine, understanding finally crystallized.

'I... I've fallen into a trap.'
