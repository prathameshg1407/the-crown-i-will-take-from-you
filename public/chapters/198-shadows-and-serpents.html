Chapter 198

Shadows and
Serpents

"The enemy of my enemy is a weapon waiting to be wielded."

Back at the Claudio mansion.

After Birna's carriage had departed, Raju made his way to Catherine.

She was still seething—fingers working at her handkerchief with such violence that the silk was beginning to tear.

"Catherine, what has gotten into you? Why do you keep taking your frustrations out on Birna? What crime has the girl committed?"

"Do you not understand why I am like this? I feel as though I am losing my mind!"

At the rebuke from Raju—the one person who had always defended her—Catherine collapsed and burst into tears.

"I cannot bear it any longer! I simply cannot!"

"..."

"Those responsible for reducing Samon to that state are thriving—flourishing—while my son suffers!"

"Tell me plainly. I can only assist you if I am certain that Samon was not involved in the plot against His Majesty."

Catherine's bloodshot eyes blazed with venom as the memories surged back.

"I am certain of it. The explosion. Samon's injuries. All of it was that girl's doing."

She bit her lip until it bled.

The memory of her niece arriving at the mansion with that mocking bouquet—the barely concealed triumph in every gesture—made her vision blur red.

Rrrip.

The handkerchief shredded in her grip.

When she spoke again, her voice had descended to something barely human—a tone that seemed impossible from a woman once celebrated for her elegance.

"I will never forgive her. Never. Not for what she has done to my son..."

But even as the words left her mouth, Catherine's rational mind understood the truth.

No matter how consuming her hatred, there was nothing she could do.

The neglected half-princess, once starved for the barest scrap of affection, was now the sole heir to a rising empire.

Meanwhile, the beautiful Duchess Claudio—who had once dominated the kingdom's social circles—had been reduced to the status of a common nobody.

Without title or power, the wretched reality was that she could not even approach Medea, let alone exact vengeance.

Catherine's teeth ground together until they ached.

"Catherine. Calm yourself. If you continue like this, you will collapse before anything else."

Catherine crumpled into Raju's arms, clutching at him with desperate hands.

He was the only salvation within her reach now—the one person she was certain would never abandon her.

"Medea. Avenge us on that girl. I am in agony over Samon. Robbie, please—kill her for me. Will you?"

Her nails dug into his arm, drawing blood through the fabric.

He showed no sign of noticing the pain.

"No, Catherine."

"..."

"Death is far too merciful. That girl should experience what we have experienced."

Something kindled in Raju's eyes—a dark, feverish light.

"I speak of days when one does not feel alive. Would that not be fair?"

Catherine froze at the oily malice coating his words.

The wild gleam in his eyes. The tremor in his voice. The audible pounding of his heart.

She lifted her head from his chest.

This was not the cunning, unflappable Raju she knew. This man, whoever he was, felt like a stranger.

A chill of unease crept down her spine.

"Robbie... what have you done?"

"Do not trouble yourself, my love. When have I ever failed you?"

His voice brimmed with quiet confidence.

"I have already set things in motion. All we need do now is wait for the opportune moment."

Like a bird in flight—its wings shattered mid-air.

That exquisite instant when it plummets to earth and snaps its neck.

"You said you have taken action? Is that why you traveled to Katzen recently...?"

There had been another purpose beyond locating a physician for Samon.

Raju nodded slowly.

"I have found someone we can trust."

He stroked Catherine's shoulder with tender affection as the tension gradually drained from her body and she sank against him.

"An enemy who harbors a grudge against the Princess every bit as deep as our own."

"...I pray that day arrives soon."

"It will come. I promise you."

The sinister malevolence that had blossomed between the two lovers hung in the air long after—a miasma with nowhere to dissipate.

— One Week Earlier —

An alley on the outskirts of the Katzen Empire

Raju adjusted his hat as he surveyed the squalid street. He had traveled to the empire in haste, ostensibly to locate a physician capable of treating Samon's injuries.

Instead of his customary gleaming attire, he wore a black robe of deliberately plain fabric.

"Count, why have we come to such a filthy, dangerous place? And without even a carriage?"

It was indeed unusual—traversing the city on foot with only a single escort.

Particularly when this was precisely the sort of slum Raju typically refused to set foot in.

"Hold your tongue. Have you forgotten we have been infiltrated by a spy? I may well be under surveillance myself."

Raju's reply was curt. His eyes swept the shadows ceaselessly, vigilant for any threat.

If not for their conspiracy against the Emperor of Valdina, how could their enemy have discovered their plans and maneuvered Samon into that trap?

The vanished butler... How did he infiltrate our household so completely?

Even now, the memory raised gooseflesh. The seamless insertion had left not a single trace.

Had the explosion not occurred—had the old servant not vanished—Raju would never have realized the man was anything other than what he appeared.

This is not good. We are losing ground.

Repeated failures. Mounting despair. At some point, hard-won instinct had begun sounding alarms within him.

As a Valdinian establishing himself in the Katzen Empire, he had endured years of struggle.

Perhaps that was why he could not shake the sensation of being positioned on a vast chessboard—a mere pawn shuffled about by an invisible hand, every square already calculated.

He could not articulate why. He simply knew.

"Damn this place."

Raju's lip curled in disgust.

The alley was so narrow that two men could not pass without brushing shoulders. A fetid stench—equal parts rot and refuse—saturated the air.

"Here it is."

His escort halted before a small tavern wedged into the alley's dead end and pushed open the wooden door.

Creeeak.

The door—so begrimed it appeared almost black—protested with every movement, its hinges rusted nearly beyond function.

Inside, the air was thick with a nauseating melange of stale alcohol, rancid food, and unwashed bodies.

"He is not on the first floor."

"Then we proceed upstairs."

Raju cast a contemptuous glance across the crowd and ascended the rickety staircase.

"...There."

He strode toward a secluded corner on the second floor.

A large man in a filthy robe occupied the shadows, drinking with the desperate intensity of one trying to drown something that refused to die.

"More! Bring me more!"

The tavern owner—a corpulent man with a perpetually sour expression—waddled over with arms crossed.

"This one again! Do you have any idea how many drinks you have consumed without paying?!"

"I told you I will pay! Do you doubt my word? Do you not recognize who stands before you?!"

The man's speech was slurred, his tongue thick with drink. Bloodshot eyes bulged from their sockets, lending him the aspect of a madman.

"You should thank me for restraining myself—for not snapping your worthless necks with a single flick of my wrist—!"

He kicked the table leg and began to rage.

"This wretch—!"

"A moment, if you please."

The owner's mounting fury evaporated the instant Raju pressed a gold coin into his palm. He became as docile as a cat offered cream.

"Just how much has this wastrel consumed? Ah, never mind. There are no other customers on the second floor regardless—speak as freely as you wish."

Raju approached the slouched figure.

"More drink, I said—"

The man was on the verge of seizing another grimy bottle when he registered Raju's presence. His bleary eyes struggled to focus.

"Well, well. That face... I know that face."

"It has been some time, General."

Raju's greeting carried a peculiar weight.

A drunkard, seemingly hiding in the forgotten corners of the Katzen Empire's underbelly.

It was Jared.
