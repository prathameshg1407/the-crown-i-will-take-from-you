Chapter 233

The Devil's
Bargain

"Miracles always demand a price—the question is who pays it."

Even after venting his fury on the loyal subordinate who had stood faithfully by his side, Samon could not quell the rage boiling in his chest.

The more he dwelled on it, the harder his teeth ground together.

"Birna, you stupid bitch! If you were going to set everything ablaze, you should have spared what was mine!"

Most of the catastrophes now drowning the Claudio family were problems Raju could have solved with a wave of his hand—or a fistful of coin. If only he were still alive.

"My lord... the port fees are coming due. What should we do?"

The most urgent crisis was the mounting cost of harbor access and storage for their contraband. Smuggling operated in shadows—and shadows required precise, untraceable payments. Any misstep would create a situation far more "difficult" than mere embarrassment.

The bills arrived daily, each one larger than the last. Watching the numbers climb felt like sliding inexorably toward a cliff's edge.

There seemed to be no escape.

"I'm sorry, but additional funding is not possible at this time."

Samon had swallowed his pride and approached even the most notorious moneylenders—the kind who charged interest in blood. Today, as on every other day, they refused him.

No one was willing to extend a hand to a man already marked for ruin.

As he hobbled out of the establishment, Samon's cane caught on an uneven stone. He stumbled, crashed to the ground.

The wooden leg splintered against the cobbles. A piece broke away and clattered into the gutter.

"Damn it all!"

The temper he had fought so hard to restrain finally exploded.

His eyes, bloodshot with hatred, looked as though they might weep crimson.

"...If only my legs were whole!"

He felt trapped on all sides. Cornered. Suffocating.

Everything was falling apart. His money dwindling. His people fleeing. His own body failing him.

If his legs still worked, he would have raced from door to door, scheme to scheme, clawing for any way out. But he couldn't. And so, piece by piece, his will crumbled.

Samon lifted his head and glared at the distant spire where Valdina's palace hung against the sky. His jaw tightened with impotent fury.

They mock me openly now. And I can do nothing.

At that moment, someone retrieved the cane he had hurled away in his rage.

"Here you are."

Cream-colored robes rippled at the edge of his vision.

That robe...

Samon's eyes widened as recognition struck.

"You're... the Saintess?"

A short while later, Samon found himself in private conversation with the Saintess—something he had never imagined possible.

What she proposed was even less imaginable.

"I can scarcely believe it. You want me to move against Medea?"

His clouded blue eyes darted nervously.

"Why would you—a Saintess—make such a suggestion?"

"Come now. Is this really the time to be particular about such things?"

A subtle mockery threaded through her beautiful smile.

"No one can touch the Medea of today. She has made herself a sanctuary—untouchable, inviolable. It is a direct challenge to me... and to God."

As their conversation deepened, Samon began to realize that the woman before him—this so-called Saintess—was nothing like the virtuous figure the world believed her to be.

Even a messenger of the Goddess can harbor such ugliness in her heart.

Yes... when we first arrived at this strait, the Saintess aided us. I thought it coincidence at the time, but now I see—it was always about destroying Medea.

"So what's in it for me?"

Samon crossed his arms.

"What do I gain by dragging Medea down?"

"I thought you hated her too. Am I wrong?"

"Yes. I hate her. I hate her so much I dream of tearing her apart every single day."

His voice dropped, bitter as ash.

"But so what? My parents are dead. And above all—"

He looked down at his ruined leg with burning contempt.

"—I can do nothing with these useless limbs. How am I supposed to challenge Medea and all of Valdina?"

Even a fool can learn.

Samon's ambition—his hunger for revenge—had once burned so fiercely it consumed every thought. But his broken body had forced that fire to gutter and die.

Interest flickered in Rachel's eyes.

"What if that leg could be restored?"

"Ridiculous."

Samon scoffed immediately.

"Don't insult me. I've been told a thousand times that even the greatest divine power cannot accomplish such a thing."

"Is the Goddess's omnipotence truly limited to holy power alone?"

Rachel laughed softly.

"I know secrets behind miracles that foolish mortals cannot even imagine. If you doubt me... allow me to demonstrate."

Moments later, Samon sat blindfolded, his fingers drumming nervously against the table.

Unable to see, he felt exposed. Vulnerable. He had no idea what the Saintess was doing.

"What exactly are you trying to show me with all this theatrical—AAAGH!"

Searing pain exploded through his leg—fire racing up nerves that should have been dead. The acrid stench of blood and burning flesh flooded his nostrils.

They've set my leg on fire! They blindfolded me and—

"Are you insane?!"

He tore off the blindfold and hurled it at Rachel.

"If you think you can treat me like some worthless toy just because I'm a cripple—"

The words died in his throat.

The leg that had been severed at the thigh...

...had grown flesh down to the knee.

"H-how is this..."

Samon's trembling hands explored the regenerated limb. Flesh. Bone. Muscle. Everything he felt beneath his palms was real.

His breath came in shallow gasps. His eyes, wide as lanterns, refused to return to normal.

Since losing his leg, he had pursued every treatment, every medicine, every procedure known to man. Not once had anyone even suggested the limb could be regrown.

"How does it feel to witness a miracle?"

Rachel's voice was honey-sweet.

"This was merely a hasty demonstration. With proper preparation, the restoration will be complete. Consider this... a preview of what your choice could earn you."

"...Truly? You can give me back my leg?"

"Do you require further proof after seeing it with your own eyes?"

Samon stared at her in silence, desperate for certainty.

Rachel shrugged, as if his doubt was merely tedious.

"I swear to God, Samon. Your leg will be restored."

Samon nodded as though in a trance.

"I'll do it. Anything. Just tell me what you need. If I can have my leg back..."

For a man who had witnessed a miracle with his own eyes, doubt was no longer possible.

In his current state of fevered hope, he felt as though he could gladly sever Medea's head himself.

"How fortunate that you understand quickly. It saves us both considerable effort."

Rachel's smile was a blade wrapped in silk.

"I'm told the old maidservant who once attended the Princess is now in your household."

"Yes. I'll have Marieu prepared for whatever you require."

Samon departed.

His clouded eyes, still burning with the afterimage of the miracle, glowed with a heat that had not touched them in years.

The Price of Miracles

"Hkk—!"

The moment Samon was gone, the white-robed priest who had been silently guarding Rachel doubled over and began to vomit blood.

The crimson fluid he expelled writhed with dark life—countless insects squirming within it. The compressed essence of sacrifices offered to forbidden magic.

"What are you thinking? Regenerating an entire leg? You don't have the reserves to waste on worthless pawns like him."

Even that brief demonstration had exacted this much pain.

Transplanting a demonic vessel—as they had done with Jared—was simple enough. But Samon Claudio wanted his original body regenerated.

That was black magic of an entirely different magnitude. The difference between wading in shallows and drowning in the abyss.

The price of such sorcery scaled with how severely it violated the Golden Rule—the fundamental laws governing reality itself.

This particular working would require nothing less than the generation of an artificial monster wave.

"The Golden Tower will never permit this."

"I don't need the Golden Tower's permission."

Rachel's voice was gentle, patient—as though instructing a slow child in obvious truths.

"Samon will be disposed of regardless. He is merely bait to draw out our true prey."

"But you swore an oath. You invoked the Goddess's name."

"And what is an oath to an invisible deity?"

"You don't understand. You cannot fathom her power—how she conquered the world in the beginning—"

"And in the end, she abandoned this land, did she not?"

Rachel's smile turned razor-sharp.

"I am here to claim the throne that vanished goddess left empty."

At Rachel's dismissive snort, the warlock abandoned his warnings and fell silent.

This woman's malice is evolving.

He observed her transformation with something between fascination and dread. She no longer hesitated to proclaim herself divine. She had learned to bait human souls with forbidden power, dangling miracles like a fisherman's lure.

This was not the same woman who had first arrived in Valdina.

Something far more dangerous had taken her place.
