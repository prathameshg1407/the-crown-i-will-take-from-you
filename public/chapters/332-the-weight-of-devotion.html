Chapter 332

The Weight of
Devotion

"Even in the darkness, the thread that binds two hearts remains unbroken."

The interior of the cave offered little refuge from the merciless cold.

Though the stone walls blocked the worst of the snow and wind, the frigid air still seeped up from the frozen ground beneath her feet. The atmosphere hung heavy and damp, each breath visible as a pale ghost before her lips.

Medea rushed to Cesare's side, her heart pounding against her ribs as she assessed his condition.

His lips were cracked and colorless, drained of all vitality. The face she knew so well—those sculpted features that could shift from cold authority to unexpected tenderness—was now marred by countless wounds, each one a testament to the battle he had fought alone.

Dried blood crusted across his back where he had torn out the blade that had pierced him. His entire body bore the evidence of violence—cuts, bruises, and deeper injuries that spoke of a struggle against impossible odds.

Medea's eyes traced the scene outside the cave entrance: the scattered remains of demonic creatures, the long trail of blood smeared across the stone floor. She understood now. He had eliminated every last threat before dragging himself to this meager shelter, finally succumbing to unconsciousness only when he could fight no longer.

"Cesare."

Her voice emerged as barely more than a whisper as she shook him gently, then with increasing urgency.

"Please—wake up. Can you hear me? I'm here now. I'm here."

The spear that had pierced his back must have been tipped with poison. When she pressed her palm to his forehead, she recoiled at the searing heat.

"His entire body... it's burning like an inferno."

With trembling hands, Medea retrieved the vial of holy water from her pack and carefully tipped it past his cracked lips.

But even as the sacred liquid disappeared down his throat, she watched with growing dread as his condition showed no improvement. The fever continued to rage unchecked.

Her heart plummeted like a stone cast into an abyss.

For one terrible, frozen moment, she could not move. The nightmare she had feared—the vision that had haunted her steps through the endless white—threatened to become reality before her eyes.

I need to alert the others. Someone must come—

With shaking fingers, Medea withdrew a smoke signal from her supplies and struck the flint to ignite it.

The spark caught, but the feeble flame flickered once, twice—and then surrendered to the pervasive dampness, extinguishing as though swallowed by the cave itself.

During her tumble through the snow earlier, the signal had been soaked through along with everything else she carried.

Outside, the blizzard had returned with renewed fury, the wind screaming past the cave mouth like a chorus of vengeful spirits.

What do I do? If we remain here like this, he will...

Each passing moment wound her anxiety tighter, a coil of dread spiraling in her chest until she could barely breathe.

Neeeigh—!

A familiar whinny pierced through the blade-sharp howl of the wind.

"Silva!"

At the cave's entrance stood a magnificent white horse, her coat gleaming like fresh snow even against the storm's gray fury.

"How did you find me? How did you possibly navigate to this place?"

As though she understood every word, Silva stamped her hooves and tossed her elegant head, gesturing with her muzzle toward something Medea had nearly forgotten—the golden thread of light still extending from her mother's necklace.

"The light... it's still connected?"

Even after guiding her to this cave—to Cesare—the luminous golden thread had not faded. It stretched onward, beckoning toward some destination yet unseen.

The journey, it seemed, was far from over.

Medea hesitated for only a heartbeat. Cesare's condition was deteriorating too rapidly to wait for rescue that might never come in this merciless storm.

Yes—following that light is far better than remaining in this frozen tomb.

Whatever lay at the end of that golden path, Medea trusted it would not lead her astray. Her mother's spirit had brought her to Cesare; surely it would guide them both to safety.

Medea worked as quickly as her numb fingers would allow, binding Cesare's wounds with strips torn from her own clothing, cushioning him as best she could.

Silva approached with the grace of her noble breeding, lowering herself to her knees so Medea could lift him onto her back.

Transferring an unconscious man onto horseback was no simple task. Despite her care, the movement jostled his injuries, and a low groan of pain escaped his lips even in his oblivion.

"I'm sorry, Cesare. Just a little longer—please hold on."

Medea brushed the hair from his furrowed brow, her touch feather-light against his fevered skin.

"Silva, follow the light as you did before. Can you see it?"

Neeeeigh!

True to her reputation as a horse of exceptional lineage, Silva bore both riders through the deep snow without faltering, her powerful legs carrying them steadily forward.

"Just a little further. Just a little more..."

As Cesare's breathing grew shallower, Medea's desperation mounted with each passing moment. Again and again, she pressed her ear to his chest, straining to hear the rhythm of his heart over the roar of the wind.

She wrapped her own cloak around him, shielding his face from the biting snow, even as the cold cut through her unprotected body like countless tiny knives.

"Faster, Silva—we must go faster!"

Medea urged the mare onward, paying no heed to the frozen needles of wind and ice that stung her cheeks raw. All that mattered was the man cradled in her arms.

And then, without warning, Silva halted.

"Silva, we cannot stop—we must keep moving—"

Medea looked up, and her words died in her throat.

"Who's there?"

A figure stood before them in the swirling white, motionless as a statue carved from ice.

Earlier

The Battle Within

A knife-edged wind sliced across Cesare's cheek, drawing a thin line of warmth before the cold reclaimed him.

Whether they pierce me through or burn me alive, the end remains the same.

The thought surfaced with crystalline clarity even in that moment of chaos. Rather than freeze in panic, Cesare chose action—hurling himself off the cliff's edge, dragging the fleeing warlock down with him into the abyss.

The warlock's desperate flames missed their mark, but Cesare's dark aura found its target with lethal precision.

"Ugh—gkh—"

The tendril of shadow carved a perfect line across the warlock's throat. The sorcerer's eyes widened in disbelief as blood fountained between his clutching fingers.

Locked together in their plummet, warlock and knight clashed with savage fury. Just as the deadly spears of ice threatened to impale them both, the dying warlock's barrier flared to life once more, sealing them within a dome of darkness.

"Hahahaha! Cesare! Let us see if you can escape this hell a second time!"

Even as his lifeblood pooled beneath him, the warlock's voice rang with manic triumph.

"You have ruined everything we built—decades of preparation, shattered by your hand. If I must descend into oblivion, I will drag you down with me!"

The hatred blazing in his eyes burned brighter than any flame.

"You have exhausted your magical reserves. Every drop of primordial darkness I possess—I pour it all into this barrier. You will drown in shadow!"

He choked on his final words, but the venom in his gaze remained until the light left his eyes entirely.

"So the barrier persists even after your death."

Cesare rose to his feet with a grimace, surveying the darkness that now surrounded him. Within the barrier, countless demonic beasts fixed their hungry gazes upon him, circling closer with each passing breath.

Grrrrrrr...

He adjusted his grip on his sword, feeling the familiar weight settle into his palm.

The wound in his back had grown stiff and unyielding, each movement sending lances of fire through his nerves. The dagger the warlock had plunged between his shoulders had clearly been tipped with some foul poison.

An inconvenience, nothing more. He had endured far worse.

This was simply another chapter in a lifetime of battles against overwhelming odds.

"SHRIEEEEK—!"

"GRAAAH!"

One. Two. Ten. Twenty.

At some point, Cesare stopped counting the beasts that fell to his blade. They came in endless waves—all of them fixated on the sole living target within the barrier.

His supplies dwindled with each passing hour. The sacred water of the World Tree, the blessed vials meant for healing—one by one, they ran dry. Eventually, even his wounds stopped closing, the regenerative magic exhausted beyond recovery.

When at last silence fell and the final creature lay motionless at his feet, the blizzard outside began to rage with renewed violence.

Cesare forced his battered body to move, searching for any refuge from the storm's fury.

Even as his strength faded, he refused to entertain thoughts of death. No—he would not permit himself such weakness.

"I will survive."

The words fell from his lips like a vow carved in stone.

He had to return to Medea.

When her face surfaced in his mind—those vivid green eyes that burned with such fierce determination even in the darkest moments—Cesare felt an impossible surge of strength flow through his failing body.

This is not the strength that comes before death. I refuse to accept that.

"Hah..."

A bitter laugh escaped him, though he could not say why.

He tried to ignore the fever climbing ever higher within him, the way his thoughts scattered like leaves in a gale.

I have not yet fulfilled her wishes. Her enemies still draw breath. I cannot stop here—not like this.

He had survived the primordial darkness once before, when it had threatened to consume him entirely. He would not fall to something so mundane as poison and cold.

His fingers twitched against the frozen stone. The cave had become a universe of shadow, infinite and empty.

What caught his dimming gaze was not the promise of a golden throne, nor visions of imperial glory.

It was a single point of light—warm as Venus hanging low on the horizon, soft as the green of spring leaves.

Without thinking, Cesare reached toward it, his arm rising of its own accord.

Because that light was his only regret.

His arm fell to the frozen floor, strength finally abandoning him.

How pitiful. I always believed I would fight until my final breath, but this ending is rather... undignified.

I hope she never learns of this. The image of her face, looking up at me like a lost child searching for an answer I could not give—it haunts me still.

I should never have left things as they were.

Cesare had always believed himself free of regret—a man who made his choices and accepted their consequences without looking back.

But now, teetering on the edge of oblivion, he finally understood why mortals clung so desperately to their might-have-beens.

I wanted to make her laugh. That was all I ever needed—to see joy replace the sorrow in those beautiful eyes. Yet I was stubborn. I held back when I should have reached forward, silent when I should have spoken.

His thoughts dissolved into a river of regret, and then—

Nothing.

Later

Awakening

"Are you mad? Sleeping through death's door like it's an afternoon nap?"

When Cesare's eyes finally opened, they found an unfamiliar ceiling above him.

Warmth. That was the first sensation that registered—true warmth, after what felt like an eternity of ice. A small room surrounded him, thick with the mingled scents of medicinal herbs and dried grass.

A middle-aged man with an impressive mustache stood over him, arms crossed, expression somewhere between exasperation and relief.

"Thank the gods you finally woke. I was beginning to think I'd be stuck with a very expensive corpse."

Cesare made to sit up—and stopped.

Something held his arm. A grip so gentle it should have been easy to break, yet somehow it anchored him more surely than iron chains.

"Your wife seems rather attached to you. Hasn't let go even in her sleep—clutches your arm like she's afraid you'll disappear the moment she releases it."

The man's grumbling faded to meaningless noise.

Silver hair spilled across the rough-woven blanket like moonlight on still water. Cesare's hand moved of its own accord, brushing the strands aside to reveal the face beneath.

Her features were drawn with exhaustion, dark circles shadowing her closed eyes. And there—along the pale curve of her cheek—a fresh scratch stood out against her skin, raw and angry.

Something twisted in Cesare's chest at the sight.

"You'll be paying that woman back for the rest of your days, mark my words. Braved a blizzard through treacherous mountain terrain, she did. Held on without rest until I'd confirmed you'd survive."

The healer shook his head in grudging admiration.

"Even when her own fever was raging, she wouldn't let go of your hand. Didn't even notice she was burning up herself. You're blessed beyond measure to have such a wife."

The words painted a picture Cesare could scarcely believe.

He had never imagined she would come for him. Never dared to hope she might brave such danger for his sake.

With infinite care, Cesare lifted his hand to her face, his fingertips hovering just above the wound on her cheek. The sight of it—this evidence of what she had endured to find him—felt like a blade sliding between his ribs.

Fury and anguish warred within him. That she had risked herself so recklessly, that she had come so close to harm because of him—

And yet...

Dea... you came looking for me.

Perhaps not with the same intensity that I feel for you—but you needed me. You reached for me.

That is enough. That is more than enough.

He could not contain the joy that bloomed within him—a humble, grateful warmth that spread through his chest like sunrise after endless night.

His fingertips traced the curve of her jaw with reverent slowness, coming to rest where her hand still clung to his arm. Gently, carefully, he wove his fingers through hers.

A large, calloused hand wrapped around her smaller one like a promise—like a vow renewed.

Cesare bent forward, his movement slow and deliberate.

His lips pressed against the back of her hand—

heavy with unspoken words, tender with devotion too vast for language.

In that quiet room, far from courts and conflicts, far from the ghosts of past and the shadows of prophecy—

Two souls rested side by side.

One in dreamless sleep, having crossed through hell to find him.

One newly awakened, holding her hand as though it were the most precious thing in all the world.

And perhaps it was.
