"A broken vessel cannot hold water—nor can a broken man hold power."

"You did this to my son!"

"Uncle, I would advise you to mind your words—particularly those you cannot take responsibility for."

Medea's voice was silk over steel.

"Unless, of course, you feel confident facing us alone."

"..."

Joaquin's fury faltered.

This girl was no longer the isolated half-princess he had once dismissed so easily. Behind those delicate shoulders stood Peleus—and beyond him, a rising empire that grew more formidable by the day.

A chill crept through Joaquin's chest. Samon had been the family's lighthouse, their beacon of hope. Now that beacon lay shattered, and the darkness felt absolute.

The phantom pain flared in his ruined eye socket—a searing reminder of what defiance had already cost him.

"Tell me, Uncle—does a beaten dog not know when to tuck its tail?"

Her laughter rang out, bright and crystalline as water droplets shattering against stone. The sound was utterly incongruous with the cruelty of her words.

"It is time you confronted reality. I know you, Uncle. You carry yourself as though you have lost everything—yet you have not surrendered your ambitions. Not truly."

"..."

"But your only son—the sole instrument capable of making those dreams manifest—now lies broken beyond repair."

Medea's eyes softened with what might have been pity. The effect was devastating.

"Continuing your bloodline will prove difficult enough. A candidate for Valdina's succession? Impossible."

Her voice was slow, almost tender—each syllable a blade slipping between Joaquin's ribs.

"What a blessing this is for us. First you, Uncle, and now dear Brother Samon. I cannot help but think the goddess herself has chosen to enlighten you."

"..."

"Abandon this futile dream. It was always beyond your grasp."

"You seem overjoyed, Medea. Positively delighted."

Joaquin's voice emerged through clenched teeth, but Medea merely dipped into a graceful curtsy.

"Then I shall take my leave, Uncle. Do take care of yourself."

A pause. Her smile sharpened.

"This will be the last time we meet while you still draw breath."

The words carried a terrible finality—the implicit assumption that Joaquin would never set foot in Valdina again.

The carriage wheels groaned as the vehicle lurched forward, raising a plume of dust in its wake.

Joaquin stood rooted to the spot, bloodshot eyes fixed on the retreating carriage until the antique Valdina crest vanished from sight.

"...Damn her."

He had not managed a single retort. Not one.

Because everything she said was true.

Joaquin had never—not for a single moment—relinquished his hunger for the throne.

Not when they took his eye. Not when they stripped him of his titles. Somewhere in the depths of his heart, an absurd hope had continued to flicker: that one day he would return to Valdina and reclaim what was rightfully his.

"But your only son, who could make that dream come true, ended up like that..."

Medea's words echoed through his skull.

My blood. My lineage. My legacy.

With Samon crippled, his long-cherished ambition had become impossible to fulfill.

Joaquin's heart raced with desperation. Was this truly the end? Was there no path forward?

Is there nothing left for me?

The question consumed him utterly.

He did not notice that he had stepped directly into Medea's trap.

At this very moment—while his son hovered between life and death, while his wife wept herself hollow—Joaquin Claudio thought only of himself.

He had been selfish to his core from the day he was born. Nothing had changed.

It cannot end like this.

Joaquin bit down on his lip until he tasted copper.

And in the fertile darkness of his mind, a poisonous thought began to bloom—fragrant as a rose, deadly as hemlock.

The Claudio Mansion

"Samon! Come to your senses!"

At last, Samon's eyes fluttered open.

"AAAAHHHHH!"

A piercing shriek tore through the morning air—reminiscent of another scream, years ago, when Birna had learned the truth of her own blood.

"A cripple?! You're telling me I'm a cripple?! You're lying!"

The empty flutter of his trouser leg felt alien. Wrong. The wooden splint where his right leg should have been, the whispered explanations about amputation—all of it seemed like an elaborate fabrication.

It would have been easier to believe if not for the dull, relentless ache pulsing through what remained of his body.

Why me? Why?

Why had this happened to him instead of Peleus?

"This is impossible! IMPOSSIBLE!"

"Samon, please—!"

Catherine's voice broke as tears streamed down her face.

"My darling, my son—you mustn't excite yourself. The doctor said you need absolute rest—"

CRASH!

Before she could finish, Samon seized the teacup beside his bed and hurled it against the wall.

"Samon!"

Raju moved to shield Catherine as Samon grabbed the doctor by the collar, dragging the terrified man close.

"Tell me the truth! Speak!"

"M-Master, what your lady mother said is correct. The leg cannot be restored—"

The doctor's voice quavered, but it was not the answer Samon wanted. His eyes bulged with animal fury, and the physician seemed to shrink into himself.

"We tried everything. Every possible treatment. There was simply no hope—"

"Shut your mouth before I tear out your tongue!"

Samon's hand shot toward the sword propped against his bedside. He meant to rise, to make good on his threat—

But the moment he tried to stand, his body betrayed him. Without a second leg to anchor his weight, he pitched forward.

THUD.

The impact of his body striking the furniture was nothing compared to the blow dealt to his pride.

This can't be happening.

His eyes went wide with horrified disbelief.

It was the simplest of movements—merely rising from a seated position. The kind of thing he had done thousands of times without thought.

And now it was beyond him.

The empty flutter of fabric where his right leg should have been drove the cold, immutable reality into his skull like a spike.

Shame and shock boiled through him, erupting like molten stone.

"Samon!"

"Master!"

He shook off the hands that reached to help him, hauling himself upright through sheer force of will. His fingers found the sword's hilt and pressed the blade against the doctor's stomach.

"Tell me you can fix it. Say you'll restore my leg—even if it costs your worthless life!"

The doctor trembled violently, certain he was about to die.

"Please—please spare me—"

"You're lying! Tell me it's a lie!"

That day, the howling of a wounded beast echoed through the Claudio mansion for hours without end.

But surviving the explosion seemed to have exhausted the last of Samon's fortune. His miseries were far from over.

Before he could even begin to accept the loss of his leg, a golden envelope arrived.

"Master..."

"What now?!"

"A letter from Her Highness the Fourth Princess."

Samon tore it open. As he read, his face contorted into something demonic.

Divorce papers.

The speed of it was breathtaking—as though someone had stationed a spy at his bedside, waiting for precisely this moment.

The Fourth Princess had endured much. She had tolerated being used as a pawn by fools and schemers. But the instant Samon's leg was severed, she glimpsed salvation.

A golden opportunity to sever the chains of this wretched marriage.

"Please. Do you truly expect me to remain shackled to a cripple for the rest of my days?"

Emperor Perdiccas II of Katzen hurled his teacup against the wall upon receiving word of Samon's accident.

He had anticipated some calamity befalling Valdina's arrogant rulers—not the destruction of the very instrument he had cultivated at such expense.

"I did not cross the strait for something so utterly worthless! I personally laid the groundwork for Valdina's rise!"

But neither the Emperor's rage nor Catherine's desperate prayers could restore what had been taken.

When Samon finished reading the divorce papers, he tore them to shreds.

"Damn it! DAMN IT ALL!"

He tried to rise—and slipped on a fragment of torn paper, his single leg failing to support him.

"I've become so pathetic I can't even stand properly!"

The fury threatened to split his chest open, but no one dared approach the raging madman.

After losing his leg, Samon became an entirely different creature.

His temper grew volcanic. The slightest frustration sent him into fits of rage. Whenever he encountered something he could no longer do on his own, he seized whatever object lay nearest and hurled it at the walls—or at the servants.

Family members were not exempt.

Even Catherine, who loved her son beyond all reason, narrowly avoided being struck by an ashtray.

He had become a tyrant ruling over his small, crumbling kingdom.

Every few days, another caretaker fled the mansion in tears, unable to endure his hysteria any longer.

Today was no different.

"Ugh—!"

Unable to master his fury, Samon thrashed across the floor. He kept slipping, kept falling—his treacherous body refusing to obey.

The man who had once commanded armies could not even command his own limbs.
