The Royal Prison

"I never thought you would be so naïve, Minister,"

Siseo chuckled, leaning casually against the iron bars.

"To still place your faith in Duke Claudio."

Etienne glared at him from the darkness of the cell.

"What nonsense are you spouting now?"

Siseo tilted his head.

"How do you think I knew about the Black Eyes?"

Etienne's breath caught.

"How did I know they were smuggling children?"

Siseo continued lightly.

"Routes. Buyers. Prices."

Silence.

"The Black Eyes operate in absolute secrecy,"

Siseo went on.

"In Valdina, only two people knew the full scope of their business."

He held up two fingers.

"You…"

"and the Regent."

Etienne's pupils shrank.

"At first,"

Siseo said, shrugging,

"I assumed it was a trap. Why would the Regent expose his own partner?"

He smiled faintly.

"Then I read the reports."

Siseo studied Etienne with open pity.

"He cut you loose, Lark."

"Offered you up like a lamb to save himself."

"The evidence didn't come from my spies."

He tapped the bars.

"It came from the Duke's office."

"Liar!"

Etienne screamed, hurling himself against the bars.

"Claudio is my ally! If I fall, he falls!"

"Does he?"

Siseo asked quietly.

"Or does he grow stronger by absorbing your assets? By inheriting your influence?"

He placed a small bottle of cheap vodka on the stone floor.

"Ask yourself one thing,"

Siseo said softly.

"Who benefits most from your silence?"

He turned and walked away.

"Enjoy the drink, Minister. It may be your last."

Clang.

The cell door echoed shut.

Etienne grabbed the bottle and hurled it against the wall.

Smash!

"Damn you!"

he roared.

But as the sharp scent of alcohol filled the cell, doubt crept in—slow, poisonous.

Only Claudio knew about the Black Eyes.

Only Claudio had motive.

And Claudio had already tried to destroy him once—

using Birna.

"He fed me to the wolves…"

Etienne whispered, his heart hammering.

"He betrayed me."

"Guard!"

he screamed.

"Guard!"

A jailer shuffled over, eyes already gleaming.

"Yes, My Lord?"

Etienne shoved a gold coin through the bars.

"Summon the Princess,"

he hissed.

"Tell Her Highness I must see her. Immediately."

Three Days Later

The prison stank of mold, sweat, and despair.

Then—

a clean, delicate scent cut through the filth.

Etienne looked up.

Silver hair gleamed in the torchlight.

A black dress whispered across the stone floor.

"Your Highness!"

he gasped, scrambling forward.

"You came!"

Medea stopped before his cell.

She examined him slowly—his sunken eyes, his filthy clothes, his trembling hands.

"Oh my,"

she murmured.

"What happened to you, Minister?"

She tilted her head.

"Where is the man who declared himself a king?"

"Your Highness, please!"

Etienne begged, clutching the bars.

"This is a frame-up! The Black Eyes betrayed me! I am loyal to Valdina!"

"Loyal?"

Medea raised a delicate eyebrow.

"The people disagree."

She folded her fan.

"They say you sold their children."

"They're calling for your execution."

Etienne's knees gave out.

"I didn't know…"

he whispered.

"I didn't know it had spread so far."

"They want to tear you apart,"

Medea continued calmly.

"Quite enthusiastically."

Etienne collapsed to the floor.

"Save me!"

he sobbed.

"Duke Claudio abandoned me! He betrayed me!"

Medea watched him carefully.

It worked.

"Save you?"

she asked coolly.

"With what, Minister? My bare mouth?"

Etienne flinched. His own words—returned like a blade.

"No! No!"

he stammered.

"I have money! A fortune! My slush fund!"

Her eyes flickered.

"A slush fund?"

"Yes!"

Etienne nodded frantically.

"More gold than you can imagine! All yours!"

With shaking hands, he pulled a folded parchment from his rags.

"This,"

he whispered reverently,

"is the key. My secret vault at the Golden Bank."

"My entire life's savings."

He held it out, eyes gleaming with desperate hope.

Take it.

Save me.

Medea accepted the parchment.

She unfolded it slowly.

"Your entire fortune,"

she mused.

"Everything!"

Etienne cried.

"Just get me out of here!"

Medea smiled.

The same soft, harmless smile she had worn at the banquet.

"Thank you, Minister,"

she said gently.

"I will put it to excellent use."

And Etienne—

for the first time—

felt hope.

The Final Move

The Minister's Surrender

Betrayed by his ally.
Abandoned by his duke.
He gave everything to the one who destroyed him.

And she smiled, knowing the web was complete.
