The Killer's
Gaze

"Next time, it will be your head."

"Wait! Your Highness!"

Maria lunged.

Her fingers curled like claws, reaching for silver hair—she would grab it, shake sense into that stupid, blank face—

Snip.

A soft, intimate sound.

Red strands drifted down like autumn leaves.

"Neril?"

Maria froze.

She looked at the floor.

Those weren't threads.

They were locks of her own hair.

"Ah—AHHH! My hair! You insane bit—"

Clang

The flat of a sword slammed into her throat.

The scream died mid-breath.

Neril

"Next time, it will be your head."

Maria couldn't breathe. Cold steel pressed into her windpipe. She stared into Neril's eyes and saw no fury there—no hatred, no hesitation.

Only the calm, empty gaze of a soldier choosing a burial site.

Neril leaned closer, her lips brushing Maria's ear.

"If you touch Her Highness again, I won't stop at your hair. I'll break every finger on your hands. Slowly."

"You… you…"

Maria stumbled back, biting her lip until it bled.

The loyal dog was gone.

In her place stood a killer.

Terror washed over her—pure, absolute. Maria turned and ran.

❧

"Neril, perhaps you should rest."

"It is nothing, Your Highness."

Neril sheathed her sword with her left hand. Her right arm was bound tight against her chest, swollen beneath layers of cloth—yet her movements were fluid, precise.

Medea's Thoughts

Medea studied her.

She is strong.

In her former life, Medea had watched the empire's finest warriors bleed and fall. She knew what strength looked like. Even crippled, Neril moved like a panther.

At full strength, she could rival a Seven-Star Knight of Kazen.

No wonder Peleus had chosen her.

"What did you say to Maria? She looked like spoiled milk."

"I merely advised caution."

"Is that so?"

Medea smiled faintly.

Behind them, the remaining maids watched with sharp, calculating eyes.

Maria was gone.

That meant opportunity.

"From today, you will divide Maria's duties among yourselves."

"Are you serious?!"

"Oh—thank you, Your Highness!"

They bowed deeply, greedy smiles hidden behind lowered heads. Serving the Princess meant lighter work, better food, access—status.

One maid ventured forward.

"Your Highness… something strange is happening. The depot sent us damp firewood. They said we must carry it ourselves."

"And the cleaners were barred from our wing. We're expected to scrub everything."

Medea nodded slowly.

"The Head Maid is retaliating."

The maids stiffened.

So it was true. The Princess had beaten Madame Quiggin.

Madness, they thought. All this—for one broken soldier?

Princess Medea

"This is my palace. And you are my people. If any of you were in Neril's place, I would have done the same."

They blinked.

"I cannot oppose the Head Maid openly. Because of me, you will suffer. I am sorry."

She drew out glittering jewels.

Ruby earrings

Gold bracelet

Sapphire necklace

"Y-Your Highness…?"

Their breath hitched.

"If we do not stand together, she will believe she can crush us."

Medea said gently, closing her fingers over theirs.

Tears welled—part greed, part astonishment.

"We'll serve you faithfully! We won't let her win!"

Later

When the room was empty—

"They're vipers. You gave them too much."

"Kindness?"

Medea looked at the empty chest and smiled—a sharp, distant curve of lips.

"Look again."

"They were gifts from the Duke and Duchess."

"Exactly."

Gaudy. Tasteless. Oversized stones set crudely.

Not royal jewelry—mockery.

Princess Medea

"They will sell them. And when they do, the capital will whisper."

She turned to the window.

'Why is the Princess selling her dowry?'

'Is she destitute?'

'Is the Duke starving her?'

"It's a small price to force my uncle's hand."

Sand in the flour
Insects in the linens

"Let her rot."

— The Princess's Palace —

"What is this garbage?"

"Sand in the flour. Throw it out."

"These sheets stink. Burn them."

Flush with cash, the maids bought their own food, wine, and laundry. Palace supplies went straight to the incinerator.

Inside a warm, fragrant room, Medea sipped tea.

Perfectly comfortable.
