The Return of the
Mad Dog

"Some beasts refuse to stay buried."

The delicate features of her pale face, with its youthful elegance, called to mind an exquisitely crafted porcelain doll.

Yet her emerald eyes—beautiful as they were—held a frigid distance, and her composed lips betrayed no emotion whatsoever.

That serene, unapproachable expression rendered her difficult for most to address directly.

"Truly an incomparable beauty. I ought to have proposed marriage before the proclamation ceremony. Had I acted then, there might have been some sliver of possibility."

Perhaps because of that very distance, the attendees found themselves unable to tear their gazes from Medea—even as they sensed the futility of their admiration.

The Imperial Princess's cold beauty was not something easily forgotten.

"Arthur, do you truly believe winning her hand would be simple? The King of Valdina—or rather, the Emperor now—surely you know how fiercely he guards his only sister? Every envoy who has dared approach with a proposal has been expelled before they could so much as complete their bow."

"Was it truly that severe? But surely the circumstances have changed? If Valdina intends to contend with Katzen, they shall require allied nations—"

"By all means, attempt it. If you possess the courage to watch Agema blades clear their scabbards the instant the Princess's name crosses your lips, then be my guest."

The prince of that minor kingdom—lacking the nerve to face the continent's most formidable warriors for the sake of a beautiful woman—turned away with eyes full of regret.

The Valdinians, meanwhile, watched the procession of envoys entering the banquet hall with undisguised pride.

"But who has Katzen dispatched as their representative?"

"I cannot say with certainty, but the Third Prince seems most likely."

"Indeed—aside from the Third Prince, there are scarcely any viable candidates. The other princes and members of the imperial family have all been systematically eliminated by the First Prince..."

Three years prior, word had swept across the continent like wildfire: the First Prince of Katzen had overcome his supposedly incurable affliction and returned to the imperial capital.

"Remarkable, is it not? Who could have predicted such a dramatic reversal in merely a few years? Cesare himself is truly extraordinary."

Cesare Dweisler-Katzen.

The man known as the Black Devil inspired terror wherever his name was spoken—yet he drew the fascination of all who heard it.

Rumors that the Katzen Emperor was actively working to contain his own son only intensified the curiosity surrounding him.

"We shall learn more details at today's banquet."

Anticipation threaded through the murmured conversations.

Everyone present secretly awaited the Katzen delegation's arrival, wondering how the elder empire would respond to the provocation of this "Brother Empire"—which had proclaimed itself their equal as if issuing a direct challenge.

The Katzen Delegation

"The banner of Katzen! They have arrived!"

As the banquet reached its appointed hour and anticipation swelled to its peak, the Katzen delegation made its entrance.

Those bearing the symbols of the Katzen Empire strode onto the crimson carpet.

"Is that not the crest of the Katzen Imperial Family? Has someone of royal blood come in person?"

"Look more closely. Do you see that medal embroidered with the red rose? It is bestowed only upon those who serve the Emperor directly."

The elderly man with snow-white hair who led the procession was of diminutive stature, his frame slight and unimposing.

Yet his expression remained utterly indifferent, as though the weight of countless stares meant nothing to him.

The Katzen courtiers accompanying him moved in perfect synchronization with his steps—a testament to his authority.

"That is the Grand Duke of Castulo—!"

The Valdinians' demeanor sharpened to razor edges as they recognized the man standing beside the chief steward.

"Wait—surely not..."

"Why has he returned?!"

The hall, which had been brimming with celebratory joy, erupted into chaos.

Every Valdinian present remembered Jason—the man who, three years ago, had led an army to support Duke Claudio's rebellion.

"The Grand Duke of Castulo has truly come? Why now? Why here?"

"Shh—lower your voice."

"What manner of shame does that man possess to show his face in this place?"

"What shame could exist in a man who attempted to plunder another nation by abetting treason?!"

Some did not even hesitate to employ profanity.

The foreign attendees, who had been so eager to witness the Katzen delegation's arrival, fell utterly silent as they absorbed the Valdinians' frigid reaction.

An unprecedented, glacial stillness descended upon the banquet hall.

Even the orchestra ceased playing. The musicians set down their instruments, refusing to perform the welcoming march.

Into that frozen silence, Peleus's voice rang out—cold as winter steel.

"It would appear the Grand Duke of Katzen has forgotten my final warning. I see he dares to set foot upon Valdinian soil once more."

Those glacial blue eyes fixed upon the smooth-featured young man standing beside the attendant.

The expressions of the Katzen delegation hardened beneath the weight of killing intent that made no attempt to conceal itself.

Even the mere observers could feel it.

This young Emperor—who had risen to power, brought the fierce nomadic tribes to their knees, and led his nation to victory—was no mere figurehead.

Someone's gaze drifted to the massive greatsword hanging at the Emperor's waist.

Logically, it could not happen—yet the atmosphere was so taut that for one breathless moment, they genuinely believed the Emperor might draw that blade and strike down the Grand Duke of Katzen where he stood.

"Emperor of Valdina, I convey the heartfelt congratulations of my lord, Perdiccas II, Supreme Sovereign of Katzen."

Despite the hostility pressing against his very skin, Jason did not flinch from the venomous stares of Valdina's vassals.

That reddish-brown hair. That gentle, disarming smile. Both remained unchanged.

I swore I would rise again. I swore I would return.

Jason savored this moment—the fulfillment of that vow made in the depths of despair.

The chill permeating the banquet hall only quickened his pulse.

A quiet satisfaction bloomed within him as he observed the faces of those who could not mask their shock and fury.

Can any of them begin to imagine the hardships I endured to stand here once more?

Jason concealed the anger rising within him as his gaze fell upon the beautiful siblings of the Valdinian royal family.

Three years ago, he had been caught red-handed supporting Claudio's rebellion—ensnared by Medea's cunning machinations.

In consequence, he had lost his fertile territories, his personal guard, and even the favor of the former Emperor.

But he remained ignorant of one crucial truth.

The entire process of his downfall—every strand of it—had been orchestrated by Medea's deft hands.

When he had been banished to the harsh northern territories and placed under the watchful eyes of the Emperor's loyal Northern Lords, despair had seemed absolute.

There appeared to be no path forward.

But it seemed the heavens had not yet abandoned him.

A Secret Bargain

"Your Highness... do you not wish to reclaim your rightful position?"

A clandestine force had extended its hand from the shadows.

"Demons! A demon has appeared!"

"Ghouls! Save us! Aaaaah!"

Strange and terrible beings—entities capable of summoning monster waves at will.

With their assistance, Jason had suppressed the frequent demonic incursions. He had also quietly eliminated those assigned to monitor his every movement.

"Were it not for His Highness the Grand Duke, we would have been annihilated!"

"Savior of the North! What would have become of us without His Highness!"

The people of the northern territories sang Jason's praises.

He filled the void left by the deceased Northern Prince, emerging as both lord and hero of those frozen lands.

Jason, you truly possess the resilience of a cockroach.

Medea's emerald gaze settled upon him for a fleeting moment.

But that was all.

Three years ago, the mad dog of the empire she had resurrected had served its purpose flawlessly.

Had the First Prince—supposedly doomed to die within a finite span—not miraculously recovered, Jason's ascent as the North's champion would have proceeded unimpeded. Eventually, he would have reclaimed his former glory.

But now that Cesare had returned, he would never permit Jason's revival.

For three years, Jason had barely managed to consolidate control over the desolate northern wastes.

He could neither recover his former fertile domains nor return to the imperial capital.

This was an outcome even his shadowy benefactors had failed to anticipate.

And so Jason's forces remained far smaller than originally planned.

It was a wretched result—one that satisfied neither his dark allies nor Jason himself.

Is this truly the end? O God, will You never grant this nation to Jason?

Yet at that moment—as though the heavens had heard his anguished cry—a second chance descended upon him like divine intervention.

"Jason. You must return immediately and render me your aid."

Rather than eliminating the nephew who had survived in that abandoned wasteland, the Emperor had inexplicably summoned him back to the imperial palace.

It defied all reason.

But when Jason arrived at the palace, he finally understood why.
