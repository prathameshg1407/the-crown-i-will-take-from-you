Chapter 267

The Beast
Awakens

"What if I had died like this?"

Cesare understood immediately.

He grimaced, recognizing the sensation—this was not the first time he had emerged from such a leaden stupor, as though dragged up from the depths of dreamless sleep.

How long this time?

He attempted to rise and promptly collapsed, his body striking the floor. His senses remained tangled, refusing to obey.

Damn it.

He flexed his hands, testing the residual numbness left by the divine punishment. His mind clawed through fog, searching for the last memory.

Confronting Leniard's rebellion...

The old man's face draining of color. A crimson light engulfing him—

Another episode. The divine punishment struck again.

There was no predicting when this malevolent power would activate—whether one called it heaven's judgment or a warlock's curse, the result was the same.

Cesare surveyed the room, teeth clenched against the lingering aftereffects that still pulsed through his limbs.

Something glinted on the pillow beside where he had lain.

A single emerald earring. Small. Familiar.

He had given it to Medea once, long ago.

How heavy must the burden have grown while I lay senseless?

This collapse had come immediately after the Emperor's death. The pressures bearing down upon her—from within the palace and without—would have been crushing.

Cesare seized the bedpost and hauled himself upright.

Guilt constricted his chest—the bitter knowledge that once again, he had abandoned Medea to face crisis alone.

"Damn it..."

And beneath the guilt, something else stirred. A nameless unease. A presentiment he could not define.

I will not understand until I see her myself.

He strode from the bedchamber.

The reception hall lay shrouded in gloom. Though the sun rode high above, clouds had swallowed it whole, casting the space in shadow—as though a storm had only just passed through.

Cesare sensed movement.

The divine punishment had not dulled his predator's instincts. He moved toward the presence, unconsciously silencing his footsteps, erasing his aura—habits forged through a lifetime of violence.

Behind a door in the corridor's blind spot, three or four figures shifted and murmured.

"So...! ...that's how it is, then?"

Muffled voices—inaudible to anyone not straining to hear.

"I never imagined Her Highness would leave us here to guard His Highness instead of bringing us to the cathedral."

As Cesare drew closer, the words sharpened into clarity.

"What—you thought I'd be content sitting here while she's at the cathedral?"

The impatient tap of a boot against stone. Gallo's voice, unmistakably.

"What if this is deliberate? Marquis Leniard—that old fox has been scheming ever since he was ordered to welcome Rachel!"

During today's coronation, Medea had assigned the Agema as her escort rather than stationing Cesare's most capable mercenaries at her side. Her intention had been to shield him from enemies who might strike while he lay vulnerable.

To Gallo, however, it felt like exile.

"Why won't Terence give me so much as a hint? Half the court has their eyes on Her Highness!"

"That paladin—Theo—he told Her Highness he would take responsibility for her and the child for the rest of his life. Asked her to leave Katzen with him. The audacity!"

Alpha's voice was strained.

"That bastard has always looked at Her Highness strangely. If the boss finds out, Theo will be the first to meet his maker."

Gallo raked his fingers through his straw-colored hair, disheveling it further.

"And Kensington—he's prepared an entire escape route! The moment things turn sour, he plans to spirit Her Highness away!"

Betrayal sharpened his voice.

"He's Katzenian, same as us! How could he stab us in the back like this?"

"With the Emperor of Valdina now present, what good would it do to obstruct Kensington's escape route?"

A colder voice cut through. Zeta.

"Her Highness's brother announced he would raise the child as Valdina's grandchild. He has already selected a new match for her—one of his own ministers. And as if on cue, Valdina's Prime Minister produced divorce papers."

"Absurd! Cesare is alive and well! How can they speak of 'next husbands' already? And you simply stood there listening?"

"While Katzen made a spectacle of bringing in a new woman and discarding Her Highness, what standing did I have to object?"

Zeta's tone carried the weight of resignation.

"Even if Her Highness chose to abandon everything and leave... I could not blame her. We have all witnessed how much she has endured."

A heavy silence followed.

Everyone present understood the impossible weight Medea now carried.

"Can we not simply hold on until the boss wakes? This is maddening. We cannot afford open war with the Agema."

"For now..."

Alpha pressed his palm to his throbbing temple.

"We follow Her Highness's orders. After the coronation, her true intentions will become clear."

"We can discuss this when Cesare awakens. Let us not act rashly—"

"Tell me."

Every head snapped toward the cold voice.

Cesare stood in the doorway.

"B-Boss...!"

Their eyes went wide. Gooseflesh rippled down their spines.

"Is there anything else I should know?"

Because fire blazed in those golden eyes—bright, furious, and utterly without mercy.

✦ ✦ ✦

Clatter. Clatter. Clatter.

The thunder of hooves erupted through the city streets at an hour when no rider should have been abroad.

"Move!"

"Out of the way—!"

Citizens scattered before the onslaught of momentum.

"Who is that madman?"

A black horse tore through the billowing dust and vanished around a corner.

"Heading for the cathedral?"

"The coronation must already be underway! If he'd wanted to attend, he should have left earlier. The gates will be sealed by now—fool won't even get inside."

Tongues clicked in disapproval.

One man in the crowd tilted his head, puzzled.

"What's wrong? Did you get hit?"

"No, it's just... wasn't that His Highness the First Prince on that black horse?"

"Ridiculous. Why would the First Prince be riding through the streets like a—"

"Watch out!"

THUNDER. THUNDER. THUNDER.

Before the crowd could recover, another wave of horses shook the earth beneath their feet.

"What in blazes is happening today?!"

Dust billowed into faces already coated with grime.

"Boss—wait! Listen to me—!"

Gallo spurred his mount desperately, struggling to keep pace.

But Cesare and his horse moved as one—a black whirlwind that no ordinary rider could hope to match.

"Captain!"

Alpha seized Gallo's shoulder as he nearly toppled from the saddle.

"The lord has awakened—isn't that enough? The coronation is already underway! We can stop that false oath before it's spoken!"

"You didn't see his eyes!"

Gallo's shout was raw with dread.

When Cesare entered this state, no force on earth could restrain him. No one could predict what destruction would follow.

Gallo remembered vividly—the Continental War. The moment when Cesare's golden eyes, which so rarely wavered, had bled into crimson madness.

"Is Dea at the coronation now?"

"His eyes were half-opened... just like that time."

That nightmarish hour when the sky above the front lines had been turned inside out.

Cesare's face remained as fierce as the north wind throughout the ride, his grip on the reins white-knuckled.

Blood seemed to roar through every vein in his body.

Is this the aftermath of divine punishment?

Or was it fury—pure and incandescent—at those who had dared covet his wife the moment he closed his eyes?

"...Vultures. Every last one of them."

The words emerged cold enough to frost the air.

He had always known.

From the very beginning, he had been wary of them all.

The paladin called Theo—Cesare had long discerned what lay beneath the man's mask of duty. The hatred and reverence tangled together, both aimed at Medea.

Jason—that damned specter—and the illusions of the past Medea could not entirely release.

Sissair, whose swallowed emotions never quite crossed the line between vassal and something more.

Cesare had perceived it all. He simply chose not to speak of it.

Who would welcome such knowledge?

He had refused to make Medea conscious of their existence—refused to burden her with awareness of men who circled her like wolves at the edge of firelight.

But in his brief absence, they had rushed to fill the void.

Even Kensington.

They had attempted to steal Medea—and his child—from him.

The moment that spark of quiet rage ignited, it erupted into an inferno beyond all control.

What if I had died like this?

All he could envision was their hands reaching for his wife. Claiming what was his.

Within his chest, fury and fear wound together—twin serpents coiling around his heart, squeezing until breath itself became agony.
