Chapter 274

The Serpent
Unmasked

"The dead do not stay silent when the living speak their sins aloud."

Satisfied that everything was prepared for her devotions, Rachel turned to face the attendant priest.

"Allow no one to enter until I return."

"Yes, Saintess."

With a curt nod of dismissal, Rachel approached the rear wall of the chapel.

Her fingers found an ancient brick, worn smooth by countless touches before hers.

Tap.

A grinding whisper of stone against stone filled the darkness.

Sssssshhhhh...

The hidden wall slowly receded, revealing a passage swallowed by shadow.

Rachel lifted her lamp and stepped into the corridor, the flame's glow catching the gleam of anticipation in her violet eyes.

Let them believe I remain in the chapel, deep in prayer.

Step by step, she drew closer to her prey.

At last, Rachel emerged from the passage, sealed the stone wall behind her, and entered the Chamber of Judgment.

Medea's body lay upon the altar like an offering.

Her eyes were closed, her features serene and still. Pale and utterly lifeless, she resembled nothing so much as a martyr from the sacred scriptures—beautiful even in death.

The Death of Medea de Valdina.

"I did it."

The words emerged as barely more than a breath.

"I won."

The taste of victory spread across her tongue, sweeter than she had ever imagined.

A smile unfurled across Rachel's lips—slow, languorous, drunk on triumph.

"All those tedious schemes to strip away your fate piece by piece—utterly pointless. In the end, I simply took matters into my own hands, and it was over in an instant."

She had accomplished what even the great warlocks—those who had grown their power in darkness for half a century—had never dared attempt.

She had destroyed the Goddess's chosen vessel.

"What good did your divine favor do you in the end? Child of Prophecy. Vessel of Venus."

Rachel's laughter rang hollow against the stone walls.

"Such grandiose titles, and look at you now—too blind to foresee your own death. How utterly pathetic."

She leaned down, her lips brushing against Medea's ear.

"While you wallowed in the blessings they showered upon you, I clawed my way forward with nothing but my own hands and my own blood."

Her voice dropped to a venomous whisper.

"I am the one who was truly chosen."

The blade hungers

Rachel drew a dagger from the folds of her vestments.

The blade gleamed with eager malice, so keen it seemed it might draw blood from the mere whisper of contact.

If a princess is discovered with a dagger buried in her heart, here in this sacred place beneath the watchful gaze of the Statue of Justice...

"Would that not appear to be divine punishment?"

The corners of Rachel's mouth stretched into something inhuman.

"The Goddess has struck down an arrogant soul who dared defy her sacred will."

And Rachel herself would be remembered as the true representative of the divine—the one who had weathered all opposition and criticism, the sole interpreter of the Goddess's intent.

She adjusted her grip on the dagger. Her left hand.

Her right hand—rotting beneath a thin veneer of black magic—was nothing more than dead flesh now. Useless.

Rachel's gaze flickered toward the statue of the Goddess towering above them.

"You did this to me."

Hatred curdled in her voice.

"This is not merely revenge against Medea. This is revenge against you."

"Watch closely now, Goddess. Witness with your own divine eyes as your beloved child is defiled unto death."

"Hm... hm... Ahahaha..."

The laughter that spilled from her lips hovered somewhere between ecstasy and weeping.

Rachel raised the dagger high.

The blade's edge aligned with Medea's heart, trembling with anticipation.

"I'll take everything that was meant to be yours. Don't be too aggrieved—when the Primordial Darkness awakens, everyone will have forgotten you ever existed."

The dagger plunged downward—

The Dead Rise

And Medea's eyes snapped open.

"—!"

In the same instant, a cold hand shot up and seized Rachel's wrist in an iron grip.

"Wh... why..."

Rachel's eyes went wide with disbelief.

A corpse returning to life? I confirmed she wasn't breathing!

"That poison was brewed from the essence of Primordial Darkness itself! There is no way you could have survived—!"

How was this possible? Had the Goddess intervened directly? Did an antidote exist?

"How... how did you..."

Rachel's voice trailed off as understanding crashed over her like ice water.

"You... you deceived me. Again."

Images flashed through her mind in rapid succession: Medea drinking deeply from the silver cup before the entire congregation. Medea's body, cold and still, with no breath stirring her lips.

She was never dead. Not from the very beginning.

Rachel's teeth ground together. Fury blazed through her skull like wildfire.

"The holy water... you never actually drank it. You even feigned death itself."

"I felt it only appropriate to match the sincerity of the Saintess's devotion."

Medea's voice emerged calm, measured—utterly unmoved by Rachel's rage.

Rachel's face contorted into something demonic.

The realization that she had been manipulated, outmaneuvered, made a fool of—it crushed her pride beneath its weight. Hatred boiled up from the depths of her soul.

"Medea..."

Rachel's eyes darted frantically around the chamber.

A confined space. No escape routes. And Medea stands before me unarmed.

"It changes nothing."

A murderous light kindled in her violet eyes.

"If you aren't dead yet, I'll simply kill you again!"

Rachel lunged, dagger slashing downward.

"DIE!"

But Medea rolled from the altar with fluid grace, evading the strike by a hair's breadth.

Rachel pursued with bloodshot eyes, blade hunting for flesh.

Kill her. I must destroy her. Only by eliminating this woman can I survive. I have to kill her!

Rachel knew with crystalline certainty: if she failed to end Medea here, her entire existence would crumble to ruin.

Yet Medea's movements were impossibly swift—darting and weaving beyond Rachel's desperate reach.

"Seize her! Now!"

Rachel screamed at the attendant priest.

"What are you waiting for? Hold her down! Don't let her escape!"

But contrary to her frantic commands, the acolyte merely stood motionless, observing the scene with detached calm.

"Are you deaf?! Catch her! KILL HER!"

Rachel lunged again, fingers clawing for Medea. For one breathless moment, she seemed within reach—

But Medea pivoted at the last instant, and Rachel's momentum carried her crashing into the wall.

The Veil Falls

The impact struck the great ceremonial flame mounted on the wall.

The massive tapestry unfurled downward like silk sliding from a rack, pooling on the floor.

And behind it stood revealed: a wall of glass.

Lanterns flared to life, illuminating the faces of those who stood beyond.

Pope Benedict. The High Priests. The Temple Knights.

All of them watching. All of them listening.

"This... this is..."

"Saintess Rachel."

The Pope's voice cut through the chamber like a blade.

"What, precisely, do you think you are doing?"

Rachel stood frozen, the blood draining from her face until she was pale as parchment.

Her gaze swept across the assembled priests in mounting horror—and then caught her own reflection in the glass wall.

Disheveled hair. Eyes blazing with naked hatred. Lips twisted into a rictus of madness.

A dagger raised, poised to plunge into Medea's heart.

She looked savage. Monstrous. Utterly unrecognizable as the serene, benevolent Saintess the world had come to know.

"Saintess..."

Even High Priest Paul—her most devoted defender—stared at her as though seeing a stranger.

Clang.

The dagger slipped from Rachel's nerveless fingers and clattered against the stone floor.

"N-no... this isn't..."

She grasped for words, flailing desperately.

"I can explain everything. Why I... No. I'm innocent. You must believe me—"

"Do you intend to deny what these old eyes have witnessed? What these old ears have heard?"

The Pope's voice rang with terrible authority.

"Have you already forgotten that you confessed—in your own words—your intention to murder the Princess and disguise it as the will of the Goddess?!"

The words she had spoken so arrogantly—mocking Medea's corpse, boasting of her own superiority—now coiled around her throat like a noose.

"No! Your Holiness, you misunderstand! I can explain—please, you must trust me—"

"Rachel."

The Pope's voice dropped to something cold and final.

"You spoke of the Primordial Darkness being resurrected."

" I'll take everything that was meant to be yours.
When the Primordial Darkness awakens,
everyone will have forgotten you. "

The Pope's eyes bore into Rachel without mercy.

In this moment, he saw not the Saintess the world had revered—but something far more sinister. An ancient enemy. A servant of the darkness that had plagued the faith since time immemorial.

"Reveal your true nature."

His voice echoed through the chamber like the tolling of a funeral bell.

"What are you truly? And what did you hope to achieve by wearing this mask of sanctity?"

Judgment

At last, the serpent stood exposed.

And there was nowhere left to hide.
