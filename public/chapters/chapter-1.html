Ambient: Rain
Chapter One

The Return
Begins

"Never again will I be foolish."

Someone tried to bring her to her senses.

"Your Highness, are you alright?"

Medea blinked. The girl's expression was blank, as if someone had stripped her of all emotion. The maid winced at the sight of the lifeless, doll‑like figure.

"Your Highness? Can you hear me?"

A silhouette was reflected in the mirror on the table: brightly shining green eyes; a smoothly drawn nose bridge flowing harmoniously into her forehead; and miniature red lips. Not a child, but not a woman either—she was in the early bloom of her teenage years.

Medea automatically lowered her head and looked at her hands.

"Here, from the demonic beast, there was a scar…"

The countless wounds she had sustained during military campaigns had disappeared without a trace. Her fair, smooth skin shone without scars.

"Is hell that obvious? I never thought I'd be young again."

The word "past" flashed across her mind, and the girl tensed.

"What about Peleus?"

"What?"

"Is he alive?"

Peleus, Medea's brother and King of Valdina, had died because of her. She stole the Sage's Stone, and the barrier protecting the country's borders fell. Demonic beasts poured into Valdina in vast herds. Peleus died trying to stem the tide of raids. Meanwhile, Medea, in the very center of the empire, fought for the throne for her husband. Only then had she understood the role of the Sage's Stone in the life of the kingdom, and why the demonic beasts infesting the continent had so far been unable to cross Valdina's borders.

"What are you saying? Of course he's alive."

As soon as Medea heard the maid's words, she jumped up.

"Where is he?"

" Deya, don't even think about coming back. Valdina is no longer safe.

I don't blame you. Live. It's all in the past now. "

The only family member who had always been on her side.

"Where are you going? He's on the battlefield. How will you see him?"

The maid caught Medea as she stood abruptly and swayed.

"On the battlefield?"

"You don't remember? You said you hit your head when you fell."

"Is he still at war? Is the conquest not over yet? He's alive."

The insistent words sounded less like a question and more like a declaration. The maid shook her head.

"This won't do. I'd better call the royal doctor," the maid sighed.

"No. I have to see him with my own eyes."

Medea ran out of the room.

"Your Highness, where are you going!"

The maid rushed after her, but met only an empty corridor.

"We're in trouble!"

The basement of the royal palace of Valdina.

In a secret place where only a few were allowed, a bright light burned. Reaching the foot of the stairs, Medea came up against a stone wall. The door swung open, and a cold blast of air hit her face. The altar at the center was illuminated—not by torches, but by the glow of the treasure lying upon it.

"On… spot."

National Treasure

The Sage's Stone

The chill of the precious stone ran through her trembling fingertips.

Transparent tears streamed down Medea's face.

"I need the Eye of the Sage. My uncle says that if I bring it, he will keep his promise and hand over the throne to me."

Jason had asked Peleus, King of Valdina, for the Eye of the Sage, but had been unceremoniously refused. In the end, he still got what he wanted—because Medea, the king's sister, fell in love with him.

"Help me, Medea. I am the rightful heir to the imperial throne of Kazen."

"If I become emperor, you will stand by my side, and our child will rule the empire. Medea, you don't want our child to grow up without you, do you?"

"I love you, Medea."

With blind tenderness.

"Sister Medea, the Duke suffers so much. If he had the Sage's Stone, he would be perfect."

"The Sage's Stone is a national treasure in name only. You can return it to me. I'll just borrow it for a while. Perhaps Peleus will understand later, when relations between our countries improve."

"We're all thinking about you."

Medea had succumbed to the sweet talk. Looking back, it seemed the tragedy had begun there. In her previous life, the stolen Sage's Stone was shattered into pieces and sealed into the border wall of Kazen.

"The Emperor broke it into pieces so that I wouldn't ask for it back."

Medea gritted her teeth.

"I'm back."

How could she not believe it, when the untouched national treasure lay before her eyes? The gods had granted her last wish.

A‑ha‑ha‑ha… A‑ha‑ha!

The reality of what was happening washed over her.

"Ancestors of Valdina, gods and spirits who protect this land…"

Medea leaned over and kissed the foot of the altar. The soft glow of the Sage's Stone enveloped her.

"Never again will I be foolish."

She would not miss this chance.

Medea raised her head. Her clear eyes flashed a bright blue against her pale face.

"Claudio."

All the honors paid to her uncle would be dragged through the mud.

"Jason."

The magnificent crown he desired would be torn from his grasp.

"I'm back."

The prophecy of rebirth shone with a dark light.

Medea emerged from the dark underground passage. Her tears had long since dried.

"Earlier, that maid said that I fell off a horse."

Even in distant memories, some moments of resentment remained distinct, making her fate all the more painful. "Cursed princess," "inferior," "child who brings misfortune"—all these labels had been given to Medea.

During the war, the late King of Valdina met a traveling dancer, fell in love with her, and made her his queen. Much was said about her origins, but no one dared criticize her. The king loved his wife so much that he even accompanied her to the battlefield. His son, Peleus, also followed his parents into battle, but his daughter, Medea, remained in the palace due to her youth and the dangers that awaited her.

I miss you so much. Please come visit me.

On her seventh birthday, Medea couldn't resist writing a letter. For the sake of their beloved daughter, the king and his wife replied without hesitation that they would come. However, on their return journey, they were ambushed by enemies who had suffered several defeats in the war. In the end, only the couple's cold coffins reached the lands of Valdina. The kingdom fell into chaos. When news spread that the king had died on the way back to his daughter, people blamed Medea for the tragedy.

"Because of you! Don't call him! You killed my son!"

At the funeral, the king's mother fainted, clutching not the coffin of her eldest son, but that of her young granddaughter.

As soon as Peleus inherited the throne, he returned to the front. The boy, not yet of age, was tasked with rebuilding the crumbling army, which had lost its leader.

In this chaos, little Medea was left alone. The king's mother hated her granddaughter, her brother was constantly away on the battlefield, and there were no other relatives on her mother's side who could protect her. Only her uncle, the regent, and his family extended a hand to her in her lonely life. They were kind and gentle to the princess in name only. Medea became their puppet, doing whatever they told her. She never realized that her uncle's growing power was aimed against her brother.

"How stupid I was."

A sweet lie and a cunning trick. Medea had seen neither truth nor sincerity.

" Mom, Dad, you left me because of me, right? "

The image of a lonely, crying girl, abandoned and blamed by everyone, overlapped with the deserted corridors of the royal palace.

But now everything was different. Medea—the strategist who had once made Jason emperor through arduous campaigns and a ruthless struggle for the throne—could now clearly see the whole picture of her uncle's plan.

"When I was about seventeen, I went to a tea party and fell off my horse."

Tea and a fall. Two events on the same day, and neither of them a coincidence. If Neril's maid hadn't rushed to save her, she would have broken her neck and died.

"But after that, Neril left the palace."

When the princess woke up, the maid was already gone.

"She left. She said that serving the royal family meant you could die at any moment."

"Neril said she'd never come back. She said that wherever she went, it couldn't be worse than here."

After that, believing she had been abandoned, Medea had simply forgotten about Neril. But common sense now whispered that things had been far more complicated.

"She wasn't the type to leave without a word."

Now that she had been given a second chance, the first thing Medea needed to do was create a safe haven. She couldn't fight alone in a palace surrounded by enemies on all sides.

"If only Neril were here with me."

If she had had just one person she could trust—someone to watch her back—her past life would not have been so hard.

"Did you hear? Jenna is dealing with one of the maids from the princess's palace."

A group of maids walked down the corridor. Medea quickly stepped out of sight.

"And do you know who it is? That one. The one who abandoned knighthood and entered the princess's palace of her own will."

"Oh my God, could it really be her? Did she do something wrong? She was the only one who worked properly there."

"She always attracted the head maid's attention. But this time they plan to send her away, using the princess's fall from her horse as an excuse. Jenna plans to punish her."

"What? That's absurd. She saved the princess's life. We all saw it."

"That's what the head maid said: if you put an earring on your nose, it becomes a nose ring. Who in this palace could go against her, hic… Your Highness?"

The maids froze when the princess suddenly blocked their way.

"Where are they?"

Medea's cold voice rang out through the corridor.
