The Saint
Arrives

"Some saviors bring salvation. Others bring ruin disguised as light."

Medea turned sharply.

"When did he arrive?"

"Just moments ago. I witnessed the standard being raised at the gate myself. However..."

Neril hesitated.

"The palace physicians and priests have been dispatched to wait outside the gates."

Medea went still.

It was highly unusual for both court physicians and priests to be summoned simultaneously.

What happened on the plains?

An ominous premonition coiled through her chest.

"Peleus, you needn't go yourself. Dispatching the Agema would be more than sufficient."
"We must destroy the source of the wave entirely. It won't be dangerous, Dea. Don't worry—I'll finish quickly and return to you."

His reassuring words, the gentle pressure of his hand upon her shoulder—none of it had eased the dread that coiled in her stomach.

Medea still remembered with terrible clarity the sight of her brother being torn apart by a monster's claws.

The howling cries of beasts trampling the grass. The screams. The blood.

All of it remained vivid—etched into her memory like scars that would never fade.

The words "Please don't go" had risen to her lips, but she had swallowed them. She could not bring herself to hold him back.

"Dea, I'll be back."

Medea's gaze fell upon the small wooden figurine standing on her table.

Everything has changed in this life. And yet... how could I have grown complacent about matters of such magnitude?

Her heartbeat quickened. The terror of that day surged back as though her body remembered what her mind had tried to bury.

The nightmare of her previous life was not dead. It still breathed within her—lurking, waiting for any opportunity to resurface and spread its black fog through her thoughts.

Medea rose abruptly.

"Kensington, we shall continue this discussion later."

"Of course, Your Highness."

Kensington noted the trace of anxiety that flickered across the Princess's otherwise composed features as she departed.

✦ ✦ ✦

The official residence of the Second District stood within the royal palace grounds, a building typically frequented only by court officials.

Today, its spacious courtyard was anything but quiet.

The King's guard had returned from the plains bearing wounds too severe to permit them passage to the main palace. Emergency treatment had been ordered on the spot.

Physicians who had been waiting in formation rushed forward the moment the Agema dismounted.

"Ahhh..."

"Hold still. I need to apply the bandages."

Soft groans of pain rose from every direction.

Medea's eyes swept across the courtyard.

The silver hair she sought was nowhere to be found among the armor stained crimson with blood.

Her pace quickened.

Thump. Thump. Thump.

Her heart pounded so loudly she could hear it in her ears—a drumbeat of impending misfortune.

"Captain!"

Neril spotted D'Angel first.

"Move the wounded to the infirmary first... Neril? Your Highness?"

D'Angel's brows rose in surprise as he noticed Medea following close behind.

He, too, was a sight—covered in dust and dried blood, his appearance far removed from his usual pristine bearing.

Medea's sharp eyes caught the edge of a bandage peeking from beneath his armor.

"Sir D'Angel, you're wounded."

"This? It's nothing."

He turned slightly, attempting to conceal the injury, but it was already visible.

"What happened out there? The Captain himself is injured, and the Agema look like they've been through a massacre."

Neril's voice was tight with concern.

D'Angel exhaled heavily, waving a hand as if to dismiss the severity.

"Don't be alarmed. Though I confess—we came dangerously close to disaster. A second wave erupted without warning..."

He began recounting the events in terse, clipped sentences.

Peleus and the Agema had successfully suppressed the initial ghoul wave that erupted from the plains.

After confirming the destruction of the wave's source, they had begun the journey home.

That was when the second wave struck.

A Lich—and with it, an entire army of ghouls.

The Iron-Blooded Knights did not panic. They counterattacked with characteristic discipline. But the enemy's numbers were overwhelming.

"No matter how many we cut down, they kept surging toward His Majesty. And leading them was a Demilich—you cannot imagine how formidable it was."

A Demilich. A demigod-level entity that had transcended its mortal form, leaving only its skull to act as vessel for its terrible power.

D'Angel's wound had been inflicted while shielding Peleus from the creature's attack.

"And Peleus? Where is he?"

Medea's voice carried an edge of urgency she could not quite suppress.

D'Angel seemed to realize, belatedly, that his vivid account had only deepened the Princess's alarm.

"His Majesty was bitten by that accursed sorcerer, but he is safe now. The purification was successful."

A familiar voice cut through the air behind them.

"D'Angel. Kindly refrain from embellishing the tale."

Peleus emerged, in the process of removing his battered armor.

His equipment was as ravaged as the rest—dented, scored, caked with grime. Yet despite his disheveled state, he carried himself with the quiet dignity of a holy warrior returned from the abyss.

"Bitten? Severely enough to require purification?"

Medea seized her brother's arm before he could protest, her eyes immediately finding the gray bite mark marring his left hand.

The traces of corruption were unmistakable—residue of the foul energy that emanated from the undead. It had likely been nearly black before the purification.

"It's nothing, Dea. I'm perfectly fine. I simply let my guard down for a moment—"

Peleus's words tumbled out in a rush as he registered his sister's pallor.

"I'm fine. Truly. It was not as though I was on death's door. We were merely surrounded by a horde of ghouls and a Demilich. A minor lapse in attention."

D'Angel snorted.

"With respect, Your Majesty—you know as well as I do what truly occurred. If not for her, you would have been in genuine peril."

Medea's breath caught.

"Her?"

"When I fell, there was someone who struck down the ghouls and purified the poison."

Peleus's explanation was characteristically brief.

D'Angel, however, could not resist elaborating.

"A woman from a village near where the ghoul wave erupted. Her holy powers awakened at precisely the right moment."

He gestured expansively, his excitement palpable.

"I wish Your Highness could have witnessed it. Only then did I truly understand why the Holy Kingdom has spent centuries awaiting the Saint. The power was... extraordinary."

A Miracle on the Plains

The sight of a demigod-level Demilich reduced to a handful of ash had shaken even the stone-faced D'Angel.

"Pure white light blanketed the plains. The ghouls—hundreds of them—were incinerated in an instant. Turned to dust before our very eyes."

How many souls across the continent had ever witnessed a true Saint in the flesh?

D'Angel's excited voice flowed past Medea's ears, barely registering.

Her attention had fixed upon something else entirely.

A figure approaching from behind her brother's back.

Golden hair catching the light.

One step.

Then another.

"Ah, there you are."

The hem of a pristine white skirt swayed gently with each step.

The woman drew closer. Her golden hair, radiant beneath the sunlight, was almost painful to look upon.

Then came soft violet eyes.

And finally—cherry-red lips curved in a gentle, benevolent smile.

"Your Highness, this is the woman I spoke of. The one who saved His Majesty and the entire Agema from the ghoul horde."

But before D'Angel could complete his introduction, Medea's lips were already moving.

Silent. Barely a whisper.

Rachel.

"This is Lady Rachel."

Why are you here?

The words lodged deep in her chest, spreading like poison through her lungs.
