Valdina Prison

The air was thick with rot and iron.

Maria lay curled on the stone floor, her ruined hands wrapped in filthy bandages. The tendons were gone. What remained were claws that burned with every pulse of her heart.

Step.

Step.

The sound echoed down the corridor.

Maria flinched, dragging herself backward as a figure appeared beyond the bars.

"It has been a while, Maria."

Her breath caught.

"Y–Your Highness!"

She collapsed forward, pressing her forehead to the cold iron.

"Please… save me. I'll do anything. I'll be loyal. I swear it."

"I cannot trust you anymore."

Medea said calmly.

"I was wrong! I was stupid! I was greedy! I'll never betray you again!"

Medea regarded her the way one might observe a wounded animal.

" Fear makes people sincere.
But only while the fear lasts. "

She reached through the bars and let a small vial drop to the floor.

Clink.

Maria stared at it.

"Drink it."

"What… what is it?"

"Grass pea extract. A slow poison. Without the antidote, your muscles will harden. Eventually, your lungs will stop."

Maria shook violently.

"I need certainty. Drink it, and I will heal your hands. I will give you the antidote every week."

Her eyes were serene.

"As long as you belong to me."

Maria understood then.

This was not mercy.

This was ownership.

With shaking fingers, she uncorked the vial and swallowed.

Medea watched without emotion.

"Good. Now I can trust you."

The Princess's Palace

When the summons came, the maids rushed forward with jewels and silks.

"No."

She chose a pale blue dress. No embroidery. No gems.

Only a simple string of pearls.

"But Your Highness—"

"Simplicity is a weapon."

Pinatelli waited outside.

"You look… restrained."

She observed.

"That is the point."

As they walked, Pinatelli lowered her voice.

"The Queen Mother is not cruel by nature. She is lonely. Grief made her sharp."

"I know. I do not confuse bitterness with malice."

Laughter drifted from ahead.

Birna.

Pinatelli frowned.

"They are lingering."

"They always do."

The Queen Mother's Chamber

Medea entered first.

She moved with exact precision—neither rushed nor timid. She stopped at the proper distance and curtsied.

"I greet Your Majesty."

The Queen Mother watched closely.

There was no hesitation. No stiffness. No resentment.

Medea was flawless.

"Sit."

She did. Straight-backed, hands folded, eyes lowered just enough.

The Queen Mother felt an unfamiliar irritation.

Why does she look… right?

"You've improved."

She said grudgingly.

"It is thanks to my teachers."

Birna snorted.

"Improved? Yesterday the instructors were fleeing in tears. What changed—did you finally stop pretending to be stupid?"

The room chilled.

Medea set her cup down carefully.

"The teacher my Aunt chose was excellent. She taught noble etiquette very well."

She lifted her eyes.

"Royal etiquette is different."

The strike landed cleanly.

Birna blinked.

"That's ridiculous! I had the same teacher!"

The Queen Mother's gaze slid to Birna.

For the first time, she looked.

The slouched posture.

The careless tone.

The unguarded familiarity.

Next to Medea's composed stillness, Birna looked loud. Unfinished.

"Birna. Sit up straight."

The Queen Mother snapped.

Birna stiffened, startled.

Medea lowered her eyes again.

In her past life, she had learned every rule to please a man who sold her to death.

Now, she followed them for herself.

The silence pressed heavy.

And for the first time—

The Queen Mother felt doubt.

Not about Medea.

But about everyone else in the room.
