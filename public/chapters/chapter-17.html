Ambient: Iron & Honor
Continuation

The Iron
Agreement

"He agreed."

"No. He agreed."

Medea walked past Neril, her steps light.

"We are moving the retired soldiers into the Royal Castle."

"…Ha."

Neril exhaled slowly, tension draining from her shoulders.

She knew her teacher better than anyone. Gillipos was iron incarnate. If he had refused, no argument—no blade—would have moved him.

I thought she would fail.

I was wrong.

"Do you want to say goodbye?" Medea asked, gesturing toward the closed study door.

Neril hesitated, eyes lingering on the heavy oak.

"No. He doesn't want to see me." She straightened. "And we should return before dinner."

— The Courtyard —

"Your Highness," the butler said, bowing deeply, "the carriage you arrived in has been… retired. We've prepared another."

Respect had replaced his earlier reserve.

The new carriage looked plain, but Medea noticed the details: steel reinforcement beneath the wood, iron-rimmed wheels.

Built for survival, not comfort.

"New carriage, same driver," Tom drawled, leaning against the wheel. "I'll get you back without a speck of dust on your dress."

Neril stepped forward.

With her back to Medea, she seized Tom by the collar and drove her fist into his solar plexus.

"Oof—!"

He folded, gasping.

"Cross me again, and I will break your legs."

"Tsk. Friendly as ever," Tom wheezed, forcing a grin while his knees shook.

What are they feeding the Royal Guard?

"You drove like that on purpose," Neril said. "You wanted to rattle her."

Tom straightened slowly.

"If she'd complained once—if she'd yelled at the soldiers, or ordered me to slow down—"

He glanced back at the mansion.

"The Marquis would've sent her home. That was the test."

A chill slid down Neril's spine.

So she endured it because she knew.

"She's playing chess," Tom muttered, "and the rest of us are kicking stones."

Then he shrugged.

"Anyway. The Master said it himself."

"Said what?"

Neril turned.

Through the purple curtains, a familiar silhouette stood at the window.

"'It wasn't foolish to take the Princess as your liege.'"

Neril didn't breathe.

"Wait here!"

She bolted.

— The Study —

"Master!"

Gillipos turned from the window, sighing. A faint twinkle lived in his eyes.

"Still impatient," he said. "Some things never change."

"I made my choice," Neril said, breathless and grinning. "So I'm taking back what's mine."

She crossed the room to the wall.

⚔️

The greatsword hung there—massive, scarred.

Her sword.

Years ago, when she'd left to serve the "useless" Princess, Gillipos had been furious.

If you serve a fool, leave your blade.

You don't get it back unless I acknowledge her.

Neril wrapped her fingers around the hilt.

It felt like coming home.

"Stay healthy, Master. I'll see you again."

"Get out," Gillipos grumbled, waving her away. "Don't keep royalty waiting."

He watched the door close.

"Stupid girl," he murmured fondly.

Then he looked down at the Princess waiting below.

She is better than me.

He had seen only mud. His disciple had found a star.

"God has not abandoned this country yet."

— A Few Days Later —

Blueprints arrived from the Marquis.

"He chose a site near the castle wall," Medea observed. "Smart. They can double as a defensive force."

She looked up.

"Neril. I need a list."

"Of?"

"Every veteran. Every family member. Names. Ages. Origins."

"So they don't drift toward the rebels," Neril realized.

"This is the King's responsibility," Neril said carefully. "You're using your entire fortune to clean up his mess."

"Are you worried I'll go broke?" Medea teased.

"Yes," Neril said honestly. "It feels like pouring water into a bottomless pit."

Medea laughed—a rare, genuine sound.

"I'm already the Cursed Princess. How much worse can it get?"

Her smile faded.

"You'd rather have me bankrupt than Valdina, wouldn't you?"

Neril hesitated. What she heard wasn't patriotism—it was atonement.

"If you lose everything, I'll take care of you."

Medea laughed softly, eyes shining.

"Then I'll live with Neril."

They reached the palace doors.

A maid waited, pale.

"Your Highness… Duchess Claudio is inside."

The warmth vanished from Medea's face.

"Let her in."
