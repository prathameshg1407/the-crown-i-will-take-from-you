The Spider's
Web

"The patient hunter weaves her snare long before the prey realizes it's trapped."

Raju nodded with unmistakable pride.

"Indeed. I'm profoundly grateful they journeyed all the way to the Straits. My dear, it means Providence itself favors our cause."

Had this been Valdina's Imperial Palace, penetrating security would have proven insurmountable. But here—in a villa—circumstances aligned differently.

Countless opportunists had flocked to El Amuz seeking fortune in this burgeoning territory. Raju had disguised himself as one such seeker, barely managing to position himself near Peleus.

Peleus's victory on the plains had cost many lives.

War reveals victors' faces—but those visages aren't always kind.

Even now, defeated soldiers who'd lost their homelands lingered in shadows, nursing poison in their hearts and praying for Peleus's death.

Raju had contacted them secretly. Among this bitter cohort, he'd selected the most capable to administer a potion to Peleus.

"Don't fret, my love. Peleus will soon prove incapable of maintaining his position."

The affection saturating his voice revealed how deeply Raju cherished Catherine.

"..."

Catherine regarded her lover with blank wonder.

Raju stood in stark contrast to her husband—who screamed at her constantly—and her son—kind but dismissive.

She massaged Raju's tense shoulders tenderly.

"I worry I've burdened you excessively."

"Nothing proves too arduous for you, my love. Not for you, not for Birna—not even for that pathetic excuse of a husband and your son."

Catherine wiped away grateful tears, her heart swelling with love for Raju.

"Joaquin? Don't even speak that pitiful creature's name!"

For three years, Catherine had witnessed nothing but her husband's pathetic cowardice.

While Samon desperately sought survival by groveling before Katzen nobles, while Birna suffered under the Empress Dowager's harassment, Joaquin simply drank himself into oblivion.

"That man who calls himself father merely escapes through alcohol! He's utterly unqualified for the title!"

House Claudio's collapse had stripped their seemingly healthy marriage to bare bones.

Joaquin no longer respected his wife. Catherine, having witnessed her husband's true depths, felt only contempt. What had sustained their union was noble status and accumulated wealth.

With those anchors severed, only ugliness remained between them.

"Catherine, I stand perpetually ready."

Raju reminded his lover that divorce remained available—an escape route she could take.

He yearned to hold her openly in morning sunlight rather than these furtive nocturnal encounters.

Catherine hesitated, biting her lip.

"I want that too. But the children..."

How would they accept this revelation? How could she tell Birna?

That Count Raju—whom Birna despises as vulgar—is actually her biological father?

Blind passion proved fleeting, but responsibility endured. Catherine still couldn't shoulder that weight with certainty.

Valdina Imperial Palace

Contrary to Raju's assumptions, the villa's security proved far from lax.

"Following the celebration, I anticipated assassins would assume His Majesty had lowered his guard. I segregated all personnel who'd joined the palace within the past three years. Then I apprehended them."

The operative Neril presented was already deceased.

"I caught him attempting to poison His Majesty's food. But he'd concealed a venom sac between his teeth."

Wide-eyed and purple-complexioned—a trained operative who'd immediately chosen death over revealing his masters.

In this process, the agent Zeta—sent by Gallo—had played a crucial role.

He'd traced the dead man's infiltration route and discovered additional operatives hiding within the palace. However, upon discovery, all chose immediate suicide.

Neril bowed her head.

"I'm ashamed I failed to capture any alive."

"It's acceptable. Sir Hertos, do you recognize this substance?"

Medea's voice remained composed.

Royal Physician Hertos frowned after tasting the white powder on his fingertip.

"Monga leaf. A medicinal herb typically used to treat cold sensitivity—but possessing the dangerous side effect of progressively amplifying aggressive temperament."

He expressed relief at the early discovery.

"Prolonged exposure would erode patience, trigger easy anger, and eventually cause violent outbursts. It gradually alters blood composition, making accurate diagnosis impossible—even for me."

"What manner of people employ such despicable methods?"

Neril's indignation was palpable.

Since they couldn't strike Peleus directly now that he reigned as Emperor, they'd chosen this insidious stratagem—gradual erosion, patient destruction.

"Your Highness, I shall definitively identify the perpetrators."

"No need. I already know."

"If someone targets me, who else but House Claudio could orchestrate such schemes?"

Peleus answered before Medea could speak. His expression remained frigid as glacial ice.

But his assumption fell slightly short of truth.

Raju is an expert in rare botanical substances...

Raju, you're begging me to take your life.

Medea's emerald eyes turned cold as winter frost.

She slid the paper containing white powder across the table.

"Neril, deliver this to Umberto. Instruct him to administer it to Raju."

Umberto currently maintained his disguise as Samon's butler, managing Claudio's household.

This positioning made adding the substance to Raju's regimen—who frequented the mansion—effortlessly simple.

"Raju? Jason's retainer?"

"Indeed. He's sailed alongside House Claudio for decades."

Medea nodded confirmation.

"Aren't you curious how this poison transforms people?"

"Yes, thanks to him, I can observe firsthand."

Peleus frowned at Neril's response.

Because returning Raju's stratagem didn't constitute the ultimate solution.

"Dea, I fail to comprehend why we permit them to continue breathing."

Peleus—who rarely referenced the past—questioned his sister directly.

"The Straits now belong to us. There's nothing more to extract. They're creatures who understand neither their place nor self-reflection. Their blood-soaked nature won't be severed until their necks are."

Though unaware of Claudio's layered depravity, battlefield experience had taught Peleus that such ambitions only death could quell.

"Dear Dea, with your tender heart, did witnessing their miserable state stir pity?"

Medea's laughter emerged like morning dew—bright, clear, utterly incongruous with the subject.

"Impossible. Peleus, you know precisely how I dismantled House Claudio."

Upon returning from war, Peleus had learned every detail of how his sister had purged the kingdom of vermin.

Yet somehow, he couldn't suppress treating her like an innocent seven-year-old.

"Forgive me, brother. I've troubled you with unnecessary concerns."

"Never apologize for that, Dea. If this serves your purpose, nothing proves impossible. Merely extending lives that can be claimed at any moment costs me nothing. Don't let it burden you."

Peleus consoled his sister, fearing she might feel wounded.

"Don't worry. All the prey has gathered in one location. No need to wait longer. It's time to spring the trap."

The Spider Reveals Her Web

A thought crystallized in Peleus's mind, sending a chill down his spine.

His sister absolutely understood Claudio's true nature. Which meant...

"Dea... you brought them here deliberately, didn't you?"

"The most patient predator
spins her web in silence—
then waits for prey to walk willingly into death."
