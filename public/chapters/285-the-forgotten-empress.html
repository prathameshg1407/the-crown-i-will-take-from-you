Chapter 285

The Forgotten
Empress

"The dead cannot speak for themselves. But sometimes, a single gesture speaks louder than a thousand words."

Cesare's brow arched when he noticed Medea's momentary hesitation before the bouquet.

His gaze fell upon the white anemones nestled among the blooms, and understanding dawned instantly.

His eyes turned to ice.

Less than half a day since we entered the capital, and already they move against her.

He was about to step forward—to intervene, to shield her from this petty trap—when Medea lifted the bouquet as though she had noticed nothing amiss.

A flicker of quiet satisfaction passed through her eyes.

"Your Highness? The First Princess?"

Rather than proceeding to the throne as expected, Medea turned away from the altar entirely.

She began walking toward the Western Empress Dowager.

The hem of her velvet gown swept gracefully over the marble steps, each movement unhurried, deliberate—utterly without impatience.

One step. Then another.

Under that piercing approach, the Western Empress's hands tightened involuntarily where they lay folded across her knees.

She noticed. And now she comes to confront me directly?

The noble ladies seated behind the Western Empress seemed to shrink with each footfall.

But the Empress forced herself to relax.

Let her come. This works in my favor.

If Medea lost her composure here—if she made a public scene—she would only reveal herself as ill-suited for court.

No matter how effectively the First Prince terrorized the nobility, he could not control the social sphere. That realm belonged to tongues sharper than any sword.

Here, one could destroy a person without ever lifting a finger.

And the Empress had nothing to fear from the First Princess's reaction. The Emperor himself would stand behind her.

When faced with a common enemy, her husband became more paranoid and stubborn than anyone.

"The First Princess approaches—"

The Western Empress opened her mouth to speak first, to seize control of the moment.

But just before reaching her, Medea turned.

Smoothly. Gracefully. As though this had been her destination all along.

What is she doing?

Every eye in the cathedral fixed upon the First Princess as she stepped back—and gently laid the bouquet of white anemones upon the empty Empress's throne.

"I wish to dedicate my first act as a member of the Imperial Family to those who have truly upheld this empire."

Her voice rang clear through the stunned silence.

For the first time, the Western Empress's composure cracked.

Medea turned to address High Priest Norman, who stood frozen at the altar.

"High Priest, surely the goddess understands when her servants follow where their conscience leads?"

"Your Highness..."

"Everyone present knows who represents the true symbol of Katzen's legitimacy at this moment."

Her green eyes swept across the assembled nobles.

"His Majesty's throne stands empty today. Therefore, I offer my tribute to the Empress in his stead."

Medea sank into a deep bow before the throne where she had placed the flowers—executing the same flawless obeisance she had performed during her oath to gods and empire.

Of course. The Empress.

Comprehension rippled through the congregation.

The Empress who had raised the First Prince. The woman who had sheltered and protected him through years of danger and hardship.

No matter how fiercely the Western and Eastern Empress Dowagers competed for influence, there was only one name carved upon the throne stone beside Perdiccas II:

Empress Eleanor.

"Yes... she was a remarkable woman. A living saint, some called her."

"She raised the First Prince through such hardship. It is only fitting that her memory receive this honor."

"If she were alive today, how moved she would be to see what he has become."

Those who remembered the late Empress spoke up one by one. Their eyes grew distant, misty with recollection.

You cunning little wretch.

The Western Empress's fingers crushed the fabric of her skirt until the silk threatened to tear.

With a single gesture, Medea had resurrected a name the Empress had spent decades trying to erase.

It was like having ice water thrown in her face.

She uses the dead Empress to imply that the First Prince inherited the legitimacy of the Imperial line through her—even though they share not a single drop of blood.

The Western Empress's gaze fixed upon Medea with the cold fury of a winter storm.

But Medea met those eyes without flinching.

Without wavering.

Meanwhile, Cesare had gone still.

How did Medea know about his mother?

Then he remembered Kensington—the man who had remained at Medea's side throughout their journey.

How much does Dea truly know?

Beyond the public knowledge that Empress Eleanor had raised him—did she understand the tangled web of love and resentment, of guilt and devotion, that still bound him to that memory?

His gaze moved between the empty throne and Medea's composed figure.

Something deep and unreadable settled in his golden eyes.

Across the cathedral, Eratosthenes had gone rigid in his seat.

Even if someone had tugged at the old monk's sleeve, he would not have noticed.

His ancient eyes, sharp between their weathered folds, fixed upon Medea with piercing intensity.

***

With memories and future intertwined, the coronation of the First Prince and his bride concluded in unexpected triumph.

Now only the ceremonial procession remained—the moment when the Crown Prince and Crown Princess would present themselves before the people of Katzen.

"Your Highness... I have done as you instructed."

Saya appeared at Medea's side to adjust her garments, whispering softly as she worked.

Medea smiled and climbed into the waiting carriage.

"Your Highnesses, the route will take you through the main square and around the city. The crowds will be considerable, but please bear with it—they have gathered specifically to see you."

Medea nodded from her seat in the open carriage.

BOOM!

Celebratory cannons thundered from every direction.

The streets running through the heart of the capital were packed with citizens, necks craning, bodies pressing forward for a glimpse of the legendary First Prince and his mysterious bride.

"Is that her? The Princess of Valdina?"

"She's our Crown Princess now! The coronation is complete. And look—His Highness the First Prince sits right beside her!"

The royal couple received the crowd's fervent attention with measured grace.

But it was the Crown Princess, rather than the Crown Prince, who captured the people's fascination.

They had grown accustomed to seeing the First Prince through countless triumphal processions. But the beautiful woman beside him—wearing the delicate coronet that marked her new station—was a revelation.

When the First Prince leaned close to whisper something, and the First Princess smiled in response, the crowd seemed to hold its collective breath.

The image of this striking pair—as perfectly matched as figures in a master painter's finest work—lifted the spirits of all who witnessed it.

"Your Highness! Princess! Welcome!"

A child's voice rang out from a second-floor window. Small hands released a cascade of wildflowers toward the passing carriage.

"HALT!"

The procession lurched to an immediate stop.

"Investigate immediately!"

Imperial guards swarmed the carriage, blades drawn instinctively at the sight of objects falling from above.

One guard leaped from his horse and seized the child by the collar, dragging the small figure down from the window.

The Katzen Imperial Family had survived countless assassination attempts—and the First Prince had faced more blades than any of them.

"Waaah!"

The child burst into terrified tears at the rough handling.

"Do you not know the penalty for raising your hand toward the Imperial carriage?"

The guard's voice cracked like a whip.

"Please—he's just a child! He didn't understand what he was doing! I beg your forgiveness—just this once!"

The boy's ashen-faced father rushed forward, bowing so low his forehead nearly touched the cobblestones.

He struggled desperately to quiet his sobbing son while awaiting judgment.

Katzen's hierarchy was absolute. The Imperial Family's arrogance matched their wealth and power, and they did not suffer presumption from commoners.

The father's eyes squeezed shut. At any moment, the man who had carved his reputation on battlefields might swing his blade.

"The child acted from a pure heart. Sir Knight, I ask that you show mercy this once."

A gentle voice cut through the tension.

"But Your Highness—"

"I am asking you to release him."

The First Princess's voice remained soft, but the quiet authority in it brooked no argument.

The guard startled at the unexpected command.

"Yes, Your Highness."

When he caught the First Prince's cold gaze upon him, he released the child entirely and retreated.

"Her Highness has shown you grace. Withdraw and observe the procession in silence."

The knight who had drawn his sword sheathed it instantly at the First Prince's words.

The father, who had prepared himself to be dragged away in chains, clutched his son and stared in disbelief.

"What are you waiting for? Do you wish to delay the procession further?"

"No! Thank you! Thank you, Your Highness!"

He bowed deeply to the First Princess before retreating.

At that moment, the child's eyes met Medea's.

She smiled—warm and genuine—and lifted the small bouquet of wildflowers from her lap. With unhurried grace, she tucked one of the blossoms behind her ear.

When the boy realized she was wearing the flowers he had thrown, his face lit up like the sun breaking through clouds.

"She's beautiful! She looks like a real princess! Even prettier than the princesses in my storybooks!"

"Shh! I told you to be quiet! Forgive us—we're leaving now!"

The father hastily covered the boy's mouth, dragging him back from what he still half-believed was the edge of disaster.

But even as they vanished into the crowd, the First Princess did not remove the flower from her hair.

The simple wildflower could not have looked more out of place beside her magnificent coronet—yet she wore it as though it were the most precious jewel in her possession.

The First Prince made no move to tell her to remove it. He merely reached over and gently brushed aside a strand of silver hair that the blossom had disturbed, tucking it behind her ear with surprising tenderness.

Even his subordinates exchanged bewildered glances. Such gentle consideration seemed entirely foreign to the man they knew.

"HOORAY!"

Even as his father's hands pulled him away, the child let out one final cheer at the sight of the Princess wearing his gift.

Medea raised her hand in a small wave of farewell.

Somewhere in the crowd, a citizen murmured as though speaking to himself:

"The Princess of Valdina treats even common children as though they were her own. That is the blood of Valdina showing through. She is nothing like the arrogant royals of Katzen."

The way she had accepted a child's humble gift—had cherished it openly, without a trace of condescension—touched something deep in the hearts of those who watched.

And then—

An extraordinary scene unfolded before the eyes of every citizen lining the street.
