Chapter 3

The Dragon's Shadow

When vengeance and ambition converge, the battlefield becomes personal.

Veins stood out prominently on the back of Medea's hand as she gripped Silva's reins with white-knuckled intensity.

He was her enemy now—but he had also been her husband for most of her life.

That intimate knowledge meant that even now, merely by sensing Jason's presence somewhere in this dark forest, Medea could anticipate his intentions with uncanny accuracy.

She knew where he was heading. What he was after. What he hoped to achieve.

Her beautiful mouth twisted into something bitter and cruel.

'Jason. Jason.'

You foolish, arrogant man.

Do you truly believe you can commit those sins and still possess me? Even after I've discovered the truth about everything—do you dare imagine I might forgive you?

"By slaying the evil dragon himself."

In her previous life, Jason had merely parasitized off Medea's strategies and efforts, claiming her victories as his own.

All the way through this treacherous forest, rage that had never been allowed to burn freely now blazed with fierce intensity.

"We're going over that mountain."

Medea turned sharply toward the expedition members, her decision already made.

"The mountain? But didn't you say we'd be descending today and moving across the plains?"

"Plans have changed. Hurry—we need to reach the canyon as quickly as possible."

Her explanation was deliberately vague.

"It appears we're not the only ones hunting the evil dragon in this forest."

"What? Who else would target the dragon? We can barely confirm other humans still inhabit these lands, and with the oracle's pronouncement—what could they possibly want with it?"

After all, they wouldn't need to sacrifice themselves to a dragon like Medea had been commanded to do.

"Someone who must claim the glory for themselves."

Medea's expression was cold as tempered steel.

Recalling her past life, it seemed Jason intended to reclaim his tarnished crown through this expedition.

How fitting. Perhaps it will serve as his coffin instead.

Medea's expedition moved with renewed urgency.

The crew quickly realized that the harsh days they'd already endured in the Ebon Forest had been relatively comfortable compared to what came next.

With minimal rest, Medea drove them relentlessly forward. The terrain grew steeper and more treacherous, but the rapid environmental changes confirmed they were taking a direct route—a dangerous shortcut.

The deeper they ventured, the more frequently they encountered demons.

However, Medea forbade the expedition from engaging in direct combat, citing the need to conserve their strength for the dragon itself.

Instead, they employed tools, poisons, and strategic misdirection to eliminate or redirect the creatures.

"It almost seems like she's deliberately herding them somewhere."

Gallo whispered the observation to Cesare with barely concealed fascination.

"Her Highness ordered me to have the falcon drop these in the southeastern section of the forest."

He showed Cesare a pouch filled with blue poison buttercup extract.

"A few days ago, it was holly spores. I wondered why the Madwolves suddenly went into a frenzy heading in that direction—then I realized the spores had detonated."

Gallo had later learned that holly spores were a primary food source for Madwolves, with the peculiar characteristic of triggering their most rabid, aggressive instincts.

After fulfilling several of Medea's "special commissions" over many days, Gallo had reached a certain conclusion.

"I'm now convinced—Her Highness is ensuring that someone on the opposite side of this forest is being systematically..."

Gallo paused, reconsidering his word choice.

She was selecting only the most life-threatening beasts for deployment. The word "hindered" seemed far too gentle.

"She wants them eliminated. But here's what puzzles me—who is her target?"

Gallo produced a half-torn piece of fabric.

"The falcon returned with this. It's Jason Castulo on the other side."

The coat of arms of the Grand Duke of Castulo was partially visible on the torn cloth.

"I thought he'd crawled out of the political abyss and disappeared somewhere—I had no idea he was navigating toward this canyon as well. How could she possibly know that? Could he truly be planning to traverse the forest and target the evil dragon in the canyon just as we are?"

It explained Medea's ruthless decision to force their breakthrough in this manner.

"He's even survived encounters with mutated creatures against impossible odds. As you know, this isn't territory where anyone survives without prior knowledge."

In other words, Jason was also heading toward the canyon, successfully avoiding the countless traps and hazards scattered throughout this forest.

Just like Medea.

The Princess of Valdina and the Grand Duke of Katzen.

Two people whose life trajectories shouldn't overlap at all were now moving in perfect, eerie synchronization throughout this expedition.

As if they were sharing the same space and time. As if they'd walked this path together before.

Cesare's elegant features twisted with unmistakable displeasure.

He picked up the fabric fragment bearing Castulo's crest, examining the half-torn emblem.

The loosely woven threads—their colors still vibrant despite the damage—hung pathetically between his fingers.

Cesare recalled Medea's face in those moments when shadows settled across her features. He'd witnessed the instances when her carefully maintained composure was soaked through with skepticism of unfathomable depth.

Every so often, that hard, ironclad control would crack.

"Boss?"

And then Cesare understood, with sudden, unwelcome clarity.

In those rare moments when Medea lost her composure, Jason was always somehow involved—either directly present or hovering in her thoughts like a specter.

His rational mind spoke coldly: The catalyst that triggers Medea's trauma may be Jason himself.

"I won't be fooled again. Jason, never again!"

Those words she'd once murmured in her sleep—words Cesare didn't fully comprehend.

They indicated a cause-and-effect relationship between Medea and Jason that could never be discovered through the Facade's considerable investigative power. Something deeper. Something more intimate and terrible.

He dropped the torn fabric deliberately.

Then he ground it deep into the muddy forest floor with his boot, twisting until it disappeared completely into the muck.

His handsome face took on a grim, dangerous cast.

"When you send the falcon next, include this with your delivery."

Cesare handed Gallo a grey leather pouch.

"This is monster musk!"

Gallo peered inside the pouch and immediately recoiled, his brow furrowing sharply.

"You want me to scatter this as well? Then every demon in the forest will converge on their position!"

During monster wave incidents, this was a synthetic musk developed by the Katzen Imperial Family specifically to gather and suppress scattered demon hordes in a single location.

"But he's your blood relative—you're going to bury him alive in this forest?"

As if the question wasn't even worth answering, Cesare idly spun his dagger between his fingers. Gallo scratched his head in bewilderment.

"I don't understand why the boss is suddenly so irritable."

Does Her Highness even know about this?

Gallo felt an unexpected pang of sympathy for Jason, condemned to death by the unprovoked whims of his own kinsman.

Ssssch—!

At that moment, a demonic vine that had been lying in wait burst from the muddy ground. It lunged toward them, thorns sharpening into deadly points.

In a rare display, Cesare personally drew his sword.

The vicious vines were severed cleanly and fell writhing to the ground.

"Disgusting."

Squelch, squelch.

But even the fragments that had fallen into the mud continued to writhe as though still alive, ugly and persistent.

Eventually, they would find each other and fuse back together, whole once more.

Several days later, the dense forest ended abruptly, as though severed by a blade.

Where the earth had cracked and split open, an enormous canyon stretched before them.

"We've arrived at last."

Steep cliffs rose on either side, their surfaces twisted and warped as if they'd been melted in primordial flames.

The river that once flowed through the canyon had long since dried up, leaving no trace of water.

Small, blackened pebbles littered the canyon floor, interspersed with broken weapons and charred bones—grim testaments to those who'd come before.

As they ventured deeper, it wasn't difficult to spot deep claw marks gouged into the rock face itself.

These were traces of fierce battles left by the humans who had previously attempted to conquer this canyon.

"It's far too quiet."

D'Angel's low observation seemed particularly loud in the oppressive silence.

The canyon was breathtakingly, unnaturally still.

As if mocking their vigilance, the sky remained clear and bright. The occasional cool breeze that brushed their cheeks felt almost peaceful—a deceptive calm.

Then, without warning—

A massive black shadow obscured the sky in an instant.

The evil dragon spread its enormous wings and soared directly over the area where the expedition stood frozen.

For a split second, every member of the expedition dove behind boulders, exchanging silent glances while adjusting their weapons with trembling hands.

Tension crystallized in the silence like ice.

"That... that's the evil dragon..."

Tom swallowed hard, his throat clicking audibly.

Though it flew at a considerable distance, merely witnessing the evil dragon spread its colossal wings made it feel as though an invisible weight were crushing down on their entire bodies.

Humans and demons alike were tangled together in its razor-sharp claws—still alive, still struggling.

Every time the creature flapped its wings—

Thud. Thud. Thud.

Severed limbs and shapeless corpses fell one by one to the canyon floor far below.

The evil dragon was not hungry. This was merely brutal slaughter conducted for its own amusement.

"Commit every detail to memory."

Even as the men grimaced and looked away, Medea's eyes never left the evil dragon—tracking it like a predator studying its prey.

"The evil dragon ensnares anyone who touches its shadow and secretes venom from its scales."

Medea's voice rang pure and authoritative.

"Therefore, be extremely careful not to step into any shadows it casts."

Her eyes—utterly devoid of fear or hesitation—inspired confidence in the hearts of every expedition member.

Victory would be in her hands by the time this ended. Just as she'd proven time and again throughout their journey.

"The operation begins now."

After one final, steadying breath, the expedition members crept silently across the canyon floor.

Each carried a bow slung across one arm. Their oil-treated arrowheads had been inscribed with Terence's magical formulae, glowing faintly with arcane power.

"Silva, are you ready?"

Smoothing her mount's pristine white mane, Medea asked softly.

The intelligent Silva whinnied quietly and rubbed his nose affectionately against her shoulder.

At that moment, Medea felt a hand close gently around her wrist.

It was Cesare.

When Medea had proposed serving as bait to lure the evil dragon, everyone had vehemently objected to the suicidal plan.

Everyone except Cesare, who had supported her decision with an infuriatingly calm expression.

"I'll be right behind you."

His voice was low and deep, without the slightest tremor.

For a fleeting moment, there was a strange quality to his presence—something that made her almost forget the monstrous reality of the evil dragon looming before them.

"So don't be afraid."

Their eyes met and held.

For reasons she couldn't quite name, something in her chest resonated strangely—a vibration she couldn't identify or suppress.

"I'm not afraid."

But Medea's response was sharp, almost defensive.

"When I bring the dragon to your position, be prepared to sever its head."

"As the Princess commands."

Cesare smiled faintly. He tightened his grip on her wrist for just a moment—a gesture of reassurance—then slowly released her.

Medea kicked her stirrups decisively.

She drove Silva forward at full gallop, racing directly into the middle of the dragon's flight path.

The wind tore at her hair and cloak as horse and rider became a streak of white against the blackened canyon floor.

Above, the dragon's massive head swiveled. Ancient, malevolent eyes locked onto the tiny figure challenging it.

For a heartbeat, the world held its breath.

Then the evil dragon roared—a sound that shook the very foundations of the earth—and dove.
