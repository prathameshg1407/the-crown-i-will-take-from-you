Chapter 245

The Weight of
Farewell

"I promised to protect you—and that promise made you afraid."

A low, rich voice brushed against her ear like velvet.

"I was going to go through with it."

Cesare spoke as though discussing the weather—not the systematic dismantling of the continent's holiest institution.

He had mobilized the Facade the moment the oracle was announced. On that very day, he had obtained the names of everyone present in the stele chamber and begun investigating their histories for any hint of impropriety.

"The Holy Kingdom is no different from any other power, in the end. Their appearance of purity is merely an illusion."

Cesare had focused his efforts on the corruption of the High Priests—the sale of ecclesiastical positions, the fabrication of heresy charges, collusion with foreign kingdoms for political gain.

"Once the High Priests' corruption is exposed to the world, the authenticity of the oracle itself will come into question."

It would not undo the prophecy already spoken.

But by striking at the central temple's credibility, Cesare had sought to create a lifeline—a path to save Medea from her fate.

Medea could scarcely believe what she was hearing.

"You're speaking of the central temple. The sanctuary of the goddess herself."

He shrugged, utterly unconcerned.

"Excluding the Pope, it's nothing but Saintesses and High Priests—and they've already rotted from within. I simply intended to cut out the decay and display it for the world to see."

His languid, honeyed voice made it sound as though the devil himself were whispering temptations in the dark.

"Your life hangs in the balance. Do you truly think the Holy Kingdom can afford to chase after a vanished princess while crying foul about oracles?"

He smiled—that dangerous, knowing smile.

"I was merely exploiting the circumstances."

This confirmed what Medea had suspected: the ship had been prepared not for an expedition, but to spirit her away from the oracle's reach.

Medea bit her lip.

His words had been delivered lightly, but she understood the weight they carried better than anyone. He had been willing to make an enemy of the entire Holy Kingdom—to prevent the oracle's execution.

For her.

It was a strange, unsettling feeling.

"You've done something unworthy of your position. I can manage my own affairs. I don't need additional variables."

She was not accustomed to this—knowing that a stranger, someone with no blood ties to her, no obligation whatsoever, had tried to protect her.

No. Not just interest...

If she allowed herself to trust him—and he betrayed her—she would never recover.

At Medea's words drawing a clear line between them, Cesare's lips twisted.

"If you say that... you know I'm not going to back off."

Something feral gleamed in his narrowed eyes. He leaned closer.

The distance between them shrank until Medea could see her own reflection in the molten gold of his irises.

For a moment, meeting his gaze felt overwhelming. She turned her head away.

He noted how her delicate brow furrowed, and when he spoke again, his voice had dropped to something low and intimate.

"Medea. Don't overthink this. I'm simply protecting what matters most to me."

"If you tell me to stop, I'll stop."

A pause.

"But not now."

Not when the entire world has turned its gaze upon you.

His eyes curved softly above the sharp bridge of his nose, but the expression only amplified the danger radiating from him.

Listening to the whispered words of this beautiful man—who seemed more devil than prince—Medea instinctively stepped back.

Cesare made no move to follow.

The faint smile that had graced his perfect features hardened into something unreadable.

Boom. Crash.

Only the sound of waves striking the hull filled the silence between them.

Meanwhile

As the ship sailed toward darkness, the messengers of the Holy Kingdom fanned out across the continent.

"The oracle has descended! By command of the Holy Father, we bring the will of the goddess!"

Valdina. Katzen. The kingdoms of Middle-earth. One by one, every nation learned the oracle's contents.

Reactions varied wildly.

"An oracle, appearing so suddenly? And with such contents... what in the world is happening?"

"This is an opportunity. The goddess has cleared away an obstacle for us."

Bewilderment. Delight. And—

Fury.

The crisp parchment of the oracle's transcript tore in Peleus's grip.

"Valdina refuses to acknowledge this as the will of the goddess."

The priest who had come as the Holy Kingdom's messenger stared at the shredded document, his face contorting with outrage.

"How dare Valdina reject a divine oracle!"

"We refuse to recognize the ravings of fools who cannot perceive even the most obvious logic."

The ice in Peleus's voice made the messenger stumble backward.

"S-Sire, if you turn your back on the oracle, disaster will befall this land! Does Valdina truly believe it can shoulder such consequences—"

But before the priest could finish, Peleus had already swept from the room.

The Emperor's barely restrained rage lingered in the air like heat from a forge.

"I ask the priest's understanding."

Sissair swallowed a heavy sigh behind his spectacles, carefully composing his expression before turning to face the agitated messenger.

"Oracle or not—where in this world exists a brother who would meekly obey an order to sacrifice his own sister?"

"But the oracle must be fulfilled to prevent catastrophe upon the continent—"

"Tell me. Do you have a family?"

The priest fell silent.

"If you were in His Majesty's position, would you push your only family member toward death without a moment's hesitation?"

The messenger had no answer.

"I do not believe the goddess would accept as a true believer one who lacks even the smallest measure of compassion."

"...I am merely a messenger sent to deliver the oracle. The authority to postpone the ceremonial day... lies beyond my power."

"Then grant us time. A brief reprieve to accept the situation. Nothing has happened yet."

Moved—or perhaps simply exhausted—by Sissair's measured persuasion, the priest's rigid posture finally softened.

"...Very well, Your Excellency."

Medea's Chambers

Peleus strode through the palace corridors, passing maids who bowed their heads and averted their eyes, until he reached Medea's bedroom.

A piece of parchment lay on her writing table, its edges fluttering in the draft.

He read the words again. And again. As though repetition might change their meaning.

"Peleus, I'm sorry to leave like this. I'll be back."

A single sentence in Medea's handwriting. And yet, somehow, all her fears and sorrows seemed contained within those few words.

Thud.

Peleus sank onto his sister's bed. Her scent still lingered in the room—faint traces of the lavender oil she favored.

He pressed his fingertips against his temples, swallowing the silent rage that threatened to consume him.

"Has there been any word from Dea?"

"Forgive me, Your Majesty. We've found nothing. It's as though she vanished completely."

D'Angel's voice was thick with shame.

Peleus reached for the small wooden figure resting beside the bed.

The crude doll he had carved for his sister on some long-ago battlefield—Medea had kept it with her all these years.

"I promised to protect you. And that promise... that's what made you afraid, isn't it?"

The thought that his sister had fled in such haste, without revealing her destination, because she feared what he might sacrifice for her—

It was almost unbearable.

"Perhaps this is for the best."

Peleus's grip tightened on the doll until blood seeped from his palm.

"Inform the Agema. They are to cease all efforts to locate Dea."

"Your Majesty—?"

"And D'Angel."

Peleus turned to face his most trusted knight.

"From this moment forward, you will pursue Medea. When you find her—stay by her side."

The Emperor's Blade

The young Emperor's handsome face had become so rigidly controlled it appeared almost expressionless.

"Once the Holy Kingdom learns that Dea has fled, they will hunt her. Protect her in my stead."

Boom.

Peleus extended his sword to D'Angel.

The massive blade, emblazoned with the eagle crest of Valdina, had never left his side since his first battle. It had been with him through every campaign, every victory, every desperate stand.

"Until she escapes safely. Until she finds a place to settle. Until she meets someone worthy and bears children. Until no danger remains—watch over her life."

"And if Dea ever asks what I wished to tell her..."

Peleus's voice caught.

"Tell her I'm certain we'll meet again. Someday."

But Peleus could not know how distant that day might be.

And if the oracle proved true—if catastrophe descended upon the continent—

He would have to defend Medea not merely against the Holy Kingdom, but against the entire world.

He would do everything in his power to eliminate the calamity, even at the cost of his own life.

If that was the only way to keep her safe.

This might be a promise he could never fulfill.

"Yes, Your Majesty."

D'Angel knelt, cradling Peleus's sword against his chest.

"I will sacrifice my life to protect Her Highness. Forgive me for failing to fulfill my duty at Your Majesty's side."

He lifted his head and met his Emperor's gaze.

"I am sorry to leave you, Your Majesty."

The young Emperor's face was carved from anguish.

"I do not know if we shall ever meet again. But serving alongside Your Majesty as we built this nation—I will carry that honor to my grave."

D'Angel paused, a bitter smile flickering across his features.

"I would pray for the goddess's blessing upon you... but perhaps that rings hollow now. Instead—may the Star of Victory shine upon Your Majesty. Always."

With those words, D'Angel rose and withdrew into the shadows.
