Chapter 229

Shadows
Conspire

"In darkness, the serpent learns to wear a saint's smile."

The basement lay at the lowest level of the Golden Tower, buried so deep that no light from the world above could reach it.

In the suffocating darkness, the warlocks had gathered in a circle. The walls wept moisture, and a pair of guttering candles provided the only illumination—pale flames that seemed on the verge of drowning in shadow.

The dim light pooled upon the black robes of those seated around the ancient stone table, casting ominous shapes that writhed with each flicker. Deep hoods concealed their faces; only their voices betrayed their identities.

"So. What do you propose we do?"

CRACK.

The warlock seated to the west slammed his palm against the stone table—that ancient medium, stained dark with centuries of blood that had seeped into its fine cracks and carved channels.

The ritual circle etched into its surface gleamed a dull, arterial red.

"I am sick unto death of being hunted by the Tower's lapdogs. And now the Holy Nation has caught our scent as well! What happens when the Knights Templar discover our trail?"

For over a century, the dark wizards had operated from the shadows—careful, cunning, powerful.

They had grown their influence gradually, drawing strength from the primordial darkness that was their origin and purpose.

But they had not yet emerged fully into the light. And now, after a cascade of recent failures, they found themselves hunted on every front.

The Magic Tower. The Holy Nation. Katzen. Valdina.

Some pursuers understood the warlocks' true purpose—to resurrect the primordial darkness and remake the world. Others chased them blindly, driven by instinct or duty.

But all were determined to expose them.

"If those fools weren't so consumed by their petty rivalries—if they had ever managed to unite against us—we would have been captured long ago!"

The endless conflicts between the great powers had become the warlocks' unlikely lifeline.

And then there was the matter of Raju.

News of his execution had reached them recently. Emperor Katzen's descent into madness was nothing new, but no one had anticipated such a dramatic reversal of fortune.

"What in all the hells is happening?"

The First Prince's sudden intervention at the very moment Medea was to be eliminated. The exposure of the former Emperor's hidden ledger. Raju's swift execution.

And now—Eratosthenes. The late Empress's father. A scholar of legendary reputation, returning to court after a decade of deliberate obscurity.

The warlocks found themselves bewildered by the cascade of unprecedented changes within Katzen's imperial palace.

"The Saintess was meant to awaken in Katzen. That was the plan from the beginning. Not in Valdina."

The stage for the Saintess's emergence—the opening act of their Grand Plan—had always been intended to be the Katzen Empire.

The mightiest nation on the continent. The only power whose collapse would send shockwaves across every shore.

They had moved against the First Prince first, recognizing him as their greatest obstacle.

If everything had proceeded as designed, Katzen should be in ruins by now—the First Prince dead, monster tides erupting across the land, the Emperor drowning in paranoid madness.

But against all reason, the First Prince had survived.

How he had escaped the primordial curse remained an utter mystery. The warlocks could only speculate that somehow, impossibly, the answer had come from Valdina.

We have always been the ones orchestrating chaos, shaping conflicts from the shadows. But this time...

This time, it feels as though we are the ones being swept along.

"Valdina's Princess remains an unacceptable variable. Had that woman not interfered, none of this would have gone wrong."

The warlock seated to the north spoke, and murmurs of agreement rippled through the circle.

At the center of every failure in Valdina stood Princess Medea.

What a remarkable coincidence.

"She carries Venus's essence—of course she vexes us at every turn. Perhaps the time has come to simply... remove her."

"Have you taken leave of your senses?"

Another voice cut sharply through the darkness.

"Have you not heard? The Emperor of Katzen has declared himself her guardian. Do you understand the implications? If we eliminate her without overwhelming justification, the consequences will be catastrophic!"

"So we simply allow this variable to persist indefinitely? Because of that woman, our entire strategy requires reconstruction from the foundation!"

The argument intensified. Voices rose. The discord among the warlocks swelled until the damp air seemed to vibrate with barely contained hostility.

Crisis and urgency had frayed their composure, but not one among them possessed a genuine solution.

"There is no need for such anxiety."

A soft voice drifted through the chamber.

The underground door swung slowly open, and pale light spilled through the gap—pushing back the darkness like morning mist yielding to dawn.

The Saintess Rachel stepped inside.

For a moment, the warlocks fell silent, struck speechless as though the oppressive dampness of the chamber had suddenly lifted.

When did this girl mature so dramatically?

When they had first dispatched her to Valdina, Rachel had been little more than a puppet—a tool shaped by the warlocks' hands. Her narrow perspective and impulsive nature had required constant management, barely concealed beneath layers of careful training.

But something had changed.

Has she finally opened her eyes? After being venerated as a Saintess, after matching wits with the mastermind behind Venus herself?

Now, even the warlocks found themselves momentarily overwhelmed by Rachel's composed bearing. They could not help but give her their full attention.

"The origin and the solution to our problems both begin with the Princess of Valdina. In the end, she is the key to everything."

The candlelight cast flickering shadows across Rachel's features.

Her pale, delicate face formed a striking contrast against the subterranean gloom. There was something in her expression—an aura of pure benevolence—that made her sanctity appear genuine, even to those who knew it was a carefully manufactured illusion.

Small wonder that an entire nation was deceived.

"I shall handle this matter personally."

"You? After squandering every resource we provided? You still presume to address us with such confidence?"

The warlock seated at the center of the table made no effort to conceal his disdain—a pointed reminder of Rachel's string of humiliating failures against Medea.

"That seems rather unfair."

But not a trace of agitation crossed Rachel's face.

"If memory serves, all those petty schemes originated from your collective wisdom. This will be the first time I have acted of my own accord."

Whether her calm was genuine or merely an imitation of someone who never lost composure remained unclear.

"You have devised an alternative approach?"

"Tell me—are curses and mind manipulation the only methods by which faith can be shattered?"

A faint smile curved Rachel's lips as she addressed the warlock's question.

"Medea has been elevated to the status of a noble hero—a symbol of perfection in the eyes of her people. But that very perfection is her vulnerability."

She paused, allowing the words to settle.

"The higher the pedestal, the more devastating the fall. When the people discover the truth—when their faith is broken—they will turn on her with all the fury of betrayed devotees."

"Elaborate."

"Medea was once called the Cursed Child, was she not?"

Silence answered her.

"The people seem to have forgotten. I shall remind them."

She lifted one slender finger toward the ceiling, then slowly rotated it downward until it pressed against the bloodstained stone of the table.

"I shall remind them that the curse which devoured her parents now threatens to consume her brother, the Emperor—and I shall deliver this truth with the Voice of God that all the world recognizes."

Divine judgment.

If such a proclamation were believed, it would shake the absolute faith the people currently placed in their Princess.

"We still possess one remaining piece on the board."

The sole surviving bearer of the Claudio bloodline. Samon.

"That wretch has already lost all public support. No one will give credence to anything he says."

"That is immaterial. We require only a spark to ignite the conflagration."

Rachel smiled.

It was a serene expression—calm, measured, almost gentle. And as the candlelight played across her features, her smile began to bear an uncanny resemblance to Medea's own.

"If I cannot destroy Medea herself..."

"...I shall destroy her world instead."
