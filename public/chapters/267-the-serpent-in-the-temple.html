Chapter 267

The Serpent
in the Temple

"A fate I have not seized with my own hands is no fate of mine."

The cold sigh that escaped Peleus seemed to settle upon the leather-bound volume resting on the table.

"Your Majesty. If I may be so bold..."

"..."

"The First Prince who accompanied me on the expedition was every bit as notorious as his reputation suggested. Sharp. Cruel. And yet—as a comrade in arms, he was utterly dependable. He was always the first to charge into danger, and he never once retreated before the enemy."

"..."

"More than anything, he guarded Her Highness with unwavering vigilance. Even when I collapsed from exhaustion, his eyes never strayed from her for a single moment. That arrogant, imperious man followed Her Highness Medea like a loyal hound."

D'Angel paused, drawing a hesitant breath.

"Your Majesty. I understand that we must remain wary of him. However, when the entire continent has set its sights upon Her Highness, there is no one beyond our own people who genuinely seeks to protect her—"

Perhaps this arrangement is not entirely without merit?

After all, did my lord not once face an even crueler future than this?

A farewell with no promise of ever seeing or finding his sister again.

Peleus's brow furrowed, as though he had grasped the words D'Angel had left unspoken.

"Very well. You may leave."

D'Angel swallowed a sigh at the frigid air that showed no sign of thawing.

'Even after learning the true significance of those certificates—the ones Sissair pointed out—he remains unmoved. Well, I've said all that needed saying.'

From this point forward, it was a matter of the heart, not the mind.

No matter what anyone did, no suitor would ever satisfy an elder brother when it came to his precious younger sister.

In truth, Peleus had never intended to look favorably upon the First Prince from the very beginning.

'How can one remain so utterly indifferent when confronted with the most dazzling treasures the continent has to offer?'

Paradoxically, the unrelenting coldness only seemed to burn hotter, like a furnace stoked by its own frost.

Medea herself—the actual bride-to-be—had merely released a brief sigh upon seeing the First Prince's gifts, then departed with nothing but the leather-bound volume of deeds in hand.

"Convey this to Sissair."

Just as D'Angel was about to take his leave, that cold voice reached him.

"Under no circumstances are we to fall behind in preparing Dea's dowry."

Even if it depleted several years' worth of the national treasury—Valdina's unwavering support for Medea must be made unmistakably clear.

In the end, it was a tacit acknowledgment of the engagement.

"As you command, Your Majesty."

D'Angel exhaled in quiet relief at Peleus's implicit approval.

Emerging from the imperial palace, D'Angel paused mid-stride.

'Until the wedding concludes... will everything proceed smoothly?'

He wouldn't be the least bit surprised if his lord changed his mind at the last moment and spirited Medea away.

"Hah..."

D'Angel released a heavy sigh.

'I suppose I'll need to remain vigilant until the ceremony is well and truly finished.'

The Holy Land of Sacramontis

Crack. Crack.

Within the Central Temple, in the Saintess's private chambers, a disturbing sound echoed—the grinding of teeth against torn fingernails.

"Medea..."

"A fate I have not seized with my own hands is no fate of mine."

"Why did you say that? What did you see? Did you know what you were saying?"

Rachel could not escape the echo of Medea's parting words.

Once again, that insufferably fortunate girl had managed to evade the false oracle in spectacular fashion. The news was no longer a secret.

"If she's realized the truth... if she's already awakened..."

Rachel muttered anxiously, then shook her head.

"No. The warlocks would have sensed it..."

The awakening of a true Saintess was accompanied by a surge of radiant light energy.

Light and darkness were eternal mirrors of one another—the dark mages had assured her that any such awakening would be impossible to conceal from their awareness.

"If she had truly awakened, she would have recognized the oracle as a forgery by now. Yet she hasn't exposed it."

A true Saintess could perceive the future with the clarity of witnessed reality.

But Rachel sensed it instinctively.

'That girl's awakening is nearly complete... or perhaps it has already begun, but her power remains incomplete.'

Droplets of blood fell from the gaps in skin torn by her own teeth.

'If Medea fully awakens... what becomes of me?'

The moment that thought crystallized, cold sweat traced its way down her spine.

'I become worthless. They'll simply dispose of me.'

Rachel knew all too well what the warlocks did with vessels that had outlived their usefulness.

For now, only Rachel herself must know of Medea's condition.

"I always knew they would discard me like refuse the moment I ceased to be useful."

Rachel's teeth ground together.

She had not devoted her entire existence to being treated as a consumable tool.

She had clawed and struggled with a single, unwavering purpose: to claim that girl's brilliant destiny as her own.

Even when that wretched woman blocked her path again and again, Rachel never surrendered.

Did they think it had been simple—for a practitioner of dark magic to establish herself as a Saintess within the Central Temple itself, the very heart of enemy territory?

And now, merely because that girl was awakening, she was expected to relinquish everything so easily?

Crack. Crack.

The jarring sound echoed once more through the silent chamber.

She tore at her nails with savage force. Fresh blood welled up where it had only just begun to clot, but she paid it no heed.

"I cannot allow this to continue."

Rachel surged to her feet, the hem of her cream-colored gown sweeping across the floor.

The woman reflected in the mirror was pale as death. Her bloodless lips curved into something unsettling.

"I should have taken that woman's life myself! Only then would Selene's destiny have become truly mine!"

"Rachel, never forget. To make her orbit your own, you must strip away everything—her family, her love, her achievements. Even her death must belong to you!"

"I must kill her with my own hands."

Rachel's voice merged with the echo of her dead mother's words.

She would move before the Dark Mages of the Golden Throne discovered the truth.

The moment Medea died, any possibility of awakening would vanish forever.

"And then... everything that belongs to that girl will naturally fall into my hands."

A single death would be enough to redeem every humiliating failure—every instance where she had failed to claim anything from Medea.

The only remaining question was execution.

'The weapons the warlocks provided are still at my disposal.'

Those who attended her as performing priests—disciples raised by the dark mages themselves, prodigies predicted to become the next masters of the Golden Throne.

'They might be capable of diverting the First Prince's attention.'

Even if none of them returned alive.

The Priests' Quarters

"Have you lost your mind? Whom exactly do you intend to murder?"

One of the disciples spat the words through clenched teeth.

The faces of the priests attending Rachel had gone rigid the moment they heard her plan.

"The child of prophecy was to be dealt with only after stripping away the entirety of her destiny—that was the command!"

"Does the Master know of this? That you intend to drag our entire operation through the mud on your own authority?"

All their suppressed fury erupted at once.

They had been dispatched to assist Rachel at their teacher's behest.

Yet due to repeated failures, they had been forced to remain within this despised Central Temple indefinitely.

Even now, at this very moment, their positions beside their master were being usurped by ambitious juniors clawing their way upward.

"The false oracle crafted by violating the Golden Rule has already failed—and now you dare propose this—"

The man's words were cut short.

A sharp knock interrupted him.

"I'm leaving. I cannot fathom what possessed you to summon us at this ungodly hour!"

These filthy, ungrateful creatures.

Venom flooded Rachel's eyes. The violet light within them pulsed with something sinister.

"Stop."

In that instant, the priest's feet froze mid-step.

A goat-shaped sigil blazed to life above his widening eyes.

"Come to me. Kneel. Press your forehead to the floor and lick my shoes."

Rachel's voice was soft, almost tender—yet saturated with something rotten, something steeped in ancient malice.

The priest's body moved of its own accord.

Thud. Thud. Thud. Crash.

There was nothing of his own will in the sequence of movements.

He simply stared up at Rachel with empty eyes and slack features.

"There now. You do as you're told. Such a good boy."

Rachel cooed with mock affection, pressing her foot against the priest's forehead and forcing the toe of her shoe deeper into his mouth.

"Gkh—! Nngh—!"

He choked on dark blood, foam gathering at the corners of his lips.

"You—you cast dark magic on one of your own?!"

The voice of another priest trembled with disbelief.

"And what makes any of you believe you're exempt?"

"You deranged woman! You'd turn your blade against your own allies?! Have you completely lost your mind?!"

Allies?

Rachel giggled.

They treated her like a thorn to be extracted at the earliest opportunity. Did they honestly believe she hadn't contemplated dealing with them first?

"Come here."

Despair flooded the priest's eyes.

In that moment, he understood.

They should never have accepted this accursed woman from the very beginning.

This girl was a calamity—a plague that spread like venom, destroying everything she touched for her own sake alone.

"No—"

The priest gasped, stumbling backward.

But it was already too late.

"If we move against the Princess now, our entire organization will be exposed—no, we cannot possibly—"

His voice grew smaller, trembling, then faded entirely.

The eyes of the performing priests went dark and vacant, all awareness draining away.

"All of you will remain silent and do precisely as I command."

Rachel had no intention of following anyone's orders ever again.

The arrogance of absolute power—the intoxication of a victor drunk on dominion—curved her lips into a cruel smile.

But Rachel was too absorbed in the satisfaction of watching her brainwashed priests prostrate themselves, foreheads striking the floor in rhythmic devotion—

—to notice the paladin concealed behind a distant pillar, watching every moment unfold in the shadows.
