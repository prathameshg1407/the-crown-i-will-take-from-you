Chapter 346

The Oracle's
Command

"When the gods speak, even the mighty must answer—or defy heaven itself."

When the First Prince and his wife entered the Imperial Hall, the Emperor and his chief ministers had already assembled.

The atmosphere within the grand chamber—its walls emblazoned with the Katzen Empire's crest—was suffocating.

Medea appeared at Cesare's side.

The messenger from the Holy Kingdom, draped in cream-colored priestly robes, immediately scowled.

"Why is this blasphemer present at a gathering meant to discuss matters of continental importance?"

Katzen's High Priest, who had accompanied Medea, stiffened at the messenger's brazen words.

I know this man represents the Holy Kingdom... but how could His Holiness send such a fool?

He'd heard rumors that the oracle had been delivered directly to Katzen's palace—bypassing him entirely—and had rushed to attend.

"Mind your tongue! How dare you show such disrespect to the First Princess? If you come as a messenger of the Holy Kingdom, conduct yourself accordingly!"

Katzen's High Priest rebuked him sharply.

The messenger from the Holy Kingdom snorted.

"Hmph. The High Priest clearly hasn't grasped the situation back home."

He cast a disdainful glance at Medea.

"Don't you know she framed our true Saintess? Because of her, His Holiness the Pope has fallen into a coma while attempting to correct her sins!"

"His Holiness is in a coma?"

Katzen's High Priest's eyes widened in shock.

"How could such critical news not be communicated to every branch of the faith across the continent? And what nonsense is this about a 'true Saintess'?"

"The Temple Knights could hardly afford bureaucratic niceties while battling such profound evil."

The messenger's tone dripped with condescension.

"Fortunately, High Priest Paul has assumed the role of Papal Vicar, and Saintess Rachel has purged the corruption and restored order to the temple."

"What?"

Katzen's High Priest's white beard trembled with outrage.

"Saintess Rachel?! Have you lost your minds? I witnessed her being caught red-handed practicing black magic! What conspiracy is this?"

Unable to contain his fury, the High Priest struck the messenger across the face.

"Have all the priests at the Central Temple gone mad? It's not enough to release a convicted criminal—now you've reinstated her as Saintess? This is a rejection of thousands of years of sacred tradition!"

"How dare you question the Central Temple's authority!"

BANG!

A heavy bronze statue crashed to the floor, hurled by the Emperor himself.

The heated argument ceased instantly.

"Save your dog fights for your own kennels. This is the Imperial Palace. Show some decorum."

Perdiccas II's voice was laced with irritation.

As if he'd been waiting for this moment, the messenger withdrew a scroll from his robes and held it aloft.

"Of course, Your Majesty. The purpose of this assembly is to convey the words of the Sublime Goddess to the nations. His Holiness the Pope has commanded it."

"Another oracle? Does your goddess have nothing better to do than chatter at mortals?"

Silence.

The High Priest bristled at Perdiccas II's irreverent tone but dared not rebuke an Emperor.

"Then hear the words of the Goddess."

Your reaction will change once you know what it says.

The messenger suppressed his anger and began to read.

"Cesare, son of Katzen—"

His voice rang through the hall.

"Wed my faithful daughter Rachel and bring peace to the land."

"This is my divine will. Whoever disobeys shall face judgment by my own hand."

The air froze.

Stunned silence descended upon the assembly.

No one could comprehend what they'd just heard.

Prince Cesare... and Saintess Rachel?

Did I hear that correctly?

But from what we just heard, Rachel was a criminal mere weeks ago!

And the First Prince already has a wife he adores!

Less than a month after announcing the Princess's pregnancy, an oracle like this?

How could the Goddess issue such a command?

Eyes turned involuntarily toward the First Prince.

Medea sat motionless, staring intently at the tabletop.

So. This is what you prepared.

A sigh escaped her lips before shock could settle.

Jason, I'm so tired of your selfishness.

He knew better than anyone the despair this oracle had caused her in her past life.

Yet he'd chosen the same tactic again—under the guise of reclaiming what he'd lost.

To justify his past betrayal by forcing her into the same situation.

This is why I could never accept you, Jason. You'll never understand.

At that moment, Medea felt Cesare's hand quietly cover hers beneath the table.

He enveloped her hand completely, holding it tight.

As if to tell her: Do not waver.

Even as they attempted to expose her deepest wound before the entire court, he reminded her that she was not alone.

That solid warmth spoke volumes.

A dry corner of her chest—long parched—felt like it had been kissed by sweet rain.

Yes. I should be celebrating.

If this was what Rachel and Jason had concocted together—

Then I have something prepared as well.

It was time to rejoice that the rats hiding in their holes had finally emerged and bared their teeth.

"Hmm!"

Meanwhile, the messenger savored the stunned silence of the court.

He prepared to recite the oracle again, triumphantly.

"Cesare, son of Katzen, wed my faithful daughter Rachel—Ugh!"

A flash of silver sliced through the air.

Shhhing—

A dagger grazed the corner of the messenger's mouth.

He clutched his torn cheek as blood poured between his fingers. The scroll tumbled to the floor.

"Th-this is madness!"

The messenger glared in the direction from which the blade had come.

If it had been a foot closer, the dagger would have pierced straight through his mouth.

"When you bark like a dog, you should expect to have your mouth torn open."

Cesare's voice was glacial.

"If only fools like you remain, the Holy Kingdom's fall is inevitable. Or did you forget I'm already married?"

His tone was more scathing than angry.

"Or have the so-called 'children of God' grown corrupt enough to openly advocate adultery?"

There was no way the Holy See could be ignorant of—or have forgotten—his marriage.

His wedding to Medea had taken place in their own Central Temple.

"B-but the oracle has been issued! The word of the Goddess takes precedence! The Goddess—"

The messenger clutched the bloodied scroll and shouted, his torn mouth garbling his words.

"Whether you're the Pope's dog or the Pope himself, listen carefully."

Cesare's voice dropped to a lethal whisper.

"If you recite that scrap of paper one more time, your neck will be the next thing torn open."

Every person in the hall straightened in their seats.

The air turned frigid, heavy with the promise of bloodshed.

Yet the messenger—oblivious to the killing intent radiating from Cesare—turned his fury toward Medea instead.

"The Saintess—the true Saintess! The wicked deeds of the First Princess have been exposed, and the Goddess has issued this oracle to correct her mistakes!"

"I see. Then words are pointless."

Cesare murmured softly.

Shing—

His sword slid free of its sheath, cutting through the air with a whisper of deadly intent.

Gods, even his draw is terrifyingly fast!

The messenger's face drained of color.

"E-even the Emperor of Valdina wouldn't dare kill an oracle's messenger! If you strike me down—"

"War? Is that your concern?"

Cesare's smile was vicious.

"Do you think I fear conflict with the Holy Kingdom and every faithful soul on the continent?"

"That sounds ideal."

He cut the messenger off mid-protest.

"It's a perfect excuse to crush everything."

He raised the tip of his blade slowly.

A dark, crackling aura gathered along the edge, rising toward the messenger's throat.

The messenger clutched the scroll to his chest, face chalk-white.

He squeezed his eyes shut as the silver blade descended in a straight arc toward him.

"First Prince."

Just as the gleaming sword was about to cleave through both messenger and scroll—

CRACK!

A crimson thunderbolt flashed through the air.
