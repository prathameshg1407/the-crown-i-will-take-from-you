"Some journeys lead toward the light. Others spiral ever deeper into darkness."

Whether she possessed the book or not, little would change.

Even during the years when she had loved him completely, the nights they shared had never been pleasant memories. Still, watching Madame Pinatelli's earnest concern, Medea swallowed a quiet laugh.

"Your Highness, you may not fully understand my meaning now, but in time, you will come to appreciate it..."

"Yes, Madame. Please don't trouble yourself on my account."

Medea nodded with practiced composure. She had no intention of taking the book with her, so she set it aside without a second glance.

"What is this?"

Yet perhaps the heavens recognized Madame Pinatelli's devotion, for the mysterious volume soon caught the attention of Medea's handmaidens.

The cover was exquisitely decorated with embroidered silk, and Neril—assuming it to be something of great value—opened it without hesitation.

She froze like a statue carved from wood.

"This... This is..."

Her face flushed crimson. It was not embarrassment that colored her cheeks, but something closer to outrage.

"Someone dared to bring such an improper thing into Her Highness's quarters!"

"Neril, what sort of book is it?"

Saya asked, craning her neck with curiosity.

"You... you don't need to know. If you open it, you'll regret it."

Neril shuddered and thrust the book away as though it had burned her fingers.

Saya's curiosity only deepened.

Judging by the silk binding, it appears to be something precious. But I shouldn't meddle without permission. I'll ask Her Highness about it later.

The conscientious young maid retrieved the book from where Neril had discarded it and placed it carefully among the luggage.

Then the chaos of departure preparations consumed her attention, and she forgot about it entirely.

And so Madame Pinatelli's instructional volume was quietly buried among Medea's belongings once more.

***

The following morning dawned bright and clear.

The Emperor of Valdina accompanied his sister to the border of the Holy Kingdom, unwilling to part with her a moment sooner than necessary.

"Dea. You must take care of yourself. If anything happens—anything at all—you must send word immediately."

"It's not as though we'll never see each other again."

Medea embraced her brother tightly.

"Peleus."

Blue eyes. Silver hair. The resemblance between them was striking—flesh and blood reflected in one another.

"I have fulfilled Father's wish to see Valdina prosper, and I have restored our mother's honor. Perhaps now... you can allow yourself a measure of freedom."

"..."

"I should have told you sooner."

Peleus turned to Menil, the vice-captain standing behind Medea, and gave a slight nod.

Menil immediately dropped to one knee.

"You need not worry, Your Majesty. We would sacrifice our very lives to protect Her Highness."

Peleus had wanted desperately to send D'Angel, the commander of the Agema, with his sister to the wolf-infested court of Katzen.

But Medea had refused absolutely, and so he had been forced to settle for the vice-commander instead.

"Menil still lacks refinement in his aura control. There is much he has yet to master."

"Would you have me bring Sir D'Angel and strip Valdina of its greatest protector?"

Would the ambitious Perdiccas II simply stand idle with a grandmaster of Valdina within his grasp?

Peleus swallowed his objections and nodded.

His blue gaze shifted to Cesare, who stood at a respectful distance, watching them.

Whenever Peleus considered the fact that the blood of Katzen's greedy emperor flowed through those veins, he felt the same chill of vigilance he experienced on the battlefield.

But he is also the man Dea chose.

He regarded his sister's judgment as sacrosanct. He had no choice but to trust her.

Peleus extended his hand.

Cesare paused—just for a moment—before accepting it.

"Thank you."

But despite his words, Peleus could not help the crushing force he applied to the grip. It was enough to make a lesser man cry out in pain.

He even clapped Cesare's shoulder in a show of camaraderie, leaning close to whisper:

"If you cause her even a moment of suffering, I will take her back to Valdina without hesitation."

He added, almost conversationally, that he had already begun considering candidates for a second brother-in-law—preferably someone more amenable, and certainly a native of his own kingdom.

"It seems you have someone in mind already."

Cesare's voice was languid, almost bored. But Peleus caught the faint spark that flickered in those golden eyes.

The grip that returned his own was equally punishing.

"Farewell, brother."

Cesare smiled without so much as a blink.

"Next time we meet, you'll be greeting your nephew."

Peleus's blue eyes turned to ice.

Cesare's smile only widened.

"What have you two been discussing for so long?"

Medea approached after completing her farewells with the others. Her eyebrows rose at the sight of their hands still clasped together.

"..."

They released each other as though burned, stepping apart in perfect unison.

They had worked together in pursuit of Rachel and her conspirators, and had seemed to develop something resembling rapport. But apparently, certain tensions remained.

"Your Highness, it is time to depart."

Medea nodded. As she moved toward the carriage, Cesare silently extended his hand to assist her.

The arm upon which she rested her fingers was steady and warm.

Rattle. Rattle.

The carriage wheels began their slow revolution.

Medea opened the window. The Valdinian delegation smiled and waved, their figures growing smaller with each passing moment.

Peleus stood motionless, watching the carriage recede into the brilliant sunlight.

He did not move from that spot until his sister's face had vanished entirely from the small carriage window.

The smile he had worn for her benefit had long since faded.

"God."

The word escaped him like an exhaled breath—impossible to tell whether it was prayer or lament.

"If You have truly chosen Dea as Your representative..."

"Please protect her."

Were it not for the imperial crown upon his head, those who witnessed him might have been unable to look away from the beautiful young sovereign, so clearly consumed by concern for the sister he had just released into an uncertain fate.

Central Shrine of the Holy Kingdom — The Dungeons

The scent of damp moss and stagnant water brushed against her nostrils. The pitch-black darkness pressed against her skin, swallowing her whole.

Then a torch flared to life before her eyes, tearing through her vision like a blade.

SPLASH!

Ice-cold water crashed down upon her head, shocking Rachel's consciousness into brutal wakefulness.

"Speak, witch."

Rachel forced her eyes open.

She could not tell how much time had passed.

How many times had the darkness receded, only to thrust her back into this frigid reality? How many nights had she endured?

One of her hands was grotesquely twisted, the fingers bent at unnatural angles. The other was bound in thick shackles inscribed with runes designed to suppress her magic.

"Bring me... the Princess Medea... and I will tell you everything..."

Yet when Rachel lifted her gaze, traces of her former beauty still lingered on her ravaged features.

It was this—this stubborn remnant of allure—that had prevented her interrogation from descending into true brutality. But Rachel felt no gratitude for the mercy.

"Do you think I don't understand your game? You're using me as bait to lure the warlocks into the open."

"So bring her. Hurry. While you waste time with your petty schemes, the warlocks are devouring this continent piece by piece."

Will you simply stand by and watch? Should you not be saving the world?

"Go back and tell your masters. I will speak only in the presence of Princess Medea..."

"Still clinging to your delusions, I see."

SPLASH!

The jailer seized another bucket and upended it over her head.

"Her Highness departed days ago. Do you still imagine she's waiting on your convenience?"

"What?"

Rachel's blood ran cold. She lunged forward, her ruined fingers clawing at the jailer's sleeve.

She had never imagined they would strip her of her final gambit so completely.

"What do you mean, departed? Where has she gone?"

"Where else? Katzen, of course! She is to become Empress!"

Rachel could not comprehend it.

"That's impossible. I'm still here..."

"Ha! What of it? Do you still believe yourself the Saintess we once revered? You're not even worthy to lift your eyes in her presence!"

The guard spat on the ground and kicked the iron grating with savage force. But Rachel seemed not to hear him, lost in the depths of her shock.

Her Highness is no longer here.

A voice echoed from the shadows behind them, low and cold, and the guard froze mid-motion.

"Her Highness has departed these lands."

Commander Leonor of the Knights Templar emerged from the darkness, clad in his distinctive cream-white armor.

"Lord Leonor..."

Rachel immediately arranged her features into an expression of pitiful vulnerability. Leonor had watched over her for so long—surely some vestige of feeling remained.

But when she met his eyes, she found only contempt.

"Sir Knight, surely you don't believe her lies? Have you truly been deceived by her schemes?"

Rachel's voice trembled artfully.

She lifted her head, taking in the filthy confines of her sealed cell—a space so small that not even a sliver of sky was visible.

She could not recall the last time she had seen sunlight.

By now, the carriage bearing Medea must be racing beneath a clear, open sky.

How is this fair? Why must I alone suffer this fate?

Bloodshot veins threaded through Rachel's eyes.

"Remove her. His Holiness has ordered her transfer to the obsidian cells."

"Yes, Commander."

The obsidian cells?

Rachel's empty murmur gave way to dawning horror as the implications settled over her.

Conditions even worse than this pit of misery awaited her.

The obsidian layer lay so deep within the central temple that even warlocks could not easily penetrate its defenses. If she was moved there...

If they abandon me...

She might rot in that abyss of absolute darkness for the remainder of her existence.

The realization that she had been driven into a corner from which there was no escape twisted Rachel's features into something barely human.
