Chapter 25

The Crown
Refused

"What good is a throne if it costs me everything I truly want?"

"You have come, Cesare."

The First Prince lowered himself before Perdiccas II, his posture impeccable despite the tension that hummed between them.

"I greet my father."

"How fares your shoulder?"

"It is nothing serious. The wound is superficial."

A lie, of course. The imperial physician had assessed the injury with grave concern—the flesh torn deep, the gash requiring weeks of treatment even with the aid of blessed waters.

Yet Perdiccas II's expression softened at his son's dismissive words.

From his elevated seat, the Emperor studied the man before him in silence.

Towering stature. Features so finely sculpted they might have been carved by a master's hand. Those striking golden eyes that seemed to pierce through pretense.

And that overwhelming presence—unapologetically dominant, making no effort to conceal its intent to command.

Even now, alone with his sovereign, Cesare betrayed not the slightest trace of deference or fear.

Once, that defiance would have ignited my fury. That fierce, insolent bearing—I would have seen it as a challenge to my authority.

But now...

Perhaps it is because I owe him my life. Hmph. How absurd.

With such formidable presence, Cesare could crush any rival power on the continent without breaking stride.

The realization brought an unexpected comfort.

Even Perdiccas II found himself startled by the shift in his own thinking.

"I am aware that I have been... difficult. That my treatment of you has been harsh."

Silence.

"However, you must understand—it was all for your benefit. For the good of this empire. Do you imagine I could allow weakness in the man who would one day sit upon the Katzen throne?"

Cesare went still. His head turned slowly, golden eyes fixing upon the Emperor with sudden, piercing focus.

He understood the implication hidden within those words.

"Indeed. I intend to announce the Crown Prince soon. You should inform your wife in advance."

Perdiccas II watched his son's frozen expression with quiet satisfaction—the contentment of an eagle that had cast its fledgling from the cliff, only to see it return with wings grown strong.

"...Why the change of heart?"

Once, such a direct question would have earned swift punishment. But the Emperor seemed to have expended his capacity for anger.

"Change? I have always had you in mind. Who in this imperial family resembles me more closely? Who is better suited to rule than you?"

The words rang hollow against the years of denial, the constant dismissal of every achievement, the endless suspicion.

"Once I rise from this sickbed, I shall formally proclaim you Crown Prince. There will be much to prepare. Above all, when you assume the position, you must care for your siblings."

Perdiccas II's voice took on a measured weight.

"They may be born of different mothers, but they share your blood. You cannot take their lives. Especially Juan... and the others as well."

"I fear I cannot accept Your Majesty's gracious offer at this time."

The First Prince's words fell like stones into still water.

Perdiccas II's expression hardened, color draining from his already pallid face.

"You... You dare speak to me in such archaic formality? As though I were some distant stranger rather than your father?"

"Are you refusing the throne?"

"Dangers still encircle Your Majesty. The political situation remains volatile. This is hardly the moment to name a successor."

A cold smile touched Cesare's lips.

"You speak of sparing the Third Prince's life."

Were our positions reversed, would Father have demanded the same of Juan? Would he have asked his favored son to spare the threat I represent?

Never. The very notion was laughable.

Cesare would forever remain the most formidable obstacle to any rival claimant.

"Even should I ascend to the position, am I to simply watch while living threats circle the throne rather than eliminate them? How can such a seat ever be called secure?"

The mention of Juan confirmed what Cesare had always suspected—that the Third Prince remained, even now, closest to the Emperor's heart.

"Perhaps... independence would serve me better than any crown."

"You insolent wretch! How dare you speak thus in my presence!"

Perdiccas II's face flushed crimson with fury. His trembling hand seized the staff beside his bed and hurled it with all his remaining strength.

It struck Cesare's wounded shoulder. The Emperor did not so much as blink.

"This is your nation! Your birthright! Your family!"

"I only wish you had said such words to those who spent years trying to kill me."

Even Cesare found himself bewildered by his own choice.

The moment he had pursued his entire life stood within arm's reach—and he was turning away from it.

To become Emperor of Katzen...

That may not be the path to keeping her.

If he claimed the throne now, while this chasm yawned between them...

No. He was certain of it. The moment he was crowned, she would leave.

"Once the contract is fulfilled, I shall depart without regret."

Her words echoed in his memory like a death knell.

"Father, focus your strength on recovery. This empire still requires you."

"You—you—!"

"I pray for Your Majesty's swift return to health."

Cesare turned and strode from the imperial chambers without looking back.

"My lord! What did His Majesty say?"

"Only that I should tend to my wounds."

The vassals must not learn that the Emperor offered me the Crown Prince position.

Not yet.

Behind him, the Emperor's furious bellowing echoed through the marble halls:

"Fool! Utter fool! Do you comprehend what opportunity you cast aside?!"

Someone watched the First Prince depart from the imperial palace, the Emperor's rage still reverberating in the distance.

"My apologies, Lord Eratosthenes. His Majesty has taken ill again. Today's audience must be rescheduled."

The elderly attendant emerged with a deep bow.

"It would appear the First Prince declined the Crown Prince position."

"How could you possibly know—"

"When I learned His Majesty had summoned the First Prince for a private audience, I suspected as much. His expression as he departed confirmed it."

Eratosthenes stroked his beard thoughtfully.

"The hunting competition must have exhausted His Majesty considerably."

The attendant nodded wearily.

"Indeed. His Majesty barely escaped that Giant Bear with his life. Nightmares plague him still—his recovery proceeds slowly."

"And the investigation into the assassination attempt?"

"The Commander of the Imperial Knights leads the inquiry, but thus far, no substantial leads have emerged."

Eratosthenes released a measured sigh.

"The current Commander only recently assumed his post—he will find thorough investigation difficult. His ties to the Saintess's faction further complicate matters."

He paused, as if struck by sudden inspiration.

"General Calvert has just returned to the capital following his tour of duty. Perhaps the investigation should be entrusted to him instead?"

The attendant's eyes brightened at the sound counsel. Amid the chaos of nursing the Emperor and managing governmental affairs, Eratosthenes's guidance fell like rain upon parched earth.

He departed with profuse gratitude.

"...Perdiccas. Your grip on life proves stubbornly tenacious."

Eratosthenes murmured softly, his voice carrying an edge that had been absent moments before.

From the folds of his robes, he produced a small glass vial. The dark liquid within caught the light like captured shadow.

"Useless now. The mutant demon's poison failed to finish the task."

He released the vial.

CRASH.

Glass shattered against marble. Black liquid pooled across the floor like spilled ink.

"It seems I shall have to act directly."

The First Prince's Palace

Sunset painted the sky in shades of dying fire as dinner was served.

Only silence filled the dining hall.

The sole sounds were the occasional clink of silver against porcelain—mechanical, rhythmic, utterly devoid of warmth.

"...A letter arrived from Sir Terrence."

When it became clear that the man across from her had no intention of speaking, Medea forced herself to break the silence.

"I shall see to it."

The reply came before she had even finished—flat, dismissive, final.

Once, he would have opened the letter himself, reading aloud Terrence's trivial reports from the Magic Tower in that low, warm voice of his.

Once, he would have ensured her favorite dishes found their way to her plate, watching with quiet satisfaction as she ate.

Now, Cesare would not even meet her eyes.

What in the world has happened between those two?

I haven't the faintest idea.

Even the servants exchanged bewildered glances, unsettled by the unprecedented frost between master and mistress.

Clatter.

Startled by the oppressive atmosphere, a maid hastily set down a serving dish and retreated as though fleeing.

When night fell, the silent war continued unabated.

Cesare strode directly to his private chamber—not the shared bedroom they had occupied since their marriage.

The door swung shut behind him.

The lock clicked into place.

That door had never once been closed between them. Not since their wedding night.

"Hah."

A short, hollow breath escaped Medea's lips.

She stared at the sealed door as though her gaze alone might force it open—as though, if she only looked hard enough, he would emerge and press a kiss to her cheek, murmuring that she had done well today, as he always did.

The door remained closed.

No kiss came. No whispered words of affection.

The ritual he had never once neglected—broken now without ceremony.

Darkness descended. The lamps were extinguished one by one.

Still, nothing changed.

Only when complete blackness had swallowed the world did the truth finally crystallize in Medea's mind.

He is pulling away from me.

The quiet realization crushed something deep within her chest.
