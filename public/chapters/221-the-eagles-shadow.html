Chapter 221

The Eagle's
Shadow

"The return of a strategist heralds storms yet unseen."

"Your Majesty, I have already made my decision—"

"No. I will not permit your departure."

The Emperor's voice rang with an authority that brooked no argument.

"Your title, your estates, everything you require—if there is anything you lack, I shall provide it myself. This empire needs you. I need you."

He summoned the chamberlain with a sharp gesture, commanding that all of Eratosthenes' possessions be retrieved from his estate and the monastery without delay.

Faced with such relentless insistence, Eratosthenes found himself outmaneuvered. A quiet sigh escaped his lips—the sound of a man accepting an outcome he had not desired.

"If my presence can be of service to Your Majesty, even briefly, then I shall remain."

He offered a final bow before departing the Golden Hall, his farewell carrying the unmistakable weight of a man who did not intend to linger at court for long.

Yet this studied indifference—this refusal to be moved even by imperial entreaty—only deepened Perdiccas II's determination.

He hesitates because of the scrutiny, nothing more. Penterus has always been cautious about drawing attention.

The Emperor remained motionless, his gaze fixed upon the doors through which Eratosthenes had vanished, turning over possibilities in his mind. At last, he nodded slowly, as though reaching an internal accord.

A man of his caliber cannot be permitted to return in such diminished circumstances. For the sake of my honor—and the Empress's legacy—he must be elevated.

"Chamberlain. Elevate Penterus to the rank of Marquis."

"...As Your Majesty commands."

The chief chamberlain, who had observed the exchange in careful silence, could not entirely conceal his shock.

Remarkable. His Majesty once regarded this man with the same wariness he reserves for Reniar. And now, with a single audience, all those years of suspicion have evaporated.

Perdiccas II had once permitted Eratosthenes to retire, fearing that a father devastated by his daughter's death might harbor thoughts of vengeance against the throne.

Yet the Emperor who stood before him now—no longer the brilliant sovereign of his youth, but an aging ruler haunted by paranoia, convinced that enemies lurked in every shadow—had welcomed Eratosthenes back without a moment's hesitation.

This will be the most spectacular return the court has witnessed in a decade. And the consequences...

The chamberlain could not begin to predict how this resurrection would reshape the balance of power. He knew only that fiercer battles lay ahead.

Oblivious to his servant's private calculations, Perdiccas II hummed a few bars of an old military hymn—a habit from younger, more victorious days.

"Chamberlain, prepare a royal decree for the Princess of Valdina. As her guardian, I shall guarantee her treatment equivalent to that of the Katzen Imperial Family. She shall receive immunity and the full protection of the Crown throughout the empire."

It was unprecedented. In all of Katzen's storied history, no foreign princess had ever been granted such privileges—protections that even imperial officials could not claim.

But if Valdina suspected the Emperor of attempting to bury the incident beneath a veneer of ceremonial honor, as Eratosthenes had warned, then the terms must be extraordinary enough to silence objection.

"Now then—what suitable gift might we bestow upon Valdina's woman? Perhaps something from the treasury reports..."

For the first time in weeks, the Emperor's mood had lifted. A long-festering problem resolved. His most trusted strategist restored to his side.

Then the image of the Valdinian Emperor surfaced in his mind, and his expression curdled.

"No. I will not waste my personal possessions on that wretched bloodline."

He drummed his fingers against the arm of his throne, considering.

"Let us search the late Emperor's legacy instead. When Raju returns with the inventory—yes, that will suffice. Such faded glories are precisely what Valdina deserves."

Silence answered him.

The Emperor, still lost in pleasant contemplation, raised his head.

"Why do you not respond? Is the matter too burdensome?"

"Not at all, Your Majesty. However..."

The chamberlain hesitated.

"There is a matter I must bring to your attention regarding the late Emperor's legacy—the assets Raju has disclosed."

The Emperor's expression darkened at once.

"Is this something so grave it will spoil the first moment of pleasure I have enjoyed in months?"

His voice had acquired an edge—the tone of a blade being drawn.

Others might have faltered beneath that glare. But the chief chamberlain, seasoned by decades of imperial service, reached into his robes and produced a bundle of parchments without hesitation.

"This intelligence was secretly conveyed to us by Raju's goddaughter. It contains the true sources and locations of the late Emperor's hidden funds."

"He found them all?"

Perdiccas II's eyes widened. A flash of naked avarice lit his features.

In his petition for repatriation, Raju had mentioned the existence of the late Emperor's secret fortune only in passing—deliberately vague, deliberately tantalizing. The precise locations and specifics had been withheld, no doubt as insurance against being disposed of once his usefulness ended.

The Emperor snatched the documents and began reading immediately, his sharp eyes devouring each line.

"Thirty thousand seom of grain from the northern provinces... five hundred thousand gold from southern salt revenues... one hundred thousand gold in secret subsidies according to undisclosed agreements..."

"Your Majesty will observe that these figures differ entirely from Raju's official report. He disclosed only the ledgers His late Majesty personally maintained."

The chamberlain's voice carried the measured urgency of a man delivering catastrophic news.

"Upon investigation, we discovered that the inheritance had already been recovered and subsequently vanished. It appears Raju liquidated the assets and laundered them as personal funds."

"What?"

A thick vein pulsed across the Emperor's forehead. His mouth twisted into something feral, and his beard trembled with barely contained fury.

"Until recently, there were indications of massive expenditures—smuggling operations, extravagant living expenses. It seems Raju intended to present falsified ledgers to conceal his embezzlement."

"This disgusting thief deserves to be torn apart!"

CLANG—!

Objects crashed against marble as the Emperor swept everything within reach from the table.

"How dare he mock me! How dare he steal what is rightfully mine! I will tear him limb from limb!"

"Your Majesty, my assessment may have been hasty given the limited time. Perhaps we should summon Raju here for a thorough interrogation—"

"No."

The Emperor's voice had gone cold—dangerously, terrifyingly cold.

"Dispatch a messenger immediately. Five hundred lashes."

The shredded parchments drifted to the floor like dying leaves.

My instincts were correct. That creature never recognized me as his master.

But learning that Raju had not merely been a subordinate—that he had been trusted enough to oversee secret imperial funds—transformed suspicion into certainty.

Why reveal this only now? Why conceal it until the very precipice of death?

The answer was obvious to Perdiccas II's fevered mind.

That treacherous wretch was waiting for an opportunity to strike from behind.

The hidden fortune Raju's goddaughter had exposed was staggering in its scope. Had those resources fallen into the wrong hands, they could have funded entire rebellions, purchased armies, reshaped the political landscape of the empire itself.

That such power had been stolen from him—by a minor functionary, a lackey from an insignificant vassal state—was an indignity that burned like acid.

Perdiccas II's lips curled into something that was not quite a smile.

"Do not cease the whipping even if he perishes before the count is complete. The punishment shall not end until his body is utterly destroyed."

Ice beneath the inferno of his rage.

"I will not permit even the soul of that filthy traitor to touch my soil!"

✦ ✦ ✦

At the very hour when Perdiccas II confronted the bitter truth of his betrayal, Marquis Reniar—who had departed the Imperial Palace one step ahead—was preparing to face shadows of his own.

A profound stillness hung over the pavilion behind the palace gardens, the kind of silence that precedes storms.

The Marquis stood motionless at the center of the arched bridge, his fingers moving slowly, almost absently, to the ring upon his hand.

At its center, wrought in gleaming gold, an eagle's head emerged from a tangle of thorny vines—predator and prisoner both.

The eagle's eyes were rubies, and in the afternoon light, they seemed to smolder with captured fire.

Lost in thought, Reniar turned the ring slowly, his gaze drifting across the mirror-still surface of the lake beyond. The water lay calm and undisturbed; in the distance, a magpie's sharp cry pierced the silence.

His expression betrayed nothing. But beneath the concealing drape of his sleeves, his fingers pressed against the gold with a force that spoke of suppressed tension—a tremor he was desperate to hide.

He curled his hands deeper into the fabric, steadying himself.

Then, from the far end of the bridge, came the measured sound of approaching footsteps.

Eratosthenes emerged into view, his pace unhurried, his bearing that of a man who had walked through palaces and battlefields alike and found neither particularly intimidating.

The two men's eyes met across the span of weathered stone.

Neither spoke.

The magpie cried out once more, and somewhere beneath the still waters, currents that had long lain dormant began, at last, to stir.
