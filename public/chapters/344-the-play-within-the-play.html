Chapter 344

The Play
Within the Play

"Sometimes the cruelest truths are told in the guise of fiction."

"I will defeat the Black Dragon and save the world from chaos!"

The tale began with the appearance of a hero clad in gleaming armor.

"I will help you. Together, with our light, we shall bring the dawn to this world once more."

Then came the beautiful Queen.

The two fell deeply in love.

The Queen aided the hero in countless ways, and together they succeeded in defeating the Black Dragon's armies and banishing the darkness.

"Queen of the Dawn... I'm sorry. But now, the light must be mine alone."

Yet the hero, blinded by ambition, betrayed the Queen.

He sought to claim the Flame of Dawn for himself.

"Oh my God! How terrible!"

Gasps rippled through the audience as the masked actors delivered their passionate performance.

Jason sat among them, his face an expressionless mask.

"Hero, you were my everything!"

His crimson eyes remained utterly still.

The play surged toward its climax.

"How can this be? Even beasts do not harm their own young!"

On stage, the actress portraying the Queen screamed, cradling a lifeless child in her arms.

Then she raised her sword to the heavens.

"Everything you loved will betray you! Even death will never accept you!"

The final curse upon the traitorous hero.

At that very moment—

Thud.

"Grand Duke Castullo?"

Jason bolted upright from his seat.

"Are you well? Your face—"

The nobleman beside him stared in alarm, but Jason fled the theater without a word.

Behind him, Medea rose quietly from her seat.

◆◆◆

Through the black veil draped over her face, Medea watched the stage with an expression devoid of emotion.

What unfolded before the audience was the story of her past life—rewritten and adapted by her own hand.

Watching Jason flee as though pursued by demons, Medea stood.

"First Princess, where are you going?"

Princess Angelique's curious voice broke the silence.

"I need some air. I'm feeling a bit faint."

"Ah, of course. I was the same when I carried Angelique. Even sitting still made me drowsy."

The Eastern Empress stepped forward graciously, smoothing the way for Medea's departure.

"You needn't follow me."

Medea addressed the maids who moved to accompany her.

"But Your Highness's safety—"

"This is my villa, not foreign territory. Who here would dare harm me?"

Dismissing her attendants, Medea walked alone down the corridor.

As she moved farther from the outdoor theater, the din of voices and music gradually faded.

Now only the click of her footsteps and the occasional whisper of her gown against marble echoed through the hall.

Then—someone blocked her path.

A shadow fell across her.

Medea halted.

The shadow made no move to step aside.

Slowly, Medea lifted the black veil.

"Grand Duke Castullo."

Jason stood before her.

"That play—you staged it for me, didn't you?"

His voice was low, but fury simmered beneath each syllable.

"I'm glad you understood."

"How could I not?"

A ragged sound—thick with suppressed emotion—tore from his throat.

"Do you want to mock me? To humiliate me? Is that what you need, Medea? Then go ahead. If that's how you vent your rage, I'll endure it as many times as you wish."

His crimson eyes glowed with dangerous intensity.

"The moment I heard he was dead, I ran to the Iceberg like a madman."

His voice twisted with bitterness.

"And you found him. You saved his damned life. That's so like you, Medea."

Silence.

"Cesare isn't anything special. Without you, he'd have been just another frozen corpse buried in the snow."

He laughed—a hollow, broken sound.

"No—he was already dying. Terminally ill. He should have died. But you saved him, Dea. You brought him back to life."

His laughter bordered on sobbing.

"But even that is nothing, isn't it?"

Jason's smile was fragile as glass.

"Compared to what you did for me, it's barely the tip of the iceberg."

"Stop talking nonsense."

"Nonsense? Yes, perhaps I am going mad."

How could I be sane?

He murmured the words like a confession.

"You did things no one else on this continent could accomplish. Only for me."

Jason's gaze grew fevered.

"I can't forget it. I still feel your hands, your touch... like a brand on my skin."

"Will you admit it now? After spending years stealing my achievements and parading as the hero?"

Despite Medea's pointed accusation, Jason didn't flinch.

"Yes. Because you loved me then."

He took a step closer.

His shadow swallowed her feet.

The clink of metal rang harshly in the silence.

"Even now, I'm the one you love more."

Medea's expression darkened. She stepped back, putting distance between them.

"You're delusional."

"When you chose him, it wasn't what you felt for me. Isn't that right?"

Medea stared at him with open disgust—a look that said even answering him was a waste of breath.

But Jason pressed on.

"He can never erase the marks I left on you. Dea, you'll carry me for the rest of your life. That's both my punishment and my salvation."

His eyes blazed crimson.

"I used to regret hurting you with every breath. But now? Now I'm almost glad for it. Because I remain etched into you. In your pain, I still exist."

There was contempt and twisted joy mingled in his voice—a man comforting himself with his own cruelty.

A heated hand seized Medea's wrist.

His gaze swept over the back of her hand.

"Do you think you deserve to touch me?"

"Of course not."

Jason's lips twisted into a bitter smile.

"Marriage is all you can manage with him."

His gaze dropped to Medea's stomach, then rose again.

"Dea, you and I had children. Can Cesare ever surpass what we shared? Lian and Leah—"

"Jason."

Her voice fell like a blade of ice.

Medea wrenched her wrist free.

"If you had even a shred of conscience, you wouldn't dare speak their names."

Jason heard the lethal edge hidden beneath her calm tone.

"It's a pity you couldn't carve out my heart, Medea."

He sneered.

"Yes."

Medea's agreement cut through him like a knife.

"It's unbearable that you still draw breath."

Jason's face contorted.

"Medea, you've stripped me of honor, power, authority—everything I had in this life. But you didn't kill me the way you destroyed the Claudios. Do you know what that means?"

He grabbed her shoulders.

"It means you still have feelings for me. It's proof that our love can never be erased."

Silence.

"You haven't forgotten me!"

"What should I do about that?"

Medea stood as immovable as a cliff against crashing waves.

"What I shared with you wasn't love."

She laughed—cold and humorless.

"Don't deny it, Medea."

"You're the one in denial. Jason, what I felt for you wasn't love. It was charity."

Medea shook her head.

"What I gave you, what I shared, what I wanted to offer—the world calls that pity."

"Liar! That's your hypocrisy talking! I'll admit it—I may have changed, but I never hid that I loved you! But you—"

Jason's eyes blazed.

"I didn't understand until I met Cesare. I didn't know what true affection was. What real love meant."

"Medea!"

He looked ready to explode.

His lips trembled. Veins bulged on the backs of his clenched fists.

"Cesare taught me. Step by step."

Medea exhaled slowly.

"Why are you so upset, Jason? You're the one who used me under the guise of love."

End Chapter 344
