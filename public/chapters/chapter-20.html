The Pawns
Advance

"A pawn is worthless—until it reaches the end."

— The Queen's Palace —

"Are you defying me?" The Queen Mother's voice climbed into a shrill pitch. "Must I beat the truth out of you?"

"If you punish me, I will endure it," Medea replied evenly. "But I will not confess to a lie simply to avoid pain."

Her voice did not waver.

She knelt straight-backed, hands folded, eyes clear. No tears. No trembling. No desperation.

The court murmured.

If she were guilty… could she really stand like that?

Unease crept into the room, thin and poisonous.

"It is strange, Grandmother," Medea continued, almost mildly. "Madame Quiggin was disciplined by me for violating Royal Law. Maria was dismissed for misconduct."

She turned her head slightly, acknowledging them without facing them.

"Is it coincidence that the only two people accusing me are the only two people I have recently punished?"

A ripple moved through the nobles.

Medea lifted her gaze to the throne.

"Are they speaking the truth," she asked calmly, "or are they borrowing your anger to exact revenge?"

The Queen Mother faltered.

Her eyes shifted—Quiggin, pale and sweating. Maria, lips pressed too tightly together.

"Oh no!" Quiggin cried, her composure cracking. "Your Majesty, do not be deceived! She twists words like a snake!"

"We—we have proof!" Maria blurted, panic edging her voice.

— The Prime Minister's Office —

Steam curled from a cup of black tea, the scent sharp and grounding.

Siseo
"So," Siseo said, leaning back, fingers steepled. "When are you going to stop lying to me and tell me why you're really here?"

Cesare stood at the window, sunlight splintering across the silver half-mask. He traced a dust mote in the air with a gloved finger.

"Let's say I enjoy Valdina's climate."

"You've gone mad," Siseo muttered. "War rot finally reached your brain."

"Do you believe I lack the soul to appreciate beauty?"

"If you had a soul," Siseo shot back, "you would have died at the Tower of the Midnight Sun."

From the fireplace, Gallo snickered. "Boss has a soul. It's just… small. And very black."

The Tower of the Midnight Sun.
A place that did not forgive weakness.

They had all survived it—Siseo, Gallo, Cesare.

Only one had walked out a ghost.

"Are you ever going to take that thing off?" Siseo asked, nodding at the mask.

"No."

"Inefficient."

"Mystery sells," Cesare said lazily. "Besides, Gallo is the pretty one."

"Hey!"

"Shut up, Gallo."

Siseo exhaled slowly. Nostalgia was a luxury.

"Your mercenaries are everywhere," he said. "Explain why Fasade is nesting in my capital during a stalemate."

"And you're explaining why you're letting it happen."

Siseo grimaced. "Fine. I need your support. If you take the rear guard, we end the war before spring."

"Valdina can't afford me."

"I know."

"And friendship doesn't pay."

"Then kill me," Siseo said flatly. "If my head buys the kingdom time, take it."

Cesare stared.

Then laughed—a short, cutting sound.

"You always back losing causes, Siseo."

"I serve the King."

"Stubborn."

Cesare stepped closer, voice dropping.

"You want out from under Kazen's boot? Kill the delegation."

Siseo froze. "What?"

"The Imperial envoys arriving tomorrow. Slaughter them. Send their heads back."

"That's insanity!"

"You are already dying slowly," Cesare replied. "This would be fast—and loud."

"The delegation includes the Imperial Princess and the Grand Duke!"

"Then don't touch them." Cesare shrugged. "Kill the First Prince instead."

Siseo stared at him.

"The Demon of the South?"

Cesare smiled behind the mask.

"Everyone bleeds."

"—Cough—" Gallo choked violently, eyes bulging. You lunatic. That's you.

— Interruption —

Crack.
The doors burst open.

"Your Excellency!" a clerk stumbled in, breathless. "Emergency report! Princess Medea has been accused of treason by the Head Maid!"

The tea cup rattled on Siseo's desk.

Cesare went very still.

"…Treason?" Siseo repeated.

Behind the mask, Cesare's smile sharpened.

So, he thought.
The pawn reaches the end of the board.

The game had begun.
