The Salon

"The light wasn't admiration. It was judgment."

"It will shine so brilliantly under the chandeliers," Medea whispered, her voice conspiratorial. "Everyone's eyes will be on you."

Birna's fingers tightened on the hem of the dress.

Her mother had been clear. Make sure Medea wears it.

But the diamonds glittered like captured lightning.

"I'll explain it to Aunt Catherine," Medea coaxed. "It would be such a waste to leave it in a box. Besides… silver suits your hair far better than mine."

That was the final push.

Birna lifted her chin.

"Fine. Since you insist, Sister. I'll wear it."

The diamond dust scratched her fingers as she lifted the skirt, rough and heavy—but intoxicating.

Minutes later, she stood before the mirror.

"Oh my—"

"You're dazzling."

"Blinding."

The maids stared.

Birna wasn't satisfied.

She reached for more.

A diamond tiara.

Sapphire earrings.

Pearl bracelets layered thick along her wrists.

She became excessive by design—a walking vault.

More, she thought, breath shallow with excitement. I need to outshine everyone. Especially her.

She glanced sideways.

Medea now stood in plain black satin. No embroidery. No jewels.

A shadow beside a star.

Perfect.

"How do you feel?" Medea asked gently.

"Like I was born for this," Birna said, grinning.

"You'll be the center of attention," Medea promised.

The Carriage

As they stepped outside, Birna paused.

"We're going to the Palace," she said casually. "Not the Duke's estate."

Medea frowned. "The Palace? I wasn't informed."

"It's a surprise," Birna said smoothly, looping her arm through Medea's.

Her grip tightened—warning, not affection.

Don't ruin this, her fingers said.

"Of course not," Medea replied softly. "Let us go."

The Royal Banquet Hall

The doors opened.

Gasps rippled through the hall.

It was obscene.

Ice sculptures rimmed in gold wept onto marble floors.

Towers of champagne glittered under crystal chandeliers.

Thousands of imported flowers drowned the air in perfume.

Even the pastries were dusted with gold leaf.

"This is grotesque," a noble whispered.

"My village froze last winter," another muttered. "And we're eating gold?"

The Queen Mother stiffened.

"Did Medea request this?" she whispered.

"The Minister of the Court says so," Pinatelli replied carefully.

Across the hall, Catherine watched the murmurs spread.

Good, she thought. Hate her.

Montega Arrives

"Come here," the Regent murmured, beckoning Catherine.

Beside him stood a broad-shouldered man in military dress, his face weathered and severe.

"Margrave Montega," Catherine said, curtsying. "Welcome back to the capital."

Montega's eyes swept the hall—cold, measuring.

"Is there justification for this excess?" he asked bluntly. "I rode here from the border. The people are starving."

Catherine dabbed her eyes.

"It wasn't our wish. But how could we refuse the Princess?"

She leaned closer, voice hushed.

"Medea insisted. Said she needed to celebrate her recovery. The Queen Mother indulges her too much…"

The Regent sighed theatrically.

"She's young. She doesn't understand money."

Montega said nothing—but his gaze hardened.

The Entrance

The herald's voice thundered.

"Her Royal Highness, Princess Medea Casey de Valdina!
And Lady Birna Robin Claudio!"

The doors swung open.

Light exploded.

Birna strode forward, half a step ahead of everyone else. Diamonds shattered the chandeliers' glow into rainbows. Jewelry chimed with every movement. She radiated wealth—aggressive, unapologetic.

Behind her walked a girl in black.

Quiet.

Composed.

Royal.

Catherine's fan slipped from her hand.

Why is Birna wearing that?!

Birna mistook the stunned silence for awe. She lifted her chin higher.

Look at me, her smile said. I am the Princess.

Montega stepped forward and bowed stiffly—to Birna.

"Her Royal Highness, Princess Medea," he said. "Montega has returned from the frontier to greet you."

Birna froze.

"Excuse me?"

"Birna!" Catherine shrieked, rushing forward.

Montega straightened slowly, eyes flicking between faces.

"If this is not Her Highness," he said coldly, "then who is she?"

Catherine's mouth opened. Closed.

"This—this is my daughter. They look alike—"

"Alike?" Montega cut in sharply.

"I see a girl walking ahead of Royal Blood. Wearing a fortune. Acting as though she owns this palace."

His gaze turned openly contemptuous.

"So this is the 'Princess' who demanded this banquet. It seems you were mistaken about which girl was making demands."

The room went deathly silent.

Catherine felt the floor vanish beneath her.

The Kill Shot

"Please don't blame her."

Medea's voice was calm.

She stepped forward from the shadows. Black silk. No jewels. No apology.

She placed a gentle hand on Birna's trembling shoulder.

"Birna was only helping me," Medea said softly. "I couldn't wear the dress. My body is still weak—it was too heavy. She kindly offered to wear it for me."

She looked at Montega with a sad, forgiving smile.

"Please don't be angry. My cousin only wanted to help."

Every word was reasonable.

Every word was lethal.

Birna finally understood.

The light wasn't admiration.

It was judgment.

And it was all on her.
