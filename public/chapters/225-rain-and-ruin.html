Chapter 225

Rain and
Ruin

"Some loves are written in blood, and end in it."

As if echoing the fury of the crowd, the crack of the whip tore through the air once more—a sound like flesh being rent from bone.

The bright sky that had burned Raju's eyes only moments before had turned the color of bruises. Storm clouds gathered with unnatural speed.

Patter... patter...

The rain began to fall.

Water struck the stone floor, mixing with the dark blood that pooled there, spreading in slick, gleaming streams across the weathered flagstones.

"Ngh... ugh..."

With each stroke of the lash, Raju's body lurched forward, convulsing. His screams had become shrieks—inhuman sounds torn from a throat too ravaged to form words. The iron-studded leather carved trenches through his flesh, and blood sprayed in arcs that painted the scaffold red.

It was impossible to tell whether the screaming came from the condemned man or from the crowd that watched.

"STOP! NO MORE!"

Catherine burst through the crowd, tears streaming down her face.

She paid no heed to the hostile eyes that followed her—hundreds of gazes sharp enough to flay skin. The crowd's fury was palpable, a living thing that pressed against her from all sides.

"Mother—!"

Samon reached for her, but his fingers closed on empty air. Catherine's dress slipped through his grasp as she threw herself forward.

She shoved past the guards with desperate, flailing arms, scrambling up onto the scaffold in a single, frantic surge.

And then she flung herself over Raju's body, wrapping her arms around his ruined back.

CRACK—!

The executioner's arm was already in motion. The whip descended before anyone could stop it, striking Catherine squarely across the shoulders.

"AAH—!"

"Cath... Cather... go... go back..."

Her scream pierced through the fog of agony that had swallowed Raju's mind. He shook his bloodied head, trying to form words through lips that had been bitten raw.

Catherine. My love. She has to be safe. She has to be far away from this.

"No! I will not leave you—not again!"

Catherine seized Raju's hand with fierce determination, her grip like iron.

Then she raised her head and fixed the messenger and executioners with a gaze of pure, burning hatred.

"Kill me as well! You craven dogs of the empire! Do you think your whips frighten me?"

Her voice rose to a shriek—the cry of a woman who had passed beyond fear.

"What could I possibly have left to lose? I gave birth to a monster and murdered my own husband! There is no filth left that can touch me!"

The desperate howl of a half-mad woman echoed across the silent square.

No one moved to stop her.

"What should we do?"

The chief executioner turned to Katzen's messenger, uncertainty written across his face.

The messenger's response was immediate—and utterly indifferent.

"She wishes to die with him? Then grant her wish. Continue!"

He had no patience for vulgar scandals—illegitimate daughters, adulterous lovers, the petty melodramas of lesser people.

"All of you—take up your whips! The full five hundred lashes must be delivered today. We cannot afford delays for such trifles!"

For he carried an imperial decree from Perdiccas II himself—a matter of far greater importance than any single execution.

"Mother! NO—!"

Samon dragged his injured body toward the scaffold, reaching for Catherine with desperate hands.

A bailiff's fist caught him across the jaw. He crashed to the ground and was kicked aside without mercy.

The messenger had no intention of halting the execution a second time.

CRACK—!

The executioners' whips fell upon Catherine's back without hesitation.

"Ah... Catherine... NGHHHH—!"

In his lover's arms, Raju released an animal howl of anguish. But he could neither flee nor shield her from the storm of leather and iron that rained down upon them both.

Catherine gripped his hand with all her remaining strength, as if to say: It's all right. I'm here.

Her face was a tapestry of agony, despair, and love—all three tangled beyond separation.

The lashes fell in a merciless rhythm.

The two lovers held each other, and slowly, their eyes closed.

Blood and rain spread together across the cold stone, seeping into every crack and crevice.

The rain grew heavier.

In the breathless moments that followed—

"...They're dead."

The executioners lowered their whips and stepped back.

"What now? The count isn't finished."

Their eyes fell upon the two motionless bodies—intertwined in death as they had been in life, as though they had sought to remain together for a hundred lifetimes.

"Ugh... what is this? Some kind of eternal love story?"

"Putting on a romantic display even at the moment of death. What a waste of everyone's time."

To most observers, it was merely another shocking, sordid execution—nothing more.

The messenger spat upon the scaffold.

"Enough. We end it here. I refuse to waste another moment on a criminal who deceived the imperial family."

He gestured sharply to his attendants.

"We must proceed to the villa immediately. Make ready!"

"A moment—the villa? You have further business at the palace?"

A Valdinian knight stepped forward, his eyes sharp with suspicion.

The messenger's gaze flickered to the royal crest emblazoned on the knight's breastplate. His demeanor shifted instantly—the irritation vanishing behind a mask of diplomatic courtesy.

"Ahem. Yes, indeed."

He touched the crimson rose emblem upon his own robes with theatrical gravity.

"His Imperial Majesty has entrusted me with a matter of considerable importance."

With a flourish, the messenger produced a second scroll from within his robes.

This one was different from the execution order—crafted of stiff, antiqued parchment, its edges gilded with gold leaf. It bore all the hallmarks of imperial ceremony.

"I bear a special letter from His Imperial Majesty to the Princess of Valdina."

He turned slightly toward the crowd, raising his voice to carry across the entire square.

"His Majesty expresses his profound regret for the... misunderstandings and unfortunate incidents that have occurred between our nations. Furthermore, he declares his noble intention to welcome Her Highness as an honored member of the Katzen Imperial Family—and to assume personal guardianship over her person."

"Allow me to clarify the significance of this decree!"

The messenger paused dramatically, clearing his throat with exaggerated gravity.

"Her Highness the Princess has become the most exalted personage not merely in this kingdom—but upon the entire continent. As a member of both the Katzen and Valdina royal houses, she now possesses dignity and authority without parallel."

His voice rose to fill every corner of the square.

"Furthermore, this decree grants Her Highness complete immunity from prosecution for any offense committed anywhere within Katzen's borders—and the extraordinary privilege of standing before His Imperial Majesty without kneeling!"

"This is a decision without precedent in all of Katzen's history! Her Highness shall enjoy the Empire's protection wherever she travels upon this continent!"

"Oh!"

The crowd's mood shifted with startling speed. Their bloodlust gave way to curiosity, and curiosity to grudging admiration.

"So the arrogant Emperor himself is bowing down to become our Princess's guardian?"

The interpretation was not quite what the messenger had intended—but it spread through the crowd like wildfire.

"Of course! Look at all these conditions he's attaching! He must be desperate to resolve the border disputes as quickly as possible!"

"If Her Highness accepts, no Katzen noble will ever be permitted to look down upon her again! How could they, when their own Emperor has sworn to protect her?"

A strange excitement rippled through the assembled Valdinians—pride swelling in hearts long accustomed to Katzen's oppression.

Their Princess, recognized as the most exalted being on the continent. There was a sweet taste of revenge in that.

"Ah! So that's why he arrived in such a magnificent carriage—to curry favor with Her Highness!"

The ostentatious vehicle that had irritated them before now seemed a fitting tribute.

The messenger felt a flicker of unease at the crowd's interpretation—it did not quite align with his master's intentions. But no matter. The important thing was to create an atmosphere in which the Valdina royal family would find it impossible to refuse.

"Given the weight of my responsibilities, I must beg your forgiveness for departing at once."

The Katzen carriage departed with the same magnificent ceremony in which it had arrived.

"Long live Valdina!"

"Long live Her Highness the Princess!"

"Let every Katzen dog kneel before us now!"

The square erupted in fervent celebration. The light rain that continued to fall seemed insignificant beside the heat of the crowd's newfound fervor.

All except one corner of the square.

Where the Claudio family stood.

Birna stared at the scaffold with empty eyes.

The messenger's proclamation had passed through her ears without leaving any impression—mere noise, meaningless sound. Her gaze was fixed upon the tangled mass of red that had once been two human beings.

She took an unsteady step backward, her pupils trembling, her lips drained of color.

Through the white fog that had swallowed her thoughts, a familiar figure came into focus.

Gray hair. Cloudy blue eyes. Her brother.

"Sa... Samon..."

Her hand reached out—trembling, desperate.

She needed him. The only family she had left. Someone to anchor her against the storm of horrors that threatened to sweep her away.

CRACK—!

Burning pain shot through her arm—as though her flesh had been seared by fire.

"Don't touch me."

Samon's voice was ice.

"You filthy creature."

He stared down at Birna with undisguised contempt.

All those years. All those years I believed she was my sister—my blood. And she was nothing but a commoner's bastard the entire time.

A thousand fires blazed within him.
