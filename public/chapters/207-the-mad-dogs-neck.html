Chapter 7

The Mad Dog's
Neck

"When questions linger and threats escalate—
Valdina bares its teeth."

Leonor's voice carried a subtle warning as Theo's expression darkened.

"Valdina is clearly attempting to bury the forced marriage angle and emphasize only the assassination attempt—to protect the Princess's reputation. Don't go spreading rumors that contradict their narrative. Understood, Theo?"

"...Yes, Commander."

Theo nodded, his wavering resolve briefly steadied by Leonor's counsel.

The young knight's face quickly regained its customary composure.

"Still... something doesn't add up. They're saying the First Prince killed Jared..."

Leonor tilted his head, lips twisting with faint doubt.

The autopsy had revealed extensive injuries: a severed hand, stab wounds to chest and shoulder, strangulation marks on the neck. Clearly, Jared had died after a vicious struggle.

But—

"The physician who examined the body reported that the man's internal organs were burned beyond recognition. Blackened, charred—like kindling reduced to ash at the slightest touch."

"Burned organs? Did the First Prince use fire magic?"

"That's uncertain. Even the most sophisticated pyromancy cannot char the interior so thoroughly while leaving the exterior intact."

Moreover, the assassins who'd attacked Emperor Peleus were equally suspicious.

The traces and auras they'd left behind were unlike anything documented on the continent.

"It was dark. Malevolent. Ancient—as though it belonged to the primordial age."

But that made no sense.

The primordial darkness was extinguished millennia ago beneath the Goddess's light.

Yet Leonor's instincts—honed through years of service to the divine—whispered warnings.

I need to investigate further. How was the disgraced General Jared connected to... them?

"Commander... is it possible this was divine punishment?"

A subordinate knight spoke hesitantly.

"Divine punishment?"

"It's in the Old Testament myths. A tale of a wicked man struck by invisible lightning—punishment from the Goddess herself. His body burned black from within."

"That's mere legend. Stories from thousands of years past—likely apocryphal."

"True. I only mentioned it because... the similarity seemed striking."

Leonor nodded absently, his gaze drifting back toward the square and its grim trophies.

The questions gnawing at him faded into the cacophony of the crowd.

* * *

Word of the corpses hanging in the square spread like wildfire through the capital.

Valdinian rage simmered, voices of condemnation directed at Katzen echoing even beyond the villa walls.

"This is excessive! Without any attempt at negotiation, you've slandered us before the populace and displayed our torn national banner. This is an egregious insult!"

The Katzen delegation protested vehemently.

"Excessive?"

Sissair's tone was desert-dry.

"That's the word you'd use when a Katzen general sneaks into our Strait like vermin and dares abduct Our Highness to force her into marriage."

His expression was devoid of emotion—yet colder than Peleus had ever seen it, offering no quarter, no room for compromise.

"Jared was recently stripped of his ten-star rank. This was completely unforeseen by us. We have no idea how he ended up here—"

The delegation genuinely seemed baffled by the incident's trajectory.

Jared kidnapped the Princess. The First Prince killed him. How did this spiral so far out of control?

"What a convenient excuse."

Crack.

The quill in Sissair's hand snapped in two.

The envoy flinched at the sharp sound, instinctively recoiling.

"You claim ignorance of a ten-star general's movements? How convenient. It almost makes one wonder if Emperor Perdiccas deliberately stripped his rank to create plausible deniability."

"Your Grace, mind your words!"

"I can no longer keep pace with your empire's schemes. Every time I assume the worst, you reveal something even more depraved. Is this also your master's design?"

The delegation member's face flushed crimson with indignation.

Immediately following the incident, both Prince Cesare and Grand Duke Jason had been repatriated to Katzen under the chamberlain's escort.

Both were imperial family. The matter was too grave for them to remain.

What this meant: among the remaining delegation, no one possessed the authority or acumen to properly assess the situation and negotiate with Valdina.

Nor could anyone swiftly adapt to the tectonic shift—Valdina, once a subjugated vassal, now wielding power.

"Jared wasn't even truly Katzen nobility. His Majesty promotes based on merit regardless of birth—the man was fortunate enough to earn his title. So it's unfair to hold the entire empire accountable for his rogue actions."

The delegation's voices now carried anger rather than deference.

"Moreover, our First Prince personally dealt with that villain. His schemes failed. Her Highness the Princess is unharmed."

There was an arrogance beneath the words: So what more do you want?

"Our empire acknowledged responsibility and offered appropriate compensation. Yet Valdina insults His Majesty to our faces."

The Katzen envoys crossed their arms, confidence swelling.

Yes. No matter how much Valdina has grown, they cannot fully escape the stigma of being a newly forged empire.

"We have no desire for war. Remember—Valdina will eventually need to find common ground."

"Common ground?"

Sissair laughed—a sound devoid of mirth.

"Do you truly believe that's possible?"

"Then what—you'll escalate to full conflict? You know as well as I that the losses would outweigh any gains."

BOOM!

A thunderous crash silenced the delegation.

"What—what was that?!"

The envoys looked around in alarm.

D'Angel had kicked the door open and strode inside.

Behind him, armed Agema guards advanced into the delegation's quarters with unwavering purpose.

In D'Angel's hand: a heavy hammer, radiating menace.

Bang!

D'Angel slammed a thick plank of wood across the villa's door and drove nails through it with brutal efficiency.

BAM! BAM! BAM!

Within moments, the delegation's quarters were sealed.

"What is the meaning of this?!"

"You will not leave this place. You will not return to your empire."

"You're taking us hostage?! How dare you detain Katzen's high-ranking envoys!"

The delegates' faces drained of color.

"Oh, you think this is the extent of it?"

Sissair gestured toward the window.

Outside, armed Agema filled the courtyard—a steel wall of warriors.

"His Majesty departed for the Central Trade Route this morning. These are merely the rear guard."

The Central Trade Route—Katzen's vital artery connecting eastern and western commerce, linking agricultural centers and resource hubs across the continent.

Grain, iron, luxury goods—all flowed through that route.

For King Peleus to seize it with Agema forces meant strangling Katzen's supply lines.

"That's impossible! The Central Route—how could you reach it so quickly?!"

"You underestimate the mobility of Agema who've roamed the plains for a decade. Soon, every supply caravan bound for Katzen through the central route will be intercepted. That will be the clearest message we can send your Emperor."

"You—are you truly prepared for war with us? Can you even handle it?!"

Sissair's response was ice-cold.

"So tell me—would you have moved without confidence?"

"That's why we've been telling you—this wasn't us!"

The delegation felt they were losing their minds.

This wasn't an order from Perdiccas II. It was Jared's rogue action!

But the bastard's dead. And we're left holding the consequences.

Valdina seemed blinded by fury, beyond reason.

The Katzen envoys glared with expressions of utter exasperation.

"...This is mutually destructive. Valdina doesn't possess the strength to face Katzen head-on. You know this, Sissair."

Sissair's lips curled into a sneer.

"Sometimes, even knowing the cost, one must act. When a rabid dog sneaks into your home, you don't negotiate—you seize it by the throat."

His voice rang with frigid finality.

"I will ensure you never dare set foot here again."

End Chapter 7
