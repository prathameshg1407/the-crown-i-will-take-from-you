Chapter 263

Bonds of Blood
& Starlight

"This bond is the will of the divine, and the oath of blood shall be made under the blessing of heaven."

She lowered herself to one knee.

A handkerchief bearing the crest of Valdina wrapped gently around Cesare's wounded finger, the pristine white fabric slowly blooming crimson where it touched his blood.

At that precise moment, every candle illuminating the central temple shuddered in unison—their flames dancing as one, swaying to some invisible wind.

The tremor was so subtle that no one seemed to notice. Yet the dance continued, persistent and reverent, as though the very light itself celebrated the convergence of two fates long written in the stars.

Weathered hands settled upon the papal scepter as Pope Benedict rose slowly to his feet.

"If this is to be done, let it be done here."

The cream-colored hem of his robes whispered against the marble floor as he descended the steps.

"Medea de Valdina. Cesare Dwysler Katzen. I hereby decree the fulfillment of the sacred covenant through the union of these two souls."

He came to stand before them, his ancient eyes holding a gravity that silenced every whisper in the hall.

"This bond is the will of the divine, and the oath of blood shall be sealed under heaven's blessing."

His voice resonated through the vaulted chamber as he invoked the sacred rites of Mithras.

"The Holy Kingdom and this humble servant shall stand as witnesses to this oracle's completion."

Medea stared at the Pope, scarcely able to conceal her astonishment.

The implications of his words struck her like a thunderbolt.

The marriage would be conducted within the Holy Kingdom itself, with the Pope presiding as officiant. This meant the bride would not be subsumed into her husband's house as mere property—she would enter this union as an equal, her nation's sovereignty and her own authority preserved intact.

For Valdina, a kingdom that had clashed with Katzen across generations, this was an unprecedented opportunity.

Never before had the Holy Kingdom presided over a royal marriage. Never before had a Pope personally witnessed such a union.

This was a political act of extraordinary significance—the Pope deliberately shattering precedent to intervene in the relations between two nations. And it was, unmistakably, a gesture of profound favor toward Medea herself.

The assembled dignitaries exchanged glances of pure shock.

"Katzen and Valdina... bound together?"

"And His Holiness himself officiating...?"

They stood transfixed, struggling to comprehend this unprecedented alliance of the three mightiest powers on the continent.

Into the stunned silence came the sound of deliberate applause.

"Magnificent! Truly magnificent! A union befitting the heroes who vanquished the evil dragon!"

It was the King of Mycenae who spoke, his weathered face alight with genuine enthusiasm.

He had felt indebted to the Princess of Valdina ever since her solution to the monster waves had spared his kingdom from annihilation. Seeing how pale and drawn she appeared despite her youth, his guilt had only deepened.

'The First Prince of Katzen! What a spectacular stroke of fortune!'

What had seemed like catastrophe had transformed into unprecedented opportunity.

Cesare was the hero of the Continental War, the man closest to inheriting the Katzen throne. Fear and envy had always walked hand in hand where he was concerned—the continental powers dreaded his strength even as they yearned to bask in even a fraction of his reflected glory.

And now? The Princess of Valdina stood upon the swiftest path to becoming Empress of the greatest empire on the continent.

Perhaps she would ascend to the very pinnacle of power—to heights more magnificent than anything she had known in all her troubled days.

The King of Mycenae permitted himself a wry smile.

'Ah, Perdiccas. The headaches you'll suffer in the days to come...'

No matter how suspicious the Emperor of Katzen might be of his own son, he could not simply strip away Cesare's achievements and legendary prowess.

The extraordinary prince had long been a source of consternation for his father. Now, with a formidable princess standing at his side, that burden would only grow heavier.

"Heh. The old wolf will finally learn that time spares no one—not even him."

Knowing Emperor Perdiccas II's temperament better than most, the King of Mycenae could not suppress a bark of genuine laughter.

But while the King of Mycenae basked in the spreading excitement, another man clung to the edge of an abyss.

Jason's eyes were bloodshot, rimmed with red.

"How could you do this to me?"

He watched his wife pledge herself to another man before his very eyes, his fingers gripping a marble pillar so desperately that his nails cracked and bled, leaving crimson smears upon the white stone.

"Your Highness, please—let me escort you to the infirmary. You should never have come here in such condition—"

"Release me!"

His subordinate's words might as well have been the wind.

"You won't even grant me the chance to atone..."

She had chosen Cesare. Of all the men in the world—Cesare, Jason's most despised political rival.

Medea had severed him with surgical precision, in the most brutal way imaginable, ensuring he could harbor no hope of reconciliation.

"Congratulations to the destined pair! Blessed by the oracle itself!"

"A union ordained by heaven! Their love shall echo through the ages!"

Amid the jubilant cheers and celebrations, Jason stood utterly alone.

The helplessness of watching his wife in another man's arms. The wretchedness of knowing he could not even voice his protest—for this was the will of the divine.

'Medea... was this your design all along?'

If she had intended to inflict upon him the same despair she had felt when she learned of his union with Rachel—she had succeeded beyond measure.

"Kkh—!"

"Your Highness!"

The agony carved through his heart more viciously than any blade.

Jason doubled over and vomited blood upon the pristine marble floor.

At that same moment, Medea turned to address the Pope.

"I recognize the extraordinary favor Your Holiness has shown me. You have my deepest gratitude."

Though she had been the architect of this new interpretation of the oracle, it was entirely due to the Pope's support that her proposal had been accepted so readily.

Without his intervention, she would have faced a protracted political battle with little guarantee of success.

"I merely wished to ensure the Goddess's will was not distorted by mortal ambition."

Warmth suffused the Pope's ancient eyes as he regarded Medea.

"Yet looking upon you now... I find myself strangely convinced that I have done what is right."

He turned aside.

"Anastasia."

"Yes, Your Holiness."

"You shall remain at Her Highness the Princess's side for the time being. Ensure that the execution of this oracle proceeds without impediment."

The assembled clergy of the Holy Kingdom exchanged startled glances.

Anastasia was widely known as one of the Pope's most trusted attendants among the senior priesthood.

"His Holiness has never shown such personal consideration before..."

This benevolent demeanor was unlike anything they had witnessed from Pope Benedict.

Even the Pope himself could not fully explain the surge of protective affection that had risen within him the moment he first beheld Medea.

He simply knew that he wished to shield her. To guard her from harm.

'As though this is how things were always meant to be.'

Conversely, he had felt an instinctive wariness toward Saintess Rachel from the very first moment of their meeting.

The Pope's gaze hardened once more as it swept past her.

"That will be all."

He departed without acknowledging Rachel's presence. The High Priests trailing in his wake did the same—not one spared her so much as a glance.

'Does she not comprehend how her conduct reflects upon the Holy Kingdom?'

Their cold dismissal was a pointed rebuke for Rachel's public confrontation with the Pope before the assembled dignitaries of the continent.

Her stubborn insistence on condemning the Princess of Valdina to death had crossed a line.

'An attitude that celebrates death in the name of rigid doctrine... That is not the conduct of a true Saintess.'

Rachel sensed the clergy's regard growing colder by the moment.

Her complexion paled. Her fingernails bit into her palms beneath the cover of her sleeves.

"Her Highness Medea is truly..."

Perhaps she had bitten the inside of her cheek too fiercely—Rachel's voice emerged strained and muffled.

"...remarkably fortunate."

"Indeed. Though I prefer not to rely upon the name of the divine for my fortune."

Medea's response was measured, her composure unshaken.

"I consider any fate I have not seized with my own hands to be no fate of mine at all."

Rachel's eyes widened.

To her ears, Medea's words carried an unmistakable implication—an accusation that Rachel herself had stolen the destiny that should have been another's.

'Could it be... has she awakened to the truth?!'

Yet no matter how intently Rachel searched, Medea's serene expression and composed bearing betrayed nothing.

'What does she know?'

Medea, for her part, caught the flicker of naked terror that passed through Rachel's violet eyes.

It was a visceral reaction—one the Saintess had not displayed even when the inherited oracle had stripped away her advantage.

'What is Rachel so desperately afraid of?'

Vision

In that instant, Medea's vision fractured.

The first fragment showed shadowed figures gathered in a subterranean chamber, their faces obscured by darkness.

The second revealed a young priest standing atop an elaborate painting, his expression unreadable.

The images vanished as swiftly as they had appeared—and in their wake, Rachel's hand began to glow with an inky black radiance.

It was reminiscent of the crimson light that had once guided Medea to the evil dragon's vulnerable scales—yet this darkness pulsed with something far more sinister. Something deeply wrong.

'The darkness... it's connected to her heart...'

The black luminescence did not remain confined to Rachel's hands. It spread in chaotic tendrils, wreathing her entire form in shadow.

Medea stepped forward, straining to see more clearly—

'I must understand what this means...'

But the vision dissolved before she could grasp its full significance.

"May the Goddess's protection rest upon this union... I certainly hope it does."

Rachel's farewell emerged in a strangled whisper, her composure visibly fraying.

Without waiting for a response, she turned and swept away with hurried steps.

"Hmph. After driving Your Highness to the very edge of destruction, she dares show her face as though nothing occurred. The shamelessness of that woman knows no bounds."

Neril glared daggers at Rachel's retreating figure.

"Neril. Can you see it?"

"See what, Your Highness?"

"The priests accompanying her..."

The clergy trailing in Rachel's wake were wreathed in the same ominous black radiance—dark light blooming from their hearts like poisonous flowers.

"How long have those priests served at the Saintess's side?"

"If I recall correctly... they are ascetics who have attended her since she first departed Valdina. Your Highness, is something amiss?"

Neril appeared entirely oblivious to anything unusual.

As though she could not perceive the sinister sight of black light emanating from the hearts of a dozen ascetic priests, spreading like dark mist through the sacred air of the Great Temple.

"Your Highness...? Are you unwell?"

The vision faded like morning mist touched by sunlight.

'Am I the only one who can perceive this? Even among so many High Priests?'

Medea's gaze swept methodically across the assembly, searching.

And then she found him.

A Templar knight stood apart from his brethren, his attention fixed upon the Saintess and her entourage with the same piercing intensity Medea herself had displayed.

After a moment, the knight turned his head with studied casualness.

Their eyes met.

"Theo! Where has your attention wandered to now?"

Commander Leonor's sharp voice cut through the moment, and the knight's gaze slid away.
