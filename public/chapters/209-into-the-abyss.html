Chapter 209

Into the
Abyss

"In darkness, even brothers become strangers—and strangers become enemies."

Perdiccas II's face had turned such a violent shade of crimson that his eyes seemed ready to burst from their sockets.

"Even the most wretched criminal remains a subject of the Empire! Such a man—slain on foreign soil, by his own royal blood!"

The Emperor's voice cracked with fury.

"Do you have any conception of the ammunition your reckless actions have provided our enemies? What you've handed to Valdina on a silver platter?"

With trembling hands, Perdiccas II seized the parchment report that had been presented to him and tore it to shreds, showering the fragments over his kneeling son like bitter snow.

Among the scattered pieces, a tattered illustration caught the light—the Katzen flag, wrapped around Jared's lifeless body like a shroud.

"Because of you, they're poised to sit upon my head! Cesare—this catastrophe is your doing!"

"Who would dare presume authority over the Emperor himself?"

Cesare's reply was so excessively pious, so dripping with false loyalty, that it could only be interpreted as mockery.

"Please, Father—spare yourself such unnecessary worry."

The performance succeeded in driving the Emperor's wrath to its absolute zenith.

"You—you insolent creature! Are you mocking me, or do you simply take me for a fool?"

"Your Majesty!"

An attendant rushed forward to steady the gasping monarch.

Clutching at his chest, Perdiccas II gripped the armrests of his throne with white-knuckled desperation, fighting to draw breath. When he finally managed to speak again, his voice had dropped to something between a wheeze and a snarl.

"Why... why..."

Deep suspicion threaded through each labored breath as he attempted to gauge his son's true intentions.

This is entirely unlike him. What game is he playing?

Cesare noticed his father's calculating gaze—those dark pupils glinting as they tried to penetrate his motives. A soft chuckle escaped him.

"Jared orchestrated my death once before. Had fate not intervened, he would certainly have tried again. I merely... accelerated the inevitable."

He paused, letting the words settle.

"I've never been one to forget a kindness. But neither do I forget a grievance."

His eyes met his father's without flinching.

"Surely the Emperor understands that better than most."

Among the twelve emperors of Katzen's history, not one had died peacefully in his bed.
Each had been slain by his own son.

Perdiccas II had been no exception to this tradition.

"Enough! I've heard enough!"

The Emperor's patience shattered completely.

"Cast him into the Abyss! Let him rot there until he comprehends the magnitude of his transgressions and comes crawling to beg forgiveness!"

The Abyss was precisely what its name suggested—the deepest dungeon beneath the imperial palace.

To reach it required descending hundreds of stone steps that spiraled into the earth's bowels, arriving at last in a realm of absolute darkness where no ray of light had ever penetrated.

The cold there was unlike anything found on the surface—a damp, gnawing chill that seeped into the marrow and paralyzed the body by degrees. The cells were cramped beyond measure, designed to strip prisoners of any sense of time or space.

Even the most hardened criminals emerged from that pitch-black hell with their sanity in tatters—if they emerged at all.

"You know that holds no terror for me."

Cesare shrugged with infuriating nonchalance and rose to his feet. The crimson rope that had bound his wrists slithered uselessly to the floor, discarded like a child's toy.

"But very well—I'll indulge in a brief stay. For the sake of appearances, if nothing else."

Then he turned toward Jason, a predatory gleam entering his eyes.

"Brother. Since we orchestrated this affair together, it seems only fitting we share the consequences."

Jason stood frozen, disbelief flooding his features. Being seized and dragged to the Empire had been humiliating enough—but the Abyss?

"What are you talking about, Cesare? You killed him. I had nothing to—"

He attempted to wrench his arm free, but Cesare's grip was iron. The First Prince leaned close, his voice dropping to a whisper meant for Jason's ears alone.

"Consider it a favor. I'll claim responsibility for Raju as well. He was your servant, after all."

"...What?"

"You're welcome to offer alternative explanations, of course. But the individual who purchased Jared's services happens to be connected to you. I wonder what conclusions our ever-suspicious Emperor might draw from that discovery."

Cesare's lips curved into something that wasn't quite a smile. The edge beneath it gleamed like a blade.

Jason's face contorted with suppressed fury and dawning fear.

How much does he know? Is he aware of my alliance with the warlocks?

But when he searched those clear golden eyes, he found nothing—no hint, no tell, no crack in the mask.

Those pupils burned with a predator's intensity, radiating the quiet menace of a beast that had already cornered its prey.

For the first time, a chilling certainty settled over Jason: no matter how he struggled, no matter what schemes he devised, he might never surpass this man.

"Until we meet again, dear brother."

"That's treason—!"

But Jason's protests went unheard. Cesare hauled him away, leaving behind only the sputtering rage of Perdiccas II.

"—Agemas... the Emperor... blocking the trade route—"

"—supplies completely disrupted—"

As they made their way toward the Abyss's entrance, a knight rushed past them, his face drawn with urgency.

"What's happening?"

The knight skidded to a halt, recognizing the princes despite their circumstances.

"Your Highness the First Prince. Your Highness the Grand Duke. Forgive me—there's been a crisis. Valdina has seized control of the central trade route and severed all supply lines to Katzen."

The knight's expression bordered on panic as he elaborated.

"We were coordinating with several minor kingdoms to import a substantial iron shipment. That entire operation has been blocked. I must report to His Majesty immediately—time is of the essence. Please excuse my haste."

Jason's face went pale.

"Wait—the Central Trade Route? Is Valdina actually prepared to declare war? Tell me everything you know—"

He lunged toward the knight, desperate for more information, but Cesare's grip tightened, yanking him back.

"Go. The Emperor will likely throw a spectacular tantrum—I'd suggest bracing yourself accordingly."

The knight, clearly unnerved by the warning, hurried away. Once he was gone, the corner of Cesare's mouth twitched upward.

So the rumors were true. The Emperor of Valdina treasures his sister as though she were his very life.

Under ordinary circumstances, such knowledge would have merely been interesting.

Now, facing an indefinite imprisonment in the Abyss, it brought him something unexpectedly close to relief.

At least there will be a formidable shield protecting her while I'm unable to do so myself.

"Our dear Emperor is going to have quite the meltdown over this."

His tone was light, almost conversational.

"Wouldn't you agree, brother?"

Jason said nothing. His glare could have set stone ablaze.

They arrived at the entrance to the Abyss—a yawning maw of darkness that seemed to swallow even the torchlight.

"Your Highness the First Prince. Your Highness the Grand Duke. I've received word of your arrival. I am prepared to receive you."

The jailer emerged from the shadows, offering a bow that was more perfunctory than reverent. Years of handling the empire's most notorious prisoners—or perhaps the corrupting influence of the Abyss itself—had stripped away any genuine deference.

What remained was an unsettling blend of contempt and hollow courtesy.

"I shall escort Your Highnesses to your respective cells. This way, if you please."

Jason stared down the black staircase. It plunged into nothingness, each step disappearing into a void that seemed to have no end.

"Cesare."

His voice was tight with barely contained rage.

"Why are you doing this to me?"

He genuinely couldn't comprehend it.

"Isn't it obvious?"

"What could possibly be obvious about—"

"Did you truly think I would allow you to leave here and go to her?"

Cesare's voice dropped, each word deliberate and cold.

"Wake up, dear brother."

Even in the encroaching darkness, his golden eyes blazed like twin flames—fierce, unwavering, utterly unafraid.

"I refuse to stand idle while you attempt to steal something I've invested my entire being to obtain."

The Imperial Palace of Valdina

Three men stood around Medea's bed, their expressions uniformly grim.

"Truly, I'm fine."

Medea offered a reassuring smile, but with bandages swathing her hands and wrapped around her throat, the gesture rang hollow.

"The fault lies with me. I let him slip through my fingers. I have no excuse."

Kensington lowered his head in shame.

Only now did Medea fully understand the anguish that had been etched into Neril's face since her return.

Jared... if only I had tracked his whereabouts sooner...

The thought haunted her—if she had been more vigilant, if she had pushed harder, perhaps she could have kept her lady safe.

"Emergencies are inevitable. It's not as though I failed to anticipate the possibility of such an attack."

Medea pressed a palm to her forehead, pushing aside the self-recrimination in the room.

"Sir Kensington, you've already flagellated yourself sufficiently. Please—continue your investigation. What has my brother done now?"

Her voice carried a note of weary exasperation.

Peleus had managed to exceed even her most extreme projections. By the time she'd regained consciousness after being returned to the palace, news had already arrived that her brother had reached the Central Trade Route.

"I hadn't expected him to mobilize so rapidly."

Had he delayed even a day or two, public opinion would have had time to fracture and dissent. But Peleus had moved with the decisive swiftness of a striking serpent.

Despite herself, Medea felt a flicker of admiration for her brother's tactical instincts and ruthless efficiency.

"What news from Katzen?"

"If you're inquiring about the First Prince's punishment—the imperial palace has been placed under heightened security, and we're still working to ascertain the precise situation. What we know for certain is that he has been taken into custody."

Kensington's voice remained steady as he continued his report.

"There is apparently fierce conflict between the factions supporting the Third Prince—led by the Western Empress—and those loyal to the First Prince. The dispute centers on the appropriate punishment for His Highness's actions."

He paused before delivering the most significant development.

"Additionally, the Emperor has revoked the First Prince's right to mobilize his private forces and ordered the dissolution of his personal army."

Medea's eyes narrowed.

The soldiers drawn from Cesare's various territories were legendary—both for their numbers and their fearsome skill. And now they were being scattered to the winds.

Perdiccas II would never pass up an opportunity to diminish his son's power.

And there's no possibility Cesare doesn't understand that.

She fell silent, lost in thought.

Unbidden, a memory surfaced: the warmth of a large hand, gentle against her cheek. The unexpected tenderness in those golden eyes.
