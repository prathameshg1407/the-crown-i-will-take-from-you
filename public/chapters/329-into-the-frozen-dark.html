Chapter 29

Into the
Frozen Dark

"I will not wait for fate to decide. I will go to him myself."

The remaining courtiers fell silent, their eyes glittering as they calculated their positions in light of the Emperor's frigid response.

"Your Highness...?"

Medea's vision fractured.

Her consciousness shattered into fragments, scattering like shards of broken glass across her mind's eye.

The first fragment: A figure in dark robes driving a blade through Cesare's back.

The second: Cesare plunging beneath the ice wall, locked in combat with the warlock, their bodies intertwined as they fell.

The third: Cesare, wounded and struggling, dragging himself across the frozen floor of a cave—until finally, his body went still.

Just as when she had faced the evil dragon, visions spanning past and present cascaded through her—

—and then vanished in an instant.

Medea found herself once again in the throne room, surrounded by the calculating gazes of courtiers.

Her green eyes grew distant, contemplative—then sharpened with sudden clarity.

"Then I shall go to the icebergs myself."

Her voice rang through the chamber, calm and absolute.

"While you all waste precious time squabbling over whether my husband lives or dies, he may well be drawing his final breaths. Surely, Your Majesty would not deny me even this?"

Her emerald gaze cut through the assembly like a whip.

Outside the imperial palace, the sun blazed with merciless heat.

Medea felt the warmth against her skin—so utterly different from the frozen grey of the ice wall she had glimpsed in her vision.

The cold that awaits at the iceberg will be incomparably worse than anything here.

Her mouth went dry. Her pace quickened.

As she rounded a corner, she collided with someone directly in her path.

"Oh dear. Sister-in-law, are you quite alright?"

The Third Prince, Juan.

Gone was the mask of genteel courtesy he typically wore. Gone, too, was the anxious pallor he had shown since his mother's imprisonment.

He appeared strangely calm. Almost... relaxed.

"In circumstances such as these, I must commend your spirit and exceptional intellect in venturing north."

His lips curved into something that was not quite a smile.

"However, surely it would be a waste to bury such remarkable talents alongside the dead?"

A mean light flickered in his eyes.

"Should you desire it, the position beside me remains vacant. The title of Empress may prove difficult to arrange... but I can certainly take excellent care of you, Sister-in-law."

His gaze traced deliberately from her temples down to her lips, dripping with poorly concealed desire.

Medea regarded him with perfect stillness.

Juan shivered slightly, though he couldn't have explained why.

This is the same woman who showed no mercy when she brought my mother low...

I covet her abilities. And that exquisite face is precisely to my taste.

"It will be difficult to accept, I know, but accept it you must. If you insist on this futile journey, what will remain when you return?"

A triumphant smile spread across Juan's face.

While Cesare lived, such thoughts had been unthinkable. But now—now he could finally reach out and take what he wanted.

CRACK.

Juan clutched his cheek and staggered backward, nearly losing his footing entirely.

"You dare—!"

"In my husband's absence, it seems the duty of correcting you falls to me, his wife."

Medea shook out her hand with casual disdain, as though brushing away something unclean.

"You share half his blood, yet you cannot even maintain basic propriety? If my husband returns alive, how will you meet his eyes?"

She stepped forward, looking down at Juan where he had crumpled.

Her voice remained utterly composed, but her eyes held winter's chill.

"You didn't say I hit you, did you?"

Juan's protest died in his throat.

"Indeed. Three years ago, many held similar confidence, as I recall. And what became of them? The First Prince has a tiresome habit of defying expectations. One can never be entirely certain."

Juan fell silent.

Despite himself, he remembered Cesare—the man who had returned hale and whole after being diagnosed with a terminal curse.

"Were I in your position, I would not let down my guard."

Medea reached out and, almost mockingly, tucked a strand of his disheveled hair behind his ear.

"You understand, don't you, Juan? The throne you covet is a place where a single moment of carelessness can see your head separated from your shoulders."

Her smile was knife-sharp.

"What a waste it would be to throw away your life for vulgar desires."

Juan retreated, and Gallo watched his back with murder in his eyes.

"Your Highness. A moment of your leave."

"Gallo—if you intend to pursue vengeance against Juan, stop where you stand."

"But he insulted both Your Highness and the Commander. Once I've cut out his tongue as payment for his loose mouth, he'll think twice before—"

All trace of Gallo's usual levity had vanished. His expression was stone.

"There is no time for such diversions, Lord Gallo. Terrence—we must return to the border immediately."

"Your Highness truly intends to accompany us? The northern reaches are treacherous beyond measure."

"Had I feared that, I would not have declared my intentions before the entire court."

Terrence hesitated, then slowly shook his head.

During the assault on the Golden Tower, the warlock stronghold that had remained hidden for half a century had proven formidable beyond imagination.

They had nearly died attempting to dismantle the magical defenses near the ice wall.

Now he understood why Cesare—despite his devotion to Medea—had refused to bring her along.

"Dark magic circles may still remain active. The danger is extreme."

To bring Medea to such a place? The moment Cesare discovered it, he would strangle Terrence with his bare hands—assuming he was still alive to do so.

"I am bound by duty to ensure the safety of the one His Highness entrusted to my care."

"He is alive."

Medea seized Terrence by the wrist.

"I saw it. We must find him immediately."

Her voice, though quiet, brimmed with unshakeable certainty.

Terrence wavered, uncertain whether to believe her or dismiss this as desperate hope.

"You saw it... What do you mean, Your Highness?"

"During your assault on the Golden Tower, the warlock at its center fled through the western gate, did he not? The man called Edra wore a dark robe."

"How... How could Your Highness possibly know that?"

Terrence's eyes went wide.

"There are moments when I perceive scenes I cannot explain. Just as I witnessed Cesare's past before I struck down the dragon."

Understanding dawned. Terrence recalled that she was the goddess's chosen vessel.

If it is the goddess's power... perhaps she truly can bring Cesare back from the brink of death.

"This may well be divine revelation. I may yet be able to save his life."

"...I understand. I shall escort Your Highness personally."

Terrence began to turn away, then paused and bowed his head.

"There is something Cesare asked of me not long ago. Should anything happen to him, I was to escort Your Highness safely back to Valdina."

The words had been delivered casually, almost in passing.

"I asked him why—why he would surrender every precious thing within reach, only to insist you be sent away."

"He answered: 'I have no intention of letting her go easily. But...'"

Terrence recalled the way Cesare's golden eyes had grown distant and cold.

It was the same look he had worn when the primordial curse had begun consuming him—when he had tried to return to the Mage's Tower alone. That terrifyingly rational, meticulously ordered expression.

"'Without me, everyone—the Emperor included—will make Medea their first target. Before that happens, take her out of Katzen.'"

Yet Cesare must have known.

Even when the curse had threatened to devour him, Terrence had refused to abandon his side.

He could not simply obey such a request—not when his lord's life hung in the balance.

"He would never approve of bringing Your Highness into such danger. But in our current circumstances, you are our only hope."

"We must move immediately. Every moment counts."

"I shall make the arrangements at once."

Medea nodded.

After Terrence departed, Medea issued swift commands to Neril, Menil, and the rest of her retinue, ordering them to prepare for immediate departure to the frozen north.

She turned to Mrs. Tolin, her expression resolute.

"During my absence, opportunists will flock to the palace seeking information. Should you discover anyone sowing discord from within, dispose of them without hesitation."

The elderly woman studied Medea—the way she commanded her people even in the depths of crisis, the clarity in her eyes that refused to yield to despair.

"Truly, you are the companion His Highness chose."

"Ensure that no information regarding the First Prince escapes these walls until my return."

"As Your Highness commands."

Medea addressed the assembled vassals of the First Prince's faction with equal severity.

"Our sole priority now is locating His Highness the First Prince. Should the Emperor or the Third Prince attempt provocation, do not respond. Do not engage."

Now, more than ever, their unity must hold firm.

"I will bring him back. Until then—hold your positions. Do not waver."

It is often said that true character reveals itself in moments of crisis.

Even before this, the First Princess had been regarded as a formidable ally.

But her willingness to cast herself into peril—when not only Cesare but she herself faced mortal danger—left an indelible impression upon every member of the First Prince's faction, within the palace and beyond.
