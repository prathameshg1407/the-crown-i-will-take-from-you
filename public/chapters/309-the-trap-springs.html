Chapter 309

The Trap Springs

"The carefully laid snare closes—but who truly caught whom?"

Without warning, the Emperor slammed his glass down with such force that wine splashed across the pristine tablecloth like blood spatter.

"If that girl had been present, I would have lost my composure entirely! On such a joyous occasion, she was born with a talent for ruining my mood."

Perdiccas II's complexion darkened further—a storm cloud gathering density.

He recalled the sharp accusations his daughter Angelique had hurled at him before her expulsion from court.

Even remembering made his blood boil afresh.

"What has she ever done correctly? Nothing but pride filled that empty head. Dull-witted and easily manipulated—she fell for their schemes like a fool."

His bloodshot eyes bulged with barely restrained fury.

Merely thinking of his lost territories—the golden island of Chardenia and the lucrative Strait of El Amuz—made his teeth grind audibly.

"It's my sin—Katzen's misfortune—to have birthed such an incompetent child."

As he spoke these words, Perdiccas II's glare fixed upon Medea.

The current possessor of his stolen lands. The heir to Valdina's bloodline.

He seemed desperate to pour all his resentment onto her instead.

"Three years ago... if not for that debacle, Valdina would still be groveling at my feet."

When his gaze fell upon Valdina's imperial emblem—now elevated to equal status—displayed atop the gifts arranged along the ballroom's periphery, Perdiccas II appeared ready to overturn the entire celebration.

"Father, please don't trouble yourself overmuch. Katzen's territories remain vast. How can that compare to the satisfaction of seeing conquered kings prostrate themselves before you?"

With an unexpectedly mild tone, Cesare deftly intercepted his father's venomous attention before it could fully focus on his wife.

Perdiccas II's eyes narrowed at his eldest son.

'Yes, flaunt your achievements. This merely proves you intend to climb atop your father's head.'

The ballroom atmosphere shifted subtly, growing tense.

Among those present, several represented nations Cesare had conquered during those very campaigns—now reduced to Katzen's vassals.

"..."

Perdiccas II drew a sharp breath, his lips twitching.

He retained sufficient political awareness not to display uncontrolled rage before representatives of conquered territories—as their supreme overlord, such behavior would undermine his authority.

"..."

The Eastern Empress trembled with fury as she glared at the Western Empress, who had so cleverly orchestrated this humiliating situation.

Yet she could only bite her lip in silence, monitoring the Emperor's expression anxiously.

'If I speak in anger, he might exile our Angelique to an even harsher frontier than her current station.'

"I must endure this."

Though maternal grief gnawed at her heart—unable to defend her daughter who'd been ridiculed as a fool before so many witnesses—she swallowed her protests.

'His Majesty's hatred burns too fiercely still... If I act rashly, Angelique will suffer worse consequences. That child... she'll be hurt further...'

"Your Majesty, while I appreciate the... spirited discussion, we cannot delay the honors ceremony much longer."

Marquis Eratosthenes broke the uncomfortable silence with his characteristically dry voice.

In his weathered hands rested a scroll bearing the imperial seal.

The elderly man's expression remained utterly indifferent, as though the emotional tempest currently engulfing the ballroom's distinguished guests held no relevance to him whatsoever.

Dressed in his austere gray monastic robes, wearing an inscrutable expression, his features seemed to fade beneath the brilliant lights—like a black-and-white sculpture extracted from a painting.

"Ah, has so much time passed already? Penteros, approach."

Perdiccas II glanced at his timepiece and nodded acknowledgment.

As Eratosthenes had noted, the honors presentation was indeed overdue.

"Wolfgang Tarvio, Vasily Sen—come forward."

The ceremony commenced with tributes from those being honored, while others waited eagerly for their names to be called.

The ballroom's energy shifted, becoming animated once more.

Perdiccas II bestowed each award exactly as Eratosthenes had submitted them on the official roster, without a single objection or modification.

This served as testament to the Emperor's absolute trust in the Marquis.

"Michelon Erma, you are credited with risking your life to restore public order in the plague-ravaged Western provinces..."

Eratosthenes, still clad in his severe gray robes, recited each honoree's accomplishments with precise formality.

Medea watched his back intently.

'Why do I sense the old man deliberately deflecting the Emperor's attention?'

'Am I reading too much into coincidence?'

"Possessing such a formidable military as Your Majesty commands is heaven's miracle and Katzen's salvation."

"Indeed, precisely so. How could Katzen possibly decline with Your Majesty's prestige shining ever brighter?"

Once the honors concluded and the banquet resumed full vigor, Perdiccas II found himself surrounded only by those skilled in artful flattery.

Their eyes betrayed a mixture of fear and ambition as they chattered ceaselessly with carefully constructed pleasantries.

"Hah! Do you imagine I cannot recognize blatant sycophancy when I hear it?"

Though he voiced the accusation bluntly, faint satisfaction colored Perdiccas II's features nonetheless.

He reclined against his throne, slowly surveying the ballroom.

The uninterrupted melody of music. Ministers observing him from beneath blazing chandeliers. Nobles seeking opportunities for advancement. Subjects bowing their heads in deference...

'It feels as though I've just ascended the throne. That glorious moment when the world first bent its neck beneath my boot.'

Even his eldest son—perpetual thorn in his side—had presented him with an elaborate golden crown woven from gilded roses.

"The arrogant wretch displayed it so openly earlier... clearly he'd been preparing this gift long before tonight."

Regardless of the fact that the presumptuous boy had bowed while presenting it, the crown's value alone made it the finest offering in the entire ballroom. Yet the Emperor couldn't bring himself to feel genuine pleasure.

'The reality remains: this empire still revolves around me.'

It was precisely then—

"SEIZE HIM! The filthy Valdinian spy!"

A thunderous shout erupted. Simultaneously, the music pervading the ballroom ceased abruptly.

The atmosphere froze instantly.

The Emperor's brow furrowed deeply.

"Captured attempting to smuggle classified correspondence from the Katzen Imperial Palace—caught red-handed!"

In the distance, Imperial Knights dragged someone forward roughly.

The man's hair hung in disarray, his armor and face smeared with blood.

"Your Majesty, this man is Valdina's spy."

The Commander of the Imperial Knights, having reached the Emperor's presence, hurled the prisoner forward brutally.

"We've been monitoring suspicious activity following internal intelligence reports. He possessed a sword containing hidden compartments for secret correspondence. Today, the moment he attempted to exit the palace gates carrying that weapon, we apprehended him."

His resonant voice echoed throughout the ballroom—deliberately projected to ensure every guest heard clearly.

"Lord Menil!"

Shocked, Neril rushed forward to support the injured man. She glared at the Commander with undisguised fury.

"It's not enough to falsely accuse him of espionage—you dared to brutalize him so severely?"

"A spy naturally resisted. Should we have sustained casualties apprehending him?"

The Knight Commander's hand moved toward his sword hilt, as though preparing to draw it against Neril herself.

CRACK!

Something struck the back of his hand with such force he nearly dropped the partially unsheathed blade.

"Ungh!"

"The Commander courts death, blood running so hot. I observe you attempting to draw steel in His Majesty's very presence."

The First Prince had flicked something—a coin? a button?—with devastating precision. The Commander ground his teeth in pain and humiliation.

'What did that bastard throw? My hand still tingles!'

Through clenched jaw, he forced words out.

"Your Highness, this matter cannot be dismissed simply through your... protective intervention."

Witnesses to this tense confrontation stood paralyzed with shock.

More people were now recognizing that the bloodied prisoner was indeed one of the First Princess's personal guards.

A former member of Emperor Valdina's elite bodyguard, his exceptional skills had earned recognition throughout the holy city in recent years.

"This accusation is preposterous slander! Why would Lord Menil possibly—"

"Must I display the evidence before you'll cease your denials?"

CLANG!

The Imperial Commander slammed the heavy sword onto the marble floor.

Folded parchment sheets spilled from the shattered handle compartment.

The Commander made deliberate eye contact with the Western Empress before twisting his lips into a triumphant sneer.

"Behold, Your Majesty! Why would anyone conceal correspondence so meticulously? And why attempt to spirit it from the Imperial Palace this very evening?"

After a moment of stunned silence, shocked murmurs erupted throughout the ballroom like wildfire.

"...It's actually real...!"

"Dear God, was he truly a spy all along?"

Every eye turned toward the First Princess and her retinue.

'If her loyal guard was genuinely a spy, as the Commander claims... did the First Prince truly know nothing?'

'What if the one we regarded as heaven's blessing is actually the curse that will destroy the empire?'

Doubt spread like poison through the assembled nobility.

"Your Majesty, I demand judgment under imperial law! Can it truly be mere coincidence that Her Highness's guard attempted this on the very evening Valdina's Chancellor visits?"

The Commander approached Perdiccas II with undisguised smugness, prostrating himself dramatically.

"This represents a scheme by Emperor Valdina—dispatching his sister to Katzen to lower our defenses, then stealing our most closely guarded secrets!"

The Emperor's frown deepened progressively into something far more menacing. His gaze swept across Medea and the fallen Menil, saturated with suspicion.

'Did they dare attempt to exploit Katzen again?'

'I could never permit such audacity! Not after they seized the Strait and elevated themselves to imperial status!'

Rage began mounting visibly in Perdiccas II's expression.

But then—

"This constitutes a grave insult to His Majesty Emperor Valdina, and represents profound diplomatic disrespect toward all Valdinians."

Valdina's young Chancellor spoke with remarkable composure given the circumstances.

"If you serve as Commander of Katzen's Imperial Knights, shouldn't you exercise sufficient prudence to accept responsibility for such accusations? If your claims prove false, are you confident you can bear the catastrophic bilateral consequences?"

The Knight Commander shot back without hesitation.

"Your statement borders on the absurd. If you persist in denial even after witnessing this evidence, Valdina will merely appear to be shamefully attempting deception before God's own eyes."

"..."

"Furthermore, Her Highness's guard has recently traveled throughout the city requesting sparring matches with our knights. Who can say whether that was truly for training... or reconnaissance to gather military intelligence?"

The Commander swept his accusatory glare across the assembled guests as though calling them all as witnesses.

"The Katzen people—myself included—showed loyalty to Her Highness the Princess despite her Valdinian bloodline. And His Highness the Prince has known only devotion to country and empire..."

His voice resonated with wounded patriotism and righteous indignation.

The trap had been sprung with devastating precision.
