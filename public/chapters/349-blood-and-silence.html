Chapter 349

Blood
and Silence

"In desperation, even the divine may fail—but love finds another way."

She reached out and touched his skin.

The warmth of a living being still radiated from him—faint, but present.

"Cesare."

The unmoving man kept his eyes closed.

Those brilliant golden eyes remained hidden beneath dark lashes. He'd lost weight, making his sculptural features stand out even more sharply.

Yet his beauty was overshadowed by the grayish pallor of his skin.

The faint spark of life seemed on the verge of extinguishing entirely—as though he teetered on the precipice of becoming a corpse.

"Wake up."

Her small hand wrapped around his index finger.

"You told me to follow the plan. Then you have to open your eyes. Please... open your eyes."

Normally, Cesare would cover her hand with his other four fingers and tickle the back of her palm.

But now—nothing.

Medea squeezed his finger tightly.

Even gripping as hard as she could, he didn't stir. Didn't open his eyes to look at her. Didn't hold her hand in return.

She stared down at him.

Only the two of them remained in the bedchamber.

Her usually inscrutable green eyes wavered.

Finally, she raised her other hand.

A small, sharp dagger glinted in the lamplight.

With a tingling pain, clear crimson welled from the tip of her index finger.

If magic and holy power won't reach him...

Her ability to defeat the evil dragon had begun when she'd mixed her blood with the dragon's.

Holy power doesn't work. But perhaps—if it's my own blood—it might...

Perhaps her blood could wake Cesare from this cursed affliction.

Please.

Her desperate hope was contained in every drop that fell to the corner of his mouth.

Medea wiped the blood from his beautiful lips.

And waited.

Silence.

Nothing happened.

Darkness deepened as night progressed.

The sky outside the window turned black. The morning star rose again.

Nothing changed.

She'd known it might not work—had tried anyway, grasping at any possibility.

Yet failure still crushed her beneath its weight.

"I... You called me your Saintess."

The word was an accusation directed at the Goddess herself.

When Medea had faced the dragon, the Goddess had shown her the way at the darkest moment. Had allowed her to prevail.

"But why now...?"

What use was being a Saintess?

Why couldn't her power—the Goddess's own power—reach him?

Or does She refuse to allow it?

"Cesare."

When she'd lost consciousness after exposing Rachel's true identity at the temple wedding, had he felt like this too?

This helpless?

"Cesare... don't make me afraid."

She bent down and pressed her pale forehead against his temple.

When the second oracle had descended and Cesare had been struck down, everyone had marveled at her unwavering composure.

Medea had never wavered before anyone.

She could only collapse—truly, completely—in front of Cesare Katzen alone.

"Cesare, I... I think I'm frightened now."

Afraid I'll lose you.

"It's absurd, isn't it? I've lived my entire life believing revenge was everything."

Her hand tightened around his finger.

"But if I have to lose you in exchange for that revenge..."

Her voice caught.

"Then none of it means anything anymore."

Medea leaned her head against his chest.

When she closed her eyes, the faint sound of his heartbeat kept her from shattering completely.

"Because of you, Cesare... I can dream of tomorrow now. I can step over yesterday's pain and wait for tomorrow's joy."

Tears traced silent paths down her pale cheeks.

—Child, it's simply not time yet.

The whisper came like wind through distant trees.

Medea didn't understand.

◆◆◆

Medea's eyes snapped open.

She'd fallen asleep beside Cesare without meaning to.

A sound at the door drew her attention—Terence and Gallo stood in the threshold.

Medea nodded, and Terence spoke.

"Marquis Leniard has been sent away. Sir Theo reports that the physician says his other arm will recover soon."

Gallo continued.

"I attempted to infiltrate Facade operatives into the Central Temple as the boss ordered, but they've tightened security significantly. We failed."

His expression darkened.

"Judging by how they've quietly sealed the temple since Sir Theo's escape, the interior has either been brainwashed or is now filled with warlocks."

"We believe they're remnants scattered across the continent—not from the Iceberg base we destroyed."

Since Cesare's collapse, these two had been reporting everything to Medea.

She studied them carefully.

They appeared as usual—but she'd spent enough time with them to read the tension hidden behind their professional masks.

"Terence. Gallo."

Gallo's dark brown eyes and Terence's bloodshot gaze turned to her.

"Don't you want to ask me as well? To accept the oracle?"

If she did, Cesare would open his eyes.

Everyone here knew that. He could rise from this bed, ascend to the throne that awaited him—without fever, without vomiting blood.

But Terence immediately shook his head.

"How could we, knowing it's a fabrication?"

When he'd entered the room, he'd seen the pale bloodstains on Medea's fingers—already crudely bandaged—and at the corner of Cesare's mouth.

What right do I have to speak before such desperation?

"Your Highness must know why Cesare kept us by your side."

His gaze dropped to Medea's belly.

"We can help you claim mastery of the imperial family here in Katzen. Either way, we will obey Your Highness's orders."

"You're already the mistress of Romagna, the First Prince's fiefdom. You have all the legitimacy you need."

Gallo attempted a mischievous wink.

"The boss arranged it all. How cunning—preparing everything so the marriage couldn't be delayed."

Medea nearly laughed despite herself.

Cesare Katzen... how far did this fierce, affectionate man plan ahead?

Warmth bloomed painfully in her chest.

"Your Highness, it's difficult—but please endure."

At that moment, urgent footsteps approached.

Neril appeared.

"Your Highness, a messenger has arrived from the Imperial Palace."

Finally, Perdiccas II had broken his patience and summoned Medea.

"Your Highness doesn't need to answer. We'll handle it."

They knew why the Emperor was calling—and there was no way the Princess should comply.

Terence turned immediately.

He intended to drive away the Emperor's messengers even if it gave the imperial family grounds to retaliate.

"No. I'll go."

Medea shook her head.

"Your Highness, but—"

Terence's displeasure was evident, but Medea's eyes remained steady.

"If we don't address this now, they'll keep coming back. You know that."

All the empire's attention was focused on the First Prince.

"Then I'll accompany you."

Gallo shrugged.

"The Prince's faction may be nominally under the Emperor, but is Facade? We're continental arms dealers. Why should we bow to any Emperor?"

At Gallo's words, Terence nodded reluctantly.

"Alpha, summon all Facade mercenary forces. I'll escort Her Highness to the Imperial Palace."

"Yes, Master Gallo."

They moved in unison and dispersed.

War clouds gathered over the palace.

◆

In the bedchamber where everyone had departed, darkness filled the silence.

Cesare lay with his eyes still closed.

Then—

His index finger twitched.

Just barely.

But it moved.
