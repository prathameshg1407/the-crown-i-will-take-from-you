Chapter 5

The Price
of Blood

"When death is done and witnesses arrive—
who will claim the sin, and who will pay the cost?"

Swish. Swish.

The sound of something heavy being dragged across the chapel floor echoed in the silence.

Medea kept her eyes squeezed shut—like a child desperately trying to keep a promise to herself.

"Ugh... ugh..."

Even when the sound of something breaking echoed from behind the altar.

Even when Jared's muffled groans finally ceased.

Thud.

Even when the body hit something with a dull, final sound.

Then—silence.

Complete. Absolute. The kind of quiet that follows only when life has been extinguished.

Medea opened her eyes.

She lowered her gaze to her trembling hands, turning them over slowly—as if searching for the last traces of humanity still clinging to her skin.

Thud. Thud.

Footsteps approached—then stopped just before her.

"...Ah."

The sharp, metallic scent of blood wafted toward her, accompanied by a voice thick with quiet despair.

Medea looked up.

Cesare stood before her, drenched in red. He seemed to notice his appearance for the first time—staring down at himself with something like regret.

"Wait here. Just a moment."

He turned sharply and strode toward a small side door attached to the chapel.

Creak. Creak. Creak.

The hinges protested as he disappeared through the doorway.

"AHHHHH! EUAAAAH!"

The priest—who had been unconscious—suddenly jolted awake with a blood-curdling scream.

His terrified cries echoed off the stone walls, reverberating through the chapel like the wail of a damned soul.

"Over here! I heard it here!"

Footsteps—hurried, anxious—thundered closer from somewhere beyond the chapel walls.

"Hah—huk... I'm dead! I'm dead! Euaaaah!"

Medea listened to the priest's convulsive screams with detached, hollow calm.

Through her fading vision, Jared's body came into clearer focus.

A pool of dark crimson spread outward from beneath him—thick, viscous, spreading like ink across the chapel floor.

He lay sprawled upon the altar where he had intended to bind her in marriage. Eyes closed. Hands folded as if in prayer.

He looked almost saintly—like a penitent seeking absolution.

He's really dead.

"Medea."

A hand seized her arm.

She raised her head slowly.

It was Jason—face pale, slicked with cold sweat.

* * *

Jason had run through the forest as though the hounds of hell were at his heels.

Please don't let me be too late. Please, please...

He hadn't known that Jared possessed the ability to alter the magic circle's coordinates independently.

The trackers he'd assigned to tail Jared had proven useless. King Peleus and the Agema guards were still scrambling to pierce the concealment spell.

If Jason hadn't threatened one of the high-ranking warlocks at swordpoint, extracting the chapel's location by force, he wouldn't have made it in time at all.

But when he finally burst through the chapel doors—slightly too late—Jason's face twisted in disgust.

Candles still flickering. A torn bridal veil discarded on the floor. A trembling, distraught priest.

That insane bastard...

He understood instantly what Jared had intended.

To forcibly marry Medea.

A worthless worm—a man who couldn't even reach her shadow—had dared to touch the woman Jason himself had never been able to claim, despite all his yearning.

Murderous rage surged through him. His grip tightened on his sword hilt.

For three years, I haven't missed a single day of training. I'll cut that bastard down myself—

"...Princess."

As Jason moved toward the altar, he caught sight of Medea—pale as death—and broke into a run.

He would swear later that in all his life, no distance had ever felt so agonizingly long.

"Medea."

She was alive. She was safe.

Relief flooded through him first—overwhelming, dizzying.

"Thank God."

He hadn't meant to say it aloud.

The arm he grasped was ice-cold. His heart pounded painfully against his ribs.

"Are you all right? Can you hear me? Do you recognize me?"

Medea's response was delayed—sluggish, as though she'd lost herself somewhere far away.

Jason had never seen her like this before.

His chest ached as he looked into those green eyes—staring blankly at Jared's corpse, unseeing, unhearing.

Then his gaze swept over her wounds. The blood staining her dress. Jared's lifeless body on the altar.

No. I'm too late.

Jason's expression twisted as he took in the torn marriage contract and the scattered gold rings.

"I... I'll take care of this."

Even as his heart bled, his calculating mind spoke.

This is my chance. The opportunity to finally have her by my side—the woman I've never been able to reach.

"Medea, choose me. I'll save you from this marriage."

Her green eyes shifted slowly to meet his.

He pressed on, voice urgent.

"My uncle won't let this stand. He'll make you pay. Are you really going to let yourself be shackled to someone like Jared—bound to him for the rest of your life?"

Silence.

"I will protect you. From my uncle. From everyone. If you just choose me—"

"Jason."

The sound of his name on her lips was achingly sweet.

"There are always conditions attached to your words."

Her voice was calm. Cool. Detached.

Even in this desperate situation—even now, when he'd come for her—she rejected him with the same quiet finality as always.

Jason's handsome face contorted as he read the meaning buried in that tone.

"Do you hate me that much? You find me more disgusting than that filthy wretch?"

Silence.

"Say something, Medea. You don't even know how I got here—"

She doesn't know I betrayed the remaining warlocks to find her.

"I can handle anything—anything—as long as I have you. I'll deal with Jared's death, too. Just say yes."

"There's no need to bargain, Jason."

A figure emerged from behind the curtain, pulling it aside with casual elegance.

Golden eyes gleamed against a face far too clean—eerily pristine compared to the bloodstained chaos around him.

His features were sculpted, aristocratic. A single streak of blood marred his temple—as though he'd forgotten to wipe it away.

Beneath the sharp line of his jaw, one crimson drop clung to his skin, creating an unsettling contrast.

"I did that."

The arrogant figure gestured toward the altar with effortless authority—his bearing unmistakable, regal.

He looked down at Jason as though addressing a subordinate.

"You..."

Jason's mouth fell open in astonishment.

He couldn't understand why Cesare was here. Couldn't process what he was saying.

No. My mind is refusing to accept this.

"Brother, if you wanted to force a bargain, you should have checked first who the goods already belong to."

The moment Cesare finished speaking, the chapel erupted into chaos.

"Your Highness! Your Highness the Princess!"

Voices—frantic, desperate—echoed through the chapel.

Behind the rushing figures, Medea could see piles of corpses littering the ground outside.

"Sister!"

Peleus.

His arms wrapped around her—warm, solid, protective. But the faint scent of blood clung to him and would not fade.

"I thought I was losing you again. I thought I'd lost you too."

Medea felt the despair radiating from him in the frantic rise and fall of his chest.

"Your Majesty, we were ambushed on the way. I'm sorry—I'm so sorry, Your Highness..."

Neril knelt, one arm drenched in blood, apologizing over and over.

Raju's rear guard—the trap he'd prepared—had not been easily overcome.

While Peleus had been frantic searching for his sister, assassins wielding strange black magic had delayed them at every turn.

Each one required precious time to subdue—time that had nearly cost Medea her life.

But it wasn't only the Valdina royal family who had arrived at the chapel.

"Dead."

The chamberlain of Emperor Perdiccas I—an elderly man with sharp, calculating eyes—examined the body, then wiped his hands on a pristine white handkerchief.

His gaze shifted between Jason and Cesare, both standing motionless as though frozen in place.

"Your Highness..."

Public Confession

"Your Majesty, there's no need for speculation. I killed him."

Medea lifted her head and looked at Cesare.

The old chamberlain—who had maintained his composure throughout the entire ordeal—stared at the First Prince as though unable to believe his own ears.

"Your Highness, the First Prince... are you confessing to the murder of General Jared?"

"That's correct."

There was no hesitation in his voice. No uncertainty.

Only absolute, unwavering conviction.

The chapel fell silent.

Every eye turned to Cesare—the First Prince of Katzen, heir to the throne, standing bloodstained and unapologetic before witnesses from two kingdoms.

He had just confessed to killing one of his father's generals.

The political ramifications were staggering.

And yet he stood there, golden eyes calm, as though he'd merely stated a simple fact.

Medea's gaze locked onto his.

He's shielding me. Taking the blame upon himself—knowing full well what it will cost him.

Her chest tightened.

She wanted to speak—to stop him, to claim responsibility herself.

But the words caught in her throat.

Because she understood.

This was his choice. His sacrifice.

And he had made it willingly—for her.

End Chapter 5
