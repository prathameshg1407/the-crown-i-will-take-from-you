Chapter 268

The Mirror
of Truth

"Sacred relics reveal what masks cannot hide."

The palace hummed with activity as preparations for the wedding reached their peak.

With the Queen Mother's passing, only Peleus and Medea remained of Valdina's royal bloodline. Since no female member of the royal family could preside over the princess's marriage ceremony, the Valdinian staff moved with extraordinary caution, determined to avoid the slightest misstep.

"Place that here, and secure that in the chest! Ensure everything is waterproofed—we cannot risk damage during transport."

Madame Pinatelli, the Chief Lady of the Imperial Household, directed the court ladies with exacting severity.

"I cannot help but worry whether Her Highness will fare well in that unfamiliar Katzen. The thought troubles me greatly."

"You needn't fret so much. Her Highness's husband is clearly devoted to her—surely that bodes well?"

The elder maids cast their gazes toward the towering stacks of gifts from the First Prince.

"Even with Emperor Katzen standing in opposition, the First Prince refuses to yield an inch. The entire imperial court's network of alliances now falls under his faction's influence."

Regardless of station, the truth of marriage remained universal.

If a husband proved to be a sturdy pillar, no storm could easily shake his wife's standing.

But these women knew from bitter experience: when circumstances reversed, even the mightiest walls crumbled from their foundations.

While Valdina's court ladies busied themselves with bridal preparations, the Princess's private chambers were consumed by matters of an entirely different nature.

"Your Highness... a messenger has arrived."

Saya approached Medea with unusual hesitation in her step.

From that tentative demeanor alone, Medea immediately recognized the source.

"From Theo?"

Saya nodded.

Several Days Earlier

A few days before Medea's departure from the kingdom, an unexpected visitor had sought an audience.

It was Theo.

The earnest young paladin had grown even bolder since their last meeting.

"Your Highness... there is something I must tell you."

After regarding Medea in silence for a long moment, he spoke with a tremor in his voice.

"I have no interest in proving myself to those who merely proclaim faith. Leave."

Medea issued the dismissal with her customary detachment.

Theo's jaw tightened.

Every word from the Princess of Valdina seemed to pierce his heart with a blade of ice.

"...You know it too, don't you?"

"Know what?"

"You sensed it. I'm certain of it. You were the only one who recognized her deception."

Medea paused.

"Are you referring to your Saintess?"

"She is not my Saintess."

Theo's response came sharp and absolute as a blade's edge.

Even Medea found herself momentarily taken aback by such decisive conviction—leaving no room for doubt or hesitation.

'I feel as though I was meant to say those words.'

This irresistible compulsion—his body moving before his mind could intervene—continued to haunt Theo.

Had he not exercised such rigid self-control, he might have fallen to his knees before the Princess of Valdina the moment he laid eyes upon her.

No—it had felt as though that was precisely what he was supposed to do.

Reverence and devotion had surged through him unbidden, like a stray hound finally discovering its true master.

'These emotions are not my own.'

Even amid the chaos of reason and feeling warring within him, Theo understood exactly what was wrong.

"I intend to uncover the truth about the Saintess."

What Theo extended toward her was a small badge fashioned in the shape of a fox.

"A young awakened priest who served at Saintess Rachel's side has recently vanished. No one is searching for him."

"It's strange—Noah disappeared right after he met with the Saintess. *Sob*... No one will listen to me, Sir. *Sob*..."

The last person that child priest—who had narrowly escaped when Rachel fabricated her false oracle—had sought out was Theo.

"We have always believed that when the Saintess appeared, the Holy Kingdom would restore justice and the world would be saved. But what is the reality?"

Theo pressed forward.

"The Holy Kingdom grows ever more arbitrary. The monster waves intensify with each passing season. A dark, malevolent aura spreads through the temple where sanctity should reign."

"..."

"I cannot believe this is mere coincidence. You must sense it as well. Is the Saintess we've placed our faith in truly a 'Saintess' at all?"

Medea shattered his expectations with her indifferent tone.

"How would I know such things? Perhaps you've sought the wrong audience. The Pope or the High Priest might prove more receptive."

"Did you truly see nothing? Am I alone in perceiving their corruption?"

Theo's eyes reddened.

His voice brimmed with the despair of one whose final sanctuary had crumbled to dust.

"I have only just escaped the chains of sacrifice myself. I have no desire to be branded a wicked woman who enchants paladins."

Medea swept past him without a backward glance.

"I want no part in those who lack the courage to verify the truth of the justice they proclaim."

Struck by her parting words, Theo lowered his head in silence.

Only when his staggering, defeated figure had vanished from sight did Medea turn.

"This belonged to Noah."

Kensington approached, indicating the fox-shaped badge Theo had left behind.

"The operative you dispatched to the Holy Land?"

Dried blood stained the badge in sinister patterns. Kensington's expression darkened.

"Yes. I had already grown suspicious when communication ceased... and now this."

His face was grim.

"What progress on the investigation into Rachel?"

"Forgive me, Your Highness. We've uncovered nothing concrete. It appears only Theo has witnessed anything unusual."

If he alone had detected what the Saintess concealed—anomalies that even the most perceptive intelligence operatives had failed to notice...

"Perhaps so."

"Your Highness, if Rachel is not truly the Saintess, why would she sacrifice the priests?"

Kensington's curiosity was evident.

"We shall discover that soon enough."

Medea shrugged, her gaze drifting toward where Theo had disappeared.

"It seems he's begun to understand on his own."

Theo—a man bound by unwavering principle.

Even if his first step had been misguided, once he recognized his error, no one pursued the truth more relentlessly.

As though he had finally grasped the reality that had eluded him.

And not long afterward, Theo's message reached Saya:

Saya—

I have witnessed Saintess Rachel using dark enchantments to enslave the priests, transforming them into her puppets. The magic she employs appears to be of the blackest sort.

This poses a grave danger to the entire continent. If you can find it in your heart to forgive my earlier foolishness, please convey this to the Princess.

The letter, hastily scrawled on parchment, bore all the marks of someone writing in desperate haste—likely to evade the Saintess's watchful eyes.

Rachel employs black magic?

Medea's index finger tapped rhythmically against the table.

The practitioners of dark magic—those who drew their power from the primordial darkness—stood in direct opposition to the Holy Kingdom, which served the Goddess who had sought to extinguish that very darkness.

The Saintess for whom the continent had waited so long... was in truth a dark sorceress.

Such a revelation could overturn the Holy Kingdom entirely.

'Which means Rachel's divine powers may well be counterfeit.'

In her previous life, the only occasion Rachel had displayed her full abilities was during her initial appearance on the continent as Saintess.

After becoming Empress of Katzen, she had departed the Holy Land and rarely exercised her powers thereafter.

The increasingly frequent monster waves. Divine power that manifested only at suspiciously convenient moments.

And that black radiance Medea had witnessed emanating from Rachel's body with her own eyes...

"If the source was actually dark magic, everything would finally make sense."

A sharp gleam entered Medea's eyes.

The Princess's Palace

"Priestess Anastasia, may I set this down here?"

Anastasia, the Pope's senior attendant, had accompanied Medea's entourage to Valdina at His Holiness's command.

She was presently assisting the palace maids in arranging the ceremonial weapons that had arrived among the wedding gifts.

"No, that seat must not be touched. It would be better to transport it within a protective chest—otherwise the consecration will remain incomplete."

At that moment, Anastasia looked up as a shadow fell across her.

It was Princess Medea.

"Priestess Anastasia."

"Oh! My apologies—I hadn't realized it had grown so late..."

Among her duties was instructing Medea in the doctrines of the temple—a mandatory ritual for any member of the imperial family who would participate in ceremonies at the Central Shrine.

"No, you mentioned we would forgo today's lesson. I wished to ask you something separately."

"Ask me...?"

Anastasia's expression turned puzzled.

Throughout their journey—even after arriving in Valdina alongside the Holy Land's delegation—the beautiful Princess had maintained her composed reserve.

This was the first time she had sought anything from Anastasia directly.

"Please, ask whatever you wish."

Since the one she served was already well-disposed toward the Princess, Anastasia resolved to answer whatever she could.

"Among the sacred relics of the temple, are there any capable of detecting corruption?"

The Princess's voice dropped to something softer.

"Corruption such as dark magic."

"Did you say dark magic?"

Anastasia tilted her head in curiosity.

'She wishes to know about dark magic—a practice that has all but vanished from the continent?'

Then again, this was the Princess who had hunted down the sacrifice herself rather than submit to the oracle's demands. Naturally, her interests would differ from most.

"Hmm... Among the relics housed in the Central Temple, there is the Covenant Cup..."

The Covenant Cup consisted of twin vessels—one wrought of gold, the other of silver.

The gold symbolized the protection of the male god; the silver represented the life force of the goddess.

Together, they formed a sacred bond when both parties drank holy water from their respective cups.

"It is said that since the Goddess herself blessed this relic, anyone tainted by dark magic who touches it will find their corruption impossible to conceal—the stain reveals itself and lingers."

As the Pope's most trusted aide, Anastasia possessed knowledge of the temple's inner workings that ordinary clergy never learned.

"Ah—and there is also the Mirror of Origin."

Anastasia's brow furrowed in recollection before her face brightened.

"It is said that the sacred relic housed in the Hall of Mirrors reflects the true nature of all things. Whether one wears a mask or a helm, it strips away every disguise. During the Holy Wars, our knights employed this relic to expose traitors who had infiltrated the Holy Land."

"Do both relics remain functional?"

"Of course. However, so much time has passed since the Holy Wars that they are scarcely used anymore. Only the eldest priests would remember them."

Anastasia added with evident pride:

"Moreover, the Holy Land of today has grown incomparably stronger than it was during the Wars. It is not a place where evildoers can easily conceal themselves."

"..."

Medea made no attempt to shatter that conviction.

"But Your Highness, why do you ask such things...?"

"Ah. I wished to inquire about certain wedding preparations."

Medea bit her lip, her expression suggesting hesitation.

"But I feared I might be imposing further upon the generosity His Holiness has already shown me..."

"Not at all! His Holiness has instructed us to accommodate Your Highness's wishes to the fullest extent possible!"

Anastasia waved away the concern.

Many grew arrogant when granted the Pope's favor, yet this Princess remained consistently humble and thoughtful.

As a devout believer, it was only natural to feel affection for such a person.

A strange light kindled in Medea's green eyes.

'Let us test you, Rachel.'

Was it truly divine power that has sustained you all this time?
