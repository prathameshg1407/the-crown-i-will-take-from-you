Chapter 168

The Second
Empire

"From the ashes of a kingdom rises the crown of an empire."

"...Very well."

The Saintess answered with a flush upon her cheeks. Her head remained bowed, her expression hidden from view—but her fingers gripped the white fabric of her skirt with such force that her knuckles had gone pale.

The Templar Commander swallowed a weary sigh, satisfied that he had at least extracted a positive response.

"Those—those ignorant savages dare insult the Saintess?!"

"Your Eminence, step away from the window."

The Commander firmly closed the curtain on the rampaging Paul.

"Drive faster! Make haste!"

They fled Valdina as though demons nipped at their heels. The majestic bearing they had displayed upon their arrival had vanished entirely.

"Your Highness."

Neril's steady voice reached Medea where she stood upon the spire, her gaze fixed upon the Holy Nation's retreating carriage.

You and I shall meet again soon.

It was merely intuition—yet she felt with bone-deep certainty that Rachel would not surrender so easily.

Medea did not avert her eyes until the carriage vanished beyond the distant horizon.

Like a predator watching its prey.

Time flowed onward, and the minor commotion caused by the newly awakened Saintess was soon buried beneath the demands of daily governance.

At last, the day arrived for the proclamation of Valdina's empire.

"Long live His Majesty Emperor Peleus!"

Peleus raised his hand in greeting from the palace balcony following the coronation ceremony. The Emperor's crown blazed with light above his silver hair, which shimmered like captured moonlight.

His mantle—adorned with golden tassels—rippled in the wind, and the newly designed Valdinian imperial emblem upon his chest radiated quiet majesty.

In his other hand, he held a blade of exquisite craftsmanship—sharp, elegant, deadly.

The citizens filling the streets before the royal palace erupted in thunderous celebration at the sight of their young, powerful, handsome Emperor.

The small northern kingdom of Valdina had become the second empire on the continent—in name and in truth.

"I never imagined I would live to witness such a day..."

Tears traced silent paths through Marquis Gilliforth's silver beard.

"Marquis, there you are."

Madam Pinatelli approached, offering her handkerchief.

"Your own eyes seem rather red as well, Madam."

"I cannot help but wonder... how moved Her Majesty the Queen Dowager would have been to behold this sight..."

She too dabbed at her tears.

The Queen Dowager—who would have rejoiced more than anyone at her kingdom's elevation to imperial status, standing equal with mighty Katzen—was not present for this triumph.

"Had it not been for Duke Claudio's treachery, she would never have suffered such heartbreak."

Madam Pinatelli's lips pressed into a bitter line.

The Queen Dowager had been devastated to learn the truth—that her own brother had not only threatened her life and conspired to murder her grandchildren, but had been colluding with the enemy nation of Katzen all along.

She had barely recovered from the wound she sustained while shielding Medea, and her former vitality never returned.

"Pinatelli... the ancestors are summoning this old woman. How can I face them with such shame upon my name?"

"Your Majesty..."

"Take my place. Protect that child. Please... carry this poor old woman's burden as well..."

Not long after taking to her bed, the Queen Dowager had passed from this world.

It seemed only yesterday that black mourning curtains had shrouded every corner of the kingdom, and the nation had been plunged into profound grief.

"Long live Valdina! May the great Valdinian Empire endure for eternity!"

Jubilation erupted throughout the streets.

Among the Valdinian crowds moved people of markedly different appearance—the indigenous inhabitants of the El Amuz Strait.

"Long live Emperor Peleus! Long live Valdina!"

They too raised their voices in celebration.

Foreign traders newly arrived in Valdina watched the festivities with bewildered expressions.

"Are those not the El Amuzians? Why do they celebrate when Valdina has claimed their lands and declared itself an empire?"

"Ah, friend—if you wish to understand something, you must learn the whole of it, not merely half."

A native merchant clicked his tongue in mild exasperation.

"This strait has become the most active trading hub on the entire continent."

When Peleus and the Agemas had wrested control of the strait from Katzen, they had immediately established their presence.

"El Amuz is Valdinian territory now, and its people are my subjects."

Yet unlike Katzen—which had ruled through fear and subjugation—Valdina extended genuine favor to the local population.

They recognized the indigenous peoples' status, permitted them to continue their traditional livelihoods, and opened the strait as a center of exchange.

With outsiders flowing in from countless lands for commerce, the borders naturally blurred—and true coexistence became possible.

"Travel through El Amuz, and you need not circumnavigate by sea. With military forces stationed here, there is no fear of corruption or lawlessness. Naturally, merchants from across the continent have gathered in droves."

The merchant shrugged matter-of-factly.

"It will not be long before Valdina becomes the dominant power on the continent. Monster waves no longer threaten our borders, and domestic prosperity has been restored."

"Indeed! And did not a Saintess awaken upon Valdinian soil? Surely the Goddess bestows her protection upon this land."

"That remains to be seen."

While others nodded in agreement, the palace merchant shook his head with surprising solemnity.

"Do you truly believe Katzen will stand idle while a new empire rises to challenge them? The Holy Nation may feign support, but behind closed doors, they conspire to steal our intelligence."

He shared what he had overheard while delivering goods to the palace—without embellishment.

"That Saintess attempted to frame Her Highness the Imperial Princess, sowing discord while gathering information about Valdina's geography and defenses."

"Truly? How could a Saintess commit such acts! I cannot believe even the Holy Nation stands against Valdina..."

"In such turbulent times, where can one find genuine favor? Those who prosper attract those who wish to share in that prosperity—nothing more. The Holy Land is no different."

The merchant's expression grew grave.

"Steel yourselves. For the foreseeable future, every power on the continent will seek to check Valdina's rise."

The foreign trader frowned at this sobering warning, wondering if he had chosen an unfortunate time to establish trade.

But another merchant—one who had operated a shop in mainland Valdina for many years—spoke with quiet conviction.

"Even with all these restrictions and challenges, I remain alive. Three years ago, I was starving—uncertain whether I would have bread to eat come morning."

As supplies had dwindled during the long war, the people's desperation had grown louder with each passing day. Those had been anxious, sorrowful times. Even now, remembering how he had endured made his vision swim.

"If Valdina can maintain this prosperity, I would ask for nothing more. Truly."

"Yes, that is the heart of it. To think we would live to see such abundance..."

"May fortune smile upon us, and may this momentum continue..."

The sun set upon the day of proclamation, each citizen carrying their own hopes and concerns into the twilight.

The Villa at El Amuz Strait

The celebration commemorating Valdina's imperial proclamation was held not within the royal castle, but at a magnificent villa constructed upon the Strait of El Amuz.

The Continental Festival of Saint Augustine—hosted annually by the Holy See—was also scheduled to take place in El Amuz this year.

Thus, congratulatory delegations from nations across the continent, alongside hundreds of distinguished guests, had converged upon this place.

As dusk painted the sky in shades of amber and violet, a procession of colorful carriages queued before the villa's entrance.

Attendees arrayed in resplendent evening attire emerged one by one, their finery catching the fading light.

The carriages, having discharged their distinguished passengers, circled the grand white fountain before departing.

"Merciful heavens..."

Soft exclamations escaped the guests as they beheld the banquet hall, illuminated by cascading chandeliers that scattered light like fallen stars.

The villa's interior proclaimed the new empire's status with unapologetic grandeur.

Floors of gleaming marble stretched beneath their feet. Walls bore elaborate mosaic tapestries depicting Valdina's founding myths in vivid color and meticulous detail.

Upon the left wall stood a statue of the Goddess carved from pure white marble, her hands extended in benevolent welcome.

Behind her, an enormous circular mirror reflected the entire banquet hall—as though the divine herself surveyed those who had gathered in celebration.

"Valdina's rise is truly remarkable. I never anticipated they would ascend so swiftly..."

"The Republic of Les and the Kingdom of Serses have both dispatched envoys. Are those two not among Katzen's oldest allies? The Katzenians must be positively seething to watch them scramble here so eagerly."

The endless stream of arriving delegations drew considerable attention and whispered commentary.

Medea stood at her brother's side, welcoming each delegation with practiced grace.

When Peleus had been proclaimed Emperor, his sister had been elevated accordingly—she now bore the title of Imperial Princess.
