Chapter 348

Behind
Closed Doors

"Even the strongest fortress cracks from within."

Outside the First Prince's chambers, a tense standoff unfolded.

"I must see Her Highness."

Marquis Leniard's voice was muffled with desperation.

Beneath his gray hair, his wrinkled forehead glistened with cold sweat. His feet shifted restlessly—betraying his anxiety.

"Step back, Marquis."

When the Marquis advanced, Gallo blocked his path.

Undeterred, the old man tried to sidestep—but Gallo moved with him, an immovable wall.

The Marquis collided with Gallo's iron-hard frame and staggered, nearly losing his balance.

"What are you doing? Chief of the Facade, have you forgotten His Excellency is an old man? He could have been seriously injured!"

The Marquis's knight protested indignantly.

Gallo shrugged.

"My apologies. But it was His Excellency who tried to force his way through."

"Marquis—"

The knight turned to Terence for support.

Instead of reprimanding Gallo, Terence addressed the Marquis directly, his tone politely firm.

"Your Excellency, Her Highness is attending to His Highness. Now is not a suitable time. Please return later."

"How many times have I been turned away? I must speak with her today!"

Unable to contain himself, Marquis Leniard raised his voice.

"How long do you think you can avoid this by barricading the door? You're only making things worse. This isn't the time to shield your master's eyes and ears in the name of loyalty!"

"What's the matter, Your Excellency? If you have so much time to waste here, why not use it to find a better solution?"

"Terence! How dare you—"

Their voices rose, echoing through the corridor.

"What's going on?"

Amid the commotion, the First Princess emerged from the bedchamber.

Her complexion was slightly paler than usual, and she'd lost some weight—but her noble bearing remained undisturbed.

Marquis Leniard caught his breath involuntarily.

In mere weeks, a cruel oracle had commanded her husband to wed another woman. That same husband, refusing the divine decree, had been struck down and now lay unconscious.

Yet he could detect no trace of despair or agitation in her expression.

Even the Marquis marveled at her composure—standing firm in the face of any storm.

This woman is truly fit to be Empress.

It was a shame he could no longer see such an unshakable leader ascend the throne.

But—

Not everything in this world bends to our will.

So it had been with his daughter, the late Empress Eleanor.

She'd pursued a forbidden relationship with her brother-in-law, desperate to see her bloodline crowned Emperor—only to die of illness before witnessing the fruits of her ambition.

It's the same now. Someone must play the villain.

His daughter's inflexibility had cost him her life.

He could not allow Cesare to meet the same end.

How far has His Highness come... how much persecution and suffering did he endure three years ago!

He could not lose the conqueror who would go down in history.

"Your Highness, please."

Marquis Leniard bowed deeply.

His usually unbending spine bent lower than it ever had.

He knew what he was about to ask was excessive—perhaps unforgivable.

"Please... allow the Prince to accept the oracle."

Medea looked down at him in silence.

The towering pillar that had supported the First Prince's faction now seemed so small and fragile.

Perhaps it was the tears clinging to his beard.

When the Princess remained silent, the Marquis's anxiety intensified.

Thud.

He dropped to his knees.

"Your Excellency!"

"This old man begs you most earnestly."

His escort tried to lift him, but the Marquis knelt firmly before Medea, pressing his forehead to the floor.

"As a subject, I know I speak of disloyalty that should never be uttered. I know I ask too much patience of Your Highness. But—"

His voice caught. Finally, with trembling lips, he forced out his innermost plea.

"Scorned though he may be... he is my last remaining blood."

Silence.

"When His Highness the First Prince recovers, he will never forget your sacrifice. Please, just this once—overlook this transgression. Isn't saving his life the priority?"

"Marquis, you're too old for this."

Gallo seized the old man's arm and hauled him roughly to his feet.

He showed no concern for the Marquis's swaying body.

His usually smiling face was rigid with barely suppressed fury.

Terence's rebuke was equally sharp.

"Who saved Cesare's life three years ago? After the Iceberg accident, if not for Her Highness, he would have died. Yet you dare make such a demand?"

"That's precisely why I'm asking—"

The Marquis's voice trembled.

"Your Highness, surely you—wise as you are—cannot be unaware of how dire the situation has become!"

Medea let out a short, mirthless laugh.

Though she could read the old Marquis's desperation clearly, she couldn't help but find dark humor in it.

That he'd invoked the topic of blood meant he was truly desperate.

"Marquis. When someone who pledges loyalty to his master's blood kneels and presses his forehead to the ground, how dare you call it a 'request'?"

This is coercion dressed as a plea.

Her voice remained calm but unyielding.

Marquis Leniard swallowed hard.

"You're straining yourself. Don't push too hard, and go back. I appreciate the Marquis's concern for His Highness. If there's good news, I'll certainly inform you."

Her tone was gentle, even thoughtful—yet utterly final.

"Your Highness..."

"Gallo, Terence—please see the Marquis out. Travel safely, Your Excellency."

The First Princess turned and retreated into the chamber.

Click.

The door closed.

Not a single concession to the Marquis's kneeling plea.

Did you truly think our Highness would yield to pressure from old worms like you?

Gallo glared at the Marquis, who stood dazed and swaying.

"Ahh..."

The Marquis groaned, lowering his head.

He had abandoned pride and dignity—and gained nothing.

Worse, this incident had irrevocably branded him with the First Princess's distrust.

His legs trembled.

"I'll escort you—"

"Leave me."

The Marquis roughly shook off Terence's supporting arm.

Terence's voice turned cold.

"Your Excellency, you must never forget—you are merely a servant of Cesare."

"You—!"

The Marquis's eyes widened.

Then he sighed heavily.

"Do you think I want this?"

That even the gentlemanly Terence had struck at the Marquis so harshly proved how furious he was.

Silence.

The Marquis staggered down the corridor.

Heavy regret followed in his wake.

◆◆◆

Creak.

Theo looked up at the sound of the door closing.

Medea stood just inside, motionless.

"Your Highness..."

Theo, who'd been standing vigil beside Cesare's bed, was at a loss.

One of the young knight's arms was still bandaged. Scars marked his face, yet they couldn't diminish the nobility characteristic of a Temple Knight.

"Your Highness, about the Marquis—"

"Yes, Theo. Continue watching over Cesare. I'll handle it."

He'd heard everything from inside the room.

Gallo and Terence arguing with Marquis Leniard.

Medea's resolute voice rejecting the Marquis's tearful pleas.

Had she stood behind the door the entire time?

Shrouded in shadows against the wall, she'd seemed so small, so perilously fragile as she'd drawn breath.

Medea released a quiet sigh.

Then she walked to the bed where Cesare lay.

"Continue. Did you sense anything?"

She spoke casually—as though returning to the conversation before the Marquis's voice had interrupted.

"Yes, Your Highness. I don't sense the same stench as Rachel."

Theo was the only one who'd been able to detect Rachel's dark aura alongside Medea.

She'd summoned him hoping—grasping at straws—that examining Cesare might reveal something.

"But that doesn't mean there's nothing. It's... bizarre."

Theo's frown deepened.

The energy he sensed at his fingertips was strangely tangled.

"I know something's there, but it's covered by something immensely powerful. I don't think we can unravel it..."

"They must have done this..."

Theo felt increasingly devastated.

Helpless. Pathetic.

"If Commander Leonor were here... would he have found something? Could he have helped?"

Silence.

Medea nodded calmly.

"Yes. You may go."

There was no blame for Theo's inability to find a cure.

Yet her eyes remained fixed on the First Prince's still form.

Theo hesitated.

Medea's gaze toward her husband made him feel as though he'd ceased to exist entirely.

Before leaving, Theo glanced back.

Medea sat at the edge of the bed, reaching toward the First Prince.

She looked down at him with a fragile, tender expression Theo had never seen before.

When she gently cupped Cesare's sleeping face, Theo's heart lurched.

He quickly stepped out and closed the door.

Yet his heart still pounded violently.

A sudden, intense jealousy surged through him—directed at the man she touched, the man she gazed upon with such devotion.

He even thought, for one dark moment, that he wished he were the one lying there instead of the First Prince.

Can I truly say this burning jealousy is merely reverence for the Saintess?

He couldn't be certain.

And that uncertainty terrified him.
