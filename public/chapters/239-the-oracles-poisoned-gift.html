"When gods speak through mortal channels, even truth becomes a weapon."

Luca extended his small finger, and pale light bloomed from the tip like a tiny star being born.

The moment that faint luminescence appeared, the Saintess's expression transformed—her face brightening with an almost predatory delight.

This child will do nicely.

"What a precious heart you possess. Indeed, I am always in need of devoted souls to assist me."

Rachel's smile was warm as summer sunlight—and just as blinding to those who failed to notice its falseness.

"Luca? What are you doing here at this hour?"

A voice cut through the darkness from the far end of the corridor. A young man in priest's vestments approached with swift, purposeful strides. He appeared to be around sixteen, with a sturdy build that spoke of labor rather than privilege.

"Brother Noah! Brother!"

Luca's face lit up with excitement.

"Oh—Saintess, this is Brother Noah! He entered the temple alongside me. He's incredible—just recently, he healed a wounded dog using nothing but his power! He's going to become a Temple Knight someday, I just know it—"

"Luca!"

The young priest closed the distance between them in three quick strides. His hand clamped firmly over Luca's mouth.

Too late, Luca remembered his brother's stern warning: never speak of the healing to anyone.

"Forgive me, Saintess."

Noah's voice was carefully deferential as he bowed.

"This child is impetuous by nature and has caused a disturbance by wandering the halls at such an improper hour. I humbly beg your pardon."

He straightened, then turned and plucked the candlestick from Luca's grasp with practiced efficiency.

"Return to your quarters. Now."

"But Brother—"

"Immediately. You haven't even completed the tasks I assigned you. I shall have to report your negligence to the senior priest if this continues."

Luca stared at Noah in bewilderment. His brother had always been gentle with him—patient, kind, protective. He had never once spoken so harshly.

He's trying to push me aside. He knows I've awakened my powers—he wants to curry favor with the Saintess for himself.

Bitter resentment welled up in Luca's young heart.

Of course Noah's divine power far exceeded his own meager light. The Saintess would naturally choose the stronger vessel.

At last—at last—the Goddess had granted Luca a chance to stand beside his idol. And now that chance was being snatched away before his very eyes.

"Just you wait!"

The furious child struck Noah's shoulder with a small fist and fled into the shadows.

Silence pooled in the corridor where only two figures remained.

"So young, yet already capable of healing through holy power. Truly, a remarkable talent has graced our temple."

Rachel was the first to break the stillness. Her voice carried notes of admiration—though something more calculating lurked beneath the honeyed tones.

"At this rate, you may well become the youngest High Priest in living memory."

"You are far too generous, Saintess."

The young man ducked his head, fidgeting with the hem of his vestments.

"It happened quite by chance—I have no notion of what the future holds. And given my origins on the streets, my standing here is... precarious at best."

As he spoke, his fingers unconsciously brushed against the string at his waist. A small badge hung there—fashioned in the shape of a red fox.

Rachel's gaze traveled slowly over the young priest, assessing every detail with cool satisfaction.

Larger. Stronger. Far more perfect.

If this one could already manifest healing power, his reserves far surpassed the pitiful glimmer the child had produced.

Better still—he was a street orphan. No family to raise questions. No connections to investigate a disappearance.

With my current position, making a lowborn priest vanish would be child's play. As simple as silencing that little boy just now.

Rachel's expression softened into something approaching maternal warmth. She looked, in that moment, like someone who had finally found the last piece of an intricate puzzle.

"Would you be willing to show me? I believe I could offer more meaningful guidance if I witnessed your power firsthand..."

The Saintess's gracious offer brought Lord Kensington's warning rushing back to the forefront of Noah's mind:

"I never imagined your awakened power would prove useful in this manner. Regardless—the Saintess may grow suspicious if you draw too close. Maintain your distance. Observe only."

"Yes, my Lord."

We should never have become entangled in this to begin with.

The instant he had spotted Luca standing before the Saintess, every fiber of his being had screamed of danger.

His body had moved before thought could catch up—pure instinct driving him to intervene.

Noah swallowed a silent sigh.

No matter. I can use this opportunity to draw closer to the target. Far better than returning to my Lord empty-handed.

He had never intended to truly join the temple. The powers he had stumbled upon by chance were merely tools—means to an end in service of his true mission.

"As you wish, Saintess."

Noah followed Rachel into the shadows.

Crunch.

The door sealed shut behind them.

Several Days Later — The Central Temple of Sacramontis

The sky stretched overhead, crystalline and bright.

The Central Temple of the Holy Nation Sacramontis stood as the beating heart of continental faith—the sacred sanctuary of the Church itself. Within its hallowed halls, divine relics pulsing with holy power were safeguarded. Oracles were pronounced from its chambers. And above all, the Pope—supreme authority of the Church—made his residence here.

Every temple and monastery scattered across the Middle Continent operated according to the sacred laws that emanated from this place.

Deep within the temple's innermost sanctum lay the Chamber of Oracle: a sealed space accessible only to those bearing the highest clearances. At its center stood a massive stone monument, its surface smooth and ancient.

The stele glowed only at moments chosen by the divine.

But now—

Ssssss...

Fragments of stone began to crumble from the monument's gleaming surface.

The elderly priest who had been dozing in his vigil snapped awake, eyes flying wide at the radiance pouring from the sacred stone.

"Wh—what—?!"

Scrape. Scrape-scratch.

Stone fell away in a cascade of dust. The bare surface beneath was eroding—reshaping itself—and with each passing moment, strokes of ancient script carved themselves into existence.

"An oracle! The Goddess speaks!"

The priest lurched to his feet and ran.

At the Same Moment — High Priest Paul's Chambers

"Mmm."

Paul rose languidly from his bed, savoring the golden sunlight streaming through the window.

Unlike his more devout colleagues—who rose before dawn to kneel in the chapel—Paul had turned over several times beneath his silken sheets before deigning to greet the morning.

"Your Eminence."

A junior priest stood waiting, proffering a towel dampened with warm water and a crystal vial of shaving oil.

Though he had been treated with considerable disdain during his time in Valdina, High Priest Paul was in truth one of the most formidable powers within the Central Temple. He presided over the council of sixteen High Priests—a body whose authority was second only to the Pope himself.

"Ah, yes. Thank you. This almost feels like being home again."

The fragrant scent of freshly pressed olives lifted Paul's spirits as the razor glided smoothly across his jaw.

Swoosh. Swoosh.

The atmosphere surrounding him was peaceful, almost meditative. He could not recall the last time he had felt so thoroughly content.

The anxieties of Valdina—that endless parade of shocking incidents—seemed to belong to another lifetime entirely.

"I do believe today shall prove to be an excellent day."

The door burst open without warning.

"Your Eminence! Catastrophe!"

"Ow—!"

Paul's hand jerked, the razor nicking his chin. Blood welled up, bright against pale skin.

"Good heavens—what in the name of the Goddess could possibly warrant such—"

The chief priest, heedless of the fury blazing in Paul's eyes, leaned close and whispered urgently into his ear.

"What? Can... can this possibly be true?"

In the span of a single minute, a kaleidoscope of expressions flickered across Paul's face. First confusion. Then alarm—his complexion draining to chalk white. And finally...

Something bright kindled in his eyes.

"Truly? That is what it proclaimed?"

Laughter bubbled up from his throat—thin and sharp, unmistakably derisive.

"Bring me my vestments. I must attend His Holiness at once."

✦ ✦ ✦

A procession of High Priests followed Pope Benedict down the sweeping marble staircase.

The Chamber of Oracle was already teeming with activity. No one present could suppress the flush of excitement rising to their cheeks. Even the most venerable priests—men who had witnessed decades of history and rarely permitted themselves displays of emotion—examined each carved stroke upon the stone's surface with barely concealed wonder.

"It has been so long since the Goddess Herself deigned to grant us revelation in this manner."

"Indeed. First She blessed us with the appearance of a Saintess—and now She chooses to address us directly."

"It is all thanks to the Saintess's presence. Since the divine agent has manifested among us, the traces of Her grace—so long faded—are returning one by one."

High Priest Paul added his voice to the chorus, his words dripping with fervent piety:

"O Goddess, I stand ready to follow Your sacred will! Grant Your humble servants the wisdom to find light even in the deepest darkness!"

His excessive veneration of the Saintess was considered unusual even among his peers—yet today, no one thought to reprimand him.

The radiance emanating from the ancient stele still flooded the chamber with ethereal light.

For decades—generations—no oracle had been inscribed upon that stone. Over time, it had come to serve primarily as a symbol: a monument to the grandeur of the old faith, rather than an active conduit of divine communication.

But now, this was an opportunity to reassert the Holy Nation's supremacy.

Countless heretical sects had risen in recent centuries, emboldened by the waning of traditional faith, daring to challenge the Church's ancient authority. Some had even styled themselves vessels of"salvation," persecuting the faithful in the name of false gods.

"This is irrefutable proof that the Goddess's power resides within our Church! Her will flows through our sacred institution once more!"

Jubilation rippled through the assembled clergy—all except one.

"Your Holiness, you seem troubled. Should this not be cause for celebration?"

Pope Benedict alone maintained his composure amidst the fervor.

The elderly pontiff, resplendent in his elaborate gold-embroidered miter, appeared as though he had stepped directly from an illuminated manuscript of ancient scripture. His expression remained utterly impassive—so still, in fact, that one might sooner mistake him for a ruthless sovereign than a compassionate shepherd of souls.

"No, it merely strikes me as... rather sudden."

What could the Goddess possibly wish to convey through a message carved without warning into sacred stone? After decades of silence, why now? Why in this manner?

"Your Holiness. The interpretation of the oracle has been completed."

A priest approached, his face pale, scroll trembling in his hands.

The Divine Oracle

"Soon, the entire continent shall be consumed
by fire and ash."

"Make Medea, Daughter of Valdina,
the Bride of the Evil Dragon."

The jubilation died as swiftly as it had risen.

One by one, the faces of the High Priests who had read the oracle turned to stone.
