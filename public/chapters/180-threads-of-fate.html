"Every soul carries invisible strings—some to guide, others to strangle."

Peleus sighed, the sound heavy with unspoken concern.

"Yes... that's exactly it."

He possessed infinite trust in his sister. If Medea declared something necessary, sound reasoning undoubtedly existed.

Yet despite her delicate, fragile appearance, his younger sister maintained fierce independence—never requesting her brother's assistance.

You still insist on shouldering everything alone...

An invisible barrier stood between the siblings—thin, transparent, yet insurmountable.

He could see Medea clearly, feel her presence tangibly—but could never reach the guarded chambers of her heart.

He couldn't voice these observations. This distance, after all, resulted from Peleus's own failures—leaving his young sister alone when she needed him most.

"Dea, your intellect surpassed mine long ago. I simply worry that your foolish brother might inadvertently disrupt the intricate game you've arranged."

Peleus exhaled slowly, concealing the bitterness seeping into his chest.

◆

In night's deepest hours, Zeta emerged from the villa.

He'd been moving silently through shadows when he suddenly halted in the darkness.

"How is Medea?"

"I came to see you."

Cesare regarded Zeta with blank inscrutability, sharp golden eyes scanning him from crown to heel.

"I have a question—"

"How was she?"

The abrupt query caught Zeta off-guard.

Haven't you already seen her?

Zeta wanted to voice that question despite its overly general nature. You witnessed her at the banquet—why ask me?

"She didn't inquire about my whereabouts?"

No.

Zeta bit his lip, suppressing the automatic response.

"What about the letter she left for Acares?"

There wasn't one.

Zeta clamped his mouth shut again as the answer nearly escaped unbidden.

"..."

Silence stretched taut between them.

"Your Highness—"

"Enough."

The cold rebuke cut like winter wind.

Unable to comprehend what had displeased his master, Zeta fidgeted with the medicine pouch in his arms.

"What's that?"

A low, rich voice emerged from darkness.

"Ah, yes. Count Raju attempted to poison Valdina's Emperor. This is the substance."

He indicated the pouch containing powdered medicine.

"Prolonged use reportedly induces ferocity and choleric temperament."

"Raju truly knows no fear. He tried again even after Valdina's elevation to empire?"

Gallo shook his head in disbelief. He felt grudging admiration for Claudio and Raju's relentless persistence.

"So, what are your orders?"

"She didn't request anything elaborate—merely instructed the agent she'd placed in Claudio's household to administer this medicine to Count Raju."

"What? Truly?"

Gallo looked as though doubting his own ears.

Cesare, showing no surprise, remained composed. Only his well-shaped eyebrows rose fractionally.

"Is that all?"

"Yes, for now. That constitutes her directive."

Cesare clicked his tongue at Zeta's earnest response.

"Ah, our Princess possesses such a kind heart."

"Pardon?"

Cesare stroked his chin as though Gallo's confusion didn't exist.

The handsome man's face, leaning against the moonlit windowsill, appeared beautiful yet somehow eerie.

"Birna Claudio's biological father is Raju, correct?"

"Yes, my lord."

Those sculpted lips curved with mischievous delight.

"Feed this to the entire Claudio family. If supplies run short, procure more."

Gallo, who'd been listening silently, choked on his tea.

"You intend to administer it to the entire family?"

"Why not?"

Cesare turned toward Gallo as though genuinely puzzled by the objection.

"...Never mind."

Gallo concluded his master was truly ruthless and calculating.

Raju would never dream that he—along with his beloved lover and daughter—was consuming the very poison he'd intended for Peleus.

How cruelly ironic. But why does it have to be under *this* lord's command?

Valdina's Princess maintained fair principles—extracting precisely the evil she'd received. To wrongdoers, such justice resembled mercy.

But his lord...

Gallo recalled the twelve vassals who'd perished attempting to poison young Cesare, and the Emperor's courtiers who'd died because their masters coveted his son's power.

Fear begets more fear. Death summons more death.

Cesare believed that even world-shaking devastation proved meaningful if it crushed the initial spark before it spread.

"...Administering it to everyone will prove difficult without detection."

Zeta hesitated, seemingly torn between his master's commands and Medea's original orders.

"Inform Medea if you must. Even if she discovers it, she won't censure me."

Cesare spoke with absolute conviction.

Valdina's Princess displays nobility in all matters, but lowly people like me need not receive her kindness.

Valdina Palace Corridors

Even at this late hour, lights throughout the villa burned bright. Guests and servants moved through hallways in constant motion.

In a blind spot at corridor's end—where observation proved impossible—two shadows stretched across dusty ground.

"I never expected to encounter you here, Theo."

Saya spoke first.

A badge identifying her as Valdina royal household member adorned her chest.

"You mentioned traveling to the Holy Lands. Everything transpired exactly as you predicted."

"Indeed. Saya, you appear healthy as well. That brings me relief."

His voice had deepened considerably compared to three years prior.

Lantern light cast shadows across the knight's cream-uniformed face.

Three years ago, the young man who'd wept over rebellion had returned as a Holy Kingdom knight—specifically, a Templar guarding the Saintess herself.

Saya regarded her twin brother, who'd grown larger and more formidable.

The young, handsome knight radiated faithfulness and strength.

"I understand the Knights Templar primarily subjugate demons."

"Yes. Our mission involves protecting people and punishing evil in these barren lands the Goddess has abandoned."

"..."

"Just as you chose to remain at the Princess's side, I've discovered my purpose. I intend to dedicate the remainder of my life to this calling."

Theo recalled his post-departure days with remarkable calm.

"Don't rationalize your actions because of me!"

"Should I feel grateful you valued that fraudulent rebellion over my life?"

Three years ago, his only sister had exposed all his contradictions, leaving him devastated.

What was I even attempting to accomplish?

With faded dreams and mounting regrets, he'd left Valdina, stumbling forward without direction.

The road to the Holy Lands proved treacherous.

Sweltering heat, scorching sun, and demons appearing frequently—attempting to claw his flesh and tear his throat.

Yet death's danger didn't trouble Theo. He already existed in hell.

When he closed his eyes, visions materialized: the rebel leader's severed head spurting blood, rebels swept away like autumn leaves.

Clearest among these memories—the omnipotent Princess's emerald eyes gazing down at him.

That cold stare felt permanently wrapped around Theo's neck.

What remains for me now?

He ran ceaselessly, breath pounding. Never resting, yet possessing nothing.

No revolution. No beliefs. Not even his sole blood relative.

He wandered like a buoy adrift in vast ocean, directionless and purposeless.

Perhaps disappearing like this wouldn't prove so terrible...

As the massive demon's maw opened to swallow Theo whole, he felt relief that the end approached.

"Have you lost your mind entirely?"

"Where am I... Why did you save me when I never asked for rescue..."

The Knights Templar, coincidentally passing while hunting demons, discovered and rescued him.

"Why does someone so young suffer such despair? You're still a man brimming with vitality. If you suffer, offer yourself to the Goddess. She will not abandon you. You are not alone."

"Ugh... ah..."

The Knight Commander recognized potential in this skeletal figure who hadn't tasted sustenance—only his eyes sparkled with life.

"In Her stead, I protect this land's suffering humanity."

Thus Theo joined the Knights Templar.

The worries tormenting him, regrets he couldn't shake, longing that bloomed like burning thirst—

Why did I need to find reasons?

The Goddess would illuminate his path.

He willingly embraced the new mission bestowed upon him.

"Every soul walks tethered—
some strings guide toward light,
others strangle in darkness."
