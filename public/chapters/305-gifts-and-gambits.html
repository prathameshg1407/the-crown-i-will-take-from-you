Chapter 305

Gifts and Gambits

"In the game of power, every gift conceals a blade."

Meanwhile, the knight staggered from the tavern and drew a ragged breath in the night air.

He stumbled forward, feigning drunkenness, but stopped abruptly when he reached the narrow street adjacent to the establishment.

"I delivered it."

His face contorted as he turned toward the lightless alley's impenetrable darkness.

"I've done everything you demanded. So please... release her now."

"We'll hold your mother until the birthday celebration concludes. You might reconsider your cooperation otherwise."

"But all I had to do was give him the sword!"

The knight lunged forward like an explosion of barely contained fury.

But in a flash of movement, he found himself face-down on the cobblestones, a heavy boot grinding his head into the ground.

Many figures lurked in that darkness—all of them formidably strong.

Something landed before his pinned face.

Severed locks of gray hair.

"Complete your task properly, or next time you'll receive your mother's fingers."

"Ungh—!"

The rage and anguished protests visible in his exposed eyes were swallowed by the surrounding darkness.

Imperial Palace.

"I know I've seen this somewhere before..."

Standing in Cesare's private study, Medea reached toward the table.

A carved piece of deer antler rested beside where the Princess's ring usually hung.

Unlike the one worn by the monk-child, this piece showed considerable wear and staining.

Medea examined the deer antler carving in detail. The surface had been worn smooth by years of handling, yet the craftsmanship remained crude—clearly carved by an amateur's hand.

"The boy who warned you belonged to the household of the Marquis of Eratosthenes."

Kensington, who had followed her inside, provided the answer.

"Eratosthenes?"

'That's Cesare's maternal grandfather.'

"Yes, Your Highness. That's correct."

"..."

Kensington waited patiently for his mistress's next question.

But Medea simply stared at the wooden piece for a long moment before turning away.

"Have you located the person I asked about?"

"Yes, I've discovered their whereabouts. Now that we've dispatched the Red Foxes, contact should be established shortly."

Medea's delicate eyelashes flickered with satisfaction.

'I expected this to take considerably longer. But the network's reach is impressive.'

In her previous life, tasks that had required years to accomplish were now being completed with remarkable speed thanks to the Red Foxes' continental reach.

"Excellent. Bring them to Katzen as soon as their identity is confirmed beyond doubt."

She turned and walked from Cesare's study with Kensington following at a respectful distance.

"And what of our comrade serving beside the Western Empress?"

"Yes, Your Highness."

"Among the court ladies attending the Western Empress, one is actually my agent. We were companions long before the Red Foxes formed—more like sisters than mere servants. She's proven her loyalty by remaining in position for the sake of my vengeance, even after I departed Katzen."

"The Western Empress still has no suspicion that this woman belongs to your household?"

"None whatsoever. They served together long before the Western Empress even entered the palace."

"Does she still wish to remain within the Katzen Imperial Family?"

Following Medea through the corridor, Kensington suppressed a sigh.

"I doubt it. After witnessing the imperial family's treatment of me, how could she possibly wish to stay longer? However, she's been waiting for the opportune moment to depart."

"That's fortunate. I want to assist her escape."

Medea gestured toward a round table.

Kensington observed a longsword wrapped in black cloth resting on its polished surface.

"This is...?"

"Perfect timing, actually. The Western Empress has extended an invitation."

"An invitation? What sort of invitation?"

'Have I missed news of a banquet being held at the Western Palace?'

As Kensington paused in confusion, Medea twisted the sword's handle as though kindly providing an explanation.

With a faint metallic click, a small compartment within the handle opened.

A neatly folded piece of parchment slipped out, covered in densely packed writing as fine as grains of sand.

It was a secret correspondence.

"Menil brought this to me. It appears the Western Empress's target isn't me this time."

"If the target isn't Your Highness, then by process of elimination, it must be His Highness the First Prince."

Kensington released a bitter laugh, his normally composed face flushing with anger.

It hadn't been long since the incident with the Emperor, yet these people continued plotting their wicked schemes without cessation. He wanted to slaughter every last conspirator.

"It's acceptable. This isn't unexpected, is it?"

Medea shrugged lightly.

"Besides, we have something prepared for the Western Empress as well. Let's utilize it for this occasion."

Medea's lips curved into a knowing smile.

"After all, it's only proper to exchange gifts."

Time flowed onward like a river.

Spring's end, fragrant with blooming flowers, faded away. Before anyone quite realized it, the city had been cloaked in summer's verdant embrace.

The Katzen Imperial Palace.

However, Emperor Perdiccas II appeared utterly unmoved by summer's vibrant greenery as he coldly hurled documents aside.

"How dare they execute such obscene schemes within my domain!"

The reports detailed how Katzen's nobility had been evading land taxes by registering their estates under commoners' names.

This brazen fraud had already spread far beyond the capital, contaminating the entire empire.

"You incompetent fools didn't even notice until he discovered it and brought it to my attention!"

And it had been Cesare who'd uncovered the corruption.

The Emperor, his pride thoroughly crushed, lashed out at his courtiers with venomous fury.

"Useless parasites, all of you. It shames me that creatures like you dare claim kinship with my bloodline."

The courtiers bowed their heads before the Emperor as he clutched at his temples.

They held their breath, sweat streaming down their backs.

The Grand Inspector of the Imperial Court bowed even deeper.

"My deepest apologies, Your Majesty. Most of the perpetrators are grand nobles, making investigation... difficult without superior authority..."

'I'm frustrated too! The grand aristocracy is nearly impossible for ordinary officials like myself to approach. How could I possibly know what occurs behind those towering estate walls?'

"What this man means to say is that he lacks the necessary authority."

"While not a capital offense, this presents an excellent opportunity to replenish the national treasury. We needn't punish many—simply make an example of one to demonstrate imperial power."

Cesare spoke in a languid tone, though his eyes gleamed with rare interest.

"This son would gladly shoulder Your Majesty's burden. What does Father think of targeting the Northern Duke... as our example?"

A perfectly sized opponent—large enough to demonstrate imperial might, yet not so powerful as to be untouchable.

But those who understood the true situation knew better.

'Of course. Among Katzen's countless high-ranking nobles, why would the First Prince specifically target the Northern Duke unless...'

At the Eastern Empress's recent tea party, the Northern Duke's daughter had insulted the First Princess. Now Cesare intended to exact the price.

The Emperor's previously furrowed brow gradually smoothed at Cesare's words.

"..."

Strangely, the rage he should have felt at his son's presumption never materialized.

Instead, his lips curved into a subtle smile.

'Cesare understands that if Father can fill his coffers, he won't object—even if it means confiscating his subjects' wealth.'

'No matter how much he despises me, he won't kick away benefits presented on a silver platter.'

By alternating between punishment and reward, Cesare carefully walked the razor's edge of what his father could tolerate.

He felt certain the Emperor would ultimately act according to his will.

Then—

"The First Prince continually exceeds Your Majesty's authority! How can he presume to arbitrarily determine punishment for Your Majesty's subjects?"

A voice sharp as a blade cut through the throne room.

Every head turned toward the speaker.
