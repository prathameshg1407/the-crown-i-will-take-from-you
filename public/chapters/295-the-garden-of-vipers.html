Chapter 295

The Garden of
Vipers

"In a palace of serpents, even a single embrace becomes a weapon."

He curled his broad frame around her like a great hunting hound burrowing into its master's arms, utterly heedless of propriety or size.

Medea tentatively stretched her arm further, patting his back in gentle, rhythmic strokes. His shoulders were so wide that her hand could only reach the ridge of his shoulder blade.

"You know... you don't need to worry about me this much. There's nothing to apologize for."

Her voice was soft, almost soothing.

"You do what you must do. I will do what I must do."

If I want to bring down the Third Prince, the Western Empress must fall first.

As long as that woman's protection remained intact, Juan would never be vulnerable. The mother was the root; the son merely a branch.

"Are you listening?"

Silence.

"Cesare?"

While Medea's mind wove strategies for the battles ahead, Cesare offered no response—only tightened his arms around her.

"If you're alright now, perhaps we should—"

"No."

In the distance, palace servants who had been approaching the garden stopped short at the sight of them. Their eyes widened before they quickly reversed course, retreating the way they had come.

The arms encircling her showed no sign of releasing their hold.

A flicker of exasperation crossed his sculpted features.

Here I am, offering sincere comfort with all my heart... and this lovely creature speaks of strategy as though discussing the weather.

What was he supposed to do with someone so beautifully, infuriatingly indifferent?

Meanwhile, in the Imperial Garden

✦ ✦ ✦

As Medea and her husband departed, the Third Prince turned eagerly to his father.

"Your Majesty, I shall practice diligently and ensure that your teachings reach my brother as well."

Silence.

Perdiccas II offered no response. His gaze had drifted past Juan entirely, fixing upon the Western Empress with an intensity that made her blood run cold.

"Your Majesty?"

The Emperor took a deliberate step backward, putting distance between himself and the Empress. When Juan reached for his sleeve, he shook the boy's hand away without a glance.

The Western Empress read the distrust swimming in her husband's bloodshot eyes. She moved toward him with carefully measured urgency.

"Third Prince! Do you intend to further agitate His Majesty with your clumsiness? Have you learned nothing from witnessing the First Prince's shameful display?"

She positioned herself to block the Emperor's line of sight, scolding Juan as a pretense to redirect attention toward Cesare's earlier offense.

"Empress. Now that I consider it... Count Montfort seems rather young to be leading the Senate."

The words came from nowhere, landing like a blade between her ribs.

"Father? What do you mean? Grandfather is the same age as the other elder statesmen—"

The Western Empress cut her son off before he could dig them deeper into the pit.

"Of course, Your Majesty. My father has expressed such concerns himself. Perhaps Your Majesty's wisdom offers him the perfect excuse to step back from such weighty responsibilities."

The First Prince's tongue is truly venomous.

Not only had he transformed the Emperor's rage into suspicion directed at her and her family, but he had also shattered the momentum the Third Prince's faction had been building so carefully.

When the Western Empress displayed forced compliance, the Emperor finally nodded with cold satisfaction.

We must retreat before the fire spreads to Juan.

"With Your Majesty's permission, we shall withdraw."

"Go."

Unlike mere hours ago, when he had detained them endlessly, the dismissal came instantly.

Proof that doubt had taken root in Perdiccas II's mind.

The moment they were beyond the palace walls, Juan turned to his mother.

"Why did you accept the Emperor's demand so meekly? Do you have any idea how long Grandfather has waited for the Chairman election?"

"Had I resisted, His Majesty's suspicion would have fallen upon us both."

The Western Empress's voice was tight.

"The moment we cross that line, every ounce of trust we have cultivated will evaporate. We would be treated exactly as Cesare is treated."

Perdiccas II's paranoia was boundless, striking without logic or warning.

For years, she had used others as shields, redirecting his suspicion toward convenient targets. But today, for the first time, those tactics had been turned against her.

The Western Empress touched her throat. It felt as though something was lodged there, choking her.

All those hours spent cultivating the Emperor's favor—gone in an instant.

Not only had she been made to look like a thorn to the Crown Prince and his wife, but her father had lost his path to the Senate Chairmanship as well.

She staggered, pressing a hand to her forehead as fury rose like bile.

"Mother!"

"I'm fine. Just a moment's dizziness."

Juan watched her with worried eyes.

She exhausts herself fretting over that feeble Emperor while Cesare runs wild. That monster, provoking Father at every turn...

The image of Cesare splitting his arrow in half—of those thick shafts shattering like twigs in his grip—flashed through Juan's mind.

A monster wearing human skin. I cannot allow such a creature to sit upon the sacred throne.

"No. No..."

The Western Empress waved her hand dismissively.

"That's not the issue..."

Juan looked at her curiously, but rather than explain, she fell into contemplative silence as they walked.

"Oh my... Your Imperial Highness, the Empress."

Juan, who had been trailing his mother in distraction, quickly straightened and offered a respectful bow.

"Juan. It has been some time."

The Eastern Empress acknowledged him with distant courtesy.

She looked radiant beneath the afternoon sun—skin dewy and flushed with roses, belying the fact that she had a grown daughter. Time had been kind to her in ways it had not been to others.

"Are you on your way to see His Majesty?"

The Western Empress spoke first. The Eastern Empress remained silent.

She had come to remind the Emperor of her exiled daughter Angelique—worried that his obsession with the Third Prince might erase Angelique from his memory entirely.

"The First Prince and his wife have just departed. The timing is... unfortunate. Perhaps return later, for your own benefit."

"And how would you know the Emperor's mood so intimately?"

The Eastern Empress's voice carried a sharp edge.

"His Majesty must be weary after instructing our Juan in archery since dawn. Though I suppose when evening comes, he will seek... other company."

It was a pointed reminder—the Western Empress held the Emperor's days, but the Eastern Empress still claimed his nights.

"The Crown Princess should pay her respects to you as well, but she refuses to listen to my suggestions. I've told her she shouldn't remain cloistered in the Crown Prince's Palace, given how people talk..."

"That won't be necessary."

The Western Empress's tone turned frigid.

"Your Highness should focus on enjoying life's beauties while you can. Since your daughter will never rule this empire, you might as well savor these... flowering days."

Why would I envy a woman tethered to a man descending into madness?

The Western Empress studied the Eastern Empress with cool detachment, reminded suddenly of how arrogant this woman had once been.

"Though you would do well to be cautious. The Crown Princess is quite clever. Since she will remain in this palace under His Majesty's protection for some time, we cannot afford to be outmaneuvered by a newcomer."

The Western Empress let her gaze drift to the horizon, as though she hadn't noticed the Eastern Empress's hands curling into fists.

"By the way... how is Angelique adjusting to her new circumstances?"

Silence.

"She will spend the rest of her days there, after all. Best she release her attachment to palace life and find peace."

The Eastern Empress smiled—a thin, brittle expression unlike any she had worn before.

"Thank you. I am so deeply grateful for your concern. Truly, I don't know how to repay such kindness. I shall never forget it."

She turned and swept away, face flushed crimson with suppressed rage. In her fury, she forgot even the basic courtesy of acknowledging Juan.

The hem of her magnificent gown billowed violently in her wake, betraying the storm within.

"Why provoke her so? What if she petitions the Emperor for Angelique's return?"

Juan's voice held a note of genuine worry. The Eastern Empress's influence had waned, but she was not entirely without power.

"Even that fool understands the current climate. Angelique has long since faded from His Majesty's thoughts. Raising the subject would only cost her what little favor she retains."

"But Mother... this seems unlike you."

Had his mother not always considered the Eastern Empress beneath her notice, unworthy even of conversation?

"Juan. Sometimes even the heavens must descend to meet the earth's gaze."

The Western Empress smiled—a slow, patient expression, as though instructing a child in the ways of the world.

She raised one elegant hand and pointed toward the retreating Eastern Empress, who had suddenly frozen mid-step.

Her finger traced a line through the air, following the direction of the other woman's stare.

There, in the distant garden, a couple stood locked in embrace.

The Crown Princess, wrapped securely in her husband's powerful arms. Silver hair catching the light. Two figures so absorbed in each other they seemed blind to the world around them.

"She is the one who will pluck flowers for me. The least I can do is show her a little... sincerity."

Above them, the sky stretched bright and endless blue—as cold and clear as the smile curving the Western Empress's lips.
