Chapter 224

Sins of the
Father

"The truth, once spoken, cannot be unspoken."

Birna's gaze swept past her mother, fixing upon the platform where Raju knelt.

He was gasping for breath, each exhale a wet rattle. Blood ran down his face like crimson sweat, streaming from a gash where the whip had caught his forehead at the wrong angle.

"Perhaps Mother has forgotten—while she was dallying with that lowborn filth—but I have not."

Birna's voice shook with barely contained fury. She drew a ragged breath before continuing.

"I am my father's only daughter. Joaquin Claudio's daughter."

She turned to face Catherine directly, her eyes blazing.

"He was nobility. A true son of this realm. As his daughter, it is my duty to avenge him. I will show Father the moment his murderer draws his last breath—"

"YOU FOOLISH GIRL!"

Catherine clutched her head, fingers clawing at her own scalp.

"Joaquin is not your father!"

Her voice split the air like a blade.

"That man—Raju—the one bleeding on that stage—he is your father! Your real father!"

Madness seemed to grip her as she surged to her feet, seizing Birna's shoulders with white-knuckled hands—heedless of the hundreds of witnesses surrounding them.

"You are Roberto Raju's daughter! Claudio merely raised you! And now you've condemned the man who gave you life to die!"

"Wh—what?"

Birna stumbled backward, staring at her mother with the eyes of a cornered animal.

"You destroyed everything!"

Catherine's voice cracked with fury.

"Everything we hid for your sake—everything I sacrificed for you—you've trampled it all into the dirt!"

I cannot believe it.

All those years Catherine had spent walking on glass, terrified that the slightest breeze might shatter her carefully constructed world.

And she had raised this. This creature who had repaid her father's lifetime of devotion with a knife in the back.

"Do you have any idea what we endured for you? How much Robbie loved you?"

Catherine's eyes burned with hatred as she stared at her daughter.

"That man is dying because of you. Everything he did—the risks, the schemes, the stolen fortune—all of it was to give you a future. To ensure you could stand tall before anyone in this world."

Her voice dropped to something venomous.

"And you reported him. Even the devil himself would show more gratitude."

When the messenger had announced the charges—embezzlement, the stolen imperial fortune—understanding had crashed over Catherine like a cold wave.

Where did the money come from? All those years of support, even after Claudio's rebellion failed...

Her lover was no god. He had taken terrible risks.

I should have asked. I should have known. Was he being reckless? Was he making dangerous choices because of us—because of me?

But she had never been a mere parasite, content to drain him dry.

Not like her. Not like this ungrateful wretch who would devour her own father alive.

"No."

Birna's voice emerged as barely a whisper, trembling with barely suppressed hysteria.

"That cannot be true. It's a lie. You're trying to deceive me—both of you—liars!"

She whirled toward the crowd, her voice rising to a desperate shriek.

"I am Claudio! Birna Claudio! Do not insult my father's name!"

But beneath the defiance, fear had begun to bleed through.

Then, from the platform, came a voice ravaged by agony.

"Birna!"

Raju's scream cut through the chaos. Though blood masked his features and pain contorted every line of his face, his eyes—fixed on his daughter—burned with terrible clarity.

"You opened the ledger! You—of all people!"

He doubled over as another spasm of pain wracked his body, but his gaze never wavered.

"That safe was sealed with blood magic—my blood! Only someone who shares it could have opened it!"

"The ledger itself is proof that you are my daughter!"

His words rang across the square like a death knell.

"Deny me all you wish—it changes nothing. This is a truth that even God Himself cannot erase!"

Blood bubbled between his cracked lips, but still he forced the words out—rage and love intertwined in equal measure.

"Everything I did—everything—was for you! I came all this way to stay by your side, to protect you... and this is how you repay me?"

CRACK—!

Raju's face twisted in unbearable agony. He bit down hard enough to draw blood, swallowing his scream.

I will not leave this place alive. There is no longer any reason to hold back.

"You repay a father's love with death!"

His voice was a ragged howl, torn from the depths of a breaking heart.

"Foolish girl! You will never know peace—not even after I am gone! My curse will follow you to the grave!"

The final words left him, and Raju collapsed upon the bloodstained wood.

"Silence! How dare you spew such lies before all these people!"

Birna's entire body had gone rigid with shock and fury. The chaos Raju had unleashed was too massive, too absolute—she could not process it.

"Mother..."

Her eyes sought Catherine with the desperate instinct of a child searching for safety in a storm.

Her trembling hands groped through the air until they found Catherine's arm, gripping so tightly her fingers seemed ready to pierce the flesh.

"Mother, answer me. Tell me it's not true. He is not—he cannot be—"

Catherine looked down at the hand clutching her arm.

Her expression had turned to stone.

With a single, vicious motion, she tore herself free—so forcefully that Birna stumbled and nearly fell.

"Deny it until your dying breath—it will change nothing."

Catherine's voice dripped with venom.

"You are the product of our adultery. And you are the devil who denounced and murdered her own father with her own hands. Nothing will ever erase that."

She was breathing hard now, each word laced with poison.

To her, Birna was no longer a daughter.

She was a murderer. An enemy. The one who had destroyed Catherine's dreams, her love, her entire world.

"Mother..."

Birna stood frozen, unable to speak.

The hatred in Catherine's eyes was unmistakable—raw, vivid, absolute. The look of someone who would gladly drive a blade through her heart.

The crowd stood in stunned silence, their gazes darting between Catherine, Birna, and the broken man on the stage.

"Wait—Princess Claudio... Joaquin's daughter... she was actually that bastard's child all along?"

A moment of breathless stillness—and then the square erupted.

"Her middle name! It's Robbie! Roberto Raju—they really were father and daughter!"

The confessions of the two lovers had kindled something in the crowd's collective memory. One by one, the pieces fell into place.

"I always wondered why Raju doted on his goddaughter so obsessively... It makes sense now! They had to be blood relatives!"

"Look at her face again—doesn't she resemble him? Gods, it was right before our eyes this entire time!"

Laughter and curses erupted in equal measure. The crowd's fury found new targets.

"How shameless can Catherine be? She passed off her bastard as Claudio's legitimate heir, played the virtuous widow, and called herself a Duchess?"

"And what of Raju? He beat to death the man who raised his own daughter! Even if he deserved execution, I pity Joaquin—betrayed so thoroughly he must be screaming in his grave!"

But the sharpest barbs were reserved for Birna.

"She strutted around claiming noble blood, treating commoners like insects beneath her heel—and she was baseborn herself this whole time?"

"A mongrel who presumed to lord over true nobles! Does that make any sense?"

The Valdinians' memories ran long and bitter.

"Have you forgotten how she tormented our Princess three years ago? Using her position as the Regent's daughter to persecute someone powerless to fight back?"

"She even stole the Princess's gown to wear at the banquet! And now we learn she's nothing but a fraud?"

"If the Regent's rebellion had succeeded, we would have been ruled by that—that creature of tainted blood!"

"Executioner! Do not stop the whipping! That criminal has mocked our nation and defiled the royal house—he must not be shown a moment's mercy!"

The angry voices of the Valdinians rose higher and higher, swelling into a thunderous chorus of condemnation.
