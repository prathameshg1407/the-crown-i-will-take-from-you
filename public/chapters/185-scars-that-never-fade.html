Interlude

Scars That Never Fade

Some wounds run deeper than flesh. Some hatreds burn hotter than reason.

No gown, however exquisite, no cosmetics, however artfully applied, could fully conceal the disfiguring scar the rebels had carved into Birna's flesh.

"Birna..."

Raju's jaw clenched as though he himself had been struck. The name escaped through gritted teeth like a wounded prayer.

His trembling fingers extended toward his daughter's shaking shoulders.

"U-Uncle Raju..."

Birna's voice broke, raw with heightened emotion.

While her own family had turned away, pretending not to see her distress, only Raju had materialized—only he had bothered to appear.

She collapsed into his arms, and tears began streaming down her face in earnest.

"What am I supposed to do now? Everyone knows—everyone can see this hideous scar..."

Raju scarcely had time to savor this unprecedented moment—his daughter, who had spent years avoiding him, maintaining careful distance, now seeking refuge in his embrace for the first time.

"They'll mock me for the rest of my life... laugh at me behind their hands... How am I supposed to live like this? How can I possibly—"

Her sobs choked off the words.

Watching his daughter struggle for breath between hitching sobs was agony incarnate.

Raju's heart fractured with each gasping cry. She was his own blood—so precious he'd never dared approach too closely, content merely to observe her from afar like a devotee worshipping at a distant shrine.

Even behind closed eyelids, her image remained vivid and indelible. That innocent, childlike spirit—even her occasional rudeness toward him had seemed endearing, proof of her vibrant personality.

He couldn't recall the last time he'd seen Birna smile with genuine happiness.

After the rebellion's failure, Birna had transformed—becoming sullen, withdrawn, prone to sudden flares of anger that seemed to ignite from nowhere.

It's all because of that woman, Medea. She's the one who destroyed my precious daughter's spirit.

Raju's teeth ground together with barely contained fury.

Yet somewhere in the depths of his consciousness—in that shadowed place where uncomfortable truths resided—Raju understood.

If one sought the true culprit, it wasn't Medea who had left that permanent scar on Birna's body and soul.

It was Jason.

Had he not abandoned Birna among the rebels, she would never have been injured.

But to assign blame to Jason meant Raju himself could not escape culpability.

During those critical days, his actions as Jason's loyal subordinate had directly resulted in Birna being isolated among hostile forces.

Even now, he continued serving Jason without question.

Thus, Raju absolved himself by redirecting his rage toward Medea—transforming her into the receptacle for all his anger and hatred.

Only through this mental gymnastics could he rationalize that he bore no responsibility for the wounds marking his daughter's flesh.

"Don't worry, Birna..."

Raju's eyes had gone bloodshot as he patted his sobbing daughter's trembling back.

"Those who inflicted this suffering upon you will pay dearly. They'll endure an existence worse than death—a life so miserable they'll beg for oblivion but find none. I swear it to you. I will make certain of it."

His voice spread through the air like a malevolent incantation, each word weighted with dark intent.

Raju made this vow while staring at Birna's tear-stained face—swearing vengeance upon those who had wounded the daughter he loved more than his own life.

"U-ughhhh... hhhh..."

"Your godfather will resolve everything. Just trust me, Birna. Trust only me..."

He would exact retribution from that arrogant woman who had caused such anguish to his precious child—his daughter whom he cherished beyond all reason.

While Birna fled the hall in distress and Catherine insinuated herself among Valdina's nobility, Samon endured the hostile glares of surrounding nobles.

"How can an entire family possess such shamelessness?"

"Samon Claudio proves himself cut from the same cloth. He barely escaped with his life thanks to the Fourth Princess's intervention—yet when she departed Katzen Palace, he pretended not to know her."

"Even wild beasts comprehend the debt of a life saved. But he clings to the Fourth Princess like a parasite while demonstrating nothing but ingratitude. Little wonder she left without a backward glance—she must have been utterly disgusted."

Samon maintained his position, absorbing the accusations from nobles who seemed deliberately unwilling to lower their voices.

His expression showed no wavering—chin raised as though the criticisms directed at him simply didn't register. One might even detect anger simmering beneath his practiced smile.

But hidden beneath his sleeve, his fist clenched so tightly that his fingernails carved deep crescents into his palm.

You dare call me shameless? How can you judge when you have no conception of what manner of monster that woman truly is?

Samon's mind spiraled back through the nightmare that had been his marriage to the Fourth Princess.

During their return journey to the empire, precisely as planned, the Princess had 'lost her child.'

The pregnancy itself had been fabricated—a fiction concocted by Raju and Samon—and the miscarriage merely represented the inevitable conclusion to their theatrical performance, one that needed to end before they reached the capital.

But what Samon had catastrophically underestimated was that Fourth Princess Angelique was no passive prop to be manipulated and discarded at their convenience.

"Do you have any comprehension of what I've sacrificed because of this worthless, fabricated brat? Do you?!"

She'd been coerced into marriage with Samon because of the supposed pregnancy.

The Fourth Princess's fury held justification—she had forfeited both her position in succession discussions and the Emperor's trust.

Yet Samon understood that even had she borne a legitimate child, she would still have wielded her whip against him with equal viciousness.

What followed was an unrelenting succession of brutal violence inflicted upon fabricated pretexts.

He thought he would lose his sanity.

She maintained enough cunning to leave his face unmarked, ensuring that no one outside his immediate family knew he lived as a husband beaten like a cur from dawn to dusk.

The Fourth Princess swung her whip whenever the mood struck her. When that proved too tedious, she simply commanded her knights to administer the beatings instead.

She treated him as though he were her sworn, lifelong enemy.

"You will never touch another woman for the remainder of your existence—only me. But I shall never again lay a hand upon you myself."

Her smile had been venomous.

"Instead, I'll ensure you spend your entire life as a living ghost—never again treated as a man."

What humiliated Samon more profoundly than the daily beatings was being forced to watch his wife cavort with male courtesans directly before his eyes.

She even ensnared his most loyal subordinates—men who had served him with devotion bordering on worship—dragging them into her depraved schemes.

Angelique exacted increasingly brutal revenge upon Samon for the fraudulent pregnancy that had trapped her.

"You raised me as a wet nurse's son, did you not, my lord? How fortuitous. Now you shall raise this bastard's seed as your own flesh and blood."

"My lord, how could I have foreseen this reversal of fortune? You who always regarded me as lowborn filth—now you'll raise my baseborn son as your legitimate heir."

They say one's environment shapes one's character.

With his master stripped of authority and the Fourth Princess's favor, Suha rapidly forgot his place and began openly taunting Samon.

"My son shall bear your noble surname, my lord. And I shall occupy your position as Her Highness's husband. I trust you'll show us proper care for the remainder of your days."

What greater humiliation could be inflicted upon a man of such towering pride and arrogance than forcing him to raise another man's child—particularly the bastard of his own lowborn subordinate—as his legitimate offspring?

Samon reached the limits of his endurance.

Either that woman dies, or I do. We cannot coexist beneath the same sky.

Several nights later—an evening like any other—it was Suha's designated turn to attend the Princess.

"Gyaaaah! Remove that thing immediately!"

But when morning arrived, Suha was discovered in the Fourth Princess's bed, dead, eyes frozen wide in final shock.

"Your Highness, it appears he consumed excessive quantities of aphrodisiac... I warned you the dosage was potent enough to prove fatal within a single night."

"Damn it all... I drank so much I can't remember anything..."

The Princess's physician remained ignorant of the truth.

Ignorant that Samon had been secretly administering concentrated drugs and alcohol to his wife for days.

He walked away from suspicion entirely unscathed.

While his wife frantically attempted to suppress news of her subordinate's death, Samon quietly disseminated rumors through carefully selected channels.

"The dissolute Fourth Princess has graduated from mere debauchery—now she's murdering her lovers!"

While the Princess's promiscuity had been common knowledge, actual deaths represented something else entirely. The rumors metastasized rapidly, spiraling beyond anyone's control.

"Angelique! Are you deliberately testing the absolute limits of my tolerance?!"

Emperor Perdiccas II's rebuke thundered through the palace halls.

But the Fourth Princess, already stripped of imperial favor and enduring altered treatment from her siblings and the nobility, had reached her own breaking point.

She possessed nothing left to lose—not even fear of her father remained.

Scars come in many forms. Some mark the flesh. Others corrupt the soul. But the deepest wounds—those inflicted by pride, shame, and displaced blame—never truly heal. They fester in darkness, breeding hatred that spans generations.
