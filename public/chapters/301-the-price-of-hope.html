Chapter 301

The Price of Hope

"Trust is not given—it is earned with blood and sacrifice."

"Do you dare to ally with me now?"

The Eastern Empress's voice held a razor's edge, each word sharp with scorn.

"How can I trust you when you've dragged my daughter through the mud?"

"If you wish to reignite old hatreds, you should first consider whom the flames will consume."

Medea's gaze remained steady, unflinching.

"It seems the Emperor is already prepared to cast you into the fire. Or am I mistaken?"

If you aim at me, you'll burn us both.

That calm statement, delivered with surgical precision, cut straight to the Empress's heart.

She swallowed a bitter laugh.

'Who in heaven's name claimed that Valdina's daughter was stupid?'

Now she understood.

How carefully this young woman had concealed her true nature beneath still waters.

"Or does Your Majesty still believe your daughter can become heir to the Empire?"

"..."

The Eastern Empress bit her lip.

She could no longer deny the truth.

Once, she had dreamed of it—the glorious day when her daughter Angelique would ascend the golden throne. But that radiant dream had long since fallen to earth and been buried beneath layers of dust and disillusionment.

Her daughter had already been pushed from the center of power.

In the brutal arena of imperial politics, the Fourth Princess was an abandoned piece.

'Even if a miracle occurred and both the First and Third Princes died, the Katzen nobility would never support Angelique as Emperor. They'd sooner resurrect one of the other princes than place her on that throne.'

'I can't even see her again, let alone dream of her coronation.'

The ruthless Emperor treated his daughter—who had inadvertently ruined his carefully laid plans—as though she were an enemy of the state.

Her once-loyal courtiers had abandoned ship in search of safer harbors. Even the young Emperor-in-waiting, who had once tried to protect his niece, now lay bedridden and powerless.

All that remained for the Eastern Empress were sleepless nights spent weeping in corners where no one could see, and a desperate longing she couldn't voice even in whispers.

"..."

"You won't believe me—not with mere words."

Medea's lips curved into a knowing smile as her fingers traced the rim of her teacup with deliberate grace.

The tea had long since cooled, and she had no intention of drinking it. But the smooth porcelain felt pleasant beneath her fingertips.

"What if the first step in building trust between us is to bring Her Highness the Fourth Princess back to this holy city?"

The Empress's head snapped up, her eyes widening.

Doubt and shock warred for dominance in her expression.

"Impossible."

The word came out harsh, dismissive.

"Your situation is so desperate that you'd promise the impossible just to gain an advantage. Did you think I'd be so easily deceived?"

"I imagine Your Majesty has endured such hardship that you can no longer allow yourself even the smallest sliver of hope."

Medea's voice remained calm, yet somehow compassionate.

"But wouldn't it be cruel to the Fourth Princess to simply give up? To abandon her when she's waiting for her mother?"

"Silence! How dare a mere girl speak so carelessly of matters you cannot understand!"

Even she—Angelique's own mother—had failed.

How desperately she had struggled to save her daughter. She had leveraged every favor, wielded every scrap of power, sacrificed wealth and dignity alike. And yet none of it had been enough to soften the Emperor's heart.

"To His Majesty, Angelique is no longer his daughter. She is the one who destroyed his ambitions and elevated your wretched Valdina. She is his wound. His failure. A living reminder of his shame."

The words spilled out unbidden, raw sincerity bleeding through her carefully maintained facade.

The Emperor's madness and obsession intensified with each passing day, and no one possessed the strength to break through his stubborn resolve.

"Every time your husband achieves another victory, every time Valdina's name rises in prominence, His Majesty thinks of Angelique. And he heaps all his frustration and blame upon her shoulders."

The Empress paused, her voice dropping to barely above a whisper.

"He will never allow her to return. Never."

How absurd.

Here she was, revealing her deepest pain to someone she should consider an enemy—a mere girl who had disrupted her plans and humiliated her supporters.

'A child who has been forgotten by everyone except me.'

Had Medea's words truly shaken her so deeply? The promise to save a daughter whom only her mother still remembered?

'I must have truly become pathetic.'

"Yes, it won't be easy. Which is precisely why it's valuable."

The First Princess's demeanor remained utterly unchanged.

Her beautiful face showed no trace of being tainted by the despair the Empress had inadvertently revealed. That serene expression belonged to someone who had never been broken, never been defeated.

It reminded the Empress painfully of how her own daughter Angelique had once carried herself.

"Actions speak louder than a hundred promises."

Medea smiled at the Empress's twisted expression.

"Let us discuss our alliance again on the day the Fourth Princess sets foot once more in the holy city. Until then, there's nothing more to say."

"Leave! Get out of my sight!"

The Eastern Empress ground her teeth, her command sharp as breaking glass.

Medea rose gracefully and offered a perfect curtsy.

"Then, Your Majesty, until that day—please remain strong."

"That arrogant little..."

Even after the First Princess departed, the Empress couldn't fully expel her fury.

She sat rigid, lips pressed into a bloodless line, breath coming in shallow bursts.

"Your Majesty..."

At the familiar voice, she looked up.

The Countess of Beaumont, Lady Evelyn, and Duchess Clarice stood before her, their faces drained of all color like corpses from which every drop of blood had been wrung.

Tears streaked their pale cheeks. Their lips trembled, utterly bloodless.

Having failed their mistress so catastrophically, all that remained was to face the Empress's wrath.

"Kill me. I dared to betray Your Majesty."

They collapsed to their knees, tears streaming down their faces.

"Pathetic creatures. Do you dare crawl back to me without shame and beg forgiveness?"

The Empress's voice dripped venom.

"Did I look so foolish that you thought I'd accept rotten servants who betrayed me?"

She seized her teacup and hurled the contents at them.

Cold tea splashed over their heads and shoulders. Shards from the shattered porcelain nicked their faces and hands, drawing thin lines of crimson.

These were women of considerable status—names that commanded respect throughout the palace.

Yet they had never experienced such humiliation.

Knowing the gravity of their failure, they bowed their heads lower still, not even attempting to wipe away the blood mixing with the dripping tea.

"I... Your Majesty... I have children... I had no choice..."

"Your Majesty... the First Prince... he threatened..."

Fragmented excuses tumbled from their lips between sobs.

The Eastern Empress's gaze turned glacial.

"Where are all those solemn oaths of loyalty you swore to me? Those promises to serve me unto death? You knew the First Prince's character—did you truly stand by my side without the resolve to die for your convictions?"

Her eyes bore into them with terrible intensity.

The fact that those she had trusted most had ruined her plans and smeared her face with filth made her want to strangle them with her bare hands.

"I'm sorry, Your Majesty... I'm truly, truly sorry... I..."

"I was blinded by fear... My judgment failed me... Please..."

The Empress listened to their pathetic whimpering with cold detachment.

According to her informants, the First Princess had employed her husband's forces to kidnap the family members of the Empress's attendants—using them as leverage to force cooperation.

'Clarice has an ailing elderly mother. Beaumont and Evelyn both have young children they adore.'

She should have anticipated that even the loyalty of a war god's devotees would crack under such pressure.

'How can I condemn them when even I haven't been able to sever my own bitter attachments?'

The Eastern Empress closed her eyes tightly.

Her rage, which had burned like an inferno moments before, suddenly extinguished as though doused by a bucket of cold water.

She released a short, heavy sigh.

"Enough. Leave. Don't show your faces until I summon you."

The tear-stained women looked up in disbelief.

The fact that the Empress wasn't ordering them beaten and cast out meant she would forgive them—eventually.

"Your Majesty, I swear this will never happen again—"

"Thank you, Your Majesty, your mercy—"

"Silence. Get out."

The Empress waved her hand dismissively.

Looking at their haggard, corpse-like faces, it was obvious how much anguish they had suffered over their captured family members—anguish inflicted by that ruthless bastard.

"Pathetic women. Everyone beneath me is pathetic... and I'll be humiliated by the First Princess because of it."

Yet despite her harsh words, the burning hatred she had harbored toward Medea had somehow... diminished.

The words the First Princess had left behind moments ago had kindled something the Empress thought long extinguished:

A fragile, dangerous sliver of hope.
