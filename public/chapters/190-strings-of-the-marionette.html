Chapter 190

Strings of the Marionette

"Why is my son—who should be watching safely from a distance—buried beneath the rubble?"

"No, Catherine! What are you doing?! Someone bring the Duchess back here!"

Catherine tried to rush back into the collapsed temple immediately, but Raju seized her arm, stopping her with desperate urgency.

Joaquin stumbled backward, his words fragmenting with disbelief.

"That can't be—that—it can't possibly be—"

There's no way that broken thing lying there is Samon. There's no way it's my son...

"Samon! Where are you?!"

Joaquin kept telling himself that his son had escaped unharmed, that he'd survived. But as his eyes swept the scene again and again, his hope withered—Samon was nowhere to be seen among the evacuees.

"Mike, didn't you leave with the young master earlier? You said you were delivering the butler's message—"

"What are you talking about? I was waiting outside the entire time."

"No, I definitely saw you inside earlier."

The moment Joaquin overheard the conflicting accounts between his subordinates, understanding struck him like ice water.

'Oh God. Samon walked straight into a trap.'

Nearby, Birna covered her mouth in shock, her eyes threatening to bulge from their sockets.

She had no idea what had happened or why the explosion had occurred so suddenly—and so catastrophically.

"If even Samon falls here... this can't be happening..."

Birna felt instinctively, viscerally, that hell had descended upon her family once more.

"..."

Medea's emerald eyes captured every nuance of her uncle's expression—the shock, the denial, the dawning horror.

They didn't know yet.

The finale she had prepared wasn't over.

CRASH! BOOM!

At that moment, the temple wall gave way completely with a thunderous roar. Massive chunks of debris tumbled down onto Samon's position, burying him entirely beneath stone and shattered bronze.

"AHHHHHHH! NOOOOO!"

Catherine's shriek tore through the air. Thick clouds of dust billowed upward like fog, obscuring everything.

— Later —

An explosion had torn through the temple.

The altar and the statue of the goddess had been almost completely obliterated.

Though considerable time had passed since the incident, the landscape of broken stone and shattered faith remained largely unchanged.

Medea stood at a distance, watching the crowd mill helplessly around the fallen statue like sheep without a shepherd.

"There! Samon is here! Hurry and save him! Someone, please—my son is buried here!"

Catherine's voice carried across the wreckage, frantic and raw with desperation. The massive stone fragments and bronze pieces covering Samon were staggering in both size and weight.

"Samon! Where are you?!"

Catherine looked on the verge of vomiting blood, her eyes wide and unseeing with terror.

"I'll give a gold piece to anyone who helps rescue him! Hurry, please!"

Finally, Raju—worried the Duchess might collapse entirely—promised a generous reward to motivate the bystanders.

Only then did people's eyes widen with interest, and they swarmed toward the rescue operation like ants to honey.

"Dea, let's go. There's no need for us to stay here any longer."

Peleus approached his sister. His blue eyes studied her closely, entirely disinterested in the fate of their cousin trapped beneath the rubble.

"Your Majesty!"

Catherine's voice rang out sharply.

She tried to rush toward Peleus, but Agemas blocked her path with the efficiency of a trained sentinel.

"Please, Your Majesty—I beg you! Have mercy and save our Samon! Are we not blood relatives?!"

Her plea was drenched in heartbreak. The raw maternal anguish in her voice moved even hardened observers to pity.

This time, at least, her desperation was genuine—unlike the calculated performance she'd given at the banquet.

Unable to penetrate the solid wall of knights surrounding the Emperor, Catherine eventually collapsed to her knees and struck her forehead against the floor.

"Please! I beg you—if you cannot forgive us, then take this aunt's life instead! Samon might be dying at this very moment! If I could only save my son, I would—"

The wall of knights parted.

A glimmer of hope flickered in Catherine's tear-filled eyes—but it wasn't Peleus who stepped forward.

It was Medea.

"Get up, Aunt."

Medea bent gracefully, took Catherine's trembling hand, and helped her to her feet.

"Of course we'll help. This is a matter of life and death for our cousin Samon. We'll do everything we can, so please don't worry, Aunt."

"M-Medea..."

Catherine's voice wavered with unexpected emotion at the seemingly warm words.

"But, Aunt—"

Medea's tone shifted imperceptibly, like a blade sliding from its sheath.

"Why didn't cousin Samon evacuate when the explosion occurred? He had ample time to escape."

Her voice remained perfectly composed, utterly undisturbed.

"I simply don't understand why he was found inside the altar."

"That—that is..."

Medea continued speaking, her grip on Catherine's wrist tightening as the Duchess tried to pull away.

"You must tell me clearly, Aunt. I can only help him if I can confirm that cousin Samon wasn't targeting His Majesty."

Catherine froze.

The pressure on her wrist was excruciating. She couldn't move—trapped like a rabbit in a snare.

'Does this girl know?'

That we were trying to kill Peleus?

"...No. That can't be right."

But Medea's question had already accomplished its purpose—highlighting Samon's suspicious behavior to every ear within range.

Catherine's mind reeled with the terrible realization.

'You knew. It was you who did this. You killed Samon—'

As if responding to her innermost thoughts, Medea smiled.

It was a clear, guileless smile—without the slightest hint of hesitation or remorse.

That smile sent ice racing down Catherine's spine.

"Ahhh—ahhhhh!"

Catherine wrenched her hand free as though Medea's touch had scalded her, then fled in sheer panic.

"Is she insane? Your Highness, are you all right?"

"Yes. I'm fine."

Medea watched Catherine's retreating figure with calm detachment.

"That shameless woman—how dare she show her face here and speak to Your Highness like that?"

Neril ground her teeth in indignation.

If the Claudios' scheme had succeeded—if Peleus had been injured and the Valdina royal family thrown into chaos—would they have helped the siblings?

No. They would have driven them straight off a cliff.

"People don't change easily."

Medea shrugged with affected nonchalance.

Even if their treason failed and House Claudio fell into ruin, their ambitions would never disappear.

'I think Aunt Catherine has figured it out by now. But what about you, Samon? Are you still oblivious?'

The Web She Wove

"Like a marionette with strings tied to both arms and legs..."

Samon Claudio.

He had embarked on this voyage across the straits harboring a secret ambition—to push Peleus aside and claim Valdina for himself.

Emperor Jason Perdiccas II of Katzen, his wife the Fourth Princess, the Saintess Rachel—all of them had ultimately acted according to Samon's will.

He must have been confident that everything would unfold according to his designs this time as well.

But he didn't know.

He had no idea that the entire process had rested in Medea's palm from the very beginning.

Months Earlier...

"Umberto, you will leave Valdina now. And you will go to Samon."

"What? Are you telling me to return to Katzen, Your Highness? Even though I was your enemy—this seems too harsh."

At the time, Umberto had looked embarrassed, assuming he was being punished for the impudent mistakes he'd made when serving as a spy for the Red Foxes under Minister Etienne.

"Does Your Highness still not trust me?"

"Your Highness, please—trust me just this once. I swear on my deceased mother's memory that my only master now is you."

Medea had patted Umberto's arm gently, dispelling his misunderstanding.

"I'm going to have you do what you do best. Just as you worked under Minister Etienne, this time you'll serve as my hands and ears."

"If that's the case, then you've chosen the perfect man for the job. I won't disappoint you, Your Highness."

True to his confidence, Umberto soon succeeded in gaining Samon's complete trust.

"Umberto, tell my uncle to come to the Straits."

And through Umberto's mouth, Medea conveyed her carefully crafted suggestions.

"Master! Master! The Valdina royal family has been elevated to an empire! They're holding an imperial proclamation ceremony in El Amuz! Of course you must attend, Master!"

"You're still family, after all. They can't possibly treat you coldly in front of everyone."

Strategic encouragements were deployed at precisely the right moments.

Samon may have believed he'd chosen to confront Medea and her brother of his own volition.

But in reality, he'd been dancing to Medea's tune the entire time.

Like a Marionette

With strings tied to both arms and legs

Every step Samon had taken.

Every decision he'd made.

Every moment he'd believed himself in control.

All of it—choreographed by Medea's invisible hand.

And now, as he lay buried beneath stone and bronze, crushed by the weight of his own ambitions, the puppet master stood in the ruins and watched her handiwork unfold.

Without pity.

Without hesitation.

Without regret.
