Ambient: Twilight
Chapter Fragment

The Slums
District 4

"The Darkness of the Beginning is not a chain. It is rot."

The old woman's blind gaze drifted, as though following something only she could see.

"You once ruled the Shadeian," Cesare said quietly. "Help me, and I will rebuild your altars. Your people will rise again."

"Hmph."

The Shaman's laugh was dry, cracked like dead leaves.

"Power always promises restoration," she rasped. "But the Darkness of the Beginning is not a chain. It is rot."

Cesare's patience thinned.

"Is there a cure?" he demanded.

Silence.

Then—soft, reverent—

"There is nothing purer than the Dawn's Droplet."

Gallo's breath hitched.

A cure.

After years of rot and seizures… a name. A key.

"What is it?" Gallo whispered, kneeling. "Where is it?"

"When the Goddess descended upon chaos," the Shaman chanted, "she wept for life. One tear fell. That tear erased the beginning."

"Where?" Cesare pressed.

The Shaman turned her head.

"This star does not orbit you, Black Star."

Terence snapped. "How dare a sinner speak to—!" (corrected to) "Explain yourself!"

"You will never find it," she croaked. "Fate has denied you."

Cough.

Black blood spilled from her mouth, splattering Cesare's boots.

"Damn it," Cesare muttered, catching her as she collapsed.

Her grip tightened on his coat.

"But… hope remains."

She dragged him closer.

"The Venus of Valdina… she will save you."

Cesare stilled.

"Venus of Valdina?"

Her breath rattled.

"Remember… the Venus…"

Her arm lifted, trembling, pointing toward the sky as twilight stars blinked awake.

"The stars return… the river shifts…"

Then—stillness.

"Grandmother?" Gallo whispered.

He shook her.

"No. No—you can't die now. You just told us—!"

"She's gone," Terence said gently.

Gallo broke.

"She knew where it was," he sobbed. "She knew how to save you—!"

Cesare rose.

"What is the Venus of Valdina?" he asked, voice steady.

No grief. No hesitation.

Only calculation.

"She didn't say it was impossible," he continued. "She said I couldn't find it."

He wiped the blood from his coat.

"Contact the homeland. I'm staying in Valdina."

Terence swallowed.

"When you meet an obstacle," his old master's words echoed, "you do not go around it. You break it."

Cesare looked once more at the corpse.

"I will reclaim my life."

The Outskirts of the Capital

Sunset

The carriage slowed.

Then stopped.

Rough voices cut through the trees.

"Tattoos," she murmured. "Asilum traffickers."

Fate.

This was the day Saya died in the previous life.

"Your Highness?" Neril asked.

Medea handed her gold.

"Efficiency first."

Neril stepped down, tossing the pouch.

"Take it and leave."

The leader weighed it.

"Generous."

Then he smiled.

"But that carriage?" His eyes slid to Medea. "Five times the price."

Torches flared.

Medea stepped out.

Silver hair caught firelight. Saya shrank behind her.

"Jackpot," the man breathed.

"Neril."

"Yes."

Steel sang.

Neril moved.

Not wild. Not loud.

Clinical.

Tendons severed. Knees shattered. Men fell screaming.

In seconds, bodies littered the road.

The leader staggered back.

We can't win.

Then he saw Medea.

Still. Unmoving.

Weak.

A mistake.

"Take her!" he screamed, lunging—

The world ended.

Neril intercepted him mid-stride.

Steel flashed once.

The scream never finished.

Blood soaked the road.

Silence fell.

Medea turned away, shielding Saya's eyes.

"Some weeds," she said calmly, "must be uprooted."
