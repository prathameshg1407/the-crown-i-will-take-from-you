Chapter 181

Diverging Paths

Some bonds are forged in blood. Others are severed by faith.

"Saya."

Theo lifted his gaze to meet his flesh-and-blood cousin, noting how the years had etched subtle changes into features he'd once known as well as his own. She was older now—still recognizable, yet undeniably different.

"I'm all right now. I've moved past that day. So you should let it go as well."

Silence hung between them.

"I won't cling to you anymore just because we share the same blood."

Saya's mind drifted back three years, to the twisted, anguished expression that had contorted her brother's face—a visage warped by infinite confusion and pain.

The composed knight standing before her now bore little resemblance to that tormented boy whose broken sounds had echoed through her nightmares.

"I finally understand what you meant back then. Perhaps... we were always meant to walk different paths."

"What are you saying?"

"The Saintess is the one destined to save this world. Supporting her is the will of the Goddess—the sacred mission entrusted to me."

Just as Saya had discovered her place at Medea's side, Theo too had found where he belonged.

"Wait, Theo—"

Her brother's expression carried an unsettling echo of High Priest Paul's—that same blind devotion to the Saintess, that same fanatical certainty.

Saya's brow furrowed in concern.

"Don't you find any of this... strange? You were at that banquet. The Goddess's will? How can you be so certain? That Saintess—she's not what she seems—"

"Enough! Princess Valdina—do you think I'm blind to your mistress deliberately sabotaging the Saintess?"

Theo's voice cut through the air like a blade. Saya's composure cracked.

"Sabotaging her? Do you have any idea that the Saintess tried to frame Her Highness? That woman—"

"Saya, if you're attempting to sow discord, then stop. Nothing you say will shake our faith."

"Theo!"

"What remains clear is this: the Goddess has chosen the Saintess. She is the one we've been waiting for—the only one capable of saving this world from its ordained end."

Saya fell silent.

Her brother truly believed the Saintess was a divine savior sent by the Goddess herself.

How is this any different from those cultists you once condemned?

The words burned on Saya's tongue, but she swallowed them back, refusing to let them escape.

"So deliver this message for me."

Theo's voice turned cold, formal.

"If your mistress attempts to slander the Saintess one more time—if she continues this conspiracy to undermine our Holy Kingdom—"

Theo drew a measured breath, his gaze turning to ice as it fixed upon his sister.

"Both you and she will pay the price."

Without waiting for a response, without a backward glance, he turned and strode away—his departure swift and final, as though severing invisible ties with each step.

Watching her brother's retreating form, Saya felt the world tilt slightly beneath her feet.

Blood relatives.

Even severed ties couldn't entirely erase affection—couldn't completely eliminate the remnants of shared history and childhood memories.

It had already pained her not knowing whether her brother lived or died these past years. But to find him alive, only to discover him placing his faith in that duplicitous Saintess...

"Theo... what has happened to you?"

Her whispered lament dissolved into the thick fog surrounding them, unanswered.

But Saya remained ignorant of one crucial detail.

It was the Princess's doing.

Theo, who had also attended that fateful banquet, had witnessed the Goddess's statue suddenly weeping tears of blood.

While others reacted with shock and awe, Theo's attention had been drawn elsewhere—directly to Medea.

Medea orchestrated all of this.

The arrival of Katzen's First Prince had shifted the situation instantaneously—transforming what should have been the Saintess's moment of glory into her worst nightmare.

"This can't be happening. It's fake—all of it. You can't deny me. I'm the Saintess. How could..."

Rachel's composure had unraveled like a spider's web catching fire, disintegrating strand by delicate strand.

It was precisely then that their eyes met.

The moment he encountered those piercing green eyes—cold, calculating, seeming to dissect him even from across the crowded hall—Theo understood with absolute certainty.

Everything had been calculated. Every move, every consequence, orchestrated at her fingertips like a master puppeteer controlling unseen strings.

Because he knew. Because he'd experienced it firsthand.

Three years prior, caught between the rebel forces and his sister's desperate pleas, Theo had worn the same pallor now draining the Saintess's complexion—that same expression of helpless terror.

The Princess of Valdina hadn't looked away.

Her gaze remained steady, unwavering—as though it mattered not at all whether Theo comprehended the truth, as though nothing would change regardless of his realization.

Did my existence ever hold even the slightest significance to that woman?

In the end, he had been the first to break eye contact.

His fingers had trembled. Long-forgotten fear had surged through his veins like ice water.

It felt as though he—not the Saintess—had become ensnared in that invisible web, helpless and struggling.

So he had done nothing.

Despite his reverence for the Goddess. Despite his devotion to the Saintess.

His sincere faith—painstakingly constructed over three years of dancing between life and death—ultimately proved powerless against the insurmountable wall that was Medea.

Shame coursed through him, followed swiftly by anger.

Why can't I escape her influence?

Then and now. Past and present.

I refuse to be shaken. I cannot allow that woman to manipulate my life again.

Theo bit down hard on his lower lip.

He fought desperately to erase the delicate face etched indelibly into his memory—a face that refused to fade no matter how hard he tried.

"Theo! Perfect timing. I must see the High Priest immediately. You'll escort the Saintess to her quarters."

Commander Leonor of the Holy Knights spotted Theo and beckoned him over.

Among the Templars, serving the Saintess was a privilege reserved for veterans—those who had proven their loyalty and skill through years of service.

The more perceptive knights recognized what this assignment signified: the Commander trusted Theo enough to groom him as a potential successor.

"I trust only you with this, Theo."

The Commander, failing to notice Theo's momentary hesitation, clapped him firmly on the shoulder.

Theo suppressed a sigh and nodded curtly.

"Yes, Commander."

The turmoil churning within his heart vanished beneath the practiced mask of a faithful, expressionless knight.

"I appreciate your care, Sir Theo."

Rachel offered him a gentle smile.

Her golden hair caught the sunlight, each strand seeming to glow with ethereal radiance. Her porcelain skin appeared almost luminescent—sacred, untouchable.

A magnificent aura surrounded the Saintess, like the brilliance of dawn breaking across a dark horizon.

Who could possibly claim this person isn't the Goddess's chosen vessel?

Rebellion surged within Theo's chest—fierce and righteous.

Guilt followed close behind, sharp and accusing, for having turned away from the Saintess out of fear of the Princess.

"Yes, Lady Rachel. I, Theodore, shall serve you faithfully until my final breath, in accordance with the Goddess's divine will."

Rachel's eyes widened slightly, as though pleasantly surprised by the fervor in his declaration. Then her expression softened into a bashful smile.

"Thank you, Sir Theo. I too shall protect the people of this land and deliver the Goddess's will until my dying day. No matter what trials I face, no matter how treacherous the path becomes—I will not falter."

Rachel's eyes momentarily glistened as recent humiliations flashed through her mind. But she blinked away the gathering moisture and spoke with renewed determination.

"This is the sacred mission the Goddess entrusted to me—the very reason I exist as the 'Saintess.'"

Yes, Saintess. I will walk beside you.

Goddess, I swear I will never waver again. I shall follow only Your path.

In that moment, Theo made his silent oath.

Never again would he allow thoughts of those green eyes to surface. Never again would he permit that silent, commanding presence to shake his resolve.

But as always, human vows are shallow things.

The future remains shrouded in chaos.

And it is the fundamental law of humanity—we cannot see even an inch ahead into the darkness that awaits.
