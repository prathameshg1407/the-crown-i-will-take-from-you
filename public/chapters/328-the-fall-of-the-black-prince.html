Chapter 328

The Fall of the
Black Prince

"In the frozen silence, hope becomes the cruelest companion of all."

Medea's eyes traced the clumsy handwriting with quiet wonder.

"...Thank you, Grandfather, for the toy you made me."

It was a letter penned by a young Cesare, brimming with childish excitement over a simple gift.

Yet the fact that it remained here, tucked away in this drawer, meant it had never been sent. The boy had never shared his gratitude with the person who had shown him kindness.

Even as a child, he understood his position—an illegitimate son, entrusted to the Eastern Empress, forbidden from forming bonds that might later be used against him.

That premature resignation. That understanding that even simple affection was beyond his reach.

The gap between the wobbly letters and the weight of isolation they represented made something in her chest ache.

When he returns, I will be the one to reach out first this time...

She would embrace that lonely back in silence. Just as he had once offered his arms to her without hesitation.

Her fingertips absently traced the edge of his desk—the place where he should be sitting—before stopping abruptly.

A commotion erupted at the bedroom entrance.

When Medea turned, familiar faces appeared behind Neril—whose complexion had gone deathly pale.

"Kuh... cough—"

"Your Highness..."

Terrence, supported by Gallo's arm, coughed violently—and blood spattered from his lips.

Then he collapsed.

"Terrence!"

As Neril rushed to catch him, Gallo stumbled forward and dropped to his knees before Medea.

His body was a canvas of wounds. His skin bore the mottled red and blue of severe frostbite. And his face—

His face was streaked with tears.

"The Commander... The Commander is..."

Before he could finish, Medea's vision wavered.

The clear sky she had been waiting beneath seemed to shatter before her eyes.

The assault on the warlock stronghold had proceeded without failure—or so it had seemed.

"...Terrence located the Golden Tower concealed behind an ice wall barrier. The Commander and I breached the center and engaged the warlocks directly. We eliminated most of them."

The interior of the Golden Tower had been a living nightmare.

Cursed mechanisms lined every corridor. Primordial darknesses—Erosion, Wraith, Grudge—were unleashed by the desperate sorcerers. Demonic test subjects, freed from their prisons, hurled themselves at the invaders in a final, savage resistance.

It was a battle that cost them half their forces.

"The warlock at the tower's heart escaped. The Commander pursued him alone."

Gallo's teeth ground together.

"Terrence had exhausted nearly all his magical reserves breaking the barrier. Edra accompanied the Commander in his place..."

When Facade's mercenaries finally caught up to provide support, they were one step too late.

"Edra was a brainwashed assassin."

Stabbed through the back, grievously wounded, Cesare had plunged over the cliff's edge—the warlock still clutched in his grasp.

The assassin had fled in the ensuing chaos.

"The moment the Commander fell, the Golden Tower's barrier reactivated. A massive avalanche followed."

Amid the torrential cascade of ice and snow, it became impossible to determine whether Cesare lay trapped within the barrier's confines or buried beneath the frozen deluge.

"Time is running out. If we don't find him before the barrier fully dissipates, he'll be swallowed into nothingness along with it."

Gallo bit his lip so hard it split open. Blood dripped unnoticed down his chin.

"Your Highness, what do we do? What should we do...?"

The man who had always worn mischief like a mask now sat frozen, paralyzed with terror.

He looked more shattered than he had three years ago, when his own death sentence had loomed.

From that alone, everyone understood: there was no hope left to speak of.

Medea swayed, one hand catching the edge of the desk to steady herself.

Why didn't I see it sooner?

The dream. The golden tower consumed by flames, collapsing upon the figure trapped within.

Was it a vision of what was to come...?

The Imperial Palace of Katzen

Word of the First Prince's fate reached the imperial court with devastating swiftness.

"Cesare is dead? What manner of nonsense is this?"

Perdiccas II could scarcely believe his ears.

That boy has survived everything. He returned from an illness no physician could explain. How could he die so... helplessly?

"It is reported that an assassin had infiltrated the First Prince's retinue."

The attendant kept his eyes lowered, his bow deep.

"During the assault on the warlock stronghold, the assassin struck. His Highness was stabbed through the heart and fell from a cliff of immeasurable depth."

Even the attendant's voice faltered mid-sentence. He, too, struggled to accept what he was conveying.

"The glory of Katzen, brought low..."

Someone muttered beneath their breath, then caught the Emperor's eye and fell silent.

"Your Majesty!"

The massive frame of Perdiccas II swayed dangerously.

CLANG!

His golden staff slipped from nerveless fingers, clattering against the marble floor.

"I am fine. I... I am fine."

Supported back onto his throne, the Emperor sat in heavy silence.

...The conquest of the continent will never be completed in my lifetime.

What wounded him most deeply was not the loss of a son.

It was the death of his ambition—the realization that his dreams of a conquering monarch's legacy would die with him.

Who will carry forth the greatness of Katzen now?

"Have you heard? The First Prince—"

"Impossible. That man? He's cheated death a dozen times."

"This time is different. Stabbed in the back atop an iceberg, then plunged off a cliff. They searched for days and found nothing."

"Still, the First Prince has escaped worse—"

"Hmph. Have you ever seen the northern icebergs? The frozen spires beneath those ice walls grow like daggers. Even if he survived the fall, a stab wound in that cold—where the snow reaches your neck and freezes men solid—is a death sentence."

News of the tragedy spread beyond the palace walls with unnatural speed.

Such rapid dissemination was impossible without deliberate intervention.

The nobles and courtiers of Katzen responded with a cacophony of competing interests.

"Your Majesty, no body has been recovered! It is far too soon to abandon hope. Dispatch a rescue expedition to the icebergs at once!"

Those who refused to believe, clinging to impossible odds.

"We... We merely await Your Majesty's command."

Those who remembered how the First Prince had defied a death sentence once before—and feared what might happen if he did so again.

"The icebergs are now shrouded in dark magic barriers. Even the formidable First Prince's forces have lost half their strength. In this moment of chaos, we cannot squander further resources!"

And those who saw opportunity in Cesare's fall.

Among the latter, none hungered for this opportunity more than Jason.

He actually struck the Golden Tower... How did he discover its location?

The warlocks' final sanctuary—the fortress they had constructed over generations—annihilated in a single assault.

Cesare's relentless tenacity made Jason's teeth grind with impotent fury.

And yet, beneath that fury, satisfaction bloomed.

Cesare and the warlocks—both have destroyed each other. The shameful connection I could never sever has been severed for me.

Everyone who knew of my involvement with them is now gone.

The Third Prince, Juan, freshly released from the dungeons, struggled to contain his elation.

Outwardly, he adopted a mask of grief, head bowed in apparent sorrow. But within, he repeated the Western Empress's whispered instructions:

"Even if Cesare lives, he must never be permitted to return."

Both Juan and Jason had reached the same conclusion: no aid would be sent to the icebergs.

Three years ago, they had failed to eliminate Cesare despite his death sentence—and had paid dearly for that failure.

This time, they would leave no room for miracles.

The First Prince's death must become the foundation of a new order.

The escalating arguments fell silent as the attendant's calm voice cut through the chamber.

"The First Princess arrives."

Medea entered the throne room with the same elegant bearing she had displayed at her coronation.

Though she must have received word of her husband's fate, no trace of tears—dried or otherwise—marred her beautiful features.

Was her husband's life truly of no concern to her? Or had she simply mastered the art of concealment?

"The Marquis of Hippolyto wears the formal robes of the Mage's Tower. Has he come in his official capacity?"

"And that fair-haired man beside her—is that not the head of Facade? So the rumors were true—the continent's greatest arms dealer serves the First Prince?"

Recognition rippled through the assembly as they identified the two figures flanking Medea.

Stunned silence followed.

With Cesare's fate uncertain, the public appearance of these two continental powers was nothing less than a declaration: the Mage's Tower and Facade stood firmly behind the First Prince's faction.

The Marquis might be excused as a childhood friend... but Facade? If the continent's premier arms merchant supports the First Prince, His Majesty will be far from pleased.

Suspicion and jealousy kindled in Perdiccas II's eyes.

"So he had secured even Facade. No wonder the arrogant wretch refused the Crown Prince title—he believed he could seize my throne whenever he wished."

"First Princess. We assume you have come upon hearing the tragic news."

Medea offered the Emperor a formal greeting.

Every eye in the chamber fixed upon her, searching for any sign, any indication of whether Cesare might yet live.

"I greet the Sun of Katzen. Forgive me for disturbing Your Majesty's peace with such grave tidings, but the matter demanded immediate attention."

"Your Majesty, we must arrange the funeral with all haste! The proper rites must be performed before the soul departs, so that the First Prince may find peace in the next world."

The Count of Montfort spoke quickly, eager to prevent any shift in the current atmosphere.

"We remain uncertain whether he lives or has perished, yet you speak already of funerals?"

Medea's lips curved into something that was not quite a smile.

"Count Montfort, one might almost think you prefer the First Prince dead."

They had heard only of his disappearance—yet they rushed to bury him.

"The First Prince ventured to the border to subjugate threats to Katzen's sovereignty—to strengthen this empire with his own hands. If you dismiss his sacrifice so lightly, tell me: who among Katzen's subjects will ever risk their lives for their country again?"

"I... That is..."

The Count found himself without words.

Unable to formulate a response, he turned his desperate gaze toward the Emperor.

"First Princess."

Perdiccas II's voice echoed through the chamber—cold, hollow, utterly devoid of the warmth he had shown his eldest son mere weeks ago.

His gaze lingered briefly on Gallo and Terrence standing behind her, then slid away with calculated dismissal.

"...The goddess grows wrathful when fortune is spurned. Perhaps this is her punishment for rejecting the blessing I offered him."

He had always been a monarch who savored sweetness and spat out anything that turned bitter.

The formidable spirit of the First Prince—so recently praised—meant nothing now that his usefulness had ended.

"Your protector is dead. I shall overlook your impudence this once. But hereafter, if you fail to guard your tongue..."

His eyes turned to ice.

"...your head will be the first to roll."
