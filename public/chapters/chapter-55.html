The Minister's Demand

“Allow it?”

Etienne laughed—a raw, scraping sound that hurt to hear.

“Do you think I am asking for your permission, Joaquin?”

“Birna is a child!” Claudio roared, slamming his fist into the table. “She is too young for marriage—far too young for you!”

“Was she too young to poison a Minister of the Crown?” Etienne hissed. “Too young to annihilate my bloodline?”

“We were allies,” Etienne spat.

“Until your daughter castrated me.”

“You have until the end of the week,” he said coldly. “Deliver the girl—or I deliver the truth.”

The Duke’s Estate

“Where is she?!”

Claudio burst into the manor like a storm.

Birna shrank back against the wall.

“Father—”

Slap.

The sound cracked through the room like a gunshot.

“Do you know what you’ve done?!” Claudio screamed. “You drugged the Minister! You ruined everything!”

He stormed out, leaving only trembling silence.

Birna collapsed, sobbing.

“Mother…”

The Sacrifice

Catherine’s expression hardened to ice.

She turned to Samon.

“Is it true? Does Etienne demand marriage?”

“Yes,” Samon said grimly. “He says Birna must replace what she destroyed.”

“A broodmare,” he added bitterly.

“That animal!” Birna shrieked. “I’d rather die!”

“Silence,” Catherine snapped.

She paced once—then stopped.

“We act first,” she said. “Before your father breaks.”

She turned to Sheila.

“Hold her.”

Two guards seized the maid.

“Write,” Catherine ordered, shoving a quill into Sheila’s shaking hand. “Confess that you acted alone. Out of jealousy.”

Sobbing, Sheila scribbled until the page was soaked with tears.

“I did it,” she cried. “Please forgive me!”

Catherine took the paper, unread.

“It is your fault,” she said coldly. “For failing your mistress.”

A guard forced a vial between Sheila’s lips.

She convulsed, choking, collapsing in a foaming heap.

Birna screamed.

“It was Sheila,” Catherine said calmly.

“She confessed. Then she chose death.”

False Sanctuary

“Pack your things.”

“Where?”

“St. Agnes Convent.”

“A convent?!”

“Sanctuary,” Catherine replied. “Even Etienne won’t defile holy ground.”

“Grow up, Birna. This is the real world.”

The Princess’s Palace

Medea watched the candle flame waver.

“A carriage left the Claudio estate last night,” Neril reported. “St. Agnes Convent.”

“So,” she murmured. “Aunt Catherine chose a scapegoat.”

“She killed the maid.”

“And hid the daughter,” Medea smiled faintly. “How predictable.”

“Send word to Umberto. Inform the Minister where Birna is.”

“Yes,” Medea said softly. “A wounded beast bites hardest when mocked.”

“And the maid—does she have family?”

“A younger sister.”

Medea’s eyes glinted like amethyst in the dark.

“There is no spy more loyal than one born of vengeance.”

The Toad Moves

The Hunt Has Begun

A girl hides behind holy walls.
A mother stains her hands with innocent blood.
And a princess fans the flames of a toad’s madness.

Run, Birna.
Run fast.
The toad is coming.
