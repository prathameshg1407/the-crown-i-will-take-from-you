Ambient: The Trial
Continuation

The
Accusation

"What they say is not true."

The Queen's Palace swallowed sound.

Medea stepped into the vast hall, her footsteps vanishing against marble floors polished by centuries of submission.

Stone roses bloomed along the walls—delicate, obsessive carvings from a time when this palace had known laughter. Now they felt like funeral wreaths.

Pale goddesses stood in silent rows, marble eyes fixed downward in eternal judgment.

Frankincense choked the air. Cold. Heavy. Funereal.

It hasn't changed, Medea thought.

Only I have.

As a child, she had feared this place. The statues had seemed alive then—waiting for her to err.

If she cried, she was scolded. If she spoke, she was corrected.

So she learned to bleed quietly.

— The Favorite —

"Grandmother!"

Birna ran ahead, skirts swishing, etiquette abandoned. She threw herself into the Queen Mother's arms like a spoiled kitten.

"My little flower," the Queen Mother murmured, stroking Birna's hair. Her sharp, aged face softened instantly. "Did you miss me?"

Then she looked up.

Her gaze landed on Medea—and hardened.

"You."

The warmth vanished as if it had never existed.

Medea walked to the center of the hall and stopped. Her posture was flawless. Her expression serene.

"Medea greets Grandmother."

"Kneel!"

The command cracked through the hall.

Medea lowered herself smoothly, her movements controlled, dignified. Not hurried. Not defiant.

The contrast was unmistakable.

Birna ignored protocol and was cherished.

Medea obeyed perfectly—and was condemned.

Whispers rippled through the court. Even Catherine took an instinctive step back.

Why is she this angry today? Usually, she barely acknowledges the girl.

Medea raised her eyes.

Behind the Queen Mother's throne, half-hidden by shadow, stood Madame Quiggin.

The Head Maid met Medea's gaze—and smiled.

A thin, triumphant smile.

"I have come as commanded," Medea said softly. "But I fear such anger may harm Grandmother's health. If I have sinned, please tell me how. I will beg forgiveness."

"Do you truly not know?" the Queen Mother snapped. "Or are you so shameless you dare feign ignorance?"

Her lips curled.

"Do you think that because you lacked a mother, you may grow into a fool without consequence?"

The hall went still.

Medea's eyes cooled.

"Why do you insult my parents?"

"Is my existence not insult enough to you?"

"How dare you!" the Queen Mother slammed her palm against the throne. "Do you still deny your sin?"

"I cannot deny what I do not know."

"Liar!"

The Queen Mother rose, trembling with fury.

"The Head Maid has confessed everything! You are secretly consorting with a man!"

Gasps tore through the hall.

Catherine stiffened. An affair? Impossible.

She had monitored Medea's life for years. There had been no men. No letters. No meetings.

Quiggin is lying, Catherine realized with horror.

She's gone mad.

— The Testimony —

Madame Quiggin stepped forward and bowed deeply.

"I beg Your Majesty's forgiveness," she said, voice heavy with false grief. "But I could no longer hide this disgrace."

She turned to Medea, eyes glistening.

"When I uncovered Her Highness's affair, she sought to silence me. She spread lies that I abused her, starved her… all to conceal her immorality."

She struck her chest dramatically.

"As a servant of Valdina, I could not allow the Royal Honor to be defiled. Yet the Princess refused to repent."

She gestured.

"Maria. Step forward."

Maria emerged from the shadows, dressed in silk, hair styled carefully—no longer a maid, but not quite a lady.

"My name is Maria. My mother was Her Highness's wet nurse."

She pressed a hand to her heart.

"The Princess made me accompany her whenever she met him. I begged her to stop. I told her it was wrong."

A tear slid down her cheek on cue.

"When I refused to assist her sin, she cast me out. She gave my room to another. She ordered the new maids to beat me."

Maria bowed her head.

"I only wished to save her."

"I apologize, Your Majesty. But I could no longer remain silent. Please, Your Highness—stop hiding behind lies."

They stood together:

the loyal Head Maid,

the wounded servant.

A perfect tableau.

A beautiful script, Medea thought calmly.

The actors are convincing. The audience is enthralled.

"Medea! You disgust me!"

This was worse than scandal.

A Princess humiliated by servants.

Dragged into filth by common hands.

"You bear royal blood," the Queen Mother spat, "yet behave like a streetwalker!"

Behind her sleeve, Quiggin smiled.

Just wait, she thought. When the Queen hears the man's name…

Then—

"What they say," Medea's voice rang out—clear, steady, impossibly calm,

"is not true."

The hall froze.

Every eye turned to the kneeling Princess.

The game had begun.
