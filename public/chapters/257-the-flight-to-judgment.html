Chapter 257
Epilogue

The Flight to Judgment

Leave the dead behind and fly toward the living war.

"We must travel directly to the Holy Land of Sacramentis."

Medea—who had been mentally calculating their timeline—frowned deeply. Quiet tension rippled across her pale features.

'Peleus.'

"I need to return immediately."

Time was bleeding away faster than she'd anticipated.

"Peleus will not yield to their demands."

Which meant war was the only remaining option.

Valdina could not afford to have the entire continent turned against them.

And before that political nightmare unfolded—

No matter how urgent the situation, there was something she absolutely could not leave behind.

"D'Angel, sever those fangs."

"Yes, Your Highness."

Before Medea's command had fully settled in the air, D'Angel's aura lashed out like a crimson whip.

CRACK—

The evil dragon's enormous fangs—each the size of a cavalry saber—were cleanly severed and fell to the canyon floor.

Even detached from the corpse, the ivory tusks gleamed with a sharp, bloodthirsty menace.

Neril wrapped the fangs in leather and began securing them with rough rope.

"Wait, Sir Neril—be careful. It's dangerous to tie them so haphazardly."

Gallo, who had been watching with folded arms, reached out to help.

As Neril tightened the strap once more, their hands brushed lightly—a fleeting touch neither acknowledged.

"It would be impossible to transport this dragon's entire corpse to the Holy Kingdom, wouldn't it?"

Tom and D'Angel gazed up at the massive carcass with visible regret.

"It's not feasible. We'd have to drag it back through the pitch-black forest, and even if we somehow managed to haul just the head, it's far too large to fit on any ship."

Gallo replied with pragmatic resignation.

However, even he felt a bitter pang of loss.

'We've completed the hunt of a lifetime—something every warrior on the continent dreams of at least once!'

In his heart, he wanted to summon every mercenary in the Facade to this location and carry the entire corpse back in triumph.

"Brother Gallo, let's at least harvest the claws."

"Excellent idea. Alpha, Delta—you two collect scales. Extract as many as you can carry."

Thud. Thud.

Gallo prodded the dead dragon's massive flank with his boot, grumbling under his breath.

"If I have to pay for medical treatment because of you, I'm stripping every last scale from your hide..."

As acting commander of the Facade, he certainly didn't lack funds—but he wore the expression of a creditor facing a particularly malicious debtor.

Then Terence raised his hand hesitantly.

"Actually... there is another way."

His complexion remained deathly pale, but his eyes gleamed with unusual clarity.

"The most powerful magical material in the entire world is lying right here."

A short while later—

"Your Highness, please board."

The giant creature summoned by Terence using the evil dragon's mana—a magnificent beast named Roc—spread its colossal wings.

Strapped to Roc's back was sturdy rope rigging and a wooden platform designed to carry the entire expedition.

Roc's wingspan was genuinely comparable to the evil dragon's. With just a few powerful beats, it seemed capable of crossing entire seas.

"Sir Mage, you should have summoned this from the beginning! Did we really need to suffer through that nightmarish forest?"

The moment Gallo's complaint left his mouth, Terence's eye twitched violently.

CRACK.

The sound of teeth grinding together echoed ominously.

Terence glared at Gallo with murderous intent.

"Do you want to die, Gallo?"

He practically snarled the words.

"Do you have any comprehension of how much magical power it requires to summon a creature capable of flying such distances while carrying this many people? Even if I died trying, it wouldn't be possible under normal circumstances."

Only by sacrificing an evil dragon's heart—an incomparably rare magical catalyst—had he been able to summon Roc at all.

"If the situation weren't so desperately urgent, I would have preserved this heart in the Tower for years of research."

"Did I hear that correctly? You used that priceless dragon heart for summoning magic?! Why didn't you just rip out your own heart while you were at it?!"

Terence could practically hear his master—the Tower Lord—screaming in anguished protest from hundreds of miles away.

"We're ready to depart."

Alpha and Delta had secured the evil dragon's severed head to Roc's talons, wiping sweat from their brows.

Since the mana consumption would be catastrophic to transport the dragon's entire corpse, the expedition had agreed to take only the head and fangs as proof of their victory.

"Your Highness... what about him?"

Neril gestured toward Jason's unconscious form sprawled on the bloodstained ground.

Silence.

"I'll handle it. This is an excellent opportunity to eliminate vermin without anyone noticing..."

Without even waiting for Medea's response, Neril adjusted her dagger with cold efficiency.

She moved toward Jason but was intercepted by D'Angel's massive frame.

"He saved Her Highness's life. Have you forgotten Agema's iron law? We do not repay life-saving grace with death."

"Valdina's honorable knights may abide by such codes, but our Facade has no such restrictions. I'll take care of it. Leave him to me and everyone else can prepare for departure!"

Claiming he knew perfectly well how to dispose of bodies discreetly, Gallo pulled a leather garrote taut between his hands.

But then—

"Enough. I'll handle it."

Gallo's eyebrows rose slightly.

"Ah... Boss, I'll take care of it. I think it would be less cruel if I did it with my own hands."

For the first time, Gallo showed something resembling sympathy for Jason.

But Cesare didn't even pretend to hear him, striding past with singular purpose.

The jet-black aura that had torn the evil dragon apart now gathered ominously around his blade.

"Wait."

At Medea's voice, Cesare's sword stopped mid-swing.

Silence descended like a shroud.

Cesare understood immediately—she was concerned for Jason's life, at least for now.

He observed them both: Jason unconscious and vulnerable, Medea looking down at him with an unreadable expression.

A slow, calculating smile curved Cesare's lips.

'So Jason's sacrifice—throwing himself in front of the dragon to save her—left a significant impression.'

'Then we definitely can't execute him here.'

If Jason's death continued to occupy Medea's thoughts, what would be the point of eliminating him at all?

"As you wish, Your Highness."

As Cesare obediently dismissed his aura, Gallo looked as though his jaw might actually detach.

'Our Boss just sheathed his sword like an obedient child at Her Highness's single word?!'

Cesare took another deliberate step forward.

Then he positioned himself directly in front of Medea, gently grasping her shoulders and turning her away from Jason's prone form.

His body naturally occupied the space between them—ensuring Medea could no longer see Jason, could no longer even think about him.

His fingertips were soft against her shoulders, yet his frame was broad and immovable. Cesare leaned down slightly.

"You're absolutely right. The final judgment should not be rendered in this desolate place."

His low, drowsy voice—speaking directly into her ear—left a dizzying resonance that lingered like warm honey.

Medea nodded slightly.

It was such a small gesture it would be missed by anyone not watching closely—but Cesare caught it immediately, understanding her unspoken intent.

He wrapped his arm more securely around her shoulders and guided her forward.

Medea didn't resist. She moved naturally in response to his touch, as though it were the most natural thing in the world.

As they passed, her peripheral vision caught a glimpse of Jason's fallen form.

"Medea... my wife... my companion..."

Those rust-colored eyes moved past him without pause, sweeping across the barren canyon instead.

She wanted to deal with him immediately—but not in this remote wasteland where no one would witness it.

A death that no one sees or knows about is meaningless.

The revenge Medea would ultimately deliver must come only after his power and honor had been dragged through the mud and trampled into dust.

If Jason truly remembered everything from his past life as Emperor, the fall would be even more exquisitely painful.

Moreover, if Jason died here in obscurity—

Just as her thoughts threatened to spiral endlessly, Cesare's thumb tapped against her shoulder.

Lightly at first. Then with more certainty.

It was like a child's subtle motion, quietly demanding attention.

"That's sufficient. You don't need to look at him any longer."

Before Medea could even attempt to glance back, Cesare's body had completely blocked her view.

Not even a strand of Jason's crimson hair remained visible.

"Let them find him."

Medea jerked her chin toward the distant canyon entrance.

In the far distance, she spotted a group of figures who had just hurried into the gorge—likely Jason's own expedition members searching for survivors.

'My Princess has exceptionally sharp eyes. When did she notice them?'

A silent smile spread across Cesare's features, reaching his golden eyes.

He nodded with something approaching canine obedience—though the expression somehow didn't diminish his natural authority in the slightest.

"Let's depart."

Medea turned away with cool indifference, and Cesare spoke as though he'd been waiting for precisely those words.

SCREEEEE!

Terence gave a shrill whistle—the signal to depart.

Roc's massive wings beat the air with thunderous force, lifting the expedition skyward.

Below, the canyon—still stained with dragon's blood and littered with the remnants of battle—grew smaller and smaller.

Jason's unconscious form became nothing more than a distant speck.

Then even that vanished from view.

As they soared above the pitch-black forest, Medea's thoughts turned toward what awaited them.

Peleus will be waiting in the Holy Land of Sacramentis.

Her brother—stubborn, principled, and utterly unwilling to bend before the Holy Nation's corrupt judgment.

The Continental Council had been convened specifically to condemn him, to strip Valdina of its autonomy and bring it under the Holy Nation's control.

They would use the pretense of monster waves and continental security to justify their power grab.

But Medea had slain the evil dragon—the very creature whose existence had been the oracle's justification for condemning her as a witch and sacrifice.

She carried its fangs and severed head as proof.

'Let them try to condemn the Dragonslayer.'

A cold smile touched Medea's lips.

'Let them try to claim I'm a cursed witch when I've done what no Saintess could—what no holy warrior in their precious Nation has accomplished in five hundred years.'

The political war ahead would be brutal.

But she had fought this battle before in her previous life—and lost because she'd been alone, powerless, betrayed.

This time, everything would be different.

As Roc carried them toward their uncertain future, Cesare remained close to Medea's side—a silent, protective presence.

The wind whipped through their hair and cloaks, but his arm stayed steady around her shoulders.

Neither spoke.

Words weren't necessary.

Behind them: a slain dragon and a broken man left to contemplate his failures.

Ahead: the Holy Land, political machinations, and the next battlefield in Medea's war for vengeance.

But for this brief moment suspended between earth and sky—

There was only the rush of wind, the steady beat of Roc's wings, and the strange, unexpected comfort of companionship freely given.
