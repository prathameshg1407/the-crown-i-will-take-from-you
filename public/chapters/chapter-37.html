The Gentle
Blade

"I will not soften. I will not stop."

"It smells pleasant."

Medea swirled the dark liquid in the porcelain cup. The surface reflected candlelight like oil.

"It is… a rare blend."

The maid said, sweat dotting her brow.

"Then drink it."

"Your Highness, I cannot—"

"Drink."

The word was not loud. It did not need to be.

The maid's fingers trembled. She knew the scent.

Halus.

The slow poison. The madness that crept in gently and never left.

The door creaked open.

"Your Highness—"

Neril stepped inside, hesitating. A second figure slipped past her.

"Wow!"

A small boy darted forward, eyes shining.

"Mom!"

He ran straight into the maid's arms.

"Jeremy? Why are you here?"

"The nice lady brought me! She said I could see the palace! She promised not to tell Grandma!"

He looked around in awe.

"It's shiny! And it smells like cookies!"

His gaze landed on Medea.

"Are you the Princess?"

"Yes."

"Where are your horns?"

Medea paused.

She knelt, bringing herself to his height.

"Horns?"

"They say the Princess is a monster. With horns. But you look like the moon."

He reached up, touching her silver hair.

Neril moved instinctively—

Medea lifted a hand.

"It's fine."

She let the child touch her.

"Jeremy. Are you thirsty?"

She lifted the cup.

The maid's blood turned to ice.

"Yes! Can I drink it?"

"Of course. There's plenty."

The boy reached.

"NO!"

The maid struck the cup from his hands.

Crash.

Porcelain shattered. Dark liquid splashed across silk and stone.

The maid collapsed, dragging Jeremy into her arms.

"Please! Forgive me! I will tell you everything!"

Jeremy began to cry.

"M-Mommy?"

"Shh. It's alright. Mommy's here."

Medea looked down at them.

Her expression did not change.

"Jeremy."

The maid flinched as if stabbed.

"I need to speak with your mother. Would you wait outside?"

She gestured to a plate.

"You may take the cookies."

Jeremy sniffed.

"Really?"

"Yes."

He followed the plate out, waving.

"I'll be right back, Mommy!"

The door closed.

The maid crawled forward, clutching Medea's dress.

"It was the Duke! He gave me the tea! Halus! He said he'd kill my family if I refused!"

"When?"

"Three years ago he approached me—but I refused! I only began four months ago!"

Four months.

Medea exhaled quietly.

Reversible.

"Go to Siseo. Confess."

"I will! I swear!"

"Your son will remain here."

The maid froze.

"He will be fed. Educated. Protected. And he will remain where I can see him."

Understanding crushed the woman's hope.

"…Yes. Everything I am is yours."

She whispered.

Late Night

The palace was quiet.

Medea sat by the window, staring into the dark.

"You saw something unpleasant."

She said.

Neril stood behind her.

"If you wish to leave, do it now. I will not soften. I will not stop."

Silence.

Then fabric rustled.

Neril knelt.

"I chose this path. I will not abandon it."

Medea closed her eyes.

"Thank you."

Outside, a night bird cried—

a thin, lonely sound.

And the palace listened.
