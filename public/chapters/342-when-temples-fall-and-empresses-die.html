Chapter 342

When Temples Fall
and Empresses Die

"In the brightness of midday, the darkest treacheries unfold."

Sunlight streamed through the palace windows, brilliant and unseasonably warm.

The cheerful weather stood in cruel contrast to the tragedy Theo now recounted from his knees, voice trembling with barely suppressed anguish.

"Paul freed Rachel from the Black Floor and seized control of the Central Temple."

Medea set down her teacup with deliberate precision.

Her expression remained composed, but her green eyes sharpened to gleaming points.

"And His Holiness the Pope?"

If Pope Benedict still lived, he would never have surrendered authority so easily.

"Paul claims His Holiness has been... divinely punished."

Theo's lip trembled as he bit down hard.

The entire catastrophe hinged on the Pope's absence.

"The false Saintess deceived the faithful, persecuting the true chosen of the Goddess! How can such grave sin be forgiven?"

Paul's voice had rung out across the temple square, fervent and fanatical.

"The Goddess has punished His Holiness and granted me, Paul, a divine revelation! The law of the Holy Nation must be reestablished! The true representative is Rachel!"

The righteous priests and temple knights had erupted in protest.

"Heretical nonsense! In what scripture does the Goddess's representative wield black magic?"

"Is this truly divine punishment, or foul play? Where is Chief Priest Agnes? Where is the papal escort, Sir Martes?"

But their objections meant nothing.

"Paul declared himself Papal Vicar and exiled every Templar who opposed him. Everyone—including Commander Leonor..."

"What are you waiting for? Seize those apostates who deny divine will! For the Goddess! For Saintess Rachel!"

Paul had sealed off the Central Temple completely, severing all communication with the outside world.

Then he'd begun the purge.

It was swift. Brutal. Methodical.

Hard to believe this was the same man who had once fallen from grace alongside Rachel when she'd been exposed as a fraud.

"For Saintess Rachel! Accept the true representative of the Goddess!"

The reinstatement ceremony was held in the temple courtyard.

Beside the dais, Temple Knights knelt on cold stone, arms bound tightly with sanctified rope.

"Paul! Have you not harmed His Holiness to claim his seat? Can you swear your innocence before God?"

Rachel's gaze found Theo among the prisoners, his eyes bloodshot with fury.

A cruel light flickered in her violet eyes.

"Ahaha. Well, well. Medea's little lapdog."

A diamond tiara gleamed atop her head—far more opulent than the one Medea had worn at her wedding.

Rachel approached slowly, savoring her captured prey.

"I should have killed you long ago..."

She seized a fistful of his hair, wrenching his head back to force eye contact.

The stench of decay and blood wafted from her breath. Theo's face twisted in revulsion.

"What a pity. But soon your master will pay her debts as well. Shouldn't I repay what I've received?"

She dragged her fingers down his jaw in a mockery of affection—as one might pet a dog.

"I won't kill you just yet. Don't worry—you're useful. After all, if you're Medea's dog, shouldn't you serve your mistress properly?"

Her nails suddenly dug into his skin.

Rip.

Blood welled up, trickling down his neck in thin crimson lines.

"Such a waste... it really is a handsome face. Ahahaha!"

Her laughter rang out—clear and chilling, echoing across the courtyard like broken bells.

◆

Theo and the Temple Knights were cast into the Black Floor—the deepest prison beneath the temple.

Their Achilles tendons were severed. Their holy power was drained away drop by agonizing drop.

Not a single ray of light penetrated that place.

It was the same prison where Rachel had once been exiled.

The cell built to seal away evil had become the cage that now imprisoned the righteous.

But neither darkness nor pain could break the will of the Temple Knights.

"Run, Theo. We'll get you out safely."

Commander Leonor and the remaining knights pooled their holy power.

They were warriors who had awakened pure divine grace.

Though their bodies were broken from wounds and torture, the holy power born of their resolve blazed brilliant and strong.

Finally, they shattered the Black Floor's formidable barrier and forced an opening for Theo's escape.

But the process was not without cost.

"Alarm! Alarm! The prisoners have escaped!"

Shouts tore through the temple corridors.

Leonor shoved Theo toward the breach.

"I won't abandon the Commander and my brothers!"

"Don't be a fool! What good is a dog's death?"

Leonor grabbed Theo's face roughly.

Blood gushed from the Commander's chest like a broken fountain—the toll of breaking through the Black Floor's wards.

The holy seal carved over his heart still glimmered faintly, burning with the last of his life force.

"You must reach the Saintess. Tell her everything. His Holiness is dead. The temple has fallen into the hands of the corrupt. Only Medea can end this suffering."

Leonor's fingers trembled as they cupped Theo's bloodied cheeks.

Theo, seeing the inevitable end reflected in his Commander's eyes, felt despair close around his throat.

"I wanted to see it with my own eyes—the day you brought them to their knees."

Leonor managed a bitter smile and pressed a silver badge into Theo's palm.

Blood stained the insignia of the Templar Commander.

"Now go!"

"I'll return. I swear it—I'll come back and save you. I'll save the temple."

Theo swallowed his scream and turned, running into the darkness.

But evading the relentless pursuit from the Holy Kingdom and reaching Katzen alone proved nearly impossible.

By the time he crossed the border, Theo had sustained grievous injuries—one arm half-severed, death creeping at his heels.

"Sir Theo?! What in the—no, we'll deal with explanations later. Secure the perimeter!"

It was a miracle he survived at all.

By sheer fortune, he encountered the Facade mercenaries on the road.

They'd been en route to the Holy Kingdom on Cesare's orders—sent to investigate the alarming silence from the Central Temple.

"Come to think of it, there's been no word from the temple for weeks..."

Gallo's face darkened as he counted back the days on his fingers.

According to Theo's account, the Facade operatives who'd infiltrated the temple would have been purged as well.

A heavy shadow settled over his features. The air grew cold.

"By annihilating the Templars and severing all contact with the outside world... what are they planning?"

Neril's voice was quiet, her expression grim.

So far, news of Rachel's reinstatement and Paul's claim to papal authority had not reached a single corner of the continent.

At that moment, hurried footsteps echoed down the corridor.

"Your Highness! Your Highness!"

"What is it?"

The door burst open.

Alpha stood in the threshold, his normally composed face flushed and urgent.

In his hand, he clutched a crumpled piece of parchment.

"The Western Empress is dead."

◆◆◆

The Western Empress—accused of orchestrating the assassination attempt on First Prince Cesare—had been found dead in her chambers.

She'd been confined to the Western Palace only days prior.

"It was poison..."

The palace physician's voice trembled.

The scent of toxic herbs still lingered in the air, mingling with the faint reek of burnt silk.

Beside her body lay a suicide note.

I lost my senses in a moment of foolishness and committed an irreparable sin...

...I will pay the price with my life. I beg only that His Majesty show mercy to the innocent Third Prince, and pray that my death may lift suspicion and punishment from him.

— The Western Empress

The Imperial Family scrambled to arrange her funeral.

Ironically, the coffin originally prepared for the First Prince now served the Western Empress.

It was an expensive birch casket—sturdy and ornate—commissioned by the Empress herself in a performative show of grief for Cesare's faction.

She could never have imagined that the coffin Cesare had prepared would one day hold her body.

The casket was lowered onto the cold marble slab.

No one could quite believe what had transpired overnight.

That this woman—so formidable, so calculating—now lay cold and still within a coffin.

Dead by her own hand.

The person who could least accept the sudden death of the Western Empress was, naturally, her only son.

The Third Prince.
