Chapter 5

The Reverse Scale

Every dragon has one weakness—a single scale that points the wrong way.

"Your Highness, are you unharmed?!"

In the same breath, silver flashed past Medea with razor-thin precision.

Cesare's greatsword slashed through the air and severed the throat of the dragon clone that had bitten Jason.

SHHHHHHAAAA—

Dark crimson blood erupted in a grotesque fountain.

D'Angel arrived moments later, staring in bewilderment at Medea standing over Jason's collapsed form.

"Castulo... why would His Highness...?"

The Grand Duke certainly hadn't seemed like the selfless type capable of such sacrifice.

However, neither Medea nor D'Angel could spare time to check Jason's condition.

Four dragon clones still remained—and they were far from finished.

"Tom, watch out!"

"Don't worry about me! Take care of yourself! Another one's heading your way!"

Though they appeared to be illusions, the flames and devastating power wielded by the clones were devastatingly real.

CRASH!

Tom—who couldn't dodge the barbed spikes protruding from one clone's tail—wrapped his arms protectively around his shoulders as the impact struck.

"Damn it—these things hit harder than the real dragon!"

He hurriedly pressed down on the gaping wound and transferred his sword to his left hand, shouting toward Neril.

Neril, Tom, Cesare's companions, and Gallo paired up to face the remaining clones head-on, refusing to retreat despite mounting injuries.

KRAAAAAAAH!

When D'Angel successfully eliminated one of the clones, the remaining three grew even more vicious.

As soon as Cesare confirmed Medea's safety, he immediately pivoted and targeted the evil dragon's main body.

His black aura swirled through the weakening prison of fire like a living thing.

CLANG! CLANG!

But the dragon's scales were so impossibly hard that even his formidable aura deflected harmlessly off its hide.

The evil dragon's crimson eyes locked onto Cesare with predatory focus.

Those eyes narrowed—as if carving the image of this particular opponent into its ancient memory—and the dragon opened its maw wide.

KWAAAAAAAAAAA—

The roar of the evil dragon—accompanied by the crushing weight of Dragon Fear—echoed through the canyon and shook the very sky.

The psychic resonance paralyzed human consciousness and imprinted a primal, overwhelming sense of terror deep in the soul.

"Damn it...!"

"Tom, don't lose your mind!"

Tom clutched his head as pain lanced through his skull.

He was particularly vulnerable to the psychic assault—the agony felt like someone driving a hammer repeatedly into his brain. Tom nearly dropped his sword from the sheer intensity.

"Break the bracelet!"

Medea's cry cut through his suffering.

Tom remembered the simple leather bracelet his Princess had given him before they entered the canyon.

A small cylinder hung from the braided knot.

CRACK!

When he shattered the cylinder against his sword's hilt, green powder poured out—dried herbs ground to fine dust.

Simultaneously, a clean, refreshing scent penetrated his nostrils.

The pain immediately subsided. His vision cleared.

Tom—having barely escaped the mental assault—breathed heavily in relief.

"Hah... hah..."

Luna Digitalis. An herb specifically effective at overcoming Dragon Fear and restoring mental clarity.

Meanwhile, after confirming Medea's safety once more, Cesare turned his full attention back to the evil dragon.

Even though Dragon Fear washed over him in full force—being closest to the dragon's massive form—he didn't waver in the slightest.

"Gallo, defensive formation! Move right!"

Cesare's black aura plunged through the prison of fire, attempting to slice through the evil dragon's armored flesh.

The dragon thrashed violently against both the wretched cage imprisoning it and the audacious human who dared challenge it.

"GRRRRRRR!"

The evil dragon's resistance intensified. The prison's flames flickered precariously, beginning to destabilize.

Terence—who had been maintaining the magical construct through sheer force of will to prevent the dragon's escape—coughed up blood.

"Gah!"

"Brother, you have to hold on just a little longer! If you release the main body now, we can't handle it!"

"I know! Just hurry up and finish this!"

But despite Terence's desperate struggles, the evil dragon's main body finally shattered the fire prison completely.

WHOOOOSH—

The unleashed dragon spread its enormous wings and launched itself toward the sky.

"No... no no no...!"

Gallo let out a strangled groan of despair.

The remaining four clones immediately ceased their attacks on the expedition members and began converging toward the main body.

The sky turned black and gray as the massive wings blotted out the sun.

"No—"

"It wants to trap us all in shadow."

Medea understood the evil dragon's strategic intention immediately.

Her sharp eyes quickly scanned the expedition's current state.

Terence bleeding from magical overexertion. Gallo limping badly. Tom unable to use his right arm.

Medea's and Cesare's other companions rushed after the retreating clones, undeterred by their accumulating injuries.

Even Cesare wasn't in perfect condition to face the main body alone.

Unless he were a god, there would be limits to how long he could sustain such intense aura usage.

'Think, Medea! THINK!'

She bit her lip hard enough to draw blood.

Everyone here was outstanding—brilliant, even.

And yet they were all in mortal danger because of her.

She had led them on this expedition based on one absurdly unrealistic plan to fell the evil dragon.

She couldn't let them die in this godforsaken canyon.

"I refuse to lose anyone."

Desperate determination resonated in her voice.

Since arriving at this gorge, moments with them had flickered past like a kaleidoscope—no fear, no anxiety, even moments of unexpected joy.

Warm memories that seemed capable of covering up the scarred recollections of her previous life's expedition.

'Think. How do I bring it down? How do I solve this?'

To do that, she needed to divert the dragon's attention from the main body soaring into the sky.

She needed it to forget its original plan and turn its focus elsewhere—toward her.

Suddenly, a pool of blood caught Medea's eye.

It was the remnants of the clone D'Angel had struck down moments earlier.

A memory surfaced—words from a teammate in her past life, overheard during a similar battle:

"Dragon blood contains will and memory. A dragon can recognize its kin by a single drop of blood—and it can identify those who threaten it, like the ancient Dragonslayers of legend."

Without hesitation, Medea drove her dagger deep into her own palm.

"Your Highness?!"

Crimson blood welled up from between pale flesh.

She plunged her clenched fist into the pool of dragon blood, then scooped up a handful of the dark, viscous liquid.

The two bloods swirled separately, refusing to merge.

The dragon's blood—sticky and impossibly dark—writhed as though alive, actively avoiding Medea's scarlet offering.

'Come. Recognize me.'

Her green eyes blazed with fierce determination.

Simultaneously, the evil dragon's wings ceased their powerful beats mid-flight.

Its bright yellow eyes—slowly rotating in their sockets—perceived Medea as a complete and absolute threat.

Medea observed this reaction for a single heartbeat, then moved her hand without hesitation.

"Your Highness, NO!"

Ignoring D'Angel's horrified shout, Medea pressed the blood-soaked hand to her lips.

Her eyes—cold and calm—showed unbreakable resolve.

"But blood that carries memory is imbued with both power and curses. The blood of evil dragons especially—it runs deeper, more tenacious. You should never consume it carelessly."

That warning echoed in her mind even as she swallowed.

Excruciating pain spread through Medea's body as though she'd swallowed a sphere of molten iron.

The dragon blood that seeped into her system flowed through her veins, refusing to integrate—as if it were tearing at her intestines from within.

A violent, irregular heartbeat seized her entire frame.

"AHHH—!"

Medea's body buckled. She nearly collapsed.

"Your Highness!"

"Medea!"

The echoing cries of her companions barely registered.

Medea bit down hard on her lip, trying desperately to override the pain with sheer willpower.

'I will survive this. Together with everyone—I will defeat this dragon and return home.'

Blood and sand dust dripped from her fists as she gripped the gravel-strewn ground.

'I'm going to change everything. I won't let the past repeat itself.'

It was at that moment—when her eyes burned with earnest desire and deep-rooted tenacity—that something shifted.

Her irises flared an impossible, luminous blue.

"...!"

Brilliant light erupted from the necklace hanging around Medea's neck.

The radiance—a mixture of pure white starlight and molten gold—seemed to embody both celestial purity and divine Venus herself.

KRAAAAAAAAAAH!

When the evil dragon's agonized screams resonated in response to the light—

All sounds fractured as though Medea had been plunged underwater.

The dragon's screams and her comrades' shouts became muffled, distorted—then eerily quiet.

Her vision shattered like a broken mirror.

Then, overlaying the fragments, clear afterimages appeared:

The first fragment: A beam of light piercing through darkness that blanketed the vast land—the first ray to shred the eternal night.

The second fragment: That light, miserably fractured and driven deep underground, where it festered in the darkness.

The third fragment: Dark rage condensing into scales and bones—and from that concentrated hatred, an evil dragon was born.

But at the precise moment of its birth, a single ray of light struck the dragon's newly-formed body.

KYAAAAAAAA—

The same agonized scream the dragon made now could be heard emanating from within the vision—as if she were witnessing a fragment of the ancient past.

The final fragment enlarged the scar—like a golden brand of incontinence—that the streak of light had left between the evil dragon's impenetrable scales.

"Your Highness!"

The vision ended as the afterimages shattered completely.

"Hah... hah..."

Medea clutched her chest, gasping for breath.

"What... what did I just see?"

"Your Highness! Get over here, NOW!"

D'Angel's shout snapped her back to reality.

The evil dragon was descending with terrifying speed, its maw gaping wide as it targeted Medea specifically.

But Medea's eyes weren't focused on those vicious fangs.

Instead, they locked onto a specific location on the dragon's massive form—

'There. That's it.'

Medea understood instinctively, with absolute certainty.

The golden scar she'd witnessed in the afterimage moments ago—

That was the evil dragon's reverse scale.

Its one fatal weakness.
