Returns

"A toast to your health, Minister."

"Samon."

Medea turned.

Samon Claudio stood behind her—tall, handsome, immaculate. His blue eyes were sharp and cold, like a shark circling beneath calm water.

"Medea," he said pleasantly. "I was so distracted tonight that I forgot to congratulate you on your recovery."

His gaze swept over her.

Not fragile.

Not foolish.

Not anymore.

She stood at the center of the hall like a quiet axis around which the nobles turned. The applause, the fervor, the sudden loyalty—it had all flowed from her words.

Did she plan this? Samon wondered. Or did she simply stumble into power?

Medea's shoulders drew inward.

"Samon… did I do something wrong?"

Her voice trembled. She lowered her eyes, fingers clutching the edge of her sleeve.

"I only repeated what Aunt Catherine and Birna told me. I didn't mean to upset anyone."

She reached out, lightly gripping his arm.

"Was I arrogant? Giving Birna the dress?"

She looked up—eyes wet, uncertain, frightened.

A perfect imitation of the girl he remembered.

Samon relaxed.

Still a fool, he decided. Just lucky. Dangerous luck—but luck nonetheless.

"Of course not. You've always been too kind for your own good. Birna… lacks restraint. I'll speak to her."

He bent and brushed a kiss across Medea's knuckles.

"Enjoy your night, Dea. It is your celebration."

He walked away.

The moment his back turned,

Medea's expression flattened.

In her past life, Samon had waited patiently while her brother bled. When Peleus returned crippled, broken, the court's whispers had turned to Samon—strong, healthy, flawless Samon—as the next King.

You want the throne, she thought coldly.
You always have.

But not this time.

The Hallway

Birna stood half-hidden behind a marble pillar, nails digging into the diamond-studded fabric of her skirt.

"Why is she smiling? She should be crying."

Everyone was looking at Medea.

Not at the diamonds.

Not at the spectacle.

At her.

"It's her fault. She set me up."

She beckoned her maid sharply.

"Give this to her."

Sheila stared at the small packet pressed into her palm, her face draining of color.

"M-miss… this is—"

"Dangerous?" Birna sneered. "Only if you get caught."

She leaned in, voice venomous.

"It will make her see things. Hallucinations. She'll scream at ghosts like last time. Everyone will think she's gone mad again."

The same trick.

Different night.

Sheila's hands shook.

"If they find out—"

"Then I'll sell you to the traffickers in Asilum," Birna said calmly. "Do you understand now?"

Sheila nodded, pale and terrified, and hurried away.

"Birna."

Samon's hand clamped around her arm.

"What are you doing?"

"Nothing," Birna snapped, wrenching free.

"Don't be stupid. Too many eyes tonight. If you cause trouble, Father won't protect you."

"I know! Leave me alone."

Samon hesitated, then turned away.

Birna watched him go, lips curling.

Too late.

The Banquet Hall

"Your Highness."

A servant offered Medea a crystal goblet.

"Berry wine. Your favorite."

Medea accepted it, breathing in the scent.

Sweet. Overripe.

And beneath it—something sharp. Artificial.

Hallucinogen, she concluded. Mild. Fast-acting.

She glanced across the hall.

Birna was watching her like a hunter watching a trap close.

Medea smiled faintly.

You really haven't learned anything.

She turned her body slightly, shielding the glass from Birna's view.

Waste not, want not.

Her gaze landed on Minister Etienne.

He stood near the buffet, flushed and unsteady, already halfway drowned in wine.

Perfect.

"Minister Etienne. It has been some time."

Medea approached.

Etienne blinked at her.

"Your Highness? You… you got taller."

He laughed at his own joke, nearly spilling his drink.

"You were impressive tonight. Saint Esther herself must've kissed your tongue."

He leaned closer, breath sour.

"Or maybe you're just good at pretending."

"Did it look like pretense? They say Saint Esther despised greed. She pierced gluttons through the belly."

Etienne's smile faltered.

"She might be watching us even now."

He waved a hand dismissively.

"Hah. Just a story."

"Is it? Tell me, Minister—who authorized this banquet?"

Etienne stiffened.

"The… the Regent."

"The Regent is not the King. Using the Royal Treasury without the King's seal is embezzlement."

"That's—this was a gift!"

"Then why are the receipts logged under the Ministry of the Court?"

She stepped closer.

"I wonder what the auditors will discover tomorrow."

Etienne's face went gray.

Medea lifted the goblet—

the poisoned wine catching the light.

"But business can wait. Tonight is about peace."

She held it out.

"A toast to your health, Minister."

Etienne stared at the glass.

"My… health?"

"It is my favorite vintage. Please."

Her eyes met his—bright, calm, merciless.

Etienne hesitated.

Then, laughing nervously, he took the glass.

"Of course. For peace."

Medea released it.

And stepped back.
