Chapter 166

The Counterfeit
Blade

"Truth cuts deeper than any steel—even when forged from lies."

How dare they make Dea cry.

Medea—who had never shed a single tear, not through war, not through tragedy—must have felt such profound injustice that she wept without making a sound.

She was Peleus's greatest pride. But she was also the tender wound he carried closest to his heart.

Peleus bore the weight of sovereignty with absolute seriousness. He pursued righteousness in all things, held himself to standards that would break lesser men.

But there existed one exception to every principle he held sacred.

His only sister.

Now, as he surveyed the chaos before him, Peleus recognized the common thread binding these strange events—the Saintess stood at their center.

"What if I simply cannot exist beneath the same sky as that woman? Would you still understand me?"

— Medea's words echoed in his memory

Something was wrong from the very beginning.

If the sharp alertness he had witnessed that day—that uncharacteristic anxiety so unlike his composed sister—had originated from this woman...

Then Peleus was prepared to take her head without hesitation.

Whether she was his benefactor or the Goddess's chosen vessel.

Even if the sin invoked divine wrath and condemned him to the deepest pit of hell.

Peleus's glacial gaze fell upon Rachel.

Her shoulders convulsed at the weight of that stare—cold, unfathomable, revealing nothing of the storm within.

When he spoke, his voice carried to every corner of the garden with crystalline clarity.

"That dagger belongs to me."

The words fell like hammer blows.

"I was the first to learn of the Saintess's injuries. I was the one who forbade her from leaving these grounds."

He paused, letting the implications sink in.

"By your own logic, Saintess, I stand far closer to the role of perpetrator than my sister ever could. So tell me—why did you point specifically at Dea?"

The assembly held its collective breath.

"Your Majesty, there is no need to lower yourself to—"

Rachel's response came halting, flustered. Peleus fixed her with eyes that had grown even colder.

"Do you truly believe that?"

"I... pardon?"

"Ha!"

High Priest Paul stepped forward with a derisive laugh.

"So His Majesty wishes to shield his little sister? I had heard the King of Valdina was a wise man capable of distinguishing virtue from vice. It seems such praise was nothing but empty flattery."

He gestured dismissively at the dagger.

"This is clearly a woman's blade—set with jewels, far too delicate for His Majesty's hand. The evidence speaks for itself."

"It was not my sister who questioned the Saintess's legitimacy. It was I."

Medea's eyes widened with surprise.

She had expected protection—but she had never imagined he would step forward to take her place entirely.

His broad back now formed an impenetrable wall before her, shielding her from every accusation, every venomous glance.

Why didn't I see it then?

Why didn't I recognize that I possessed a family who would protect me completely—without condition, without cost?

Why did I never even try to discover that truth?

The regret was fleeting.

I will not miss it this time.

As Medea clenched her fist beneath the concealment of her sleeves, Paul's voice rose to a thunderous pitch.

"Your Majesty, are you now confessing to denying your faith in the Goddess? Do you not fear being branded a heretic before all the continent?"

At that moment, Sissair bent gracefully and retrieved the fallen dagger.

He examined the blade with meticulous care, turning it beneath his spectacles—and then, inexplicably, he laughed.

A soft, knowing chuckle that made no attempt to hide itself.

Paul's brow furrowed with indignation.

"Superstition, Your Eminence..."

Sissair's smile remained fixed in place.

"Let us assume, for argument's sake, that these peculiar objects were indeed intended to harm Miss Rachel—or rather, the Saintess. But does something not strike you as peculiar?"

Without warning, Sissair pressed his palm against the blade's edge—pressing down with deliberate force, as though attempting to sweep it clean.

By all logic, the steel should have carved deep into his flesh.

"Sir—!"

"Observe closely."

He held up his unmarked hand for all to see.

"No wound. This is nothing more than an expertly crafted counterfeit."

"What... what nonsense is this?"

Rachel's blood ran cold.

That cannot be true...!

But even as denial surged through her, she noticed what she had missed before—the dagger's luster had faded, its gleam nothing like the distinctive radiance that genuine mithril possessed.

Mithril was among the rarest natural resources in existence.

Only three veins had ever been discovered across the entire continent, each located within natural fortresses so treacherous that access—let alone extraction—remained nearly impossible.

Demand so vastly exceeded supply that the metal had become the exclusive province of the wealthiest nobles and most powerful royalty.

Rachel—a wandering gypsy who had never laid eyes upon genuine mithril—possessed no means of distinguishing the authentic from the false.

I retrieved the correct dagger—I am certain of it!

Rachel bit down hard on her lip.

The warlocks had taught her curses and black magic sufficient to kill. But detailed instruction regarding weapons and materials? Such knowledge had never been part of her education.

She had been deceived by an imitation designed specifically to be discovered.

"This blade is not mithril, nor does it belong to Her Highness. Therefore, it would be entirely improper to suggest that the Princess attempted any harm against the Saintess."

Sissair's gaze settled upon Rachel, and behind his spectacles, mockery glittered.

"It would appear... that someone wished very much for you to believe otherwise."

The momentum had shifted entirely.

Now it was the Holy Nation delegation—so recently triumphant in their accusations—who found themselves cornered.

The Templar Commander bit the inside of his cheek in frustration.

Damn it all. I should have examined the dagger before permitting this spectacle.

The High Priest and the Valdinians had been so absorbed in their verbal combat that basic verification had been neglected entirely.

He had accepted the Saintess's claims without question—behavior utterly uncharacteristic of a seasoned commander. Why had he trusted so blindly?

"So the dagger does not belong to me after all..."

Medea's voice cut through the tension—calm, measured, carrying not a trace of accusation.

"What a pity. I had expected greater discernment from the Goddess's chosen representative. To act with such haste, such carelessness..."

Though her words contained no direct rebuke, Rachel felt them like a brand upon her skin.

The meaning beneath the surface was unmistakable: You lack the qualities befitting a true Saintess.

"I... I never..."

"It now appears certain that someone has deliberately attempted to frame Her Highness the Princess."

Sissair's smile remained pleasant, but his eyes had turned to ice.

"To fall into such rage over a curse cast with a counterfeit blade... Does that not seem rather fragile for one who claims to represent the divine?"

"Y-you cannot confirm this was not the work of some Valdinian! Even if the Princess herself is innocent, someone within this kingdom might have—"

Paul clung desperately to any alternative that did not implicate the Saintess herself.

"Why would the Saintess orchestrate such a thing? With priests from the Holy Nation arriving, there would be no benefit to damaging relations with Valdina."

The Templar Commander added his voice in Rachel's defense.

"Unless, of course, the intention was to sow discord."

Sissair's response came swift and merciless.

"Her Highness the Princess is Valdina's guiding light. To tarnish her reputation would inevitably destabilize the entire kingdom. I find that a far more plausible motive, don't you?"

"Preposterous!"

Why is the conditioning failing? It should be taking hold by now...!

Rachel stepped forward, refusing the accusations with every fiber of her being—unaware that every mark she had so painstakingly implanted had already vanished.

Yet even drenched in perspiration, her face exposed and vulnerable, she managed to project an image of wounded innocence that stirred sympathy in many hearts.

But not all hearts were so easily moved.

"Your Majesty... surely you believe me?"

Rachel reached toward Peleus with trembling hands, stumbling forward as though her legs might give way at any moment.

"I risked my very life to save you. Why would I ever—"

"Saintess! Please, it's dangerous to move so suddenly—"

A maid rushed forward to support her.

In her haste, the maid's foot caught on the edge of a carpet.

She stumbled, and the rug twisted beneath her weight, pulling away from the floor—

Revealing something that should never have been there.

A sheet of paper, covered in intricate markings and carefully inked notations.

Evidence

"This is... the structural diagram of our royal palace."

Every corridor. Every chamber. Every secret passage.

All of it meticulously documented—hidden beneath the Saintess's floor.

" The counterfeit blade had failed to cut.
But the truth emerging from beneath the carpet
would draw blood aplenty. "
