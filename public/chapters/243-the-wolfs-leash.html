Chapter 243

The Wolf's
Leash

"Choose me by your own hand—before I destroy all your other options."

Tom and Kensington's eyes flew wide as the First Prince addressed the Princess by her given name—without title, without ceremony, as though they were intimates of long standing.

Medea released a slow, measured breath.

"...Everyone, leave us."

Her retainers departed with expressions that demanded explanations she had no intention of providing.

By the time the door closed, Cesare had already claimed the seat opposite Medea, his golden eyes sweeping across the continental map and the flags marking each strategic point.

His outstretched fingers traced the map's edge with deceptive gentleness.

Yet despite his languid posture, an undercurrent of tension radiated from him—something cold and sharp that seemed to freeze the very air.

"Cesare."

"It has been a while."

A straight nose, a razor-sharp jawline, features so perfectly symmetrical they seemed crafted rather than born. An arrogant gaze that regarded everything beneath it as already conquered.

The beautiful man—reminiscent of some ancient sculptor's most ambitious work—stood before her without a single attempt at concealment.

Medea's brow furrowed. He had made no effort whatsoever to disguise his identity. As if he simply didn't care who might recognize him.

"You should be more mindful of your position. After everything you've done—"

"I believe I've missed this face."

The tender interruption cut through her cold reprimand as though he hadn't heard a word of it.

Medea paused, momentarily thrown.

"Your Highness the First Prince. Are you confessing to the murder of General Jared?"

In the chaos of the oracle, she had nearly forgotten—he had been summoned back to the Empire immediately after disposing of Jared on her behalf.

"I heard you were confined to your territory. Have you been pardoned?"

Medea found herself unable to meet his gaze directly. The memory of warm hands cupping her cheeks still lingered too close to the surface.

"This is hardly the time to concern yourself with me."

Cesare's laughter was soft, almost musical—but edged with something dangerous.

"To slay the dragon herself... the Princess of Valdina is truly fearless."

The tail end of his amusement carried an unmistakable sharpness.

He ran a hand through his dark hair, the gesture betraying a rare flicker of agitation.

Now that they were alone, Cesare abandoned the veneer of casual indifference he had worn before her retainers.

"If the evil dragon ceases to exist, they cannot take you as its bride. The oracle becomes impossible to fulfill."

Unlike Medea's subordinates, who had struggled to contain their shock, Cesare showed no surprise whatsoever.

Even when she had been a powerless figurehead princess, no adversity had ever brought her to her knees. Why should a mere oracle prove any different?

But what he had not anticipated—what cut deeper than any blade—

From beginning to end, her plan had excluded him entirely.

She had not reached out to him. Not once.

It left Cesare feeling hollow in a way he could not name.

Unlike him—inexorably drawn to her, helpless against the pull—the careful distance Medea maintained between them had not diminished in the slightest.

What would have happened if he hadn't learned of the oracle and raced across the continent to Valdina?

"If I had arrived any later, you would already be gone, wouldn't you?"

He seized her jaw—firmly, but without pain—forcing her to meet his eyes.

"You were going to sail off with that collection of fools and hunt the evil dragon yourself."

His golden gaze burned into hers.

"Leaving me behind?"

Even the furrow of his brow was beautiful—like a masterwork marred by a single, deliberate flaw. But beneath that beauty, something unusually raw pulsed: impatience. Desperation.

"Why? Dea, why?"

His voice dropped, almost plaintive.

"I told you I would be your sword. You're far too brilliant not to have calculated my usefulness."

Medea pressed her lips together, silent.

Why hadn't she considered Cesare?

But...

What she intended to do—defying the oracle, challenging the gods themselves—was the ultimate taboo on this continent. She could not easily share such a plan with anyone.

And to consider the heir of an enemy nation as a companion in this endeavor...

Her entire life. Her fate.

"So use me. Your finest sword."

She had been destroyed once before by the warmth of false affection—sweet lies that had razed her country and slaughtered her family.

How could she allow herself to hope again? To trust the flattering words of another beautiful face?

Medea lifted her chin and held Cesare's gaze without flinching.

A hint of bitter mockery flickered across her delicate features—words she would never speak aloud, but which her eyes communicated with crystalline clarity.

The moment he saw that expression, Cesare understood.

"Ah. The brilliant Princess cannot bring herself to trust me."

His voice was soft. Almost wondering.

"My oath meant nothing to you. Not even the smallest part of it reached your heart."

The corners of his mouth curved upward. He was smiling—but the air around them turned to ice.

"You adorable coward."

The sneer twisted through his perfect features.

"You need to hold your opponent's leash to feel safe. The assurance that if they betray you, you can strangle them immediately."

His eyes glittered with something between amusement and anguish.

"But Medea... you don't realize. You already hold mine."

The Curse

Cesare's hand moved roughly to his chest.

"I have not been awaiting death because of some minor ailment."

Tuduk.

His linen shirt tore away, buttons scattering across the floor, revealing the sculpted planes of his chest—firm muscle beneath sun-bronzed skin.

"What are you—"

"It is a curse of the Primordial Darkness."

A firm hand cupped her chin, preventing her from turning away.

"Look. This is the vestige of my most wretched, most vulnerable hour."

Where his heart should have been, the skin had turned black as pitch. Faint tendrils of shadow pulsed beneath the surface like dark veins, spreading outward with each beat.

"I can still feel it—the agony of my heart hardening with every breath I take. There are nights I lie awake until dawn, unable to close my eyes. And I know... I know this wretched moment when I nearly surrendered to death will haunt me for whatever remains of my life."

His voice, threaded with ragged breaths, dropped to something low and raw.

"Dea. Now you know my weakness. You know how to destroy the black demon that all the continent fears."

He took her hand and wrapped it around his throat—as though fastening a collar around his own neck.

"So command me. Point me toward your enemies. Let me cut them down and make your wishes reality."

Medea stared at him.

Those green eyes—calculating, assessing—studied him as a hunter might evaluate whether a hound could truly be controlled.

Without warning, he pressed his lips to the back of her hand.

It was less the gesture of a loyal dog proving its harmlessness—and more the calculated submission of a wolf baring its throat while keeping its fangs hidden.

Yet even in supplication, his true nature bled through.

"Dea. Choose me. By your own hand."

Before I dismantle every other option, leaving you no choice but me.

The words that escaped his clenched jaw were imperious, insidious—and desperately earnest.

"I will never step beyond the boundaries you draw."

The Pact

Medea hesitated.

Then, slowly, she reached out.

The tips of her fingers brushed across his skin—barely a whisper of contact.

But it was enough.

Cesare's golden eyes blazed with triumph.

"The evil dragon will never have its bride."

He had never been anyone's faithful hound. But to be included in Medea's plans—even if he had forced his way in—filled him with a savage joy.

It was proof that he had finally crossed into her circle.

Later

Of all Medea's retainers, none welcomed Cesare's presence less than Tom.

"Your Highness, I cannot fathom how you could trust the Emperor of Katzen's own flesh and blood to accompany us! You never know when he might stab us in the back!"

The sheer pressure of the First Prince's aura sent involuntary shivers racing down Tom's spine. His hand twitched toward his sword before he could stop himself.

"I have never been an enemy of the Princess."

"A likely story—"

Mid-sentence, Tom froze.

Cesare had produced a silver mask and raised it to his face.

"That's... Acares?"

Beneath the half-mask, the sharp jawline and sculpted mouth were unmistakable.

There was no way Tom could fail to recognize that familiar silver disguise.

Three years ago, this man had stood in for Medea during a swordsmanship duel against Jared of Katzen.

He had emerged victorious—and secured for Valdina both glory and desperately needed aid.

He was the mercenary captain of the Facade, whose support had been instrumental in Valdina's rise to power.

"They were... the same person? All along?"

Tom's gaze darted between Medea and Cesare, incredulous.

"Your Highness—did you know?"

Kensington, too, had encountered both the mercenary Acares three years prior and the current First Prince Cesare. Now, at last, every scattered piece fell into place.

The spymaster's expression hardened with reluctant admiration.

For all these years, he had believed the true master of the Facade—the stateless arms network—to be some shadowy merchant lord. Never once had he suspected the First Prince of Katzen.

Even Perdiccas II might not grasp the true nature of his reclusive, supposedly sickly son.

How many secrets did this man conceal beneath that beautiful countenance?

The depth of deception was staggering.

"We must depart as swiftly as possible. We'll need a sturdy vessel capable of making the long voyage to the Forest of Eternal Darkness."

"The ship is ready. It awaits you at the harbor."

Cesare's lips curved into a knowing smirk as he vaulted effortlessly back through the window.

"We sail the moment you've finished your preparations."

He departed as lightly as he had arrived—but the weight of his presence lingered in the room long after he was gone.

With Cesare gone, Neril and Saya threw themselves into preparations for the voyage to the Forest of Eternal Darkness.

"Your Highness. Is something else troubling you?"

Kensington studied Medea, who had fallen into brooding silence.

Tak. Tak.

The only sound in the room was the rhythmic tap of her index finger against the table.

"There is one matter that continues to gnaw at me."

Beyond the complication of the First Prince joining her expedition—a beast who had fastened his own leash and thrust it into her hands—something more fundamental disturbed her.

A shadow fell between Medea's delicate brows, as though she wandered through an endless tunnel with no light at its end.

"Why now?"

She had always known an oracle would come eventually. But the timing—and the fact that it targeted her specifically—felt profoundly wrong.

'The future I knew is changing.'

"It's happening too fast."

"Kensington. What did your investigation uncover about that wave during Jared's incident?"

Medea's thoughts turned to the monster wave that had erupted in the strait's waters during Jared's attempted abduction.

Neither the location nor the species of demonic beasts that had emerged matched the known patterns of El Amuz. It had been artificial—deliberately triggered.

"This is the first recorded instance of a terrestrial wave manifesting in the Strait of El Amuz, Your Highness. Our scholars remain baffled."

Tak. Tak.

Her finger continued its restless percussion against the table.

Rachel's appearance... followed by a series of demonic waves... and now an oracle.

"...Something is wrong."

A cold unease coiled in Medea's chest.

"Kensington. What progress has been made on the investigation into Rachel?"
