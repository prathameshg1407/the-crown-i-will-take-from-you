Chapter XII

The Prodigal
Daughter

"Better a fool you can see through than a serpent wearing your son's face."

"Your Majesty, how can this be just?"

Even Count Montfort—who had spent decades bending to the Emperor's every whim—could not endure this final indignity in silence.

"Does Your Majesty not know better than anyone how devoted Catherine has been? How faithfully she has served the crown?"

But it was the Western Empress's son who possessed the greater cunning.

"Father, I submit entirely to your wisdom."

The Third Prince prostrated himself before the Emperor's throne.

He had watched his mother being dragged from the tower mere hours ago. His face was haggard, drained of color—yet not a flicker of resentment crossed his features.

Father suspects me. I cannot afford to be discarded—not now.

The thought of Mother locked in that cold, filthy cell tears at my heart.

But first—I must dispel his suspicions.

"When I ascend to the throne, I shall ensure my mother is compensated for every moment of suffering she has endured."

And he would have his vengeance upon those who had orchestrated her downfall.

I will grind them all to dust. Cesare. The Princess of Valdina. Every last one.

"I beg Your Majesty's forgiveness for any distress I may have caused. My devotion to you remains absolute."

"You truly knew nothing of your mother's... activities?"

"I swear it, Father. There was much she kept from me..."

Do not show the same panic you displayed at the banquet. Stay calm. Stay composed.

Juan needed to establish his innocence and begin planning his next move.

But therein lay the problem: his very composure struck Perdiccas II as deeply suspicious.

"Leave."

"Father, if I have given offense—"

"Do not make me repeat myself."

The Third Prince was escorted out—more pushed than guided—by the imperial guards.

Perdiccas II pressed his fingers to his throbbing temples.

"Every one of my children conceals darkness within."

His mother lay imprisoned, humiliated beyond measure—and Juan's response was to perform this elaborate pantomime of filial devotion?

It only reinforced the Emperor's suspicion that every honeyed word the Third Prince had ever spoken was calculated manipulation.

"Cesare is too dangerous. Juan I can no longer trust..."

Perdiccas II felt the walls closing in around him.

His sons were wrapped in so many layers of deception that he could not glimpse their true hearts—if they possessed hearts at all.

His antipathy toward Juan burned particularly hot.

"A son who speaks only what his father wishes to hear—how am I to know when he plants a dagger in my back?"

And if Juan was not truly his son—

I will not place a cuckoo upon my throne.

"Better the child who speaks uncomfortable truths than one who hides behind pretty lies. Better someone transparent—like Angelique."

The thought struck him like lightning.

His forgotten daughter. The impudent, arrogant creature who had become such a nuisance that he had banished her from his sight.

"Fine! Then I shall remove myself from Your Majesty's precious sight! I'm leaving the capital!"

"Do you wish to see me bite off my own tongue and die? I've had enough of those paper-pushing beggars!"

Angelique had never bothered to conceal anything. So foolish, so transparent—one could read her every thought as easily as a child's primer.

She had never feared her father. She had spoken her mind, thrown tantrums, made herself insufferable.

That very foolishness is what made her fall so easily into Valdina's trap.

Yet the character flaws that had led to Angelique's exile now presented themselves in a different light entirely.

For an Emperor drowning in suspicion of his remaining children, predictability had become the highest virtue.

Perdiccas II found himself longing for his foolish, transparent daughter—the one he could read at a glance, the one who could never escape the palm of his hand.

The old anger flickered... and dimmed.

"Chamberlain."

The Emperor turned.

"Summon Angelique back to the capital."

"Have you heard? The Fourth Princess has returned from exile!"

"Of course! The entire capital is buzzing. They say His Majesty has ordered the construction of a new palace for her."

A noblewoman interjected with a knowing smirk.

"Hmph! The capital isn't excited merely because of the Fourth Princess. It's the Western Empress's imprisonment that has everyone talking."

"We all assumed the Emperor's anger would cool—that she would somehow recover her position. But given how matters have progressed..."

"It seems increasingly unlikely."

By remarkable coincidence, the Fourth Princess's return and the Western Empress's imprisonment had occurred almost simultaneously.

Throughout the capital, the nobility could speak of little else. The positions of the two Empresses had reversed overnight—heaven and earth exchanging places.

"The Eastern Empress has begun receiving vassals again. Perhaps the scattered supporters of the Fourth Princess will reunite."

"And Count Montfort—or should I say Viscount Montfort now—will he simply accept this? His faction is already clashing openly with the Third Prince's allies."

The Third Prince's faction, reeling from their unexpected catastrophe, and the Fourth Princess's faction, newly emboldened, collided with increasing ferocity.

Katzen's capital had not witnessed such political turbulence in decades.

Yet amid the chaos, one place remained conspicuously tranquil: the First Prince's villa.

Saya approached her mistress with a hesitant question.

"Your Highness... is it true? The Western Empress and the Grand Duke—did they truly...?"

If so, it would explain why Medea had secretly arranged for the Grand Duke's presence during the Emperor's birthday celebration.

"If that's the case, then Katzen is no different from our own Valdina."

Saya recalled the precedent of Catherine Claudio—the woman who had raised her illegitimate daughter Birna as the legitimate heir of Duke Claudio.

She had witnessed firsthand the ugliness that festered behind the gilded facades of nobility.

It seemed natural to suspect the same pattern here.

"No. The Western Empress is nothing like my aunt."

Medea shook her head slowly.

"Catherine possesses too much pride to commit such an indiscretion. Her sense of self-worth is absolute—she would never permit herself to do anything she considered shameful."

Juan was, without question, the biological son of Perdiccas II.

Which meant the Western Empress's fury would burn all the hotter.

She had been dragged down from the pinnacle of power she had enjoyed for decades—destroyed utterly by accusations of a sin she had never committed.

"By now, she will have pieced together what happened. She knows who orchestrated her downfall."

A pause.

"And I imagine she very much wants me dead."

"Your Highness?"

"Me. And her."

Medea's voice was soft, almost contemplative.

"Not all reunions are born of longing, Saya."

It was precisely as Medea had predicted.

Deep in the night, within the imperial prison reserved for members of the royal family, silent darkness pooled like ink.

The Western Empress—once the most powerful woman in the empire—was consumed not by sorrow, but by incandescent rage.

"...You should never have come!"

Despite the Emperor's strict orders forbidding all visitors, a figure had penetrated the prison's defenses.

That he had risked his very life to reach her spoke volumes about how much the prisoner in this iron cage still meant to him.

But when Alfred finally stood before the Western Empress, she greeted him not with joy, but with naked hatred.

"Did you return because ruining me once wasn't enough? Did you come to witness the abyss you've cast me into?"

The prison designated for imperial prisoners was not the wretched pit reserved for common criminals. It maintained a certain standard of comfort.

Perhaps that explained why the Western Empress's complexion, though pale, retained its aristocratic refinement.

But prison was still prison. And for a woman of her towering pride, the humiliation was devastating beyond measure.

"...Catherine. It has been twenty years."

His eyes held not anger but profound disappointment—and beneath that, regret.

"Twenty years since we last spoke. And this is all you have to say to me?"

A pause.

"All this, for the sake of a man who treats you so cruelly?"

"That is precisely why!"

The words tore from her throat—a suppressed scream finally given voice.

"Why did you come? You swore you would never return! So why appear now, just to push me over the edge?"

"Why am I here?"

Alfred's expression shifted to bewilderment.

"Catherine—you summoned me."

"I... summoned you?"

"You contacted me first. If you hadn't—"

He trailed off, the words dying on his lips.

The Western Empress drew a shuddering breath, as though she hadn't heard a word he'd said.

"...You laid your trap with exquisite precision, didn't you, Medea? How terribly cunning you've proven to be."

Traces of the Red Foxes had been discovered in her betrayed maid's quarters.

Circumstantial evidence had accumulated, piece by damning piece—each one calculated to inflame the Emperor's suspicions.

None of it could be easily explained away. Regaining Perdiccas II's trust would be nearly impossible.

The Western Empress gritted her teeth until her jaw ached.

Her chin trembled, veins standing out against pale skin.

And in the darkness of her cell, surrounded by cold stone walls, Catherine of House Montfort—once the most feared woman in the Katzen Empire—began to contemplate her revenge.
