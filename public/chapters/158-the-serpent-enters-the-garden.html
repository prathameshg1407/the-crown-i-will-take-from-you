The Serpent
Enters the Garden

"Even paradise is not safe when the serpent learns to smile."

Even Medea could not have foreseen this.

That Rachel would walk into her palace on her own two feet—uninvited, unannounced, and smiling.

Why now? Why here?

Questions surged, unanswerable. Reason and raw emotion collided within her, forming a maelstrom she could barely contain.

The enemy's throat—so sweetly exposed before her—was temptation incarnate. Good and evil intertwined in a single, exquisite moment.

I must not let emotion rule me.

She knew the tales of fools who had thrown away everything for a single, blinding instant of vengeance.

Knock. Knock.

Her index finger drummed against the desk—each tap a hammer blow against the wood.

The crystal vase upon the table trembled faintly.

Medea reached out. Her fingers closed around a perfect, long-stemmed rose.

Crack.

Petals shattered beneath her grip. Thorns pierced her palm, drawing beads of crimson.

She rose without a sound, ignoring the sting. The scent of crushed roses filled the air—cloying, almost nauseating.

✦ ✦ ✦

The Central Palace—heart of Valdina's power.

The Agema stationed outside the Emperor's private chambers greeted Rachel with warmth.

"His Majesty remains in his quarters. The aftereffects of the corruption still linger, it seems."

Their demeanor toward her was markedly friendly.

"The purification is not yet complete."

"I know I am only permitted an audience at His Majesty's request. Yet I have come unbidden..."

Rachel produced a small wooden box with a practiced air of humility.

"Please, accept this for the knights. Share it among yourselves."

Within lay several vials glowing with brilliant azure light.

Menil—deputy commander of the Agema—recognized them instantly.

"Holy water."

"Yes. I exhausted my previous supply. These are freshly made."

A faint blush colored her cheeks, as though embarrassed by her own generosity.

"It may not compare to the temple's, but it is better than nothing."

"There is no comparison. Holy water blessed by the Saintess herself? It is priceless."

Menil accepted the gift without hesitation, gratitude plain upon his face.

For his comrades still suffering the lingering taint of ghoul corruption.

"You must be weary yourself, Lady Rachel. Perhaps you should rest—"

"I am well enough. It is only natural that I offer what aid I can to those who protected me."

Rachel glanced around, ensuring privacy, then lowered her voice.

"Please, allow me to see His Majesty."

For a moment, a strange sigil—a goat's horn—flashed in Menil's eyes. It vanished as quickly as it appeared.

He nodded, expression oddly vacant.

"Of course, my lady. His Majesty is within."

Creak.

The door opened.

Rachel's smile widened as she stepped across the threshold.

✦ ✦ ✦

The Emperor's study lay buried beneath mountains of parchment.

Peleus had adapted swiftly to palace life, but the relentless tide of state affairs kept him chained to his desk.

"Peleus."

Even when Medea entered, her brother remained half-obscured by stacks of documents.

"Ah, you're here."

"How is your health?"

His handsome features were unchanged—save for a lingering pallor.

"Fine."

"If you were truly fine, you would not still be confined here."

Peleus offered a gentle smile, reaching to brush a strand of hair from her face.

"This brother will always stand at your side. Sometimes you worry as though I might vanish from the world."

Medea's smile was bittersweet. Peleus could be startlingly perceptive at the oddest moments.

"Oh—Her Highness the Princess is here as well."

A strand of golden hair caught the light from behind a pillar.

Medea froze.

"Why are you here?"

Her gaze shifted to Menil, who had entered with Rachel.

The knight shrank beneath the weight of her stare.

"Ah—Lady Rachel came to complete His Majesty's purification—"

"Forgive me, Your Highness. I insisted. I feared His Majesty still suffered lingering pain from the corruption."

Rachel stepped forward, composure regained, her smile as serene as ever.

She extended a small wooden box.

"I prepared this holy water myself. Please accept it, Your Highness, and forgive my presumption."

The vials within glowed with soft blue light.

"It may be humble in strength, but its purity is unmatched. With the goddess's blessing upon Your Highness's grace, I am certain it would be of help."

Medea did not move to accept it.

Rachel continued to hold the box, smile unwavering.

The silence grew heavy.

"Have I offended once more? If so, I beg your forgiveness for my ignorance."

Tears welled in her violet eyes—perfect, practiced sorrow.

"Your Highness, I can personally attest to Lady Rachel's power. Her holy water surpasses even the temple's—"

"So you allowed her entry without announcement or verification?"

Medea's voice cut like a blade.

"Since when has the Agema—the Emperor's own guard—grown so lax?"

Menil paled.

He could not recall why he had admitted her. The memory was... hazy.

"I... I accept full responsibility. Punish me as you see fit."

"Menil is relieved of duty. Henceforth, D'Angel will oversee security of the central palace."

Peleus rose, his voice calm but unyielding.

He turned to Rachel.

"Rachel. Though you have not yet received formal baptism, you are a representative of the Holy Kingdom. Ensure this never happens again."

"Your Majesty, at least allow me to complete the purification—"

"D'Angel. Escort her out."

"Your Majesty!"

Rachel reached out in desperation—but D'Angel was already moving, taking her firmly by the arm.

A short, startled cry escaped her as she was led away.
