The Interrogation Room

"Your Highness!"

Umberto cried, dropping to his knees.

"Please—save my master! The Knights have taken him!"

He pressed his forehead to the floor, shoulders shaking with carefully practiced despair.

Medea watched him in silence.

"Your master?"

she asked at last, tilting her head.

"How strange."

She tapped her fan lightly against the table.

"Has something happened to the Count of Kensington already?"

Umberto froze.

The word hit him like a blade to the throat.

Kensington.

The Spymaster of the Kazen Empire.

The man who commanded the Red Foxes.

"I… I don't know what you mean,"

Umberto said, too quickly.

"My master is Minister Etienne."

"Oh?"

Medea smiled gently.

"Then you should be relieved. Kensington is far more difficult to replace."

The room felt suddenly airless.

"Don't be frightened,"

Medea continued, her tone almost kind.

"You followed my instructions perfectly. The banquet was… inspired."

She slid a narrow strip of paper across the table.

Umberto's eyes dropped to it.

The ink.

The paper.

The precise, emotionless handwriting.

Identical to the messages he had been receiving for weeks.

Release the Toad into the Market.

The blood drained from his face.

The candles.

The drug.

The Minister's breakdown.

The schism between Etienne and the Regent.

It wasn't Kensington.

It was her.

"You…"

Umberto whispered.

"You targeted the Minister?"

Cold steel touched his neck.

"Careful,"

Neril said, blade steady.

"This is absurd!"

Umberto shouted, scrambling for bravado.

"I am a loyal servant of Valdina!"

Medea sighed.

"Umberto. We are far past lies."

She leaned forward, emerald eyes sharp.

"The question isn't how I know who you are."

Her fan snapped shut.

"The question is whether you want to live."

Silence fell.

Umberto straightened slowly.

"I will not beg,"

he said.

"If I die here, I die for my country."

His hand twitched—just once.

A second was all he needed to reach the dagger in his boot.

"You seem confident,"

he said softly.

"Are you prepared for the consequences if I silence you?"

Medea didn't blink.

"If you kill me,"

she replied calmly,

"you don't silence anything."

She placed a sealed letter on the table.

"You start a war."

Her gaze bored into him.

"Killing the Princess of Valdina is not intelligence gathering."

"It is a declaration."

Umberto's breath caught.

She was right.

The Empire wasn't ready.

Kensington wasn't ready.

He slowly relaxed his hand.

"…What do you want?"

"Deliver this,"

Medea said.

"To your master. The Count of Kensington."

"You want me as a messenger?"

"I am letting you walk out alive,"

Medea said softly.

"Remember that generosity."

Umberto took the letter with trembling fingers.

He bowed—low, deep, sincere.

She is not a puppet,

he thought in horror.

She is the hand on the strings.

He backed out of the room without turning his back.

The Royal Prison

Clang.

Clang.

Prime Minister Siseo's footsteps echoed through the damp corridor.

He stopped before a heavy iron cell.

"Minister Etienne,"

he said pleasantly.

"You look… refreshed."

Etienne lunged to the bars.

"Siseo! You snake!"

Thud!

"As soon as I get out of here, I'll tear you apart!"

"Is that so?"

Siseo asked mildly.

"Odd. I haven't accused you of anything untrue."

"Duke Claudio will save me!"

Etienne roared.

"He needs me!"

Siseo smiled.

"Does he?"

Etienne hesitated.

"The Duke is… occupied,"

Siseo continued.

"Many fires to put out. Do you truly believe he has time for a condemned man?"

"I have leverage!"

"Leverage works best while you're alive,"

Siseo said gently.

"Once you're executed for treason, it becomes very quiet."

The color drained from Etienne's face.

"He won't let me die,"

he whispered.

"He knows what I know."

"Perhaps,"

Siseo shrugged.

"Or perhaps,"

"he's counting on your death to bury the past."

He began to walk off.

"Wait!"

Etienne screamed.

"Come back!"

Siseo didn't turn.

The iron corridor swallowed his footsteps.

And in the silence, doubt took root—

slow,

cold,

and impossible to uproot.

The Trap Springs

The Spider's Web

A spy returns home with trembling hands.
A minister sits alone in the dark.
And a princess smiles, counting pieces on the board.
