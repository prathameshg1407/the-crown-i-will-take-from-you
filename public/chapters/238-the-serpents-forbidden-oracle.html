Chapter 238

The Serpent's
Forbidden Gambit

"Even darkness fears those who would remake the laws of heaven."

The Lowest Level of the Golden Tower

Damp cold clung to the chamber like a living thing, seeping into stone and bone alike. Around the ancient round table, darkness pooled so thick it seemed to swallow even the memory of light.

"You spoke with such confidence. Yet your bait perished before you could even implicate the Princess."

The black magician in the northern seat spoke with studied indifference, but beneath that measured tone lurked something sharp as a freshly honed blade.

At the memorial service, the unexpected had occurred: Marieu had slain Samon before Rachel could spring her trap.

Once again, the scheme to destroy Medea had crumbled to ash.

"And how, precisely, is it my fault that the fool got himself killed? The woman harbored a grudge—she acted on it. That was beyond my control."

"In the end, you returned to the Holy Land empty-handed."

The northern magician's contempt dripped like venom.

"You commandeered the Tower's finest assets, and yet you never even drew your sword. Tell me—is it because you were raised as a counterfeit that your mind cannot keep pace with the genuine article?"

The moment Medea's name surfaced, Rachel's composure—which had been holding steady behind a wall of sardonic amusement—shattered completely.

"You speak of counterfeits? How rich, coming from those who cling so desperately to their fake Saintess."

Her beautiful voice made no attempt to mask its bitterness.

"Tell me: who among you could possibly replace me? Without my presence, do you truly believe you can resurrect the Primordial Darkness?"

The Goddess herself had extinguished the Primordial Darkness in ages past.

Therefore, to resurrect that ancient shadow, one must first disrupt the divine order—shatter the delicate balance upon which this world rested.

The greatest obstacle to such an endeavor was the Saintess: the Goddess's chosen representative in the mortal realm. So long as she drew breath, divine power would remain intact.

Which meant...

"If the true agent's fate is corrupted, the connection to the Goddess can be severed."

Rachel's eyes glittered with dangerous certainty.

"So tell me again—between the two of us, who is truly expendable here?"

CRACK—!

She spat directly into the face of the northern magician.

"If I fail to steal Medea's destiny, everything you have labored to prepare will amount to nothing. Nothing."

"Insolent wretch! You dare forget the favors I have bestowed upon you?!"

The insulted warlock surged to his feet, dark energy crackling around his form—

BANG—!

"Enough."

The warlock seated at the table's center struck the ancient stone with his palm. Raw magical power reverberated through the chamber, making the air itself hum with barely contained force.

"This is not the time for petty squabbling. The First Prince of Katzen has begun pursuing us in earnest. My disciple fled—and died in the attempt. It is possible he has already discovered the existence of the Golden Tower itself."

"Impossible! Even the Master of the Magic Tower has not succeeded in uncovering us!"

A tremor passed through the assembly. Shock rippled across the faces of those seated upon the golden thrones.

Cesare Dweisler Katzen.

The first mortal to survive the touch of Primordial Darkness was now hunting that very darkness—seeking to drag them into the light.

"This is precisely why I insisted we eliminate him. He represents the single greatest threat to our designs."

The black magician in the northeastern seat spoke. His specialty was the darkest of curses—ancient, insidious, inescapable.

The primordial curse that had once consumed Prince Cesare was his creation.

The twelve warlocks who occupied these golden thrones each commanded a different aspect of darkness: Erosion. Senility. Corruption. Decay. Together, they could manipulate shadow in all its myriad forms.

"I know not how he clawed his way back from the brink of death. But it is too early for despair."

The central warlock's voice cut through the mounting tension.

"Medea Valdina has not yet awakened her power of foresight—the true gift of the Saintess. Our window of opportunity remains open."

He turned his hooded gaze toward Rachel.

"Though I should add... your time grows increasingly short."

The voice was deep, almost gentle—yet more menacing than any overt threat. It sent ice crawling down her spine.

"Rachel. This is your final opportunity."

"Understood. But this time, I require your full and unconditional support."

Rachel's eyes took on a calculating gleam.

"A simple trial will not suffice. I need something more potent—something that will bind her so completely she cannot move, cannot think, cannot breathe without my permission."

She recalled Medea's emerald eyes, regarding her with that infuriating composure. Those crystalline depths that seemed to pierce through every deception, every carefully constructed lie...

Rachel's finger traced the worn grooves of the ancient magic circle carved into the table's surface.

"I require an oracle."

"...How dare you! Do you comprehend what manner of magic you speak of so carelessly?!"

The northern magician—the very one who had traded insults with Rachel moments before—shot to his feet in outrage.

Oracle.

The Goddess's own will, conveyed directly to the mortal realm.
A divine providence existing beyond any boundary
that black magic could ever hope to breach.

Even the Primordial Darkness itself could not touch an oracle. The very notion of a mortal attempting to manipulate divine prophecy was beyond heresy—it was cosmic blasphemy.

In all the centuries of its existence, the Golden Tower had never dared approach such forbidden territory.

"It cannot be done. To violate the Golden Rule—the fundamental law governing this world—would invite catastrophic karmic retribution."

"We have no room left for retreat."

Rachel's voice turned sharp as broken glass.

"You said it yourself: if Medea awakens her true power, we are finished. Is there any more certain method to prevent that than this?"

"You who have merely dipped your toes in our waters—you cannot fathom how merciless the laws of this world truly are. If the Golden Rule discovers that our darkness has trespassed into the domain of the divine..."

Despite Rachel's impassioned arguments, the Tower remained intractable.

"Proceed with the trial as originally planned. Is that understood?"

Silence.

The central warlock, sensing Rachel's unspoken defiance, extended his hand. Cold fingers wrapped around her throat with serpentine precision.

"Rachel. Answer me."

"Ahh—haak—yes—stop—please—!"

Only when she nodded in submission did the crushing pressure relent.

"I said I understand! Just... keep your subordinates from spitting in my face."

Hidden Resolve

But the warlocks failed to perceive what festered beneath her compliance.

Just as they had secretly cultivated darkness behind the Goddess's back, so too does evil—once rooted—refuse to die.

Incompetent cowards. Very well. I shall find my own path around the Golden Rule.

The false Saintess had resolved to shatter the ultimate taboo.

As Rachel departed the Golden Tower, her eyes blazed with fierce, terrible anticipation.

Deep Night — Sacramontis, the Holy Land

The Central Temple lay submerged in a silence so profound that not even moonlight dared intrude.

Colossal pillars of pristine white marble rose like petrified giants, supporting the vaulted heavens above. The air hung motionless, carrying a chill that seemed to caress the skin of any soul foolish enough to wander these corridors after dark.

"Luca! Did I not instruct you to place the polished candles upon the altar?"

Even when the rest of the world slumbered, the nights proved grueling for newly ordained priests.

"You miserable little rat—you stumble into the Central Temple through sheer luck, and suddenly you fancy yourself important? Don't make me laugh!"

Luca was so small, so stunted in his growth, that the priestly robes he'd been issued dragged across the floor with every step.

But the senior priest showed not a shred of sympathy.

"Clean every candle behind the altar and transfer them to the storage chamber. You will not sleep until the task is complete!"

"Y-yes... yes, sir..."

Luca clutched his throbbing forehead—fresh bruises blooming beneath honey-colored curls—and nodded meekly.

Tears prickled at the corners of his eyes, but the poorhouse had taught him long ago that weeping only invited harsher treatment.

"I'm not afraid. I'm not afraid..."

Cradling candles in both trembling hands, Luca made his way toward the storeroom. The corridor stretched before him, swallowed by impenetrable shadow.

To keep his fear at bay, he sang softly under his breath:

"I will grow strong and vanquish the demons...
Like the Saintess...
I will become the light..."

And then—

CLANG—!

A sharp, splintering crash echoed from the empty prayer chamber.

Luca nearly dropped every precious candle in his arms.

"Wh-what?! Who's there—!"

He stumbled forward in the darkness—and collided with a figure emerging from the shadows. The candle holder slipped from his grasp, clattering against stone.

"S-Saintess! It's you, isn't it? Are you hurt?"

Even in the guttering candlelight, her golden hair seemed to glow. That gentle, lovely face—the one Luca had dreamed of since the day he first arrived.

"As expected of one so devout—praying at such a late hour!"

Luca's round eyes shone with pure, uncomplicated awe. He paid no heed to the shattered fragments of a goddess statue scattered across the floor behind her.

"I am truly honored to meet you like this!"

In his overwhelming joy, the child failed to notice the flicker of irritation that crossed the Saintess's beautiful features.

Nor did he realize that she had emerged not from the prayer chamber—but from the restricted study beyond.

"Back during the welcoming procession—I threw a lily toward your carriage! Oh, no, wait—that's not right. A few days ago, during the return ceremony, I polished the seat where the Saintess would sit. You... you probably don't remember..."

"Ah. Yes. Well done."

Rachel offered a thin smile, her mind already elsewhere.

If the sacrifice involves holy power, it should escape the Golden Rule's detection.

She had been reviewing ancient texts in the restricted library—records confirming that oracles of old had incorporated elements of divine energy.

If that was true, then a counterfeit oracle fueled by sacrificed holy power might be accepted as genuine—as though it had truly originated from the Goddess herself.

The problem is finding a suitable sacrifice.

Currently, aside from the Pope, only the High Priests and the Knights Templar could wield holy power.

But none of them could be used as a sacrifice without inviting disaster.

The High Priests are too prominent—their disappearance would be noticed immediately. And the Knights Templar... far too dangerous. Commander Leonor would grow suspicious the moment one of her paladins vanished.

What am I to do?

She had found the path forward, but the door remained locked—and she possessed no key.

Crack... crack...

Rachel gnawed at her fingernails, glaring at the statue that stood in the corner.

That serene marble face seemed to mock her with its perfect tranquility.

"As though you have any right to judge... with a soul already rotted through."

She raised her hand. Dark energy coiled around her fingers, manifesting as a sigil shaped like twisted goat horns.

CRACK—!

The statue of the Goddess exploded into a thousand pieces.

That was the sound that had drawn Luca—the child now standing before her with tears glistening on his cheeks and wonder written across his face.

"I... I also wish to defeat demons and save the poor, just like the Saintess does."

He wiped at his eyes, voice trembling with earnest passion.

"My divine power is still weak—barely awakened—but..."

Rachel, who had been turning to leave, went perfectly still.

"I dream of serving you, Saintess. But I'm from such humble origins... I know I'm not worthy. Still, I swear I would work harder than anyone! I would give everything—"

"Have you awakened your power?"

Rachel's eyes gleamed—the hungry, patient gaze of a predator who has finally spotted prey.
