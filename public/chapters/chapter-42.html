The Princess's
Palace

"Pity is a shield—and a useful one."

"Oh my, how cute!"

The maids crowded around Saya, pinching her cheeks and stuffing sweets into her hands.

"Have you ever eaten macaroons? They're heavenly!"

Saya chewed happily, eyes shining.

"They taste like clouds!"

Laughter filled the room.

The fierce territorial instincts of the palace staff dissolved almost instantly. Saya was small, quick, and endlessly helpful. She volunteered for the dirtiest work without complaint and disarmed suspicion with her tragic honesty.

From the doorway, Neril watched quietly.

"Your Highness," she murmured. "Is it wise to let her spread that story? Pity can be… dangerous."

"Pity is a shield," Medea replied without looking away. "And a useful one. She understands that."

Later, Saya scampered up to Medea, practically bouncing on her heels.

"Sister Medea! You promised to attend the Marquis's tea party with me!"

Medea blinked.

Sister?

Then she noticed Birna approaching down the corridor.

Saya gasped theatrically, clapping a hand over her mouth.

"Oh no—how careless of me! I forgot my place."

She turned to Birna, eyes wide with innocent confusion.

"I'm so sorry, Lady Claudio. I heard you call Her Highness 'sister,' so I thought it was permitted. Where did I ever get such an idea?"

Birna's face flushed crimson.

It wasn't loud.

It wasn't rude.

It was devastating.

A street girl—correcting a Duke's daughter on etiquette.

Neril barely suppressed a laugh.

"She's vicious," the knight admitted under her breath.

"She remembers the rain," Medea said softly. "She remembers that I stood in front of her. That kind of loyalty can't be bought."

Saya's integration was complete.

Even the Queen Mother praised Medea for saving "that poor child." The rumor spread quickly: Princess Medea was benevolent, protective, generous.

Theo's brilliance lives on in his sister, Medea thought.

The Study

"Your Highness," Neril said later. "About the old woman in Asilum Street."

Medea looked up. "Did Tom find her?"

"No. But he found something else."

Neril placed a report on the desk.

"Mercenaries from Fasade were sighted in the slums that day."

Medea frowned. "Fasade? The continental arms dealers?"

"Yes. Tom tried to follow them, but they were professionals. A high-ranking officer was with them. They blocked the street, searched briefly, then vanished."

Medea tapped her finger once against the desk.

Why would Fasade be interested in a slum prophet?

In her past life, they had stayed hidden until much later.

This was new.

"Did Tom find the old woman?" Medea asked again.

"No."

Neril hesitated.

"Your Highness… do you believe her prophecy?"

"That my future is chaos?" Medea smiled faintly.

"No—about the Dawn's Droplet. About you bearing the blood of the Sage. The will of the Goddess."

Medea went still.

Blood of the Sage.

Valdina—the land protected by the Philosopher's Stone. Legend claimed its creator was the Royal Family's ancestor.

"…Wait here," Medea said, standing abruptly. "I need to confirm something."

The Royal Altar

Cold. Silent.

The Philosopher's Stone floated above the altar, pulsing with gentle blue light.

Medea stepped closer.

"The blood of the Sage…" she whispered.

She reached out.

The stone hummed beneath her fingers.

"Ancestor of Valdina," she murmured. "If you can hear me… tell me what I must do."

Sting.

Pain shot through her finger.

A jagged edge—unpolished, razor-sharp—caught her skin.

A single drop of blood fell.

Crack.

White light exploded through the chamber.

Medea shielded her eyes, heart pounding.

When the light faded, the Philosopher's Stone had split open like a geode.

Inside lay a crystal cylinder no larger than a finger.

She lifted it.

The Stone sealed itself instantly—whole, pristine, pulsing as before.

Only the cut on her finger proved it was real.

The cylinder was engraved with a tiny goddess. Droplet patterns spiraled down its surface. Inside, silvery liquid flowed against gravity.

"…Dawn's Droplet," Medea breathed.

The Shaman had spoken truth.

District 1 — The Opera House

"Did you enjoy the performance, Your Highness?"

Catherine smiled brightly, her gown blooming like a rose in spring. Birna stood beside her, bored and restless.

Another outing. Another attempt to display unity.

"It must be suffocating to remain in the palace," Catherine cooed. "See how beautiful the world is?"

Beautiful, Medea thought. And gilded. Like a cage.

"I remembered you liked this opera," Catherine continued. "So I hired an orchestra from the Empire. Just for you."

She squeezed Medea's hand.

Look how much I spend on you.

Medea withdrew her hand gently.

"It was unpleasant," she said calmly. "The soprano screeched. The tempo was rushed."

Catherine's smile cracked.

Do you know how much that cost?

She swallowed her fury, forcing a tender expression.

"Oh dear. I failed you, my precious girl."

She patted Medea's arm.

"But don't worry."

Medea looked up.

"Tonight," Catherine said sweetly, eyes glittering, "you won't be disappointed."

"Tonight?"

"The banquet!" Catherine beamed. "To celebrate your recovery."

Her smile sharpened.

"The grandest feast
Valdina has ever seen."
