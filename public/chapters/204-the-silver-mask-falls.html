Chapter 204

The Silver Mask
Falls

"When masks crumble and identities are revealed—
vengeance finds its sharpest edge."

Yes... you..."

Jared's voice died in his throat as he stared at the silver mask.

Why in the hells is the Facade here?!

Intelligence had confirmed it—the mercenary leader had not accompanied the Princess on this journey to the Strait.

Yet here he stood.

Before Jared could process the impossibility of it—

Grab!

The man seized him again with terrifying ease and hurled him across the chapel.

"Ugh—!"

CRASH!

Jared's body slammed into the stone wall with bone-shattering force. Cracks spider-webbed across the ancient masonry.

The man turned.

Medea watched him approach.

The chapel—gray, desolate, suffocating in its oppressive gloom—seemed to fade into nothingness around him.

He was the only thing with color in that dreary space.

Behind him, she could almost see flames—golden and fierce, blazing against the shadows.

She stared, transfixed, as he knelt before her and lowered his head to examine her condition.

"Medea."

His voice was low, taut with barely controlled rage.

His chest rose and fell rapidly. The air around him shimmered with killing intent so palpable it was almost visible—cold, blue, lethal.

Somewhere in the distance, she thought she could hear the grinding of teeth.

He took her hands in his—large, warm, impossibly gentle despite the violence thrumming beneath his skin.

When he looked down at her palms—cut and bleeding from where she'd gripped the shattered statue—his eyes became molten gold.

"You bastard—!"

Jared's sword came slashing down at the man's unprotected back.

The masked figure didn't even turn.

His own blade flashed from its sheath in a blur of silver.

Clang!

Steel met steel. Jared's strike was deflected with contemptuous ease—sent careening harmlessly to the side.

In the same fluid motion, Cesare moved between Jared and Medea, cutting off any approach.

"I'll kill you! I'll tear you apart, Acares! I'm not the same man you knew before!"

Jared charged forward, screaming.

Blood streamed from his shattered skull—where it had struck the wall—running into one eye and painting half his face crimson. He looked feral. Desperate.

And his boast was not entirely empty.

His sword cleaved through everything it touched—stone, wood, air itself seemed to split before the troll-enhanced strength coursing through his grafted hand.

But raw power meant nothing against precision honed over decades.

And it meant even less against Cesare's wrath.

CLANG!

Jared's sword flew from his grip.

He screamed as his wrist—the one with the troll's finger still attached—spun through the air, severed cleanly at the joint.

"AHHHHHHH! Why?! Why, why, WHY?!"

The warlocks had promised him omnipotence. They had sworn their dark magic would make him invincible.

So why can't I surpass him?!

The mercenary who had once seemed like an insurmountable wall now appeared as something far more terrifying—a black tidal wave, vast and merciless, swallowing everything in its path.

Cesare's blade did not stop.

"Ugh—!"

In a flash of silver light, both of Jared's legs gave out beneath him. His tendons severed. He collapsed to his knees.

Left shoulder—pierced by Medea's dagger. Right wrist—severed by the mercenary. Legs—cut down like wheat before the scythe.

Next comes the neck.

But instead of delivering the final blow, Cesare grabbed Jared by the scruff of his neck.

He dragged him across the chapel floor like a beaten dog—leaving a long, dark trail of blood in his wake.

"Why... why...?"

You're not going to kill me?

For a brief, foolish moment, hope flickered in Jared's terror-filled eyes.

Then the mercenary threw him down at Medea's feet.

And Jared understood.

"Dea."

The single word—her name, spoken with restrained tenderness—was all he said.

That was enough.

The atmosphere shifted.

Jared realized with dawning horror what this was: a gift. The mercenary was presenting him to the Princess—her personal opportunity for revenge.

"No—nooooo!"

"Quiet."

A rough hand—almost tender in its mockery—stuffed a cloth into Jared's mouth, silencing his screams.

Jared should have known.

Just as he had sought to crush Medea by borrowing the warlocks' power, the reverse was equally possible.

The roles of predator and prey could reverse in an instant.

The cat could become the mouse.

The Princess seemed to understand this too.

The mercenary's large, calloused hand covered Medea's smaller one—still clenched around the hilt of the mithril dagger.

The same blade that had failed to reach Jared's heart only moments before.

"Mmmph—!"

"It's all right. Do whatever you wish."

It was unclear who the words were meant for—Medea, or himself.

Medea stepped forward and stood before Jared.

Even though she had lived an entire lifetime again, some things never changed.

Jason, who could not abandon his lust for the throne.

And Jared, who could not let go of his hatred for her.

"You... you won't stop, will you?"

"Mmmmph!"

"Jared."

He struggled—thrashing weakly—but pinned between Medea before him and the mercenary behind, he could not move.

Her green eyes darkened.

"Neither will I."

A single, deliberate stroke.

The blade slid into flesh—slow, methodical. The sound was soft. Almost gentle.

Squelch.

Crimson droplets splattered across the white fabric of her dress.

It was impossible to read the emotion in her deepening emerald gaze.

Just moments ago, Jared wanted to see these eyes broken. Bent. Filled with despair.

Now those same unyielding eyes stare down at him—and there is nothing in them but cold, methodical purpose.

"Princess, if you enjoyed flirting with the Archduke, what about giving this humble servant a chance? Let's have our little talks without your husband knowing, hmm?"

"What would happen if I left? Who would slay your dragons for you then?"

"Mmmmph!"

"Actually... I wanted to do this back then, too."

Medea's quiet words echoed over Jared's muffled groans.

"Mother! Mooooom!"

"Lian—!"

To the man who had threatened her. Who had tormented her child.

She didn't want him to die quickly—painlessly, by poison or a clean stroke to the heart.

No.

She wanted to tear at his flesh with her own hands. To pierce his heart slowly, deliberately, and watch the light fade from his eyes.

"Ugh..."

The sight of Jared's red eyes—growing wider, filling with the helpless terror of the weak—was unfamiliar.

Yet strangely... welcome.

Yes. Perhaps this is what I've wanted to see all along.

The moment when those arrogant crimson eyes are flooded with the same despair he once tried to inflict on her.

Just as she had felt. Just as Medea had suffered.

The strange, almost vulgar joy rising in her chest could only be explained that way.

"I will never be weak again."

She whispered the words like a prayer.

"You will never... take anything from me again."

The scent of blood seeped through the cracks in the chapel walls.

The green of her eyes had darkened until they appeared almost black.

"That's enough."

A firm hand—gentle despite its strength—blocked her vision.

"You don't need to shoulder the final blow."

Medea's racing thoughts, her feverish focus, suddenly stilled at that touch.

"I... I..."

Even as she stammered, a soft voice murmured near her ear.

"It's all right. Just close your eyes for a moment."

He took the dagger from her trembling hand.

Blood dripped from the blade, spattering across her dress.

"Wait."

As he rose to finish what she'd begun, Medea's hand shot out and grabbed his sleeve.

"...No."

"Dea—"

"The Emperor has not yet abdicated. Do you plan to create a political weakness by killing one of his generals? They'll use it to bind you."

The mercenary froze at her words.

Surprise flickered across his features—then transformed into something else.

He laughed softly.

Identity Revealed

"When did you realize?"

That the mercenary of the Facade... is Cesare, First Prince of Katzen?

"...When you saved me from the explosion."

Every time she'd looked into those clear golden eyes, she'd felt a nagging sense of familiarity—something inexplicable, just out of reach.

But in that moment of rescue, certainty had crystallized.

The power struggle between Emperor Perdiccas I and the First Prince was already at a boiling point.

If Cesare were to kill one of his father's generals—no matter how justified—it would become a chain to bind him, a weapon for his enemies to wield.

Medea knew this.

And she could not allow Acares—no, Cesare—to be shackled for her sake.

"Medea... you always exceed my expectations."

The sound of the silver mask hitting the stone floor echoed through the chapel.

Clatter.

"Then you'll remember what I told you."

His exposed face—no longer hidden behind silver—revealed golden eyes blazing with an emotion so intense it was almost overwhelming.

He reached out, thumb gently stroking her bloodstained cheek.

"I am yours. So take as much as you desire."

Silence.

"Your finest blade."

He leaned closer—slowly, deliberately—and pressed his lips to her forehead.

Warmth radiated from that single point of contact, spreading through her like wine.

Medea did not pull away.

She couldn't.

The warmth enveloped her completely—comforting yet suffocating, like being coiled in a serpent's embrace.

"Wait here. Yes?"

The question was asked while gently closing her eyes with his fingertips.

Could I refuse even if I wanted to?

Medea found herself nodding as if in a trance.

He held her trembling hands briefly—steadying them, grounding her—before slowly rising to his feet.

Behind him, Jared's broken form lay bleeding on the chapel floor.

But Cesare's golden eyes remained fixed on Medea alone—
as though nothing else in the world mattered.

End Chapter 4
