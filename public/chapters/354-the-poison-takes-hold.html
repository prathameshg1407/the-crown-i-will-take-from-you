Chapter 254

The Poison Takes Hold

"Not a single day passed when I didn't dream of ending your breath."

The head attendant gave Eratosthenes an uncertain look, clearly doubting whether the elderly Marquis could handle the Emperor's fury alone.

"Yes, but..."

Eratosthenes closed his eyes briefly, then opened them with deliberate calm.

"Wasn't the Marquis always better than anyone at managing His Majesty's tempers?"

It had been the same when Eratosthenes emerged from his long self-imposed exile, returning to court as a monk.

Overnight, he'd dissolved the Emperor's suspicions and secured the marquisate. The attendant's worry was groundless.

The head servant exchanged glances with the others.

With collective sighs of relief, they hurried from the Emperor's chambers, grateful to escape.

"Penteros! You heard him too! What kind of curse did Cesare pour on me?"

After dismissing the last of his attendants, Perdiccas II remained in a state of agitation.

"Since both the First Prince and First Princess oppose you so vehemently, perhaps it's better to abandon this forced oracle. I fear it will only breed further trouble."

"Stop? Stop? I cannot!"

At Eratosthenes' suggestion, Perdiccas II shook his head violently.

Thick veins bulged along the Emperor's neck.

"Those bastards look at me with such contempt, toying with me as if I were nothing! How can I simply let them do as they please? Absolutely not! Perdiccas did not survive this long by bowing to insolent children!"

His eyes blazed with mingled rage and wounded pride.

Perdiccas II reached for the glass of water beside him.

His throat burned from the exertion of shouting.

"Have tea instead. Cold water irritates Your Majesty's throat."

Eratosthenes approached slowly and tilted the golden pitcher.

Shimmering liquid filled the glass with a gentle sound.

Perdiccas II's hand paused momentarily as he reached for the teacup—a fleeting hesitation no observer would notice.

Glug, glug.

But when he watched Eratosthenes pour tea into his own glass and drink it calmly, Perdiccas II followed suit without further thought.

Indeed, as Eratosthenes had promised, the warm tea soothed his inflamed throat and settled his churning stomach.

His overheated mind gradually cooled, allowing rational thought to return.

"I'll make them kneel before me. Make them bow their heads in my presence. But how? How should I accomplish this... Hmm..."

His eyes brightened with malicious inspiration. He clapped his hands together.

"Yes—the child! They don't have a child yet. A child is most vulnerable when still in its mother's womb."

As his anger subsided, the Emperor's insidious cruelty raised its head.

"After he loses his child due to the chaos he's created, he'll understand his mistake painfully."

When you possess something precious, you cannot afford to be reckless.

That couple remained intoxicated with their own power, still unfamiliar with how the world truly worked.

Eratosthenes said nothing.

"I am still the supreme ruler of Katzen, yet they speak to me with such audacity. They must pay the price for everything. Don't you agree?"

"Will you truly do this?"

Eratosthenes asked in a parched voice.

Rather than curiosity, his tone suggested he was confirming the Emperor's resolve.

Perdiccas II responded with indignation, as though the answer were obvious.

"Ha! Not a single person who's shown me such foolishness has survived on this vast continent! They should be grateful I've allowed them to keep their lives! What else could they want?"

"Your Majesty... isn't the fruit being overshadowed by rotten roots?"

Eratosthenes' indifferent voice cut through the chamber.

"Why can't you praise him? Instead, you try to transform him into a beast like yourself."

The Emperor went still.

Rotten roots. Beast.

Perdiccas wondered if he'd misheard.

"What... what did you just say, Penteros?"

"I said I'm grateful there's no beast beneath the beast."

Silence.

"It seems the Goddess hasn't entirely abandoned this country after all."

Perdiccas II turned his head slowly toward him.

His eyes blazed with murderous intent—fierce enough to kill the man before him. The contrast was stark against Eratosthenes, who appeared as fragile as a dried twig ready to snap.

At least physically, the Emperor held the advantage.

"Penteros, have you lost your mind?"

"Perhaps."

Eratosthenes' voice remained utterly level, devoid of inflection.

"I've been this way for quite some time. You're only noticing now?"

Gone was any pretense of addressing him as Emperor.

Eratosthenes spoke with the directness of their old teacher-student dynamic from decades past.

"When Eleanor died because of you, did you believe yourself sane? Your delusions are grand. You've been mad for a very long time."

"You treacherous—!"

Perdiccas II's face flushed crimson.

The anger that had momentarily subsided now surged through his veins with renewed force.

"You've circled me with a dagger at your back all these years! You've been aiming for my life—"

Drip.

The Emperor's words died unfinished.

Blood trickled from his nose.

"What—what is this?"

Simultaneously, Perdiccas II coughed violently, spattering blood from his mouth.

His pristine robes instantly stained crimson.

"Ugh... urgh...!"

Pain lanced through his chest as though something were tearing him apart from within.

It was agony so foreign and overwhelming that his eyes widened in shock.

The Emperor looked up at Eratosthenes with horrified comprehension.

"It's taking effect."

But there was no urgency in the Marquis's weathered features. No surprise whatsoever.

The calmness of that wrinkled face bordered on cold detachment.

"You... you poisoned me...!"

Perdiccas II recalled the glass Eratosthenes had handed him moments ago.

His face contorted grotesquely.

Blood vessels swelled as though ready to burst. Heat coursed through them like molten metal.

He gasped through his open mouth, glaring at Eratosthenes with betrayed fury.

"Poisoning the Emperor is punishable by summary execution! Do you think you'll leave this place alive?"

"What fool would I be to neglect my own survival? It's sufficient that you die before day's end."

Eratosthenes' voice remained eerily calm—almost mocking.

The Emperor's mind reeled.

He couldn't believe it.

Penteros—whom he'd trusted above all others in the empire—now sought his death.

Did this mean decades of loyalty, of suppressed resentment and concealed anger, had all been an immaculate performance?

Meanwhile, the agony spread like wildfire through his body.

Every bone felt as though it were being consumed by flames.

"Please! Penteros, where's the antidote? I—I made a mistake!"

Perdiccas II's mind worked frantically.

"I won't punish you! I understand your position—a hundred times, a thousand times over! I'll apologize to Eleanor myself! I'll commission a golden monument for her grave! Will that suffice? Please?"

He wrung his hands desperately.

He feigned remorse toward the late Empress, attempting to pivot once more.

But Eratosthenes' eyes remained utterly tranquil.

"Penteros, have you truly gone mad? Are you senile? Do you know who I am? I am the master of this empire, of all Katzen!"

"In my eyes, I see only a contemptible wretch."

Eratosthenes' mockery was absolute.

"Eleanor... it is my eternal regret that I bound that precious child to a ruin like you with my own hands."

Perdiccas II's survival instincts proved formidable.

He seized his staff and swung it wildly.

The sharp tip tore through Eratosthenes' robes, leaving a vicious gash across his chest.

"Give it to me! The antidote—now!"

"There is none. Nothing. There was never an antidote to begin with."

Eratosthenes looked down at him with something approaching pity.

Vengeance Unveiled

"Don't feel wronged, Perdiccas."

Eratosthenes' voice carried the weight of decades.

"From the day Eleanor departed this world until this very moment, there has not been a single day when I didn't contemplate severing your breath."

He paused, watching the poison work through the Emperor's veins.

"This was always the end waiting for you. I simply chose when it would arrive."

End of Chapter 254
