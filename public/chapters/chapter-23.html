Ambient: Detonation
The Final Move

Did the Princess
Wrap the Bracelet?

"The thought detonated in Maria's mind."

Did the Princess wrap Samon's bracelet in the Minister's handkerchief?

The thought detonated in Maria's mind.

She understood then.

The bracelet.

The handkerchief.

The order in which they were found.

Medea had known everything—

the plot,

the lovers,

the betrayal.

Not discovered. Not suspected.

Known.

When Maria met the Princess's green eyes, it felt like being submerged in ice.

"Well… Your Highness… I—I made a mistake…"

voice breaking

Princess Medea

"It is unfortunate. Maria forgot her place. But she is still my maid. I cannot simply discard her."

She turned to the Queen Mother.

"Grandmother. Please allow Maria to marry Minister Etienne. Their affection must be sincere, if they exchange such intimate tokens."

The words were gentle.

The effect was catastrophic.

"No—!"

Maria screamed and lunged forward, collapsing at Medea's feet.

"Please! Save me!"

The abyss yawned open beneath her.

Marriage to Etienne.

The fate she had designed for Medea.

To be locked beside that lecherous old man.

To rot in a gilded cage, discarded and mocked.

Death would have been kinder.

Princess Medea

"Save you? But Minister Etienne is an excellent match. A high-ranking noble. Immensely wealthy. And clearly devoted."

She tilted her head.

"Isn't this the future you desired? A noble husband?"

A noble husband.

That was your price, wasn't it?

"Congratulations, Maria. It is a master's duty to send a loyal servant to a better life."

The words landed like funeral rites.

— The Mind Shatters —

"No! It was a lie!"

Maria's mind shattered.

"The Head Maid ordered it! She gave me the handkerchief! She told me to hide it in Your Highness's room!"

The court erupted.

"She said the Regent abandoned her! She wanted revenge! She promised to make me a noblewoman—said she could erase my origins!"

Quiggin shrieked and lunged, only to be seized by guards.

"Shut up! Shut your filthy mouth!"

But Maria couldn't stop.

"It was all her! All of it!"

The truth spilled out, raw and uncontrollable.

A slander plot.

Against a Royal Princess.

By the Regent's own Head Maid.

Maria crawled forward, clutching Medea's dress.

"I'll do anything! Please forgive me! Your Highness—save me!"

In her eyes flickered hope.

She still believed Medea was weak.

Just like before.

You looked like this in my past life too, Medea thought.

When you sold me to the rebels.

"You betrayed me for ambition. Then. And now."

She bent down, placing her hand over Maria's.

To the court, it looked merciful.

But Maria felt the cold—each finger pried loose, one by one.

"Don't blame me, Maria. You pushed me first."

Their eyes met.

There was nothing there.

Maria's pupils dilated. Her body went slack.

She collapsed unconscious at Medea's feet.

— The Fall —

"What a farce."

the Queen Mother muttered, rubbing her temples

Baroness Pinatelli

Pinatelli stepped forward, voice precise and merciless.

"To slander the Blood of Valdina. To drag the Royal House into disgrace for personal revenge."

She turned toward Quiggin.

"This is treason."

The Queen Mother's gaze hardened.

"Take them away."

"No! It's a trap! Catherine—help me!"

Catherine looked away.

The Head Maid was already dead.

"Cut her tendons. Beat her until she can no longer speak."

"I will see to it," Siseo said, bowing.

Quiggin's screams echoed until the doors slammed shut.

Silence followed.

The court turned toward Medea.

Surely now—comfort. Vindication. Apology.

Medea remained still.

She knew better.

— Even Innocent —
Queen Mother

"Hmph."

The Queen Mother's eyes burned with contempt.

"When you hold the King's Seal and play at authority, you invite chaos."

Even innocent, Medea was at fault.

"I've heard of your behavior. Beating servants. Purging your staff. Do you think the Seal was given for childish tantrums?"

She stood.

"This house is cursed to have a sister like you. Foolish. Immature. Unfit."

She extended her hand.

"Give me the Seal."

Duchess Catherine

Catherine stepped forward smoothly.

"She meant no harm, Mother. She is young. She doesn't understand the palace."

Poison wrapped in silk.

"Please be generous. Apologize. Promise it won't happen again."

turning to Medea

Medea did not bow.

She looked at the Queen Mother.

Then at Catherine.

And for the first time that day—

She smiled.
