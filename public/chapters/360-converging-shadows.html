Chapter 260

Converging Shadows

"Three players move toward the cathedral—each believing they hold the winning hand."

The Castullo mansion crouched in darkness, so dilapidated it appeared abandoned.

In its deepest basement, a black-robed warlock hung suspended within a semicircle drawn beneath flickering torchlight, his tongue lolling uselessly.

"Castullo, damn you—!"

Every time he opened his mouth, blood sprayed. His condition had deteriorated beyond recognition.

"Tell me."

Jason thrust the torch beneath the warlock's chin.

"You know the formula for temporal reversal magic."

"How many times must I repeat myself?! Reversing time is impossible! Do you think yourself a god?!"

The warlock's face twisted as he shouted hoarsely.

He'd never imagined finding himself in this position.

His ally in fabricating the false oracle—the ambitious, treacherous man he'd thought firmly under control—had transformed into a raving madman who now tortured him mercilessly.

"I find it difficult to believe that those who can manufacture fake oracles and gods lack this knowledge."

Jason shook his head with feigned disappointment.

"In exchange for accepting darkness, you must suffer equivalent pain from the light. Divine fire proves remarkably effective at tormenting souls."

Jason poured oil over the torch.

Holy oil.

"It seems God hasn't abandoned me yet. Despite consorting so closely with warlocks, I remain unscathed. How fortunate."

"That violates the Golden Rule! You're insane! Completely mad! Even the warlocks of the Golden Tower never dared—!"

Jason's eyes flashed dangerously.

Golden Rule. Divine wrath.

If God had truly wished to stop him, Jason would have returned to dust long ago.

He gestured silently.

"Yes, Your Highness."

His subordinate advanced with the torch.

"Aaaaargh!"

The warlock's scream split the basement air.

Each time the holy oil's flame touched his flesh, it didn't merely burn—it seared his very soul while simultaneously keeping him conscious.

Amidst desperate screams, the warlock gasped out:

"My... my brothers are coming to Katzen. If they discover what you've done—"

"Speak. If you wish to see them alive. How does one reverse time?"

Sizzle.

The smell of burning flesh filled the confined space.

"Stop! Please, I'll tell you!"

The warlock finally broke.

"The further back in time you wish to travel, the larger the magic circle must be! More sacrifices are required! When activated, everyone within the circle becomes bound to it—sacrificed!"

He revealed the complete process of temporal black magic.

"The more sacrifices possess specific abilities—knights who've mastered aura, priests wielding holy power, sorcerers who've mastered mana—the more potent the offering becomes, and the higher the success rate."

Silence.

"When my brothers arrive, they can assist you—"

The warlock looked up at him pleadingly.

First priority: escape this madman's clutches.

"...Yes."

Jason had obtained all the information he required.

The corners of his mouth curled slowly.

"We'll meet them soon enough."

Slash!

Before the warlock could finish his sentence, steel flashed.

Blood sprayed across the semicircle.

"Ghh... ghhh..."

The warlock clutched his throat, pitching forward.

His final words drowned in bubbling crimson.

Jason turned away, murmuring:

"A gathering of knights, priests, and wizards..."

In the distance, bells tolled—announcing the Emperor's death.

Jason's mouth twisted into a cruel smile.

Within his line of sight rested a gilded invitation on the table.

Whether addressed to Medea or Cesare, it mattered not—both were pieces on his board.

Jason's Vow

"The Coronation of Cesare."

The cathedral where the ceremony would be held.

In that most sacred moment when her beloved ascended the throne.

"Medea..."

I'll reveal it right before your eyes.

"Soon, we'll be together again."

He looked upward.

In that blood-soaked basement, Jason's crimson eyes gleamed with cruel anticipation.

Late Night, Imperial Palace

The Imperial Library's innermost sanctum—the Forbidden Books section—lay silent as a tomb.

In that stillness, the sound of iron scraping and locks clicking echoed.

In pitch darkness without a single candle, a black-robed figure crept down the aisle.

Meow~

A cat blocked his path, crying out.

Black fur gleamed in the darkness.

"Gods above, you startled me!"

The robed man bit his lip nervously.

Why did I agree to this for a mere thousand gold coins?

The man was a newly hired palace servant.

When he'd first arrived, senior servants had informed him of rumors—servants beaten to death by Perdiccas II supposedly haunted this library.

"All their joints broken, scratching across the floor..."

With recent whispers that the Emperor's untimely death might be the curse of his wronged victims, the man's hands trembled.

"Find it quickly. He promised payment once this job's complete."

He extracted a dust-covered tome from a corner shelf and opened it.

The cover bore a crimson pattern.

Within lay detailed architectural drawings of the cathedral's interior.

The servant produced oiled parchment and began copying hurriedly.

Then—

A faint sound echoed from the darkness at the corridor's end.

The terrified servant's head whipped around.

With the Emperor's funeral and the First Prince's divine punishment, security has never been tighter.

If I'm caught, they might execute me on the spot.

No amount of gold is worth my life.

He grabbed his incomplete copy and fled the library.

Shortly after, Medea emerged from the shadows like a curtain falling away.

Neril followed behind her.

"Well done, Neril. Your stealth skills haven't dulled."

Medea approached the shelf where the servant had been crouched.

In his haste, he'd left a thick tome protruding clumsily from the shelf.

Medea reached for it.

Opening the volume revealed the exact page the servant had been examining.

"Jason..."

So you've finally chosen a path divergent from Rachel's.

A smile tugged at the corners of Medea's mouth.

Her index finger tapped thoughtfully against the desk as she studied the drawing.

Whoooosh—

An unexpected wind gusted through the library.

On the opposite wall hung a massive portrait of the First Emperor, with a small table positioned beneath it.

Flutter flutter flutter—

Pages of a thick volume on that table turned of their own accord.

The founding document of the Katzen Empire.

As though someone wished to show her something specific, the pages stopped their rapid turning.

On the revealed surface appeared an illustration of a stone slab bearing the imperial family's crest.

"The Oath Stone..."

This was no stranger to Medea.

At the empire's founding, the First Emperor had carved his eternal vow to the Empress upon it.

A stone reputedly the source of the Katzen Imperial bloodline itself.

[...The First Emperor hewn the rock imbued with primordial power and made it the foundation of imperial authority.]

Medea, reading the text beneath the illustration, paused.

I was correct.

Her green eyes flashed with understanding.

Western Border of Katzen

A warm tailwind blew, markedly different from the frigid northern gales.

The temperate climate and verdant expanse visible beyond the horizon spoke eloquently of Katzen's inherent wealth.

On a hill overlooking the agricultural plains, a man sat astride a white stallion.

Silver hair rippled in the scattering breeze. The greatsword strapped to his waist—large as a child—gleamed in the sunlight.

Like a pillar of crystalline ice given human form, an inexorable cold radiated from his presence.

"Your Majesty, if we cross this river, we enter Katzen proper."

This was Peleus, young Emperor of proud Valdina, who'd matured considerably in a single year.

His silver eyes surveyed the border with calculating intensity.

Behind him, an elite force waited in perfect formation—warriors who'd marched across the continent to reach this place.

All for one purpose.

"My sister is there."

The wind carried his words across the border.

Three forces now converged on Katzen's heart.

Three players, each convinced they held the advantage.

But only one knew the stage had already been set—and the trap carefully baited.

End of Chapter 260
