Chapter 260

The Dragon's
Head

"Proof delivered in blood and bone—undeniable, impossible to ignore."

Rachel swept her gaze across the assembled rulers at the round table, letting her attention linger deliberately on the representatives from the smaller kingdoms—those most vulnerable, most desperate.

"This is not a suitable solution for our dire circumstances. Whether it's the Philosopher's Stone or some other hidden secret—how many more innocent people will be slaughtered by demons while we waste time searching for mysteries buried in Valdina's protected lands?"

She paused, allowing the weight of her words to settle over the chamber like a shroud.

"Katzen may be a great power with resources sufficient to weather whatever consequences arise. But the rest of the continent cannot afford such luxuries. As the Saint and proxy of the Goddess's will, I am deeply concerned for those who will suffer."

Her tone dripped with false compassion—concern weaponized into political leverage.

"Exactly! Emperor of Katzen—how far do you intend to exploit the blood and suffering of this continent?!"

"If you and Valdina unite to extract profit from our desperation, we will treat you as enemies without hesitation! We will resist to the bitter end!"

Voices rose in agreement, building upon one another like kindling catching flame.

'Dea... why did you come back?'

Peleus did not look at Medea—did not even acknowledge her presence—as though by ignoring her, he could somehow protect her from what was to come.

But his hand, resting near the greatsword at his waist, trembled ever so slightly.

He closed his eyes, jaw clenched tight.

Part of him—the brother, not the Emperor—had desperately wished she would abandon her duty and simply flee. Disappear beyond the continent's borders where none of this madness could touch her.

He had been a man who never backed down, standing like an unyielding stone wall against countless pressures and criticisms.

This was the first moment that wall began to crack.

"Katzen will lend its full strength to Valdina. What could possibly be impossible if the two greatest empires of the continent joined forces?"

"And what does Katzen want in return?"

"Something small and shiny will suffice."

Count de Montfort's smile was practiced, pleasant—and utterly untrustworthy.

Katzen—a nation that had been Valdina's enemy throughout most of recorded history—would never have offered protection without substantial motivation.

But whatever hidden agenda drove them, if their alliance could save his sister...

'I will grasp even a rotting hand if it means pulling her from the fire.'

Peleus locked eyes with Count de Montfort across the table.

The Count, recognizing the Emperor's wavering resolve, began to rise with a satisfied smile—

"The Goddess can express Her will through oracles at will."

Medea's voice cut through the rising commotion like a blade through silk.

She walked step by measured step toward the round table, her bearing regal despite the hostility radiating from every corner of the chamber.

"However, divine revelation should not be wielded as an excuse to avoid responsibility as leaders."

"Can any of you swear before the Goddess that you truly did everything in your power when the continent was being consumed by demonic forces?"

Silence answered her.

"Or are you simply using the oracle as convenient justification to place all blame on me? Is this the attitude of those who claim to faithfully follow divine will?"

An uncomfortable tension settled over the assembly like frost. Her words had struck bone.

"While you persecuted me and my kingdom, forcing me into the life of a fugitive, I traveled to the Land of the Dead to find a solution to save this continent. To locate the evil dragon—the source of disaster spoken of in the oracle."

Medea gestured subtly.

Her knights stepped forward and placed something on the round table with ceremonial precision.

The sound of thick leather straps being loosened echoed through the sacred space.

Layer by layer, the protective wrapping fell away to reveal what lay beneath: a massive dragon's fang, sharp as a executioner's blade, gleaming with an unnatural, eerie light.

"And I killed the evil dragon."

An otherworldly glow emanated from the fang's razor-sharp tip.

"With the dragon—the vessel of the oracle's prophecy—now dead, the Goddess's wrath will no longer fall upon the continent."

"What—nonsense!"

"What are you saying?!"

Kings leapt to their feet. The assembly erupted in chaos. People couldn't believe their ears.

"...The evil dragon?"

Peleus's eyes flew open wide.

It felt as though his heart had been ripped from his chest and thrown off a cliff.

Medea—his sister—hadn't run away to save herself. She'd gone to confront the evil dragon?

"The Goddess commanded me to become the bride of the evil dragon. However..."

Medea pronounced the next word with crystalline clarity, her voice carrying to every corner of the temple.

"Since the evil dragon who was to be my companion no longer exists in this world, the oracle unfortunately cannot be fulfilled."

She emphasized companion deliberately—a pointed reminder to those who had pushed her toward sacrifice while knowing full well that"bride" was merely a euphemism for victim.

"That... that cannot be..."

"The evil dragon has been destroyed."

"Haven't you noticed while examining the map? No demonic corruption appears here—in what was once the Dragon's Lair."

She pointed to a location at the edge of the holy map.

The Valley of Death, where the evil dragon had made its nest, now showed as pristine blue—purified.

The High Priests, who remembered that same region as a festering black wound on the map, hesitated.

"And you expect us to believe this? That you—the Princess of Valdina—single-handedly defeated the evil dragon?"

"What's so difficult to believe?"

"It's preposterous! Even our Holy Nation dared not approach the dragon! How could a Princess—a woman who isn't even an Aura user—"

High Priest Paul's voice cracked with indignant disbelief.

"You say 'dared not approach.' How interesting."

Medea's tone turned frigid.

"That land was also inhabited by people of this continent. Yet I witnessed hell there—demons descending every other day, dragon's poison spreading like plague through the soil and water."

The faces of those who knew the Holy Nation's shameful history—their complete inability to do anything about the dragon's lair—went pale.

"Why was the Goddess's protection, which supposedly covers the entire continent, conspicuously absent from only that place? Unless..."

She turned her penetrating green gaze directly on Paul.

"Unless the Holy Nation deliberately chose to ignore the Land of Death and abandon its people."

"How dare you—this is beyond rude—"

"That is why the words of High Priest Paul—who cries so passionately about continental suffering—ring so hollow. If the Holy Nation truly heard the cries of all people equally, how could it leave that region to suffer so miserably?"

"Of course, that's not what matters most right now."

She raised the dragon's fang high above her head.

A sharp, visceral tension ran up the spines of everyone present.

"This is absurd!"

"Your Highness... how could this possibly be true? You're not even an Aura wielder! Even the most elite warriors on the continent wouldn't dare attempt such a thing!"

People didn't want to believe it.

No—they couldn't believe it.

"How will you prove this is truly the fang of an evil dragon? None of us here have ever seen such a creature! How can you prove you're not lying—that you actually confronted and killed a real dragon?"

"Isn't this essentially a confession that you ignored the oracle's true meaning and rejected your duty?"

Distrust rapidly metastasized into open accusation.

In their eyes, she remained what she had always been: an orphaned princess—fragile, beautiful, delicate.

How could she have hunted and killed the evil dragon that even they—hardened warriors and powerful rulers—had been too frightened to approach?

They couldn't believe it. They refused to believe it.

Because if it were true, then what had they been doing all this time?

Wouldn't that make Medea's accusation of incompetence and cowardice absolutely correct?

Their anxiety quickly transmuted into rage—the last refuge of the exposed and ashamed.

"Where did you even acquire this thing?! How dare you bring a fake artifact to insult the Goddess!"

"Do you not fear divine retribution?! How far will you go to deceive us?!"

"Fortunately..."

Medea's lips curved into a faint, knowing smile.

"It seems I am not the only one here who must answer for the fate of the continent."

People frowned, struggling to parse the ambiguous statement.

And then—

SCREEEEE—!

A deafening avian cry tore through the air from outside the temple.

Every head reflexively snapped upward toward the sky.

KWAAAANG—!

A massive object hurtled through the air, nearly grazing the temple's vaulted roof before crashing through one of the high windows.

There was no time to scream.

Locke—Medea's bonded griffin—released the enormous mass he'd been carrying in his talons.

The Proof

KRAKOOM!

The marble floor of the great hall shook violently with the force of impact. Silver candlesticks toppled from the round table, clattering to the ground with sharp, discordant notes.

The sickening sound of splintering bone. The overwhelming, metallic stench of blood flooding their nostrils.

The moment people saw what Locke had deposited in the center of the temple, they froze.

Completely. Utterly. Paralyzed.

"Ah..."

A king from one of the smaller nations trembled, lips moving soundlessly.

The word"dragon" died in his throat, unspoken.

The severed head of the evil dragon lay sprawled across the sacred temple floor.

Its jaw gaped open in a perpetual roar of rage, revealing rows of fangs like obsidian daggers. Midnight-black scales, stained and crusted with dried blood, gleamed dully in the temple's light.

But worst of all were the eyes.

Vicious yellow orbs, still filled with malevolent hatred even in death, seemed to glare at the assembly as though pouring out curses from beyond the grave.

"Huh... huh..."

They couldn't bring themselves to look directly into those wide-open eyes. Couldn't even muster the breath to scream.

The stench of death and ancient malice rolled off the dragon's corpse in waves, filling the temple with an atmosphere of primal terror.

At that moment, amidst the stunned silence and barely suppressed groans of horror, a figure leapt down from above.

He landed with casual grace directly atop the dragon's severed head—one boot planted firmly on the bridge of its snout in a gesture of absolute dominance.

A deep, mocking voice cut through the air like a blade, dripping with contempt for their frozen fear.

"Are you going to claim this is fake as well?"

His golden eyes blazed sharply through disheveled hair, scanning the assembly with predatory intensity.

Not one person dared to meet that gaze.

The severed head of the evil dragon—undeniable proof sprawled across their sacred temple floor, reeking of death and ancient power.

The fang in Medea's hand.

And now this warrior, standing triumphant atop the skull of the continent's greatest terror.

What could they possibly say?
