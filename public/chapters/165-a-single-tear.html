Chapter 165

A Single
Tear

"In a well-crafted play, truth holds no relevance."

Rachel continued her performance, her eyes brimming with carefully manufactured fear as she gazed upon Medea.

"The Princess also commanded that I remain confined to the cottage house. She forbade me from coming and going within the palace without explicit permission—insisted I never leave these grounds."

Comprehension dawned upon the bewildered onlookers as they pieced together Rachel's implications.

The evidence formed a damning constellation:

  • Traces of a horrific curse discovered in the cottage house
  • A rat's severed foreleg mirroring the exact location of the Saintess's wound
  • And now—the Saintess herself pointing to the Princess as the architect of this malevolent sorcery

The assembly fractured along predictable lines.

"Your Eminence, cease these absurd accusations at once! What manner of insolence is this?!"

The Valdinians erupted in fury. How dare anyone suggest their Princess would perpetrate such villainy within the walls of her own palace?

D'Angelo's face flushed crimson with humiliation and rage.

But alongside the Valdinians stood the Holy Land delegation—and they immediately closed ranks around Rachel.

"Would the Saintess level such accusations against the Princess without cause?"

Rachel pressed her advantage, her voice trembling with exquisite pathos.

"I understand that my presence is... unwelcome to Her Highness."

She paused, allowing a sob to escape.

"Nevertheless, Your Highness, I am but a humble servant of the Goddess. I beg you—even if you cannot regard me with favor, show me some measure of mercy. I harbor no ambition to usurp your position."

The final words dissolved into pitiful weeping, each tear calculated to pierce the hearts of those watching.

A murmur rippled through the crowd.

Come to think of it... the relationship between the Saintess and His Majesty the King was never simple.

Rumors had circulated among the nobility—whispers that Valdina might soon witness the crowning of a new Queen.

The bond between Peleus and Medea was legendary in its strength.

But what if, beneath that beautiful façade of sibling devotion, lurked something more troubling? What if the Princess harbored an obsessive possessiveness toward her brother—a jealousy fierce enough to drive her to slander an innocent Saintess, simply to keep her from the King's side?

High Priest Paul, his fury mounting with each passing moment, seized the dagger and thrust it skyward.

"Behold! Here lies the proof!"

He brandished the weapon triumphantly before the Valdinian royals.

"Do you see? Here—quite coincidentally—is the royal emblem of Valdina. And beneath it, Her Highness's own initials."

"Your Eminence. I would advise you to choose your words with greater care."

Sissair's warning hung in the air like frost.

But Paul only raised the dagger higher, practically thrusting it into the Prime Minister's face.

"Can you not see this insignia? If your eyesight fails you, I shall bring it closer!"

The evidence gleamed in the afternoon light—the Valdinian royal crest and the letter 'M' engraved unmistakably upon the blade.

Among all the royals, only Medea bore a name beginning with that letter.

The Prosecution

The case against the Princess appeared damning:

  • Six rat carcasses arranged in demonic ritual formation
  • One carcass impaled upon a mithril dagger, its severed limb mirroring the Saintess's wound
  • The dagger bearing Medea's personal insignia

"Your Highness the Princess..."

"Can you explain this?"

Every gaze in the garden pivoted toward Medea.

"Oh, Goddess... I cannot believe this..."

Tears cascaded down Rachel's cheeks in an endless stream.

"Forgive me, Your Highness. I never imagined you would misunderstand me so gravely. I never dreamed you would despise me enough to... to curse me..."

She wept again, her slender shoulders shaking.

"This is all my fault. I have shattered the peace of the royal family... created a rift between His Majesty and his beloved sister..."

Maintaining control over so many minds is inherently unstable. But now is the moment. I must push Medea over the precipice while the opportunity exists.

Rachel understood the risk. Overextending her influence might fracture the conditioning she had so painstakingly established. But the chance to destroy Medea was too precious to squander.

"Your Highness, what is the meaning of this?"

Elinor, Commander of the Knights Templar, stepped forward, his tone sharpening with accusation at the Princess's continued silence.

Even the Agema knights turned toward their mistress, confusion and denial warring in their eyes.

"Your Highness... surely this cannot be true?"

"Please, tell them. Deny it. This is impossible."

Their voices carried the desperate hope of those watching their world crumble.

To frame a Saintess was tantamount to attacking the Goddess herself. And the accused was the Princess of Valdina.

"Ha! As if she would confess of her own accord! I have heard much of the Princess's infamous reputation!"

Unable to contain himself any longer, Paul hurled the dagger to the ground.

Steel sang as every Agema surrounding the Princess drew their weapons in unison. Though Rachel's conditioning had clouded their minds, their bodies responded instinctively to the insult against their liege.

But the Princess at the eye of this hurricane remained utterly still.

She offered no defense. No denial.

Her emerald gaze drifted across the scene with dreamlike detachment—passing over her loyal Agemas, lingering on the frothing High Priest and his Templars, finally settling on the scattered evidence of the ritual strewn across the earth.

Sometimes, reality and memory intertwine...

As though this moment were not the first time she had lived through such a scene, memories long buried rose unbidden to the surface.

"Jason, please—it was my fault. I never meant to—"

"Silence, Medea. How dare you speak after what you've done? Rachel has committed no wrong."

No one had listened then.

No matter what Medea said or did, the narrative remained immutable: the wicked woman consumed by ambition versus the pure Saintess who sought only peace.

"Your Highness, speak! What have you to say for yourself?"

Paul's voice cut through the silence.

And then—

A single tear traced its way down Medea's cheek.

Why had she fought so desperately to prove herself?

Why had the truth mattered so much?

In a performance this perfectly orchestrated, truth held no relevance whatsoever.

Understanding that bitter truth now, a sound escaped her lips—something between silent laughter and a scream that reached no one.

Medea, you foolish, pitiful creature.

The garden fell absolutely silent.

Even Paul, who had been raging mere moments before, found himself struck mute.

When they beheld that single crystalline droplet tracing its path down her expressionless face, every heart in the assembly seized as though they had committed some irreversible transgression.

The Princess offered no excuses. No apologies.

Whether she admitted guilt or proclaimed innocence remained impossible to discern from that marble countenance.

And yet—that solitary tear struck the Agemas like a boulder hurled from the heavens.

Rachel's black magic may have corroded their minds.

But it had never touched their hearts.

"Your Eminence."

D'Angelo was the first to recover. In one fluid motion, he drew his blade and pressed its edge against Paul's throat.

"Do not presume that I shall tolerate further disrespect toward Valdina."

His eyes burned with cold fire.

"Unless you wish your tongue to part company with the floor, I suggest you guard it more carefully."

"This—this is outrageous! You dare threaten me?!"

The Knights Templar stiffened, hands moving to their hilts as they felt the killing intent radiating from the Agemas.

Every Agema knight now stood shoulder to shoulder with D'Angelo, forming an unbroken wall against the Templars.

The standoff crackled with barely restrained violence.

Paul had made a fatal miscalculation.

He should have confined his insults to Valdina as a nation.

The moment he spoke the Princess's name with contempt, the fury that erupted in the Agemas' chests shattered Rachel's conditioning like glass.

Months of painstaking work. Months of pouring counterfeit power into them, of carefully implanting marks upon their souls.

Had Rachel understood how fragile her hold truly was, she would never have pushed so hard.

But the damage was done.

Neither faction would yield. The tension stretched to its breaking point.

Then Peleus raised his hand.

"Enough."

A single word, soft as falling snow.

Yet it carried the weight of an avalanche.

Killing intent radiated from him in waves, freezing the very air. Every breath became visible, crystallizing in the sudden, terrible cold.

The garden had become a frozen battlefield, and at its center stood the King of Valdina—his sunken eyes burning with barely contained wrath.

" One tear had accomplished
what months of manipulation could not undo—
it reminded them whose side they truly stood upon. "
