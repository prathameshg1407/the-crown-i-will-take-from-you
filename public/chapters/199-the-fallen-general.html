Chapter 199

The Fallen
General

"A man who has lost everything has nothing left to fear—except hope."

But all of that was now ancient history.

After his humiliating defeat at the hands of Acares—the mercenary who had fought as Medea's champion in the fateful duel.

To be precise: since the moment his ring finger had been severed by that blade.

"General, what has brought you to such a wretched state?"

"Are you still mocking me when you already know the answer? Have you come to gloat over my ruin?"

Bloodshot eyes, bulging with resentment, fixed upon Raju.

"If not, then why seek me out at all?"

The voice that emerged was nothing like the Jared of old.

Once, he had been a cruel and lecherous man who left a trail of victims wherever he went. But when word spread that he could no longer wield a sword, those who had long awaited his downfall finally struck.

Even the First Prince—whom Jared had once believed would protect him until death—had risen from the ashes and delivered the killing blow to his former general's career.

Stripped of his rank as a ten-star general, Jared had been cast into the gutters of this wretched slum, forever glancing over his shoulder, waiting for assassins who could arrive at any moment.

"I have just returned from the Strait of El Amuz. I attended the proclamation ceremony of the Valdinian Empire."

"..."

"I am certain the General has heard the news as well. Quite the remarkable coincidence, wouldn't you agree?"

Raju continued smoothly, pretending not to notice how Jared had gone rigid.

"Had it not been for that wager on that particular day, that insignificant little kingdom would never have risen so high. They have reaped extraordinary rewards—while the General plummeted from the heavens to... this."

"..."

Beneath the filthy sleeves of his robe, Jared's hands twitched involuntarily.

The empty space where his ring finger had been seemed to scream, as though reliving the nightmare of that day.

Yes. I have never forgotten it. Not for a single moment.

That duel had stripped him of everything.

If only he had not been so enchanted by the Princess's beauty. If only he had not accepted the Fourth Princess's reckless wager.

No—if only he had never raised his blade against that mercenary from Façade—

"Do you not desire revenge?"

"What?"

Raju's voice sliced through Jared's brooding reverie.

"The Continental Festival has just concluded. Princess Medea will escort the departing guests to the port on the border's outskirts. In other words—this is the first and last opportunity to act without the Emperor or Agemas watching."

"..."

"Soon, the Princess will return to her homeland of Valdina. Once she crosses that threshold, the path to vengeance will grow infinitely more treacherous. But you were once a ten-star general, were you not? Surely you do not intend to simply... fade into the shadows of history?"

"Hmph. For one of Jason's lapdogs, you certainly have a long tongue."

Jared grumbled, pulling back his hood and scratching irritably at the back of his neck.

Raju permitted himself a faint smile.

He is wavering. My words are taking root.

He paused deliberately, letting the silence amplify his next words.

"Consider this: should you succeed, the Princess's husband would gain legitimate claim to Valdina's succession."

In reality, even if Peleus were to die, the succession would never pass to Medea—let alone to a foreigner like Jared.

But what matters is that this fool, tangled in his own delusions, believes it possible.

"Setting aside the matter of avenging your hand—this path offers both retribution and profit in a single stroke."

Notably, neither Jared nor Raju spoke of the true architect of Jared's destruction: the mercenary Acares from Façade.

The fear was too immense. They both understood, on some primal level, that there was no possibility of victory against that man.

Amid such humiliating self-awareness, was it not human nature to reach instead for the tempting fruit dangling within grasp—the fruit called Medea?

But what can I possibly accomplish with hands that can no longer hold a sword?

Jared's expression had grown hungry, but now his gaze dropped to his ruined hand beneath the sleeve. Hatred twisted his features.

"There is no need for the General to concern himself with that particular obstacle."

Raju spoke as though he had been waiting for precisely this moment.

"You will dance with a blade once more—every bit as gracefully as before."

"What?! Explain yourself! How is such a thing possible?!"

Jared seized Raju's wrist with desperate urgency, his bloodshot eyes blazing.

"I cannot restore your finger. But I can replace it. I must warn you, however—the method is... unorthodox. I fear the General may find it too distasteful to accept."

"It matters not. Since when have I concerned myself with such trivialities? If I can hold a sword again—"

Raju clapped his hands together in satisfaction, having extracted precisely the answer he sought.

"Then we shall proceed. Certain individuals will seek you out shortly. Once your... treatment is complete, make your way to the strait."

Jared nodded. Whatever hesitation had lingered within him had now been extinguished.

❧

As they departed the tavern, Raju's escort ventured a question.

"Master, is it wise to conceal the fact that the First Prince is currently at the strait?"

Having been cornered and nearly destroyed by the First Prince, Jared had learned to avoid any location where that man might be found.

"Had I revealed the truth, he would never have agreed. Besides, few among the Katzen nobility know that the First Prince resides in Valdina. How would Jared, rotting in this forgotten corner, have learned of it?"

"But what if he discovers the truth later?"

"By then, he will already have set foot in the strait—with no avenue of retreat. And once his hand has been... treated, he will fear nothing. Not even the First Prince."

Raju's voice carried a dark certainty.

"It is not merely new fingers I have arranged for him."

Raju examined his own hands. The palms were scorched black.

He had summoned the dark mages himself—using the pendant he had stolen from Jason.

"How dare a dog of the Grand Duke presume to summon us?"

The moment the words left the shadows, black smoke had shot forth like a serpent, striking Raju's palm.

The flesh peeled away, searing red before charring to black.

"Ghhhhk—!"

Raju had clenched his teeth against the agony and forced himself to speak.

"Great Arbiters of Darkness—before I confess the sins of this wretched servant, there is something you must know."

And so he had revealed his plan.

The dark mages had been displeased that one of Jason's men had dared approach them without sanction. But when they learned his target was the Princess of Valdina, they had agreed to lend their power.

Their ultimate objective was nothing less than the overthrow of the continent—the resurrection of the primordial darkness.

The first step had been the Katzen Empire. If that superpower were to collapse, the shockwaves would destabilize the entire continent.

To that end, the dark mages had attempted to eliminate the First Prince, Cesare—the greatest obstacle to their ambitions.

But impossibly, incredibly, the First Prince had survived the Curse of Origins.

Their grand design had ended in failure.

Having failed to claim Katzen, the warlocks had turned their hungry gaze toward a new target: the rising Empire of Valdina.

Yet at every turn, the Princess of Valdina continued to thwart their schemes.
