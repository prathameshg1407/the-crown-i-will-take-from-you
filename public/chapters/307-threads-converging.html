"In a dance of deception, every player wears a mask."

"Yes, I'm so glad you came today, Lord Sissair."

Medea smiled warmly.

Unlike her usual measured composure, she made no attempt to hide her genuine pleasure at encountering someone from her homeland.

Moreover, if anyone from Valdina could be considered a perfect diplomatic counterpart, it would be Sissair.

"How is everyone faring back home?"

Sissair found himself staring at the Princess—his young mistress, now the First Princess of Katzen.

Medea had dressed her silver hair with pearls, and wore an exquisite mauve silk gown.

Her fair skin bore a delicate pink flush, and her clear green eyes sparkled with unbridled joy—making her appear like some ethereal sprite newly emerged into the mortal realm.

"..."

In Sissair's eyes, an image superimposed itself over the woman before him—Valdina's beloved young Princess.

Growing up bathed in the late King's affection, her childhood had been vibrant and carefree.

Bitterness welled within him as he witnessed that scene once more—a memory already fading in his mind—not in their cherished Valdina, but here in foreign Katzen.

"Everyone is thriving so well that His Majesty's concerns seem entirely unnecessary. They all... miss Your Highness. That's the truth. His Majesty... everyone."

To avoid dwelling on why Medea appeared more radiant than the chandelier illuminating the ballroom, Sissair had to literally bite his tongue.

"I have numerous messages to deliver to Your Highness, including letters from His Majesty. After the banquet concludes, I'll send someone with them."

"Are you planning to depart immediately following the birthday celebration, my lord?"

A faint shadow of disappointment crossed Medea's expression—though Sissair, struggling with his own composure, didn't notice. The sadness revealed itself unconsciously.

'Her face says: I thought you would stay longer.'

Without realizing it, Sissair clenched his fists.

'Then I might grasp what I cannot otherwise reach.'

"Yes, forgive me. There are many pressing matters that require—"

"Surely we can dispense with the poison first?"

A languid voice interjected.

A shadow fell between them.

Cesare.

His raven-black hair caught the light with a luster darker than midnight itself.

The gold imperial embroidery adorning his black military uniform's sharp collar provided the only element of softness to the man's otherwise severe appearance.

"Though I'd prefer you stay longer. Even for the sake of those awaiting your return."

Something light as air settled on Medea's shoulders.

A lace cape woven with silver threads—created by elven hands. Instant warmth flooded through her previously chilled skin.

Cesare draped the delicate cape over his wife with remarkable skill, handling the fragile fabric that could tear at the slightest mishandling.

"If you leave in such haste, Dea will be disappointed."

He made no effort to conceal his devotion to his wife.

The invitation for Sissair to stay at his palace seemed a natural extension of that protective attitude.

"..."

Their eyes met for an instant.

The wife's servant. The husband's companion.

That was the full extent of the distance between them now.

"Damn you, Cesare. Creating excuses—"

"Call it contradiction or cowardice if you wish. I can no longer afford to concern myself with appearances."

A long-standing friendship lay shattered between them. Neither could recall their final moments together as friends.

Or perhaps they simply chose not to remember.

"..."

Behind spectacles made opaque by reflected light, Sissair remained silent for a long moment before bowing formally.

"Thank you for your gracious consideration. In that case, I shall remain for several days. However, as a representative of His Majesty and Valdina, I wish to avoid any personal misunderstandings. Therefore, I'll arrange separate accommodations."

His smooth voice, diplomatically flawless, conveyed nothing beyond professional courtesy.

"...As you wish."

However, no one noticed that Cesare's expression hardened slightly at such a response.

Just then, the music's melody shifted. It was time for the royal family's dance.

"Medea, we must go."

Cesare gently squeezed his wife's hand.

She nodded and turned toward Sissair one final time.

"Lord Sissair, Katzen's nights are at their most pleasant during this season. It would be a shame to miss them entirely. You should venture out later to experience it."

Though phrased as a casual suggestion, Sissair immediately recognized Medea's implicit instruction.

'She's trying to communicate something beyond prying eyes.'

For a moment, his heart raced, wondering if she intended to convey her desire to return to Valdina.

But he quickly dismissed the notion.

'If that were her intent, she wouldn't speak so openly in Cesare's presence.'

"Yes... I believe I am fatigued and could use some fresh air."

Sissair watched Medea's back as she moved toward the dance floor.

Even that view became obscured as Cesare's arms enveloped her protectively.

Eventually, Sissair turned away.

The grand ballroom's corridor.

Katzen's cool night breeze greeted him from the city beyond.

It was the moment when the warmth quietly burning within him finally extinguished.

"Oh!"

A middle-aged lady rushing through the hallway stumbled, her heel catching on the hem of her dark dress.

She managed to grasp a stone statue lining the corridor, narrowly avoiding a violent fall.

However, the brooch fastened to her collar came loose during the sudden movement, clattering across the floor.

"Oh dear, where did it go? It must have fallen somewhere nearby..."

The lady searched with hunched shoulders.

"Madam, are you looking for this?"

Sissair paused and bent to retrieve the brooch at his feet.

"..."

He froze.

A small, intricately engraved red fox adorned the brooch's reverse side.

The pattern was so minute he wouldn't have noticed it unless he'd deliberately turned it over and examined it at close range.

"Oh, there it is! Thank you so much."

The lady approached Sissair with evident relief.

"I would have been in terrible trouble if I'd lost it. It's an important keepsake I absolutely cannot misplace. Especially on a day such as today..."

As she accepted the brooch, the lady smiled brightly.

A folded piece of parchment pressed into his palm where their fingers touched.

"To such a kind gentleman, may the joy of today's birthday celebration endure eternally."

The lady curtsied slightly and walked past him.

An entirely ordinary encounter that no one would think twice about.

Sissair stopped in his tracks.

'Her Highness has an agent embedded beside the Eastern Empress?'

He'd already confirmed his mistress was currently engaged near the Eastern Empress before returning to the ballroom.

Menil, Neril, and the Princess's other Valdinian guards remained in the banquet hall, yet they'd sent a stranger to convey this message...

'Someone is orchestrating something involving Valdina tonight.'

With an impassive expression, Sissair slipped the parchment deep into his pocket.

Then, as though nothing had transpired, he resumed walking.

Meanwhile, a carriage entered the Katzen Imperial Palace grounds fashionably late.

The decorative carvings adorning it bore no resemblance to Katzen's aesthetic—they were distinctly foreign in style.

The carriage door opened, revealing a middle-aged man who descended with practiced grace.

"Welcome to His Majesty's birthday celebration. The grand ballroom is this way—"

"I know the way."

When a palace attendant attempted to guide him, the man waved dismissively and strode forward with certainty.

A purple coat with exotic patterns adorned his tall, slender frame.

Though time had left faint traces on the man's features, he remained strikingly handsome.

One could easily imagine he'd broken countless hearts in his youth.

"Good heavens, is that you? Alfred!"

Having left the bustling ballroom to stroll through the palace gardens, a Katzen nobleman spotted the man and opened his arms in welcome.

"It's been quite some time."

"How many years has it been? You haven't changed at all! Time's demons must have granted you special favor."

The nobleman clicked his tongue admiringly.

Unlike himself—already plagued by sagging jowls and a receding hairline—he gazed enviously at the newcomer's thick hair and trim waistline.

"Indeed, it's been decades since I last set foot in Katzen. I'd thought I might die without ever returning."

"Oh, you heartless creature! How many times have I sent communications with no response whatsoever? You're truly inconsiderate."

As the nobleman grumbled fondly, the man called Alfred allowed his gaze to drift across the imperial palace's dome, a faint smile playing at his lips.

Back in the ballroom...

Sharp tension creased the corners of the Western Empress's eyes.

"Princess Strauss is notably absent tonight. It appears true that the Northern Empress personally traveled to the provinces to collect her daughter, Your Majesty."

Her lady-in-waiting Bridget whispered cautiously near the Empress's ear.

Dressed in an elegant dark gown befitting a close confidante of the Western Empress, Bridget blended seamlessly into the splendid ballroom.

"Provincial gossip suggests the First Prince targeted the Northern Empress precisely because her daughter antagonized the First Princess."

The Western Empress set down her wine glass. Her gaze swept over the empty seat to her left.

The First Princess's designated position—on the same elevated platform as herself.

'After all, acting now was the correct decision. When the Emperor openly despises that upstart, why does he suddenly decide to preserve appearances like this?'

Even the Emperor's whims worked in the First Princess's favor. That foreign girl proved remarkably adept at exploiting them.

'If I'd been careless, that bright-eyed little schemer would be attempting to elevate herself above my head by now.'

She stared coldly at the young couple standing close together, as though observing a pair of particularly noxious insects.
