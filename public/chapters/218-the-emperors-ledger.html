Chapter 218

The Emperor's
Ledger

"Gold has a way of changing hands—especially when no one is watching."

Birna did not neglect to reinforce her threat, brandishing the black ledger like a weapon.

"Should you think otherwise, I will have you torn limb from limb and fed to the street dogs. I may lack strength, but disposing of a lowly maid is well within my means."

The servant dropped into a trembling bow, her forehead nearly touching the carriage floor.

"I would never betray you, my lady. Please—you must believe me."

Birna watched with narrowed eyes as the maid slipped from the carriage and vanished around the corner toward the delegation's quarters.

"You're finished, Raju."

She bit at her thumbnail, muttering the words like a dark incantation.

What she did not know—could not have known—was what transpired the moment her servant disappeared from view.

"It's been quite some time, Umberto. You abandoned me in that pit and yet you seem to be thriving."

The maid had not taken three steps past the corner before the ledger changed hands—passed to a man who had been waiting in the shadows, his presence utterly unremarkable.

"Extracting you was all I was required to do."

His voice was flat, professional.

"Hmph. Is that so."

The young woman's tone had transformed entirely—the trembling servant replaced by something far sharper.

"Well done, regardless. Return when circumstances permit. Your assignment here is concluded."

As the man turned to leave, the red rose badge pinned to his chest caught the fading light—the unmistakable symbol of those who served the Emperor directly.

The Valdina Palace

Chambers of the Princess

Birna had ambitiously surrendered Raju's lifeline into Medea's hands, convinced she was orchestrating her own revenge.

The rust-colored eyes that gazed down at the black ledger displayed nothing but calm indifference.

"The Emperor's secret treasury...! Your Highness, how did you—this is—!"

Kensington could not contain his astonishment.

His shock only deepened as he examined the figures documented within—sums so vast they beggared belief.

"Raju... the audacity of the man. How did he conceive of hiding and siphoning funds on this scale for so long?"

It was as though Perdiccas II had been struck from behind with an iron mace.

"How did Your Highness discover this? That he possessed the Emperor of Katzen's legacy?"

What astounded Kensington even more than the ledger itself was Medea—who had somehow divined the existence of documents no one else had known existed.

"I did not know with certainty. I could only speculate."

Throughout both her past life and her present one, regardless of what calamities befell the world around him, Raju had continued to live in extraordinary wealth.

"I suspected there must be some hidden source sustaining him. But I confess, Sir Kensington—I did not anticipate it would be the Emperor of Katzen's personal treasury. Fortune smiled upon us."

Kensington marveled inwardly.

She suspected the true source of Raju's wealth, then manipulated his own daughter to retrieve the evidence.

Can such a complex and meticulous operation truly be dismissed as mere luck?

"And now, Sir Kensington, I require your expertise."

Medea's fingers traced the ledger's spine.

"This document requires... certain modifications."

"You wish for the Emperor to discover it—and find nothing but empty vaults?"

Kensington understood immediately.

Neither he nor Medea had the slightest intention of gifting Perdiccas II with a new source of wealth.

"We must proceed with the utmost caution. Should he gain even the faintest suspicion of our involvement, the consequences would be severe."

Medea's warning carried an edge of steel.

"Leave everything to me, Your Highness. I will not disappoint you."

Rarely did Kensington express such absolute conviction.

But in this matter, his confidence was well-founded.

Erasing traces, creating confusion with false leads, laundering funds through untraceable channels—these were skills he had honed over decades of service.

Moreover, this particular task he would execute with special care—a final gift to his old master.

"By the time word reaches Persepolis, the Emperor will not find so much as a single gold coin from what is documented here."

A thin smile crossed his weathered face.

"He will ransack empty warehouses, chase phantom accounts, grasp at shadows."

If he had never known of its existence, ignorance would have been painless. But to know—to see the figures, to calculate the wealth—and then to find it all vanished?

How furious the Emperor would be. How maddening the loss.

The mere thought filled Kensington with quiet euphoria.

Persepolis — The Imperial Capital of Katzen

The Golden Chamber

"Your Majesty, no one who has dishonored Katzen's name should be exempt from judgment."

In the golden chamber—that monument to imperial splendor—Empress Su's solemn voice resonated like the toll of a bell.

Her attire was refined without ostentation, her dark hair drawn back in a severe knot. She possessed none of the late Empress's legendary beauty, yet there was an understated dignity about her—the quiet authority of a woman who had earned her place.

Her thin lips rarely curved into smiles. Her deep-set eyes missed nothing.

As the focal point of the Third Prince's faction—locked in perpetual competition with the First Prince—she wielded considerable power.

Indeed, since Cesare's return, only her son had survived the First Prince's systematic dismantling of his rivals. One by one, the Emperor's other children had fallen like dominoes beneath Cesare's counteroffensive.

She would not allow this opportunity to slip through her fingers.

"The First Prince executed His Majesty's subject without authorization, undermining imperial authority and provoking conflict with Valdina."

Her voice carried the precision of a blade.

"How can a man who kills servants on personal whim be trusted to inherit Your Majesty's throne? How can the people place their faith in such a ruler?"

Cold ambition flickered behind her composed expression.

"The harm the First Prince has inflicted upon Katzen is substantial. Your Majesty must deliver decisive punishment and establish a clear precedent."

Even as she denounced Cesare, she did not neglect to flatter Perdiccas II—a careful balance of accusation and deference.

"I must respectfully disagree with Her Majesty the Empress."

The Marquis de Reniar, who had stood motionless across the chamber, finally spoke.

"This old man cannot accept the assertion that His Highness the First Prince has caused harm to the Empire."

White-haired and deeply lined, he bore his years with dignity. His imposing stature and imperial bearing remained undiminished by age.

As the father of Cesare's mother—the late Empress—and the cornerstone of the First Prince's faction, he stood as the natural counterweight to Empress Su's ambitions.

"His Highness acted to preserve Katzen's honor. Can you imagine, Your Majesty, how circumstances might have deteriorated had Jared been permitted to live?"

"Need I remind the court that this man attacked the Emperor on Valdinan soil—and was caught in the very act of attempting to violate his own sister?"

Reniar's voice hardened.

"Had His Highness Cesare not dealt with him immediately—had Jared perished at Valdinan hands instead—the incident would have ignited into something far more catastrophic."

He pressed forward, his argument gaining momentum.

"Furthermore, His Highness has already sought absolution for his actions. He disbanded his private forces. He ventured into the Abyss of his own volition, risking his life in service to the Empire."

His gaze fixed upon the Empress.

"And yet Her Majesty continues to enumerate his sins. Forgive this old man's humble confusion, but I fail to comprehend what purpose such persistence serves."

"You claim Cesare acted for Katzen's benefit?"

Empress Su's tone turned glacial.

"And yet the result is our subject imprisoned, our sovereign territory violated by Valdina's young Emperor. Did he not, in fact, make matters considerably worse?"

"Your Majesty the Empress employs the word 'violated' rather liberally. Those of us who survived the great wars understand that—"

"By all means, Marquis, continue your attempts to diminish me."

Her eyes were chips of ice.

"I speak of obvious and documented excesses. The First Prince is neither His Majesty—the supreme ruler of this Empire—nor is he the designated heir to the throne. Not yet."

She let the words hang in the air.

"And yet he acts with such unbridled arrogance. When he truly becomes master of this realm... will either His Majesty or I be permitted to draw breath?"

At these final words—so carefully crafted to inflame Perdiccas II's deepest insecurities—the Marquis de Reniar's expression darkened.

"The Empress Su raises a valid point."

Perdiccas II, who had observed the exchange in brooding silence, finally spoke.

"Your Majesty—"

"Does the Marquis presume to instruct me?"

The Emperor's gaze fell upon Reniar like a physical weight.

His eyes—which had watched the proceedings with unreadable calculation—now blazed with sudden, consuming fire.

The Emperor's madness had always been mercurial. Sometimes it manifested as cold as winter ice; other times it erupted like molten stone.

But when confronted with his eldest son—that towering presence who seemed to diminish him merely by existing—his reaction remained constant:

Vigilance. Hatred. Suspicion. Fear.

He is the enemy. An enemy who covets my throne, who threatens everything I have built.

Watching the unbridled hatred seep into the Emperor's dark pupils, the Marquis de Reniar suppressed a sigh.

How can those eyes belong to a father gazing upon his own child?

The thought remained unspoken, buried deep.

The chamber hung suspended in taut silence—neither faction willing to yield, the air thick with unspoken threats.

Then, cutting through the tension like a blade through silk, the Chief of Staff stepped forward.
