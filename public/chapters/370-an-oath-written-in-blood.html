Chapter 270

An Oath Written
in Blood

"My companion is only Medea—and no one else can take her place."

The atmosphere within the cathedral inverted in an instant.

The First Prince's sudden appearance rendered meaningless every rumor of his impending death from divine punishment. His body had clearly suffered—the lines of his face had grown sharper, more severe—yet the energy emanating from him was anything but diminished.

If anything, he appeared more dangerous. More compelling.

"The First Prince has arrived! The Emperor of Katzen has arrived!"

Stunned guests leapt to their feet, but Cesare's golden eyes searched for only one person.

Amid the sea of faces surrounding the altar—amid the chaos and ceremony—he found Medea instantly.

And beside her, Rachel, standing proudly before the Oath Stone.

Something cracked inside his skull.

It was the sound of the last thread of restraint he had been clinging to—snapping clean.

"Your Highness—no, His Majesty has graced us with his presence! All rise and pay respects!"

The Katzen ambassador, belatedly remembering his role, called out the formal greeting. But the words did not reach Cesare.

The flames of fury rising in his chest had already begun devouring his reason.

Terence hurried toward him—Cesare's appearance had been neither expected nor prepared for.

"Cesare, there is something I must tell you—"

"I believe I have heard enough explanations."

Cesare strode onto the crimson carpet.

The shattered remnants of the bronze doors crunched beneath his boots, the sound echoing through the vaulted hall louder than any proclamation.

Medea watched him draw nearer, step by deliberate step.

Those golden eyes were fixed upon her alone—as though no one else existed. As though no one else mattered.

"Cesare."

She swallowed the emotions threatening to overwhelm her. Every eye in the cathedral was watching. Rachel stood on the very edge of the trap Medea had so carefully prepared.

No matter how deeply that face pierced her heart, she could not allow a fleeting sentiment to unravel everything.

"Cesare, we can speak later. For now—"

"Do not worry, Dea."

His whisper was meant for her ears alone.

"I have no intention of interfering with your plans."

"But do not forget."

His voice dropped lower still.

"I will remain your sharpest sword until the end."

He renewed the oath to his only companion—sworn until his final breath.

His purpose remained unchanged: to cut down every enemy that dared stand in her way.

Medea glimpsed the flame burning within those golden eyes—a fire that bordered on madness.

Her heart constricted.

"...What are you planning?"

She reached for him, but he slipped from her grasp by a hair's breadth. Cesare had already turned and was striding toward the altar.

Meanwhile, Paul clutched at the collar hidden beneath his vestments. Cold sweat beaded at his temples.

How is this man still standing? He should be hovering between life and death!

The black magic that had enacted the divine punishment was agony beyond what even seasoned warlocks could endure.

After a moment's confusion, Paul checked the Oath Stone and recovered his composure.

"Ahem! Katzen's rightful master has arrived at last!"

He exchanged a meaningful glance with Rachel. The imperial crown rested upon a velvet cushion at the altar—once it adorned Cesare's head, he would become sovereign of this great empire.

How could they surrender such a prize now?

Paul spread his arms in welcome as Cesare approached.

"In lieu of the High Priest of Katzen, I shall personally have the honor of crowning—"

But Cesare walked past him without a glance.

His steps halted before the Oath Stone.

Murmurs rippled through the assembly. The prince had bypassed the crowning ceremony entirely.

"Has he confused the order of proceedings?"

"What on earth is he thinking?"

Amid the whispered confusion, Peleus watched with cold eyes as Rachel curtsied demurely before Cesare.

If not for my sister's burden, I would have taken Dea and departed this wretched land the moment we arrived.

"I am truly overjoyed to see Your Majesty in good health."

When Cesare turned from Medea and approached her, Rachel bit the inside of her cheek to suppress her elation.

"If Your Majesty follows the will of the Goddess, all present suffering shall be washed away. This kingdom will flourish eternally under your reign."

She bowed her head, one hand resting upon her heart—the gesture of a noblewoman rather than a priestess.

"I, Rachel, shall wholeheartedly support Your Majesty—as your companion and as the Goddess's representative. Will you permit me to complete the ceremony?"

A faint smile curved her lips.

The oath ceremony, interrupted by Cesare's dramatic entrance, was about to resume—

"You are all aware, I trust, of what this Oath Stone has meant to Katzen since the founding of our nation."

A leisurely voice stopped Rachel mid-motion.

Cesare was smiling.

It was a smile so beautiful it stole the breath from all who beheld it.

"I shall be the first to swear upon this sacred stone. What say you, Saintess?"

"If that is Your Majesty's will, then I must follow."

Rachel inclined her head and stepped back from the altar.

Cesare's golden gaze swept over the Oath Stone.

He already understood Medea's plan—why she had permitted Rachel's name to be entered into the imperial registry.

"...The First King carved a rock imbued with the power of the Beginning and made it the imperial throne. That sacred oath shall contain only truth beneath the breath of the Goddess."

If this stone tablet truly contained the sanctity of the Katzen imperial bloodline—if it held the great power that had protected the realm for centuries—

Then the moment Rachel carved her name upon it, the stone would sense and reject the dark corruption within her.

That is precisely what you intended, Dea.

The collision between the sacred Oath Stone and Rachel—who claimed to be the Goddess's representative—would create suspicion too profound to dismiss.

The power of the Oath Stone was known only to Medea, a direct descendant of the royal line who had lived through two lifetimes. The black magicians and Rachel could never have anticipated it.

But there remained one problem.

Cesare himself.

Even if it serves a calculated purpose—I cannot tolerate your name being erased from this stone, or that woman's being inscribed as my companion.

Rachel had stolen everything from Medea in her past life.

He would not permit his wife's fate to be usurped again—not for a single moment in this one.

In the end, what matters is forcing a clash between the imperial power and the black magic, is it not?

There was another way—one that would trigger the reaction without requiring Rachel's name to replace Medea's.

You may not agree with my methods, Dea. But the result will be the same.

He would succeed upon the path she had discovered.

Even if it cost him everything—so long as it spared her any harm.

Cesare placed his hand upon the stone slab.

"I, Cesare Dwisler Katzen, swear an unbreakable oath upon this sacred stone—where the eternal covenant between the First King and his companion is forever inscribed."

An unbreakable oath?

The assembled guests held their breath, eyes wide with astonishment.

"This shall be an eternal vow, spoken in the name of truth—one that even the divine cannot alter."

He drew his blade and sliced across his own palm.

A drop of blood welled from the wound and fell upon the ancient stone.

"...Ah!"

Rachel could not contain a small cry of delight.

She whipped her head toward Medea, violet eyes blazing with unbearable joy.

"Did you hear that? Your husband has declared our union an eternal covenant!"

What honor! What magnificence—to have forged an unbreakable oath that surpassed even the promise of the Founding Emperor!

It rendered the first oracle utterly insignificant by comparison.

How terrified he must have been of divine punishment, to have completely forgotten the vow he once made to you.

And that original oath had burdened Medea until her dying breath.

While Rachel floated in her ecstasy—

Upon the Oath Stone, Cesare's blood spread where holy oil should have been.

What Medea did not know: Cesare understood that the divinity imbued within the stone responded with far greater power to the blood of his lineage.

The crimson droplet seeped across the name of the Emperor.

SHHHHK—!

The tablet of the oracle unfurled across the stone slab.

SLAM!

Cesare's dagger plunged into the parchment, pinning it to the stone.

His voice rang through the cathedral like a blade unsheathed.

"My companion is only
Medea Valdina Katzen.
No one else can take her place."

What?

Rachel's smile froze upon her face.

At the pinnacle of her elation, it was as though a boulder had plummeted from the heavens and crushed her joy to dust.

"What... what..."

She stared at Cesare, the corners of her lips still twitching upward in a rictus of disbelief.

No—she stared at the violent maelstrom of golden flame surrounding him, watching as his oath carved itself into the ancient stone.

"What are you talking about?! AAAAAH!"

Rachel's face contorted—twisted into something demonic.
