Chapter 338

The Dead
Return

"Reports of my death have been greatly exaggerated."

Perdiccas II regarded the Third Prince with calculating eyes.

His eldest son, Cesare, had vanished on the glacier. The First Princess who had gone after him had likewise disappeared without trace.

Tch. I had not planned to name a Crown Prince so soon...

Yet with the First Prince's death all but confirmed, public sentiment had begun to waver dangerously. The empire's borders grew restless as neighboring nations sensed weakness.

The Third Prince was far from ideal—but hesitation was no longer an option.

"Of course, Father!"

Juan's agreement came with eager haste.

"Entrust this responsibility to me, and I swear I shall never betray Your Majesty's expectations."

Behind his measured words, his pupils blazed with barely contained fire.

The heavens have granted me another chance!

Cesare—that insurmountable mountain, that obstacle I could never hope to climb—has finally crumbled to nothing.

And now fortune compounds upon fortune, each stroke of luck building upon the last.

Juan struggled to suppress the smile threatening to split his face.

Perhaps my luck was never poor at all. Perhaps it was simply... blocked. By that damned bastard Cesare.

The moment I learned of his death, my path became crystal clear.

His eyes, carefully schooled to hide his elation, sought out a familiar figure across the chamber.

Mother.

Their gazes met across the throne room.

Shortly after the announcement of Cesare's disappearance, Perdiccas II had ordered the Western Empress released from her tower prison. A calculated decision—if he intended to elevate Juan, he could hardly keep the boy's mother locked away as a criminal.

The woman who emerged bore little resemblance to the proud, elegant empress of years past. The tower had stripped away her arrogance, leaving behind something hollow and watchful.

Do not worry, Mother. Every humiliation, every indignity you suffered—I will repay them all.

Take care of yourself, my son.

The Western Empress inclined her head almost imperceptibly, acknowledging the message in her son's eyes. She could read the resentment burning there—hatred for the father who treated all his children as mere pieces on a game board.

Rest assured. I understand exactly how merciless Father can be.

Juan had no intention of being caught off guard. He schooled his features back into the mask of a dutiful son and bowed low before the Emperor.

"Then it is settled. We shall proceed with His Royal Highness the First Prince's funeral before the coronation ceremony. Does anyone object?"

A collective sigh rippled through the assembled court.

Life and death, it seemed, could exchange places in the span of a single breath. Who could have predicted this reversal? The First Prince—so vital, so invincible—now to be mourned. And the Third Prince, whose prospects had seemed utterly ruined, suddenly risen to unprecedented heights.

CRASH!

The great doors flew open, slamming against stone with thunderous force.

A figure stood silhouetted against the blazing light of the corridor beyond. His shadow stretched impossibly long across the marble floor, reaching toward the throne like an accusation.

"Strange, is it not? To plan a funeral for someone who is very much alive."

The man who stepped forward radiated an aura of barely contained violence—as though he had clawed his way back from the depths of hell itself and brought the cold fury of that place with him.

Those who looked closely could almost see the phantom of a blizzard swirling at his back.

"Your Highness! The First Prince!"

"How—how is this possible?!"

"He lives! He lives!"

Gasps and exclamations erupted throughout the chamber—a cacophony of shock, disbelief, and for some, undisguised joy.

"Juan."

Cesare's golden eyes found his half-brother, and a smile curved his lips—sharp and utterly devoid of warmth.

"What a pity. Your elder brother has returned to reclaim what was briefly within your grasp."

The casual mockery in his voice, the complete absence of anything resembling regret, made Juan's blood boil.

"...Explain yourself. What happened?"

Perdiccas II's voice emerged flat and controlled as he faced his eldest son—the son who had, against all odds, returned from certain death.

Cesare shrugged with practiced indifference.

"Those who wish me dead are hardly in short supply, Father. You know this as well as anyone. The attempt was crude—no different from countless others. Though I will concede that this particular assassin showed... somewhat more commitment than usual."

His gaze slid meaningfully toward the Western Empress.

The cold edge in that look—like a blade pressed against tender flesh—made her visibly flinch.

"But then, you already know all about that. Don't you?"

The warlock who had driven a poisoned blade into his back had been her creature. Her operative.

Perdiccas II regarded his eldest son in silence.

A strange cocktail of emotions churned behind his faded eyes—relief mingled with contempt, pride wrestling with something darker.

Part of him felt genuine gladness. Cesare was, after all, the most brilliant of his children—the one most likely to make Katzen the undisputed master of the continent. To have him return alive from the very threshold of death was nothing short of miraculous.

But another part of him—a twisted, fearful part—recoiled from that same tenacity.

He was born with a will to survive as stubborn as my own.

And the moment he acknowledged that resilience, anxiety raised its ugly head once more.

As always, his warped nature won out.

Marquis Leonard stepped forward, his face alight with relief at seeing Cesare alive.

"Your Majesty, the mastermind behind this assassination attempt clearly resides within the palace walls. A thorough investigation must be conducted at once."

"Your Majesty, we implore you—issue the command."

The First Prince's vassals closed ranks behind their lord. The master of the Façade and the formidable war mage stood at attention. Disciples of the Mage Tower and commanders of mercenary companies alike pressed forward, their combined presence forming an unmistakable wall of pressure directed at the throne.

All of them loyal to Cesare. All of them demanding justice.

Anger and jealousy surged through Perdiccas II's chest.

He refused to believe their accusations. No—he refused to even consider them.

"You returned alive. That should be sufficient."

His voice carried the same cold indifference he had shown Cesare since childhood.

No investigation would be permitted. No inquiry convened.

It was the same dismissive reaction he had displayed upon first learning of his son's disappearance—and now, it served as a silent declaration: the Third Prince and the Western Empress would face no consequences.

"Your Majesty! How can you—"

"Silence! How much more chaos does he intend to bring upon my empire?"

The Emperor's voice cracked like a whip.

"Had he not ventured onto that accursed glacier in the first place, none of this would have occurred! Do you have any conception of how many affairs of state were delayed? How much manpower and treasure was squandered in the search for him?"

The court fell into frozen silence.

Had the Emperor's hostility toward his own son truly reached such depths?

Every eye turned to the First Prince.

Surely even he would break under the weight of such paternal cruelty? A father who cared nothing for his son's survival—who actively shielded those who had tried to murder him?

Yet Cesare's expression remained utterly impassive.

Only those who looked closely might have noticed the deep, resigned shadow that had settled into his golden eyes. The look of a man who had long since stopped expecting anything different.

The tension in the chamber threatened to spiral beyond control—

"If I may be so bold as to pose a question, Your Majesty."

The voice was soft, almost weary, yet it cut through the chaos with startling clarity.

Medea stepped forward from where she had been standing quietly at Cesare's side.

Perdiccas II's frown deepened as his gaze fell upon her. The journey had clearly been arduous, yet she appeared somehow more composed than when he had last seen her—a strange serenity settling over her pale features.

"If this incident was not merely an attempt on the Crown Prince's life..."

Medea's hand drifted to rest upon her belly, the gesture deliberate and unmistakable.

"...but also constituted an attempt to assassinate an imperial grandchild—a direct assault upon the continuation of the imperial bloodline itself—would that not alter the situation considerably?"

The Revelation

A New Heir

The First Princess's words fell upon the court like a thunderbolt.

"Your... Your Highness. What... what did you just..."

Voices erupted in a confused tumult. The murmuring swelled into open exclamations, punctuated by gasps and—from somewhere in the crowd—what sounded distinctly like hiccups of shock.

Perdiccas II was no exception.

"What... what did you mean by that?"

His beard trembled visibly.

Medea lowered her eyes demurely and inclined her head in confirmation.

"Summon the physician! Immediately! Where is the palace doctor?!"

The Emperor's roar echoed through the chamber. His eyes had gone wide, bloodshot with sudden intensity.

The palace physician was summoned with all haste. He examined the First Princess with trembling hands, acutely aware of every pair of eyes fixed upon him.

When he straightened at last, his face had broken into an enormous smile.

"Congratulations, Your Majesty! The First Princess is indeed with child!"

Perdiccas II's eyes went wide.

An imperial grandchild. A direct heir to Cesare's line.

Everything had changed.

The dead had returned from the glacier.

And they had brought with them the promise of new life—

a weapon more powerful than any sword.
