Chapter XI

Ghosts
& Crowns

"The dead do not rest easy when their work remains unfinished."

"I will make you Emperor."

Those words echoed through Cesare's mind as he stood alone in the gallery, surrounded by portraits of dead monarchs.

She had returned to this empire—this Katzen, where the scars of her previous life still festered beneath the surface. And once again, the brutal machinery of succession had begun to turn.

The guilt of having drawn Medea back into this perilous game weighed upon Cesare's chest like a stone. It had been there since the moment she set foot on Katzen soil.

Always.

For Medea, Katzen is nothing but wounds. A festering nest of vipers where the blade of power lurks behind every smile.

This cruel empire—which even he could not bring himself to abandon—was no place for her.

She deserves a world where she can forget. Where she can rest. Where she need prove nothing to anyone.

Somewhere warm. Somewhere calm. Somewhere she can simply... breathe.

He knew this truth better than anyone.

And yet—was it not he who had led her back here?

The question haunted him. It returned each time he looked at her, each time she risked herself for his sake.

"..."

Cesare lifted his gaze to the portrait of the First Emperor that dominated the wall before him.

Even rendered in oils and gilt, the throne blazed with an almost supernatural brilliance.

Fragments of memory flickered across his golden eyes like light through broken glass.

A childhood spent in the imperial palace's cold shadow.

The lonely estate of Romagna.

His first battlefield, where blood taught him what words never could.

Years of hardship. Medals earned. Scars collected.

Until at last—the title of First Prince.

The reason I endured. The summit I clawed my way toward.

Strange, how at this moment, that glittering throne no longer seemed as radiant as it once had.

He looked down at his hands.

Katzen... or Medea.

If I could choose only one—

"...There are some things that cannot be surrendered."

The creak of hinges broke his reverie.

Gallo's head appeared around the door frame, his expression somewhere between relief and exasperation.

"There you are, my lord. I've been searching everywhere. Her Highness is ready to depart—shall we return to the palace together?"

He stepped inside, boots echoing against marble.

"...Romagna is too austere. And Alpanon's weather is perpetually dreary."

"I beg your pardon? What's brought this on so suddenly?"

Cesare's gaze remained fixed on the painted throne, his lips curving into something almost wistful.

"Gallo."

"Yes, my lord?"

"What do you suppose independence would look like?"

"...What?"

Gallo stared at him as though he had begun speaking in tongues.

Whether or not Cesare noticed his companion's shock, his voice remained perfectly indifferent as he turned away from the portrait.

"Somewhere warmer. More peaceful than this place."

In the days following the Emperor's birthday celebration, the aftermath of that disastrous evening continued to ripple through the imperial palace.

For Perdiccas II, however, mere scandal was no longer his primary concern.

"Your Majesty, we have confirmed that the ceremonial sword was among the tributes presented three years ago."

"Meaning the Western Empress appropriated imperial tribute for herself."

As it happened, the estate that had offered the tribute belonged to vassals aligned with the Third Prince's faction.

"Further discrepancies have been discovered between official records and actual inventory. The investigation continues."

The Western Empress could never have imagined that this trifling keepsake—secretly set aside for her son—would transform into damning evidence at precisely the worst possible moment.

"Once the full findings are compiled, Your Majesty—"

"Hmph! This cannot be an isolated incident. How long has that woman been conducting her affairs behind my back?"

The Emperor's fury erupted like a volcano.

To be stabbed in the back by a political ally—one with whom he had joined hands specifically to contain the First Prince—the wound to his pride festered with each passing hour.

"And that man—Alfred! Is he still in Katzen? Cast him into the abyss! How dare he make a fool of me, parading about with such insufferable arrogance?"

The image of Alfred shielding the Western Empress in the banquet hall—standing before the throne as though he had every right—remained seared into Perdiccas II's memory like a brand.

"Your Majesty, the situation remains delicate. There is no concrete evidence proving a definitive relationship between the Grand Duke and Her Majesty. Moreover, he currently holds diplomatic status."

Even an emperor could not execute a foreign envoy on impulse without considering the international repercussions.

Furthermore, the Third Prince's faction had mounted a vigorous defense over the past several days.

Knowing that the Western Empress's fall would drag them down with her, they feared the Emperor's wrath—yet they pressed him relentlessly to show clemency.

"You still haven't found definitive proof? Am I to simply wait while this humiliation festers?"

Perdiccas II's voice cracked with barely contained rage.

"However, Your Majesty, there is something else that demands your attention."

The chief attendant's voice dropped.

"During the search of the Western Palace... we discovered this."

He turned over a small badge, revealing the insignia engraved upon its face.

A crimson fox.

"What?"

Perdiccas II shot to his feet, his face draining of color.

He had believed he would never see that symbol again.

The Red Fox. Emblem of the clandestine spy network once operated by the late Duke Kensington.

"The agents I failed to eliminate three years ago... they still live?"

He remembered the day his knights had stormed the Red Foxes' headquarters—only to find it abandoned, swept clean of any trace.

Subsequent pursuit teams had failed, one after another. Each failure had gnawed at the Emperor's composure.

I had convinced myself that with Kensington gone, they would have disbanded. Scattered to the winds.

"And all this time, they've been nesting in my palace like vermin!"

Had evidence of adultery been found, it would have been preferable.

The discovery of Red Fox traces within the imperial palace meant something far more sinister: that Kensington's specter had never truly departed.

Right here. At the very heart of his power.

A spark of suspicion ignited in Perdiccas II's mind.

"Was the Western Empress collaborating with Kensington?"

Or perhaps—

"Is Kensington truly dead?"

The Emperor's face contorted with paranoid fury.

"Your Majesty, I personally verified the Duke's death. There can be no mistake."

The chief attendant spoke with quiet certainty. The dead did not return.

"He's returned from the grave! Kensington harbored a vicious hatred for me—and now he strikes from beyond death itself!"

But the Emperor's frenzy had moved beyond the reach of reason.

"He means to take my throne!"

The golden staff in his hand arced through the air and shattered a gilded statue.

When the sedative the chief attendant had desperately administered finally took effect, the floor was littered with broken ornaments.

The madness and terror that had blazed in the Emperor's eyes had dimmed—but they had not vanished entirely.

The chief attendant swallowed silently.

Whatever the truth may be, as long as Kensington's name is involved, the Western Empress cannot hope to escape.

He recalled that the Emperor sometimes spoke of nightmares—dreams in which Kensington's shattered skull turned toward him, eyes burning with undying hatred.

In those dreams, not only his enemies appeared, but also the loyal servants he had unjustly purged.

"Alfred... the Western Empress... Kensington... Juan..."

The Emperor's fingers drummed against the armrest of his throne.

"What if the reason Catherine has shown such devoted support for Juan... was to further some vendetta against me?"

And if she had reached out to Kensington's spy network for that purpose—

The veins on the back of his hand bulged as his grip tightened.

In trying to contain Cesare, I allowed Juan to grow too powerful.

I cultivated a monster in my second son while obsessing over the first.

And now—what if Catherine turns that weapon against me?

The memory of Alfred's defiant face flashed before him.

"Transfer the Western Empress to the prison. No visitors are to be permitted."

The Emperor's voice was harsh as shattered glass.

"Not even Juan. And as for Count Montfort—demote his title to Viscount, effective immediately."

Word of the Emperor's decree spread like wildfire.

The Third Prince's faction descended into chaos.

They had anticipated that the Western Empress's punishment would be temporary—a brief period of disgrace followed by quiet rehabilitation.

Instead, her sentence had grown more severe with each passing day.

To strip her father of his title on the grounds of his daughter's alleged infidelity—

It was an irreparable humiliation.

Even if the Western Empress somehow clawed her way back to favor, this disgrace would follow her like a shadow for the rest of her days.

A punishment designed not merely to wound—but to annihilate.

End Chapter XI
