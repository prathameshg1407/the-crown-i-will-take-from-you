Chapter 335

Where Starlight
Falls

"In the quiet between heartbeats, two souls finally found their way home."

Starlight spilled like liquid silver across the glacier, the surrounding peaks draped in mantles of pristine snow.

The mountains held their silence close, broken only by the occasional rustle of creatures in the underbrush, the soft crackle and pop of burning kindling. The bonfire's warmth rose to embrace their visible breath, turning it to gossamer wisps that vanished into the darkness.

It was a night heavy with solitude and profound stillness—the kind that made one feel as though they were the last souls remaining in all the world.

And then, gradually, the fingers that had been clutching Cesare's sleeve went slack.

Those luminous green eyes—always so watchful, so guarded—had finally drifted closed. The soft rhythm of her breathing whispered against his chest.

Cesare remained motionless, gazing down at the woman who had fallen asleep in his arms.

In all the time he had known her, this was the first moment Medea had allowed herself to be so completely vulnerable in his presence.

The bonfire's gentle flames cast dancing shadows beneath the sweep of her dark lashes. The warmth of her body pressed against his was almost unbearably sweet.

Like a single lamp illuminating an endless darkness, this small, precious being in his arms had the power to thaw even the most frozen chambers of his heart.

"Medea."

The soft sound of her breathing was the only response—and somehow, that was enough. More than enough.

Cesare drew her closer, gathering her more securely into his embrace. He brushed the loose strands of silver hair from her face and pressed a gentle kiss to her forehead.

She was so fragile. Hold too tightly and she might shatter; loosen his grip for even a moment and she might slip away like water through his fingers.

His hands trembled slightly, though whether from the effort of restraint or the depth of his emotion, he could not say.

The night deepened around them. The flames began to fade, their dancing light growing dim, and the mountain cold crept closer, pressing against exposed skin with icy fingers.

Cesare rose carefully, cradling her in his arms, and made his way toward the cottage.

The door swung open to reveal only silence. No presence stirred within—the house stood empty and still.

His eyes found a note scrawled hastily on the wooden table:

"An emergency has arisen—a patient in the village beyond the eastern ridge requires our attention. We shall return in a few days. Please make use of our home and the medicinal supplies as you see fit. Consider it payment for your eventual treatment."

— Mr. & Mrs. Jenkins

Cesare had sensed their departure earlier, while he sat before the campfire with Medea in his arms.

I assumed they would return before long.

"A few days..."

It seemed they would have to wait for Gallo and the search party to find their way here.

He climbed the stairs in silence, each weathered step groaning beneath his weight. The sound echoed through the unusually quiet house like a heartbeat.

The bedroom door yielded to his touch, revealing a small but comfortable space suffused with warmth. The scent of dried herbs lingered in the air, and a neatly made bed awaited them.

With infinite care, Cesare lowered Medea onto the soft sheets. He drew the blanket up over her shoulders, tucking it gently around her sleeping form.

He was just beginning to straighten—just starting to pull away—when slender arms wound around his neck and dragged him down.

Their lips met.

Or perhaps it was the weight of his body settling over hers.

In the haze of that moment, Medea could not tell which came first.

"You—!"

Golden fire erupted in his eyes, blazing like the sun at its zenith.

With a sharp intake of breath, Cesare clenched his jaw and forced his arms straight, creating a cage of distance between their entangled bodies.

But Medea's hands refused to release him.

She could hear him curse beneath his breath—something low and fervent, half prayer and half blasphemy.

"If you truly don't want this... tell me now."

Medea lifted her chin and pressed her lips to the sharp line of his jaw.

A sound escaped him—something caught between a groan of pleasure and a sigh of utter despair.

The dutiful husband who had intended only to put his wife gently to bed had clearly not anticipated this turn of events.

"I... I know what I... what this..."

Medea watched as he struggled to form coherent words, his teeth grinding together with the effort of restraint. Something in her chest ached at the sight of him pulling away, the warmth of his body fading.

When she tried to rise after him, his hand pressed firmly against her shoulder—a gentle but unyielding weight that held her in place.

"Wait. Medea. Not here—"

He dragged a hand down his face, and when he spoke again, his words tumbled out in fragments, as though his usually ordered mind had scattered to pieces.

"I said I would wait. I meant it. But my patience has limits—no, that's not—what I mean is—"

Not here. Not like this—not for the first time.

This moment, the one they had both been waiting for, the culmination of so much longing and restraint—it deserved more than a borrowed cabin in the wilderness.

Even amid the beauty of snow-covered peaks and peaceful solitude, this humble cottage felt... inadequate.

But in his determination to give her something worthy, he had forgotten one crucial detail.

The choice had never been his alone to make.

"Why wait? If that's what troubles you... then don't."

Medea reached up and turned his face toward hers, forcing their eyes to meet.

In truth, she had been awake from the moment he lifted her into his arms and carried her from the fireside. She had felt every careful step, every tender adjustment of his hold.

And deep within her heart, emotions surged like waves against a shore—too powerful now to deny or suppress.

She no longer wished to look away from what she felt.

She wanted him. Cesare Katzen.

She wanted to confirm, in the most undeniable way possible, that they were bound to each other—that this life, this fate, was entirely different from the nightmare she had lived before.

Their lips met again, soft and searching.

"Cesare. Don't go."

In that instant, something like the light of dawn

broke across his golden eyes.

Surprise. Joy. A hope so fierce it bordered on pain.

No one else will ever see him like this.

She made that decision in the space of a heartbeat.

The strongest man in the world. The most beautiful. And he looked at her as though she were his entire universe.

He belonged to her—completely, irrevocably.

"Tonight... I want to be with you. Only you."

The words fell from her lips like a request.

But in truth, they were a command.

Crack.

It was the sound of the last thread of his restraint

finally snapping.

In an instant, they were tangled together—limbs intertwined, breath mingling, the boundaries between them dissolving like morning frost.

Amid the overwhelming tide of sensation, Medea found herself thinking, distantly, that perhaps she should have been more careful about awakening something so long restrained.

"Ah—!"

Heat bloomed across her cheeks, and her nails carved desperate crescents into the broad expanse of his back.

"Dea."

His voice was rough velvet against her ear as he brushed the damp hair from her forehead. The fire in his gaze never dimmed—not for a single moment.

He kissed away everything—tears, perspiration, every trace of her past sorrows—as though he intended to consume them all and leave nothing behind but the present.

Her thoughts dissolved into molten gold. Whenever the fear arose that she might lose herself entirely, her hands found his, their fingers intertwining like vows.

A time of warmth. Of tenderness. Of indelible marks left upon each other's souls.

There were moments she wanted to sink her teeth into his shoulder, moments she wanted to flee from the overwhelming weight of his devotion.

But his arms would not release her. And she found, to her wonder, that she did not truly wish them to.

So this is what it means... to be truly connected to another heart.

A Few Days Later

The Light of Morning

The cheerful chorus of birdsong drew Medea slowly from the depths of sleep.

When her eyes finally opened, she found herself alone in the room once more.

"...This is madness..."

The words escaped her in a dazed murmur.

"How many days has it been since we've left this room?"

Her fingers drifted to her lips. They felt swollen, tender, slightly chapped from overuse.

It was as though someone had taken the world she thought she understood, shattered it into a thousand pieces, and reassembled it into something entirely new. Everything felt strange and unfamiliar—and yet somehow, inexplicably right.

I never knew. I never imagined physical intimacy could feel like... this.

Why did I ever believe I loved Jason?

Whatever I felt for him in my previous life... it was never anything close to this.

At that moment, the door creaked open.

A man entered—devastatingly handsome in a way that made her breath catch even now, even after everything.

"Good morning, Dea."

His voice was low and unhurried, settling through the languorous air like honey. Morning sunlight streamed through the window to crown his dark hair with threads of gold.

His shirt hung loosely unbuttoned, revealing the sculpted planes of his chest and abdomen—a body that might have been carved by divine hands with particular attention to detail.

Medea fixed him with a glare.

If every muscle in her body weren't aching with the evidence of recent exertion, she would have thrown something at him.

But he approached anyway, utterly unrepentant, and leaned down to press his cheek against hers. His hair, still slightly damp, brushed cool against her shoulder.

She pushed at his chest, heat flooding her face.

Before she could attempt to rise from the bed, his arms wrapped around her, holding her firmly in place.

"What are you doing back here?"

Her struggles stilled abruptly.

Fresh crimson was seeping through the bandages wrapped around his chest.

"Nothing serious. Is the temperature acceptable?"

He dismissed her concern with practiced ease, dropping light kisses along the curve of her neck, the sensitive skin at her temple.

And then, before she could protest further, he was lifting her—carrying her toward the corner of the room where a wooden bathtub sat waiting, steam rising from the water within.

He lowered her gently into the warmth. The water embraced her aching limbs, fragrant with medicinal herbs, and she could not entirely suppress the sigh of relief that escaped her.

"...You may leave now."

Medea's command emerged somewhat less forcefully than intended.

At the corner of his mouth, a smile appeared.

"But surely Your Highness requires assistance."

There was laughter hiding in his low voice as he watched her shift in the water, her movements still awkward and uncertain—like a newborn fawn finding its legs.

He leaned close enough to press another kiss to her damp cheek, the gesture impossibly tender for a man with such dangerous hands.

When she turned her head away, flustered, he simply produced a soft sponge, already worked into a gentle lather.

"Shall I wash your hair?"

"You? As if you've ever done such a thing in your life."

Medea scoffed, though her voice lacked its usual edge.

The image of Cesare—proud, imperious Cesare—carefully attending to someone's bath was so absurd it defied imagination.

The corners of his eyes crinkled with amusement.

"You should know by now... I excel at everything I attempt. From the very first try."

His gaze, playful yet still holding that banked hunger from the night before, traveled slowly across her bare shoulders.

SPLASH—!

The wet sponge struck the side of the tub and tumbled to the floor.

"...Out. Now."

Despite the sharpness of her tone, her ears and cheeks had flushed a deep, telling pink.

"As you command. I suppose I must content myself with preparing Your Highness's breakfast instead."

Cesare's quiet laughter filled the room as he pressed one final kiss to her wet cheek before retreating.

The door clicked shut behind him.

Medea sank lower into the bath, submerging herself until only her eyes remained above the waterline.

Steam rose around her in gentle spirals, but she suspected the heat in her face had little to do with the temperature of the water.

Everything had changed.

And yet, somehow, everything finally felt exactly as it should be.
