Chapter 258

The Goddess Strikes Again

"When defiance meets divine wrath, even conviction cannot shield the condemned."

Terence's pointed observation carried an edge Leniard couldn't ignore.

"Our lord Cesare has issued no orders yet. Her Highness has said nothing either."

"You frustrating fool! Don't you comprehend how critical this moment is? Moreover, how can we be certain Her Highness's opinion truly serves our lord's interests?"

Marquis Leniard pressed his fingertips to his temple, adopting a rational, warning tone.

"Circumstances have changed. His Highness's ascension to the throne may not necessarily benefit Her Highness. If she harbors conflicting intentions, can you handle her cunning and capabilities?"

If Cesare ascended the throne, diplomatic pressure from the Holy Kingdom and continental politics could force him into marriage with the Saintess.

From the First Princess's perspective, wouldn't she want to prevent her husband's coronation entirely?

"This senile fool—I should have torn that venomous tongue from your mouth long ago."

"Gallo!"

Gallo, who'd been listening with mounting fury, strode toward Marquis Leniard with clenched fists.

Nevertheless, the Marquis didn't flinch.

"The only certainty I possess now is bloodline. Those who wish solely for His Highness's wellbeing without ulterior motives."

Haven't we witnessed countless times over the years that even exceptional individuals like the Western Empress are, in certain respects, profoundly selfish?

"Let us prepare for the coronation. I'll inform His Highness myself."

Terence, Gallo, and the imperial attendants said nothing—an implicit refusal.

"These stubborn fools, really—"

"What touching loyalty."

A cool voice cut through the tension.

"Your Highness...!"

It was Cesare.

Marquis Leniard reflexively reached toward him.

"Is your condition stable? How do you feel?"

Cesare merely looked down at him with indifference.

A cold gaze that permitted no proximity.

Recognizing the distance being enforced, Leniard hesitated, lowered his hand, and stepped back.

"Your Highness."

Only then did he notice Medea beside Cesare. Leniard bowed deeply.

His heart hammered like a war drum. Had they overheard his earlier warnings?

"You're not entirely wrong."

Cesare's voice continued.

"It would be wasteful to simply hand over this prize you've guarded so diligently to others."

"Yes, Your Highness. We've been preparing for the coronation extensively. It won't require much time."

He politely outlined his future plans.

While mourning Perdiccas II's death, he emphasized the importance of ensuring the First Prince didn't appear to be seizing the throne too hastily before continental observers.

It was a strategy worthy of Katzen's diplomatic finesse and balance.

"I'll dispatch royal invitations to kingdoms across the continent, including Valdina, along with formal announcements. Considering the coronation's significance..."

Marquis Leniard paused momentarily before completing his thought.

"The Holy Kingdom cannot be excluded either."

"Your Highness, I cannot permit you to defy the oracle again and suffer further consequences. What an opportune moment this presents..."

However, Cesare didn't miss the gleam of satisfaction in the old man's eyes.

He lifted the corners of his mouth in a stiff approximation of a smile.

"An excellent suggestion. The Marquis has grasped the situation shrewdly."

At Cesare's apparent approval, Leniard exhaled with relief.

Marquis Leniard felt certain Cesare had also heard the warning he'd issued to the First Princess moments ago.

Yet the fact that he dared not mention it now meant Cesare's heart had shifted as well.

'Well, that's how power works. No matter how besotted you are with your wife, you have no choice but to adapt.'

Only by resolving the divine punishment could he safely occupy the throne.

This had been true for Perdiccas II, and for countless rulers who'd preceded him.

Cesare, who surpassed them all in ambition, would prove no exception—or so Leniard convinced himself.

"State your command. Your Highness... no, according to Your Highness's will, I shall ensure this transition is accepted naturally—not merely by the empire and its people, but across the continent."

Leniard bowed deeply.

"Indeed. In that case, I'll entrust you with the role of continental ambassador."

"Your Highness?"

Marquis Leniard looked up sharply. An impassive gaze regarded him.

"What do you mean?"

"As you noted, we require someone to facilitate our diplomacy. The most critical relationship at present is Valdina, and in this delicate situation, you alone, Marquis, possess the credibility to negotiate with them."

The coronation ceremony loomed imminent.

At such a juncture, assigning him a task that could easily be delegated to a junior official was absurd.

'This means he won't grant me any honor whatsoever.'

Moreover, the person he'd be meeting was from Valdina.

The fearsome reputation of Valdina's Emperor—known as the Holy Slayer—was well-documented even to Marquis Leniard.

Who would bear the brunt of his fury toward Cesare, who'd abandoned his sister to choose a Saintess?

Why dare assign me a mission where survival itself is uncertain...

"If you're going to undertake this, you might as well bring your entire clan. This won't be simple—in the end, bloodline is all one can truly trust."

Cesare threw Leniard's earlier words back at him, simultaneously expressing his intent to exile the Marquis from the capital entirely.

"Your Highness! Are you severing me?"

Marquis Leniard could no longer suppress his outrage.

"Have you forgotten what I've endured for Your Highness? If my words and actions were problematic, you should have warned me! The insult of cutting off your most loyal supporter and callously expelling me—!"

"Did you require a warning?"

The cool voice rang with finality.

"An incompetent subject who cannot discern his monarch's will should naturally be removed. Haven't you learned even that much over the years?"

Cesare's eyes were frigid as glacial ice.

In that moment, Marquis Leniard realized Cesare knew everything.

The desires he harbored. The future he'd envisioned.

And even the pressure he'd applied to the First Princess days ago.

"Your Highness..."

"Was it presumptuous of me to expect you'd recognize your own limitations?"

Bitter laughter edged his words.

"Leniard, do you wish to claim kinship with me through blood?"

The sharp question carried no trace of warmth.

"If bloodline is what matters, I have high expectations indeed."

Cesare walked slowly toward the throne and raised his hand.

Along the throne's backing, bloodstains that hadn't been completely wiped away smeared across his fingertips.

"My grandfather—who shared no blood with me—created this vacancy by eliminating my biological father."

Marquis Leniard's face drained of color.

"Then you, who share my blood... what can you offer me?"

"What... what are you saying..."

Like light suddenly flooding a darkened room, details that had escaped notice began crystallizing in the Marquis's awareness.

Perdiccas II's undisclosed body. The sealed palace gates. The faint scent of blood that had lingered since he'd entered the Imperial Palace.

The strange expressions on the imperial attendants' and Cesare's retinue's faces...

"It wasn't an accident... So Eratosthenes... In the end... His Majesty..."

A sound emerged from Leniard—neither quite moan nor sigh.

Eratosthenes had truly been a different caliber of man.

Until the very end, he'd departed without greed or regret.

"Eratosthenes... he..."

Perhaps I never could.

As he did.

He couldn't kill the Emperor for Cesare's sake and bear that sin, nor possess the conviction to disappear quietly afterward.

Even if the blood flowing through that young man matched his own.

What use were bloodlines?

Once the shallowness of his devotion had been exposed, Marquis Leniard no longer even qualified to claim kinship.

"Your Highness, I..."

He came to his senses as though ice water had been thrown over him.

Only then did Leniard comprehend the foolish, false dreams he'd been nurturing.

"Your Highness, please forgive my overreach. I was blinded momentarily."

Even in this, Eratosthenes was correct.

The Vow Unbroken

"I'll state this plainly. Engrave it in your mind."

Cesare turned briefly, making eye contact with Medea.

"No matter what state I occupy or where I sit, my will shall not waver."

"Abandon the notion of secretly imposing that oracle. Rachel becoming my companion is out of the question—"

At that instant, massive crimson light erupted.

Cesare's body went rigid.

"Even if the world turns upside down, it will not be overturned."

"No! Why...!"

Simultaneously, Terence's scream rang out.

Cesare looked back. His eyes found Medea's.

Crack!

Medea watched the enormous divine whip coil around him once more.

"Cesare!"

Where the light faded, he collapsed to the floor.

Bearing fresh scars from divine punishment.

"Damn it, this is your fault! You said something that shouldn't have been spoken! Our lord barely regained consciousness...!"

"I... I..."

Marquis Leniard's face, gripped by Gallo's iron hands, turned ashen.

While Cesare had been speaking, divine punishment had struck again. He couldn't shake the conviction that it was his fault.

"I'm sorry. I'm..."

"Gallo, stop."

"You witnessed it too! Why did our lord, who was fine, suddenly collapse again? Why is the Goddess punishing him once more?"

Gallo erupted with fury.

Amid the chaos, only Medea remained composed.

"Move Cesare immediately. We must keep his unconsciousness secret."

No one could know.

"I'm sorry. It's all due to this old man's greed. I'm an unworthy guest... I've endangered His Highness again."

Marquis Leniard collapsed to his knees.

Looking down at the tears soaking his beard, Medea said nothing.

"Your Highness."

Terence approached Medea, who sat beside Cesare's prone form.

He rubbed his temples anxiously.

Cesare unconscious again. The scars of divine wrath glowing crimson.

The situation had repeated itself, and his head spun.

It felt as though the Goddess were playing a cruel game—dangling hope before snatching it away repeatedly.

I'm exhausted and anxious—but what about Medea?

"Are you all right?"

Medea didn't respond to Terence.

She was lost in thought.

'There were no signs until he entered the palace.'

The Cesare I found was pale with marks of divine punishment, but he wasn't unconscious.

'What changed? What triggered this?'

End of Chapter 258
