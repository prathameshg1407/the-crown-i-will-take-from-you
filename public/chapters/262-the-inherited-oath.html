Chapter 262

The Inherited
Oath

"Regardless of God's will, may you grant this sacred promise."

Although the catastrophe had been halted by the valor of Your Highnesses, the oracle itself remains unfulfilled in its truest sense.

Rachel turned to face the Pope directly, her posture one of perfect deference—yet her words carried an edge sharp enough to cut.

"The will of the Goddess was clear: 'Let Medea, daughter of Valdina, become the bride of the evil dragon.' The dragon has been slain, yes—but the oracle's demand was never truly satisfied."

Her gaze slid toward Medea, cool and calculating.

"Forgive my presumption, Your Holiness, but you are not the Goddess herself. Is it not... somewhat presumptuous to declare Her will fulfilled on your own authority?"

Her voice remained soft, almost gentle—yet the malice coiled within those words ran as deep as any abyss.

'Don't interfere, old man. I've barely managed to hold onto my position despite defying the Golden Covenant. I won't let you undo everything now.'

Rachel had no intention of allowing Medea even a moment to breathe.

A single crystalline tear traced its way down her porcelain cheek. The droplet caught the light, shimmering with all the apparent anguish of one who bore humanity's suffering upon her shoulders.

"In the end, it is the innocent who will pay the price. I fear the flames of divine wrath may yet descend upon this land once more."

"Indeed! Who among us can guarantee this will be the end? If we treat the Goddess's will with such casual disregard and disaster strikes again—who shall bear the responsibility?"

High Priest Paul seized upon her words with theatrical fervor, his face flushed with righteous indignation.

The assembly hung on every word, transfixed.

Paul cast a sharp glance toward the other High Priests, silently demanding their support.

"Your Holiness, perhaps this is not a matter to be decided so hastily. We implore you to reconsider."

Murmurs of concern rippled through the crowd, voices rising from every corner of the hall.

"What does the Saintess propose?"

Rachel answered without hesitation, her composure unshaken.

"The covenant must be honored. Whether the evil dragon lives or dies is immaterial—Medea, daughter of Valdina, must exist as the dragon's bride. That designation cannot simply be erased."

"She would chain our Princess to that title forever...!"

The Agemas growled, their collective fury barely restrained.

As the assembly grew restless, several among them released heavy sighs.

The Princess had finally escaped the shadow of death—only to be branded for life as the bride of a slain monster. Even if she survived, who would take a woman cursed by such an ominous oracle as their wife?

Her fate remained suspended in uncertainty.

And yet, selfish though it was, the continental lords felt a quiet relief wash over them.

'If the oracle is satisfied in this manner, perhaps the Goddess's wrath will finally abate.'

The seeds of doubt Rachel had planted moments before had taken root, stirring unease in every heart.

"I cannot condone the Saintess's willingness to sacrifice the living for the sake of doctrine."

The Pope's frigid gaze settled upon Rachel with crushing weight.

Those ancient, inscrutable eyes seemed to pierce straight through her—as though attempting to excavate the truth buried beneath her pious facade.

Rachel's fingers curled tightly within her sleeves, nails biting into her palms.

'He hasn't discovered my true identity. Not yet. No one knows. No one can know. I am nothing more than an impostor wearing a Saintess's face.'

A chill slithered down her spine.

She lifted her gaze—and found Peleus watching her.

'I should never have let you live.'

Cold bitterness radiated from those emerald eyes, directed at the woman who sought to drive his sister to ruin.

It was then that Cesare, who had been lounging against a pillar with aristocratic indifference, straightened and spoke.

"The Saintess speaks truly. The word of the divine must never be defied."

His smile radiated arrogant ease, his words delivered with deliberate casualness.

To all appearances, he seemed to be reinforcing Rachel's argument.

Rachel hesitated. Her violet eyes fixed upon him with sudden suspicion.

'Why would he help me? This man has never done anything without ulterior motive.'

Time and again, he had torn through her carefully laid plans—and those of the warlocks she served—with brutal efficiency.

A faint whisper of unease flickered in her eyes.

"The evil dragon is no more, yet the oracle remains in effect. Therefore, its covenant should pass to the one who struck the beast down."

Silence crashed over the hall like a wave.

Then—an explosion of whispers.

"...Inheritance?!"

Rachel froze, her composure cracking.

What is the First Prince saying?

Cesare's smile spread slowly—a thing of chilling delight upon that dangerously beautiful face.

He drew his sword.

SHRIIIING.

The silver blade sang as it cut through the air, a mournful cry of metal.

With deliberate grace, he drove the point into the marble floor.

Then, slowly, he drew his fingertips along the edge.

Blood welled from the cut—his own crimson mingling with the dark gore of the evil dragon still clinging to the steel.

Unlike Medea's blood, which had remained separate and distinct, Cesare's essence—once touched by the same primordial darkness—merged seamlessly with the dragon's.

"I, Cesare Dwysler Katzen, claim the Princess of Valdina as my bride—in fulfillment of the sacred oracle."

He raised his blood-soaked finger and traced a symbol upon his cheek.

Beside those golden eyes that burned like twin suns, a vivid crimson cross now blazed.

Piety and madness. Sanctity and cruelty. The contradictions merged into something terrifying—and utterly mesmerizing.

He knelt.

Those burning golden eyes lifted to meet Medea's.

"Regardless of the divine will—may you grant me this sacred promise."

The words were aimed at Medea alone.

Whether the"you" he spoke of meant the Goddess or the woman before him remained deliberately unclear.

Sunlight streamed through the high windows, casting half his face in radiant gold while the other half drowned in shadow.

Light and darkness intertwined—a knight offering sacred oath to his lady, or a demon extending temptation to his prey.

No one could speak.

The scene before them seemed to hover between fantasy and reality, leaving the assembly unable to distinguish one from the other.

"This is impossible!"

Rachel's voice cut through the spell like shattering glass.

"Does the Saintess still find cause for objection?"

Cesare tilted his head slightly, that infuriating smile never wavering. His posture radiated insufferable confidence.

"You cannot twist the oracle to suit your purposes—"

Rage and humiliation surged through Rachel's chest like an eruption.

Cesare's smile widened.

"Twist it? I am merely following the oracle's intent. I claim Princess Medea as my bride—precisely as you yourself demanded. How, then, can you object?"

He spoke with the exaggerated patience of an adult explaining simple logic to a difficult child, each word designed to turn her stomach.

"The subject of the covenant—"

You are the problem! You are the one distorting everything!

Rachel barely managed to swallow the outburst clawing at her throat.

"Your Highness, the covenant... it cannot be interpreted so freely. By what authority do you claim the right to inherit the oracle?"

The answer came not from Cesare—but from the Pope himself.

"It seems many have forgotten, but there exists a second oracle as well."

The Second Oracle

"Their fate is known only to those chosen by gods and stars. In the name of the bride, the continent shall be saved from destruction."

The Pope recited the ancient words with quiet gravity, his inscrutable gaze sweeping between Cesare and Rachel.

His weathered eyes narrowed, moving thoughtfully between Medea and the Saintess.

"As the first oracle commanded, the evil dragon's bride was chosen. However, since the First Prince struck down the beast, it is fitting that the covenant pass to him through blood."

He paused, releasing a silent sigh.

"Yet the fulfillment of the final oracle requires that the continent be saved 'in the name of the bride'..."

The Pope's ancient gaze settled upon Medea.

"Ultimately, the key to completing this prophecy rests in the hands of Her Highness the Princess."

"Does anyone present challenge this interpretation?"

His voice was quiet, yet it carried a pressure that filled the entire hall.

"Be warned—those who put forth flawed logic will be expected to pay a commensurate price."

Rachel's teeth sank into her lower lip.

The very words she had wielded to entrap Medea had become chains binding her own hands.

She could find no doctrine, no precedent, no argument to deny the second oracle's validity.

Paul chewed his lip in impotent fury, his face flushing crimson.

Without sparing either of them a glance, the Pope turned to Medea.

"Medea, Princess of Valdina. Will you accept this inherited covenant?"

Every eye in the assembly fixed upon her.

She raised her head.

Her gaze found Peleus first—her brother, already moving toward her, halted by the silent command in her eyes.

No. Not yet.

Then those emerald eyes drifted to Rachel.

Beyond the Saintess's mask of shock and humiliation, the statue of the Goddess stood in benevolent silence, her carved smile serene and unchanging.

And finally—at the far end of the hall—Jason.

He lingered at the threshold, his body broken and bruised, dragging himself toward the assembly.

How fitting that they should look so alike. They were partners once, after all.

A faint smile curved Medea's lips.

Silent Vow

Whether you are truly divine or merely another puppet master—it matters not.

I will gladly stand in your path, whoever you are.

Watch how you struggle when your own pieces turn against you.

I will tear apart the destiny you have written for me.

In this very place where your love once bore fruit—I will plant the seeds of your ruin.

The light in her green eyes turned cold. Brilliant. Unyielding.

"I am Medea de Valdina."

Her pale hand reached out, slender fingers wrapping around the hilt of Cesare's blood-stained sword.

Those golden eyes remained fixed upon her—watching, waiting, burning.

"I accept this path the Goddess has laid before me."

Her voice rang through the Great Hall—calm, clear, and utterly resolute.
