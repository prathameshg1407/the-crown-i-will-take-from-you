Outside — The Garden Tree

"They say there's a book written by a Sage in the Royal Library," Gallo whispered, shifting on the branch. "Maybe it mentions this Venus thing—"

Cesare didn't answer.

His golden eyes were fixed on the lit window of the break room.

Inside, Minister Etienne was unraveling.

He watched the man slam a glass into the wall, scream at his servant, then seize another bottle and drink like a dying animal.

Cesare frowned.

That instability… it wasn't natural.

Then the image replayed itself in his mind.

The banquet hall.

The Princess holding a crystal goblet.

Swirling the wine.

Never drinking.

Then—handing it to Etienne.

"…Ha."

Cesare laughed suddenly, sharp and genuine.

"Boss?" Gallo blinked. "Did something finally snap?"

"Who called the Princess of Valdina an idiot?"

He leaned back against the tree trunk, amused.

"She just fed that toad his own poison."

Gallo's mouth fell open.

"…You're kidding."

Cesare looked back toward the hall, where Medea was smiling politely at nobles, every inch the harmless royal.

Where in the world did such a vicious little fox come from?

"Change of plans," Cesare said, dropping lightly from the tree.

"What? But the mission—"

"Forget the mission. I want to meet her."

The Banquet Hall

Medea drifted through the crowd, black silk whispering around her ankles.

It will take time, she thought, glancing once toward the break room.
But when it starts… it will be ugly.

"Your Highness!"

Marchioness Aspasia waved her over, eyes bright.

"You were magnificent tonight. That speech—brilliant."

"Thank you, Marchioness."

Aspasia leaned closer, voice dropping.

"Do you see those men by the ice sculpture? The ones who look like they belong on a battlefield?"

Medea followed her gaze.

Two figures stood near the eastern wall.

One was blond, smiling, loud—clearly enjoying himself.

The other—

Tall. Broad-shouldered.

An iron half-mask hid his eyes.

A scar carved a jagged path along his jaw.

"That's Fasade. The loud one is Gallo. The masked one is his right hand—Acares."

A ripple of gossip swept nearby.

"Look at that scar…"

"They say he was burned as a child."

"Hideous—but those shoulders…"

"They say he burned an entire tribe in Achel. Men, women, children."

A shudder passed through the group.

An older noblewoman sniffed.

"Hmph. Sounds like the First Prince of Kazen. Cesare Dweisler Katzen."

Medea's attention sharpened.

"The Black Devil?" someone whispered.

"Still alive?"

"Barely. Poisoned on the frontier, rotting away somewhere."

"Good riddance. A monster who poisoned his own people doesn't deserve a throne."

Nervous laughter followed.

Ten feet away, behind an iron mask, Cesare listened to his own funeral with mild amusement.

Rotting away?

Waiting to die?

The rumors improve every year.

"Boss," Gallo muttered, nudging him. "Tone it down. You're leaking murderous intent."

Cesare ignored him.

His gaze had found Medea.

She stood calmly amidst the gossip, untouched by fear or awe. The words monster and devil slid past her like smoke.

The Venus of Valdina.

"Move," Cesare said.

The crowd parted instinctively as he stepped forward—like water before a blade.

The Corner of the Hall

Birna watched from behind a pillar, macaron forgotten in her hand.

It should be happening by now.

She waited for screaming.

For collapse.

For madness.

Nothing.

Medea only smiled. Elegant. Untouched.

"Why isn't it working?" Birna hissed.

Then the room shifted.

"Oh gods," someone whispered. "He's coming this way."

Birna looked up.

The masked mercenary was walking straight toward Medea.

He stopped before her,

casting a long shadow over her small frame.

The hall fell silent.

Medea lifted her eyes.

Gold met Green

"Princess Medea,"

Cesare said, voice low and rough as steel dragged across stone.

"May I have this dance?"
