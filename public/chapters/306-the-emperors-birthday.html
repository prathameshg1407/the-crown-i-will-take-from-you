Chapter 306

The Emperor's Birthday

"In glittering celebrations, old wounds fester beneath the surface."

All heads turned toward the source of the voice.

Marquis Eratosthenes stood at the far end of the throne room from Cesare, dressed in austere gray monastic robes.

"Then what superior solution does the Marquis propose?"

Cesare's tone dripped with sarcasm.

Marquis Leniard stepped forward as though to defend the First Prince.

"The Marquis also manages vast estates. I don't believe he has grounds to speak on this matter."

Eratosthenes' sharp retort made Leniard pause mid-step.

'If Eratosthenes targets the First Prince's noble supporters' wealth, those flames will consume us all.'

"Ah, so the scholar who once cared only for books has developed such keen interest in political affairs since his recent... elevation."

The Marquis of Leniard retreated with a strained smile.

However, Cesare remained utterly unmoved by Eratosthenes' implicit threat, meeting it with mockery instead.

"It appears the Northern Empress isn't the only one struggling beneath the weight of a newly bestowed position."

"Perhaps. Though I imagine it's far less burdensome than wearing a crown that never fit to begin with."

Eratosthenes struck directly at Cesare's most vulnerable point.

'Suggesting the position of First Prince is undeserved for someone of uncertain legitimacy.'

"..."

The moment their words collided, an arctic chill descended upon the throne room.

The assembled courtiers fell silent, scarcely daring to breathe.

No one could easily acknowledge what everyone knew—that these two had once been grandfather and grandson.

"Ahem."

Emperor Perdiccas II cleared his throat deliberately.

While everyone else remained paralyzed with fear of his firstborn son, only Eratosthenes dared publicly denounce Cesare.

On the other hand, if he sided with Eratosthenes' position, he would soon have justification to confiscate the nobles' ill-gotten wealth.

Perdiccas II's expression became inscrutable—impossible to determine whether he felt pleasure or displeasure.

"Very well. The First Prince shall oversee this matter. It's high time we curtailed the Northern Duke's influence."

The Emperor's decree fell like a hammer.

'The gold coins extracted from the Northern Duke will line my coffers, while the resentment falls entirely on Cesare's shoulders.'

Perdiccas II had no reason whatsoever to refuse such an arrangement.

"This audience is concluded. All of you may withdraw."

With the dismissal pronounced, Cesare fixed Eratosthenes with a glacial stare as he passed.

The face that slowly moved past the elderly man radiated pure arrogance.

Neither bothered hiding their mutual contempt.

A flash of satisfaction gleamed in Perdiccas II's eyes.

"Penteros, you understand, don't you? You recognize his viciousness. However, there's no club quite as effective as mine for crushing the arrogant."

While the others filed out, Perdiccas II himself descended from his throne and clapped Eratosthenes comfortably on the shoulder.

"Your Majesty has always chosen the path of moderation and wisdom. Unfortunately, the First Prince fails to recognize that divine insight. It is heaven's misfortune."

Eratosthenes' voice rustled like dried autumn grass.

Yet beneath those humble words lay precise calculation designed to scratch the Emperor's particular itch.

"This failure is also a sin of my daughter and myself—we cultivated him poorly. I beg Your Majesty's punishment."

"Nonsense, Penteros. You're not seriously trying to shoulder blame, are you?"

Perdiccas II shook his head, suppressing pleased laughter.

Praise for himself combined with sharp criticism of Cesare—it soaked into the Emperor's heart like welcome rain.

"I thought the affection we once shared would vanish so easily..."

He suddenly recalled that he'd recently distanced himself from Eratosthenes, suspecting the Marquis might have conspired with Cesare regarding the marriage arrangement.

But after witnessing him attack Cesare without hesitation, Perdiccas II wondered if he'd turned his back too hastily.

'It doesn't make sense. Penteros cherished his only daughter so deeply... If not for Cesare, Eleanor would still be alive.'

'Waving imperial banners and spreading his wings despite being a mere bastard. How Eleanor's heart must have ached watching him.'

Perdiccas II clung to the firm belief that he bore no responsibility for his wife's death—certainly not himself.

'Cesare... That wicked creature ultimately killed Eleanor. It's all because of him.'

Perhaps.

His inferiority complex toward his eldest son—who expanded his power relentlessly—twisted his heart so thoroughly that he could only maintain sanity by believing this lie.

"Return in peace. I always remember your dedicated service."

Perdiccas II regarded Eratosthenes with unexpected gentleness.

"I'm entrusting you with determining the list of those to be honored at my upcoming birthday celebration."

"..."

"This time, I shall personally present the awards. Please exercise even greater judgment than usual. You're worthy of such responsibility, Penteros."

'This is compensation for the cold treatment I've shown recently.'

It would restore Eratosthenes' position—which had wavered somewhat—directly through imperial favor.

"I am humbled beyond measure, Your Majesty. I shall not squander the trust you place in this unworthy old servant."

Eratosthenes bowed his head quietly, clearly understanding the implication.

After the Marquis withdrew, Perdiccas II settled onto his throne in brief, satisfied contentment.

"Your Majesty—"

"Enough."

Rather than irritably swatting away the handkerchief offered to wipe his perspiration, he accepted it and dabbed his forehead.

"Still, since Cesare confronted the formidable Northern Duke on my behalf, he deserves appropriate reward, doesn't he?"

Perdiccas II clicked his tongue.

Truthfully, he didn't want to surrender even a fingernail's worth of advantage if it meant his eldest son gaining more power than he already possessed.

However, father and son weren't the only witnesses present today.

He couldn't ignore the dozens of pairs of eyes watching, nor the expectations of his ministers.

Perdiccas II's brow furrowed momentarily before his gaze sharpened with decision.

"Chamberlain. Adjust the First Prince's seating at the birthday celebration to a more prominent position."

"Yes, Your Majesty. Shall I designate it as a standing position of honor?"

His finger casually indicated the northern section of the wall map.

"Depending on how much he extracts from the Northern Duke, it might be prudent to seat him beside the Eastern Empress for now."

News of the minor uproar at the Eastern Empress's recent tea party—concerning the First Princess's conduct—had already reached the Emperor's ears.

However, Perdiccas II declined to punish his daughter-in-law on behalf of the humiliated Northern Duke.

The fact that the Princess had boldly invoked her immunity actually pleased him.

'It took considerable effort to grant Valdina's daughter such extensive privileges... Better if she uses them for trivial matters like this.'

Perdiccas II had never been sincere—not even toward his wife.

The sweet emotion called affection remained forever subordinate to his will and machinations. His treatment of the Eastern Empress proved no different.

After all, his first priority was—and always would be—himself.

"If I can pacify Cesare by granting him a mere seat assignment, I've purchased his compliance cheaply indeed."

He wouldn't refuse such a paltry reward, however insignificant.

The Chief Chamberlain, observing this exchange, suppressed an internal sigh.

'What if trying to dismiss this so casually results in paying a far steeper price later...'

'Didn't the same thing happen when you granted Valdina's daughter all those privileges?'

Unable to foresee what butterfly effects today's trivial decisions might trigger, the Chief Chamberlain could only worry in silence.

The day of the imperial birthday celebration dawned at last.

The chandelier in the grand ballroom—constructed from a thousand crystals symbolizing prayers for the Emperor's birth and longevity—blazed so brilliantly it nearly hurt to behold.

Perhaps because the national treasury had swelled considerably after extracting substantial "apologies" from great nobles including the Northern Duke, this year's celebration far surpassed last year's splendor.

After delegations from各 nations presented their gifts, the banquet commenced in earnest.

Valdina—the First Princess's homeland—had also dispatched an envoy representing the imperial family.

"Your Highness, I trust you have been well?"

"Lord Sissair!"

Joy illuminated Medea's green eyes at the handsome man's greeting.

The spectacles perched on the bridge of his nose lent him a somewhat austere air, yet even that detail couldn't obscure his striking appearance.

The somewhat lean but impeccably groomed young man stood out conspicuously among Katzen's ornately decorated aristocracy.

Curious glances continuously fell upon him from every corner of the ballroom.

"I heard Lord D'Angel was scheduled to attend. I was surprised to learn you'd arrived instead."

"Yes, Lord D'Angel was originally designated, but the assignment changed just before departure."

Sissair explained quietly.

"Lord D'Angel was quite disappointed, actually. The competition to see Your Highness again was fierce among us all. However, His Majesty ultimately determined I was most suitable after considering various... circumstances."

Emperor Peleus trusted Sissair implicitly.

He possessed an unshakeable demeanor in any crisis, razor-sharp judgment, and cool rationality that thoroughly controlled unnecessary emotions.

He was the perfect envoy to dispatch to an empire with such delicate diplomatic relations.

However, Peleus could not have known one crucial detail.

Despite all his renowned composure and prestige...

The moment Sissair laid eyes on Medea in this glittering ballroom, his heart trembled violently for the first time in his carefully controlled life.
