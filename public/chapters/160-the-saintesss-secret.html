"Healing requires a sacrifice. But whose?"

Knock. Knock.

A sharp rap at the door shattered the heavy silence.

"Your Highness, urgent news from the border. The Knights Templar have arrived."

D'Angel's voice filtered through the wood, tense with anticipation.

Medea exhaled a breath she hadn't realized she was holding.

Ah. I missed my chance.

The Holy Kingdom had moved far faster than anticipated.

It hasn't even been four days since Peleus returned. They must have been waiting for the Saintess's descent—anticipating the moment.

The moment the Knights Templar entered the capital, their first demand would be to see Rachel.

If she vanished—or worse, if she was found dead—Valdina would bear the full weight of their wrath. There was simply no time left to stage a disappearance or an accident.

With the Imperial Proclamation imminent, turning the Holy Kingdom into an enemy while simultaneously confronting Katzen would be suicidal.

Medea had no intention of sacrificing the nation she had built from ashes merely to settle a grudge from a past life.

"...Your Highness?"

Rachel's timid voice broke her reverie.

"You are truly... fortunate."

With practiced fluidity, the dagger slipped back into the folds of Medea's sleeve. The movement was so swift, so seamless, that Rachel saw nothing.

"...Pardon?"

"Your wounds have not worsened. That is fortunate. I shall summon the court physician to attend to you further. Rest now. The Templars will arrive soon, and you must look your best for them."

Rachel bowed her head, relief washing over her features.

"I cannot thank you enough for your kindness, Your Highness."

"Then."

Click.

The door closed. The Princess was gone.

The moment she was alone, Rachel's legs gave out. She slid down the wall, collapsing onto the floor in a heap of white skirts.

Her body refused to obey her commands. Strength had abandoned her limbs entirely.

A cold, inexplicable terror crawled down her spine, seizing every nerve.

Why...?

The Princess had shown no outward sign of hostility. Her face had been calm, her voice steady.

Yet for the entire duration of their time together, Rachel had felt like prey paralyzed before a predator. A rabbit caught in the gaze of a wolf.

"Why on earth..."

It was a long time before her trembling ceased.

✦ ✦ ✦

Medea stepped out into the corridor, inhaling the crisp, bright air.

Perhaps that was why she noticed him too late.

"Peleus."

Her brother leaned against the wall, arms crossed, waiting.

"Ah—Rachel's injuries are deeper than expected. I thought it best to have Sir Hestos examine them personally."

Medea offered a sweet, flawless smile—the perfect picture of a concerned hostess—and turned to leave.

"Dea. Why did you try to kill that woman?"

Medea froze.

"Kill her? Brother, what nonsense is this?"

She offered a light shrug, her composure recovering instantly.

"With the Knights Templar at our doorstep? Who would be foolish enough to attempt such a thing? Unless one wished to be branded the enemy of the Saintess across the entire continent."

It was a perfectly rational answer.

But Peleus knew better.

If D'Angel had not interrupted with news of the Templars... would her answer have been the same?

"Dea. Don't lie to me."

His blue eyes—clearer than the summer sky—bored into hers. They saw everything. They always had.

He had sensed the murderous intent radiating from her, sharp and undeniable.

"I will always be on your side. You know this. I simply want to know why."

It was so unlike her.

Medea had cut down traitors, rebels, and tyrants without hesitation. But there had always been a cause. A reason. Justice.

She had never once turned her blade against the innocent.

So why this girl? Why a powerless, naive Saintess from the frontier?

Medea lowered her eyes, unable to meet his gaze.

"And if there is no reason?"

"That's impossible. You don't act without reason."

Medea's lips curved into a faint, self-mocking smile.

"Peleus. What if there truly is none? What if I simply... cannot exist under the same sky as that woman? Would you still understand me?"

Her voice was cold. Brittle.

Peleus swallowed his shock.

Beneath her mask of indifference, he glimpsed—just for a moment—an abyss of pain he had never known existed.

"Dea... what happened? Was there something else? Someone else besides Claudio who hurt you?"

Medea remained silent.

I cannot tell you.

This karma was born of her own foolishness. Peleus had been a victim of her mistakes in the past life.

This burden belonged to her alone. It was not something she could share—especially not with him.

"Nothing..."

She took a quiet breath, burying the agony deep once more.

"It's nothing. Just a bad joke."

"Dea..."

"I'm returning to my quarters now. It's been a long morning, and I'm tired."

She turned away with a gentle smile. Peleus did not stop her.

It was a mercy. If he had pressed further, her composure might have shattered entirely.

Don't worry, Peleus. I will end this with my own hands.

✦ ✦ ✦

The cottage house stood silent and splendid.

Once the residence of Duke Claudio, its interior rivaled even the main palace in opulence.

Rachel sat before the grand vanity mirror, gnawing at her lip.

Even here—in her own private sanctuary—the goosebumps refused to fade.

She stared at her reflection. Beautiful. Perfect.

"Why was I... so afraid of her?"

The memory of those green eyes made her heart race. She took a deep, steadying breath.

She didn't threaten me. She didn't harm me.

Yet Rachel felt a surge of shame. She had trembled like cattle before a predator.

"Pull yourself together. There is nothing to fear."

She spoke to her reflection, forcing confidence into her voice.

"Soon, she will be the one afraid of you. Not the other way around."

Rustle.

She unwound the bandages Medea had applied.

The wound lay exposed—red, raw, flesh torn apart in hideous furrows.

Rachel placed her palm over the injury.

A sigil appeared on her skin—black as ink, shaped like the curled horns of a goat.

The air warped around her hand.

Sssssss—

The gore clinging to the wound lifted, drawn upward as if by suction. The black goat-sigil devoured the blood and corrupted flesh in an instant, swallowing it like a gluttonous beast.

Then it vanished without a sound.

Rachel ran her fingers over her arm.

In the mirror, the skin was white, smooth, flawless. As if she had never been injured at all.

"Ahahaha... Ahahahaha!"

Laughter bubbled up from her throat, though she grimaced as a splitting headache struck her temples.

"You see? Who is there to fear?"

She looked down at her palm, where the goat had disappeared.

Healing. Miracles. The power of God.

The power that the people of this continent crave and revere above all else—it is in my hands.

"Whether this power is real or fake... what does it matter? I am the chosen one."

The Serpent's Envy

"Medea... Oh, Medea."

She recalled those arrogant green eyes—looking down at her as though Rachel were something too lowly to touch the hem of her gown.

"Do you know why you can ignore me like that? It is only because I haven't taken anything from you yet."

Rachel had been forced to bend her knee. To act the frightened beast.

"You are so arrogant, so ungrateful for what you have. It is time someone taught you the value of loss."

If things had gone according to plan...

That noble lineage. The affection of that family. The loyalty of subjects. The love of the people.

All of it should have been mine.
