Part Two
Chapter 156

The Saintess
of Katzen

"The light that saves one kingdom is the shadow that destroys another."

The Holy Kingdom rejoiced.

A Saintess had been born at last—one whose divine power had awakened in the distant north, within the territory of Valdina.

Emissaries were dispatched from the Holy See itself to escort her back. The continent buzzed with the news.

Meanwhile, in her previous life, Medea had been far too preoccupied to notice the exact moment of Rachel's arrival.

A Memory of What Was

She had been fighting tooth and nail for the throne of Katzen against Emperor Perdiccas II.

She had claimed the glory of slaying the dragon for Jason, transforming him into a hero in the people's eyes.

She had leveraged every ounce of her influence—her kingdom's might, her own reputation—to place him upon the imperial throne.

"Dea, wait just a little longer. Once the palace is secure, I will bring you and Lian to my side. You will be Empress of this empire."

Their engagement had not yet been formalized. The coronation had not yet taken place. Medea had remained outside the palace walls.

And then Rachel arrived.

To "bless" the new Emperor.

With her gentle beauty and harmless demeanor, she swiftly charmed the court. She became Jason's confidante. The people's darling.

And then the oracle descended.

"The Emperor's companion must be none but the Saintess."

Jason accepted it without hesitation. Invoking divine will, he and Rachel bound Medea hand and foot.

Rachel became Empress.

Medea—pregnant with Leah—could only watch their wedding from afar.

Her sacrifices, her victories, her very existence—all were erased beneath Rachel's radiant halo.

But Rachel had not been content with that.

She poisoned Lian, the heir Medea had borne Jason.

And when Medea still lived, Rachel sent assassins after Leah.

If Jason had used Medea openly, Rachel had struck from the shadows.

She had taken everything—glory, family, life.

And in the end, she won.

Medea stared at the woman before her.

Why has everything changed?

In her previous life, Rachel's powers had first manifested within the Katzen Empire itself—earning her immediate recognition as the Saintess.

Yet here she stood, in Valdina.

"We have already sent word through the temple. The Knights Templar will arrive soon to escort Lady Rachel back to the Holy Kingdom."

D'Angel's voice brimmed with excitement.

It was understandable. The Holy Kingdom had awaited a Saintess for centuries. That her awakening had occurred within Valdina's borders—mere days before the Imperial Proclamation—would only elevate the kingdom's prestige across the continent.

Medea said nothing.

No joy. No celebration. No reaction at all.

Beneath her serene mask, she was biting the inside of her cheek hard enough to taste blood—fighting to maintain composure.

"...Your Highness?"

The silence stretched, unnaturally long.

D'Angel glanced back, concern creasing his brow.

"What is your name?"

Medea's voice was soft, almost gentle.

Rachel hesitated, clearly surprised by the informal address. Even D'Angel had referred to her as the future Saintess, yet the Princess spoke as though addressing a commoner.

A flicker of something—amusement?—passed across Rachel's lovely features.

"...My name is Rachel."

She met Medea's gaze with a serene smile.

"Your Highness Princess Medea. Sir D'Angel and the Agema have spoken of you often, always with the highest praise..."

Her voice was warm, melodic—like sunlight made audible.

"But seeing you in person, I realize their words did you no justice. You are truly beautiful."

Her violet eyes shone with what appeared to be genuine admiration.

"Even from the remote village where I was born, tales of your grace and wisdom reached us. Valdina's rise is owed entirely to you."

"And His Majesty loves you so dearly. He faced the demon-infested plains with peace of mind, knowing you would guide the kingdom in his absence."

Neril, standing behind Medea, frowned.

The words were honeyed, overflowing with praise—yet they grated like sand against the skin.

To speak of the Princess "guiding the kingdom" in the King's absence—anyone unfamiliar with the siblings' bond might interpret it as ambition. As usurpation.

After the treason of Regent Claudio, Valdina remained acutely sensitive to any suggestion of power grabs.

Even those who knew of Peleus and Medea's devotion to one another would find such phrasing... unsettling.

"If you hail from the frontier, where exactly are you from?"

Rachel answered without hesitation.

"As Sir D'Angel mentioned, I come from a small village in the Orpheus Mountains, south of the plains. It is modest, but the air is clean—"

"The Orpheus Mountains fall under Katzen's dominion."

Medea interrupted smoothly.

"Then it would be difficult to regard you as truly Valdinian. One raised in a vassal state of Katzen could scarcely grasp the full significance of His Majesty personally venturing into demon-infested lands."

"Whether those plains or this palace—all of it is territory and people protected by Valdina."

Rachel blinked, momentarily speechless.

Then her gentle brows lifted, and tears welled in her violet eyes.

"Please forgive me. I spoke out of turn and offended Your Highness."

She looked as though her courage had crumbled beneath the weight of Medea's cool regard.

Medea observed her calmly.

"Not at all. I merely pointed out that perspective is shaped by one's origins."

The air grew colder.

Was the Princess implying that Rachel's humble, foreign roots rendered her unfit to understand?

Medea had never been one to wield her status as a weapon against the innocent. Yet now...

Even Peleus seemed taken aback by his sister's uncharacteristic frost.

The gathered knights and physicians exchanged uneasy glances.

No one dared intervene.
