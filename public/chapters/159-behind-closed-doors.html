Behind
Closed Doors

"Some wounds never heal—and some memories never fade."

Rachel's arm had turned an angry red where D'Angel gripped it.

"Forgive me, Lady Rachel."

D'Angel's brow creased with concern as he released her.

"Could it be... the injury you sustained during the ghoul attack still hasn't healed?"

That battle had occurred weeks ago.

Weeks—and the Saintess's wound remains unhealed? While the King and the Agema have nearly fully recovered under her ministrations?

"My body does not respond to holy energy. I must rely upon medicine and natural healing alone."

Rachel's smile was tinged with gentle melancholy.

"If the Goddess granted me the power to heal countless others, and the only price is that my own wounds remain... then I can only be grateful."

Her voice was warm, her humility seemingly genuine—the very qualities that made it clear why heaven had chosen a simple village girl to bear such a gift.

"Even so, you cannot simply leave such a wound untended. Why did you not speak of this sooner? I would have summoned a physician immediately."

D'Angel's frown deepened. If the Saintess suffered harm while in Valdina's care, the kingdom would bear the blame.

"My healing is slow regardless of treatment. And in a foreign land, I could not so easily reveal myself to strangers. Besides—if word spread that the Goddess's chosen representative was injured, would it not damage morale?"

She lowered her eyes, a faint blush coloring her cheeks.

"That is why... His Majesty alone knew. He personally tended to my bandages."

D'Angel stiffened, suddenly acutely aware that Rachel was a young woman of marriageable age. He understood, now, why she had been reluctant to bare her wounds to just anyone.

"That is why I came to see His Majesty today. There are things I cannot easily share with others..."

Rachel cast a sidelong glance at Peleus, her flush deepening.

The implication hung in the air—that the secret she and the King shared was more than a simple injury.

Unfortunately for her, Peleus was a man who neither recognized nor cared to recognize such subtle feminine overtures.

"The royal physician, Sir Hertos, is known for his discretion. Lady Rachel need not fear rumors."

His expression betrayed nothing as he offered this practical solution.

"But Your Majesty... you cannot know how fatal even a whisper of scandal can be to a woman's life. And I am to enter the temple soon—my reputation must be beyond reproach."

Her lashes glistened with unshed tears.

"Please, Your Majesty. You are the only one I can trust here..."

It was a pitiable sight—a young woman, orphaned by ghouls, clinging to the one person who had saved her from that horror.

D'Angel found himself caught between two impossible positions. His Majesty clearly wished her gone, yet he could not in good conscience leave her wound untreated...

"Then I shall tend to it."

Medea's voice cut through the tension like a blade.

Rachel froze, clearly startled by the unexpected offer.

"Surely you would not mistrust the King's own sister?"

"Oh—no, of course not."

Rachel shook her head reflexively, though her eyes betrayed unease.

"But... my wounds are unsightly. Blood and grime. I would not wish to distress Your Highness—"

"Nonsense. Have you forgotten that Valdina has been at war for years? I am quite accustomed to bandaging wounds. Come."

Without waiting for a response, Medea took Rachel by the arm and guided her toward the adjoining room.

"Lady Rachel, rest assured—our Princess is braver and wiser than any knight. You are in excellent hands."

D'Angel's relief was palpable. A potential scandal neatly averted.

Her Highness would move heaven and earth for His Majesty's sake, regardless of her personal feelings.

"Then... I place myself in Your Highness's care."

"Very well. Come."

Medea led Rachel through the doorway.

Peleus watched his sister's retreating back in silence, his expression unreadable.

✦ ✦ ✦

The small antechamber where the King rested between duties was windowless and dim.

Click.

The door closed behind them.

Now there were only two: Medea and Rachel.

"I—I spoke thoughtlessly earlier. I am ashamed to present such an unsightly appearance before Your Highness. Perhaps I should tend to this later—"

Rachel shifted toward the door, clearly uncomfortable with this sudden intimacy.

The Princess paid her no heed.

"Remove the bandages. I need to see the wound before I can treat it."

"It is truly hideous..."

Medea said nothing. The silence pressed down like a physical weight.

Left with no alternative, Rachel slowly unwound the cloth.

She had not lied about the ghoul attack.

The wound was grotesque—deep gouges that looked as though claws had raked through the flesh.

Medea regarded it without expression.

"Indeed. Quite deep. It must have been painful."

Her voice was soft. Her green eyes held nothing—no sympathy, no disgust. Only the cool, clinical gaze of someone examining meat.

Rachel shivered involuntarily.

"How can you... look at it so calmly?"

A Mother's Memory

"Mother, I don't want to go to the palace. May I stay by your side?"

"I'll remain with Father—I can protect you and Leah. Very well... then I shall go."

He had been only seven years old.

Lian—so proud, so brave for his tender age.

His death had been so sudden, so final, that even now Medea sometimes imagined his last moments in that palace. The poison sliding down his throat. His small body convulsing.

Did you cry for me, my son? Did you wonder why your foolish mother did not come?

I pray you did not suffer. I pray death came swiftly.

A shudder passed through Medea—so faint it was nearly invisible.

For a moment, she could hear it: her son's final breath, echoing across the years.

Fire kindled in her calm eyes.

Beneath her sleeve, her nails bit into her palm—sharp, grounding pain that drove away the fog of memory.

She focused once more on the beautiful woman before her.

Rachel. In this life, she has done nothing yet.

But she is too dangerous to ignore.

In the life before, this woman had stolen everything—Medea's place, her achievements, her children—without shedding a single drop of blood.

Even after poisoning Lian, she had emerged unscathed. The Empress falsely accused. The people's darling.

A serpent in lamb's wool. Lower your guard for even an instant, and you will find her coils already wrapped around your throat.

When opportunity presents itself, you must seize it.

Beyond this door stand Peleus and D'Angel.

Disposing of Rachel without alerting them would be... difficult.

But even if they discovered the truth—this was Valdina. Medea's domain. She was confident she could manage the aftermath.

After a moment's deliberation, her green eyes grew cold.

Her fingers found the handle of the dagger concealed beneath her sleeve.
