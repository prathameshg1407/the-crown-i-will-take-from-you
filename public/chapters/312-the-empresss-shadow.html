Chapter 312

The Ghosts of
Gilded Chains

"Some prisons are made of gold, and some jailers wear crowns."

A voice cut through the frozen silence—calm, yet carrying an edge that refused to yield.

"Have Katzen's knights gone blind? A foreign knight dares threaten the imperial family, and they simply stand there watching?"

The words rang through the banquet hall like a bell struck at midnight.

The Western Empress's eyes widened, trembling violently as she lifted her gaze toward the shadow that had fallen across her.

Recognition struck her like a physical blow.

"Unhand me! How dare you!"

She wrenched herself free from the man who had instinctively shielded her, spinning on him with sudden violence.

SLAP!

Her palm connected with his cheek, the sound sharp as cracking ice.

"I am the Empress of this empire. What gives you the right to obstruct my path?"

It was a reaction utterly unlike the woman who had built her reputation on grace and restraint. Fury blazed in her eyes—aimed not at her attacker, but at her would-be protector.

The moment her hand fell, her attention snapped away from the man she had struck. Her gaze flew to the throne, desperate, searching.

"Your Majesty, please believe me."

She moved toward Perdiccas II, her voice pitched high with urgency.

"There is only you. There has only ever been you—"

But the Emperor's expression remained carved from stone.

"How many rush to save you."

The Emperor's voice dripped with contempt.

"Even at the cost of their own lives. How heartwarming—to witness such devotion to my Empress."

The mockery in his tone was unmistakable. The Western Empress swallowed the despair rising in her throat, forcing her voice steady.

"Their concern is merely an extension of Your Majesty's grace. They fear for you, not for me. Everything I am exists because of your favor."

Her words were smooth, practiced—the verbal silk she had woven so many times before.

But tonight, the silk would not hold.

Had she been thinking clearly, the Western Empress would have recognized defeat. She would have retreated, regrouped, lived to scheme another day.

But clarity had abandoned her the moment he appeared.

The man she had loved with all the reckless passion of youth. The man she had convinced herself she'd forgotten. The man whose presence now unraveled every carefully constructed lie she had ever told.

"There is only Your Majesty for the rest of my life."

Even as she spoke the words, she knew them for what they were: hollow. False. A performance so transparent that even she could see through it.

Panic clawed at her composure. She had to redirect the Emperor's attention—had to give him a target for his rage that wasn't her.

"Your Majesty, I implore you."

She gestured sharply toward Menil and the foreign man.

"Punish these reckless fools who would sully my honor with their implications. They are the ones who deserve your wrath."

She was no longer merely denying her connection to the man—she was demanding his destruction.

But Perdiccas II had already heard the name spoken moments before.

"Catherine."

The Emperor's voice cracked like thunder.

"You—!"

His mind assembled the fragments: The man's name. His noble bearing. His history with Katzen's aristocracy.

This was no random interloper. This was the former Grand Duke of Carlyle—and once, before Catherine had become the Western Empress, he had been her betrothed.

"Do you still think me fool enough to be deceived—while he stands before us?"

Something snapped behind the Emperor's eyes. The last fraying thread of his patience, severed completely.

He seized the Western Empress's wrist with brutal force. In his other hand, his golden staff gleamed like captured sunlight.

And then—

He struck.

"Aa—!"

The staff connected with her body, and the Western Empress was thrown from the elevated platform like a discarded doll.

Her magnificent gown billowed as she fell, a cascade of silk and jewels tumbling through the air. She struck the marble floor with a sound that silenced every whisper in the hall.

When she raised her head, she was unrecognizable. Her elaborate coiffure had come undone, dark hair spilling across her face. Crimson marks bloomed on her pale skin. Her dress—that exquisite dress—was stained with Menil's blood from their earlier confrontation.

The noble Empress who had gazed down upon the world from her gilded height now lay in ruins on the cold stone floor.

"...Your Majesty."

Her voice emerged as barely more than a breath.

It was not the physical pain that devastated her—it was the eyes. Every guest in the banquet hall stared at her fallen form with naked shock.

The Emperor's moods had always been mercurial, swinging between benevolence and cruelty like a pendulum. But this—publicly casting down the mistress of the inner palace—this was unprecedented.

No one moved to help her.

Those who might have stepped forward shrank back, terrified that the Emperor's fury might redirect toward anyone who showed the Empress sympathy.

Not even Juan—her own son—dared speak. Not even her father, Count Montfort, could find words.

"How dare... How dare you..."

Perdiccas II's own voice faltered.

He had not intended to react with such violence. The rage had seized him before conscious thought could intervene, and now he stood with his staff still raised, genuinely startled by his own brutality.

Thud. Thud.

Footsteps. Deliberate and unhesitating, they echoed through the silence like a heartbeat.

A shadow fell across the Western Empress where she lay crumpled on the floor.

"How merciless the Emperor of Katzen reveals himself to be—striking down even the woman who has shared his life."

In a room frozen by fear, this man alone moved without hesitation.

Strong hands gripped her arms and lifted her to her feet.

No.

A silent scream built in Catherine's chest.

This damned man hasn't changed. All these years, and he still can't read the politics unfolding around him.

His righteousness had always been his defining trait—that unwavering moral compass that had once made her love him.

Now it was a weapon aimed at her heart.

You fool. You're not protecting me. You're burying me deeper.

"I beg Your Majesty's forgiveness."

Another nobleman from the Katzen delegation rushed forward, seizing Alfred's arm and bowing deeply to the Emperor.

"Alfred, have you lost your mind? Apologize to His Majesty and Her Majesty the Western Empress immediately. Step back—now!"

But Alfred did not step back.

He stood perfectly straight, meeting the Emperor's gaze with the calm defiance of a man who had nothing left to lose.

"So this is how the Supreme Lord of the Empire conducts himself? By brutalizing foreign envoys?"

His voice rang through the hall, clear and unwavering.

"You believe your empire will never see sunset—that your arrogance will endure forever. But nothing lasts, Your Majesty. Nothing."

"Alfred, you've gone mad!"

His companion's voice cracked with desperation. But Alfred seemed deaf to the warning, deaf to the danger radiating from the throne.

Throughout the crowd, whispers began to swell like rising water.

"Alfred... where have I heard that name?"

"Wait—isn't that the Grand Duke of Carlyle? The one who studied alongside the imperial family before suddenly abandoning his position?"

An elderly nobleman's eyes widened with recognition.

"Why would anyone abandon such a golden future? Was there some scandal?"

"No, no—the Grand Duke was different from those dissolute aristocrats. I heard he developed a passion for painting, quarreled endlessly with his father over it, and eventually renounced his titles to pursue art abroad..."

Someone paused, scratching their chin thoughtfully.

"But wasn't that around the same time the Western Empress entered the palace?"

A meaningful silence rippled through the crowd as understanding dawned on face after face.

So this was the man from the secret letters?

And he left Katzen precisely when his former fiancée chose the Emperor over him?

And now, all these years later, she still cannot forget him...

The pieces arranged themselves into a damning portrait.

Amid the churning speculation, Alfred gently steadied the Western Empress on her feet.

A familiar scent—something she had not breathed in decades—brushed against her senses. Catherine squeezed her eyes shut as if in physical pain.

The image did not escape the Emperor's notice.

Despite the disparity in their attire—she in her stained, ruined finery; he in the simpler garments of a man who had forsaken nobility—they looked remarkably right together.

The Western Empress, even humiliated, retained an aristocratic bearing that decades of palace life had ingrained in her very bones. And Alfred, despite his long exile, still carried himself with the quiet authority of a man born to power.

They matched.

Perdiccas II's eyes blazed.

He was a man haunted by the specter of his own mortality—a man who found himself resenting the youth of his own descendants, who saw each young face as a reminder of everything time had stolen from him.

And here stood a man his own age who had defied those years.

Where Perdiccas felt his body growing weak and his mind growing suspicious, Alfred radiated a vitality that seemed almost supernatural. He stood straight. He spoke without tremor. He looked like a man with decades of life still burning bright within him.

How?

The question was poison, corroding the Emperor from within.

How can he still shine while I fade into this pathetic twilight?

Veins rose on the back of the Emperor's hand as his grip tightened on the golden armrest.

The throne room held its breath.

And somewhere in the crowd, someone was surely already composing the message that would spread this scandal to every corner of the empire before dawn.
