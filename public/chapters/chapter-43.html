The Trap
Is Set

"The blade had been handed over willingly."

"Too many things have happened lately," Catherine sighed, pressing a hand to her chest. "I've been so worried about you that I nearly collapsed."

Her eyes glistened as she looked at Medea—maternal, tender, convincing.

"To ward off misfortune and celebrate your recovery, we've prepared a banquet at the Duke's estate. Just for you, my dear."

Catherine was certain of her victory.

A girl starved of affection would never refuse a public display of love.

She had already secured the Queen Mother's approval.

"Mother, we must show the world how much we cherish Medea. A grand celebration will silence all criticism."

Wracked with guilt over her recent cruelty, the Queen Mother had agreed without suspicion.

Tonight, Medea would be destroyed.

Count Montega would see a Princess drowning in luxury while the kingdom starved. The public would turn. The Queen Mother would retreat—back to Birna.

Catherine smiled warmly.

Medea smiled back.

I remember this banquet, Medea thought, biting the inside of her cheek.

A Memory of Ruin

In her previous life, this night had broken her.

She had worn the dress they gave her—fragile, radiant, treacherous. She had stepped into the brilliant hall, dazzled by chandeliers and false smiles, believing she was loved.

Then came the whispers.

"Wicked woman. Drinking the people's blood."

"That dress costs more than a village earns in a year."

Then the "accident."

Someone stepped on her train.

The fabric tore like paper.

Rrrip—

had echoed through the hall.

Laughter followed.

No one helped her. No one covered her. They only stared as she fled, sobbing, locking herself away for months.

Isolation.

Shame.

Silence.

Exactly what the Claudios wanted.

Not this time, Medea thought, her green eyes cooling to ice.

The Gift

"A banquet?" Medea asked, feigning surprise. "Tonight? How sudden."

"It's a surprise," Birna chirped, though her smile was tight. "Mother arranged everything so you wouldn't be inconvenienced."

Catherine gestured. Servants carried in large boxes tied with silk ribbons.

"Get ready, my dear. We want you to shine."

The Salon

"For today," Medea said calmly, "Maria will assist me."

She pointed to the maid with bandaged hands.

"Yes, Your Highness," Maria whispered, bowing deeply.

Catherine and Birna exchanged a glance.

Medea asked the Queen Mother to release that traitor? Catherine thought, amused.

How sentimental. How foolish.

"We'll leave you to it," Catherine said lightly, her gaze sharp. Make sure she wears it.

Birna nodded. "Of course."

Once they were gone, the seamstresses unveiled the centerpiece.

Silver silk.

Dusting of crushed diamonds.

Moonlight woven into fabric.

Gasps filled the room.

"You look like you're wearing the stars," a maid whispered.

Medea examined her reflection.

Ethereal.

Perfect.

And fatally flawed.

The weave was too thin. The diamond dust too heavy. One careless step, one firm tug—and the dress would disintegrate.

A beautiful execution.

"It's… pretty," Birna muttered, fists clenched.

Her own dress—a tasteful blue—looked dull in comparison.

Why does she get the diamond gown?

I'm the Duke's daughter.

Then—

"No," Medea said softly.

The room froze.

"I can't wear it."

Birna stared. "What?"

"It's too heavy," Medea sighed, lifting the skirt slightly. "After my fall, my body is weak. The diamonds pull. I won't be able to dance."

She turned to Maria. "Bring the black one."

Maria hurried off.

"But Your Highness!" a seamstress protested. "This is a masterpiece!"

Medea looked troubled. "It's my aunt's gift. It would be rude not to show it… but I physically can't wear it."

She turned to Birna.

"Birna," she said gently, "would you wear it for me?"

The room went silent.

Birna's eyes widened.

"Me?"

"It's a Claudio family treasure," Medea reasoned. "It stays in the family. And you resemble Aunt Catherine far more than I do. It would suit you better."

The silver dress shimmered under the chandelier.

Birna didn't see the trap.

She saw diamonds.

Prestige.

Victory.

"Well…" she tried to hide her excitement. "If you insist, Sister. I suppose I could do you a favor."

Medea smiled.

"Thank you, Birna. You're too kind."

The blade had been handed over
willingly.
