Chapter 297

Shadows and
Schemes

"Every secret is a blade—the question is whose throat it finds."

"There is something I need you to arrange, Sir Kensington."

Medea slid three small portraits across the table. Kensington's eyes widened as he recognized the faces.

"Countess Beaumont, Lady Evelyn, Dean Clarice... These are all confidantes of the Eastern Empress."

Countess Beaumont had been the Eastern Empress's childhood companion, raised alongside her from infancy.

Lady Evelyn had once served as a financial officer in the Crown Prince's household before rising to manage the Eastern Empress's personal affairs.

Dean Clarice presided over the Saint's Ladies' Academy, responsible for educating both royalty and noble daughters—including the Fourth Princess Angelique.

"Your Highness, shall I investigate their vulnerabilities? I will begin immediately. However, I must confess—I judged these women to be uninvolved in political matters and did not assign Foxes to monitor them."

He paused, frustration flickering across his features.

"Even with all resources deployed, I cannot guarantee meaningful intelligence before the tea party."

"There is no need for investigation."

Medea's voice was calm.

"I already know everything."

She touched the portrait of Countess Beaumont with a delicate finger.

"Do you recall when the golden chest sent to the Eastern Empress vanished without explanation?"

"I... yes, Your Highness."

"The culprit was Countess Beaumont. When the Empress became barren due to poisoning, the Countess stole everything of value. The Empress never pursued the matter—she feared the scandal would harm her daughter's prospects."

"This... Your Highness, is this truly...?"

Kensington's eyes had gone wide.

"Lady Evelyn was widowed young. She arranged an 'accident' that left her husband's eldest son permanently disabled—ensuring her own newborn would inherit the estate."

Medea continued without pause, her tone as casual as if she were discussing the weather.

"And the noble Dean Clarice, that paragon of saintly virtue? She has an illegitimate child—the product of an affair with a married nobleman."

"Your Highness... can this possibly be true? Are these women really...?"

"Well."

Medea shrugged with elegant indifference, extending the portraits toward him.

"I cannot say with certainty. Have the Foxes relay this information to the parties involved. They will know on their own whether it is true."

But Kensington understood perfectly.

Every word is truth. These are their leashes now.

Had there been any doubt, his mistress would never have issued the order.

"Your Highness... how did you come to possess such knowledge?"

These were not ordinary scandals—mere bribery or corruption that any competent spymaster might uncover.

A vassal who betrayed her mistress at the most vulnerable moment.

A succession battle disguised as tragedy.

The hidden depravity of a woman who wore virtue like a mask.

These were truths that could shatter lives in an instant—secrets so toxic that their mere whisper would reshape the world around them.

"I will move immediately. With these weapons in hand, whatever scheme the Eastern Empress has devised, her confidantes will have no choice but to abandon her."

"At the very least, they will try to protect me."

Medea smiled—a knowing, calculating expression.

"Because the moment I fall... that will be the day they fall as well."

"Please exercise caution, Your Highness."

Kensington's eyes held genuine concern.

The Katzen Imperial Family is as deep and dark as the mountains that surround it. Even those as lofty and strong as peaks cannot survive beneath its suffocating shadow.

The memory of Empress Eleanor rose unbidden in his mind.

He lowered his head in a deep bow.

Kensington's instincts were sharp—honed by decades of service in Katzen's treacherous court.

He was correct that a trap awaited at the tea party.

The problem was... he was not the only one who had noticed.

The Entertainment District — Several Days Prior

✦ ✦ ✦

The innermost chamber of an exclusive club—accessible only to the highest ranks of nobility—flickered with firelight.

"More. Bring me more! Is there no wine left in this godforsaken place?"

Jason shook the empty bottle roughly. Nothing emerged but the cloying scent of alcohol.

He was thoroughly, devastatingly drunk.

His once-lustrous hair hung in matted tangles. The impeccable garments that had once marked him as royalty were rumpled and stained.

His skin, once smooth, had grown sallow and rough. The flush of alcohol made him look almost corpse-like in the dancing firelight.

"Your Highness, you have already consumed far too much. Perhaps it would be wise to—"

"Did I not tell you to bring more?"

CRASH!

The bottle shattered against the fireplace, glass spraying across the stone.

The servant fled with a terrified squeak, scurrying to fetch more wine.

"How... how could you... to me..."

Left alone, Jason muttered into the flames.

Beyond the flickering fire, phantom images wavered—Cesare and Medea, standing together. A couple.

Medea's husband was not him. It was that hateful creature who had been a blight upon his existence since childhood.

"She's mine. She was my woman. Not you—me... ME!"

Jealousy. Rage. Impotence.

Unable to swallow the raw, festering emotions, Jason lurched to his feet and swept everything from the table in a violent arc.

CRASH!

"I'll take it back. Everything you stole from me...!"

Just as he was about to descend into another fit of rage, a soft hand touched his chin.

"Your Highness, please... who has wounded your heart so deeply?"

Sweet innocence. Coy words. She was one of the club's singers—a woman practiced in the art of soothing powerful men.

She settled beside him as though blind to the chaos of shattered glass beneath her feet, focusing entirely on the man before her.

"What could you possibly do?"

Jason snorted, making no effort to hide his contempt even as he allowed her touch.

"Have you been to the gambling halls again? I told you those places are dangerous—nothing but rough, frightening men. What would happen if someone as noble as Your Highness were hurt?"

"If I don't go there..."

Even when he provoked fights, even when he threw punches until his knuckles bled—her image never faded.

"Your Highness...?"

Gahee blinked as he seized her chin roughly, turning her face this way and that in the firelight.

"Open your eyes wider. Don't smile so much."

"I... Your Highness, that hurts—"

"Look at me as though you cannot bear how much you love me. As though I am the only thing in your world. As though you would do anything for me..."

Perhaps because he had missed her until his very bones ached, it was not difficult to superimpose Medea's face over this woman's features.

"Medea. Dea, my wife... my love..."

He bent toward her.

Gahee stiffened. It was not merely the overwhelming stench of alcohol that made her freeze.

Medea?

Working in an establishment of this caliber, she had memorized the names of every royal and high noble.

It cannot be coincidence that the name he calls is the same as the Crown Princess...

But they are family. Even if the Grand Duke of Castullo has fallen from grace, surely he would not be so shameless as to covet his cousin's wife?

"Your Highness, please be gentle with me. I am to perform at the tea party Her Majesty the Empress is hosting in a few days."

She pouted prettily, pushing against his chest.

"The new Crown Princess will be attending. Even someone as lowly as myself wouldn't want to appear all bruised, would I?"

"The Crown Princess? At the Eastern Empress's tea party?"

Jason's unfocused eyes snapped into sudden, terrible clarity.

Gahee felt a chill crawl down her spine.

"There are no other guests? No others?"

"Only myself and three or four of my colleagues. It's our first time visiting the palace—we're all terribly excited. Who could have imagined we would meet such exalted personages?"

Jason no longer heard her voice.

He knew the Eastern Empress.

A woman of rigid class consciousness, the mother of Princess Angelique—she would never permit common singers to perform at her intimate gatherings.

Unless...

This tea party is a trap. And Medea is the target.

Look at what happens when you marry that bastard Cesare, Medea. You gain more enemies. Face more danger than you ever did in your previous life.

A strange light kindled in Jason's bloodshot eyes.

This is my chance. I will make her see that he is the worst choice she could have made.

"Your Highness? Where are you going?"

He rose abruptly, nearly toppling Gahee in his haste.

The groom waiting outside the club started in surprise. He had expected His Highness to remain until morning, given how heavily he'd been drinking.

"Take me to the Northern Prince's mansion."

Three years ago, the Emperor had stripped Jason of the Prince of Castullo's title and exiled the Northern Barons to Armandi.

Jason had allied himself with dark practitioners to eliminate those barons—the Emperor's loyalists at the time. The current lords of the North were, if not his allies, at least bound to him by mutual knowledge of each other's sins.

The Northern Prince's daughter has coveted Cesare for years. There was even talk of an engagement once.

She had remained in the capital rather than follow her family north—all because Cesare was here.

"At this hour, Your Highness?"

Before the coachman could voice his hesitation, another command cut through the night air.

"And I've heard the Eastern Empress is hosting a tea party. Find me an invitation."

"Your Highness...?"

"Locate Countess Olcaine Delgano. It shouldn't prove difficult. Now—drive."

The carriage lurched into motion, wheels clattering against cobblestones.

Jason gazed out the window at the pitch-black night.

The darkness was absolute. Suffocating.

Just like my life without her.
