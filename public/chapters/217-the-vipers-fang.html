The Viper's
Fang

"Blood demands blood. Even from those we once called family."

Birna's hand flew upward, fingers curled into claws, ready to rake across Medea's perfect face.

She never made contact.

Neril's arm intercepted her mid-swing, catching her wrist and twisting with practiced efficiency. Birna found herself thrown to the ground, muddy water splashing across her face in a cold, humiliating cascade.

"You may find this difficult to believe, but I care for you more than you realize."

Medea's voice drifted down, soft as silk.

"Despite your insolence, your limbs remain intact. That should tell you something."

She lowered herself gracefully, bringing her eyes level with Birna's mud-streaked face.

"It seems the power of blood is inescapable after all. I find myself showing more consideration to my uncle's daughter than to my aunt, with whom I share no such ties."

Her voice dropped to a whisper, intimate as a shared secret.

"As you know, I cannot speak openly about what happened to my uncle. So I had to ensure you learned the truth... subtly."

In truth, she had no luxury left for pretending kindness.

The resentment toward her country she had buried deep in her heart. The hatred for what had been done to her children she had carved into her blade.

Since her return, Medea had been forced to suppress her vengeance, to focus on the pressing matters at hand. But she had never forgotten. Not for a single moment.

The memories rose unbidden—Birna's voice from another lifetime, dripping with false concern:

"Sister, the Empress will never rest until you and Leah are dead. We must divert her attention."

"Please, leave Leah with me. I will escape with her while you draw the enemy's gaze. I swear on my life—I will protect her as if she were my own."

Every word had been calculated. Every promise designed to manipulate Medea's desperate love for her child.

Had the circumstances not been so dire—with Jason and Rachel closing in from all sides—she would never have believed such cunning lies so easily.

And I placed Leah in her hands myself.

The phantom sensation of holding that small pouch—soaked through with a child's blood—still haunted her sleepless nights.

Medea concealed the cold fury beneath a mask of serenity and leaned closer to Birna.

"Now comes the moment of choice, dear cousin."

A pale hand lifted Birna's chin, forcing their eyes to meet.

"Whose daughter will you remain? Claudio's... or Catherine's?"

The question hung in the air like a blade.

Will she pursue futile vengeance for a father already reduced to ash? Or will she secure a comfortable future with her living mother?

If she were Samon—that calculating creature who prioritized efficiency above all else—the answer would be obvious.

But Birna is different.

The blood of Valdina's royal house flowed through her veins. That heritage was her pride, her identity—the foundation upon which she had built her entire sense of self.

"Of course, if you choose to abandon the pride of royalty and live a diminished life in Raju's shadow, I will understand completely."

Medea's smile was gentle. Her words were poison.

"Not everyone possesses the strength to walk the path of nobility. That is, perhaps, one advantage of being born to a lesser branch."

She watched with satisfaction as the barb struck home—saw Birna's inferiority complex flare to life behind her eyes, saw the rage kindle and catch.

"Farewell, Birna. The next time we meet, I suppose I shall greet you as Raju's daughter."

She straightened, her black gown settling around her like folded wings.

"Do ensure you've learned proper etiquette by then."

Fire blazed in Birna's grief-stricken eyes. Medea smiled.

The prey has entered the trap.

El Amuz Strait — District B

Raju's Mansion

"Lady Birna! What a surprise—we received no word of your coming!"

Raju's secretary could not conceal his delight at seeing her appear at the mansion's entrance.

He had only just returned from visiting his master in the isolation cell. The Count had ordered preparations for departure—the estate's assets to be liquidated, belongings packed. Repatriation to Katzen was imminent.

"The Count's residence in the capital should be prepared as well. Catherine and Birna will be residing there henceforth."

The secretary had been privately embarrassed by the instruction.

Has the master forgotten everything that has transpired?

Who would willingly face their parent's murderer again? The fact that Birna had not returned since that terrible day—when the Claudio maid had arrived to fetch her in such haste—spoke volumes.

Though he thought his master overly optimistic, the secretary also felt a pang of sadness. Their relationship, built over so many years, appeared to be ending.

Raju had devoted himself to Birna as though she were his own daughter.

And now, here she stood.

"I merely wished to visit briefly. I hope that's acceptable?"

Birna's voice was soft, almost fragile.

"The funeral left me feeling rather... despondent."

"Of course, of course! You are always welcome here, my lady. Please, stay as long as you wish."

The secretary's enthusiasm was almost pathetic in its earnestness. It seemed his master's years of investment had not been entirely in vain after all.

"I was so worried about you, Lady Birna—you cannot imagine my relief at seeing you here. I shall instruct the kitchen to prepare a meal immediately. Shall I summon the theater troupe to lift your spirits?"

"No. That won't be necessary."

Birna shook her head, her expression wan.

"I only wish for quiet and solitude. Please ensure no one disturbs me."

She ascended to her chambers with only a single maid in attendance.

The secretary watched her go, noting the reddened eyes, the torn lip, the crumpled handkerchief clutched in white-knuckled fingers. The poor girl was clearly still drowning in grief.

"Of course, my lady. I shall inform the staff. Rest well."

The moment the door closed behind her, Birna's expression transformed.

The grief vanished. The fragility evaporated. What remained was cold, burning fury.

"Abominable."

She snatched a letter opener from the desk and drove it into the nearest pillow.

Again. And again. And again.

The metal bit into soft cotton, muffling the violence of her movements.

"Remain here."

She addressed the maid without looking up, her breathing ragged.

"If anyone comes, tell them I am sleeping."

Having vented the worst of her rage, Birna slipped from the room and made her way to the third floor.

She did not hesitate. She had been here before.

Two sharp knocks, and she entered Raju's private study—the chamber where entry was strictly forbidden.

Birna moved directly to the mahogany desk.

Its simple appearance belied its true nature—hidden compartments honeycombbed throughout its structure, secrets nested within secrets.

Her fingers found the deepest drawer, the one that concealed the black vault at its center.

She reached inside.

A sharp, tingling pain lanced through her fingertips—and with a soft click, the safe door swung open.

So. Blood is the key.

She withdrew the black ledger within. Beneath it lay a stack of aged parchments she had failed to notice during her first visit—too shocked by the discovery that this book contained the late Emperor's secret accounts.

"My dearest Robbie,

Even if the world forbids us, never forget—my heart belongs to you. Always.

— Your Cath"

The ink had faded. The edges had curled with age. But the handwriting was unmistakable.

Her mother's.

Birna's fist closed around the letter, crushing it into a tight ball.

"How could you..."

Before coming here, she had followed Catherine from the funeral—had trailed her mother's hurried steps to their inevitable destination.

She had seen it with her own eyes.

Catherine at the isolation prison. The two of them pressed together through the iron bars, clinging to each other like lovers too long apart.

"The two of you are lovers."

Medea's words echoed in her skull, undeniable now.

Her mother and her godfather had been deceiving them all—her father, herself—for years. Perhaps decades.

"I will destroy you."

The words emerged as a hiss.

"I will never let either of you escape."

Her pricked finger throbbed. She raised it to her lips and sucked away the black drop of blood that had welled there, swallowing it down.

Even if I poisoned this safe, it would never be enough to purge Raju's wickedness from this world.

"Leaving so soon, my lady? I've asked the chef to prepare dinner—surely you'll stay for the meal?"

The secretary's face fell as Birna descended the stairs, clearly preparing to depart.

"There are eyes everywhere. It would be wiser to return home before dark."

"Ah... yes, of course. You're quite right."

He wrung his hands.

"But you will visit again, won't you?"

Silence.

"Forgive me if this is presumptuous, my lady, but the master truly does care for you. I know the timing is... inappropriate, but I felt you should know."

Birna inclined her head.

Of course she knew. Her godfather had loved her, had devoted himself to her happiness with a father's devotion.

"Rest assured. I, too, shall never forget—and I will most certainly repay him for everything."

Her unwavering gaze held a light the secretary could not quite interpret.

Inside the carriage, Birna withdrew the black ledger she had smuggled from Raju's study and pressed it into her maid's hands.

"Deliver this to Samon."

She paused.

"No. Wait. Not Samon."

This ledger represented Raju's inexhaustible source of wealth—and Samon was entangled in the very smuggling operations it documented.

If her calculating brother got his hands on this, he would think only of filling his own coffers, not avenging their father.

"Turn the carriage around. We're going to the Katzenian delegation's quarters."

The general himself had returned to Katzen, but a contingent of his men remained in the strait—ostensibly to inspect the delegation, but in truth to monitor Raju during the Emperor's absence.

She had once heard Raju mention, in passing, that this observer was a man of considerable age and experience.

"My lady, the delegation is currently under surveillance. If you appear there in person, you will attract considerable attention."

The maid's voice was cautious but firm.

"Moreover, everyone knows you are the Count's goddaughter. Your presence would raise... questions."

Birna bit her lip, acknowledging the wisdom in her servant's words. She could not simply waltz into the delegation and announce her intention to denounce Raju.

"If you have a message to deliver, allow me to go in your stead. I am confident I can avoid detection."

"You were the one who came to fetch me that day, weren't you? When Father died."

The maid had proven her loyalty already—she had been the first to inform Birna of Joaquin's death, circumventing Catherine's attempts to conceal the truth.

"Yes, my lady."

"Very well. Among the delegation, look for those wearing garments embroidered with red roses."

The red rose was a symbol granted only to those who served the Emperor directly—which meant the wearer would be the person responsible for surveillance.

"Deliver my message to them. I will wait here and watch. Return immediately."

The maid nodded and slipped from the carriage, vanishing into the gathering dusk.

Birna sat back against the cushions, her fingers tracing the spine of the black ledger.

You took everything from me, Raju. My father. My family. My future.

Now I will take everything from you.
