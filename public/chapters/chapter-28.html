Ambient: Palace
Chapter Continuation

The Queen
Mother's
Reckoning

"When did Royal Blood become cheaper than convenience?"

"You are telling me," the Queen Mother said softly, too softly, "that the Royal Physician refused to treat the Princess… because of a paper cut?"

The maid dropped to her knees, forehead striking stone.

"I have committed a mortal sin. Please forgive me, Your Majesty."

The Queen Mother stared past her.

"Go back. Do not mention my presence. Tell him the Princess is dying."

She paused.

"If he still does not come—you will be banished."

"Yes, Your Majesty!"

The maid fled as if chased by death itself.

Silence returned to the corridor.

The Queen Mother's hands trembled.

"What has this palace become? When did Royal Blood become cheaper than convenience?"

"He serves the Duke. Power bends loyalty. The weak are trampled. That is the natural order—unless corrected."

Pinatelli replied evenly.

The Queen Mother closed her eyes.

If they treated Medea this way while she held the King's Seal…

What would happen if it were taken from her?

She would not merely be ignored.

She would be devoured.

"It is the end. The end of decency."

Twenty Minutes Later

The door slammed open.

"For the Goddess' sake, where is she?"

A man burst in, coat unbuttoned, medical bag swinging carelessly. His face was twisted in irritation, not concern.

"Why summon me over a faint? The Princess collapses if the wind blows too hard."

He tossed his bag aside without looking at the bed.

"She's fine. Not dying. Not even close. If Lady Birna's cut gets infected because I was dragged here, who will answer for it?"

He sneered toward the shadows.

"The Princess has such an ugly temper. Demanding I abandon the Duke's daughter for her—pathetic."

A step echoed.

The air shifted.

"A stringless kite? Is that what you call my granddaughter?"

The doctor froze.

The Queen Mother stood in the light.

The man's face drained of color.

"Y–Your Majesty… why are you—"

He dropped instantly, forehead slamming the floor.

"Please! Kill me cleanly!"

"If you wish to die, I will be merciful."

She turned to the guards.

"Strip him of his title. Exile him and his family from the capital. If he ever returns—execute him."

"Your Majesty! Mercy—!"

"Silence him."

Pinatelli stuffed a cloth into his mouth. The guards dragged him out, his screams reduced to muffled animal sounds.

The Queen Mother did not blink.

"Is Sir Hertos here?"

"Yes, Your Majesty. He arrived moments ago."

Good.

That alone was a declaration.

The Bedroom

Hertos worked swiftly, respectfully. No wasted movement. No unnecessary words.

"How is she?"

"Severe exhaustion. Dehydration. Mental collapse."

He studied Medea's face.

"These are conditions I see in soldiers after prolonged combat. Five days without rest or water… I do not understand how a child endured this."

The Queen Mother flinched.

"If this continues, do not be surprised if the Goddess calls her home."

"Nonsense! Fix her."

" Fix her… back to when, Your Majesty? "

Before the punishment?

Before the funeral?

Before she was left alone among wolves?

The Queen Mother said nothing.

She remembered a laughing infant in her son's arms.

Look, Mother. She has your eyes.

Those eyes were dull now.

"I…"

Her voice broke. She turned away.

The Palace Grounds

Night had fallen.

The Queen Mother stopped mid-step.

"What is that?"

She pointed.

A building blazed with light. Music. Laughter.

"The Cottage House. Residence of Duchess Claudio and Lady Birna."

Pinatelli said quietly.

The Queen Mother laughed.

A hollow, bitter sound.

"A cottage? It outshines the palace."

She looked back.

Medea's wing lay dark. Silent. Cold.

A mausoleum.

While the Princess lay dying, the Claudios feasted.

"I have been blind.
Too long."

The Next Day

Madame Quiggin was dead.

She succumbed to her "injuries" before trial.

Or perhaps… she was silenced.

It did not matter.

"Find everything."

"Every coin. Every letter. Every accomplice."

Under her authority, the purge began.

The Reckoning

And for the first time in years—

The Palace Trembled
