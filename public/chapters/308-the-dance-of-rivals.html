Chapter 308

The Dance of Rivals

"In the ballroom's glittering light, every gesture is a weapon."

First Prince Cesare rarely attended his father's birthday celebrations—more often absent than present due to countless military campaigns and foreign conquests.

Even when he did make an appearance, he typically showed his face briefly before departing again.

This was the same man who had ruthlessly shattered the courage of countless young noblewomen with a single arrogant, glacial stare when they dared request a dance.

Yet now, watching the Prince guide his wife's small hand with smooth, practiced steps across the floor, the assembled guests realized for the first time that he could wear the face of an ordinary lover.

The hem of her mauve gown fluttered gracefully across the cream-colored marble.

His firm posture and black dress shoes supported her movements with unwavering stability.

The couple moved in flawless synchronization.

Even the most critical observers had to admit—whatever one thought of the young couple's individual characters, their appearance together was breathtakingly harmonious.

After the dance concluded, Cesare personally brought his wife a sparkling beverage before any servant could intervene, then carefully removed the cumbersome lace cape from her shoulders.

'Good heavens, since when has he been so attentive?'

The Emperor frowned deeply.

The sight of the First Prince attending solely to his wife with such focused devotion struck him as somehow... pitiful.

And the First Princess, accepting such treatment as though it were perfectly natural.

"Pinch me. Are my eyes failing with age? Is the First Prince truly behaving like... that?"

"They say falling in love transforms a person entirely... I never imagined the First Prince would show such tender care for anyone."

Whispers rippled through the crowd as people witnessed the notoriously arrogant First Prince displaying such open devotion without the slightest hesitation.

Consequently, the fact that the First Princess sat at the same elevated table as the Empresses barely registered as remarkable.

The Western Empress's eyes grew even colder.

From the Katzen people's perspective, it boded ill that the cruel, bloodthirsty First Prince should be perceived as a man capable of genuine affection.

He'd achieved supremacy in the imperial succession struggle through military achievements and raw force—crushing his brothers beneath his boot.

However, obedience gained through the sword and fear proved inherently temporary.

Prince Cesare had failed to win the Katzen people's hearts. Her son Juan had skillfully exploited that weakness.

"A throne without popular support will inevitably topple. That gives our Juan his opportunity."

Yet now, the First Princess was systematically addressing her husband's shortcomings one by one.

'And most critically—Valdina stands behind that girl.'

If Cesare consolidated his path to the throne with imperial backing, her son's position would become precarious indeed.

Considerable hostility burned in the Western Empress's gaze as it fixed upon Medea.

'Do you truly believe your husband differs from the rest? As long as that despicable Emperor's blood flows through his veins, he remains the same.'

The Western Empress sneered inwardly.

Even the affection they displayed now would only endure until the throne was secured.

No—his father had already discarded that mask before ascending the throne. Cesare, that wicked creature, might prove even swifter to abandon pretense.

"You don't realize you're working to strengthen your husband's position, you foolish child."

What use was political acumen in the end?

Ultimately, the First Princess—like the Eastern Empress—was merely another clumsy fool intoxicated by superficial affection, unable to perceive the true face that would inevitably reveal itself when power beckoned.

In any case, Cesare's mask would be stripped away this very evening.

"No matter how beneficial Valdina's support proves, it won't prevent the crisis of harboring the enemy nation's spy."

Even given his status as First Prince, he couldn't simply overlook his wife's knight leaking classified information to a foreign power.

The Western Empress narrowed her eyes meaningfully.

Noticing her mistress's signal, Bridget nodded subtly.

Everything had proceeded smoothly.

"What troubles you?"

Cesare spoke as though he could sense only his wife's attention among the countless gazes upon them.

"Do you feel the hook setting?"

Whether drawn by the ballroom's diverted attention or by awareness of the finale they'd carefully prepared, the Western Empress's gaze occasionally drifted toward the couple.

"Juan hasn't taken the bait yet, but his mother has swallowed it whole."

The fishing line piercing her gills was slowly, inexorably tightening.

The smile in his golden eyes deepened with predatory satisfaction.

"..."

Only Medea could detect that this man—appearing even more devastatingly beautiful beneath the chandelier's radiance—now stood on a razor's edge.

The more his languid smile deepened like a painted portrait, the less she could decipher his true emotions.

'How many hidden blades must we dodge beneath this dazzlingly magnificent celebration to maintain this facade of happiness?'

This was, after all, a place where every aspect of her existence remained under scrutiny.

Medea quietly touched his pinky finger beneath the table.

She simply wanted to convey that from this moment forward, at least he didn't fight alone.

"...My wife is kind indeed."

Sensing that delicate touch, Cesare's fingers gently brushed the back of her hand in return.

At the Emperor's table in the grand ballroom...

"Your Majesty, please open your mouth."

Wearing a subtle smile, the Eastern Empress rose gracefully beside the Emperor to dab at the corners of his mouth with a silk napkin.

She understood his preferences better than anyone.

With elegant fingers, she selected only the delicacies that suited the Emperor's particular tastes, arranging them artfully on a silver plate.

"Truly, you're the only one who thinks of my comfort. Attendant! Send the chest of black pearls the Principality of Ribea presented to me—deliver it to the Eastern Palace."

Pearls alone commanded precious value, but an entire chest filled with rare black pearls as a gift... The envious gazes of noblewomen throughout the hall turned toward the Eastern Empress.

"It was presented to celebrate Your Majesty's honored birthday. How could I accept it so casually?"

Despite the Eastern Empress's demure refusal, Perdiccas II insisted on the gift, praising how radiant she appeared this evening.

However, his attention proved fleeting.

"Father! Did you enjoy the performance I prepared especially for you?"

Soon the theatrical production arranged by the Western Empress and Third Prince commenced, and the Eastern Empress's moment in the spotlight was thoroughly eclipsed.

"This is the finest gift imaginable! Juan, you've made me profoundly happy."

Perdiccas II was so moved he climbed onto the stage to clasp his son's hand.

The brief play that had just concluded depicted an unknown hero who bravely defeated his enemies to ultimately become Emperor.

The performance ended amid tension and thunderous applause. In the final scene, when the protagonist seated upon the throne removed his golden mask, the audience gasped in collective shock.

The actor who had delivered such a flawless performance—so convincing no one suspected his true identity—was none other than Third Prince Juan himself.

"Even if I can only follow in the Emperor's footsteps, my life will be successful. Yet even that requires applying my own talents and efforts—incomparable to an Emperor chosen by birth alone."

The play had been adapted to showcase only the most glorious moments of Perdiccas II's life.

Watching the Third Prince perform with such passion that perspiration dripped through his golden armor and mask, the Emperor became overwhelmed with paternal pride.

His face flushed with joy until it resembled an overripe tomato.

"Sallow and stiff... Which proves worse—a yellow buffoon or an exposed fraud?"

Cesare observed his brother and muttered under his breath.

"A blue mask would have been more appropriate here."

'But alas, no such thing exists. What's to be done?'

Contrary to his wry tone, his voice contained no trace of bitterness at being sidelined by their father, nor any sense of crisis regarding the Third Prince's advancement.

Amid the ballroom's clamor, only Medea heard his words.

She had to bite her lip to suppress inappropriate laughter.

In any case, the Third Prince's performance—which satisfied Perdiccas II's ravenous hunger for validation more than any number of lavish gifts—received the highest praise of the evening.

Riding that wave of approval, the Eastern Empress let slip a carefully calculated remark.

"If only Angelique could have been present, she surely would have brought Your Majesty equal joy. It's such a pity."

Her words emerged soft as silk, her expression painted with maternal sorrow.

"That child possesses such a pure disposition—she would have delighted His Majesty just as much as Juan has."

"..."

In that instant, the smile vanished from Perdiccas II's face.

The comment struck like a viper's bite. He unconsciously widened the distance between himself and Angelique's mother.

"Ha! If I'd been entertained twice over, this empire would have torn itself apart!"

His chilling rebuke shattered even the gentle melody flowing through the ballroom.

The music faltered. Silence descended like a guillotine blade.
