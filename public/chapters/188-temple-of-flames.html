Chapter 188

Temple of Flames

"In the chaos of destruction, the hunter never loses sight of her prey."

"You mean there's no need for me to dirty my precious hands? Especially today, when the slopes of Valdina will echo long and far?"

A faint crease appeared between his handsome brows, as though he genuinely couldn't understand her hesitation.

He knew that Medea had just attempted to kill his cousin—and yet he acted as though it didn't matter in the slightest.

"If you simply give the word, there are people waiting eagerly to thrust their swords at him in your stead. Why not use them?"

If anything, his tone suggested that the First Prince's feelings had been hurt by the fact that Medea had tried to deal with Jason herself.

Those generous lips still didn't hide their displeasure.

Despite her surprise, Medea recognized the validity of his point. At the proclamation of Valdina's empire—the pinnacle of a Continental Festival that would never be repeated—she had nearly spilled that worthless bastard's blood in public.

Yet what struck her as truly surprising, even awkward, was the person with whom she was having this conversation: Jason's blood relative and Katzen's First Prince, Cesare.

Though this was her first time facing him directly at the proclamation ceremony, Medea knew the true nature of Cesare, First Prince of Katzen, quite well.

Or rather, she thought she knew.

Born to conquer and reign.

He stood at the summit and looked down upon all below. He existed on parallel lines that would never intersect with hers.

"Your Highness, even a sword will rust if you don't use it."

"Your Highness, please refrain from using such excessive titles."

If Emperor Perdiccas II heard that his son was treating the Princess of Valdina—an enemy nation—with such courtesy, he would likely be apoplectic with rage.

The affectionate demeanor of this man, who moments ago had been pressuring Jason without mercy, continued to exceed her expectations.

'Is he truly that grateful that I saved him from his terminal condition?'

But Medea knew better.

She should not accept without reservation the kindness he appeared to offer, simply because she had once been the First Prince's savior.

Because if she let her guard down for even a moment, he would bite through her throat—just as he had with so many other powerful figures who had fallen before him.

"...I would like to express my gratitude once more for the kindness the First Prince has shown."

Medea bowed her head slightly in formal acknowledgment, pretending not to notice the deeper implications of his words. She kept the details of her interactions with the First Prince deliberately vague and public.

She understood his point—but that was as far as she would accept his assistance.

This was the line she drew, and it was also the line that had to exist between Valdina and Katzen.

The First Prince seemed to grasp her meaning. He paused.

How will he react? Will he be offended that I've rejected his kindness, or will he try to demand something new in return?

Their eyes met.

The First Prince's golden eyes resembled a brilliant sun—or perhaps the swirling vortex of a demon that consumed everything in its path.

At that moment, a soft, breathy laugh escaped from between those perfectly sculpted lips.

Only when the corners of his eyes curved into crescents did Medea realize—the First Prince was smiling.

"Your Highness, you need not fear me. Simply focus on how to wield this new weapon."

"..."

"Even if I say it like this, you won't believe me anyway."

He departed with flawless etiquette, his exit as elegant as everything else about him.

It had been a light parting—but the impression he left behind was anything but.

All the lingering traces Jason had imprinted on her thoughts were swept away entirely by Cesare's presence.

Medea swallowed a sigh.

'What kind of person... is the First Prince of Katzen?'

In her past life, she felt as though every judgment she'd made about the man she'd heard so much about—Cesare Dwisler Katzen—had been utterly, fundamentally wrong.

Ding. Ding. Ding.

The temple bell rang three times, its sonorous tone reverberating through the vast hall.

"Take your seats!"

"Let us begin!"

It was time for the ceremony to pray for the safety and success of the Continental Festival. Though ostensibly a celebration, it was also a sacred week of prayer to honor the saints.

The ceremony would conclude with five days and nights of prayers dedicated to St. Augustine.

Presiding over this Continental Ceremony on behalf of the Pope himself, Emperor Peleus ascended the altar.

Instead of his imperial crown, he wore a laurel wreath upon his silver hair. Clad in white ceremonial robes, he looked every inch a member of the holy clergy.

"God, let our names arise from Your fountain, and let them guard our journey from death to life, from darkness to Your wonderful light."

From the silver chalice he tilted, luminous blue holy water flowed forth, soaking the flowers decorating the Continental altar.

"May You protect us!"

"May You protect us!"

The assembled crowd shouted in unison.

His refined movements, the way the light caught his silver hair, his noble features framed by white robes—all of it possessed the power to inspire genuine faith in those who watched.

High Priest Paul, observing from the side, felt a pang of dismay as he watched the ceremony being conducted with more fervor than even the Holy Father himself could muster.

"Why do I feel like I'm witnessing His Holiness the Pope in action?"

"Hey, you! Stop muttering nonsense and pray properly! You'll chase away all the good fortune!"

Meanwhile, the festive atmosphere inside the temple continued to swell, joy and reverence mingling in the air.

Psssshhh—!

"What—what?!"

Flames erupted simultaneously from the pillars supporting the temple.

"Fire! Is that smoke coming from over there?"

As people glanced around in confusion, someone with a keener eye muttered uncertainly:

"...Performance?"

BANG!

An explosion erupted directly behind the altar.

"Your Majesty!"

"Protect His Majesty!"

The Agemas—Valdina's elite imperial guard—surged toward Peleus. Only then did the gathered masses realize the gravity of the situation.

BANG! BANG!

As if to underscore the urgency, continuous explosions thundered from every corner of the temple.

"Get out! Get out! Get out!"

"Gyaaaah—!"

"Ahhh!"

Everyone inside the hall surged toward the entrance in a frenzied stampede, desperate to escape the collapsing debris raining down from above.

But Medea remained utterly motionless, as though nailed to the ground.

Her green eyes swept methodically across the rapidly disintegrating altar.

'Where is Samon?'

Did he flee already?

Her delicate brow furrowed in concentration. There was still time remaining before the final, catastrophic explosion that would shatter the temple's interior completely.

'I have to make certain.'

Medea's hand slipped into her sleeve, fingers closing around the concealed arrow hidden there.

If her plan had failed—if Samon had somehow escaped—she intended to kill him right then and there, in the midst of the chaos.

It didn't matter to Medea if the aftereffects of the explosion reached her as well.

She was confident she could eliminate Samon and extract herself before the final detonation.

'Even if I suffer minor injuries, it's not a terrible price to pay.'

If this became an incident in which even the Princess of Valdina was affected, no one would dare suspect Valdina itself as the culprit.

Medea's green eyes deepened to the shade of a hunter preparing for the kill.

'Samon. You must fall here.'

It was absolutely essential to eliminate the most dangerous, singular focal point of the current Claudio family.

Amidst the screaming crowds and falling stone, Medea stood perfectly still—a statue of lethal calm in a temple engulfed by flames.
