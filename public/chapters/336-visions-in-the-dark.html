The Dream

A Golden Road

Medea dreamed.

She stood once more at the Tablet of Oaths, her hand raised in solemn pledge—the coronation of Katzen's new era, and she as Cesare's wife.

Her name gleamed upon the golden parchment as she turned to face the assembled crowd. Celebratory flowers descended like gentle rain, petals gathering at her feet in drifts of color.

The sun wrapped her in warmth, and before her stretched a path gilded with light—a road she had never walked in any lifetime.

It was as though the universe itself sought to compensate for every bitter memory of her previous existence, when she had stood beside Jason yet never been acknowledged as his true companion.

"Medea."

Cesare stood at her side, his hand extended toward her.

The path they would travel together was new and unknown, yet she felt no fear. The road gleamed golden beneath their feet. Pollen drifted through the air with each step she took, and the hand that held hers was strong and steady.

She knew with perfect certainty that their fingers would never part.

A strange sensation enveloped her—calm and protected, safe in a way she had never known—

"Medea de Valdina."

The scene shattered and reformed.

She stood now in the great hall of the Central Temple.

"Let it be witnessed here and now: Medea de Valdina and Cesare Dweisler Katzen. As ordained by divine prophecy, I hereby declare the completion of the Oracle's bond."

"This union is the will of the heavens. The oath sealed in blood shall be sanctified under divine blessing."

This was the moment the Oracle binding Medea had been reestablished through Cesare's intervention. Pope Benedict inscribed the sacred Mithras with ceremonial precision.

"The Holy See, and this humble servant of God, shall stand as eternal witnesses to this covenant."

But as the Pope pronounced his blessing, the Mithras slipped from her grasp and clattered to the ground.

Medea bent to retrieve it—and froze.

Blood.

The cream-colored sacred seal lay half-submerged in a spreading pool of crimson, the blood so thick it had darkened to the color of mud.

Medea raised her eyes.

The Central Temple had been consumed by impenetrable darkness. Where light had streamed through sacred windows, only shadow remained.

She noticed shapes scattered across the floor—round, motionless forms. With trembling hands, she reached for the nearest one and turned it over.

Bodies. People lay strewn across the temple floor like discarded dolls.

"His Holiness the Pope... Grand Administrator Leonor..."

Their faces had gone gray, drained of all vitality. No breath stirred their chests. No life remained behind their open, staring eyes.

And then—finally—

Her hands found one last figure, still twitching with the fading remnants of life. She turned the body over, and recognition struck her like a physical blow.

"Theo...?"

Medea's eyes flew open.

Cesare was gazing down at her, his hand still clasped firmly around hers.

"A nightmare?"

His voice was low and gentle as he cupped her cheek, his thumb tracing across her damp skin to wipe away the cold sweat that had gathered there. The touch was suffused with quiet tenderness.

"...I'm fine now."

"Your heart is still racing."

He must have felt her pulse thundering beneath his fingertips. His handsome features creased with concern as he studied her face.

"...I believe something has happened at the Central Temple."

Cesare's expression sharpened immediately.

No reports had reached them concerning the temple. But he had learned that Medea sometimes experienced strange visions—premonitions that invariably proved connected to reality.

It was through such a vision that she had discovered him dying on the glacier.

"I'll dispatch word to Gallo at once. We have operatives within the temple itself—they should be able to confirm or deny what you've seen."

Medea nodded, some of the tension leaving her shoulders.

The warmth of his palm against her cheek, the steady drip of melting snow from the cottage roof—these small, grounding details helped soothe her racing heart.

"...We have company?"

She had become aware of other presences in the house—the cottage that had belonged to just the two of them for days now suddenly felt crowded with life.

A slow smile curved Cesare's lips.

"The search party has finally found us. Come down when you're ready."

Medea glanced toward the window. The blizzard that had held them captive had finally relented, leaving behind a world of pristine white silence.

Downstairs

The Reunion

Medea descended the stairs with measured steps, still adjusting to the sensation of having others nearby after days of solitude with Cesare.

"Your Highness!"

Neriel rushed forward, propriety forgotten in her relief.

Behind her stood Menil, Gallo, and Terrence—the party that had set out to find Cesare on this frozen peak.

"Thank the heavens you're safe. Praise be to God."

Before Medea could respond, Neriel had wrapped her in a fierce embrace—a breach of protocol that spoke volumes about the fear she must have endured during the search.

Gallo, too, was visibly moved. He rubbed at the corners of his eyes, his usually jovial demeanor replaced by raw emotion.

"Dear heavens. I thought my heart would give out from the worry. If anything had happened to Your Highness, I wouldn't have been able to face His Majesty the Emperor of Valdina."

The man who was always quick with a joke or a mischievous grin now looked utterly drained.

"I'm sorry. I've caused everyone a great deal of concern."

As she spoke, Medea gently patted Neriel's back in reassurance.

"Neriel, I should have been more careful. You must have been beside yourself with worry."

"No, Your Highness—the fault lies entirely with me. I failed in my duty as your escort. I will accept whatever punishment you deem appropriate."

But as Neriel spoke, her words trailed into silence. Her keen eyes had fixed upon something—a particular detail on her mistress's person.

Specifically, the pale column of Medea's neck.

Neriel took a deliberate step backward.

"Neriel?"

The knight's gaze swung between Medea and Cesare with growing intensity.

"In a dangerous situation... while we were searching... Your Highness, did he..."

Her voice emerged strangled with barely contained fury. Her fists clenched at her sides, and her hand drifted toward the sword at her hip.

"Lady Neriel—please, let us remain calm!"

Gallo lunged to restrain her.

"Release me! How dare you side with that—that scoundrel!"

"Lady Neriel, I understand your feelings, but consider—I did help rescue His Highness from certain death!"

Gallo's protest was equal parts desperate and indignant.

The master's moods are volatile enough on the best of days! People have lost their heads for far less! He's not exactly known for treating acquaintances with patience!

Even Menil, usually oblivious to such matters, had begun to piece things together.

It was obvious to anyone with eyes: in the days they had spent searching through the blizzard, something fundamental had shifted between Medea and Cesare.

"..."

"I hadn't expected Neriel to notice quite so quickly."

Medea bit her lip, heat creeping up her neck.

Of all people, her fiercely protective knight-escort would be the one to catch what others might overlook. Neriel glared at Cesare with all the ferocity of a mother hen defending her chick.

"...That's enough, Neriel. Please calm yourself."

Medea reached out to grip her knight's sleeve, though her own ears had flushed crimson.

"Release her, Gallo."

A deep voice cut through the tension.

"The fault lies with me. I was... impetuous. Careless."

Medea's head snapped up. Sensing her embarrassment, Cesare had chosen to shoulder the blame entirely.

"However, I suspect neither Dea nor I have any interest in a formal trial. How about a duel instead?"

The corner of his mouth curved lazily upward.

"You may swing at me until your heart is satisfied."

As Medea turned to stare at him, he added with casual assurance:

"Don't worry. I won't sustain any serious injuries."

Gallo could scarcely believe his ears.

"When did the master start speaking so... gently? And since when does he offer to be someone's sparring dummy? This is unprecedented! I've been at his side my entire life, and I've been on the receiving end of those 'sparring sessions' more times than I can count!"

"Then you're welcome to join as well."

Cesare replied with disturbing agreeableness.

"Master... are you feeling quite well?"

Gallo stared at him as though he'd sprouted a second head.

"Perhaps the injury was to your mind rather than your back?"

The last part was muttered under his breath, though not quite quietly enough.

"Careful—it's hot."

Ignoring Gallo entirely, Cesare pressed a steaming cup of tea into Medea's hands.

His movements were careful and attentive, almost protective—the way one might handle something precious and fragile. Yet as he steadied the cup in her grasp, the tip of his finger traced a deliberate, playful path across the back of her hand.

Stop that.

Medea pinched the back of his hand in warning and turned her face away, certain her cheeks must be aflame.

Somehow, she had developed an even thinner skin than before. At this rate, she would need to flee the room entirely.

"Your Highness... we are truly grateful for your safe return."

Terrence stepped forward with a bow, mercifully breaking the tension.

"You are our savior. If not for Your Highness, I cannot bear to imagine what would have become of His Highness—of all of us."

Without Medea's intervention, Cesare would surely have perished, buried somewhere beneath the endless snow of the glacier.

Terrence lowered himself to one knee and reached for her hand, intending to press his lips to her knuckles—a formal gesture of profound gratitude among mages.

"Don't even dream of it. What exactly did you do to deserve such an honor?"

Cesare's foot connected with Terrence's shoulder, sending him sprawling backward. His golden eyes had gone cold as winter ice.

This was retribution—for Terrence's decision to disobey orders and bring Medea to this dangerous peak in the first place.

"But surely—it's because of my actions that Her Highness was here to save you at all? Doesn't that count for something?"

"Terrence. You've grown as talkative as Gallo."

One look at Cesare's expression sent Terrence scrambling for safer ground.

"In any case, we're relieved beyond measure that everyone is safe. A considerable amount of time passed while we waited for the blizzard to subside. The church is rife with speculation about whether the two of you survived."

Terrence wisely redirected the conversation to matters of state.

"The Western Empress has been released from the tower. Officially, His Majesty announced that he chose to pardon her in recognition of her years of service. In truth, of course, such clemency would never have been granted without the Emperor's direct intervention."

Cesare's lips twisted into a sardonic smile.

"Master, it appears the Emperor has had yet another change of heart."

"How refreshingly predictable."

His smile sharpened.

"If this sudden show of generosity was truly meant for my benefit, then I must have come closer to death than I realized. It seems Father saw the lantern flickering at my bedside and decided to hedge his bets."

There was no trace of filial sentiment in his voice—no pain at the thought of a father who had abandoned him, no gratitude for this belated gesture.

"His talent for reading which way the wind blows has always been his greatest skill."

Perdiccas II must have calculated swiftly upon receiving news of Cesare's brush with death. If he was going to be forced to pass the throne to the Third Prince regardless, better to curry favor now by releasing the prisoner's mother.

"The Western Empress has wasted no time in reclaiming her influence within the palace. The Fourth Princess was discovered attempting to poison her and has been confined to her chambers indefinitely. The Northern Empress has been stripped of her authority over the inner court."

Princess Angelique—the Fourth Princess who had once sought Medea and Cesare's assistance in opposing the Third Prince.

The Western Empress had made an example of her, a clear warning to any who might consider similar treachery in the future.

The world beyond the glacier had continued to turn.

Powers had shifted, alliances had crumbled and reformed.

And somewhere in the heart of the Central Temple, darkness was gathering.
