Chapter 6

Ropes and
Retribution

"One prince bound in chains, one kingdom's rage made manifest—
and a message written in corpses."

I understand. Yes."

Cesare extended both hands without hesitation.

The Katzen Imperial knights who accompanied the chamberlain exchanged uncertain glances as they bound his wrists with rope—their expressions questioning whether this was truly the right course of action.

"Your Highness the Grand Duke must accompany us as well. You were present at the scene."

Jason was also taken into custody alongside the First Prince.

"The Princess of Valdina—"

"You should bind them tighter. If you tie them so loosely, it will look as though you're inviting me to escape."

Cesare's tone was utterly indifferent.

The chamberlain fell silent.

He realizes. The Prince is telling me to close this matter here—to ask no further questions of Valdina.

"...As you wish, Your Highness."

The chamberlain lowered his head and offered a quiet warning.

"Your Highness's position may be jeopardized by this incident."

What is this man thinking?

The chamberlain could not comprehend it.

Jared belongs to neither the Emperor's faction nor the First Prince's. He's merely a disposable pawn. Why would His Highness shoulder this burden?

The succession war had already tilted decisively in Cesare's favor. Without extraordinary missteps, this man would have ascended the throne with ease.

If only he hadn't committed this act just now.

"You've returned with such difficulty, Your Highness. His Majesty will not let this opportunity pass."

Cesare's lips curved into a faint, sardonic smile.

"When has he ever?"

There had never been a time when Emperor Perdiccas I failed to seize what rightfully belonged to Cesare.

The chamberlain had no response.

Cesare clapped him lightly on the shoulder.

"I value loyalty. That's why I trust you."

"...Your Highness."

The old chamberlain felt his chest tighten as he watched the First Prince walk away—back straight, stride confident, more regal than any crowned king.

Medea watched as Cesare was led away, bound in rope.

Even in that moment, their eyes did not meet.

His consideration. Even now.

It felt unfamiliar.

This man—whom the world called the Black Devil—had granted her the satisfaction of vengeance while taking upon himself the consequences.

And she was the object of his protection.

"Your Highness, you need not fear me. Though I say this, you won't believe me anyway."

"Dea."

At her brother's call, Medea finally tore her gaze from Cesare's retreating back.

Somehow, I feel that image will remain with me for a long time.

The First Prince, who had been pointedly ignoring Medea's stare, suddenly lifted his head and locked eyes with Peleus.

His clear golden gaze did not waver. Instead, it flicked deliberately toward something behind the altar.

Peleus followed the direction—and spotted a blade protruding slightly from the edge of the curtain.

A dagger.

Thrown beside Jared's corpse.

Peleus gave an almost imperceptible nod.

It was the moment when two men's intentions aligned—united in their desire to protect Medea.

Peleus lowered his gaze to his sister, still cradled in his arms.

His eyes turned ice-cold as he noticed the thin red wound on her neck.

"Your Majesty, regarding this matter—"

"Valdina requires no further explanation from Katzen."

Peleus's voice rang out—cool, cutting, final.

"So, Katzen—interfere no more."

The chamberlain swallowed a sigh at Emperor Valdina's words.

The unspoken meaning was unmistakable: he was rejecting any Katzen influence over this incident.

But there was nothing to say. The weight of responsibility Katzen bore in this situation was crushing.

If only the Princess had killed Jared, it might have been quietly suppressed. But now that Prince Cesare has confessed publicly... what leverage does Valdina owe us?

His Majesty Perdiccas will rage for weeks.

He glanced at the young Emperor of Valdina, who radiated a cold, lethal aura.

When the chamberlain had first heard that Jared kidnapped the Princess, he'd mobilized quickly. During his search, he'd stumbled upon Peleus—cutting through a band of assassins with terrifying efficiency.

The conspirators had clearly sought to exploit the Emperor's distraction over his sister's peril.

The assassination squad—composed entirely of aura-wielding swordsmen—had been formidable.

But Peleus's greatsword was like emotionless ice.

Its trajectory was clean, precise, unyielding—cutting down enemies like autumn leaves before a winter gale.

Even the speed was astonishing.

Even His Majesty's personal guard couldn't dispatch such opponents so swiftly.

In the quiet, refined violence—devoid of blood-lust or fury—the chamberlain sensed a single thread of longing. Directed toward the Emperor's sister.

An involuntary groan escaped his lips.

Valdina is beyond our reach now.

He understood, suddenly and with finality, that Katzen would never again control the kingdom this man protected.

"Yes. I shall summon you soon."

The chamberlain swallowed another sigh.

The wind through the dreary forest wailed mournfully—as though echoing his despair.

* * *

The sun rose over Valdina's capital.

"Ugh, my back..."

A merchant stretched, shoulders aching beneath the weight of his loaded pack.

He'd left home before dawn to deliver goods within the castle walls.

"'Bring it immediately after unloading from the ship,' they said. Even with extra coin, I should've refused. What's the damned rush?"

Grumbling, the man entered the main square—and stopped dead.

The street was packed with people.

"Why so many at this hour? Gods above—"

His face went pale.

Above the chattering crowd, hanging from tall poles in the square, were long, swaying objects.

Corpses. Twisting slowly in the wind.

"By the gods, what is that?!"

"Assassins! Sent to kill the Valdina royal family—to murder His Majesty!"

"What?! Who would dare—"

"Who else?! Those damned Katzen bastards. See the one in the center?"

A passerby pointed toward a red-haired corpse hanging from the central pillar.

Beside it, a golden scroll bore writing large enough for anyone to read:

This man, a general of Katzen, sought to take His Majesty's life and failed. Katzen's vain ambitions shall remain here as a monument to their folly—mocked alongside this wretch's corpse.

"That's him! The bastard general who harassed Her Highness the Princess three years ago!"

"And he tried to abduct Her Highness again—to lure her from the palace and assassinate her!"

The passerby lowered his voice conspiratorially.

"That son of a bitch deserves to burn in the hells for eternity!"

The merchant quickly joined the crowd, cursing the dead Jared with fervent rage.

"If Katzen keeps this up, we can't tolerate it! Let's march on their capital and take Emperor Perdiccas's head! Show that incompetent bastard the price of crossing Valdina!"

The Valdinians' fury blazed.

Every passerby spat at the hanging corpses without exception.

Even foreign visitors remaining in the Strait expressed shock at Katzen's audacity.

Yet beneath the anger, a chill ran through the crowd.

The corpses hanging in the square were twisted grotesquely—limbs bent at unnatural angles.

Jared's body looked far more brutalized than those flanking him.

One hand severed completely. Stab wounds riddling his torso.

Most striking of all: a Katzen banner wrapped around his body, torn and shredded.

Though their eyes were closed, their frozen expressions of agony made it easy to imagine the terror and pain of their final moments.

If this was a warning to Valdina's enemies, nothing could be clearer.

"I have heard Emperor Peleus is young yet just—and possesses a keen understanding of propaganda. He knows Perdiccas II's character well."

Leonor, Commander of the Knights Templar of the Holy Kingdom, sighed in grudging admiration.

He leaned against a window overlooking the square, deep in thought.

Why tear the Katzen flag and drape it over Jared's corpse?

It was a clear provocation from Peleus to Perdiccas—while simultaneously marking Jared's allegiance to Katzen.

Even the Pope I serve wouldn't mock Katzen so openly.

"Is he truly prepared to go to war with Katzen? It won't be easy for such a young nation."

"But what can Katzen say? They secretly sent a ten-star general to kidnap the Princess and force her into marriage—then were caught red-handed attempting regicide..."

"Wait—he was trying to marry the Princess?"

The question came from Theo, cutting through the conversation.

The other knight raised an eyebrow in puzzlement.

"Yes. The First Prince stopped him before the vows were spoken."

"..."

When Theo heard those words, something ignited in his chest.

It felt like anger—but he couldn't understand why the Princess's affairs stirred such fierce emotion within him.
