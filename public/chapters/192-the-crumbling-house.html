Chapter 192

The Crumbling
House

"The goddess embraces the righteous—and strikes down the wicked."

Our master possesses no such carelessness.

After delivering his report and departing the Princess's palace, Kensington found himself quietly marveling at his mistress. Even now, she sat embroidering her next move with perfect composure—as if the explosion had been merely one stitch in a larger, more intricate pattern.

How would the Fourth Princess react to her husband's misfortune? The woman who despised Samon with every fiber of her being—what would she do when she learned of his fate?

Kensington was awed by the precision with which Medea had orchestrated Samon's descent. Each calculated step had driven the man closer to the cliff's edge, body and mind alike.

"Lord Kensington, I am frightened."

Neril's voice broke the silence as they walked side by side through the palace corridors.

"Frightened? Of what?"

"Of Her Highness."

Kensington halted mid-step, uncertain he had heard correctly.

"Valdina is no longer the fragile kingdom it once was. We have grown powerful enough to rival Katzen itself. And yet Her Highness refuses to step back from the front lines."

Today had been no different. Medea had ordered Neril to remain at Peleus's side throughout the festival.

"You are the only one I can trust with this. Will you do it for me, Neril?"

"Do not concern yourself with me, Your Highness. If all proceeds according to plan, there will be nothing to fear."

Neril had understood the reasoning behind the order—to ensure Peleus's safety and prevent any interference on his part. But understanding did not quiet her fear.

No matter how meticulously one prepared, the unexpected always found a way through.

Neril's hands tightened at her sides.

The image remained seared into her memory: shards of stained glass hurtling toward Medea like a storm of knives. A lifetime spent as a knight had taught her that life and death were separated by nothing more than a heartbeat.

"Her Highness is not a god. One day, something will happen that cannot be undone."

"..."

"Yet no one can stop her. How could they? I cannot even begin to fathom the depths of her vision."

Neril bit her lip, frustration bleeding into her voice.

Kensington laid a steadying hand on her shoulder. He understood—both the knight's anguish and the princess's burden.

"Not all of Her Highness's decisions can be comprehended by minds such as ours. I would not presume to stop her. But I understand your torment."

"..."

"We can only do what lies within our power—protect her to the utmost, even as we grapple with our own doubts and fears."

"Yes."

Neril nodded slowly.

"Perhaps one day you will look back on this moment and smile, remembering it as nothing more than a passing worry."

"...I pray you are right."

Their footsteps faded into the distance, leaving Neril's deepening unease behind like a shadow that refused to lift.

❧

Several days had passed since the explosion shattered the Temple of El Amuz on the opening day of the Continental Festival.

An investigation was swiftly launched to determine the precise circumstances of the destruction.

"The temple—destroyed by an explosion? This reeks of heretics seeking to suppress the old faith! Leave no stone unturned—find every last clue!"

Priests of the Holy Nation, High Priest Paul among them, descended upon the ruins like vultures.

While Samon Claudio teetered between life and death, the investigators combed through the wreckage with ruthless efficiency.

And they found exactly what Medea had left for them to find.

"A substantial quantity of gunpowder was concealed in the cellar beneath the altar—directly below where His Majesty performed the sacred rites."

"Someone attempted to assassinate the Emperor."

The investigation swiftly transformed into a manhunt for the would-be regicides.

For the Claudio family, it was nothing short of catastrophic.

Rather than receiving compensation for their son's grievous injuries, they now faced the very real threat of exposure and ruin.

And Samon's condition was dire.

Despite the family's frantic efforts, no physician—however skilled—could restore what had been lost.

"There is nothing more I can do. Focus on rehabilitation."

Even the renowned doctor Raju had secretly smuggled back from the Katzen Empire could only shake his head in defeat.

"Saintess, I beg of you! Please help our son! Grant the goddess's miracle to Samon!"

"How dare you speak our Saintess's name so casually! Remove this woman at once!"

Catherine's desperate plea to see Saintess Rachel ended before it began—she was turned away without so much as a glimpse of the holy woman's face.

The holy water they managed to procure proved equally useless.

In all the recorded history of the Holy Nation, no miracle had ever restored a limb already severed from the body.

That night, the crowds gathered on St. Augustine Street numbered in the thousands.

News of Samon Claudio's lost leg had spread across the continent like wildfire.

"A tragedy, to be sure—but seeing as it's a Claudio, I can't say I'm shedding any tears!"

A thoroughly inebriated man raised his beer in mock celebration.

"Tell me this—why was he down there in the first place? Everyone else was upstairs enjoying the festival, and he's skulking alone beneath the altar like some kind of rat?"

"Exactly! Suspicious, isn't it? They found gunpowder right where he was standing. I'd wager he planned to blow up His Majesty—only to have the whole thing explode in his face instead!"

The sparks of suspicion Medea had carefully planted through her network of informants now blazed into open flame.

"The goddess knew! She saw his wickedness and struck him down before he could act!"

"The goddess protects the Emperor of Valdina! No treacherous cur can lay a finger on His Majesty!"

"If only Samon Claudio had known that, he might have kept his limbs!"

"One leg's ruined for certain—but I wonder about the one in the middle!"

Raucous laughter erupted through the tavern.

Praise for Peleus and mockery of the Claudios grew in equal measure—one rising as the other fell.

"Why? Why has it come to this?!"

Joaquin felt as though he were losing his mind. Public sentiment had turned in directions he could never have anticipated—just as it had when Minister Etienne had humiliated his son and daughter before the court.

He seemed to have aged decades in the span of days.

"MEDEAAAAA!"

A piercing shriek tore through the mansion. It came from Samon's chamber.

"What is happening?! Have you all gone mad?!"

Joaquin shoved past the paralyzed servants and found his wife hurling objects across the room like a woman possessed.

"Mad? I should be mad! That girl—that wretched Medea—she's mocking us! Mocking our Samon!"

Catherine's face was flushed crimson, her expression twisted with rage.

"What are you—"

Joaquin's words died in his throat.

Scattered across the floor lay a basket of flowers. Beside it, shattered bottles that had once contained holy water.

"Look! Look with your own eyes!"

She thrust a gilt-edged card into his hands.

"They say the righteous are embraced by the gods, while the wicked are struck by the divine hammer. I pray that with the goddess's protection, dear Brother Samon recovers swiftly."

The card bore no signature. No seal.

Yet there was no mistaking that elegant, precise handwriting—nor the identity of someone who would spare no expense to twist the knife.

"Medea—did that girl come here in person?"

"Can you not see it with your own eyes?! That—that creature—Samon—!"

Catherine's words dissolved into incoherent sobbing as she collapsed beside her unconscious son's bed.

Joaquin crushed the card in his fist.

When Samon had begged and pleaded to see her, Medea had never once shown her face. But now—now that he lay broken and ruined—she had come in person?

What else could this be but naked mockery?

"The insolence... the sheer audacity..."

Joaquin's bloodshot eyes caught sight of a carriage through the window, standing in the mansion's courtyard.

The moment he recognized the royal coat of arms of Valdina emblazoned on its side, his vision went red.

"There! Stop her!"

Medea was just stepping into her carriage when Joaquin burst from the mansion like a man aflame.

"Medea, you damned woman!"

He hurled the crumpled card at her with all his strength. It struck the hem of her gown and fell to the cobblestones.

Swish.

Neril's hand flew to her sword hilt—but a pale, slender hand came to rest on her forearm, stilling the motion.

Medea turned to face her uncle with an expression of gentle concern.

"I am sorry, Uncle. I had not imagined you would distort my kindness so severely—though I confess I did not expect gratitude."

The soft arch of her brows. The honeyed cadence of her voice. The warmth in her tone.

She was indistinguishable from the Medea of old.

The same Medea who had once deceived Joaquin utterly—playing the role of a powerless half-princess while systematically dismantling everything he had built.

"You. This was your doing, Medea!"

Joaquin's voice cracked with fury and dawning horror.

He did not know the specifics. He could not trace the threads of her conspiracy.

But he had been deceived by her once before—had lost his title, his power, everything—and the instinct born of that humiliation screamed the truth at him now.

Medea was behind this. All of it.
