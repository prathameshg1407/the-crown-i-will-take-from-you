Chapter 164

Six Dead
Rats

"The devil's number lies buried beneath sacred ground."

"This... this is catastrophic..."

Paul tore away the blood-soaked bandages, and the words died in his throat. The wound beneath was a nightmare made flesh—putrid, festering, the edges blackened with corruption.

"Can you not sense the demonic taint?!"

"These are the marks of a Lich's claws. It appears the Saintess sustained this injury during the Ghoul Wave."

The Templar Commander examined the wounds with grim familiarity. Years of battling demonic forces had made him intimately acquainted with such afflictions.

"How could they allow the Saintess's condition to deteriorate to such an extent?! Have those Valdinians taken leave of their senses entirely?!"

Paul's weathered face contorted with fury.

"I will not let this stand. I shall confront them this very moment—"

"Your Eminence, there will be ample time to express your displeasure after we have saved the Saintess. Every moment is precious. Please, attend to her."

The Templar Commander intercepted Paul before he could storm from the cottage.

Paul knew the Commander spoke wisely. Among the Holy Nation's delegation, his divine power was paramount. Swallowing his rage with visible effort, he returned to the Saintess's bedside.

The High Priest's abilities should provide some measure of relief.

The Templar Commander's brow furrowed deeply. Something whispered that this ordeal would not conclude so simply.

Word of the Saintess's collapse spread through Valdina's palace like wildfire—though the true nature of her injuries remained shrouded in secrecy.

"Should the faithful learn that the Holy One suffers from demonic corruption, their devotion will waver. Instead, let us focus our efforts on restoring the Saintess to health."

"As you command, Your Eminence."

But contrary to their fervent prayers, Rachel showed no signs of recovery.

As fever ravaged her body and death's shadow crept ever closer, Paul felt his sanity beginning to fray.

"Why does her strength continue to fade? Neither medicine nor holy water yields any effect!"

Paul's eyes grew bloodshot with exhaustion and dread. The prospect of losing the long-awaited Saintess—of returning her to the Goddess's embrace before she had even begun her sacred work—was unthinkable.

He would prevent that outcome at any cost.

"Your Eminence... might this be a case of prophetic fever?"

The Templar Commander spoke with careful hesitation.

"If neither medicine nor divine power can restore her, perhaps she has encountered some malevolent presence nearby. Her suffering may herald an imminent catastrophe here in Valdina."

Throughout the Holy Land's long history, there had been Saintesses who experienced visions of the future through their very flesh—enduring the coming calamity before it arrived.

"Do not speak such foolishness! How could an emissary sent by the Goddess herself be touched by evil spirits?"

Before Paul could finish his rebuke, Rachel's eyes fluttered open.

"Oh... I am frightened. I sense it... the Goddess speaks..."

Her voice emerged distant, fevered, as though she gazed upon something beyond mortal sight.

Frightened?

Paul quickly pressed holy water to her cracked lips. Only then did the crimson flush begin to recede from her cheeks. With great effort, she began to speak.

"I dreamed... a terrible dream."

Her voice trembled with remembered horror.

"I was surrounded by six malevolent spirits. They reached for me from beneath the earth... clawing up through dense undergrowth... desperate to devour me..."

A shudder wracked her slender frame.

"Oh... I am so frightened..."

Paul's mind raced.

Something about her fevered ramblings struck a discordant note. The aftermath of the Ghoul Wave on the distant plains could not account for the specificity of her vision.

"Surely not..."

"Your Eminence—!"

A sudden memory seized him. Without warning, Paul bolted from the room.

Something had nagged at his senses since his arrival.

This building...

His gaze swept across the landscaping surrounding the cottage house, settling on the most prominent tree among them.

The malevolent energy that had no place amid such beauty had been scratching at his consciousness the entire time.

Paul dropped to his knees and began to dig like a man possessed.

He gave no thought to the dirt accumulating beneath his aged fingernails, no concern for the mud staining his cream-colored vestments.

"Your Eminence! What in the Goddess's name are you doing?!"

The Templar Commander watched in bewildered alarm, acutely aware of the court ladies gaping at the spectacle of a High Priest frantically excavating the grounds of a foreign monarch's palace.

"Please forgive this intrusion. The High Priest is sometimes given to... impulsive actions. I humbly request your understanding—"

But there was no need for apologies.

"Look! This is why!"

The reason for Paul's frenzy emerged from the overturned earth.

"What—what in all the hells is that?!"

Screams erupted from the maids.

Six dead rats, bound together in a grotesque bundle, lay exposed before the High Priest.

The Curse Revealed

"Wh... what manner of abomination...?"

Rachel's face, still supported by the Templar Commander, drained to the pallor of fresh parchment.

"I knew it. My suspicions are confirmed."

Paul's entire body trembled—not with fear, but with righteous fury.

Resting atop the rodent corpses lay a dagger.

The right foreleg of each rat had been severed, the dismembered limbs arranged beneath the gleaming golden blade.

The sight was as bizarre as it was horrifying. Six—the devil's number—represented by vermin corpses, buried directly beneath the Saintess's chamber.

"This is unmistakably a curse directed at the Saintess!"

Paul declared his verdict without hesitation.

The Saintess's raging fever, her inexplicable suffering—all of it stemmed from this vile ritual.

"What manner of wretched creature would dare slander the Goddess's chosen representative?! They shall be cast into the deepest pit of hell, condemned to wander the eternal flames for all eternity!"

Paul lifted the dagger from amid the carnage. The brilliant silver blade caught the sunlight, throwing prismatic reflections across the garden.

Though flecks of dried blood marred its surface, the weapon's composition was unmistakable.

"That is mithril."

Someone spoke as though entranced.

Mithril—rarer than diamonds, more precious than gold. A natural resource so scarce that entire kingdoms had gone to war over single deposits.

Judging from the dried blood staining the blade, it had been used to sever the rats' forelegs before burial.

What manner of monster would employ such a treasured weapon for so base a curse?

And then—the detail that made Paul's blood run cold.

The severed forelegs all came from the same side as Rachel's festering wound.

"Her Highness!"

Rachel's scream shattered the horrified silence.

Moments Earlier — The Palace Corridors

While High Priest Paul had been frantically excavating the garden, word of the Saintess's sudden illness had reached the Valdinian court.

"Your Majesty, do my eyes deceive me? What precisely is the High Priest doing over there?"

Sissair halted mid-stride, his eyes narrowing behind his spectacles. Beside him walked Peleus and Medea.

"We have already dispatched Sir Hertos. Priests, physicians, senators—we have extended every possible courtesy."

"Peleus. You understand that we must demonstrate goodwill first, regardless of our personal feelings? This is the Holy Nation we are dealing with."

At Medea's counsel, the party proceeded toward the cottage house. The King's Guard, D'Angelo among them, followed in their wake.

As they entered, the rising commotion caused Peleus to raise a hand, bringing the procession to an abrupt halt.

The moment he glimpsed the putrid rat carcasses strewn across the earth, instinct took over. He shifted his position, placing himself between Medea and the grotesque display.

"Do you see, Your Majesty?"

Paul's voice rang with accusation.

"These abominations were buried here, within your palace grounds. The air was so saturated with cursed implements that our Saintess was driven to the brink of death by fever. How does Valdina intend to explain this atrocity?"

His trembling words barely contained his fury toward the kingdom.

How long had he awaited the Goddess's chosen emissary—only to nearly lose her through such vile treachery?

Peleus's cold blue eyes swept across the scene, methodically assessing every detail.

And then Rachel made her move.

"Her Highness...!"

Having confirmed that every relevant party had assembled, she allowed her eyes to widen with dawning horror. Her trembling hand rose, finger extended.

It pointed directly at Medea.

"Her Highness the Princess... she knew of my injuries."

Every gaze in the garden pivoted toward the Princess, who had been so suddenly thrust to the center of the gathering storm.
