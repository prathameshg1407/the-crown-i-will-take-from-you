Chapter 331

Through the
Veil of Snow

"In the silence between heartbeats, she finally heard the truth she had been running from."

Where am I?

When Medea finally emerged from her daze, she found herself standing utterly alone in the heart of an endless white expanse. The realization struck her like a blade of ice—somewhere along the treacherous path, she had strayed impossibly far from her companions.

"Cesare!"

Her desperate cry dissolved into the hollow echoes of the mountains, swallowed whole by the vast indifference of stone and sky.

The cave where Cesare had been seen in the phantom sculpture's vision was nowhere to be found. The mountain's steep flanks lay buried beneath layers of treacherous snow, each step a gamble between solid ground and a plunge into the abyss. Recognizing any landmark—let alone locating a hidden cave—was an exercise in futility.

Shhhhh... crack.

A savage blizzard descended without warning.

The snowflakes that lashed against her face cut like shards of glass, each one drawing invisible lines of pain across her exposed skin. With every labored step, her feet sank deeper into the drifts, the snow clutching at her ankles like desperate hands.

The relentless snowfall erased her footprints almost as quickly as she made them, obliterating any hope of retracing her path.

Nothing but white. An infinite, suffocating white in every direction.

Medea pressed forward, guided by nothing but instinct, until even that fragile compass spun uselessly. At some imperceptible moment, she realized she had lost all sense of direction entirely.

The sky darkened overhead as twilight crept in, yet the merciless wind showed no sign of relenting. The profound isolation—the bone-deep terror of facing nature's vast indifference utterly alone—wrapped around her chest like iron bands, squeezing until each breath became a struggle.

Did Cesare endure this same torment?

The thought surfaced unbidden, bringing with it a cascade of realizations.

She understood now—why he had launched that relentless assault against the Golden Tower, why he had hunted down the warlocks with such merciless precision.

"It was all... for me, wasn't it?"

The truth had crystallized during her journey here. Under ordinary circumstances, Cesare would have bided his time, waiting for the warlocks to overreach, to break their own taboos and expose themselves before striking. Patience had always been one of his greatest weapons.

But this time, he had abandoned caution entirely. The sole reason he had moved to eradicate every warlock still hidden in shadow—even those who posed no immediate threat—was to ensure Medea's safety.

It was vengeance by proxy. Retribution for the life she had lived in her previous existence, when those same dark practitioners had manipulated her fate like puppeteers pulling at silken strings.

If not for me, he would never have had to endure this frozen hell.

Medea's teeth found her lower lip, biting down until she tasted copper.

What if I truly cannot find him? What if somewhere in this endless expanse of mountain and ice, I fail to reach Cesare in time? If that happens, I...

It was then that the voice came.

"Ahahaha! What's this, Medea? Are those tears I see? How utterly pathetic!"

A voice she knew too well—one she had never wished to hear again—pierced through the howling wind.

Medea's head snapped upward.

Rachel stood before her, lips curved in that familiar, poisonous smile.

"Well then... why don't you just die?"

CRASH—!

The instant Rachel lunged, Medea's blade sang through the air in a deadly arc, cleaving straight through the specter's form.

The gray figure shaped from swirling snow dissolved into silence, scattering like ash on the wind.

An afterimage—nothing more than a phantom conjured from the residual darkness still lingering within the warlock's barrier.

"Didn't I warn you, girl? Everyone who stays close to you meets their end. You should know that by now."

Claudio's familiar, contemptuous voice emerged from the storm, his form materializing from shadow and frost. Medea's sword flashed again, and he too dispersed into nothing.

One after another, the phantoms manifested from the darkness—each one bearing a face from her nightmares—and one by one, she struck them down.

"Mama, please save me! Mama, I don't want to die!"

"Mama!"

But this final apparition stopped her cold.

When her children emerged from the swirling tempest, Medea could not move. Her limbs refused to obey, her sword arm frozen mid-strike.

The small figure of her son, who had been weeping at her feet, began to grow—stretching, distorting—until it transformed into Rachel's mocking silhouette.

"Mama, why did you send Lian away?"

No—it was Rachel, wearing Lian's voice like a stolen mask, each word designed to cut deeper than any blade.

The hand that gripped her sword trembled uncontrollably.

"Ahahaha! Did you truly believe this life would be any different? Such arrogance! Look around you—who stands beside you now? No one. Not a single soul!"

The demon wearing Rachel's face giggled with malicious glee, circling Medea's staggering form like a predator savoring its prey.

The gray blizzard spiraling around her grew denser, more suffocating, closing in from all sides.

You know the truth. This is nothing but an illusion—dark magic designed to break you.

Medea sank her teeth into her lip again, fighting desperately against the darkness threatening to consume her sanity.

But when she saw the children—when their innocent faces appeared before her—maintaining her grip on reality became nearly impossible, even with the sharp, metallic taste of blood grounding her.

"Cesare will die because of you, just like all the others! Just like your brother! Just like your children!"

"They didn't die."

Her voice emerged as barely a whisper.

"Oh, but they will! And you—you'll be devoured here, your bones scattered across this frozen wasteland without even a corpse to mourn! If only you had stayed away, they might all still be alive!"

"STOP!"

Medea pressed her palms against her ears, shutting out the poisonous words. And in that silence, she spoke—half to herself, half as a desperate prayer.

"No. That isn't true. None of it is true."

He's alive. And he will return to me. I know he will...

A single, ice-cold snowflake drifted down to rest against the corner of her burning eyes.

Something deep within her—something she had buried so far down she'd nearly forgotten its existence—finally broke free and surged to the surface.

Only now, at the precipice of despair, did she recognize it for what it was.

Fear.

"Please..."

The word escaped her lips like a whispered confession, barely audible above the wind.

Even Medea herself had not realized how desperately intertwined her heart had become with Cesare's fate—how the thought of his death felt less like loss and more like the unraveling of her own existence.

"God, please... No—anyone who can hear me..."

Help me. Guide me to him.

Since the moment she had been granted a second chance—since she had traveled back through the currents of time with vengeance burning in her heart—Medea had systematically severed herself from every emotion that might cloud her judgment.

Anything that could become a hindrance to her goals was ruthlessly excised.

I know better than anyone how love can blind a person. How it can transform them into a fool.

She had witnessed firsthand how blind devotion could destroy everything it touched, leading even the wisest souls down the path to ruin and despair.

And so, Medea had been afraid. She had spent years avoiding that terrifying vulnerability, sidestepping it at every turn, burying it beneath layers of ice and purpose.

She had yearned for this life to be different—had prayed for it with every fiber of her being—yet when confronted with the most significant change of all, she had retreated. One step back, then another, always maintaining that careful distance.

"Medea. Give me your command. Point me toward your enemies, and I will cut them down. Tell me your wishes, and I will make them reality."

Among all the changes this new life had brought, the most profound—the true turning point of Medea's existence—had been Cesare of Katzen.

"If you command me to stop, I will stop. But not now—not when the entire world has turned its blade against you."

He had shielded her without hesitation, positioning himself between Medea and the sharpened edge of the world's judgment.

Cesare alone had worked to erase the wounds of her previous life—those scars that ran so deep they had become part of her soul—gently replacing them with new memories, new moments, a new timeline unmarked by tragedy.

No.

I refuse to lose him. I cannot lose him—not like this.

A fierce, desperate longing surged through her veins like wildfire.

In a place within herself where even Medea had believed nothing remained—where she had been certain that all her capacity for feeling had turned to ash and scattered on forgotten winds—something stirred.

And in that moment of perfect clarity, she finally understood.

"Cesare..."

She understood his feelings for her. And more terrifying still—she understood her own.

"I'm sorry for always doing things my own way, Dea."

She should never have let him walk away that day.

His final words should not have been an apology—should not have been him taking the blame for caring too much, for loving too openly.

She should have reached out and caught those retreating fingertips. She should have held on and refused to let go. She should have spoken the truth that had been lodged in her throat like a stone.

I feel the same way you do.

"I won't retreat anymore. I won't hide from this any longer. So please..."

Cesare—bring him back to me.

Alone in the howling darkness, stripped of every hope she had clung to, with no expectation of salvation—only then did her true feelings finally break through the frozen surface of her heart.

It was then that something impossible happened.

The necklace resting against her chest—the one she had carried without truly understanding its purpose—rose gently into the air of its own accord, floating before her like a living thing.

The edges of her vision began to blur, colors bleeding into one another as though reality itself was being painted anew.

A faint, luminous shape materialized before her—indistinct at first, then gradually coalescing into the figure of a woman.

Silver hair, identical to Medea's own. Eyes the deep, verdant green of spring leaves. Features that seemed hauntingly, achingly familiar.

The woman extended her hand toward Medea, palm upturned in gentle invitation.

WHOOSH—!

In that same instant, Rachel's mocking specter shattered like glass. The oppressive gray blizzard that had surrounded Medea dissolved into nothing, swept away as though it had never existed.

The woman took Medea's trembling hand in her own—her touch warm despite the impossible cold—and helped her rise from where she had fallen to her knees.

Then, with infinite tenderness, she lifted the golden thread of light extending from the necklace and pressed it directly into Medea's palm.

"..."

The eyes gazing down at her held such kindness, such profound sorrow, that Medea felt her breath catch in her chest.

And woven through that gaze was something else—a thread of longing so deep it transcended death itself.

"...Mother?"

The word emerged as barely a whisper, uncertain and raw.

A memory stirred in the deepest recesses of Medea's mind—ancient, half-forgotten, buried so long ago that she had sealed it away on the far side of consciousness. But she had seen this face before. She knew it the way a child knows the sound of a lullaby.

Instead of answering, the woman simply smiled—a soft, sorrowful curve of her lips—and leaned forward to press a gentle kiss to Medea's forehead.

Then she reached out once more, touching the golden beam of light that stretched from the floating necklace.

Tinnng—!

A crystalline note rang out, pure as a harp string plucked by divine fingers.

And in that same instant, the woman vanished—dissolving into motes of golden light that drifted upward like prayers ascending to heaven.

The blizzard had ceased entirely. When Medea looked up, she realized the storm had stopped completely, the world around her gone still and silent.

"...Mother, was that you? Did you help me?"

Medea raised a trembling hand to her cheek, wiping away the tear that had traced a warm path down her frozen skin. She swallowed against the surge of longing that threatened to overwhelm her—emotions too vast and too old to name.

No more phantoms manifested before her eyes. The malicious shadows had retreated, their power broken.

In their place, a ribbon of golden light stretched across the white landscape, luminous and unwavering—a path traced across the snow like the orbital trail of Venus across the night sky.

Medea followed the golden thread, placing one foot before the other, step by careful step.

And finally—at long last—she stopped.

"Cesare!"

Before her stood the dark mouth of a cave, waiting in the mountainside like an answer to prayer.

At last, Medea had found the courage to face her own heart.

And in doing so, she had found her way back to him.

End of Chapter 331
