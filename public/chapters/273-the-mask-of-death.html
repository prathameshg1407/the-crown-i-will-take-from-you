Chapter 273

The Mask of
Death

"The dead do not speak—but sometimes, they listen."

Shortly after Medea's unsteady departure from the corridor, an attendant appeared at Rachel's side.

A moment later, a cup jostled by a careless acolyte toppled across the table.

Splash!

"My sincerest apologies, Saintess!"

The liquid soaked through Rachel's cream-colored vestments. High Priest Paul's face contorted with fury as he rounded on the trembling acolyte.

"You clumsy fool! How dare you—"

In his outrage, Paul slammed his palm against the table.

The impact sent his chair scraping backward—and its edge caught Rachel's right hand.

"—!"

Rachel's face twisted with agony, though not a sound escaped her lips.

"Saintess? Are you injured?"

Paul leaned forward in alarm, scanning her hands for any sign of harm. But her pale fingers appeared unmarked, pristine as ever.

"It's nothing of consequence. Please, calm yourself, High Priest."

Rachel turned smoothly away, tucking her right hand beneath the folds of her sleeve.

Her serene smile never wavered as she adjusted her damp garments.

"A simple accident. I'll need to change my robes, that's all. Please—don't punish him too severely on my account."

She glided from the corridor as though nothing had happened, swallowing the pain that clawed down her spine like a living thing.

The central temple's passageways lay shrouded in thick darkness, silent as a held breath.

The acolyte from moments before materialized at Rachel's side, a shadow among shadows.

"The one guarding her—is it dealt with?"

"Yes."

His hood pulled low over his features, he led the way through the gloom.

The chapel door groaned open on ancient hinges.

Inside, Neril lay crumpled on the stone floor—unconscious, a dagger buried in her side, dark blood trailing from the corner of her mouth. The evidence of a brutal struggle marked the space around her.

A knight of fewer than ten stars, and she caused this much trouble?

Rachel's lip curled with disdain.

The memory of how that woman had shadowed Medea like a devoted hound only sharpened her irritation.

"Well done."

Rachel stepped forward and ground her heel into the back of Neril's hand.

She pressed down with all her weight, as though trying to pulverize the bones beneath her foot—but the unconscious knight gave no response.

Satisfied, Rachel moved deeper into the chapel.

And there, slumped against the cold stone wall, lay Medea.

Rachel's heart hammered against her ribs.

She approached with quick, eager steps—then slowed, crouching deliberately before the motionless princess.

She held her fingers beneath Medea's nose.

Nothing.

Only the faintest residual warmth lingered against her skin. No breath stirred. No pulse beat.

The clammy coldness distinctive of death seeped into her fingertips.

"Ahaha! Finally—it's done!"

A cry of pure, rapturous triumph burst from Rachel's throat.

"Mother! I did it!"

Venus and prophecy—all of it ends here. This wretched girl will never draw breath again.

"Child of prophecy? What a jest. It's finished. Now everything is—"

Rachel caught herself, checking the time. She couldn't afford a prolonged absence. The toast had yet to begin.

"...Take the body to the Chamber of Judgment."

She issued the command quietly to the priest hovering at her side.

This wretch's usefulness isn't exhausted yet.

The Chamber of Judgment would serve as the perfect stage for unveiling this grand finale to the world.

Rachel swallowed her surging joy in silence.

Shadows stir in darkness

"Bring her to the Chamber of Judgment."

With that cold command, Rachel turned her back and departed the chapel.

The acolyte carried the princess's limp form through the darkened corridors toward the Chamber of Judgment.

He never saw the shadow detach itself from the darkness behind him.

"Wha—!"

A silver blade flashed like summer lightning, piercing his chest before he could draw breath. Simultaneously, a short knife kissed the nape of his neck.

Before he could scream, the priest crumpled to the floor.

As Medea's body began to fall, strong arms caught her from the shadows.

Moonlight streaming through a high window illuminated the man's face.

Theo—the handsome paladin, his expression carved from granite.

He cradled Medea with infinite care, carrying her deeper into the Chamber before laying her gently upon the cold altar.

His breath came ragged. He swallowed hard.

"Your Highness."

Silence.

His trembling hand reached toward her—then drew back, pressing instead against the unforgiving stone of the altar.

She lay utterly still, eyes closed, unmoving as a marble effigy.

"...Are you all right?"

Nothing.

Ice flooded his veins.

Doubt

I followed the plan precisely. So why... why isn't she waking?

He had switched the cups before she lost consciousness. What Medea had drunk wasn't Rachel's poison—it was a carefully formulated compound designed to suspend breath and pulse temporarily, deceiving even close examination.

The dosage and concentration had been calculated with precision. Timing and efficacy accounted for down to the final second.

He had prepared everything perfectly. Or so he had believed.

What if I made a mistake?

Had the dosage been too potent? Had her constitution reacted differently than anticipated? Was there some unforeseen complication he hadn't accounted for?

Dark imaginings clawed at the edges of his mind, threatening to consume him.

"I should never have agreed to use her body as bait."

This cold, merciless woman who treated everyone—including herself—as nothing more than chess pieces to be sacrificed upon her altar of vengeance.

The silence of her unmoving form pressed against Theo like a blade at his throat.

"I'm terrified the operation will fail. That we'll squander our one chance to expose the false Saintess. That's why I'm afraid."

He told himself this was the sole reason for his terror.

There could be no other.

"Wake up!"

Just as the cold began seeping into his fingertips—

Medea's eyes opened.

Those cool, indifferent green eyes—exactly as he remembered them—gazed up at him with crystalline clarity.

"Thank the Goddess..."

Theo's forehead dropped against the altar as relief crashed through him in overwhelming waves.

Medea sat up slowly, utterly unmoved by his emotional display.

"The Covenant Cup. Is it prepared?"

Her voice emerged calm and businesslike—as though she hadn't just returned from the threshold of death itself.

"Yes, Your Highness. Everything is in place."

Theo composed himself and answered with renewed steadiness.

In mere moments, when the Saintess's true nature was revealed, the genuine Covenant Cup—the one Rachel had poisoned—would be presented to the Pope and High Priests as irrefutable evidence.

"Your Highness... how did you know she would choose the Chamber of Judgment?"

The question had haunted him since receiving his orders.

From distant Valdina, without any knowledge of the temple's inner workings, the Princess had predicted the Saintess's actions with uncanny precision.

Rachel would inevitably seek to extract maximum value from my death.

"She needs to present my demise as divine punishment—a means of restoring her tarnished authority as Saintess."

Medea's gaze swept across the chamber, taking in every shadow, every hidden corner.

"There is no stage more suited to such a performance than this sacred place, where the Goddess herself is said to perceive sins invisible to mortal eyes and render perfect justice."

"But Rachel focused only on what she expected to see."

A faint smile touched Medea's lips.

"She saw the statue of the Goddess towering at the center. She failed to notice the rest."

The Stage Is Set

Behind the altar, what appeared to be an enormous ceremonial flame was in truth a cleverly disguised glass wall—a hidden observation gallery where witnesses could observe everything that transpired within the chamber, unseen.

And beside it, concealed behind a casually draped curtain, hung the Mirror of Essence—transferred from the Mirror Room for this precise purpose.

A mirror that stripped away all pretense and revealed only truth.

A wall of silent witnesses.

A stage prepared for one performer alone.

"Now then—it's time for our audience to take their seats."

At Medea's quiet command, Theo straightened and gave a firm nod.

The snake approaches

Back in the central corridor, the celebration continued unabated.

Music and laughter filled the air like perfume. Elderly guests lingered at tables laden with crystal, savoring glasses of World Tree nectar. On the dance floor, young nobles twirled in elegant pairs to the gentle melody of harps.

Rachel surveyed the scene from the entrance, her violet eyes sweeping across the oblivious revelers.

No one had noticed the Princess's prolonged absence.

"When the sacred hymn sounds, I shall return to bestow my blessing upon you all."

Rachel's voice carried its customary gentle warmth.

"For now, I must withdraw to attend to my duties of prayer."

With the Pope and High Priests already departed, her excuse was accepted without question.

Rachel slipped from the corridor.

Fools. All of them—laughing and dancing without the faintest inkling of what has transpired.

Her gaze drifted toward the Agema knights, who had joined the mercenaries in boisterous displays of martial prowess, their laughter echoing through the hall.

That joyful noise will soon transform into wailing.

Rachel's heart raced with anticipation.

She savored the image that bloomed in her mind: those bright, carefree faces drowning one by one in the despair that was about to descend upon them.

Moments later, she reached the Chapel of the Virgin.

Rachel stepped inside.

And walked directly into the trap that had been laid for her.
