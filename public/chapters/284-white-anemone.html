"Every flower carries a message. Some carry poison. The wise know which is which."

The First Prince escorted her with impeccable precision, positioning himself as a shield between her and the watching crowd as they ascended the cathedral steps together.

Perhaps because their grand wedding had already taken place in the Holy Kingdom, the formal ceremony here carried a different weight—less spectacle, more gravity.

Standing at Medea's side, Cesare appeared almost... ordinary. Like any man who cherished his wife.

Surely that cannot be the First Prince. Perhaps some devil has donned his face as a mask.

When he caught Medea's eye and smiled—genuinely smiled—the assembled nobles found themselves rubbing their eyes in disbelief.

This was not the cold, dangerous predator they had all learned to fear. This version of the First Prince was utterly unfamiliar.

The Prince and his bride walked the length of the crimson carpet stretching toward the altar.

The pipe organ swelled belatedly into life, filling the vaulted space with ancient, ceremonial melody.

"Your Highness the First Prince. Your Highness the Princess."

"We offer our congratulations."

As the couple drew near, the highest-ranking nobles of Katzen rose from their seats in succession.

Only those who outranked the First Prince were permitted to remain seated during his approach—namely, the Emperor and his consorts.

But the Emperor had not deigned to attend.

His throne stood conspicuously empty.

Cesare's gaze fell upon that vacant seat, and the warmth in his eyes froze to ice.

First he refuses to attend the wedding. Now the coronation as well.

The insult was deliberate. Unmistakable.

At the altar's end stood an elderly figure robed in ceremonial vestments, holy oil and sacred texts arranged before him.

"This humble servant of God, Norman, greets His Highness the First Prince and Her Highness the Princess."

The old man's voice emerged as a low, reverent murmur.

High Priest Norman had originally hailed from the Holy Kingdom, but decades spent presiding over Katzen's branch of the old church had shifted his loyalties. He now served the Imperial Family far more faithfully than he served distant Rome.

His rheumy eyes swept over Medea in silent assessment.

He recalled the urgent missive the Pope had dispatched not long ago.

This woman... a true Saintess, they claim.

His weathered lips pressed into a thin, disapproving line.

"Render the Saintess every assistance," His Holiness commands. The old fool must be losing his faculties—swayed by a pair of pretty blue eyes.

A flicker of disdain crossed Norman's noble features.

He had no intention of treating this young woman as anything sacred until he witnessed proof with his own eyes.

***

The ritual commenced.

Medea and Cesare knelt before the altar, voices rising in unison as they recited the ancient vows. They lit the imperial candlesticks, flames dancing in the dim cathedral light.

"Place your hands upon the Seat of the Oath."

Their palms came to rest upon the stone tablet, fingers overlapping.

This tablet—legend held—bore the eternal covenant sworn by the first Emperor to his Empress at the founding of the realm. It was revered as the primal symbol of Katzen's legitimacy, the sacred origin from which all imperial bloodlines descended.

"I offer this blood and this breath to You, Almighty One."

After the oaths to gods and empire had been spoken, High Priest Norman anointed their joined hands with consecrated oil.

Upon the ancient stone, the oil spread like liquid gold—and from its surface, golden flames rose.

Cesare Dwysler Katzen.
Medea Casey de Katzen.

Beneath the dense lineage of Katzen's royal house, Medea's name was inscribed beside Cesare's.

Medea stared at the flickering flames, her green eyes distant, as though gazing into another time entirely.

I wanted this so desperately once.

Old memories superimposed themselves upon the golden light.

The years she had spent at Jason's side. Her children in her arms. The endless, exhausting struggle to establish roots in Katzen's hostile soil.

The hunger that was never satisfied. The attachment that was never returned.

Perhaps it had been the knowledge—always present, never acknowledged—that she was not truly his wife. Not in any way that mattered.

A gentle pressure around her fingers drew Medea back to the present.

She looked up.

Clear golden eyes gazed down at her, steady and warm.

Cesare's thumb traced a slow arc across the back of her hand.

The gesture was silent. But it spoke volumes.

It felt like comfort offered to a lonely past—to a woman who had drifted like a rootless plant, never finding purchase, never finding home.

Medea closed her eyes, feeling warmth bloom beneath her skin.

Without conscious thought, she slipped her fingers between his, her slender pinky curling around his ring finger and holding tight.

The interlaced grip quieted the anxiety rising in her chest, stilling the restless current of her thoughts.

The assembled nobles watched the ceremony unfold—and found themselves increasingly unsettled.

How can she be so flawless?

They had been so distracted by the First Prince's unprecedented gentleness that they had failed to notice until now.

The Princess of Valdina had not committed a single misstep.

How is this possible?

Her kneeling posture during the oath had been refined to perfection—not a tremor, not a wavering line.

When reciting the vows, she had pronounced the archaic phrases and ceremonial rhythms with flawless precision—something even most members of Katzen's own royal family struggled to achieve.

And when she lit the sacred bronze tower, her movements were so natural she might have been born within these walls.

Someone's lips curled in barely concealed frustration.

"Hmph. How desperately she must have practiced. Standing beside His Highness the First Prince! She must have rehearsed until she collapsed, terrified of embarrassing herself."

But everyone present understood the truth.

Katzen's court etiquette could not be mastered overnight. The empire prided itself on protocols so thorough, so deliberately complex, that they served as barriers against outsiders.

The noble ladies who had anticipated the Princess's clumsy fumbling now hastily raised their fans to hide their chagrin.

The Western Empress Dowager observed Medea upon the altar, her gaze cold as winter stone.

"I suspected she would prove formidable. She exceeds my expectations at every turn."

Her eyes hardened.

"I have no intention of allowing this Valdina woman to sink her claws into my empire."

In this matter, at least, she and the Eastern Empress Dowager stood united.

Neither woman intended to cede an inch of influence to the new Princess.

She may not know how to kneel properly yet.

That proud neck needed to be bent—just once. Broken against a wall hard enough to leave a permanent impression.

Once someone learned the taste of defeat, they learned to bow preemptively. That was simply the way of the world.

The Western Empress's eyes met those of the High Priest upon the altar.

She lowered her chin in the faintest of signals.

***

"Medea Casey of Katzen."

The joint ceremony had concluded. Now came the individual rites, and Cesare's portion was complete.

"Born of Valdina's blood, yet now breathing Katzen's air. You entered this realm as a foreigner, but from this moment forward, you shall become the blood and bone of this land."

High Priest Norman touched consecrated oil to Medea's forehead.

"By oath sworn before God, and in the name of the Imperial House of Katzen, I hereby recognize you as the lawful consort of First Prince Cesare Dwysler Katzen—and as Crown Princess of this Empire."

A delicate coronet descended upon Medea's silver hair.

It was lighter than an Empress's crown, but its brilliant gleam left no doubt of the authority it represented.

Medea turned slowly to face the congregation.

A collective intake of breath rippled through the cathedral.

"Your Highness. You must now offer tribute."

At Norman's words, priests emerged bearing silver trays laden with ceremonial bouquets.

"Upon this sacred seat of succession, where you have sworn your oath, you shall honor the gods and this empire by presenting your offering."

Norman selected the largest, most resplendent bouquet from the tray and extended it toward Medea.

Each petal was immaculate, the blooms so fresh their fragrance perfumed the air the moment she reached for them.

Medea's hand paused mid-motion.

Her gaze fixed upon the High Priest.

Then, slowly, she surveyed the cathedral—the altar where she and Cesare stood, elevated before the eyes of every noble in attendance.

"Is something amiss, Your Highness?"

Norman's voice carried a note of impatience.

But Medea's green eyes had begun to gleam with a light that revealed nothing of her thoughts.

What an amusing little game.

Medea's fingertips brushed the edge of the bouquet.

Nestled among the blossoms were white anemones.

To an outsider—to anyone unfamiliar with Katzen's history—they would seem perfectly innocent. Beautiful, even.

But white anemones have only one meaning in this empire.

They were flowers of mourning—traditionally offered to commemorate a usurped bloodline.

Legend held that the Crown Prince who died during Katzen's founding had clutched white anemones in his final moments. Because of that grim symbolism, they had been forbidden from all imperial ceremonies ever since.

If Medea placed this bouquet upon the throne...

The Emperor will erupt. He'll see it as a deliberate attack on his legitimacy.

Who arranged this? The Eastern Empress would never dare touch such a sensitive nerve—she remains too dependent on his favor.

Which leaves only one possibility.

The Western Empress Dowager. She alone possessed both the boldness and the motive to strike at her husband's deepest wound.

I haven't even set foot in the imperial palace, and already the attacks begin.

A faint smile curved Medea's lips.

You haven't changed at all, have you?

The coronation was not yet complete, yet the Western Empress had already moved to destroy her.

She means to break my neck before I can gain any foothold. A princess who earns the Emperor's hatred will never hold power in this court.

Medea's fingers closed around the bouquet's stem.

Her smile did not waver.
