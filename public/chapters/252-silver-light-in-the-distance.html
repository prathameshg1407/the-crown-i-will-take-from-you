Chapter 252

Silver Light in the Distance

When the past arrives uninvited, even the strongest defenses begin to crack.

At the campfire where the three men had disappeared, Gallo sat alone, drinking mead with methodical determination.

"Gallo, why do you keep pouring that down your throat when you're just going to vomit it all back up?"

Terence's voice carried the weary exasperation of someone who'd seen this pattern too many times before.

"Honestly? If it weren't for Her Highness today, I'd be dead. I'm sitting here trying to figure out why I'm still breathing and not rotting in demon guts."

Late at night, the sweet scent of honey wine seemed to loosen tongues and unlock hearts. Gallo poured out his confession like the liquor flowing into his cup.

"I was... surprised, I suppose. This forest wasn't as manageable as I'd imagined."

He lowered his head, staring into the amber depths of his drink.

"For a moment, I felt genuine fear. We'll soon face the evil dragon itself, and I found myself wondering—can I actually be useful? Even if Her Highness knows this forest like the back of her hand, will we truly be able to destroy that monster?"

His fingertips trembled almost imperceptibly around the wooden cup.

"But on the other hand, I'm... excited. It's been so long since I've risked everything like this. I can't even properly explain the feeling..."

"This is territory even seasoned rangers find unfamiliar. Your reactions are perfectly natural—emotional and human."

"Hah, don't talk like you're some kind of ancient sage."

Pssht—!

In that instant, Gallo's eyes snapped toward the darkness. With reflexes honed by countless battles, he hurled his axe like a bolt of lightning.

The sharp iron blade sliced through the air with lethal intent, its whistle promising violence.

But instead of cleaving through demon flesh, the weapon was caught mid-flight—plucked from the air like a child's toy in a massive hand.

Gallo's brow furrowed dangerously.

A bipedal, humanoid demon capable of using tools. One that can mask its presence until it's practically on top of you...

His hand moved to his sword, every muscle coiling for the fight to come—

"Your Highness, are you here?"

A deep, resonant voice emerged from the shadowed figure—unmistakably human.

The dim firelight crept across the stranger's torso, illuminating details that had been hidden in darkness. His armor was filthy, covered in dried crimson blood and forest debris, but on his chest plate was engraved an unmistakable symbol: Agema's running horse.

"Captain D'Angel?"

The Sylvarand blanket slipped from Neril's hands, forgotten.

The next morning, thanks to the combined magic of the World Tree sap and the Sylvarand's enchantment, Medea woke refreshed—free of nightmares for the first time in recent memory.

That relief, however, evaporated the moment she spotted a familiar face among the expedition members.

"Peleus sent you?"

She pressed her hand to her forehead, already feeling the beginnings of a headache.

"Your Highness, with all due respect—how could you possibly leave out Agema when hunting an evil dragon?"

D'Angel, who had already been briefed on Kensington's plans, expressed his reproach with the bluntness of a loyal soldier.

"Your Highness, that's precisely why I insisted on bringing the Captain. Look at what he accomplished—he traversed this forest alone, without a guide, and found us. That's not skill. That's madness."

Tom shook his head in grudging admiration.

'That man... he might not actually be human.'

"How did I navigate here? I followed human traces. Who else would venture this deep into the forest besides you? Anyone else, and we'd have had a very different kind of meeting."

"And the demons you encountered?"

"I killed them."

The matter-of-fact delivery explained the demon blood coating D'Angel's armor when he'd first appeared.

"But regardless—you are our princess. I knew that the strong, unbreakable bloodline of Valdina would never simply flee from danger."

D'Angel turned and bowed deeply to both the Facade and Cesare, his gesture carrying the weight of genuine respect.

Though it pained him that Medea had needed to seek help from the Prince of the Katzen Empire...

"Valdina never forgets kindness or debts. I will not forget this hand extended to my Princess. His Majesty will remember as well."

This declaration, naturally, was made without Peleus's explicit consent.

D'Angel studied Cesare carefully—the First Prince who had provided Medea with material support, emotional stability, and even his own presence on an expedition to hunt an evil dragon.

'No one risks their life for others in a battle with uncertain outcomes. Not even for their own subordinates, typically.'

Yet this Prince had revealed his true identity, discarding the mask of"Facade" he'd maintained for decades.

D'Angel, who had spent countless years crossing the firing lines of battlefields, understood this truth more keenly than most.

"I, too, will dedicate my utmost effort to completing Her Highness Medea's mission."

Thud.

He struck the sword he carried on his back against the ground—a ceremonial gesture of commitment.

"On behalf of His Majesty."

Medea froze when she recognized the weapon.

"Peleus... is he all right? Was he worried about me?"

D'Angel swallowed the torrent of words Peleus had entrusted him to convey—the concerns, the warnings, the affection—and condensed them all into a single sentence.

"Yes, Your Highness. His Majesty is waiting for your return."

And so, the ten-star knight D'Angel joined their expedition.

D'Angel's addition brought dramatic momentum to the expedition's progress—though not necessarily the comfortable kind.

"Where's the time for rest? Even now, the Holy Nation's forces are drawing closer with every passing hour!"

"Tom, on your feet! Do you want your backside roasted? What's the point of making camp here? We can push on for at least another hour!"

Gallo discovered the secret behind Agema's legendary mobility across vast plains: relentless, one-sided threats and an unyielding whip hand.

Perhaps Agema's fearsome reputation wasn't entirely unintentional.

"We must advance our timeline by at least a day! You never know what variables might arise when facing the evil dragon!"

"Demons ahead? What are you avoiding them for? Strike first! Use your body to hold the line!"

D'Angel seemed to have completely forgotten that Cesare's party were foreigners, treating everyone with equal, merciless authority regardless of rank.

"Boss, say something. We're the Facade, not Agema. How about we settle the hierarchy between ten-star knights first?"

Gallo, unable to endure any longer, attempted to subtly incite Cesare to intervene.

Cesare simply crossed his arms and snorted with amusement.

"I like his style."

Moreover, the straightforward D'Angel didn't overthink Medea's uncanny foresight.

"Have you all forgotten who our Princess Medea is? The one who surveyed battlefields from a thousand miles away and rescued Valdina from the clutches of corruption?"

But his praise didn't stop there.

"That's absolutely right. I've been rendered speechless by tales of Her Highness more times than I can count."

"They say true pioneers navigate through pitch darkness as though it were broad daylight on a well-lit road."

D'Angel's chest swelled with pride.

"The continent reveres the Saintess as the Goddess's representative, but—hah!—that's only because the Holy Nation conceals all her wicked schemes. Our Princess is far more kind and pious than that woman could ever—"

If that were true, the oracle wouldn't have designated me as a sacrifice to the evil dragon.

"That's enough, D'Angel."

Medea's voice was firm, cutting off the praise before it could spiral further.

They had just reached higher elevation to survey the path leading to the canyon where the evil dragon's lair was located.

The thick forest gradually thinned, and their view expanded dramatically. From this vantage point, they could see clear across to the opposite side of the vast woodland.

It was then that a flash of silver light burst from the distant mountain range—unmistakable even in broad daylight. The silvery halo spread outward, sparkling with an intensity that made them squint.

Simultaneously, the trees in that area swayed like waves in a storm, and colorful smoke erupted skyward in vivid plumes.

"That's a white light bomb."

It was a distinctly human artifact—exceedingly rare to see deployed in the depths of a natural forest.

Which meant there was another group of rangers exploring those distant mountains.

The moment she processed those words, Medea went rigid.

The leaves swayed delicately on that silvery rim. Medea recognized the rainbow-colored smoke billowing from afar all too well.

Shadow Tentacles.

The smoke was released by parasitic demons called Shadow Tentacles when they died—the highest-level parasitic beasts that inhabited only this pitch-black forest, creatures that fed by absorbing darkness itself.

They were as intangible as darkness itself. No weapon—not sword, spear, nor knife—could cut them. Poison had no effect. Magic barely touched them.

The only method to eliminate this legion of demons was with concentrated light.

But how could one remove giant tentacles in a dense forest where the canopy blocked out the sky?

'By detonating all available white light bombs simultaneously and annihilating them with that radiance.'

It was a technique Medea had discovered only after losing five companions in her previous life.

The existence of the Shadow Demons and the solution to defeating them had only become known to the continent after the expedition team successfully defeated the evil dragon—information bought with blood and published posthumously.

Which meant the only person who could employ that specific method in this timeline, right now, was...

'Jason.'

Medea's gaze fixed on the distant ridge, her eyes tracing the silver light's fading glow.

She could feel it—a certainty that went deeper than logic, settled in the marrow of her bones.

'You finally came too.'

The words echoed in her mind, heavy with the weight of inevitability.

The Emperor had arrived in the forest.

Her hands clenched at her sides, knuckles white beneath her gloves.

She had known, intellectually, that their paths would cross eventually. The evil dragon drew them all like moths to flame—the Emperor seeking glory, the Saintess seeking divine validation, and Medea seeking...

What am I seeking? Revenge? Closure? Proof that I'm no longer the fool who loved him?

But knowing something and confronting it were entirely different matters.

The man who had murdered her children. Who had poisoned her with his own hands. Who had smiled as he destroyed everything she'd sacrificed for him.

That man was now mere miles away, advancing through the same forest, breathing the same air.

"Medea."

Cesare's voice cut through her spiraling thoughts. When she looked up, his golden eyes were studying her with unsettling perception.

"You know who that is."

It wasn't a question.

She nodded once, the gesture economical and controlled.

"The Emperor's expedition. They're using the same route we discovered in—"

She caught herself just in time.

"—the same route any competent strategist would choose. We should expect to encounter them before reaching the dragon's lair."

"The Emperor himself is here?"

D'Angel's expression hardened. His hand moved instinctively toward his sword.

"Your Highness, if you wish it, I can ensure our paths never cross. There are ways to... discourage their progress."

The unspoken offer hung in the air like smoke.

"No."

Medea's voice was steady, though her heart raced.

"We proceed as planned. Let them come. Let them waste their resources on the Shadow Tentacles and congratulate themselves on their cleverness."

She turned away from the distant silver light, her expression carefully neutral.

"After all, we have nothing to fear from the Emperor's expedition. We're all here for the same purpose, aren't we? To slay the evil dragon and save the continent."

The words tasted like ash in her mouth.

Because they both knew—Medea and the Emperor—that neither of them was here purely for noble purposes.

He sought the glory that would cement his legacy as the greatest ruler in history.

And she?

She was here to ensure he never got it.
