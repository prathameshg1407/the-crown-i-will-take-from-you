Chapter 253

The Son's Reckoning

"Sometimes I wish I could drain every drop of your cursed blood from my veins."

Terence rushed to Cesare's side, steadying him with practiced hands.

"You've been unconscious for weeks. Do you have any idea how worried I've been? Just like three years ago, helpless, unable to do anything..."

His voice trembled, reflecting the anxiety that had gripped his heart for days.

In front of his closest friend, Terence finally allowed the relief washing over him to soften his features.

"Where is Dea?"

Cesare's gaze swept the chamber with sharp urgency.

His last memory was of refusing the second oracle at the imperial palace—the oppressive divine energy crushing down on him until consciousness fled.

"Her Highness is... at the imperial palace."

"The imperial palace?"

"The Emperor summoned her."

At Terence's words, Cesare's golden eyes darkened instantly.

Even without elaboration, he understood what must have transpired while he lay unconscious.

It wasn't difficult to imagine the criticism and pressure Medea had endured alone.

"Cesare, where are you going?"

Terence caught his arm as he tried to push past.

"Gallo accompanied Her Highness. She took the entire Facade with her. Everything should be fine."

"Should be fine?"

Cesare's voice rang cold as winter steel.

Though he'd only just risen from his sickbed, traces of divine punishment still marking his skin, his intimidating presence remained utterly unchanged.

He knew intimately how despicable those who shared his blood could become.

He couldn't fathom what harm the Emperor might inflict using his life as leverage. How could he possibly remain idle?

When Cesare recalled those green eyes wavering before he'd lost consciousness, something in his chest seemed to plummet into an abyss.

"I'm going to the imperial palace."

By the time Cesare reached the imperial palace, however, Medea had already departed.

"It seems our carriages passed each other on the road. I received word from Gallo—she's heading to the villa."

Terence, who'd accompanied him despite his concerns, held up a note from Gallo.

The parchment also detailed the demands Perdiccas II had made of Medea.

Cesare snatched the message, his jaw tightening as he scanned the contents.

Then he turned on his heel.

"Where are you going now? Not back to the palace—!"

Before Terence could finish his protest, he found himself following his friend's determined stride toward the Emperor's chambers.

"Oh, Your Highness, the First Prince...! But how—!"

The palace attendants who spotted Cesare audibly gasped.

The First Prince, who'd been hovering at death's threshold mere hours ago, now stood before them fully conscious and mobile.

"Thank the heavens, we knew you'd recover safely. His Majesty wasn't the only one who was worried—"

"Move."

The word emerged frozen, carrying enough weight to send servants scattering.

Bang!

Cesare slammed the doors open without ceremony.

Perdiccas II had finally managed to calm his fury toward his daughter-in-law.

As if mocking that brief moment of composure, the doors exploded inward like a tempest unleashed.

"Cesare!"

A cry rang out—impossible to determine whether it carried joy or anger.

Perdiccas II's eyes swept over Cesare from head to toe.

Rather than the loving gaze of a father concerned for his son's health, his assessment resembled a merchant inspecting merchandise for defects before closing a deal.

"Now that you've returned from death's doorway, have you forgotten who your father is?"

Perdiccas II's brow furrowed as he snapped the rebuke.

"It seems Father has grown senile with age."

Cesare dispensed with traditional courtesies entirely.

He advanced toward the throne, his expression no longer carefully neutral.

Golden eyes that seemed to pierce through flesh and bone met the Emperor's gaze directly.

"You commanded me to cast aside my wife and raise another woman's child."

The icy pronouncement echoed through the chamber.

Simultaneously, the Emperor felt stunned by the rage emanating from his son in palpable waves.

Cesare understood perfectly what the oracle meant to Medea.

To Medea, enduring this pressure alone while I lay unconscious... didn't the Emperor's words feel like reliving the nightmare of her past life?

The thought made his blood boil.

Cesare's hands clenched involuntarily, knuckles white.

"Hah! So they came running to tattle already? Yes, that's what I said. What of it?"

Perdiccas II raised his voice defensively.

"It was for your sake! You were dying, teetering on the edge of oblivion! How could I remain idle as your father?"

Cesare's eyes turned glacial.

"I never asked for your intervention. Strange that someone who's never acted as a father now attempts to play the role."

Perdiccas II's face contorted with rage.

"What did you say? You may be drunk on marital affection, turning a blind eye to the empire's fate, but I—as its sovereign—cannot!"

The Emperor slammed his golden scepter against the marble floor.

"Still, I tried to be fair, to consider your future! It's that Valdina woman who spurned the opportunity I graciously offered!"

Just thinking of Medea reignited the Emperor's fury.

"She doesn't care whether you live or die! Without hesitation, she declared she'd return to Valdina with your child!"

Cesare's jaw clenched violently.

Return to Valdina.

Medea intended to give up on him.

How much did it cost me to reach her?

After endlessly battering against that impenetrable wall, he'd barely been granted permission to stand at her side.

And this man before him had destroyed in moments what had taken so long to build.

A ringing filled Cesare's ears—the sound of something fundamental fracturing.

"You bastard! Now I see what kind of foolish woman you've bound yourself to!"

Murderous intent erupted from Cesare in an unrefined wave, crashing against the Emperor.

His hands trembled as dark energy began manifesting beneath his knuckles.

Crash!

The violent sound of shattering porcelain cut through the tension.

Perdiccas II's tirade died mid-sentence.

"Forgive me. This old man's hands aren't as steady as they once were."

Cesare's head snapped toward the voice.

Where the ornate vase had stood moments before, the Marquis of Eratosthenes now waited.

His expressionless face wasn't directed at the Emperor—but at Cesare.

Though they'd never truly acknowledged each other's existence, turned away from what might have been...

Regain your senses.

Cesare read the unspoken message clearly.

Eratosthenes understood that mere seconds ago, Perdiccas II had nearly lost his life to his enraged eldest son.

Both men knew—breaking that vase to interrupt the moment was proof the Marquis wouldn't tolerate patricide, regardless of justification.

"Ah. The Marquis remains ever loyal to the imperial family."

The flames that had blazed in Cesare's golden eyes extinguished instantly.

Mockery twisted his sculpted features as he stared at Eratosthenes.

The derision cut sharper than any he'd directed at the Emperor.

Of course. We don't share a single drop of blood.

It would be absurd to expect the Marquis to suddenly cross the battle lines and shield him from the Emperor's wrath.

How contradictory—to claim he'd abandoned all expectations of others, yet still hope for something from this old man when even his own father provided nothing.

Was that brief moment of connection merely fleeting sentiment? What guarantee existed that Eratosthenes wouldn't ultimately choose the Emperor's side, even now?

"Let's speak truthfully, Father. You're concerned about your own position, not this country."

Cesare turned back to Perdiccas II.

The murderous energy that had gathered in his hands dissipated—but that boded no better for the Emperor.

"Usurping the throne from your nephew. Conducting an affair with your brother's wife. Driving your own children to fratricide. All of this, truly for the empire's sake?"

He paused, letting the accusations settle.

"Even a dog would laugh at such claims."

The Emperor had to endure his son's brutal honesty—words most would never dare speak aloud.

"You stake the nation's fortune on your terrible selfishness time and again. Tell me—aren't you afraid of our ancestors' wrath? Of divine punishment for dragging House Katzen's name through the mud?"

The same verbal blade Medea had wielded against Perdiccas II earlier now found its mark again.

"You... you dare...!"

Coming from his own son, the attack struck even deeper.

The Emperor clutched his chest, momentarily breathless.

"But don't concern yourself, Father. I'll bear our ancestors' anger appropriately. After all, I'm a different breed entirely."

Cesare mocked the Emperor's distress with cold indifference.

"I will not betray my wife as you did. I won't treat her like worn clothing—useful when convenient, discarded when not. I won't leave her to wither like a neglected tree."

"What—what are you saying?"

"In life and in death, my only wife is Medea. I'll state this once more with what courtesy your blood in my veins affords you."

Cesare approached and leaned down, his voice dropping to an intimate whisper only the Emperor could hear.

"Even if you're reborn a thousand times, you'll never understand or embody this noble faith."

"You—you—!"

"So cease this nonsense. While I still remember we share blood."

"You insane bastard! I am your father! I gave you life, raised you! To speak to your father with such—"

"Raised me?"

The sharp retort cut through the Emperor's bluster.

"You raised me well, did you? All you ever did was fight me and abandon me..."

Cesare's gaze flickered momentarily toward Eratosthenes before returning to the Emperor.

"It was the late Empress who transformed this beast into something human."

"You... you dare...!"

The Emperor found himself speechless.

He knew better than anyone that no excuse could refute that truth.

"Don't worry. I'm acutely aware that I'm your son. So much so that sometimes I want to drain every drop of your cursed blood from my body and erase it entirely."

Perdiccas II's face turned ashen.

Never before had he heard such unfiltered honesty from Cesare.

"I'm leaving now. I need to see my wife. I'll leave these parasites to Your Majesty's tender care."

Cesare turned without hesitation.

No expectation remained. No excitement.

Perdiccas II sat disheveled upon his throne, devastated by the verbal onslaught, looking pitifully diminished.

"How dare you! I am the supreme ruler of this empire! As my son! As my subject! You must obey whatever I command! How dare you!"

The Emperor shouted at his son's retreating back.

His crown wobbled precariously atop his head.

Bang!

His only answer was the thunderous slam of doors closing.

"That bastard! Summon him back immediately! What are you all standing there for? Run! Drag Cesare back here!"

Perdiccas II descended into a full tantrum.

"Your Highness, the First Prince! Your Highness!"

"Hurry and bring him back! You useless fools!"

The attendants scattered in panic, fleeing the Emperor's hysteria.

How could a married couple take turns humiliating His Majesty like this?

From the First Princess to the First Prince—it seemed they'd conspired to give the Emperor an aneurysm, one after another.

"Please, Your Majesty, calm yourself."

At that moment, Eratosthenes' measured voice cut through the chaos.

The remaining attendants looked toward him as though he were their salvation.

End of Chapter 253
