Chapter 244

Toward the
Abyss

"When it comes to the Princess of Valdina, my predictions are always wrong."

Kensington's expression turned grave as he continued his report.

"Even before this, Your Highness, we had dispatched one of our branch members to the Holy Kingdom at your command. He was a young man who had awakened holy powers—we arranged for him to enter as a priest. But after he crossed into the central temple... all contact was lost."

Medea's eyes sharpened.

"While I'm away, continue investigating his whereabouts. He wouldn't have simply vanished. The timing is far too convenient—he disappeared immediately after Rachel returned to the central temple."

"Yes, Your Highness. I will inform you the moment we uncover anything."

Kensington shifted his cane to his other hand, his knuckles whitening around the grip.

"I regret that my body has become such a burden. I should be accompanying you, not remaining behind."

Though he bowed his head, his voice emerged steadier than it had been in some time.

Kensington had to admit the truth to himself.

The moment the First Prince announced he would join the expedition, the quiet anxiety that had been gnawing at Kensington's heart simply... dissolved.

If only one could strip away the label of "Prince of Katzen"—

In truth, there was no man better suited to sever a dragon's head.

The Quayside

The Strait of El Amuz — A hidden dock behind a veil of coastal brush

A galley rested at anchor, its sleek hull concealed from prying eyes by the natural screen of overgrown shrubs.

"Boss? Why are you here alone? I thought you were escorting the Princess."

"The destination has changed."

"Changed to where?"

"Death Valley. We'll sail directly to the Forest of Eternal Darkness."

Gallo rubbed his ears, certain he had misheard. Cesare's clipped response had stripped away every detail—Medea's resolve, the oracle's threat—and delivered only the bare conclusion.

"Wait. I think something's wrong with my hearing. Where did you say we're going?"

"Death Valley. Through the Forest of Eternal Darkness."

Gallo touched his ears again, more frantically this time.

"Boss, that forest—that's where mutant beasts unlike anything seen on this continent are said to appear. You're telling me we're going to walk through it? On foot? Did I misunderstand something?"

"You heard correctly."

"Just like that? Out of nowhere?"

Gallo's voice climbed an octave.

"Boss, has life become boring? Did you somehow acquire a spare?"

Then, as realization struck, Gallo clutched his head with both hands and let out a strangled cry. A few strands of straw-colored hair drifted mournfully to the ground.

"This is about evading the oracle, isn't it? Whose insane idea was it to destroy the evil dragon? Her Highness the Princess? Or you?"

In truth, Gallo couldn't be certain. He had witnessed both of them act in ways that defied all common sense.

"Quiet. We depart the moment she arrives. Make preparations."

Cesare offered no further explanation. He simply issued his orders and turned away.

Gallo stared at his retreating back, incredulous.

Something was different. The faint corners of Cesare's mouth seemed... elevated. A subtle light flickered in those golden eyes—something that might almost be called satisfaction.

His expression remained cold enough that an ordinary person would have noticed nothing. But Gallo was not ordinary.

"Boss, what did you eat this morning? Why are you in such a good mood? Who marches toward death looking this pleased?"

"Are you having second thoughts?"

"Absolutely not!"

Cesare threw another spark of provocation without missing a beat.

"If you don't want to come, leave now. We'll need to reduce our numbers before breaking through the forest anyway."

"Who wouldn't go?! Who would let me stay behind?! Have you forgotten our oath—to die on the same day, at the same hour!"

Gallo, burning with indignation, whirled on Terence without warning.

"The one who actually needs to stay behind is him! He complained the entire journey from Katzen. How is he supposed to survive that hellish forest?"

Cesare turned to Terence, one eyebrow raised in silent inquiry.

"You—you're not actually going to listen to that idiot and abandon me, are you?"

Terence's voice carried genuine disbelief.

"You're going to face an evil dragon without a wizard?"

"Ha! Boss, if he can't even make it to the canyon, having a wizard won't matter much."

"Shut your mouth. Cesare—if you leave me here, I'll blow a hole through your stomach. Consider that a promise."

"...You may come."

Permission granted—albeit a beat too late.

Terence, who was treated as a VIP everywhere he went on the continent, had volunteered his services only to find himself threatening violence just to secure a place on this suicide mission.

He released a long, aggrieved sigh.

The Following Dawn

The Strait of El Amuz — Before the morning mist had cleared

Chararak—!

The mooring rope snapped free from the dock.

"Hoist the sails!"

The moment Medea and her party cleared the palace gates and boarded, the vessel surged forward, carving through the waves.

Medea stood on the deck, watching the palace grow smaller against the horizon. Pale dawn light broke through the lingering darkness, gilding the distant spires.

'Peleus... hold on a little longer.'

The sin of leaving her only brother without a word of farewell could be atoned for with the head of an evil dragon.

The Vessel

The ship Cesare had prepared exceeded all expectations.

Its long, sleek hull was designed to slice through open water with minimal resistance. The prow bristled with spears and shields, ready for naval combat. The sails, woven from thick, resilient fabric, looked capable of weathering any storm.

Despite its considerable size and the remarkable speed at which it cut through the swells, the deck remained eerily stable—barely a tremor underfoot.

"This will be Her Highness's cabin. Given the limited time for preparation, it may fall short of expectations—please forgive any deficiencies."

Terence's apology proved unnecessary.

The interior was nothing short of remarkable. Supple leather chairs occupied every corner. The bed was layered with thick wool blankets. An intricately carved table held a bronze lamp filled with herbs said to ward off seasickness. A storage alcove at the room's edge kept fresh provisions readily available.

Every detail had been considered—meticulous touches designed to ensure comfort during an extended voyage.

It was impossible to prepare a ship this thoroughly in merely a few days.

The careful design, inside and out, spoke of a vessel built for precisely this kind of journey.

Medea was not the only one to notice.

"Your Highness... did the First Prince know in advance that we would be departing on this expedition?"

Medea shook her head slowly at Neril's question.

"This was never meant to be an expedition."

"Then...?"

"The ship is already prepared. It awaits you at the harbor."

Somehow, she felt she already knew the answer.

On deck, sailors moved with practiced efficiency—mercenaries all, bearing the mark of the Facade on their arms.

Medea spotted the tall figure standing at the prow and made her way toward him.

"The High Priest? For now, we should—"

"—what are you holding there? Ah! Your Highness the Princess!"

Gallo had been deep in conversation with Cesare when he noticed Medea's approach and hastily offered his greeting.

"We'll continue this later."

"Understood."

As Gallo nodded and withdrew, the corner of a cream-colored document peeked from beneath his arm.

Curious.

Medea was still watching Gallo's retreating back when the ship lurched, caught by a sudden swell.

"You shouldn't be out here. It's dangerous."

A strong hand steadied her before she could stumble.

Cesare's golden eyes swept over her, checking for any sign that the rough waves had dampened her clothing.

Then, unexpectedly, Medea spoke.

"That day. When you came to me—were you planning to help me escape?"

Cesare's eyebrow arched at the abrupt question.

"...Does it matter now? We've already chosen our destination."

"Don't deflect."

"If I answer, will you promise not to pretend ignorance?"

The corners of his mouth lifted as he parried her question with his own.

He watched her eyes narrow, observed the way her full lips pressed together in displeasure. He noted how her delicate hands curled into fists so small he could have covered them entirely with one of his own.

Cesare had to suppress a laugh at her transparent irritation.

"In the end, this is the right path. Running away would never have suited you, Medea."

Yes. This is Medea Valdina.

For a woman cursed by fate itself to become a sacrifice—there was neither fear nor terror in those clear green eyes.

When Cesare looked into that resolute face gazing up at him, something shifted in his chest.

A faint smile touched his lips.

The tension that had wound through his entire body since he'd begun his mad dash toward Valdina finally began to ease.

His outstretched finger brushed a strand of hair from her face—displaced by the salt-laden wind.

"When it comes to the Princess of Valdina, my predictions are always wrong."

A woman who would defy an oracle and march to slay an evil dragon with her own hands.

He swallowed his laughter.

"It seems there was no need to poison the Holy Kingdom after all."

"What?"

Medea, who had been about to swat away his presumptuous hand, went still.

The document in Gallo's possession just now...

A thought sparked through her mind.

"Cesare. Tell me you didn't do something to the Holy Kingdom."

Cesare's smile deepened—faint but unmistakable—surprised that she had intuited his actions from so few clues.
