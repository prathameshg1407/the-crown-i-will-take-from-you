Chapter Continuation

The Trap
Springs

"If she can't bite, she deserves to be eaten."

"Sorry. I didn't realize you had guests."

An aide slipped in behind Siseo, pale and sweating. He leaned in, whispering urgently.

"The Head Maid went straight to the Queen Mother. She claims the Princess has a secret lover."

Siseo stilled.

"A lover?"

"The rumor is… Minister Etienne."

Pain blossomed behind Siseo's eyes.

That bloated parasite?

It was a trap—coarse, vindictive, and coordinated. Quiggin and Etienne, moving together. If it succeeded, it wouldn't just destroy Medea. It would poison the court itself.

Princess, I told you to stay invisible.

But he remembered the King's last words, spoken quietly before marching to war.

"Take care of Medea."

"We'll finish this later,"

Siseo said abruptly, already pulling on his coat.

Cesare watched him go with mild amusement.

"Boss,"

Gallo chuckled, stretching.

"The curtain's up."

Cesare moved to the window, eyes following Siseo's carriage.

"Do you think she survives?"

Gallo asked, genuinely curious.

"Little girl versus hyenas?"

"If she can't bite,"

Cesare said flatly,

"she deserves to be eaten."

— The Queen Mother's Palace —

The hall vibrated with scandal.

"A token?"

the Queen Mother demanded, voice trembling.

"You claim there is a pledge?"

"Yes, Your Majesty!"

Maria sobbed, forehead pressed to the floor.

"An object engraved with his name! A promise of marriage!"

She lifted her tear-streaked face.

"It's in her jewelry box. She gazed at it every night."

The Queen Mother's fingers crushed the armrest.

In Valdina, exchanging a token was engagement in all but name.

For a Princess, it was betrayal.

"If it exists,"

the Queen Mother said coldly,

"we will see it."

Her gaze shifted to the shadows.

"Pinatelli."

"Yes, Your Majesty."

Baroness Pinatelli

Baroness Pinatelli stepped forward—tall, austere, dressed in unadorned black. Twenty years of loyalty, no favors owed, no secrets sold.

"Search the Princess's palace. Every corner. Bring me proof."

Pinatelli bowed and departed without a word.

The doors shut like a coffin lid.

The Queen Mother looked down at Medea.

"Do you object?"

"No,"

Medea replied calmly.

The room stirred.

The Queen Mother fanned herself sharply, fury simmering. Catherine tried to catch Quiggin's eye—stop this, you idiot—but the Head Maid didn't look back.

She was intoxicated.

If Etienne becomes family, I won't need the Duchess. I won't need anyone.

Birna yawned loudly.

Medea knelt motionless, spine straight, eyes clear—untouched by the heat around her.

"The Prime Minister has arrived!"

Siseo strode in, coat still dusted from the road.

"I greet Your Majesty."

Relief flickered across the Queen Mother's face.

"Sir Siseo. Good."

"What is the matter?"

he asked, feigning ignorance.

"The Princess has disgraced us,"

the Queen Mother snapped.

Siseo looked at Medea.

She met his gaze without blinking.

She isn't afraid.

Why isn't she afraid?

Quiggin's fingers tightened. Why was he here?

It doesn't matter, she reassured herself. Pinatelli cannot be bought.

The doors opened again.

"Your Majesty, Baroness Pinatelli has returned."

Pinatelli entered carrying a small velvet-wrapped bundle.

Quiggin smiled.

The Queen Mother leaned forward.

"Well?"

"I carried out your command,"

Pinatelli said evenly.

"I searched the Princess's jewelry box, precisely as instructed."

A pause.

The air tightened.

"I found this."

She unwrapped the velvet.
