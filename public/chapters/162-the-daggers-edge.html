"All it takes is a single drop of doubt to shatter the most steadfast belief."

"Put in more effort. You cannot possibly be surrendering before the game has even begun?"

The voice from the pendant turned sharp with displeasure.

"You cannot even strip away the one family she has left—and yet you expect to bring Venus into your orbit?"

A harsh crackle, then silence.

The connection severed without warning. Within the pendant, the blackened image had already reverted to the serene visage of the benevolent goddess.

Rachel gnawed at her fingernails, frustration clawing at her composure.

"Damn it all. If only Medea would show herself..."

The King remained beyond her reach. Even the Agema knights she had painstakingly conditioned seemed to forget her existence whenever that woman was present.

Beyond the tactical failures, something else nagged at her—an unsettling sensation that had prickled along her spine during her last encounter with Medea.

Could it be... Has she awakened?

Selene—the true priestess of Shadeia—had been driven from her people by Rachel's mother. And Medea was Selene's only daughter.

It was entirely possible that traces of divine foresight flowed through those veins.

"No. That cannot be true."

Rachel shook her head, banishing the thought.

"If she possessed the Sight, she would never have allowed me through the palace gates. She would have shielded her precious brother from danger."

The omnipotence of the Goddess was unmistakable when it manifested. The temples would have sensed it. The Saintess the continent awaited with bated breath would not remain silent.

Rachel bit her lip hard enough to taste copper, her nails carving crescents into her palms.

"Medea has not awakened. Not yet."

She repeated the words like a prayer, and a thin sigh of relief escaped her.

"There is still time. It is not too late."

A dark joy crept into her voice.

On this continent, there would be only one priestess of Shadeia. Only one Saintess.

And that name will be Rachel—no one else.

The torn bandages lay scattered at her feet like shed skin.

Rachel studied her reflection in the wooden mirror, methodically adjusting her appearance—smoothing errant strands of hair, dabbing color onto her cheeks.

When she was finished, she drew a slow, steadying breath.

"First, I must strip Medea of her position as Princess. To accomplish that, I need only remind everyone of what she truly is—an unfortunate vessel of curses."

It would not be simple. Medea had earned the people's adoration as the hero who had quelled the rebellion and safeguarded the realm.

But Rachel had experience dismantling such reputations.

A single drop of doubt is all it takes to fracture even the most unshakeable faith.

Just as Selene had been banished and killed before she could awaken to her divine power, so too would her daughter meet the same fate.

"The Knights Templar will arrive soon."

The stage was set. The audience, assembled. All that remained were the actors to perform their roles.

Rachel's gaze drifted to the torn bandages on the floor, and her eyes gleamed with malice.

The Cottage House — Several Days Later

Waves of lustrous gold cascaded over her shoulders, spilling across the silk of her gown. The strands caught the light with an almost liquid sheen.

"Saintess, how can anyone be so beautiful?"

The maid's voice brimmed with awe as she drew the brush through Rachel's hair.

Since arriving at the palace as a guest of King Valdina, Rachel had swiftly captured the hearts of the court ladies. Her gentle demeanor and the warm radiance of her holy power left no one unmoved.

"Have you heard about the Saintess? She eradicated an entire horde of ghouls and saved His Majesty's life! They say her holy power is extraordinary—powerful enough that the Holy Nation itself came calling."

"And yet she's so humble! Every day she visits the slums to tend to sick children. She even shares her blessings with us lowly servants. A woman of her station, who could stand second only to the Pope himself!"

Through kindness and grace, Rachel had won the people's favor with remarkable speed.

Even the maid now attending her had been assigned by Medea's own order.

"When she first arrived, the Princess humiliated her in front of everyone. Her Highness truly goes too far sometimes—especially considering the Saintess is His Majesty's savior!"

The maid's lips pursed with indignation.

This particular servant was simple and credulous—the perfect instrument. A few minor healings had been enough to secure her complete devotion.

Drawing such souls to her side required nothing more than a few well-placed words.

"I only wished to offer His Majesty treatment, but my timing was poor. I fear I may have given offense to Her Highness."

Rachel's brow creased with practiced concern.

"Perhaps if I were to place a divine blessing upon something she treasures, she might look upon me more favorably?"

"Hmm..."

The maid tapped her chin thoughtfully.

"You visit the Princess's palace regularly, do you not? Is there anything among her possessions that might serve for a blessing?"

"Well... I'm not certain—"

"Think carefully. Something with her name inscribed upon it... something unmistakably hers."

As Rachel's voice deepened, the maid's eyes grew distant and unfocused.

"Last year, for Her Highness's birthday, His Majesty presented her with a mithril dagger. Her name is engraved upon the hilt."

A brilliant smile illuminated Rachel's face.

"That would be perfect. Could you bring it to me? Without anyone's knowledge?"

Above the maid's glazed eyes, a mark shimmered into existence—curved like a goat's horn.

"Of course. I used to serve in the Princess's household. I remain close with Saya, her personal attendant."

The words emerged in a monotone, devoid of will.

"Yes. I know."

Rachel inclined her head graciously. Had she possessed more power, she might have ensnared additional servants—but maintaining the Agemas' conditioning while still pursuing Peleus left her resources stretched thin.

"That is precisely why I am entrusting this task to you."

The maid nodded with fervent, empty enthusiasm.

The Princess's Palace

"Your Highness, that woman who calls herself a Saintess is truly peculiar!"

Saya's voice trembled with barely contained fury.

"Now she's corrupting the people around me with her strange influence!"

"What has happened?"

Medea's question was calm, measured.

Saya drew a steadying breath and recounted the incident.

"Anji attempted to enter my study?"

"Yes, Your Highness. Neril caught her lurking about in the early hours yesterday. She fled the moment she heard footsteps."

Saya's eyes narrowed as she lamented the ingratitude of a former colleague.

"Before you decide on Anji's punishment, there is something else you should know. I've been piecing things together, and I recalled—not long ago, she approached me with questions. About your dagger, Your Highness."

She thought she was being subtle, but please—I was trained in alleys where thieves would steal your wages and bread with a smile on their faces.

Saya snorted inwardly at the amateur attempt.

"Ever since she was transferred to the cottage house and received those so-called 'blessings,' she's been singing the Saintess's praises. I thought it strange from the start."

She paused, tilting her head.

"Your Highness... why are you laughing?"

Medea's sudden laughter had caught Saya off guard.

A dagger... So Rachel has already begun her preparations.

"I believe I understand what she's after. It's reassuring to see some things haven't changed."

Rachel's methods remained as cowardly as ever—manipulating others like chess pieces, never dirtying her own hands.

"Let them take it."

"What? Your Highness, you cannot be serious! Do you realize what's in that study?"

"It matters little. The genuine article is here."

A drawer slid open with a quiet squeak.

From within, Medea withdrew a silver dagger—its blade gleaming, its craftsmanship unmistakable.

"I would never leave Peleus's gift unprotected. The one in the study is merely a replica."

"Ah..."

Understanding dawned on Saya's face.

"Consider it a gift from me."

"A gift? For whom?"

"You'll discover that soon enough. Don't trouble yourself over it."

A faint smile played at the corners of Medea's lips.

"Besides... Anji is hardly the only piece on the board."

There were plenty of chess pieces awaiting their turn—all positioned beneath Medea's careful orchestration.

Heh.
