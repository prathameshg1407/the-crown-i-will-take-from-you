Chapter 314

The Shifting
Tides

"In the game of empires, today's victor is tomorrow's pawn."

Perdiccas II turned his attention to the bloodied knight of Valdina, who still wore an expression of bewildered disbelief as she received the legendary blade.

"From this moment forward, you are a knight who bears my personal sword. Should anyone dare harm you without my explicit permission, they shall face the full weight of my imperial wrath."

It was, on its surface, a ceremonial gesture—a diplomatic olive branch extended to soothe Valdina's ruffled pride. But the sword itself was no mere token. It was genuine, priceless, and unmistakably real.

"...I am deeply grateful, Your Imperial Majesty."

As Menil accepted the weapon, envious gazes flickered through the assembled Katzen nobility like flames catching on dry tinder.

A few bruises and a superficial wound from the Western Empress's blade—and in return, she receives a sword that all the gold in the treasury could not purchase.

Perdiccas II, oblivious to the covetous thoughts swirling among his subjects, composed his features into an expression of imperial dignity. He would permit no further displays of weakness this evening.

"Eastern Empress."

The Emperor's voice carried an edge of paternal warmth—though anyone paying attention could detect the steel beneath.

"You shall assume governance of the Inner Palace henceforth. I trust you will execute your duties without forgetting your place."

The warning embedded within those honeyed words sent ice through the Eastern Empress's veins.

"Your Majesty's generosity knows no bounds."

She had noticed, however, that the Emperor's fury had begun to subside—if only slightly. With practiced grace, she brushed her fingers against the back of his hand in a gesture of tender submission.

"I confess I lack confidence in managing the Inner Palace. But I am utterly certain of one thing: my devotion to serving Your Majesty will never waver."

In the art of soothing the imperial ego, the Eastern Empress possessed far greater expertise than her fallen rival ever had.

"Indeed. You understand what matters most. That will suffice."

The tension in the Emperor's eyes eased fractionally.

He pressed his fingertips to his temples. A dull ache pulsed behind his eyes, each throb like a mallet striking an anvil.

With deliberate slowness, Perdiccas II rose from the throne. An attendant materialized instantly at his side, pressing the golden staff into his waiting palm.

"Continue the festivities. This is my birthday celebration, after all. We cannot allow a mere domestic disturbance to derail the evening entirely."

Even in departure, he refused to permit the banquet's interruption. His wife's indiscretions might be laid bare before the world, but he would not allow anyone to believe his imperial composure had been shaken.

"Resume the performance."

"At once, Your Majesty."

The musicians launched into their melody once more—though the notes trembled with an uncertainty that had not existed before. No one seemed to notice the difference.

Every mind in the banquet hall churned with private calculations.

With the Western Empress disgraced, what becomes of the succession? Can she possibly recover from this?

Should I withdraw my support now, before I'm tainted by association?

A hundred silent deliberations unfolded simultaneously—including those of the Emperor himself.

When did Catherine decide to betray me? And if she did... what does that mean for Juan?

"Father, allow me to escort you."

The Third Prince approached, his expression carefully neutral despite the exhaustion that had drained the color from his face.

Perdiccas II studied his son's features with new, suspicious eyes. Unconsciously, he searched for any trace of the Grand Duke of Carlyle in those familiar contours.

He found none. Only echoes of the Western Empress stared back at him.

I must not act rashly. Not yet. Juan is all I have left now.

His gaze drifted to the opposite side of the hall, where the First Prince stood apart, his posture relaxed yet watchful.

"...Stand aside."

Perdiccas II swept past his son without a backward glance.

The warm father who had embraced Juan so emotionally just hours before had vanished entirely—replaced by something cold and calculating.

"Father..."

The Third Prince moved to follow, only to find his path blocked by the Emperor's personal guard.

Perdiccas II departed the banquet hall in silence.

Only when he had passed beyond the range of curious ears did he gesture for his attendant to approach.

"Come closer."

"Yes, Your Majesty."

His voice dropped to a whisper—soft as silk, sharp as a blade.

"Investigate every instance when the Western Empress's correspondence left the palace. Every letter. Every messenger. Every recipient."

This betrayal, he was certain, had not blossomed overnight. There would be roots—and he intended to tear them all from the earth.

Meanwhile, the Eastern Empress had observed Medea's quiet departure from the banquet hall.

"Heavens, what an ordeal this has been. I simply must refresh my appearance—I'm positively a wreck."

"Of course, Your Majesty."

Under the pretense of touching up her makeup, she slipped away from the festivities.

She found one of the First Princess's tall attendants standing guard outside the ladies' chamber. With a quick gesture, she dismissed her own maids.

"Wait for me here."

Inside, a young maid was arranging the First Princess's hair. The Eastern Empress's eyes glittered with barely contained excitement.

"Saya, please give us a moment. Your Majesty—you honor me with your presence."

Medea rose from her seat, her expression as serene as a mountain lake. One would never have guessed she stood at the center of the evening's catastrophic upheaval.

"It was you, wasn't it?"

But this time, the Eastern Empress did not glare with the resentment she had displayed at their last encounter.

"How did you manage it?"

She seized Medea's hands, grinning like a child who had discovered treasure.

"Alfred! I had forgotten he even existed—and after he vanished from Katzen, no one could trace his whereabouts. How on earth did you find him?"

The chamber was blessedly distant from the banquet hall. Only here, free from the risk of eavesdroppers, did the Eastern Empress permit herself to release the emotions she had been strangling all evening.

"You cannot imagine how many humiliations I've swallowed because of that woman!"

Her voice swelled with vindictive joy.

"And now—the tables have turned completely! It seems the old proverb holds true: no flower blooms forever, no tree stays green through every season."

The memory of the Western Empress—struck down, sprawled on the floor in ruins—would warm the Eastern Empress's heart for years to come.

"The Inner Palace is mine at last! She will never steal it from me again!"

"I am pleased for Your Majesty."

"Your role in this was invaluable. I shall not forget what you've done."

The Eastern Empress clasped Medea's hands once more, her face alight with feverish hope.

"With the Western Empress cast down, her son's position surely crumbles as well. If I could bring Angelique back to court now—"

"Your Majesty."

Medea's grip tightened gently around the Eastern Empress's wrist.

"I would strongly advise against mentioning the Fourth Princess to His Majesty at this juncture."

The Eastern Empress's expression curdled.

"Why not? The Western Empress has finally fallen!"

"Precisely. And if His Majesty senses that the decision was not entirely his own—if he perceives that he has been maneuvered—upon whose head do you suppose his resentment will fall?"

Confusion still clouded the Eastern Empress's features.

"Consider: would Your Majesty truly wish to send your daughter back to exile mere days after securing her return to the capital?"

"No... No, that cannot happen..."

"Then wait."

"..."

"Even if Your Majesty takes no action whatsoever, the Fourth Princess will return to the capital. Naturally. Inevitably."

Medea held the Eastern Empress's gaze with quiet intensity.

"But how? In circumstances like these, how could she possibly—"

"You must wait."

Though her tone remained measured, the warning beneath was unmistakable: I will not tolerate interference.

The Eastern Empress clutched at the fabric of her gown.

This slip of a girl found a crack in the Western Empress's armor—that spotless, unassailable armor—and brought her crashing down in a single evening.

Those green eyes, calm as they were, carried an unsettling weight. The Eastern Empress found herself suppressing a shiver.

If I act recklessly... might Angelique suffer the consequences?

"...Very well. If that is your counsel, I shall follow it."

She did not fully comprehend Medea's strategy. But the First Princess had just accomplished what the Eastern Empress had dreamed of for decades—dismantling the Western Empress's seemingly impenetrable position.

She had little choice but to trust.

"If it means Angelique returns to me, I will do whatever you ask. Just promise me—"

"Have I ever failed to deliver what I promised?"

Medea smiled—serene, assured.

The Eastern Empress departed.

"Ha! 'Your merit was invaluable.' 'I shall not forget.' Was she not standing in the same banquet hall as everyone else just now?"

Saya, who had observed the entire exchange, made no effort to mask her irritation.

"Without Your Highness's intervention, the Eastern Empress would have remained beneath the Western Empress's heel for the rest of her natural life! Now the Emperor hands her the Inner Palace on a silver platter, and she speaks as though she did Your Highness a favor?"

Medea reached out and stroked the young maid's hair gently.

"Peace, Saya."

But the fire in the girl's eyes refused to dim.

"Your Highness, does this truly not anger you? People behave one way when they're begging at the back door and quite another when they're strutting out the front!"

She gritted her teeth, her cheeks puffing with barely contained fury.

"And that Fourth Princess of hers—even if the Eastern Empress birthed her, who knows what manner of chaos that vicious girl will cause if she actually returns!"

But Medea's expression remained undisturbed.

"You needn't worry about the Eastern Empress. She cannot possibly defend what she has just taken from the Western Empress."

A quiet smile played at the corners of her lips.

"Consider: previously, the Western Empress directed all her hostility toward me alone. Now she must divide her attention—targeting both myself and the Eastern Empress."

"With the Western Empress's faction fragmented, the Third Prince's supporters become far easier to manage."

Her smile deepened.

"The Eastern Empress will serve as a rather useful shield for us in the conflicts to come."

Only then did Saya's indignation begin to cool. She shook her head slowly, weariness settling over her features.

"The person smiling at the start of this banquet is not the same one smiling at its end."

It was the truth.

In the span of mere hours, the positions of the Eastern and Western Empresses had reversed completely—heaven and earth exchanging places.

The ferocity of Katzen's court politics left Saya momentarily breathless.

"The true work begins now. What remains is for the Emperor to uncover the full truth."

Medea turned her gaze toward the window.

Through the narrow pane, she could discern the silhouette of Perdiccas II in the distant palace gardens, illuminated by wavering lamplight.

He who had betrayed his wife and his mistress alike—who had ruled over them with absolute authority—now found himself, for the first time, standing on the opposite side of that equation.

Tonight's events would sear themselves into his memory. The wound might be shallow for now, but it would fester.

And eventually, Perdiccas II would come to understand.

What he had so freely given to others—distrust in place of faith, betrayal in answer to loyalty—would one day return to him.

Every debt would be collected. Every coin repaid in full.

"Send word to Kensington."

Medea's voice echoed low in the quiet chamber.

"It is time to move."
