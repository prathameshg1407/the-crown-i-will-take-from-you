Ambient: Schemes
The Hunt Begins

The New
Regime

"I am deceiving her... but I move at the Princess's command."

"You are the only one who can fix this mess. That way… I can finally feel safe."

The Queen Mother said quietly.

Her hand rested on Pinatelli's shoulder. It was heavy—not with authority, but with age, fear, and regret.

Pinatelli bowed deeply, hiding the turmoil in her chest.

I am deceiving her, she thought.

I serve the Queen Mother… but I move at the Princess's command.

The guilt bit sharply.

Then she remembered the chapel.

The cold stone.

The dying girl kneeling while vultures circled.

For Valdina's sake, she steadied herself.

God will forgive me.

"I will not disappoint you, Your Majesty. I will give my life to this task."

The Queen Mother nodded.

"Good. I trust you."

The New Regime

The announcement struck the palace like thunder.

Marchioness Pinatelli
appointed
Royal Head Maid

The response was immediate—and brutal.

→ Long-serving officials were dismissed overnight.

→ Accounts were audited without warning.

→ Private chambers were searched.

→ Spies vanished.

Some fled.

Some begged.

Some were never seen again.

The palace's invisible hierarchy collapsed and reformed in days.

And at its center stood Pinatelli—backed by the Queen Mother, untouchable.

Which meant one thing was now undeniable:

Princess Medea was no longer prey.

The Princess's Palace

The transformation was ruthless.

Workmen stripped the wing down to its bones.

"Match the Queen Mother's residence. Royal storage only. No substitutions."

Pinatelli ordered.

Cold stone was warmed.

Rotting wood replaced.

Rough cotton exchanged for silk.

Within days, the palace no longer whispered abandonment.

It proclaimed blood.

"I greet Your Highness."

Pinatelli bowed deeply as Medea entered. The maids snapped to attention—fear now mixed with respect.

"You've been busy."

Medea observed lightly.

"It is overdue."

"The air feels stale. Would you join me in the garden?"

Pinatelli understood the invitation for what it was.

They sat beneath filtered sunlight, far from listening ears.

"It unfolded exactly as you said. The palace stands upright again."

"The Seal did its work."

"It will take months to clean Quiggin's corruption. She poisoned everything she touched."

"I believe in you."

She paused, fingers resting against porcelain.

"There is… something else."

Pinatelli leaned forward.

"While tracking my former maids, I heard rumors that Quiggin was selling children."

The air changed.

"Selling… children?"

Pinatelli repeated.

"One maid told me. She vanished the next day."

Silence.

"If servants of the Royal Household were traded like cattle, that stain will never wash out."

Pinatelli's jaw tightened.

"I will investigate immediately."

"The internal affairs were overseen by Quiggin and the Minister of the Court. Perhaps Minister Etienne noticed something unusual."

"He was… a victim as well."

Pinatelli said cautiously.

"Exactly. Victims often see what others miss."

Medea replied with a gentle smile.

The seed was planted.

The Office of the Minister of the Court

Etienne flung the newspaper onto his desk.

FORMER HEAD MAID DIES IN PRISON

"Tsk. Useless woman."

He wasn't grieving.

He was irritated.

Quiggin had been convenient.

Reliable.

Discrete.

Efficient.

Now—

"My supply is cut. Such a nuisance."

"Minister, the new Head Maid has arrived."

A servant announced.

Etienne lurched upright, hastily buttoning his coat.

"Madame Pinatelli! Finally, someone competent!"

He slammed his hand on the desk, theatrics on full display.

"To think Quiggin dared harm the Princess! The Queen Mother's wisdom is unmatched!"

Pinatelli watched him the way one watches a diseased animal.

"I am investigating Quiggin's other crimes. There are rumors of human trafficking."

Etienne froze.

Just for a breath.

Then—

laughter.

"Good heavens! Such barbarity! Even demons would recoil!"

"I see."

Pinatelli stood.

"As you oversaw internal affairs with her, I trust you'll assist."

"Of course! Of course! Perhaps we could discuss this with Her Majesty? Over tea?"

"The Queen Mother prefers results. Good day, Minister."

The door closed.

Etienne's smile vanished.

"How did she smell it? I buried it perfectly."

He kicked the desk in frustration.

"Damn Quiggin. Even dead, she poisons me."

He slumped into his chair.

"I'll lie low. No new… acquisitions."

The Princess's Palace

"He's cautious. But shaken."

Pinatelli reported later.

"He always was. Rot spreads quietly."

She turned.

"Neril."

"Yes, Your Highness."

"Your friend Tom—he knows the streets."

"Yes."

Medea handed her a parchment.

Ingredients were listed in careful script.

Unstable.

Reactive.

"Ask him to run an errand."

Neril scanned it once, then nodded.

Medea gazed toward the Minister's wing.

Quiggin was gone.

The Regent's left arm severed.

Now—

the right arm would bleed.

Minister Etienne didn't know it yet.

But the hunt had already begun.
