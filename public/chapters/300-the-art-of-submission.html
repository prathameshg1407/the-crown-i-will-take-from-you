"Fear teaches faster than any blade—and leaves deeper scars."

Caught in the Crown Princess's impassive gaze, Princess Strauss felt terror bloom in her chest like frost spreading across glass.

The burning heat of her slapped cheeks faded to nothing. Something far more primal had taken hold.

What if she actually kills me?

Immunity. With that single word, the Crown Princess could tear her apart and walk away unscathed—as pristine and untouchable as before.

Like the worm still impaled on that dessert fork, she would be crushed while her killer remained beautiful and blameless.

Princess Strauss's gaze darted across the greenhouse, desperate for an ally.

Every face turned away. No one would meet her eyes. They feared even the chance of drawing attention to themselves.

No... no one will help me.

A primal survival instinct clawed its way up her throat.

"I was... rude..."

The words came out strangled.

"I'm afraid I cannot hear you clearly."

"Forgive me for my rudeness... I beg of you."

"Still unclear."

The Crown Princess's voice remained perfectly calm. Her gaze—blank, indifferent—looked down upon the Princess as though observing something beneath notice.

In that terrible moment, Princess Strauss remembered her nursemaid's warning as she had left the mansion that morning.

"Be careful, my lady. They say the Crown Princess never forgives those who touch her family. Even when Valdina was still a minor kingdom, she threw her gloves at the Fourth Princess's feet the moment that woman insulted her father."

I thought I could exploit that weakness. Provoke her into a scene I could use against her.

I should never have touched her mother.

But it was far too late for regret.

Princess Strauss understood now—understood with the bone-deep certainty of prey caught in a predator's jaws.

This merciless game would not end until she gave complete and utter surrender.

"Ah—!"

A collective gasp rippled through the gathering.

Princess Strauss had lifted her teacup—and upended it over her own head.

Dark liquid cascaded through her elaborately pinned hair, soaking the pearl ornaments, staining her expensive gown in spreading blotches of brown.

"I was blind and foolish... I showed unforgivable disrespect to Your Highness the Crown Princess. Please... please withdraw your anger... I beg you..."

It was absolute capitulation, forced by the raw instinct to survive.

Something dripped down her cheeks—tea or tears, she could no longer tell. She had been raised as her father's treasured only daughter, never once humbling herself before anyone.

Until now.

The assembled nobles watched in horrified silence as the Princess knelt in her own degradation.

No one dared treat this as entertainment any longer.

Because they understood: Princess Strauss was merely the example.

The true message was meant for them all.

I can devour anyone I choose. Whenever I choose.

"Of course. You must be terribly uncomfortable after such an unfortunate accident. Please, return home and rest."

Medea's voice had warmed with apparent concern—as though she hadn't just forced a noblewoman to publicly debase herself.

"Yes... Your Highness..."

Princess Strauss scrambled to her feet and fled, never once raising her head.

"I trust everyone understands? The Eastern Empress treasures us all so deeply—she would never permit such unpleasantness within the imperial palace."

Medea watched the retreating figure with a gentle smile.

It was not a question. It was a declaration.

She spoke as though she owned this tea party—as though she needed no permission from the Eastern Empress at all.

The participants swallowed their breath.

We assumed the Crown Princess would be gentler than the First Prince...

But where was that supposed gentleness now? Where was the kindness?

She had just ground the Eastern Empress's dignity into dust before the entire gathering.

Perhaps the black devil would only accept his own kind as a lifelong companion. She is exactly like him—fierce and merciless, as though not a drop of blood would flow if you stabbed her.

At least the First Prince warns you with his grim appearance. But this one...

That fragile, delicate exterior. That soft-spoken manner.

Anyone deceived by such a facade would find their skull crushed before they realized their mistake—just like Princess Strauss.

No one dared speak.

The dessert fork still rested on the table, the impaled worm a silent warning.

The nobles kept their gazes lowered, frantically reviewing their own conduct—had they shown hostility? Committed some offense they'd forgotten?

In that moment, the Eastern Empress ceased to exist for them.

They had no attention to spare for anyone else when decapitation loomed at any moment.

The Eastern Empress's fingers tightened on her gown, crushing the delicate fabric into a mass of wrinkles.

By the time the frigid tea party concluded and the last guest had departed, those wrinkles had only multiplied.

"Are you satisfied now?"

When only the two of them remained, the Eastern Empress spat the words through clenched teeth.

"Now I understand how my Angelique suffered. Ensnared by a serpent like you—how could she possibly have endured? You must have taken such pleasure in tearing off her limbs one by one."

Medea smiled.

"You flatter me. The First Prince is quite skilled at such persuasion techniques. Even with individuals precious to Your Majesty, the process wasn't particularly difficult."

"You threaten my people's lives and now you boast of it?"

"I returned them with all their limbs intact. If Your Majesty still finds cause for complaint... perhaps my sincerity would have been better conveyed had I left marks upon their fine skin."

Medea shrugged with elegant indifference.

The Eastern Empress's beautiful jaw trembled. It took every ounce of control—decades of warfare against the Western Empress—to prevent herself from exploding.

"Crown Princess."

Her voice dropped to something cold and dangerous.

"Do you have any idea how many women I've watched preen over their little tricks before their heads rolled?"

"Your Majesty need not regard me as an enemy."

The menacing atmosphere that had gripped the greenhouse vanished. In its place bloomed something soft and warm, like a spring breeze.

The smile on Medea's lips remained unchanged.

"The Fourth Princess rendered considerable service to Valdina. How could I ever forget such generosity? To speak plainly... I am on your side."

Something in the Eastern Empress's eyes flickered—then ignited.

Unable to contain herself any longer, she seized a teacup and hurled it across the room.

CRASH!

"Are you... are you mocking us? Mother and daughter both?"

Who had reduced Angelique to her current state?

Despite the violent shattering of porcelain, Medea did not flinch.

"It was the Fourth Princess who proposed offering me as sacrifice and invited wolves to hunt me down. Does Your Majesty truly wish to claim that her alliance with Samon Claudio was somehow my doing?"

"You... you...!"

No one had ever spoken so bluntly to the Eastern Empress's face.

Her entire body trembled with barely contained fury.

"Even so, if Your Majesty chooses to see things that way... then I have misjudged you entirely."

Despite the Empress's rage, Medea clicked her tongue with an expression of mild disappointment. Those sea-green eyes swept over the bronzed beauty before her.

"I had believed you to be a woman of considerable power—one who had endured decades of battle against the Western Empress."

"Ha! Now your true colors emerge."

The Eastern Empress exhaled with a bitter sneer.

"You think to place yourself above me."

Her gaze fell upon the crimson signet ring adorning Medea's finger.

"The Emperor's protection. Your husband's seal. You believe this entire empire now rests in your hands."

Her teeth ground together.

"Is that why you've grown so arrogant?"

But Medea only smiled—peacefully, as though the Empress's hatred simply did not exist.

"When there is a common enemy, today's adversary can become tomorrow's ally."

"What...?"

"The First Prince Cesare. The Third Prince Juan."

Medea's gaze hardened with sudden intensity.

"Of the two men who remain in contention for the throne—which poses the greater threat to Your Majesty's safety, should he ascend?"

The Eastern Empress fell silent.

"The First Prince, whose wife owes a debt of gratitude to the Fourth Princess? Or the Third Prince, whose mother is the Western Empress herself?"

"You—!"

"Allow me to simplify."

Medea's voice grew softer still—almost gentle.

"To preserve the comfort and position Your Majesty currently enjoys... with whom can you coexist?"

End Chapter 300
