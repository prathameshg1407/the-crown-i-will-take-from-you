Chapter 194

The Serpent's
Comfort

"The sweetest poison is the one that tastes like salvation."

"Silence! Hold your tongue!"

Crimson droplets trickled from the fresh wound at Samon's temple as his screams tore through the chamber. In his frenzy, he had cut himself on one of the countless fragments still littering the floor—remnants of everything he had shattered.

"Worthless vermin—every last one of you deserves to die!"

With blood streaming down his face and features twisted into a mask of pure malice, he looked less like a nobleman and more like a demon clawed fresh from the depths of hell.

No one dared approach. The servants pressed themselves against the walls, paralyzed by terror.

Then a woman stepped forward.

"Lean on me."

She wiped the blood and grime from his face with gentle hands, then braced herself to help him rise.

"Before I tear off your face, I'll—"

Samon's threat died in his throat. He blinked, unable to process what he was seeing.

"Marieu?"

The woman smiled—warm, steady, utterly unafraid.

"Yes, darling. It's me."

"Is it truly you? Why are you here...?"

For a single, suspended moment, his madness receded. Shock had accomplished what no amount of pleading could.

"We can speak later. For now—someone help me!"

At Marieu's call, the servants finally dared to move forward.

"Get away from me! What are you doing?! Remove this woman from my sight immediately!"

But no sooner had they approached than Samon's fury reignited. He shoved Marieu away with violent force, his composure shattering once more.

The shame was unbearable. To be seen like this—broken, crippled, pathetic—by the very woman he had discarded like a worn-out garment.

He didn't even notice the blood still flowing freely from his temple.

"Master!"

The physician examining his wounds wore an expression of grave concern.

"If he continues like this, the damage will worsen significantly. The healing process will be delayed by weeks. I recommend a sedative to calm him."

"Yes, doctor. Whatever you think best. Please—don't leave. Stay and help him."

Marieu's calm, measured response only inflamed Samon further. He clawed at his own clothes, tearing fabric, hurling anything within reach.

How dare she! How dare that insignificant creature witness my humiliation!

"Did you not hear me?! Make her invisible to me! You bring this woman here to watch me like some pitiful spectacle?! Do you want me to die of rage?! I'll have you all hanged!"

"Yes, yes—I'm leaving. Please, just try to remain calm."

Even after the servants had escorted Marieu from the room and she vanished from view, Samon's breath came in ragged gasps.

"Treacherous filth! You dare parade that woman before me?! Do you think because I'm crippled you can treat me like a fool?!"

"My lord, Master—we only meant to—"

"When I speak, you close your mouth and obey! Are you mocking me because I cannot walk?!"

Blood began seeping from his wound anew, staining the bandages crimson.

"Shall I demonstrate that I can still tear you apart, one by one?! SHALL I?!"

In desperation, a terrified servant pressed a cloth soaked in sedative against Samon's face.

Slowly, heavily, his eyelids descended.

❧

That evening—the evening Samon had driven Marieu away—darkness settled over the mansion like a shroud.

Two maids passed by his chamber, their footsteps soft on the polished floor.

"Strange, isn't it? So quiet in there."

"Can you blame him? He worked himself into such a state they had to drug him unconscious."

"But he should have woken by now..."

"The sedative must still be working. And honestly, what's the point of worrying about it? The moment he opens his eyes, he'll be finding fault with everything and screaming at everyone again."

The unusual silence had loosened the servants' tongues. With the master unconscious and no threat of his explosive temper, their vigilance had grown lax.

One maid glanced around to ensure they were alone, then let her voice drop to a conspiratorial whisper.

"He only terrorizes us—the lowborn ones. Notice how he never dares open his mouth to the Valdinians or the royal family?"

"What can he do? He's lost any chance of becoming Emperor now. So he takes it out on us instead."

The servants of this household were as calculating and cunning as their master. They had learned from him well. And now that he was disabled—now that his path to power had been severed along with his leg—his threats no longer carried the weight they once had.

"If we don't like it, we can always leave. Find employment elsewhere. Isn't that right? As the master himself said—who would want to remain bound to a cripple?"

"Exactly. Come, let's hurry along before he wakes and we get caught in another tirade. It's been an exhausting day."

Their whispered gossip faded as they quickened their pace down the corridor.

Samon's chamber remained silent.

"Ugh... ugh..."

He was lost in nightmares.

Brilliant crimson flames danced before his eyes. Above him, chunks of stone wall rained down like the wrath of an angry god.

He was trapped in that moment. That terrible, endless moment.

"Please... no... no..."

In the dream, Samon lay pinned beneath the rubble, unable to move. His right leg—what remained of it—was nothing but agony, crushed beyond recognition.

Help me. Save me!

He screamed and screamed, but his tongue refused to cooperate. No sound emerged. Tears burned behind his eyes.

I don't want to die. I don't want to die. Please, please...

Just as his terror reached its peak—just as the darkness threatened to swallow him whole—a gentle hand touched his face.

Warmth spread through his rigid body. Soft fingers carefully wiped away the sweat beading on his forehead.

Pat. Pat.

A palm pressed gently against his chest, over his racing heart.

"Darling, be at peace. Don't suffer. Rest now."

The voice was thick with tears, trembling with concern.

And somehow—impossibly—he felt his screaming body grow calm.

The stone walls that had imprisoned him began to recede. The nightmare loosened its grip, retreating like a tide withdrawing from the shore.

Samon's eyes fluttered open, still clouded with sleep.

He needed to see her. The one who had ended that horrible vision.

"Marieu... why are you still... here?"

But when he registered the red-haired beauty seated at his bedside, his expression curdled.

"Darling, you need help."

He slapped away the pale hand reaching toward his bandages.

His teeth ground together. Fury simmered in his chest—fury born of shame. She had witnessed him at his most vulnerable, thrashing and whimpering like a frightened child.

"I want to help you."

"Get out! Have you come to mock me?! Don't you know I'm having you prosecuted for abandonment?!"

Marieu shook her head slowly. Her large eyes glistened with unshed tears, but beneath them lay something steady and unwavering.

"I simply could not bear to watch you suffer. Even now, tormented by nightmares, crying out in your sleep..."

"..."

"There is no one at your side. Where is the Fourth Princess? Where is your family? Everyone fears you. No one truly understands the pain you carry—how much you are suffering. That is why I came."

Samon fell silent.

He could not refute her words.

Before the explosion, there had been no shortage of visitors to the mansion—sycophants and opportunists who calculated that Samon might yet replace Peleus and claim the throne.

But now that his claim to succession had been obliterated, there was nothing. Not even a letter from the Emperor of Katzen.

He had been abandoned.

The saying that a person's heart differs when arriving versus leaving had never felt so brutally real.

Samon fixed Marieu with a hard, searching stare. Testing her. Probing for the lie beneath the sweetness.

"Silence! You wretched creature! Do you think you can worm your way back into my good graces after everything?! I don't need your pity! Stop this pathetic performance and leave!"

"I despise myself for this. I still resent you for what you did to me. But when I heard you were suffering... I couldn't remain idle. What was I supposed to do?"

Tears spilled down Marieu's cheeks.

"Don't say anything more. I understand what you mean. Just... let me stay."

Samon raised his arm and draped it across his eyes. Darkness enveloped him like a burial shroud.

"Just sleep. Don't think about anything. I'll be here..."

"...If I open my eyes and see you still here, I won't let you leave alive."

"Darling, sleep now. This time, you'll have only sweet dreams."

A warm hand patted his chest in a gentle, rhythmic cadence.

And true to her promise, the nightmares did not return that night.

❧

The following morning, Samon awoke to sunlight streaming through the window.

And there, curled beside him, was Marieu—fast asleep.

"..."

"Good heavens! How did that woman get in here?! I'll remove her at once!"

A servant who had entered to check on the master rolled up her sleeves and advanced toward Marieu, reaching for a fistful of red hair.

"Leave her."

"...Pardon, my lord?"
