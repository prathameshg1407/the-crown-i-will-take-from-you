Fasade Headquarters

"Boss. Look."

Gallo flicked a stiff card across the table.

It slid to a stop, catching the lamplight. Gold dust glittered across thick ivory paper, gaudy and wasteful.

The White Rose Mansion

District Two

An invitation from the Prince Regent.

Cesare didn't look up. He continued sharpening his dagger, the whetstone singing softly.

"The Royal Family claims the treasury is empty. Yet the Regent sprinkles gold like sand."

He snorted.

"He invited Acares. That's you."

"Ignore it."

"We can't. If Fasade wants to stay in Valdina, we play noble games. You said you'd reveal yourself eventually."

He rolled a die across the table.

It landed on a skull.

"And besides, it's a banquet celebrating the Princess's recovery. Aren't you curious?"

Gallo added, grinning.

The dagger paused.

Rain-soaked stone. Blue lips. A girl who knelt until her body nearly failed.

"She dismantles his power, and he throws her a party."

Cesare murmured.

Gallo leaned in.

"Oh, it gets better. Yesterday, she sent a boy to the market."

"So?"

"Hemlock. Nightshade. And a very specific root."

Cesare's eyes lifted.

"The kind that causes permanent impotence."

Silence.

Gallo burst out laughing.

"Who do you think she's planning to—"

The dagger left Cesare's hand.

It embedded itself in the wall, quivering, a finger's breadth from Gallo's ear.

"Too much imagination."

Cesare said flatly.

"Message received! Working now!"

Gallo squeaked, scrambling away.

The door slammed.

"Come out, Terence."

A concealed panel slid aside. A tall man with thin features and bloodshot eyes stepped out, holding a glass of steaming green liquid.

"Gallo is exhausting."

Terence muttered.

Cesare took the glass and drank it in one swallow. Bitter. Ashy.

"The tremors?"

"Gone."

Terence checked his pulse anyway. His frown deepened.

"You had another seizure."

Cesare said nothing.

"It's accelerating. Three years in, and the curse is tightening. We should go to the Magic Tower. My master might slow it."

"And then what? Wait politely for my heart to turn to stone?"

He stood.

"That isn't me."

"Is death preferable?"

"There's no need for a gentle end."

Cesare placed a hand over his chest. The beat beneath was heavy—dull.

" I gamble.
And I don't fold. "

Golden eyes burned.

"I conquer this curse, or I burn everything down trying."

The Princess's Palace

Morning light spilled through silk curtains.

"Your Highness, a message from the Marquis."

Neril said, entering.

Medea read.

The Casey Aid Center is complete. Over half the retired soldiers have relocated.

Good.

The rebels had lost their backbone.

Her gaze moved on.

Regarding 'Theo'—no such soldier exists. However, records show a deceased veteran with a twin brother named Theo.

Medea smiled.

Found you.

"Neril. Have the Marquis locate him. Offer him an administrative post at the Center."

"Yes, Your Highness."

Medea's eyes drifted to the gold-dusted card on her desk.

A Banquet Celebrating
the Recovery of
Princess Medea

A stage.

A snare.

A public execution of sympathy.

"Prepare my dress."

She said calmly.

Neril hesitated.

"The Regent's invitation—"

"I know exactly what it is. And exactly how to ruin it."

She stood.

Her eyes gleamed.

"We're going to a party."
