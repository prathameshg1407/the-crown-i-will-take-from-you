The Nightmare

Between Dream and Waking

When Medea's consciousness surfaced, silence enveloped her like a burial shroud.

Beside her lay Cesare.

They had carried him through that merciless blizzard, but his body had grown cold beneath her hands. The faint heartbeat she had clung to—the fragile rhythm that had been her only reassurance—had fallen silent.

Her own heart thundered against her ribs, wild and desperate.

Cesare.

She reached for him with trembling hands, shaking his shoulders, searching for any sign of life.

But those golden eyes—the ones that had always looked at her with such unwavering intensity—remained closed. No flicker of recognition. No warmth.

No...

Her voice refused to emerge. The world had become a vacuum, all sound swallowed by an oppressive ringing that filled her skull.

And with each passing second, his body grew colder still.

"CESARE—!"

Medea jolted awake with a gasp that tore through her chest.

The faint, earthy scent of medicinal herbs brushed against her senses. Above her stretched an unfamiliar gray ceiling, its rough wooden beams swimming slowly into focus.

A dream. It was only a dream.

Her fingers clutched at the rough linen sheets, knuckles white with the force of her grip. Her heart continued its frantic rhythm, refusing to believe in safety.

She could not separate the nightmare she had just escaped from the hazy reality that surrounded her now. The boundaries between terror and truth had blurred beyond recognition.

Her last memory before darkness claimed her: fingers locked around the fabric of Cesare's sleeve, refusing to let go even as consciousness slipped away.

Without thinking, Medea's hand reached across the bed, searching for him.

Her palm found only cold, empty sheets.

She sat up abruptly, heart plummeting into a void of fresh terror. She was alone. The space beside her bore the impression of another body, but he was gone.

Did he survive?

Or was I too late after all?

"No... please, no..."

Heat rushed behind her eyes, threatening to overflow. A broken sound—half sob, half prayer—escaped her lips.

At that precise moment, the door creaked open.

A silhouette filled the low doorframe—shoulders so broad they nearly brushed both sides, frame so tall he had to duck beneath the lintel.

Bandages wrapped his torso, visible through the loose flannel shirt that hung open at his chest. Despite the wounds, his shoulders remained powerful, his presence commanding.

A cold gust swept through the open door, stirring dark hair that fell across features she had memorized in another lifetime.

The moment she recognized him, Medea was already moving.

"Cesare!"

The Confession

Words Long Unspoken

Every room in Mr. Jenkins' modest cottage had been built to accommodate its owner's unremarkable stature. For a man of Cesare's height, the doorframes presented a constant challenge.

And so, when he bent to enter the room, his reaction came a heartbeat too late.

Medea launched herself at him before he could straighten.

He caught her instinctively, arms wrapping around her trembling form, pulling her tight against his chest to steady her. The movement sent bolts of pain through his wounded body, sharp and insistent.

"...Dea?"

But such pain was nothing. Less than nothing.

"Are you hurt? Tell me what's wrong—where does it pain you?"

"You... you're..."

Medea could not complete the sentence. Her arms locked around his neck, her face buried against his shoulder, her entire body shaking with suppressed sobs.

Cesare froze.

In all the time he had known her—through every trial and tribulation, through schemes and battles and moments that would have broken lesser souls—he had never seen Medea weep like this. Never witnessed her composure shatter so completely, her breath coming in ragged gasps, tears streaming unchecked down her pale cheeks.

His expression grew grave, every line of his face sharpening with concern.

"Dea, speak to me. Slowly—tell me where it hurts. What happened while I was gone?"

He had remained at her side through every moment of her recovery, never straying further than arm's reach. Only minutes ago had he stepped out briefly, hoping to find some means of communicating with the outside world.

What could possibly have occurred in so short a time?

"I thought... I was certain..."

The words emerged broken, interrupted by trembling breaths.

"I was so sure I would lose you. That everyone I care for is destined to slip through my fingers."

Cesare went rigid, his arms tightening around her involuntarily.

"I shouldn't have let you leave like that. The thought that our last words might have been... that I never told you..."

She was speaking rapidly now, the words tumbling out as though a dam had finally broken.

"I regretted it every moment. I need you to know—I want to stay by your side. My feelings... they mirror yours. I've just been too much of a coward to admit it."

"...Dea. Do you understand what you're saying?"

Cesare's hands cupped her face with impossible gentleness, tilting it upward until their eyes met. His expression was carefully controlled, but something blazed in those golden depths—something barely contained.

"I need you to be certain. Because once I have you, I will never let go. Even if you wish it. Even if you beg."

He knew himself too well. For years, he had waited for even a fragment of her heart. If she gave herself to him now, no force in existence would pry her from his grasp.

"There will be no taking this back."

"I know. I know I'm a coward—I've always known."

Fresh tears spilled down her cheeks, but she did not look away.

"I understand everything, and still I'm terrified. If you were to betray me... I don't think I could survive it. I don't think I could ever recover."

She was laying herself bare before him—every fear, every selfish thought, every shameful weakness she had hidden for so long.

"I know how selfish this sounds. I've been using you for my own purposes, manipulating circumstances to serve my goals..."

Her voice grew smaller, more fragile.

"I don't deserve—"

"Stop."

His voice cut through her self-recrimination like a blade.

"Do you think I've forgotten? I was the one who pressed you. The one who insisted you use me however you saw fit—who begged for even that much of your attention."

A muscle ticked in his jaw.

"Convincing the Emperor to change course doesn't mean you were using me. You have never used me."

He paused, and in that silence, he recalled his final words before departing—the careless, cutting things he had said in his frustration.

In his arms, Medea was still trembling.

She thought I was happy to come to her rescue. That finding her waiting was enough.

But I'm the one who drove her to this brink.

"You cunning woman."

The words emerged rough, almost angry—but the anger was directed entirely inward.

"...No. I am the one at fault."

He could imagine it now—how his reckless words must have echoed in her mind during every frozen moment of their separation. How she had replayed them again and again while fighting through that hellish blizzard to find him.

The realization carved through his chest like a dull blade.

"It was my own cowardice that drove me to speak so thoughtlessly."

He pressed his lips to her temple, letting the words fall against her skin like a confession.

"I have been afraid. Terrified that you would leave me. That once I helped you claim the throne, you would cast me aside without a second thought."

Medea's eyes widened, lifting to meet his gaze.

"You may laugh at me if you wish. I know how pathetic it sounds."

The man who had never once bowed before any power—who had seized everything he desired through sheer force of will—now stood before her with his pride in ruins.

"But hear me now, Dea."

His voice dropped, raw and unguarded.

"The thought of losing you frightens me more than the thought of never becoming Emperor."

Silence stretched between them, heavy and profound.

All because he craved her heart. All because he wanted to be wanted.

"...You did nothing wrong."

Medea's voice was barely a whisper.

"From the very beginning until now, everything has been my choice. My will."

Something vast and tender swelled within her chest—a warmth that threatened to overflow.

She could no longer contain what she felt for this man.

Without conscious thought, Medea rose on her toes and pressed her lips to the sharp line of his jaw.

Cesare turned to stone.

Every muscle in his body locked into rigid stillness, as though the slightest movement might shatter the moment into fragments.

Those golden eyes—deep as any abyss—fixed upon her face with an intensity that stole her breath. She could not tell whether the hunger burning in them was being unleashed or ruthlessly restrained.

Medea lifted her chin and kissed him again.

"I want you to understand my feelings."

She pressed her lips to his like someone knocking gently at a door that had been locked for far too long.

Once.

Twice.

"Ah...!"

The third time, he consumed her.

One moment she was offering the gentlest of touches; the next, his mouth had claimed hers entirely. His tongue swept past her parted lips without hesitation, exploring the warmth within, giving her no opportunity to breathe, no space to retreat.

Medea's eyes went wide, then fluttered closed.

Her head tilted back of its own accord as a firm hand cradled the nape of her neck, holding her exactly where he wanted her. His other arm wrapped around her waist, crushing her against him until not a whisper of space remained between their bodies.

It was then that fire ignited between them.

He moved her backward with the unstoppable force of a storm—gentle in its direction, inexorable in its power.

How is this possible...

Throughout her previous life, Medea had considered herself detached from such physical matters. The intimacies between men and women had seemed distant things—obligations to be endured rather than experiences to be savored.

But nothing in her existence had prepared her for this.

Jason had never kissed her with such ferocity, such consuming hunger. He had never made her feel as though she were being devoured and worshipped in the same breath.

"Dea."

His voice scraped low and rough, as though dragged across gravel.

The heat that had been smoldering in his golden eyes seemed to spread through her entire body in an instant—racing through her veins, pooling low in her stomach, stealing the air from her lungs.

No—it spread through the entire room, until the very air grew thick and warm around them.

Her body burned. Her thoughts scattered. She felt as though she stood within the heart of a flame, unable to escape, uncertain if she even wanted to.

Cesare drew breath in short, controlled intervals, as though he could sense every tremor running through her and was measuring his restraint accordingly.

But he never broke the kiss.

Not once.

In that small room, far from empires and enemies,
two souls finally spoke the truth they had carried in silence.

And in the space between words,
they found something neither had dared to hope for.
