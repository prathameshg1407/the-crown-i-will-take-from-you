Ambient: Defiance
The Stand

The
Unbowed

"A rock planted in a rising tide"

Duchess Catherine

"Your Highness, please. This is not the time to be stubborn. Think of your poor Aunt—"

clasping her hands as if in prayer

Her voice trembled with rehearsed concern.

It was pitiful.

Medea did not look at her.

She stood unmoving, a rock planted in a rising tide, her expression calm enough to be infuriating.

Queen Mother

The Queen Mother's restraint finally shattered.

"Are you deaf because you are arrogant? Do you think that Seal makes you untouchable? You vicious little creature! You will ruin everyone around you—just like you ruined your parents!"

The words struck like knives.

In her past life, they would have destroyed her. She would have collapsed, sobbing, begging to be loved.

But that child was dead.

Let the hatred come. Let it deepen.

One day, it would explode—right in the Queen Mother's face.

"I cannot give you the answer you want. So I will not speak falsely."

"You dare disobey me? You refuse to hand over the Seal?"

The court held its breath.

— Siseo's Calculation —
Lord Siseo

Siseo's brow furrowed.

He had always considered Medea a liability—a puppet, a weak point the Regent exploited. Rationally, surrendering the Seal would stabilize things.

But this… this was no longer governance. It was cruelty.

"Your Highness, this matter carries grave consequences. Perhaps you should reconsider—"

Princess Medea

"My brother is the King."

Her voice sliced cleanly through the noise.

"Privately, he is my family. Publicly, he is the Sovereign of Valdina. This Seal was granted by the highest authority in the land."

She turned to the throne.

"Grandmother, you may punish me as kin. But you do not have the authority to revoke a royal decree."

The room froze.

"If even the Queen Mother can overturn the King's orders out of personal anger, then who will obey the King's laws tomorrow?"

Silence.

Siseo went still.

Catherine's breath caught.

The logic was merciless—and flawless.

If the Queen Mother seized the Seal, she would publicly undermine Peleus's authority. She would declare that royal law bent to emotion.

That was an invitation to treason.

The Queen Mother's face twisted, rage burning into humiliation.

"There is nothing wrong with what you said. I have no authority to take the Seal."

Relief flickered—then died.

"However—"

she smiled, slow and cruel

"As your grandmother, I do have the right to punish your insolence."

Medea did not move.

"You admitted your rudeness yourself."

The Queen Mother stood.

"You will kneel in the chapel and offer prayers of penance. You will remain there until the Goddess herself reveals forgiveness."

A sharp intake of breath rippled through the court.

Divine revelation was a miracle. Even saints waited lifetimes.

This was not penance.

It was slow execution.

"No one is to give this child even a sip of water until she repents."

"Your Majesty—"

Siseo stepped forward.

"Enough. This is a family matter. Return to your duties."

— The Tree in the Storm —

Siseo hesitated.

He looked at Medea.

She stood straight, alone, unbowed—like a tree refusing to break in a storm.

There was no fear in her eyes.

Only resolve.

I can't save her, Siseo realized grimly.

Not without defying the Queen herself.

He bowed stiffly and left.

Outside, cold air bit into his lungs.

"Your Majesty, return quickly. You must win. Or this palace will devour itself."

murmured to the absent King, staring up at the sky

— The Loyal Servant —
Neril

The doors opened again—hard, fast footsteps.

"Your Majesty!"

Neril threw herself to the floor, forehead striking stone.

"The Princess is injured! She is still recovering! She cannot survive such punishment—please!"

"Who is this vermin?"

the Queen Mother demanded.

"I am her maid! Punish me instead! I beg you!"

"Remove her."

Princess Medea

"Move, Neril."

Medea stepped in front of her.

"I offended my Grandmother. I will bear the punishment."

She met the Queen Mother's eyes.

No fear.

No plea.

Only acceptance.

"Hah. You truly are a hateful child."

"Go. Now."

"Yes."

Medea bowed once and walked away, head high.

— Catherine's Fear —

Behind her, Catherine spoke hesitantly.

"Mother… please lessen the severity. She is frail. If she falls ill—"

"Enough! If you have time to worry about that wretch, attend to your own house!"

Catherine froze.

Birna tugged her sleeve.

"Mother, why do you care? She ignored Grandmother completely."

"Quiet!"

Catherine dragged her daughter away, heart pounding.

This was spiraling out of control.

Quiggin had fallen.

Maria had confessed.

The Regent's shadow was exposed.

And worst of all—

Someone had predicted everything.

Someone had turned the trap inside out.

Catherine glanced back at the empty hall.

She had always believed Medea was a piece on the board.

For the first time, she wondered—

What if Medea was the hand moving them?

Fear,

cold and unfamiliar,

settled into her chest.
