The Princess's Palace

"Gilliforth located them. Last sighting—Asilum Street. Slums on the eastern outskirts."

Neril said, handing over the report.

"Asilum Street…"

Medea repeated softly.

Neril hesitated.

"Shall I fetch Theo?"

"No."

Medea folded the parchment.

"In my last life, Theo hated Valdina. If he sees Royal Guard armor, he will vanish—or draw blood."

Theo was not merely a rebel.

He was the spine of the rebellion.

A strategist forged by grief. A man whose rage had once turned starving mobs into an army that breached the capital gates.

If he lived, the future could burn.

If he died, it could still rot.

I won't decide his fate through reports.

"I will go myself."

Asilum Street

The smell struck first.

Rotting refuse. Open sewage. Human despair left too long in the sun.

Neril's hand slid instinctively toward her sword.

"This place is…"

She trailed off.

"Miserable."

Medea finished.

Her gaze lingered on hollow-eyed children and gaunt figures huddled in doorways.

"When people have nothing, they stop fearing consequences. That's where revolutions are born."

They moved through the alleyways in plain cloaks, but the lie was obvious. Their skin was too clean. Their posture too straight. Hunger watched them from every shadow.

"Miss. Care for a fortune?"

A rasp croaked.

Neril half-drew her blade.

An old woman sat cross-legged on a frayed mat, hood low, hands twisted like dead roots.

"Go."

Neril warned.

"Wait."

Medea tossed a copper coin onto the mat.

"I don't need my future told. I already know it."

The old woman chuckled—a dry, cracking sound.

"Do you?"

A pause.

"If you're living again, child… surely things will be different."

Medea froze.

Slowly, she turned.

"What did you say?"

Click. Click.

Cracked crystal beads rolled between the woman's fingers.

"Sit."

Against her better judgment, Medea sat.

"This life won't be kinder. Chaos blocks your road. They wish to bury the Saint and steal her light."

The beads fell into an uneven pattern.

"But fear not. A great star falls with you. A Black Star. He will cleave your path."

"I don't need him. I don't need a savior."

She had trusted once.

That trust had killed her.

"I'll carve my fate myself."

The old woman smacked her tongue.

"Stubborn child. The squishy things will try to eat you again. They crave your light."

"If I'm devoured twice, then I deserve it."

The woman smiled.

Blood leaked from the corner of her mouth.

"Have you heard of the Dawn's Droplet?"

"No."

"A divine medicine. It erases all things—sickness, memory… even destiny. When the tower falls, it will save the Black Star."

She coughed, swaying.

"Heaven turned back the stars for you. Don't waste it."

"You're bleeding. Neril—"

"No."

The woman pushed her hand away, grip unexpectedly strong.

"My story ends here."

She squeezed Medea's fingers.

"You carry the blood of Sages. The will of the Goddess. Only you can choose the end."

Medea pulled back, shaken.

"Neril. Bring her to me. I need answers."

Neril rushed back.

Moments later, she returned pale.

"She's gone."

"What?"

"No mat. No beads. No footprints. Even the dust is undisturbed."

As if she had never been.

A Shabby Hut

"Saya! You're back!"

Angela leaned over the broken fence.

"Yes. I found work."

Saya said, clutching a loaf of black bread.

"Alone? Child, it's dangerous. Traffickers prowl these streets."

Saya nodded stiffly.

"You should go to the Casey Aid Center. Near the castle wall. Safe. Your father was a veteran, wasn't he?"

"I'll… think about it."

Saya slipped inside and locked the door.

Military village.

Clean water. Food. Safety.

She slid down the door, shaking.

"How can I go? Theo is wanted."

Her brother had beaten a corrupt guard for stealing their food. He fled to avoid execution, leaving her behind.

If she registered at the Aid Center, they would check records.

Theo would be caught the moment he returned.

Her mother died giving birth to her.

Her father bled out years after the war.

And now Theo was gone.

She was alone.

A knock sounded.

Saya froze.

"Who is it?"

"We're looking for Theo."

A calm female voice answered.
