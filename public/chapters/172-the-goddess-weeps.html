Chapter 172

The Goddess
Weeps

"Even divine statues shed tears when false prophets speak."

A murmur of agreement rippled through the assembled dignitaries as another voice joined the chorus supporting the Saintess.

"These are words truly worthy of the Goddess's representative. Since you have received this extraordinary calling by divine will, perhaps the Emperor of Valdina should demonstrate the mercy our Goddess teaches?"

"..."

Joaquin and Jason exchanged furtive glances, unable to fathom why the Holy Kingdom had chosen to champion their cause. Yet with this unexpected alliance, the impossible—returning to Valdina—suddenly seemed within reach.

I don't understand what's happening, but circumstances are aligning more favorably than anticipated. If I simply ride this momentum...

"Your Majesty, *sob*... please, show us the Goddess's mercy."

Gazing down at Joaquin's theatrical display, Jason recalled the clandestine meeting from several days prior—before his departure for Valdina.

Secret benefactors had visited him then.

"I cannot fulfill the Emperor's command to restore House Claudio to the Valdina royal family. Those siblings would never accept such a thing."

"Don't concern yourself. You have allies there as well. Your Highness, you will obtain exactly what you desire."

Could he be the ally they mentioned...?

Jason's eyes fixed sharply on High Priest Paul, who continued his impassioned sermon.

"Every soul across this continent who witnesses Your Majesty's clemency today will remember your grace. When news of this spreads, His Holiness the Pope himself will be pleased."

Paul wielded the Pope's name like a subtle blade, applying pressure through implied authority.

You arrogant northerners—no matter how high you soar, do you truly believe you can dismiss our Holy Kingdom? We must engrave in their minds that ignoring us means forfeiting any future on this continent.

What had begun as protection of Claudio for the Saintess's sake had gradually transformed into a matter of wounded pride. Perhaps he still couldn't shake the humiliating memory of his hasty retreat from Valdina three months ago.

"..."

Emperor Peleus remained silent, his expression unreadable.

Paul turned toward Medea, the corners of his mouth curling upward with calculated malice.

"What say you, Princess of Valdina? Do you believe we should sever all familial bonds and condemn them at any cost?"

I will expose that witch's true nature for the Saintess's sake!

In High Priest Paul's eyes—clouded as they were by months of manipulation at Rachel's hands—Medea represented the axis of evil, conspiring to harm the blessed Saintess.

It was a question designed to ensnare, constructed so that any response would invite criticism.

Medea gently grasped Peleus's sleeve, staying him as he moved to intervene.

Rachel... you've positioned Paul as your shield, your proxy in attacking me. Your tactics remain unchanged.

Just as in her previous life, Rachel preferred to strike from behind layers of protection.

The corners of Medea's lips lifted in anticipation.

The more you hide, the harder I will drag you into the light.

No more veils. No more shadows.

In her past life, Rachel had been venerated as the heroine who saved the continent—despite never leaving the safety of the Holy Lands and Katzen. She'd never slain an evil dragon, never suppressed demonic waves, but simply remained cloistered in peaceful temples.

Yet the world had worshipped her solely for the title of Saintess.

Watching the assembled nobles sway like reeds at Rachel's every gesture, Medea realized that pattern would repeat itself in this life as well.

Unless she acted.

Then I shall cast stones at that unshakeable faith.

Every eye in the banquet hall fixed upon Princess Valdina, awaiting her answer with bated breath.

"The Saintess has declared that we must forgive the transgressions of blood relatives."

Medea's lips curved into a serene smile.

"..."

"But tell me—since when has the Goddess's doctrine commanded unconditional forgiveness of sinners?"

Her emerald eyes turned to Rachel with crystalline clarity.

"To accept all without righteous judgment would contradict divine will itself."

Her calm voice struck like an open palm across Rachel's pristine cheek.

"The Goddess's mercy means discerning love—not limitless permission. Saintess, can the latter truly be called providence? I simply fail to comprehend."

Medea tilted her head with an expression of genuine puzzlement.

"That... that is..."

Rachel stood frozen, struck speechless by the sudden arrow flying toward her. The color drained from her face.

Paul rushed to defend his stunned charge.

"Princess! How dare you debate doctrine before the Goddess's chosen representative? What arrogance!"

"How could I presume to deny what the divine agent proclaims? However, if this truly represents the Goddess's will, then naturally I must accept it."

Paul seized upon her measured words, his eyes lighting with triumph.

"Are you saying the Princess will take personal responsibility for House Claudio's restoration?"

"High Priest, first we must establish whether the Goddess truly wishes to allow these rebels back into Valdina."

Medea's observation landed like a barbed hook.

"Ha! The Princess's beauty and virtue match her sterling reputation. However, this old servant must speak plainly."

Paul, rising to her provocation as if his very piety had been questioned, elevated his voice with righteous fervor.

"The Goddess abhors the arrogant! When Icarus plummeted from the heavens with broken wings, he learned too late the punishment for his hubris—for daring to challenge the Gods themselves!"

When the Princess offered no immediate response, Paul pressed forward with increasing confidence.

"Do you not comprehend who stands before you? The Saintess conveys the words of the Absolute! Her will *is* the Goddess's will, so how dare you question the divine—"

"GYAAAAH—!"

The Goddess Statue Weeps Blood

Paul's sermon shattered mid-sentence, cut off by a piercing scream of terror.

"Look! The statue of the Goddess—it's weeping tears of blood!"

A noblewoman pointed with trembling fingers toward the statue, her face ashen.

"Impossible! How can stone shed tears?"

"There—look there!"

A nobleman thrust his arm toward the banquet hall's wall. Those who followed his gesture erupted in gasps of shock and awe.

Crimson tears indeed flowed from both eyes of the Goddess statue mounted upon the wall.

The blood-red stains stood in stark relief against the statue's white marble surface. Twin rivulets descended from each eye, lending the divine visage an expression of grotesque sorrow.

What manner of omen is this?

Even Rachel and Paul stood momentarily paralyzed, struggling to comprehend the impossible sight before them.

Then someone's voice rang out with terrible clarity.

"The Goddess expresses Her fury! You have distorted divine will, and She weeps tears of blood in divine sorrow!"

The crowd murmured agreement—the interpretation resonated with undeniable logic.

The Goddess has manifested Her judgment.

"That cannot be! It's a deception—She would never deny me. I am the Saintess, how could She possibly—"

Rachel's composure shattered. She moved toward the statue, desperation bleeding through her carefully maintained facade.

Medea's voice cut through the chaos like a blade through silk.

"Since Your Eminence considers the Saintess equivalent to the Goddess Herself, do you now proclaim that a weeping divine statue is fraudulent?"

The expressions of the Holy Kingdom's priests transformed at Medea's pointed question.

However eagerly they had anticipated the Saintess's arrival, a representative remained merely that—a vessel, never the deity itself.

Paul froze, his own earlier words echoing with damning clarity in his mind.

"When Icarus plummeted with broken wings, he learned too late the punishment for his hubris—for daring to challenge the Gods themselves!"

Divine punishment for those who presumed equality with the divine.

"...Saintess..."

Rachel's carefully constructed facade crumbled as even the High Priest—her most devoted fanatic—regarded her with dawning uncertainty.

"Princess, don't you *dare* manipulate this situation!"

Rachel's sharp retort shattered the reverent atmosphere.

It stood in jarring contrast to the serene, gentle Saintess she had portrayed mere moments before.

Medea observed the transformation with barely concealed fascination.

Rachel... I've never witnessed this side of you before. Your expression was always so perfectly composed whenever we met in my previous life.

Seeing her lose control, watching confusion and desperation war across those features—it was both strange and deeply satisfying.

"The first crack in a perfect facade
reveals the flawed foundation beneath."
