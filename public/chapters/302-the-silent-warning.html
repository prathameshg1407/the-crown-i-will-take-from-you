Chapter 302

The Silent Warning

"Even those who cannot speak have stories to tell."

The Eastern Empress's hand drummed against the table, her fingers beating a steady rhythm on the fine tablecloth.

As the repetitive sound continued, her anger slowly ebbed away, replaced by unwelcome thoughts that crept into the silence.

'Could that girl truly bring Angelique back?'

The Western Empress's words echoed mockingly in her mind:

"Angelique will have to stay there for the rest of her life. You should put aside your regrets and resign yourself to reality."

At the memory, her nails dug deep into the tablecloth. Tears spilled down her cheeks before she could stop them.

Anger and grief rose together like twin flames.

'What use is this dazzling glass greenhouse? What good is the Emperor's eternal affection, all this comfort and splendor?'

She couldn't even dare to speak her child's name to the man she'd shared her life with for decades.

Imperial favor was a fickle thing—harvested the moment the Emperor's heart turned elsewhere.

Yet she clung to it desperately, displayed it like armor, because she knew better than anyone that the moment she lost it, she couldn't even guarantee her daughter's safety.

"If the First Prince's son ascends to the throne, the Western Empress will never allow Angelique to live."

How many years had they spent locked in this bitter rivalry?

It wouldn't be surprising if Angelique was found dead one day, screaming her last breath in some forgotten corner.

Yet even the First Prince, with his notoriously cruel disposition, seemed manageable by comparison.

"If the mother and daughter refrain from causing trouble, I can overlook their existence."

It appeared the monster cared enough for his wife to grant her his personal seal.

The reason the nobles at today's tea party had bowed so low wasn't solely due to the First Princess's immunity from prosecution.

That seal gleaming on the young woman's slender finger served as a constant reminder of the First Prince's formidable presence standing behind her.

"God..."

The Eastern Empress's voice caught in her throat.

Her daughter—raised so carefully that it wouldn't hurt even if she placed her in her own eyes.

"If that girl truly brings Angelique back..."

The Empress looked skyward, her eyes burning with desperate determination.

"I'll abandon all my ambitions and take their hands. I swear it."

But like the white clouds drifting across the sunny sky, that hope seemed impossibly distant.

"Please, Your Majesty the Eastern Empress..."

"Forgive us..."

The wailing of the three women, dragged out by palace maids, ceased abruptly once they reached a secluded corridor where no prying eyes could find them.

They raised their heads and exchanged furtive glances.

"How...?"

The Countess of Beaumont's fingers trembled as she clutched at her sleeves.

"Did everything work out exactly as the First Princess predicted?"

Her words made them all recall the same conversation in their minds:

"The reason you betrayed your mistress is because you were threatened—your families held hostage, their lives dangling by a thread."

"She understands Her Majesty's character better than we do, despite serving the Empress for decades..."

By weaving truth and lies together with surgical precision, the First Princess had created a narrative the Empress could accept. She had predicted exactly how the Empress would react at every stage.

That elaborate psychological manipulation had emerged from the mind of a young princess who had arrived in Katzen less than half a year ago.

"Without our cooperation, today's tea party wouldn't have proceeded as planned."

Lady Evelyn rubbed her arms nervously.

"At least Her Majesty will never discover the real reason we betrayed her."

And so they could remain at the Eastern Empress's side.

If the Empress knew their true motivations, she would never have shown them mercy.

"But our leashes... they're still firmly in the First Princess's hands."

Duchess Clarice spoke softly, and a heavy, uncomfortable silence descended.

They were fortunate to continue their lives as before.

Yet the anxiety that followed couldn't be dispelled by anything.

'We never know when or how the First Princess will choose to tighten this leash again.'

They hadn't escaped. Not truly. They remained imprisoned in the palm of the First Princess's hand.

A collective sigh whispered through the corridor.

"Your Highness, do you truly intend to bring the Fourth Princess back?"

Saya asked as they emerged from the glass greenhouse into brilliant sunlight.

"Yes."

"But what if the Fourth Princess, once returned... what if she suddenly turns against you? What if she aims for your position?"

Saya couldn't hide her concern, remembering the broken woman she'd seen trembling in Valdina.

"A bird that has already lost its wings cannot easily fly, no matter which wind blows."

Medea's tone remained soft yet unshakable.

"Besides, over the past three years, countless predators have been circling her, waiting to tear at her throat."

During the height of the succession crisis, it wasn't only the Western Empress who envied the Fourth Princess—who had once monopolized the Emperor's affection.

"I don't believe the Fourth Princess possesses the disposition to overcome such prolonged hardship and rise again. But even if she does somehow manage it... it's acceptable."

That was precisely why Medea had sent the Eastern Empress's entourage back to their mistress.

Once the Fourth Princess lost the Eastern Empress's backing, she would crumble once more.

Medea laughed quietly, her delicate fingers tracing patterns through the air as though plucking invisible strings—manipulating marionettes dangling beneath her fingertips.

After leaving the Eastern Palace, the two women walked briskly toward where their carriage waited.

Whether aware or oblivious to the eventful tea party that had just transpired, the sky remained brilliantly clear, and the imperial palace stretched vast and beautiful in all directions.

"Your Highness, I don't think this is the right way."

Saya looked around, confused.

To reach the carriage waiting area, they shouldn't be seeing this particular bridge and lake.

Few people passed through this section.

"I think we exited through the wrong door from the glass greenhouse. There were three identical doors..."

The Katzen Imperial Palace was notoriously labyrinthine—so vast and complex that even those who had spent decades within its walls could easily lose their way.

Though Medea knew every corner of Katzen's streets and alleys, she possessed limited knowledge of the palace's interior.

'It's a place I was never permitted to enter in my past life.'

Medea nodded in acknowledgment.

"Oh, it looks like there are palace maids over there."

Saya spotted human figures in the distance beyond the pier, near the lake's edge, and was about to redirect their steps.

Rustle.

As Medea straightened, alert, a small hand shot out from the nearby bushes and grabbed Saya's wrist.

"Who—!"

Saya tried to shake off the grip sharply, but quickly discovered the hand belonged to a young boy.

A small monk wearing a worn, shabby investigation robe. His mouth pressed into a tight line, his eyes wide and earnest.

The boy looked directly at Saya and shook his head vigorously.

"...What?"

Saya asked. The boy shook his head again with even greater urgency.

Then, with his large, clear eyes, he pointed deliberately to Saya, to Medea, and then toward a different bridge in the opposite direction.

"What are you trying to say..."

"Saya, I believe he's attempting to stop us. He's warning us not to go that way."

At Medea's words, Saya suddenly noticed the boy was missing one ear.

The moment she realized the child was deaf, Saya ceased her attempts to shake free and nodded slowly, deliberately.

The boy's face brightened immediately, relief washing over his features.

Then he turned and held out a cluster of fresh red roses he'd been holding in his other hand, offering them to Medea.

"Are you giving these to Her Highness?"

Saya initially assumed the gesture was a tribute to the First Princess. But Medea understood differently.

"Red roses are the symbol of the Katzen Emperor."

"Wait—is the Emperor nearby?"

The boy carefully read the shape of Medea's lips and nodded confirmation.

The stems of the freshly picked roses released a fresh, green fragrance.

"You're afraid I'll encounter His Majesty and get into trouble, so you came to warn me?"

Medea spoke clearly, enunciating each word. The boy nodded again vigorously.

But when Medea examined the boy's hands, she found no scratches whatsoever.

Which meant it wasn't the boy himself who had picked the roses to deliver the warning.

Medea's gaze swept the surrounding area.

In the distance, another gray robe similar to the boy's appeared briefly through the dense foliage before vanishing.

"Oh! The Eastern Palace is that direction? Thank you so much!"

Meanwhile, Saya deciphered the directions the boy indicated through his animated hand gestures and bowed her gratitude.

She quickly leaned close to whisper to Medea.

"Your Highness, I'll go ahead briefly to verify whether his directions are correct."

Despite the boy's clear, innocent eyes, Saya didn't seem to trust him completely.

"No, we'll go together."

"If it's a trap, Your Highness could be in danger."

"Which is precisely why you need to stay with me."

'I'll need you to protect me if things go wrong.'

Medea followed Saya and removed her bracelet, offering it to the boy as a token of gratitude.

The boy flailed his hands, frantically refusing the valuable gift.

Medea knelt down to fasten the bracelet around his thin wrist anyway. As the boy bowed his head in embarrassed acceptance, the collar he wore became visible through the gap in his monk's robe.

A piece of carved wood in the shape of a deer's antler hung from the leather strap.

"This..."

Medea's fingers reached toward the pendant, her eyes narrowing with sudden recognition.
