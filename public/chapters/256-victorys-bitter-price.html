Chapter 256

Victory's Bitter Price

The dragon falls, but new enemies rise from the shadows of triumph.

"D'Angel—do you see that golden glow? On the dragon's left wing?"

"Gold? Where are you looking?"

Perhaps the location was visible only to Medea's eyes—a gift purchased with dragon's blood and visions of ancient light.

"Follow me!"

They needed to change tactics immediately.

Medea urged Silva toward the highest terrain available, scanning for the optimal vantage point.

"D'Angel—when I give the signal, throw me toward that creature's left wing."

"...What?"

D'Angel looked as though he doubted his own ears, even in this dire situation.

Throw? Who? Where exactly am I throwing anyone?

"There's no time to explain! You need to launch me as high as possible!"

Medea gripped her sword with white-knuckled determination.

"Now!"

The moment the evil dragon—descending with deadly intent—reached out to seize Medea with razor-sharp talons—

She launched herself into the air.

Her silver hair rippled behind her like scattered starlight painting the sky.

SHHHNK!

Medea thrust her blade directly into the brilliantly glowing golden thread she alone could perceive.

It was a hairline fracture—a crack so minuscule it would be completely invisible against the dragon's black scales without the guiding light.

Yet even after hundreds of years, this ancient wound had never healed. It remained the only exposed flesh on the dragon's otherwise impenetrable hide.

"KRAAAAAAAAAH!"

The instant Medea's sword pierced the reverse scale, the dragon's agonized shriek tore through the heavens.

This was entirely different from the earth-shaking roar from moments before. This sound contained nothing but pure, unadulterated pain.

The evil dragon writhed in excruciating torment.

It spun its massive body violently, desperate to dislodge the tiny figure clinging to its wing.

Violent air currents whipped through the canyon like a hurricane.

"Just... a little... more!"

Medea's vision shook wildly. The air itself seemed to twist and distort around her.

But she refused to release the sword hilt embedded deep in the dragon's flesh.

'If only I had just a bit more strength—!'

She could sever these wings entirely and end this nightmare.

But Medea's current power wasn't sufficient. Simply maintaining her grip as the dragon thrashed was overwhelming enough.

"Damn it... Medea..."

In that critical moment, a powerful arm reached out from behind her, gripping her fragile hand with firm, heated warmth.

"This reckless... foolish... stubborn..."

Ragged breathing scattered overhead.

Cesare—who had somehow positioned himself behind Medea—swallowed the rest of his words before completing the sentence.

His heart pounded so violently she could feel each beat reverberating through her back.

"...Cesare."

Simultaneously, the sharp screech of tearing air and the biting wind vanished.

It felt surreal—as though an unbreakable shield had materialized around them both.

"Don't let go of my hand."

Cesare placed both his hands over Medea's, ensuring she couldn't release her grip even if she wanted to.

A razor-sharp aura of black light enveloped Medea's sword where their fingers overlapped.

His dark aura was denser than midnight itself—like a black star burning clearly even against the night sky.

"I'll count to three. One... two..."

Neither of them realized their faces were so close their lips nearly brushed each other's ears.

Cesare drove the blade deeper into the dragon's wound with crushing, merciless force.

Medea concentrated every ounce of her remaining strength, barely maintaining clarity through her fading consciousness.

"Now!"

The moment their combined power channeled into the sword's tip—

CRACK—SNAP—TEAR!

With a sickening sound of flesh and bone being rent asunder—

The dragon's wing split cleanly in two.

Blood and obsidian scales rained down from the sky like a morbid deluge.

"KRAAAAAAAAAAH!"

The dragon's desperate, dying scream tore through the air as it plummeted toward the earth like a falling meteor.

Just before the dragon's massive corpse struck the canyon floor, Cesare leaped clear—carrying Medea securely in his arms.

"KRAAAASH!"

"Your Highness!"

Neril and D'Angel sprinted toward them.

Eyes filled with worry, fear, relief, and a thousand other emotions fixed on Medea's bloodied form.

BOOM!!

The clones—having lost cohesion with the main body's death—also crashed to the ground, their torn wings useless.

Terrible screams echoed through the smoke-filled whirlwind rising from the valley floor.

"Is it... is it actually dead?"

Gallo and Tom approached the fallen dragon with extreme hesitation, as though unable to trust their own eyes.

They poked cautiously at the corner of its gaping maw with their sword sheaths—each movement executed with such exaggerated care it bordered on comical.

"Ugh..."

The evil dragon's eyes remained wide open, frozen in an expression that suggested it hadn't expected this ending.

Silent screams of agony still seemed to emanate from its twisted mouth.

But there was no spark of life remaining.

"It's dead! The evil dragon is DEAD! Our Princess defeated the evil dragon! Her Highness is the Dragonslayer!"

"We can go home now! I'm actually going home!"

Tom and Gallo—both covered head-to-toe in blood and gore—embraced each other and cheered with wild abandon.

Medea stood motionless, staring blankly.

They had survived. No one had died.

They had actually killed the evil dragon.

Even standing directly before the creature's corpse, it didn't feel remotely real.

Medea stared intently into the dragon's yellow eyes—now clouded and lightless.

She had swallowed dragon's blood and witnessed strange afterimages of ancient light.

Even the memory from moments ago—hanging onto those massive wings, spinning through the air while fighting for her life—

It all felt hazy and distant, like fragments of a fading dream.

Then, without warning, her body was roughly spun around.

"Excessive courage becomes recklessness! Were you actually planning to consume dragon's blood?!"

The fierce voice—stripped of all composure—sounded so furious it bordered on terrifying.

His firm hands frantically checked the marks left on Medea's hands, arms, and exposed skin.

"Cesare, wait—"

"First Prince, what do you think you're doing to our Princess?! Step back immediately!"

Cesare had begun trying to remove Medea's armor, but Neril's blade intercepted him.

Yet his fierce golden eyes remained locked solely on Medea, seemingly unaware of his own actions.

"Do you understand that the evil dragon was born from primordial darkness? Do you want to take that curse upon yourself?!"

Only then did Medea comprehend his reaction.

Cesare himself carried a curse left by the darkness of creation—a affliction that had driven him to the brink of death repeatedly.

"It's fine. I don't believe anything will happen to me."

Medea recalled the moment she'd witnessed those afterimages alongside the brilliant light—the instant when all pain left by the dragon's blood had completely vanished.

Somehow, she felt absolutely confident everything would be fine.

Cesare, naturally, didn't share her optimism.

"Terence! Come examine her immediately!"

He summoned Terence urgently, not even noticing the blood still trickling from his injured companion's mouth.

"It is... as Her Highness stated. I cannot detect any traces of darkness in her body."

Terence wore a peculiar expression.

"I've read that dragon blood contains dark curses... I'll consult with my Master when we return. But for now, I genuinely don't believe it will cause significant problems."

"Again. Check again."

Terence finally snapped at Cesare's paranoid obsession—which was sacrificing even the joy of their hard-won victory.

Having expended too much magical power, Terence's already dark-circled eyes looked as though he might collapse at any moment.

"Damn it! I've examined her repeatedly! I'm absolutely certain nothing adverse will occur before we reach Valdina!"

Medea extended her hand—

And incredibly, gently squeezed Cesare's trembling fingertips.

When those golden eyes—which never seemed to settle—turned and fixed on her, Medea met his gaze without flinching.

"Cesare."

"..."

"You know it had to be done that way. If we hadn't diverted its attention, the one lying dead down there wouldn't be the dragon—it would be us."

Cesare bit down on the inside of his cheek hard enough to taste blood.

Even after she shattered a perfectly rational man's composure and dragged him through hell, she still looked after him so tenderly.

How could he have known that the rage boiling inside him would cool at the touch of this slender hand extended first?

Meanwhile, Neril's eyes widened at Medea's words.

This wasn't the first time her Princess had sacrificed herself to achieve an objective.

But Medea had never once explained her actions to anyone. She'd never expected understanding.

Yet now—for the first time—she was attempting to make the First Prince of Katzen understand her choice.

While still holding onto his hand, which she'd grasped without conscious thought.

"Cesare."

Medea urged him without realizing she was doing so.

"Everyone survived. That makes it acceptable."

She'd assumed he would understand, given his willingness to take calculated risks for maximum efficiency.

She'd had such expectations of him.

'Since when?'

She paused at that thought, frozen.

Screee! Screee!

A falcon that had been circling overhead suddenly descended toward the expedition team.

"I apologize for interrupting, but we don't have time to waste."

Gallo pulled the parchment tied to the falcon's leg and unrolled it.

"Monster waves are occurring across the entire continent. The Holy Nation has召oned a Continental Council."

"Damn it—they're not even giving us a moment to breathe. What fresh hell do they want from us now?"

D'Angel's eyes blazed with anger as he gripped his greatsword.

Blood from his dragon-inflicted wounds hadn't even dried yet.

Gallo's face hardened as he continued reading, all traces of earlier joy evaporating completely.

"The Emperor of Valdina... has been summoned to stand before the Judgment Table."

Silence fell like a executioner's blade.

Medea's expression didn't change.

Her green eyes simply darkened—becoming deep, fathomless pools.

'So it begins.'

The dragon had been only the first battle.

Now came the real war—one fought not with swords and magic, but with politics, manipulation, and the kind of cruelty that wore holy vestments.

"How long do we have?"

Her voice was steady. Cold. Utterly controlled.

"Three weeks, Your Highness. The Council convenes in the Holy Capital."

Medea looked at each member of her expedition in turn—these people who had risked everything for her impossible plan.

"Then we leave at dawn. There's work to be done."
