Chapter XIV

The False Prophet's
Deliverance

"The chains that bind the devil are forged by those who believe themselves righteous."

Jason leaned closer, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper.

His words carried the weight of revelation—carefully crafted, exquisitely timed.

Paul could still recall the horror of that day: the dragon's eyes snapping open, blazing with malevolent intelligence.

Yes... that was precisely when everything changed. When everyone suddenly began praising Princess Medea and condemning Saintess Rachel!

"Of course. That explains everything."

In the end, people inevitably twist the truth to fit what they desperately wish to believe.

"The Saintess's arm—when it withered in the Mirror Chamber—that was the curse of the vile dragon!"

Paul lurched to his feet, nearly toppling his chair.

"I must return to the Central Temple immediately. They must be told!"

"And what good would that accomplish? Everyone there remains under the dragon's curse."

Jason shook his head slowly and extended a velvet-wrapped bundle toward Paul.

"What matters most is opening your own eyes—freeing yourself from the curse of the evil dragon."

Nestled within the velvet cloth lay a small vial containing a viscous black liquid that seemed to drink in the light.

Paul unstoppered the vial and recoiled immediately, his face contorting in disgust.

"Good heavens—what is this foulness?"

"Dragon's blood. Consume it, and you shall be liberated from the creature's curse."

Jason lowered his eyes, affecting an expression of profound regret.

"Alas, the dragon has already been slain. This is all that remains in my possession."

"Then I shall guard it carefully."

Paul seized the vial and tucked it into his robes with reverent care.

"The most vital thing is to free myself from this curse first."

A fierce determination blazed in his bloodshot eyes—the fervor of a man who has finally discovered validation for everything he believed.

"Summon my carriage!"

After Paul's hasty departure, Jason slipped silently from the tavern.

A bitter wind scraped across his cheeks like a blade.

His gaze shifted toward a shadow-choked alleyway.

"Here."

He extended a cloth-wrapped cup toward the figure concealed in the darkness—the same vessel Paul had drunk from, still bearing traces of his saliva.

"Do you believe it will succeed?"

Jason gave a curt nod.

"I've woven the compulsion deep. There will be no complications. Execute the plan precisely as we discussed—and take particular care to evade the Templar Knights stationed on the pitch-black floor."

Having once counted Rachel among the temple's highest priests, Jason possessed intimate knowledge of the sanctum's most closely guarded secrets.

"Since Your Highness has come this far, why not accompany us yourself?"

Jason pressed his hood lower, his jaw tight.

"I have my own watchers to contend with. I've already provided you with detailed maps of the Central Temple's interior."

A grinding of teeth punctuated his words.

Evading surveillance to reach Sacramontis from Katzen had been no simple feat.

"My involvement in this matter must never come to light. Is that understood?"

The hooded figure inclined his head in acknowledgment.

Pale moonlight spilled into the alley, casting Jason's face in stark relief.

On the other man's cheek, a faint pattern was visible—curved like the horns of a goat.

"We never anticipated such generous assistance from the Grand Duke. It seems we chose our cornerstone for the great work most wisely."

They intend to use me until I'm cornered with nowhere left to turn.

Jason saw through the shallow machination immediately. His lips twisted in disgust.

"Hold your tongue. Were it not for the Templar Knights and the First Prince breathing down my neck, I would put a blade through your throat this instant. You have no time for idle prattle."

With the false Saintess Rachel's exposure, the warlocks who had lurked in the empire's shadows found themselves dragged into the unforgiving light.

Not only had they lost their most promising successor, but the three great powers—the Holy See, Katzen, and Valdina—had jointly issued warrants for their capture.

"Do not presume to entangle me in your doomed schemes. The debt you owe was paid in full when you used me as your puppet."

Jason's crimson eyes flared with barely contained fury.

"Enough scheming. Complete the task properly. If you squander this opportunity, there will not be another."

The hem of his black cloak melted into the darkness.

Left alone, the warlock let out a low, sardonic chuckle.

Drawing lines now, are we?

"Your Highness has waded far too deep into these waters to pretend otherwise."

The pitch-black floor of the Central Temple.

In this prison devoid of light, footsteps echoed like distant thunder.

Rachel, who had been curled with her head buried between her knees, raised her face at the sound.

"...High Priest Paul?"

Through the haze of her vision, a familiar silhouette materialized beyond the bars.

"High Priest!"

She lunged toward the grate, her fingers wrapping around the iron bars.

"Please—you must believe me! I was deceived! I was used!"

Her voice cracked and splintered. Her delicate hands trembled, knuckles white against the metal.

Yet behind her wide, pleading eyes, calculation churned with cold precision.

I knew Paul would be the most likely to come.

His fanatical belief in her as the true Saintess—combined with his pathological refusal to admit error—made him the perfect mark.

"It's a trap! Everyone has been deceived! In the name of the Goddess—"

A hand shot through the bars.

Fingers seized her collar and wrenched her forward with savage force.

CLANG!

Rachel's skull cracked against the iron grate.

"Spare me the theatrics."

"H-High Priest Paul...?"

Rachel stared up at him in genuine shock.

How could Paul—the man who had worshipped her like a goddess—treat her with such brutality?

Then she saw it.

Crawling across Paul's cheek, a black sigil in the shape of a goat's horn.

"Save your precious acting skills for another audience."

He lifted her face, examining her with cold appraisal.

"What a pity. I should have told you far sooner."

Rachel wiped the blood from her split lip with the back of her hand.

And then she laughed.

The pitiful, wounded-animal expression melted away, replaced by something serpentine and knowing. The transformation sent an involuntary chill down Paul's spine.

"Come to silence me, then? You know I never betrayed you. Even rotting in this cell, I never sold you out."

"And was that loyalty for our sake? Or merely because exposing us would only tighten the noose around your own neck?"

His grip shifted to her throat, lifting her bodily through the gap in the bars.

"Ghhk—! Kkhhh—!"

Rachel's legs kicked uselessly as she clawed at his fingers.

"Since our existence has been exposed, it would be prudent to reduce you to ash and scatter you to the winds..."

Fwsshh.

The strangling grip loosened.

Rachel crumpled to the floor, clutching her bruised throat, gasping for breath.

Simultaneously, dark energy crackled through the air where she had stood.

The iron bars twisted like taffy pulled by invisible hands.

SHRIEK! SHRIEK!

Metal screamed as it was rent apart. A gap opened in the cage—wide enough for a person to slip through.

"Come out. Your legacy still has purpose to serve."

Behind Paul, the temple knights and prison guards lay scattered across the floor.

Their eyes—which had watched Rachel with such vigilant hostility—were now closed, their faces ashen. Black smoke curled from their mouths, their noses, their ears.

Clank. Clank.

The chains that had bound Rachel's ankles fell away, clattering against the stone with an almost musical resonance.

A smile spread across Rachel's face—wide, almost too wide, as though her lips might tear.

Step. Step.

She crossed the threshold of her cage.

"Understand this clearly: this is the final time we will save you."

As he passed her, the warlock spat at her feet—a thick, clouded glob of contempt.

"Of course."

But even that degrading gesture could not diminish the euphoria swelling within her.

"Fear not. Victory shall ultimately be ours."

Her voice had taken on a reverent quality—the same tone she had once used to intone prayers beneath the goddess's altar.

The Chosen One

"The world has not abandoned me."

Medea. You sought to destroy me utterly.

But the world itself will not permit my end.

"Is this not proof that I am the chosen one?"

Ahahahahaha—!

Unhinged laughter echoed through the lightless depths beneath the temple.

And somewhere above, in the halls of the holy, no one heard a thing.

End Chapter XIV
