Chapter 226

The Shattered
Mirror

"When the mask falls, what remains is either truth—or madness."

Now that Raju—the source of all their funding—lay dead upon the scaffold, every catastrophe Birna had ever caused came flooding back through Samon's mind.

"Brother... please don't do this. Please, Samon. Don't abandon me too..."

"Do not call me that!"

"Please... Father, Mother..."

A desperate, pleading note had entered Birna's voice—the sound of someone drowning, grasping for anything that might save her.

"You're all I have left now. Please..."

"Father? Are you referring to the corpse bleeding out over there?"

Samon seized Birna by the collar, hauling her upward until her feet barely touched the ground.

"Ngh—! Ugh—!"

"When I think of all those years I wasted—treating a common cuckoo's chick as my own sister, caring for her, protecting her—"

His voice shook with barely contained rage.

"I could flay the flesh from my own bones and it would not be penance enough. I always wondered where that cheap, seething temper of yours came from. Now I know."

"S-Samon, please—it's me—I'm Claudio—I'm Father's daughter—please—"

The words emerged hoarse and strangled, barely comprehensible. But Samon understood every syllable.

"Speak that name one more time."

His eyes had become something inhuman—more demon than man.

He hurled her away like contaminated refuse.

"Utter that name again, and I will tear your mouth open with my bare hands and strangle you with your own tongue."

Samon made no effort to conceal his hatred.

"Was it not enough that you executed your own father? You had to murder your mother as well?"

"Sa... Samon..."

"But then, where else would such a seed take root? The shamelessness, the desperate clinging—it's all the same rotten blood."

From his perspective, he had every reason to despise her.

Because of Birna's denunciation, he had lost Raju—his most powerful patron. Worse still, when his mother's affair was exposed, she had perished alongside her lover, leaving him an orphan in a single afternoon.

Even when he had believed Birna to be his true sister, Samon had viewed her as nothing more than a troublesome burden—a constant source of problems that dragged him down.

Now? There was no possibility he would show the slightest warmth to a counterfeit sibling.

"Darling, please—you're overexerting yourself."

Mariu stepped forward, her voice gentle with concern.

"You've been through too much today. I'm worried you'll injure yourself again, and that would truly upset me. Come now—lean on me. Yes, that's it..."

She wrapped a supportive arm around Samon, who was gasping for breath through his fury, and guided him away from the square.

Neither of them looked back.

The crowd watched them depart, leaving Birna crumpled and alone.

"Rather harsh, wouldn't you say? After all those years growing up as siblings..."

"Harsh? I would have done the same in his position. Though I do wonder—is Samon Claudio even legitimate? Perhaps he's a fraud as well?"

"Who can say? Everyone who knew the truth is dead now."

The names of Samon and Birna floated through the crowd's chatter, mingling with talk of the messenger's earlier proclamation—Medea's newfound glory.

"So that woman actually reported her own biological father?"

"Well... it seems she didn't know either. But even so—even if she was ignorant of the blood between them—how could she betray someone who had cared for her so completely?"

Every whispered word penetrated the heavy rainfall and reached Birna's ears.

Her senses had become unnaturally acute—sharp enough to catch every sidelong glance, every mocking syllable directed her way.

Dozens of eyes pierced her like needles. She felt flayed. Exposed.

How... how did it come to this?

Birna stood frozen, uncomprehending.

She looked down at her hands.

Ugly hands. Covered in wounds and clotted blood.

This is not Claudio's blood. This is what Raju left me?

Flesh and bone inherited from a commoner. A lowborn wretch who had crawled through the dirt.

She began to scratch at her own skin.

Her entire body itched—as though insects were burrowing beneath the surface.

"Long live Her Highness Medea!"

"Long live Valdina!"

The crowd's jubilant cries echoed through the square, each repetition of Medea's name driving into Birna like a blade.

I thought she was the one stealing my place.

But the truth was far simpler, far more devastating.

From the very beginning, Birna had never been worthy to touch the hem of Medea's gown.

All those years of jealousy. All that simmering hatred. The countless small cruelties she had inflicted upon the Princess—

None of it had mattered. It had all been futile struggle. She had never possessed a choice to begin with.

"Long live Her Highness Medea!"

She has become Katzen's imperial family. Elevated from a powerless princess to royalty of two empires. And now...

Birna bit her lip until copper flooded her mouth.

She had plummeted into the cesspit—and Medea had ascended to heights beyond imagining.

Birna could no longer even lift her gaze to look upon that brilliance. The radiant light Medea now emitted was so blinding that even its shadow could not reach the depths where Birna now dwelt.

Her fingernails dug into her palms until blood welled between her fingers.

"No."

Birna could not accept it. Would not accept it.

It cannot be true. Filthy blood cannot flow through my veins.

Her fingertips began to tremble.

"No... no, I am not... that creature's daughter..."

She stumbled backward, muttering. The words grew louder.

"I AM NOT SOME FILTHY COMMONER!"

Her scream tore through the rain-soaked air.

The crowd flinched, drawing back as though from a rabid animal.

"I am Claudio! I carry the blood of Valdina's nobility! Sacred and pure!"

She clawed at her hair with wild, frantic fingers, tearing at the pink strands until they became a tangled, matted mess.

Drops of blood ran down her face where her nails had scored her scalp—she did not seem to notice.

Cold rain streamed down her cheeks, indistinguishable from tears.

"AAAAAH—!"

Birna lunged forward, scrambling up onto the bloodstained platform.

She stood over the intertwined bodies of Catherine and Raju, staring down at them with hollow, burning eyes.

Then she kicked the corpse.

"Tell me! Say it was a lie! You were trying to frame me, weren't you—you vicious bastards!"

THUD. THUD.

She didn't notice the sticky liquid coating her shoes.

"Speak! Confess! I am the Regent's daughter! I avenged my father! AHAHAHA!"

Wild laughter erupted from her throat.

"How dare you mock Claudio?! I'll destroy you! I'll—AHAHAHAHA—GYAAAAH!"

Her gaze fell upon the blood soaking her feet. The laughter transformed instantly into a shriek of horror.

Birna collapsed, tearing off her shoes and hurling them away as though they were venomous.

Her bare white feet stood in stark, obscene contrast to the dark red mud that covered the scaffold.

"I didn't know... if I had known..."

I wouldn't have done it. I truly didn't know. Mother, please forgive me. I was wrong. Raju—Uncle—please forgive me...

She was sobbing now, great heaving gasps that shook her entire frame.

Then, suddenly, her tear-streaked face lifted. Her expression twisted into something feral.

"Good riddance! AHAHAHA! You thought you could make a fool of Birna Claudio?! Look at me now! THIS IS WHO I AM!"

She kicked the bodies again.

"MOTHER! No—forgive me! Please! PLEASE!"

Birna cycled between weeping and laughing, laughing and weeping—her demeanor shifting so rapidly it seemed two people inhabited the same body.

She stumbled across the wet stone, sometimes barefoot in the filth, sometimes clutching her soaked hair and wailing.

"NOOOOO! I AM CLAUDIO!"

After what seemed an eternity of chaos, she let out one final, piercing scream—and fled the square.

"She's completely... lost her mind."

The crowd gaped in stunned silence as Birna's figure receded into the rain.

"I am a descendant of royalty... I carry precious blood..."

Her screams faded into echoes, swallowed by the storm.

No one followed.

Birna had no family left. No friends. No allies.

She had nothing at all.

The Princess's Palace — The Villa

"I implore Your Highness to accept."

The imperial messenger knelt before Medea, his posture one of elaborate deference.

Upon the crimson cushion in his hands rested a dazzling coronet—diamonds and precious gems catching the light with every slight movement.

"Your Highness possesses the singular ability to calm the tensions between our nations and restore lasting peace. His Imperial Majesty has heard much of Your Highness's sterling reputation and generous spirit. He has been most profoundly impressed—indeed, he has not spared his praise."

The messenger was lavishing Medea with honeyed words, desperate to secure her favor.

"I am certain the Emperor of Valdina will be equally delighted by this arrangement."

In other words, he wished to curry favor with Peleus as well.

His Majesty was most emphatic—I must secure a definitive answer from the Princess.

The reason Perdiccas II had dispatched his envoy bearing such extravagant gifts was, ultimately, to defuse the tensions that had reached a boiling point between the two nations.

"Is it not time for all parties to return to their rightful places? The Emperor of Valdina to his throne here, and our delegation to our beloved homeland?"

Silence.

The Princess gazed down at the coronet and the ornate letter with an expression of perfect indifference—as though the messenger's eager expectations were entirely beneath her notice.
