CHAPTER 378

A New Sun
Rises

"From the ashes of vengeance, hope blooms eternal."

Even though Medea's visit to Valdina hadn't yet been scheduled, Peleus was already preparing a nursery for his niece in the palace.

It had taken three or four months to arrange everything—from cushions to curtains, even the bed frills—all in Beatrice's favorite color: a warm, cheerful carrot-orange.

"Wake up from your dream! Would Her Majesty Medea send her beloved daughter to this distant land? You'd have better luck if she gave you the entire Katzen Empire instead!"

Silence.

"And stop making wooden dolls in the office! Why is there sawdust in the sacred Emperor's chambers?!"

D'Angel threw his hands up in exasperation.

The young Emperor, who'd been carving wooden dolls by hand while missing his younger sister, now whittled adorable toys while waiting for his niece.

"Sissair, go outside and spar with D'Angel. D'Angel, try not to get injured."

In the end, Peleus decided to escape the confrontation entirely.

"You're throwing yourself to the wolves and running away?!"

Sissair shook his head, his face a portrait of betrayal.

Just as Peleus was about to leave, he paused at the door.

Sissair looked at his lord with one last shred of hope—which promptly evaporated.

"Oh, D'Angel—don't forget to bring gifts for Dea and Via!"

These days, Peleus's thoughts revolved solely around his sister and niece.

"The sword and miniature brooch I commissioned from the dwarves have arrived. You must bring them. How precious Via will look wearing them at the coronation!"

"Of course! I'm presenting them to Her Majesty myself—how could I be careless?"

D'Angel's massive hand clapped Sissair's shoulder like a friendly boulder.

"Come on, Sissair! Let's rebuild that withered lily-like body into a sturdy oak!"

A fresh breeze swept through the open window, carrying their voices away.

The Coronation Day

Katzen Capital, Persepolis

The day of the coronation dawned bright and clear.

The newly rebuilt cathedral stood larger and more majestic than ever before.

Brilliant stained glass illuminated the widened corridors and grand entrance—so radiant that not a single shadow could hide.

"Kaaaha!"

A child's bright, infectious laughter filled the air.

The baby princess, cradled in Neriel's arms, played gleefully with Gallo and Tom's pale hair, clutching the strands in both tiny fists.

The young monk—who'd inherited the surnames of Saya and Eratosthenes—would hand her a wooden toy whenever she reached out her chubby hands.

"Beeh..."

"Your Highness, you must rest now."

The baby princess, momentarily distracted, flailed her arms excitedly as Terence whispered an enchantment, sending tiny magical butterflies fluttering around her. Her plump arms and rosy cheeks were utterly adorable.

DONG...

The grand bell tolled, signaling the coronation's commencement.

Those assembled in the cathedral drew a collective breath, welcoming the momentous occasion.

From the Holy Nation came Pope Anastasia and Grand Master Theo. From Valdina arrived D'Angel, Madame Pinatelli, the Imperial Lady-in-Waiting, and the Marquis of Gillifort.

"For the eternal prosperity of the glorious Katzen Empire, I hereby proclaim the ascension of His Imperial Majesty."

The Coronation

At last, Medea stood in the place where emperors were crowned.

Now she was not merely his companion, but the sole representative of the divine on this continent.

"Cesare Dweisler Katzen."

Cesare, clad in black velvet robes with the Katzen Imperial crest gleaming on both shoulders, knelt before her.

"Do you swear to protect the peace and order of this empire? Will you rule justly, never turning a blind eye to the suffering of your subjects?"

He gazed up at Medea.

Though he still held no faith in any deity, he could at least now understand the reverence so many across the continent felt for the goddess.

Slowly, he bowed his head.

Then, with utmost gentleness, he pressed his lips to the back of Medea's hand.

Reverence emanated from every line of his posture—an attitude of perfect submission.

It was far too loyal for someone destined to become master of this vast empire.

Because it was dedicated solely to Medea—not to the goddess, not to the empire.

Cesare raised his head.

"...I swear."

Those clear golden eyes—visible only to Medea—would never change.

Not until his reign ended, his body returned to dust, and the eternal oath he'd made was fulfilled.

A white hand lifted the crown.

Pure light flowed along its golden edges like liquid sunshine.

"With this, a new sun rises over Katzen."

Medea slowly lowered the crown onto Cesare's head.

"LONG LIVE THE EMPEROR!"

A thunderous cheer shook the cathedral to its foundations.

BOOM! BOOM!

Celebratory cannons exploded outside, their vibrations traveling through the marble floor.

The cathedral doors swung wide.

The cheering crowd hurled flower petals and golden confetti, which swirled on the wind and danced through the air like blessing made visible.

After the ceremony, banquets and festivals erupted throughout the palace and across the imperial capital.

Flags hung from every street. Celebratory cannons fired from each gate, multiplying the joy.

As everyone raised their glasses to toast the new empire—

"Where did Their Majesties go?"

"Her Highness the Princess isn't here either!"

The courtiers looked around in confusion.

But the day's true protagonists were nowhere to be found.

While everyone feasted and celebrated, the Emperor they sought stood on a green hilltop.

In his arms, he held the world's smallest—and most precious—treasure.

A golden, sun-warmed breeze swept across the verdant hills.

"Cheche!"

"Stop that."

Cesare tapped Beatrice's tiny nose with mock sternness.

Instantly, the child's face puffed up like rising bread dough.

"Boo..."

"Say 'Papa.' Papa."

Before he could finish—

"Whoa! Cheche!"

Cesare drew a deep breath.

Though her mouth wasn't fully developed for speech yet, the fact that she could somehow pronounce her father's nickname was remarkable.

"Boo! Boo!"

Enraged at her father's audacity in correcting her, Beatrice pummeled his shoulder with her tiny fist.

"No. Only your mother can call me that."

Though he refused firmly, Cesare couldn't resist kissing her plump, soft cheek.

"Let's wait quietly until Mom comes back, yes?"

He raised his head, shifting his gaze.

To Medea, standing on the cliff's edge overlooking the endless horizon.

Medea stood on the sun-drenched clifftop.

The place where she'd once sent her eldest child, Lian.

Where warm starlight shone and cool sea breezes blew.

"Lian."

A peaceful azure horizon greeted her.

White foam from the emerald sea crashed gently against the cliffs below.

"...Mom took too long, didn't she?"

The faded crown fragment slipped from her fingers, tumbling down the cliff face.

Final Farewell

She knew there would always be a place in her heart for them.

But now it was truly time to let them go.

"Sleep peacefully now."

A soft breeze caressed her cheeks as though offering comfort.

"And if someday—even years from now—you think of your mother..."

Her voice dispersed gently, mingling with the sound of waves.

"Just once more. Even in my dreams.
