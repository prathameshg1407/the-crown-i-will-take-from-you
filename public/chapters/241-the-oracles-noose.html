Chapter 241

The Oracle's
Noose

"Even if God and the world conspire against me... I will not yield."

At that moment

The Imperial Palace of Valdina

The porcelain cup shattered against marble, its sharp crack slicing through the heavy silence of the throne room.

"What is this madness about an evil dragon?"

Peleus's voice thundered through the chamber. Like Cesare, who commanded an extensive network of informants throughout the Holy Kingdom through his Facade operatives, Medea maintained her own intelligence apparatus—the Red Fox, with its headquarters embedded deep within enemy territory.

She had intercepted the oracle before the temple's messengers could even set foot on Valdinan soil.

"Dea, you mustn't give this nonsense a second thought. We'll fortify the walls. Triple the guard. No one will breach our defenses—"

But his words rang hollow, betrayed by the porcelain shards scattered at his feet—evidence of the composure he could not maintain.

"Brother..."

Peleus dragged a trembling hand across his face, unable to meet her eyes. He who had never faltered before any enemy now seemed consumed by a rising tide of despair.

Yes. That strange unease that had haunted him for weeks. The nightmare that had torn him from sleep in the darkest hours of the morning.

The Recurring Dream

"Mama, Papa, where's Peleus? Please... don't leave me alone. Take me too. Please?"

Little Medea, barely old enough to understand, weeping amid the flames.

In the dream, he had struggled desperately to reach his crying sister, but with every step forward, she drifted further away—her sobs echoing through an endless void until—

"Medea! No—!"

He always woke before he could save her.

The devastation that followed was so visceral, so complete, that he could never fall back asleep. Instead, he would rise in the dead of night and make his way to the palace, compelled by an inexplicable need to confirm that his sister still breathed.

And tonight, of all nights, Kensington had delivered this news.

Perhaps it had all been a harbinger of this moment.

"Dea. Listen to me."

Peleus gripped her shoulders, his blue eyes blazing with desperate intensity.

"You are safe. Your brother is here. I will protect you with my life. No one—no one—will lay a finger on you. Do you understand? Dea, you—"

He was oscillating between nightmare and reality, his legendary composure crumbling for the first time in his life.

"Your Majesty..."

Even Sissair could not hide his turmoil. He understood, perhaps better than anyone, why Peleus—who had always placed duty and responsibility above all else—was losing his reason for the first time.

Quietly, Sissair tucked his hands into his sleeves. To hide the trembling. To conceal how his fingertips had gone white from clenching.

"The Bride of the Evil Dragon? That doesn't even make sense! She'd be killed!"

D'Angel was more agitated than any of them, practically vibrating with fury.

"Your Majesty! The moment those temple dogs set foot in Valdina, I'll cut them down myself. How dare they—these damned—!"

Before he could storm out, Medea caught his arm with a subtle gesture.

The Divine Proclamation

"...Is it truly an oracle?"

Medea's voice remained steady, her composure unbroken—outwardly no different from usual.

"Yes, Your Highness. Dozens of high-ranking priests witnessed the new inscription appear upon the sacred stele."

"Let the Emperor unite with the Holy Maiden and restore the world's rightful order. This sacred covenant must never be defied. Those who deny the flow of divine will shall perish in mortal darkness."

Medea, who had maintained her iron composure throughout, suddenly swayed. Her hand shot out to grip the edge of the table—the only thing keeping her upright.

"Dea!"

"Your Highness!"

She pushed away Peleus and Sissair's reaching hands, fighting to catch her breath.

The moment she read the oracle, her vision had gone black.

The trauma she had buried deepest—the memory she could never bear to revisit—was rising to swallow her whole.

"The Bride of the Evil Dragon..."

This was the evil dragon she had slain alongside Jason's expedition in her previous life.

Medea's hands tensed against the table, tendons standing out like cords beneath her pale skin.

'The world itself won't leave me in peace.'

She swallowed hard, forcing down the bile rising in her throat.

After completing her revenge against House Claudio, her next targets had been Jason and Rachel. But the moment she had turned her attention toward the Saintess, this oracle had descended.

Was this the will of the goddess? Removing her as a threat to protect her precious chosen one?

Medea's teeth ground together.

"Kensington. How far has the oracle spread?"

"We received word ahead of the official announcement, Your Highness. But by now, messengers have surely been dispatched from the central temple to every nation on the continent."

Within days, a herald would arrive in Valdina.

In the suffocating silence that followed, a heavy voice cut through like a blade.

"D'Angel. Go to the border."

Peleus's tone had turned cold. Deadly.

"The herald of the Holy Kingdom has never arrived in this land—and never will."

"He's a messenger bearing a divine oracle. Peleus, do you understand what it means to eliminate him?"

Medea seized her brother's arm, reading his intent immediately.

"If this confrontation is unavoidable, it's wiser to wait and observe. We cannot afford to act rashly—"

"Dea. Are you asking me to surrender you?"

For the first time in her memory, Peleus did not yield to her counsel. He stood immovable, blocking her path.

Peleus knew this instinctively.

"Sacrifice yourself for this country? The way you've always done?"

An oracle was absolute. If he allowed himself to be swept up in the doctrine of divine command, he would be forced to surrender his only sister to the dragon without lifting a finger to stop it.

His jaw clenched so hard the muscles jumped beneath his skin.

Unconsciously, his hand dropped to his hip—to the place where his greatsword would have rested on the battlefield.

"Even if it means returning to that terrible war once more—"

He would not back down.

"I am the Emperor of this nation. I will never accept this oracle."

His blue eyes blazed, unwavering.

"Dea, you must return to the palace. Leave this matter to your brother."

"...Peleus."

"I said go back!"

This time, he would not listen to his beloved sister.

Peleus ordered Neril and Kensington to escort Medea from the throne room, his tone brooking no argument.

Driven back to her chambers, Medea curled her fingers inward until her nails bit into her palms.

It wasn't the first time she had felt trapped in a maze with no exit. The sensation of walls closing in from every direction, of ropes tightening around her throat.

"Your Highness. Forgive me, but this is not the time for stillness."

After a moment's hesitation, Kensington leaned close and lowered his voice.

"According to our sources, the Katzen Empire's spy embedded within the central temple has also conveyed this intelligence to their Emperor."

Medea's eyes sharpened.

"Upon receiving the news, Perdiccas II immediately summoned a special envoy and ordered his border garrison to prepare for deployment to Valdina."

She went very still.

"I believe he intends to claim territory in exchange for supporting His Majesty against the oracle... Your Highness?"

Kensington trailed off, startled by the sudden blaze in the Princess's rust-colored eyes.

They burned like twin flames of pure, concentrated fury.

"He doesn't want territory."

Medea's fists clenched at her sides.

She understood immediately what Perdiccas II was truly after.

"He wants the Philosopher's Stone. My uncle promised him the throne if he delivered it. That bargain still stands."

The Philosopher's Stone—Valdina's most sacred national treasure, the artifact that protected their very borders.

In my previous life, the Emperor shattered the Stone I stole and buried the fragments along the Katzen border.

Perdiccas II had never abandoned his obsession with acquiring it.

He would demand the Stone as the price for supporting Valdina against the oracle. And Peleus—desperate to protect her—would surrender their nation's most precious treasure without hesitation.

Valdina would lose the Philosopher's Stone.
And history would repeat itself.

I cannot allow that to happen.

A cold, mirthless smile curved Medea's lips.

'There will always be time to repay your kindness, Uncle.'

'Yes. What's the difference between then and now?'

She could not afford to let emotion cloud her judgment.

Now, more than ever, she needed to think clearly.

She had to step back. To see the entire board.

Only then could she escape this trap without being swept away by circumstances beyond her control.

Her eyes kindled with renewed determination.

Medea looked down at the oracle once more.

"Your Highness. If you command it, I will accompany you into exile."

Her courtiers could no longer conceal their distress. They stood guard around her like wolves protecting their young.

"The Red Fox's network spans the entire continent. We can disappear. They would never find us."

"Escape..."

Medea's gaze drifted toward the window.

No. That is not the answer.

They could never outrun the stigma of defying a divine oracle. The shame would pursue them to the ends of the earth.

Defiance

Medea lifted her head.

There was no fear of the oracle in her face.

Her rust-colored eyes blazed like flames that could never be extinguished. Her pale features reflected a will of iron—one that would never bend, never break.

So the goddess truly intends to stop me from pursuing Rachel.

'If it is your divine will that I be destroyed in this second life—'

I refuse.

Even if you loop a noose around my neck and drag me before your throne, I will struggle until my final breath.

"Bring me a map."

Even if God and the world itself conspired to deny her vengeance, Medea would not surrender.

"I am not running away!"

The map of the continent unfurled before her eyes.
