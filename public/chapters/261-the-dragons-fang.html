Chapter 261

The Dragon's
Fang

"What could prove death more clearly than this?"

Beneath the fluttering hem of his black cloak, dark crimson stained the fabric—the lifeblood of the fallen evil dragon.

Fresh gore still dripped from his blade, each droplet striking the marble floor with a wet echo. He moved as though forged from the dragon's own blood, every step leaving behind a viscous crimson trail that resonated through the silent hall.

"The First Prince... His Highness the First Prince?"

Count Montfort rubbed his eyes in disbelief. As the Empress's father and grandfather to the Third Prince, he had spent years positioning himself as Cesare's most formidable political adversary.

'He should be suppressing the monster waves at the border. Why in God's name is he here?'

Surely his aging eyes were deceiving him.

"If you possess eyes, then use them properly."

Cesare's sword arced gracefully through the air, flicking droplets of dragon blood across the empty space before him. The severed fang of the Princess of Valdina fit perfectly into the gore-slicked evidence he presented.

"The First Prince of Katzen graciously accompanied me on this diplomatic mission. I trust this will dispel any lingering doubts."

When Medea's voice rang out, a collective breath of relief rippled through the assembled crowd.

The Princess of Valdina had not come alone.

"To have that Blood Conqueror at her side..."

"He's cheated death more times than any man alive!"

This was the prince who had carved victory from impossible odds time and again, crossing battlefields like a demon incarnate. With such a legend standing beside her, the tale of slaying an evil dragon suddenly seemed far less fantastical.

Medea observed the whispers spreading like wildfire through the assembly.

'I anticipated they would be more willing to believe with him present...'

A silent, bitter smile played at the corners of her lips.

Cesare's brutal reputation—that very quality which had once inspired horror and revulsion—now served as their badge of credibility.

"Preposterous! No mortal could fell a demonic dragon through strength alone!"

Yet even as doubts surfaced, they were swiftly crushed.

"If you remain unconvinced, perhaps confronting death with your eyes wide open would prove more illuminating. I'm happy to assist."

"I—!"

His blade sang through the air, slicing cleanly through the dragon's preserved flesh.

Blood and viscera sprayed across the polished floor. The King of Molicia, screaming, collapsed to his knees as the gruesome evidence splattered against his robes.

"N-no, I—"

Comprehension dawned a heartbeat too late.

This man, like the Emperor of Valdina before him, held no regard for morality or justification. Continental order, the sanctity of thrones—such things meant nothing to him.

One careless word, and any of them could become the next crimson stain on his blade.

The assembly fell into uneasy silence.

A faint smile flickered in those brilliant golden eyes, vanishing as quickly as it had appeared.

They feared Cesare for his strength, for the blood that followed in his wake.

But they failed to recognize the true danger.

The naked blade before them was merely a distraction. The invisible sword—the one that would carve their fates without warning—stood silently at the center of the round table.

The architect behind the veil. The one who had orchestrated every move to bring them to this moment.

"As you all know, the evil dragon represents the final remnant of primordial darkness—a curse left upon this earth since the beginning of time."

Her calm voice drifted through the frigid air of the Great Hall like morning mist.

CRACK!

Medea drove the dragon's fang straight into the center of the continental map.

WHOOSH!

A violent gust erupted from the point of impact.

No—not merely wind. A maelstrom of dark energy spiraled outward as the fang's malevolent power was absorbed into the enchanted parchment. The force was so tremendous that cracks spiderwebbed through the marble beneath the round table.

"Dear God—the darkness contained within the dragon is overwhelming!"

"Look! The demons are retreating!"

The black markers on the map—each representing a demonic horde—scattered in frantic disarray. Unable to withstand the dragon's superior magic, they fled in terror.

"The waves are collapsing!"

Dark blood from the fang seeped across the map's surface, pressing down upon the lesser demons with overwhelming force.

"While I transported the evil dragon's remains here, His Highness the First Prince traversed the entire continent at my request."

Medea had devised a method to close every monster wave simultaneously.

The key lay in the hierarchical nature of demonic creatures—predator and prey relationships that persisted even among monsters.

She had first glimpsed this truth when she witnessed the wyvern fleeing from the evil dragon, abandoning the Dark Forest for the Swamp of Gluttony.

"KREEEEAAAAH!"

"GRAAAOOOOH!"

Bestial roars erupted beyond the temple walls.

As the evil dragon's presence pressed down upon them through the enchanted map, the demonic beasts found themselves with nowhere to flee. In their terror, they began retreating into the very epicenters from which they had emerged—the dimensional rifts that served as both cradle and grave.

One by one, the wave epicenters across the continent sealed themselves shut.

And then—stillness.

A profound silence descended, as though the monstrous tide that had threatened to consume the world had simply... receded.

"This... this cannot be..."

No one could find words. They stood frozen, struggling to comprehend the phenomenon unfolding before their eyes.

Then, slowly:

"Merciful Goddess..."

"The catastrophe has ended! The divine wrath has ceased!"

The rulers of the smaller kingdoms erupted in a mixture of fear and overwhelming emotion. Though not everyone present shared in the celebration.

'Damn it all. This cannot be happening. His Majesty was counting on this...'

Count Montfort of Katzen trembled with barely suppressed fury. Beside him, High Priest Paul's fists clenched white at his sides.

"It is far too early for celebration. These are merely symbols on a map. Without physical verification—"

"Your Holiness. I, Leonor, Commander of the Knights Templar, have returned with my report."

The Great Hall's doors swung open, and a powerful voice cut through the tension.

Commander Leonor strode forward, his Templar Knights kneeling in formation behind him. Their appearance was far from dignified—their pristine white robes now coated in dust and grime, their bodies clearly exhausted from their pursuit of Medea.

"Perfect timing! Report at once—tell us what you witnessed!"

"..."

"Speak! Reveal the truth you observed with your own eyes!"

"The waves have completely vanished. Every last one, including those in the Holy Land itself."

Leonor's response came without acknowledging High Priest Paul's ashen expression.

"That is the unvarnished truth the High Priest seeks."

Despite his evident fatigue, Leonor's eyes blazed with conviction.

He and his knights had witnessed the impossible with their own eyes—hordes of demons hurling themselves back into the epicenters, desperate to escape the evil dragon's gaze as it hung from Locke's talons overhead.

Having seen such a miracle firsthand, how could they not be moved?

"Princess of Valdina. First Prince of Katzen."

Pope Benedict's voice drew all attention back to the matter at hand. His question, delivered with the full weight of his authority as head of both the ancient priesthood and the Holy Kingdom, redirected focus from the monster waves to the oracle itself.

"Is the evil dragon truly dead?"

"Your Holiness, what could possibly prove death more clearly than this?"

Cesare gestured toward the dragon's severed head and bloodied fangs, his arrogant laugh echoing through the hall.

The Pope met those brilliant golden eyes and found himself transported six years into the past—to the moment when this same prince had pressed a blade against his throat, demanding a cure for his curse.

'Perhaps it was inevitable. Having conquered the Curse of Origins, who else could slay a dragon born from that same primordial darkness?'

The feat was as miraculous as it was fitting.

Yet the Princess of Valdina presented a different puzzle entirely.

She had been destined for sacrifice according to the oracle—bound to that fate without the protection of any chosen deity.

"And yet you have walked the path of a prophet."

The Pope's aged eyes fixed upon Medea with an expression that mingled sorrow and something approaching admiration.

"Since the role assigned to you by the oracle has been fulfilled through the dragon's destruction, the fate demanded by that prophecy has lost its meaning."

His aged fingers pressed together in contemplation.

"Medea, Princess of Valdina. I hereby release you from the burden of the oracle."

The words hung in the air—a decree from the highest religious authority in the realm.

Peleus released a breath he hadn't realized he'd been holding.

The Agemas exchanged glances, no longer able to suppress the joy that threatened to overwhelm them. Every hardship they had endured, every impossible battle they had fought on this harrowing expedition—it had all been for this single moment.

They would have repeated that grueling journey a hundred times over for such an outcome.

But before the assembly could fully absorb the Pope's pronouncement—

"Your Holiness, I must respectfully object."

A melodious voice, elegant and perfectly measured, cut through the moment of triumph.

"To declare that fate has lost its meaning... is that not a dangerous precedent? Surely the will of the Goddess should be honored precisely as it was written."

The Saintess stepped forward, her serene smile never wavering.
