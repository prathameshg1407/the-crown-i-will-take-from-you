The
Storm

"The aftermath of Quiggin's death was not silence. It was a storm."

The aftermath of Quiggin's death was not silence.

It was a storm.

Royal investigators descended on her estates like carrion birds. Hidden vaults were cracked open. Ledgers surfaced—some burned, some half-submerged in oil, some meticulously coded.

The theft had lasted years.

Then one name appeared again and again.

Black Eyes.

The Royal Family's "official supplier."

→ Invoices inflated threefold.

→ Emergency funds rerouted.

→ Goods paid for but never delivered.

The excess vanished—split neatly between the Head Maid and a shadow network of intermediaries.

When the Royal Knights raided Black Eyes' warehouses, they found them abandoned.

The owners had fled.

The ledgers were gone.

But the damage was already done.

The Regent's Office

"Damn it!"

Joaquin Claudio slammed his fist into the desk hard enough to rattle the inkstones.

"We lost everything? The ledgers, the routes—everything?"

"The Queen Mother authorized raids on twenty ministries. The orders carried the King's Seal. No one dared stall."

Catherine said, pacing.

"And Etienne? That useless toad is the Minister of the Court. Where is he?"

"Hiding. Someone crippled him in the chapel courtyard. He believes it was an assassination attempt. He won't leave his estate."

Catherine admitted through clenched teeth.

Joaquin cursed viciously.

Without Etienne to obstruct proceedings, the Queen Mother had nationalized the supply contracts overnight.

Black Eyes was finished.

And with it—

—the Regent's private treasury.

"I needed that gold. The rebels are already demanding payment. I used the last installment to buy back those cursed jewels to preserve your reputation."

"Do not pin this on me! How was I to know Quiggin would lose control? Or that your mother would suddenly wake up?"

Silence fell.

Medea—the useless puppet—was still alive.

And now, the Regent was bleeding.

The Queen Mother's Palace

Joaquin straightened his coat.

"We must see my mother. Immediately. We need a new Head Maid—someone loyal."

The audience was granted.

The reception was ice-cold.

"A new Head Maid? The palace is still bleeding from the last one you endorsed."

The Queen Mother asked without looking up from her tea.

"Mother, I admit my failure in trusting her. But these are large matters. You should have consulted me before raiding the ministries."

The Queen Mother looked up at last.

"How long should I have waited? Until she sold the roof over my head?"

"Mother, please. We only wish to help. If disorder spreads, the Princess's honor—"

Catherine cut in, voice trembling with manufactured sincerity.

" Honor? "

The Queen Mother set her teacup down.

It rang like a verdict.

"Catherine, you called Quiggin your childhood friend. Are you telling me you knew nothing of her character for ten years?"

"I was deceived! She hid her poison well! I am as shocked as you are!"

"Yes. You must have been very busy."

Catherine stiffened.

"Too busy to notice your friend was stealing from the Crown.

Too busy to notice the Royal Physician refused to treat a dying Princess—because your daughter had a paper cut."

Catherine's breath caught.

"And now, you will leave."

"Leave? Leave where?"

"The Cottage House. Pack your belongings. You will return to the Duke's estate."

The room went silent.

For years, the Cottage House—luxurious, central, coveted—had been their proof of power. A residence within the palace walls was not hospitality.

It was status.

"Mother, are you evicting us?"

Joaquin said slowly, a dangerous edge beneath the politeness.

"Collateral relatives should not reside in the Royal Palace. I allowed it because I was lonely. My leniency has invited contempt."

"But Mother! We stay to protect you—"

"Is it protection,
or proximity?"

She looked at them.

Really looked.

Not as family.

As predators measuring distance.

"Go. My head aches."

They left with faces carved from fury.

Afterward

Pinatelli poured fresh tea.

"Are you well, Your Majesty?"

"Hyenas. They circle anything that weakens."

She closed her eyes.

"I have been in the fog too long."

Then—

"Pinatelli."

"Yes, Your Majesty."

"There is no one else I trust. Take the position."

Pinatelli froze.

"Your Majesty?"

"Become Royal Head Maid. Manage this house. Protect what remains."

"I swore to serve you personally—"

"And I swore to protect this family. For Valdina's sake, God will forgive us both."

The Queen Mother interrupted, gripping her hand.

Pinatelli looked into her eyes.

She saw fear there.

Not of enemies.

Of her own son.

"I accept."

She said softly.

"Good. Clean house, Pinatelli."

Before the wolves finish their meal.
