Hierarchy & Power

The Wolf
& The Dog

"If you raise a dog in a wolf's den... will the dog understand when it is told to bark?"

"If the teacher doesn't suit you, we will replace her."

The Queen Mother silenced Birna with a flick of her fingers, cutting off the complaint before it could fester. Her gaze never left Medea.

"And you, for the only Princess of this country… you dress far too simply."

The pale blue dress. The single string of pearls. Elegant, but restrained. Almost ascetic.

She has no mother to guide her, the Queen Mother realized with a sharp pang.

And I allowed that neglect.

"Pinatelli. The box."

Pinatelli stepped forward and placed a large velvet chest on the table. When she opened it, the room inhaled.

Rubies like frozen blood.

Emeralds deep as ancient forests.

A diamond tiara that caught the light like a crown stolen from the sun.

"My personal collection. Choose one."

It was an apology.

And a test.

Medea did not gasp. She did not reach.

She studied the Queen Mother instead.

For a brief, uncomfortable moment, the old woman felt seen.

Does she think I'm buying forgiveness?

"Just choose."

The Queen Mother said gruffly.

"Thank you, Grandmother."

Medea's hand hovered.

Birna's chest tightened.

That's the coronation tiara.

That sapphire necklace was promised to me.

Every second Medea delayed felt like theft.

"If you're taking that long, I'll go first!"

Birna lunged forward, shoving Medea aside and snatching the sapphire necklace.

"Look, Grandma! It matches my eyes perfectly!"

Silence.

Then—

BAM.

The Queen Mother slammed her palm onto the table. Cups rattled violently.

Birna flinched.

"This is outrageous."

"H–Grandma?"

"I did not offer it to you. And you do not lay hands on the Princess."

"But she was slow! It's just a—"

"Enough!"

The Queen Mother rose.

"You address her as Your Highness. You are Birna Robin Claudio. She is Medea Valdina."

Her voice cut like steel.

"You are a guest in this palace. She is your host. She outranks you."

Birna's face crumpled.

"A… a guest?"

She whispered.

"How long will you act as if status is inherited by proximity? Where did you learn to shove royalty?"

Tears spilled freely now.

"You're being cruel! I hate you!"

She threw the necklace onto the table and fled.

The doors slammed.

The Queen Mother sank back into her chair, pressing fingers to her temples.

"That child… I let this go on too long."

"Shall I bring her back?"

Pinatelli asked quietly.

"No."

A pause.

"She must learn."

Medea spoke then, gently.

"Grandmother… please don't blame Birna alone."

The Queen Mother looked up.

"We were raised together. What I had, Birna had. Often, what I lacked, she received."

She tilted her head slightly.

" If you raise a dog in a wolf's den and treat it like a wolf…
will the dog understand when it is told to bark? "

"And when the pack looks at it, do they see a wolf? Or a confused dog?"

The truth struck clean and merciless.

The Queen Mother closed her eyes.

She had blurred the lines.

She had let subjects forget they were subjects.

Medea turned back to the chest as if nothing momentous had been said.

"May I choose this?"

She lifted a fan from the corner.

Dark, rippled metal. Purple silk.

Not ornament—Damascus steel.

"A fan? Choose something finer."

"I like this."

Medea's thumb traced the ribs—strong enough to stop a blade.

"Very well."

Medea curtsied and left.

The Queen Mother stared at the empty doorway.

"She is right. They should never have been in the den."

A pause.

"Pinatelli."

"Yes, Your Majesty."

"Summon Count Montega. Call the Royal Relatives to the capital."

Pinatelli's eyes widened.

"The Royal Council?"

"Yes."

The Queen Mother's gaze hardened.

"It is time the wolves remembered who owns the territory."

The Duke's Estate

Birna sobbed uncontrollably.

"A necklace—she yelled at me over a necklace!"

Catherine went pale.

"You ran out? In front of the Queen Mother?"

She hissed.

"She sided with Medea!"

"You idiot!"

Catherine grabbed her daughter's shoulders.

"We are already on thin ice! Evicted, investigated—and you humiliate her to her face?"

"But Medea—"

"Forget Medea!"

Catherine paced, panic bleeding through her composure.

"If the Queen Mother turns fully against us…"

Her voice dropped to a whisper.

"…we are finished."

For the first time, Catherine understood.

Medea was no longer reacting.

She was deciding.
