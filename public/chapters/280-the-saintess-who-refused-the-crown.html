# Chapter 280: The Saintess Who Refused the CrownThe Saintess Who Refused the Crown
Chapter 280

The Saintess Who
Refused the Crown

"Power without strings attached is the greatest power of all."

The question hung in the air like smoke that refused to dissipate.

If the Princess of Valdina was the true Saintess, why had the oracle designated her as a sacrifice?

No matter how insidious black magic might be, forging a divine oracle was an unthinkable sacrilege—a taboo so heinous it defied imagination itself.

Therefore, the High Priests simply could not conceive that Rachel had manipulated the oracle through dark sorcery. Such a thing was beyond their capacity to believe.

"Perhaps... if Your Highness could visit just once a month—ah, no, every two months—or even once every six months..."

The High Priest's voice trailed off pathetically as he grasped at straws.

"In the Holy Kingdom, you have no right to demand anything of my sister in her capacity as Saintess."

Peleus's voice cut through the room like a blade of ice.

"The High Priests know better than anyone that you don't deserve such consideration."

His tone was so frigid it left no room for negotiation. The priests shifted uncomfortably but said nothing, their feet shuffling against the marble floor.

Cesare let out a derisive snort.

The priests' faces flushed crimson at the blatant mockery, but despite their open mouths, not a single excuse emerged. They had none to offer.

"Your Highness, Princess Medea..."

Crushed between the fierce pressure emanating from both the Emperor of Valdina and the First Prince of Katzen, the priests found themselves utterly paralyzed.

What had begun as a negotiation now felt like standing at the edge of a precipice, waiting for an avalanche of imperial wrath to consume them.

Naturally, every eye in the chamber turned toward Princess Medea.

She alone seemed capable of communicating with these formidable men without invoking their fury.

Medea observed the shifting currents of power in the room with calm, analytical silence.

'The Holy Nation has already lost the initiative.'

The catastrophic error of failing to recognize the true Saintess—and worse, nearly offering her as a sacrificial victim—could not be undone by any means.

But.

'We cannot afford to make an enemy of the Holy Nation.'

Despite its tarnished reputation following the warlock infiltration and decades of moral decay, it remained one of the three great powers on the continent.

'I cannot simply discard such a valuable shield.'

Her emerald eyes glittered with calculated intent.

'Besides, Cesare's coronation still awaits.'

Soon after departing the Holy Kingdom for Katzen, the official coronation ceremony would take place—the moment when Cesare would be formally recognized as the First Prince and heir apparent.

It would be the first stage in establishing her presence among the people of Katzen.

'If we delay even by a single day, securing my position in Katzen will become infinitely more difficult.'

She still harbored the desire to finish Rachel with her own hands—to complete her vengeance personally, to watch the light fade from those deceitful eyes.

But she couldn't allow herself to be consumed by bitter emotion. Not now.

'There are still battles ahead. Perdiccas II. Jason. Both must be dealt with in Katzen.'

Therefore, her relationship with the Holy Nation had to flow in a direction that yielded optimal results—emotions set firmly aside.

As Medea's silence stretched longer, the anxiety in the room grew palpable. Priests exchanged nervous glances.

"If I may suggest... why not accept informal courtesies from Her Highness the Princess?"

The High Priest standing beside the Pope ventured carefully.

"Even if Your Highness does not wish to be officially proclaimed as the Saintess, you could still utilize all the authority and influence granted to that sacred office by the Holy Kingdom."

Cesare nearly laughed aloud when he saw the brief spark of interest flash through Medea's eyes before vanishing without a trace.

'Did she orchestrate this from the very beginning?'

A masterful strategy—enjoying maximum benefits while bearing minimum obligations.

"The temple knights could escort Your Highness wherever you travel, and the High Priests could accompany you in all official—"

"I require neither escorts nor attending priests."

Medea's interruption was smooth and absolute.

Cesare recognized immediately that she hadn't relinquished control of the negotiation—this wasn't the only concession she was maneuvering to extract.

"As for escorts in particular, I don't believe yours would prove superior to those I already possess."

She gestured subtly toward the Facade and the Agemas standing at attention behind her—an elite guard that had proven its worth in dragon's blood.

"But surely—"

The High Priests found themselves momentarily speechless.

Unlike Rachel, this Saintess was no mere representative of divine will that could be controlled and manipulated.

She was the sole sister of the Emperor of a newborn empire, and now the wife of the First Prince of the old empire that still held continental hegemony.

Moreover, she had personally slain an evil dragon. This was not a woman who could be bent to the Holy Nation's will through gentle pressure or political maneuvering.

"There is one more matter."

Medea's voice cut through their anxious deliberations.

"By now you understand perfectly well that the priestesses of Shadeia were the true representatives you sought all along."

She turned her penetrating gaze directly upon the Pope.

"Find and protect the remaining Shadeia tribes across the continent. And publicly acknowledge the persecution the Holy Kingdom has inflicted upon them throughout the centuries."

Shadeia? What in the world...?

Many of the ministers tilted their heads in confusion, exchanging bewildered glances.

Only a handful of the more learned priests visibly tensed.

A strange fracture appeared in the atmosphere of the papal audience chamber—a divide between those who knew the truth and those who didn't even know such a truth existed.

"Absolutely not! Shadeia are heretics!"

The shrill objection rang out like a clarion call.

High Priest Paul had shot halfway out of his seat, his face contorted with righteous fury.

"They are heretics who used distorted practices to falsely claim the Goddess's will! They proclaimed themselves priestesses and sowed chaos across the world!"

He had tried to endure it—truly, he had. But this was too much.

For Paul, whose faith was arrogantly intertwined with the Holy State's institutional power, acknowledging the Shadeia was an absolute taboo. It could not—should not—be accepted under any circumstances.

"Why would the Holy Woman acknowledge these false apostles and invite such evil into our midst?"

But Medea remained utterly unmoved.

"If you intend to hide your origins until the very end, then I will not accept this title you're so desperate to bestow upon me."

"Your Highness, please reconsider—"

She looked past the agitated priests directly to the Pope. Her emerald eyes were crystalline and clear, patiently awaiting his answer.

Silence stretched like a taut wire.

How much time passed in that weighted quiet?

"...I will do as you ask."

The Pope swallowed a heavy sigh and nodded slowly.

It was time to unveil the truth—that the Holy Kingdom's origins were far from honorable, and that this shameful secret had been buried for far too long.

'The moment that was always destined to arrive has simply come during my tenure.'

"Your Holiness!"

Paul cried out in horror, his voice breaking.

"Look carefully at what stands before us. Do you not understand what is truly important to this Holy State?"

At the Pope's pointed words, the other High Priests finally drew a sharp collective breath.

They understood at last.

'The moment we lose our true divine representative, the very legitimacy of the Holy Nation we're so desperate to protect will be shaken to its foundations.'

They still needed the existence of a Saintess so desperately that they had no choice but to accept her conditions.

There was no alternative.

A tacit agreement settled over the chamber like morning frost.

It was precisely at that moment of uneasy resolution that—

"Your Holiness..."

The priests standing guard at the entrance to the papal office shifted awkwardly.

Beside them stood a jailer clad in ash-gray armor, his expression troubled.

"What is it?"

The Pope's voice was strained from the already chaotic atmosphere.

"It's... the prisoner Rachel, Your Holiness. She's demanding to see Her Highness the Princess. She claims she has something important to tell Her Highness directly..."

"What?"

The air in the chamber turned oppressive, heavy as storm clouds before lightning strikes.

Ever since the false Saintess's true identity had been exposed, the Holy Nation had subjected Rachel to relentless interrogation and torture, attempting to extract the location of the warlocks' stronghold.

Yet she had remained obstinately silent through it all.

Until now.

For the first time, she had spoken—to request an audience with Medea.

"She says... if we allow her to meet with Her Highness the Princess, she will reveal the warlocks' stronghold..."

"How dare a condemned sinner—one who has deceived the entire Holy Kingdom—make such a presumptuous request?"

The Pope's voice trembled with barely contained fury.

"But Your Holiness, she's offering to tell us where those vile creatures have established their base of operations..."

"We've searched everywhere without finding even a trace. If we finally have a lead and allow it to slip through our fingers..."

No matter how extensively they'd hunted, the warlocks had never been brought to the surface.

"There may not be another opportunity like this, Your Holiness. Her Highness the Princess... please, judge this matter with wisdom."

Though the priest addressed the Pope, everyone present understood the reality.

Only Princess Medea possessed the authority to decide this matter.

'Besides, she's already a condemned criminal awaiting execution. Perhaps on her final journey, she wishes to see Her Highness and repent for her sins.'

'If our Holy Kingdom can eliminate the warlock threat, we might be able to salvage some measure of honor from this disgrace.'

The High Priests gazed at Medea with faces full of desperate hope.

It was not difficult for Medea to recognize the naked anticipation gleaming in their eyes.

Her own eyes turned glacial.

'Those who claim to worship the divine are remarkably greedy.'

First, they'd asked her to assume the duties of a Saintess. Then they'd requested permission to carry out Rachel's execution themselves. And now they wanted her to visit the prisoner as well?

"No. It is impossible."

The Princess's answer to the assembled priests was as short as it was absolute.

The audience chamber fell into stunned silence once more. She hadn't even considered their request—simply rejected it outright.

"But...! Holy Woman, I beg you to reconsider this decision."

"Despite mobilizing all the resources of the Holy Kingdom, we haven't been able to determine the exact location of the warlocks' lair—"

"That is precisely why it's a trap."

Medea's voice was cold and cutting.

"The warlocks have operated in shadow and silence for half a century. Do you truly believe they wouldn't anticipate the possibility of their location being exposed when they inserted Rachel into this central temple?"

Silence.

The priests who had been protesting so fervently found themselves with nothing to say.

"Have you examined her mouth for curses?"

Medea asked with pointed calm.

"...There is a particular curse in black magic that binds the victim's tongue—prohibiting them from revealing certain information on pain of death."

Lenyar, the commander of the Knights Templar, spoke up reluctantly.

"It's possible that Rachel isn't refusing to speak... but rather that she cannot speak."

Medea nodded calmly, as if this merely confirmed what she already knew.

"If I go to see her, she will denounce me as a fraud. She'll claim she's fallen victim to my machinations and produce 'evidence' or stage phenomena designed to shake your faith to its very foundations."

"What could a condemned sinner wearing magical restraints possibly accomplish? I believe Your Highness's concerns are excessive."

"We won't be deceived by her silver tongue again!"

Some priests attempted to object, their voices gaining confidence.

However, Medea refused to lower her guard even slightly.

"Won't you?"

Her gaze swept the chamber and found High Priest Paul among the assembly. Her rust-colored stare lingered on him long enough that others couldn't help but notice.

"You who were deceived by her honeyed words and demanded the oracle's fulfillment?"

It was a direct slap to the face—a calculated blow to the pride of High Priest Paul, who had been at the forefront of protecting Rachel.

'This... this woman... comparing us to heretics...!'

Paul's face flushed crimson like a brilliant sunset, shame burning through him.

Veins bulged across his thick neck.

Yet neither he nor anyone else present dared to refute the Princess's accusation.

'What can we possibly say when we've proven to ourselves just how shallow that faith truly was?'

The Pope, maintaining his composure while others drowned in their shame and humiliation, turned to High Priest Anastasia.

"...Go and examine the prisoner yourself."

"Faith without doubt is more dangerous than heresy."

— Princess Medea of Valdina

After a considerable interval, High Priest Anastasia returned from the dungeons where Rachel was being held.

Every eye in the papal chamber fixed upon her as she entered, searching her expression for answers.

The weight of what she'd discovered hung heavy on her shoulders.

End Chapter 280

"The greatest trap is the one baited with truth."
