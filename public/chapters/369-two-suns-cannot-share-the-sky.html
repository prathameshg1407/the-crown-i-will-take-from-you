Chapter 369

Two Suns Cannot
Share the Sky

"Only when one dies can the other truly shine."

She stood at the head of the First Prince's procession.

Every eye in the cathedral fixed upon her instantly.

The First Princess's diamond tiara glittered above her proudly raised head. Her gown was deepest midnight blue, her expression carved from stone—a mask of solemnity and composure that seemed to cost her everything to maintain.

"She is officiating the coronation in her comatose husband's stead...?"

"A pity, is it not? She is about to be cast aside by the oracle's decree."

Whispers rose and fell like waves throughout the assembly.

The First Princess's presence was so commanding that for a moment, the Saintess herself was forgotten.

Perhaps it is human nature to find another's fall more fascinating than their suffering.

The First Princess hailed from Valdinian royalty. She had known neither limit nor defeat—her trajectory had been one of ceaseless ascent. Her lineage, her authority, her husband who adored her alone—she had been the envy of all.

And so, while some genuinely mourned this sudden reversal of fortune, more than a few felt a secret, shameful relief.

But the mood shifted abruptly.

CRACK.

The armrests of the seats where Valdinian officials sat—those who had overheard the whispered gossip—suddenly splintered and fell away.

Before the echo could fade, Peleus's glacial gaze swept through the crowd like a blade.

Silence descended instantly.

The assembly grew watchful, voices dying in throats.

Even amid the tension, Jason never once looked away from Medea—the spectacle she had become for the gathered nobility.

"There was quite the commotion when the Emperor of Valdina arrived at the palace. They say he attempted to bring the First Princess home, but judging by how the Agema were expelled without so much as setting foot inside... it seems he failed to persuade his sister."

The previous night, Jason's spies had been among the many eyes watching the First Prince's residence.

A hollow laugh caught in his throat.

Oh, how achingly familiar.

Her stubborn devotion to her husband—ignoring even her beloved brother's pleas—remained unchanged across lifetimes.

The only difference was that now, that boundless affection belonged to Cesare.

The knowledge carved itself into his bones.

No matter. Her remaining here serves my purpose.

Had Peleus succeeded in spiriting Medea back to Valdina, the grand magic Jason had prepared for this day could never have been activated.

Reclaiming her would have become... difficult.

Far better this way. Soon enough, he would wash clean the stain of her cold-hearted rejection.

Jason lifted his gaze to the statue of the Goddess standing before the stained glass windows.

He composed his features as though nothing had occurred, but his heart thundered against his ribs.

The moment to reclaim her draws near.

✦ ✦ ✦

BOOM.

The heavy bronze doors swung shut, their resonance filling the cathedral like a death knell.

Katzen's High Priest ascended the podium, wearing the sacred mitre. Yet his robes were pure white—not the cream vestments that signified allegiance to the Holy Kingdom.

To wear the mitre bestowed by the Pope while refusing the cream robe... The old fool declares that even if the Pope acknowledges this union, he himself does not.

A derisive snort escaped Paul.

It matters not. Once Rachel becomes Empress of Katzen, this entire nation falls into our hands. That stubborn relic can be dealt with at leisure.

"The coronation ceremony shall now commence."

The throne where the Emperor should have sat stood empty. Beneath the cascade of colored light from the stained glass above, that absence was painfully apparent.

"For the eternal glory of the Katzen Empire, I proclaim the name that shall inherit the Emperor's mantle."

The High Priest's voice rang through the vaulted space.

"Let the First Prince's wife stand as proxy and speak the oath on his behalf."

Rachel watched as Medea approached the altar, swore the Emperor's sacred vows, and received the anointing of holy oil upon her brow and crown.

Medea performed each ritual without faltering, radiating restrained dignity.

In this moment—standing as the Emperor's representative—she occupied the highest position in the cathedral.

Rachel's fingers clenched, creasing the fabric of her gown. She forced herself to relax.

There cannot be two suns in one sky.

The instant Medea appeared, all the light Rachel possessed seemed to drain away—absorbed into that other woman's radiance.

Only when one dies can the other truly shine.

Her late mother's words had been no exaggeration.

Rachel endured the searing jealousy because she knew: that lofty brilliance would soon plummet to the very depths of the earth.

When the blessing for the Emperor concluded, the moment arrived at last—the fulfillment of the oracle.

"Make way! Stand aside!"

As though his cue had come, Paul strode forward, murmuring arcane phrases beneath his breath.

With a theatrical flourish, he unfurled the second oracle.

The words were inscribed in letters large enough for all to read:

Divine Proclamation

"Thus speaks the Goddess:
Cesare, son of Katzen, shall wed my faithful daughter Rachel
and bring peace unto the land."

Paul's voice rose above the murmuring assembly.

"This is Her divine will. Whosoever defies it shall be judged by Her own hand."

The gathered dignitaries absorbed the oracle's meaning, exchanging glances.

"The First Prince refused, and divine punishment struck him down—just as the oracle foretold."

"At least he has seen reason now."

Suppressing the muttered commentary, the High Priest continued.

"Therefore, Princess Medea shall execute the oracle on His Majesty's behalf, performing the rite that welcomes Saintess Rachel as His Highness's companion."

He turned toward her.

Medea inclined her head without hesitation.

But Rachel noticed—beneath the flowing sleeves, the hands holding the ceremonial plate trembled ever so slightly.

Whisper... whisper...

Rachel rose and began her processional walk.

The rustle of her antique robes brushing the crimson carpet filled the cathedral's silence.

"I give thanks for being called to this sacred place, where I may serve the Goddess's will."

She ascended the platform where Medea stood and bowed her head with practiced grace.

"I am prepared to swear the oath as the Goddess's representative and the First Prince's true companion."

Paul beamed with satisfaction and guided her toward the Oath Stone.

Every eye in the cathedral followed her.

Rachel gazed down at the ancient stone.

There, beside Cesare's name, Medea's was carved deep into the rock.

Soon that name will vanish. And in its place... mine.

Her violet eyes, clouded with vindictive glee, rose to meet Medea's gaze in open defiance.

Did you think I would end like that, Medea?

Look at me.

I stand here, alive, to drag you down from your throne.

Their eyes locked.

Slowly, deliberately, Rachel's lips curved into a smile.

The smile of a victor.

I won.

Mother... I finally did it. I did it.

Can you see her face right now?

Her future. Her husband. Her position—all mine. I have seized her fate and made it my own.

Watch closely as despair fills those arrogant eyes.

If this had not been a coronation, she would have laughed aloud.

It was then—

The corners of Rachel's mouth, still twitching with joy, froze mid-expression.

CRASH!

The massive bronze doors exploded inward.

The impact reverberated through the marble floor like thunder, a detonation that shook the very foundations of the cathedral.

"Wh—what is—?!"

Guests recoiled, heads snapping toward the entrance.

Brilliant white light poured through the shattered doorway.

Silhouetted against that radiance stood the outline of a man.

As eyes adjusted to the blinding glare, his features slowly resolved into focus.

The protagonist of today's coronation.

The First Prince.
