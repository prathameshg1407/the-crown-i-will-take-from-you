Chapter 187

The Edge of Madness

"When hatred burns bright enough, it blinds even the one who carries it."

Jason knew with absolute certainty that dreams and reality were different.

Yet he could not comprehend the inexplicable resentment that surged within him each time he saw Medea wearing this distant, impenetrable mask.

Medea paused.

Her fingertips trembled. Her delicate eyelashes fluttered like butterfly wings caught in a gentle breeze.

'He's had a dream.'

A dream of Jason and Medea together. No—it was more than a dream. It was a truth that once existed.

Somehow, she felt she already knew without needing to hear it spoken aloud.

What does he see in those dreams? What memories does he relive?

"I love you, Medea. I am you, and you are me. We are one body."

At that time, he had seemed like the only savior who could pull her from her lonely reality.

"When I become Emperor, you will stand by my side, and our child will rule the empire."

Medea had liked the future Jason painted for them so much that she hadn't hesitated to give him everything.

"..."

Medea opened her eyes again.

The memories that had been floating through her consciousness shattered mercilessly, dissolving like smoke.

She stared at Jason, her expression unreadable. A strange, unnatural light glimmered in her green eyes.

'Goddess... are you testing me?'

She wondered how long she could continue to compound this interest. How much more suffering could she extract?

"Sometimes, Your Highness renders me powerless with what I have endured for so long."

Her voice was soft, almost a whisper.

"...Sir, are you having that dream too? Can you understand what I feel?"

Jason's face brightened instantly, interpreting her words exactly as he wished to hear them.

"It's a shame about the children. But since you're here, you won't be lonely. That's fortunate."

The face before her shifted, blurred. Emperor Jason's features superimposed themselves over this younger, smoother visage—both versions covered in the same gray pallor of death.

Even those cold, pitiless eyes that had looked down at her as she wept, clutching her daughter's blood-soaked pouch.

"Farewell, Medea. Your deeds will be remembered deeply by me."

Her vision blurred at the edges.

She understood intellectually that she was drifting further from reality, that her grip on reason was weakening—but her heart refused to accept it.

'What are you waiting for? He's right in front of you.'

A dark voice whispered seductively in the back of her mind.

'The opportunity is now.'

In that moment, an overwhelming desire to kill surged through her like wildfire.

The fact that Jason was a member of Katzen's royal family, that this was a Continental Festival attended by ambassadors from every nation—none of it could restrain the burning impulse.

It was as though crimson flames had risen before her eyes, blocking out everything else.

The sword hanging at his waist caught her attention.

Medea extended her hand toward Jason.

'Three steps.'

In her mind, she visualized drawing his sword, calculated the precise trajectory required to seize Jason and drive the blade through his heart.

"Jason..."

"...!"

Hearing his name called, Jason unconsciously stepped toward Medea, joy overflowing in his chest.

'Two steps.'

Only one step remained.

It was the moment when Medea took her final step and reached out her hand—

"Blocking the road would be... unfortunate."

A smooth, fluid motion caught her wrist. A wall of black silk appeared in front of Medea.

"Huh!"

Simultaneously, Jason was hurled backward by an immense force that struck his side. The sudden impact left him dazed, breath knocked from his lungs.

"In this crowded place with so many people around? Really?"

"Cesare—! What do you think you're doing?!"

Jason had no idea how or what had struck him. When his vision cleared, he found Cesare standing over him, his face twisted in fury.

"What am I doing? I'm simply saving my dear brother from embarrassment. Katzen's diplomatic corps is terribly disorganized."

A national disgrace, really.

The tone was friendly, almost cordial—but the slow, deliberate cadence seemed designed specifically to put Jason on edge.

At the same time, Cesare lightly tapped Medea's wrist twice where he held it behind his back, then released it.

"..."

It was a small touch, barely perceptible—but it was enough to bring Medea back to her senses.

She took a step back and drew a shaky breath as Cesare bent down as though to help Jason to his feet.

"Get up—"

"Aren't you the Emperor's hunting dog? Then you should only bark when commanded."

The whisper was low enough that only Jason could hear it.

"Your arrogance reaches the heavens, Cesare. Do you think the entire world bows at your feet? I am the ambassador His Majesty personally appointed—"

"Precisely."

Cesare shrugged elegantly. He leaned down slightly, meeting Jason's eyes at level.

"Let's end this here. If the hunting dog is caught wagging its tail for someone else, won't that be... problematic?"

He nodded subtly behind him.

A chamberlain stood there, watching Jason carefully—a pointed reminder that he remained under the Emperor's constant scrutiny.

"It's none of your concern. Keep your useless advice to yourself."

Jason glared at Cesare, his face deathly pale.

'It was the first time she approached me of her own accord.'

He despised the First Prince for interrupting at such a critical moment, for wasting this precious opportunity.

It felt as though smoke was slipping through his fingers. Jason's eyes burned red with suppressed fury.

"Medea, I—"

"Brother."

The moment Jason spoke her name, Cesare's golden eyes glowed with dark intensity.

"Did what I said sound like advice to you?"

Jason froze at the razor-sharp energy that seemed to brush against his throat—a stark contrast to the faint smile still playing on Cesare's lips. There was not even a trace of amusement in those golden eyes.

"..."

Jason frowned, his brow furrowing deeply.

He felt as though his ribs were being crushed by the oppressive weight of that gaze.

'What have I done to provoke him? Is this the instinct of the weak submitting to the strong?'

"..."

At the same time, his mind conjured an image of Medea standing behind Cesare, watching this humiliation unfold.

He bit his lip and forced himself to speak in a controlled voice.

"Princess. I apologize for my rudeness. Let us continue our conversation another time."

"..."

"Let go. I'll walk on my own two feet."

Roughly shaking off the hand of the First Prince's knight who stood behind him, Jason finally departed.

"..."

'She must be watching this pathetic display.'

His back felt as though it were on fire. Jason clenched his fists so tightly his knuckles went white.

It was the first time he had hated that damned cousin so intensely—for denying him even the slightest chance to save face before the woman who occupied his heart.

But at the same time, Jason felt it deep in his bones, an undeniable truth he could no longer ignore.

The positions he and Medea occupied had shifted drastically over the past three years.

Medea was now the Princess of the Valdina Empire—protected, powerful, untouchable.

And he? He was nothing more than a fallen member of the royal family, living as the Emperor's dog, driven away by a single cold glance from the First Prince.

With every step Jason took, shame and anxiety surged within him. A profound sense of wretchedness gripped him down to his very toes.

Medea watched Jason leave.

From the way he tried to hold himself upright, she could read his emotions clearly.

They were the same shade as the feelings Medea herself had harbored for him in her past life.

Perhaps Jason hadn't been entirely wrong when he claimed they were connected.

'Jason. Now it is your turn to be mine.'

Though no one had intended or desired it, a situation had been created where everyone came to understand their respective positions.

But it wasn't over yet.

'Feel more.'

The moment when love withers and dies. The moment when sacrifice is met with betrayal. The moment when, in the end, there is no one and nothing left by your side.

Just as the flames were about to flare up again in Medea's calm green eyes—

Tap. Tap.

A faint, feather-light touch against her wrist, then it was gone.

"Just as there is weight to an incident, so too is there proper use for a person."

A cool, rich voice whispered softly, close enough to tickle her ear.

"If there are beings as precious as treasure, there are also those for whom even the dust rolling on the floor holds more value."

"What are you talking about—"

The tone of his speech was affectionate, as though he were teaching a young child. Regardless of the gentle form, the content itself was razor-sharp.

"I am informing Your Highness that my brother's life is not so important."

Medea looked up in shock, her eyes widening.

Golden eyes met green, calm and unfathomable.

The message was clear: Do not waste yourself on him.
