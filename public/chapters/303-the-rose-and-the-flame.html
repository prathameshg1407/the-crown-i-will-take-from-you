Chapter 303

The Rose and the Flame

"Jealousy is a fire that consumes reason—and she welcomed the burn."

Medea wondered where she had seen that pendant before, but when she looked up, the boy had already vanished from sight.

That watchful gaze observing them from afar had disappeared as well.

"Tell Kensington to investigate their identities later. I want to know who they are."

"Yes, Your Highness. I'll see to it immediately."

Fortunately, the path the boy had indicated proved correct.

Soon after, the ornate dome of the Winter Palace came into view once more.

The movement of palace attendants became visible again, and the path forward grew familiar.

The two women successfully exited the Eastern Palace without further incident.

A massive black carriage bearing the First Prince's coat of arms stood waiting on the verdant grass to greet them.

"Zeta!"

Saya ran toward him with an expression of relief.

"Why did it take so long? I thought something had happened. I was about to storm the Eastern Palace again."

Zeta frowned with uncharacteristic severity.

"It's my fault. I'll explain everything when we return. Nothing serious occurred."

Saya started to mention they'd nearly encountered the Emperor, but noticing the curious onlookers gathering nearby, she quickly fell silent.

"The roses in the Eastern Palace were quite lovely, so we delayed slightly. I apologize for causing concern."

"No, Your Highness. Actually, I was worried..."

Zeta scratched his head as though wanting to say more, but soon thought better of it and closed his mouth.

"Why didn't you escort me to the greenhouse yourself?"

Certainly, if Zeta had been present, there would have been no misdirection through the Imperial Palace's labyrinthine corridors.

However, while his combat skills were flawless, he wasn't as finely attuned as Saya to the subtle battles of nerves and psychological warfare between courtiers.

'If it had been Zeta or Neryl who accompanied me, the moment they saw the seat prepared by the Eastern Empress, they would have drawn their swords without hesitation.'

The mental image made Medea smile faintly.

"No, that's not why I—"

Medea didn't register Zeta's unusually flustered tone.

"Hm?"

Suddenly, her waist was seized.

Her body lifted effortlessly into the air. The familiar scent of musk filled her senses, followed by the dizzying sensation of floating.

Someone had lifted her up and pulled her into an embrace.

With his measured steps, the hem of Medea's dress swayed gently, as though carried by a spring breeze.

"I'm here to collect you, Dea."

Glossy black hair. Eyes brilliant as molten gold.

The First Prince, looking even more devastatingly beautiful today, stood before her.

"Forgive me, Your Highness. I informed my lord of the situation. When you didn't emerge, I worried something might have happened inside..."

Zeta bowed his head apologetically, as was his nature.

But Medea understood the timeline better than that.

The time she'd spent alone with the Eastern Empress in the glass greenhouse had been relatively brief.

In other words, it was far too soon for Cesare to have received Zeta's message and arrived from wherever he'd been.

"You were planning to come from the beginning, weren't you?"

She should have known when he'd so suddenly offered to escort her home that morning.

Medea glanced at him sidelong, and Cesare kissed her cheek as though confirming she'd guessed correctly.

"Speed is essential in... let's call it crisis management. I was on standby, ready to intervene the moment I was called."

'Zeta clicked his tongue internally. There's no one with greater status than the Emperor in this entire city. Does my lord truly believe Her Highness will accept such transparent nonsense?'

"So, what was the result?"

Medea detected a faint note of anticipation in Cesare's softly asked question.

"I didn't kill anyone."

"Ah, that's unfortunate."

"I did slap a princess, though."

Medea easily located Princess Strauss among the astonished faces peering over Cesare's shoulder.

"It seems she's admired you for quite some time."

She'd managed to catch that particular piece of gossip whispered behind fans at the tea party.

Actually, she'd heard everything—every rumor, every speculation.

Princess Strauss had apparently pursued the First Prince even after his marriage.

It was also said that the Marquis de Lennyar—head of the First Prince's maternal family and Cesare's grandfather—had once proposed a marriage alliance between them.

"So?"

'What does that have to do with me?'

Cesare looked genuinely baffled, as though he'd been informed he'd sprouted horns.

"I don't even know who that is."

"Is it because there are so many you can't narrow it down to just one?"

Medea's voice carried an edge of disbelief.

At today's tea party, she'd learned about every young noblewoman who'd ever been mentioned as a potential match for the First Prince.

"...Are you jealous?"

"Absolutely not."

Medea snorted as though the very suggestion were absurd.

Yet despite her words, her eyes weren't focused on Cesare—they were locked on Princess Strauss.

The Princess's almond-shaped eyes were wide as she gazed at Cesare with undisguised emotion.

Far more intense than when Medea had struck her earlier.

That gaze overflowed with affection and longing for Cesare.

Medea's body went rigid.

'I... I can't look at him like that.'

At least when it came to expressions of pure love, Medea realized that Princess Strauss far surpassed her.

She had never felt this emotion before in her entire life, but the moment she recognized it, a subtle sense of inferiority crept into her heart.

Lost innocence. Naivety that doesn't question affection. Longing directed solely at one person without calculation.

'Things that no longer exist within me are still alive and thriving in her.'

Without realizing it, Medea tightened her grip on Cesare's arm.

"Dea?"

The face of the most dazzling man in this splendid imperial palace came into sharp focus.

Dark eyebrows drawn together in a slight frown. A straight, aristocratic nose. Golden eyes that carefully read her expression, examining every detail.

Veins stood out on the back of his elegant hand—as though proving his claim to her.

Lips that spoke her name like a benediction. The firm arm she held now. The hot pulse flowing beneath his skin.

'I won't let you be taken away.'

Deep within Medea's heart, a fierce possessiveness awakened—foreign even to herself.

She'd thought everything had been burned to ashes in her previous life, yet somehow this ember still smoldered within her.

"Dea, what's wrong?"

Cesare noticed her gaze fixed on something over his shoulder.

As he started to turn his head, Medea seized his face between her palms and held him steady.

"It's nothing."

In front of Princess Strauss—in fact, before everyone watching—she felt a naive, childish desire to demonstrate exactly whom this man belonged to.

Even though she was vaguely aware this wasn't like her usual self, and that she would likely regret this moment later, Medea reached up.

Cesare, like an obedient hound following his master's command, reveled in this strange transformation in his wife.

A small white hand brushed through his hair.

"A parting gift."

Even as his wife tucked a cluster of deep red roses behind his ear, he bent down to maintain eye contact with her.

"...Which is the gift—the flower, or you?"

The beautiful man who blended seamlessly with the dark rose asked with playful mischief dancing in his eyes.

"..."

When Medea pressed her lips together and said nothing, he burst into laughter.

What had happened to his wife, who always stood outside emotional boundaries, to make her take such a bold step?

Whatever had occurred in the Eastern Palace to serve as catalyst, Cesare was simply grateful.

Having no intention of rejecting the affection he'd been waiting for, he immediately captured her small hand and pressed his lips to her palm.

"The Eastern Empress doesn't host tea parties regularly?"

'If not, I'll petition the Emperor to make it mandatory.'

"Shall I recall my brothers from their posts? There are too few members of the royal family remaining in the palace right now for daily tea parties."

White fingers pinched his arm in warning.

As though he hadn't expected a response anyway, Cesare embraced her tightly once more and lifted her effortlessly.

He laughed—a sound of pure delight.

The smile that escaped through his well-shaped mouth was dazzling as it fractured into fragments of sunlight.

Medea surrendered to the impulse of the moment and wrapped her arms around Cesare's neck.

And just as he had done to her, she buried her face against his shoulder.

She didn't even notice that he froze at the sensation of her lips brushing his neck—nor did she see him struggle to suppress the deep blue flame spreading through his chest.

'I thought it would be fine.'

'Even if this intense emotion gripping me now is merely possessive desire for this man...'

'I believe this man I hold so tightly in my arms will gladly remain at my side.'

Meanwhile, at that same hour, in the Western Palace of the Katzen Imperial complex...

"I heard a new High Priest sent by the Holy State has arrived at the cathedral."

The Western Empress received the report from her chief lady-in-waiting.

"What news from Bridget? I sent her a communication some time ago, but I've yet to receive a response."

She looked toward the middle-aged woman standing respectfully to her side.
