Chapter 323

The Blood of
Salvation

"In the moment of crisis, true nature reveals itself—and true opportunity emerges."

How was this possible?

For the imperial hunting competition, every dangerous predator within Serwood Forest had been systematically eliminated. This was no ordinary beast—it was a monster.

Could this truly be coincidence? Or was someone attempting to assassinate the Emperor?

"A Giant Bear... This is a top-tier monster..."

The Captain's voice trembled with grim recognition. A creature of this caliber could annihilate an entire squadron of upper-middle rank knights single-handedly.

Worse still, the Emperor had charged ahead in pursuit of the white stag, putting considerable distance between himself and his main guard contingent.

The remaining knights drew their weapons, tension crackling through the air like lightning before a storm.

"Protect His Majesty!"

The unspoken truth hung heavy between them: if the Emperor fell here, none of them would leave this forest alive.

Yet not everyone viewed this sudden crisis with dread.

This is it. This is my moment. The heavens haven't abandoned me after all!

Juan, who had spent his entire life sheltered within the Western Empress's protective embrace, had never truly faced mortal danger. He could not comprehend the lethal reality of a Giant Bear.

Instead, he saw only opportunity—the dramatic chance he had desperately prayed for, the moment that would finally thaw his father's frozen heart.

"Father! I will protect you!"

Juan sprinted toward the Emperor, nocking an arrow with practiced speed. He drew the bowstring taut, aim steady on the creature's massive chest.

TWANG!

The arrow flew true—and bounced harmlessly off the monster's hide, clattering uselessly to the ground.

"Impossible—!"

The beast didn't even flinch. If anything, the futile attack only amplified its rage.

The Giant Bear's bloodshot eyes blazed with renewed fury.

"Rescue His Majesty first! Hurry—AAAARGH!"

The creature moved with impossible speed. One massive paw swept through the air like a thunderbolt, cleaving the nearest guard clean in half.

Then it pivoted toward Juan.

Chaos erupted.

Knights fell screaming. Horses shrieked as their flanks were torn open. The air filled with the copper stench of blood and the wet sounds of rending flesh.

"Hah... hah..."

Juan staggered backward, splattered with the blood of a knight who had died mere inches from him. The man's sword lay abandoned at his feet, joining the growing carpet of scattered weapons.

The Giant Bear turned its terrible gaze toward Perdiccas II, still prone on the ground where he had fallen.

The Emperor's face drained of all color.

"Nngh..."

"Someone—anyone! His Majesty is in mortal danger! Come quickly!"

Juan frantically searched the corpse of a fallen knight, fingers closing around a red smoke signal. He ignited it with trembling hands—the emergency beacon for the hunting competition's rescue team.

GRAAAAAWR—!

But the beast granted him no reprieve. No hope. Those massive forepaws descended again.

"Juan..."

In mere moments, every guard had been slaughtered. Only Juan and the Emperor remained—and Perdiccas II had shattered his ankle in the fall from his horse. He could not move.

"AAAAAHHH—!"

In that instant, Juan's mind went blank.

The throne. His father. Power. Ambition.

All of it evaporated, leaving only the primal, animal instinct to survive.

"I'M SORRY, FATHER!"

He hurled his quiver to the ground and fled, crashing blindly through the underbrush, screaming for the forest to release him.

"You... you...!"

Perdiccas II watched his son's retreating back as if a boulder had struck him between the eyes.

The Emperor was a man who could betray anyone without hesitation—but who could never tolerate being betrayed himself.

Years of carefully maintained illusions crumbled in that single, devastating moment. What remained was only contempt, bitter and absolute.

Abandoned by his own child before a savage monster. Left to cower like a cornered rat.

The wound carved into his pride would never fully heal.

CRASH!

Rather than pursue its fleeing prey, the Giant Bear turned back to the meal already before it.

Those massive paws rose once more.

When Cesare and Medea finally located the Emperor's party, the scene before them was one of utter devastation.

Juan, fleeing in wild-eyed terror from his own father. Perdiccas II, sprawled helpless on the ground, shame and despair etched into every line of his face.

"FORGIVE ME, FATHER!"

The Third Prince's screamed apology echoed through the trees as he vanished into the undergrowth.

Cesare witnessed it all—the moment of ultimate humiliation painted across his father's features.

And he did not immediately move to help.

"A Giant Bear?"

Cesare's murmur held only mild surprise. No urgency. No fear. Even surrounded by carnage, his composure remained absolute.

Medea also reined in her horse, her mind racing.

A monster? But that's not what I planted. The hallucinogenic herbs and siren stone powder should have merely induced visions of danger...

Her plan had been elegant in its simplicity: trigger the Emperor's paranoia, make him believe his own son meant him harm, then have Cesare appear as savior at the crucial moment.

That salvation would have been branded into his memory like rain after a drought. His perception of Cesare—and of Juan—would have shifted forever.

But this? This was something else entirely.

Someone else also made their move during today's hunt. Who would dare target the Emperor?

Questions could wait. Right now, only one thing mattered.

"AAAAHHH—!"

"SOMEONE HELP!"

The torn screams of terror reverberated through the forest.

The Giant Bear had turned its full attention to the Emperor, closing in for the kill.

Yet the space beside Medea remained empty. Cesare had not moved.

"Cesare."

Medea looked up at him, reading the conflict written in the tension of his grip on the reins.

Years of insults. Years of coldness. Years of rejection.

His heart had dried to ash long ago.

Let him die? Or save him?

The choice hung suspended between them.

"We cannot let him die here."

Medea's voice was steady, pragmatic.

Too many witnesses populated this hunting ground. An"accidental death" would never be believed—and suspicion would inevitably fall upon whoever might benefit from the Emperor's demise.

Cesare still did not move. He watched like a man observing something very far away—detached, almost indifferent.

A bystander to his own father's death.

Medea made her decision.

She snapped Silva's reins, and the white mare surged forward like an arrow loosed from a bow.

Medea positioned herself between the fallen Emperor and the monster's advance.

The Giant Bear's forepaw descended—

—and light exploded across her vision.

Cesare's blade pierced the creature's heart with surgical precision.

In the same motion, he swept Medea into his arms, shielding her body with his own.

To Perdiccas II—terrified, broken, watching through tear-blurred eyes—it appeared that his eldest son had thrown himself into death's path to save his father.

THUD.

The Giant Bear collapsed, its massive body striking the earth with enough force to shake the trees.

Cesare staggered.

In the final instant before the beast fell, its razor claws had grazed his shoulder. Blood welled from the torn flesh, staining his hunting attire crimson.

"Cesare!"

"You saved your father."

Perdiccas II's eyes glistened with an emotion he had not felt in decades.

Instead of the imperial"this Emperor" he had always wielded to maintain hierarchy, different words left his lips:

"Father."

He seized Cesare's hand. Squeezed it with desperate strength.

"This father will never forget what you did today."

Silence.

"Never, do you hear me?"

Still, Cesare offered no response.

Perhaps because he had just saved the man's life, Perdiccas II interpreted even his son's silence as sincerity—the quiet humility of a filial child.

"Your Majesty! Are you unharmed?"

The Imperial Knights who had spotted the smoke signal finally arrived, crashing through the underbrush in a thunder of hooves.

They froze at the sight before them: the Giant Bear, cleaved nearly in two. The Emperor, bloodied but alive. The First Prince, wounded and standing over both.

Not far away, they discovered the Third Prince—unconscious, his clothes stained with blood, drool leaking from the corner of his slack mouth.

Two knights lifted his limp form onto a stretcher.

"You pathetic, worthless coward!"

Perdiccas II's voice cracked like a whip as he glared down at his unconscious son.

"You would flee to save your own miserable hide? Abandon your father to be devoured by beasts? You dare call yourself my son? Do you even consider me your father?!"

Each word dripped with venomous contempt. The memory of that moment—the terror, the abandonment, the shame—festered in his chest like poison.

Perdiccas II's rage knew no bounds.

He spat upon the body of the unconscious Third Prince.

Nearby, Angelique watched the spectacle in silence, her expression unreadable.
