Blood

"The deepest betrayals come from those who share your blood."

The square fell into breathless silence.

The smile on Raju's lips froze, then shattered. His face became a mask of incomprehension—features locked in place as though time itself had stopped.

"You have committed heinous murder upon foreign soil, tarnished the honor of your sovereign and your great nation, and sown discord between two empires. These are not light offenses."

"I hereby decree: five hundred lashes, followed by immediate execution."

"What? What did you say?"

Raju's voice tore from his throat, raw with disbelief.

"No—that cannot be true!"

Confusion and terror tangled in equal measure.

"Execution? You're not taking me to Katzen?"

For a heartbeat, the crowd shared his disbelief. Then understanding broke across them like a wave.

"Execution! Execution!"

"Justice! Justice!"

Cheers erupted. Laughter and gasps of astonishment collided in a cacophony that swelled to fill every corner of the square.

"No! Something is wrong!"

The messenger regarded him with the cold indifference of carved stone, then turned his gaze toward the base of the platform.

Two executioners mounted the stage—massive men, each bearing a whip of thick leather studded with cruel iron protrusions.

"His Majesty would never abandon me! This is madness! A false accusation!"

Desperation cracked through Raju's voice as he stumbled backward, away from the advancing executioners. But the chains around his ankles dragged at every step, and the crowd pressed in from below, sealing every escape.

"This is a mistake! He promised to bring me home! He promised—"

In a final, frantic surge, Raju lunged toward the messenger, bound hands raised like weapons.

"Ah—!"

A boot caught him in the chest. He crashed to the wooden planks, breath driven from his lungs.

CRACK—!

The executioner's whip descended without mercy, carving a line of fire across Raju's back.

"AAAAGH—!"

A scream of pure agony ripped through the square—the sound of a man feeling his flesh tear from bone.

"You have shown no remorse, Roberto Raju. Not only have you sabotaged the diplomacy of two nations—you concealed the late Emperor's testament and embezzled his inheritance for your personal gain."

The messenger's voice rose above the crack of leather and the prisoner's cries, cold with righteous contempt.

CRACK—!

"Ngh—!"

"You dared to deceive His Imperial Majesty—and still you hoped to survive? Such audacity."

The messenger's lip curled with disgust.

"His Majesty was particularly incensed to learn of your embezzlement of the southern salt revenues—profits far exceeding even the subsidies from your secret arrangements with the Anspiras lords."

"H-how... how did you..."

Anspiras. The southern salt trade...

Words the Emperor could not possibly know. Should not possibly know.

In my letter, I mentioned only that the late Emperor had left certain records. How could they have learned the specifics?

The exact details of the former Emperor's legacy existed in only one place: the original ledger. That ledger was hidden deep within a private chamber that only Raju himself ever entered. The safe that contained it was sealed with a blood-recognition spell—a lock that could only be opened by someone who shared his bloodline.

Impossible. No one can open it. No one except—

A terrible light flickered in the depths of his understanding.

" Birna, where are you going at this hour? Your mother will worry. "

"Come now. Why not visit this old man's study? Stay as long as you like."

No. It cannot be.

His head turned, slowly, mechanically—searching the crowd with the desperation of a drowning man.

And there she was.

Birna. His daughter. The girl who had kept her head bowed throughout the proceedings.

She was looking at him now.

And she was smiling.

"BIRNAAA—!"

"HOW DARE YOU!"

His anguished cry echoed through the square, raw and ragged, even as another lash descended upon his ruined flesh.

✦ ✦ ✦

When the messenger's words fell, the color drained from Catherine's face.

This cannot be real...

Not repatriation. Execution.

Five hundred lashes. No man, however strong, could survive such a punishment. The Emperor had no intention of letting Raju live.

The rose-colored world Catherine had been building—the future she had finally dared to believe in—shattered around her like glass falling from a great height.

Why? Why?

Before she could process what was happening, the first lash fell.

CRACK—!

"AAAAH—!"

CRACK—! CRACK—!

"No! NO!"

Catherine lunged toward the stage, hands reaching for the man whose screams carved themselves into her soul.

"This cannot be happening! Something is wrong—something is wrong!"

"Remove yourself. Unless you wish to share his fate."

The messenger's gaze was pitiless.

"His Majesty has commanded that the punishment proceed without mercy."

At his signal, guards seized Catherine's arms and legs, dragging her back from the platform as she thrashed and screamed.

"No... no..."

Tears streamed down her beautiful face, mingling with the dust of the square.

After all these wasted years—after everything—we were finally so close...

She could not bear to watch Raju's flesh split open beneath the relentless lashes.

Then his voice tore through the chaos.

"BIRNAAA! HOW DARE YOU!"

Catherine froze.

In that moment, she became aware of a strange, impossible stillness beside her.

Amid the crowd's shock, amid the screams and the crack of leather—her daughter stood utterly silent.

As though she had known. As though none of this surprised her.

No. It cannot be.

Catherine turned slowly, dread pooling in her stomach like ice water.

Her daughter's eyes remained fixed on the stage where Raju writhed.

And on those lips—those soft, familiar lips—curved the ghost of a smile.

I must have imagined it. Birna would never—our daughter, of all people—

With trembling hands, Catherine gripped her daughter's shoulder.

"Birna..."

Her voice emerged as barely a whisper.

"Yes?"

Birna turned, her expression placid. Almost cheerful.

"What is it, Mother?"

"You... you reported him."

"Heh."

A sneer curled across Birna's lips—slow, deliberate, savoring.

"Why, Mother? Are you sad? Because your paramour is dying?"

"What—"

"The filth of it makes me sick. How long has this been going on?"

Birna's gaze raked over Catherine with undisguised revulsion.

"The maids told me everything. A few nights ago, they heard a woman's voice in that man's chambers. Moaning. Gasping."

Her lip curled.

"In our house, Mother. While you play the grieving widow. It's disgusting. It's contemptible."

Memories surfaced unbidden—the years before Birna knew, when the relationship was still hidden behind closed doors and careful lies.

"I could have forgiven much."

Birna's voice went flat. Cold.

"But Father was a Prince. At the very least, you might have maintained some pretense of standards."

The image of her father's final moments flickered behind her eyes—that swollen, ruined face, so incongruous an end for noble blood.

He died a death that shamed his lineage. And Mother would have replaced him with... that.

"What are you saying? What have you done?"

"Why do you look so shocked?"

Birna's smile sharpened into something cruel.

"Yes, Mother. I did it. I reported him. That's why he screams like a slaughtered pig."

"The fool showed me everything—his private chambers, his secrets—all to win my affection. He thought he could make me love him."

A laugh escaped her, bright and brittle.

"So I found the ledger. I learned exactly how much of the former Emperor's fortune he had stolen. And I sent every detail to Katzen."

Her eyes glittered.

"The Emperor must have lost his mind with rage. All that stolen wealth, used to prop up Raju's pathetic pretensions of power."

"BIRNA—!"

Catherine's scream tore from her chest as she lunged toward her daughter.

"Don't touch me with your filthy hands!"

Birna shoved her backward—hard, vicious—and Catherine crashed to the flagstones.

Looking down at her mother, crumpled in the dirt, Birna's expression held nothing but contempt.

Her eyes burned with the cold fire of betrayal avenged.

"Did you truly believe I would allow my father's murderer to simply... walk away?"
