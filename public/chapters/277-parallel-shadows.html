Chapter 277

Parallel
Shadows

"Trust, once shattered, leaves wounds that echo across lifetimes."

Beneath the lamplight, their shadows stretched long and sharp across the wall—clean, distinct lines that refused to overlap.

Like two men standing on parallel paths, destined never to intersect.

"You are remarkably generous in tolerating the impudence of this lowly man, Your Highness."

Sissair's voice was ice wrapped in silk.

"The rumors I heard, the truths I believed I understood... none of it was real."

"Sissair—"

"I never imagined that a Finance Minister's position could be so thoroughly degraded. Your Highness was right all along. How amusing we must have seemed—those of us who were simply too weak to be wicked."

"Enough. I don't understand what you're—"

"You made a fool of me!"

In a burst of fury, Sissair seized Cesare by the throat and slammed him against the wall.

"Was it entertaining? You and Gallo, deceiving me for so long?"

Raw betrayal bled through the rage in his voice.

"'Look at this, Sissair! The severed head of the evil dragon that our Princess and the First Prince burned. Impressive, isn't it?'"

"'When did you become so familiar with each other? You were gaping in surprise just moments ago!'"

"'Pay attention—what do you see on the severed neck?'"

The cut marks left on the dragon's neck. A sword technique used exclusively in the Tower of the White Night.

"'The First Prince... he's the one who struck it down?'"

"'Of course! You should have seen the black aura stretching out, enveloping those impenetrable scales!'"

Sissair's cold gaze bore into Cesare.

This man he had once trusted more deeply than anyone. This man he had believed in enough to extend his hand during his nation's darkest hour.

"Acares. The Tower of the White Night. The Continental War."

A bitter laugh escaped him.

"Of all the words you've ever spoken, was there anything that could be called truth?"

Cesare remained silent.

No protest. No wavering in his expression. He simply accepted the accusation in utter stillness.

He knew he would not be understood—especially not by someone like Sissair, who had always walked the narrow path of unwavering principle.

My life has been threatened since the moment I drew my first breath.

Whether that environment had shaped him this way, or whether he had been born thus—he could no longer say.

But even if he could return to that moment, he would not reveal his true identity to a friend named Sissair. He would not hand his enemies such a devastating weakness.

"Damn you, Acares—say something! Make excuses!"

That tranquil composure only inflamed Sissair's fury.

Was it so meaningless?

Did I tear down my walls and pour my heart into a relationship so shallow it could be overturned in an instant?

The ground of his homeland still supported his feet, but somewhere beyond—at a distance he could not see—he heard the unmistakable sound of a dam collapsing.

One of the foundations he had always leaned upon, crumbling irreversibly.

A bloody laugh escaped Sissair's throat.

"You always urged me to leave Valdina and join your side. Now I understand—there was a calculated reason for everything."

The First Prince of Katzen.

Overcoming the weakness of his unfortunate birth, he had walked a tightrope with the Emperor, mastering the art of manipulation while consolidating his own power.

Sissair had nearly been swayed by the First Prince's clever schemes.

"How pathetic that I once considered you my closest friend. And now you intend to toy with Her Highness the Princess as well."

Something flickered in Cesare's eyes.

"Did you deceive Her Highness the same way? Hiding your true intentions toward Valdina behind a facade of sincerity? Was there anything in your calculations that wasn't fabricated?"

CRACK.

The silence shattered. Cesare wrenched Sissair's hand from his throat with violent force.

"Blame me all you wish. You're hardly the first, and you certainly won't be the last."

The Truth Within

The composure that had remained unshaken through countless blows finally cracked.

"Friends? Ha. Do you know who the first person I ever killed was?"

Cesare's voice turned savage.

"The wet nurse who raised me. Then the guard who grew up at my side like a brother. Then every retainer who had become parents to me in place of the Emperor."

"...What?"

"Everyone who ever loved me eventually tried to kill me. Do you truly believe I cannot endure your anger?"

But then his voice dropped—lower, softer. And as it softened, the blade within it grew sharper.

"But Dea is different."

"Say whatever you wish about me. I can accept it all. But if you dare cast doubt upon my love for Dea—"

His golden eyes blazed in the darkness, feral and absolute.

"—I will not forgive you."

Like a predator marking the boundary of its territory by pure instinct.

"Acares, you..."

Elegant fingers reached out to straighten Sissair's disheveled collar.

Then paused.

The corners of Cesare's mouth twisted into something that might have been a smile.

"Call it contradiction. Call it cowardice. I haven't the luxury of caring what others think."

Cesare stepped back and walked away.

There was no regret in that retreating figure. No anger. No lingering glance.

As though he had never expected anything in the first place.

The Vigil

How much time had passed?

Deep night had fallen. Even those kept awake by worry and anxiety had finally surrendered their weary heads to exhaustion.

Cesare entered Medea's bedchamber.

She lay so still, eyes closed, that he could scarcely detect her breathing.

Gazing down at the dark sweep of her lashes against pallid cheeks, he released a slow breath.

In that fleeting moment, the decade they had shared since first meeting at the Tower of the White Night flashed before his eyes.

Strange, how loss never grew familiar. No matter how many times he experienced it, each new wound cut as fresh as the first.

"Everyone who ever loved me tried to kill me in the end."

The words had come from his own lips, yet they felt like someone else's prophecy.

I hope it won't be you, Medea.

Not you. Anyone but you.

He swallowed the ache that pressed against his chest.

I want to know you better. Deeper. Without concealment.

I will protect you so completely that you cannot leave.

So that you will stay by my side.

Cesare bent low, pressing his forehead gently against hers.

He looked rather like a great black hound curled beside its master—though neither the hound nor the master would have cared.

The faint sweetness at the tip of his nose was at once strange and achingly familiar.

Even with her right beside him, her presence felt impossibly distant.

"Wake up, Medea."

Yet even as he spoke, his own eyes drifted closed.

And from somewhere beyond sight, a white light bloomed—enveloping them both.

The Mirror's Fragments

Moments earlier—when she had emerged from the Chamber of Judgment and found herself facing Jason—Medea had lost consciousness.

Something vast and overwhelming had struck her.

Again... just like before...

The white radiance coalesced into a great mirror—shattered into massive shards.

First Fragment

Rachel's face materialized within the first shard.

She wore not her cream-colored saintly vestments, but a magnificent golden gown befitting an Empress.

"Before we eliminate Medea, we must dispose of her brother first."

Rachel murmured to herself as she pruned a small potted tree upon the table.

"It would have been convenient if we'd stripped away that wretch's entire family, but I doubt the King could manage that at the time..."

Her voice sharpened with irritation.

"Honestly, what use is a king of such a pathetic little kingdom?"

Delicate fingers snapped another branch from the pot. And another. Until only a single limb remained.

"Not much longer now. Just a little while more..."

Second Fragment

The vision shifted. A younger Rachel's face, alight with ambition.

"Your Majesty! Are you listening?"

Though the voice was Rachel's, the reflection showed Jason.

The vitality of youth had drained from him; his features had grown haggard with the passage of years. Yet the ravenous hunger in his eyes remained unchanged.

"The King of Valdina keeps demanding Medea's return. It's becoming rather troublesome. And if Medea were to die..."

Jason nodded at Rachel's delicate complaint.

"He'll turn his blade against me. Neither the woman nor her brother ever knew when to surrender. They'll always find a way to regain their footing and threaten me. They're too dangerous—for me and for this empire."

"But Your Majesty is the champion who slew the evil dragon! The hero who claimed the throne! Surely you can handle them with ease."

Rachel draped herself around Jason's neck, whispering honeyed words into his ear. Yet his expression remained troubled.

Medea understood why. She knew exactly why Jason could never feel secure.

Because you accomplished nothing yourself.

That was why he feared losing everything to Medea. That was why he had ultimately chosen to destroy her.

He wanted her annihilated so completely that she would never dare raise a blade against him again.

The Forgery

Rachel knew nothing of the truth the expedition members had buried, but she and Jason were united in their goal: the Valdina siblings had to be eliminated.

"I'll send this to Valdina now. I'm not certain it will work."

Jason nodded.

"Desperate as his kingdom's situation may be, he still loves his sister dearly. It won't be ineffective."

He glanced casually at the parchment in Rachel's hand.

The document materialized before Medea's eyes, floating above the mirror shard.

She went rigid.

That letter... I never wrote that.

"Peleus, help me.
I am fleeing Katzen.
Come to the Valdina border to rescue me."

— Written in Medea's own hand. A hand she had never set to paper.
