The Dragon's
Shadow

"If God leads me to death, then I shall carve my own path forward."

Tom lingered before the closed door, worrying his lower lip between his teeth until it threatened to bleed.

"Tom, Her Highness expressly commanded that no one enter until she summons us."

Neril's voice was firm, but Tom could hear the strain beneath it.

"But Neril—you know we can't simply wait here forever. The Holy Kingdom's messenger draws closer with every passing hour."

The freckled young man's mouth curved into a crooked smile—his usual playfulness stripped away, replaced by something harder. Something that looked disturbingly like acceptance.

"Lord Kensington, perhaps we should divide our roles now, while we still can. Should we encounter the Knights Templar... you escort Her Highness to safety while I hold them off."

The words carried the weight of a man who had already made peace with his death.

"The red alert has already been sounded throughout the network. The moment Her Highness gives the order, we are prepared to evacuate Valdina immediately."

Kensington inclined his head in acknowledgment.

"But Tom—it is far too early to assume the worst. Let us not make any presumptions until Her Highness speaks."

In truth, Kensington had felt a measure of relief when Medea dismissed everyone and locked herself away with only the map for company.

"I am not running away."

Those words still echoed in his mind.

Her Highness must have a plan.

Even confronted with a crisis as unprecedented as a divine oracle, Kensington had drawn confidence from the Princess's unshakeable composure.

If this was truly the woman he had come to serve—the woman who, with her frail and gentle appearance, had evaded the predatory gaze of Perdiccas II—

If she was the architect who had raised Valdina from the ashes of near-collapse and forged it into the power it was today—

Then surely she would find a way through this darkness as well.

But this—this was something else entirely.

'She intends to sever the evil dragon's head herself?'

Not merely rejecting the oracle—but eliminating its very source.

"Precisely. If the evil dragon ceases to exist, they cannot take me as its bride. The oracle becomes impossible to fulfill."

Medea's voice was calm. Measured. As though she were discussing trade routes rather than hunting a creature of nightmare.

"But Your Highness—this is... this is unprecedented."

Kensington's breath caught in his throat.

He was an atheist. He had never believed in gods or their power.

But even Kensington could not deny the strangling influence of the goddess whose doctrine had taken root across the entire continent.

Tom and Neril stood frozen, equally speechless.

Silence pooled in the chamber like spilled ink.

It was Medea's voice that cut through the stillness no one else dared break.

"There is nowhere left to retreat."

Her tone remained level, but beneath it lay iron.

"If God Himself leads me toward death, then I have no choice but to carve my own path forward."

Kensington forced his stunned expression into something resembling composure and attempted to assess the plan's feasibility with cold rationality.

"Where... how would we even locate the evil dragon's lair?"

"Here."

Medea's finger descended upon the map, touching a flag positioned at its rightmost edge.

The Path to the Dragon

The Valley of Death

Tom read the words inscribed beneath the black flag: "The End of the Continent."

Neril's gaze traced the series of flags stretching from Katzen across the breadth of the continent.

"And these markers—what do they represent?"

"The path to the Valley of Death."

This was the route Medea had traveled in her previous life as part of Jason's expedition to slay the evil dragon.

"To reach the valley, one must pass through the Fields of the Dead, the Desert of Bones, the Swamp of Gluttony, and finally the Forest of Eternal Darkness."

Her pale fingers swept across the map, brushing each flag in succession.

"Beyond the pitch-black forest lies the valley—and the dragon's lair."

"But Your Highness, even with knowledge of the lair's location, such a journey would consume far too much time. The distance alone—"

Kensington's objection was valid. In her previous life, Medea's expedition had required five years to complete the round trip.

But circumstances then were vastly different from now.

Back then, they had neither known the dragon's precise location nor possessed a direct route. They had been forced to begin from Katzen Castle and push inland through hostile territory.

The journey had been prolonged by treacherous terrain, endless battles against demonic beasts, and the necessity of forging alliances across scattered islands and kingdoms.

Now, however, Medea's position was incomparably stronger.

The Strait of El Amuz has fallen under Valdina's control.

"I know a shortcut."

Medea lifted the flag nearest to the Valley of Death.

"Observe. The Forest of Eternal Darkness hugs the coastline. It was formed along the shore."

"If we sail through the Strait of El Amuz and take the sea route, we can reach the forest directly—"

Kensington completed the thought, understanding dawning in his eyes.

Medea inclined her head in confirmation.

"By departing from the strait, we bypass every intermediate obstacle and arrive at the Forest of Darkness immediately."

"Ten days at the earliest. A fortnight at most. Once we navigate through the forest, the valley lies just beyond."

Medea traced the final leg of the journey.

"We can return to Valdina well before the temple's deadline expires."

She paused, her expression growing serious.

"However, passage through the Forest of Eternal Darkness will be far from simple. If we are to return in time, we must traverse it as swiftly as possible."

"So the matter of time is resolved. But the crucial question remains—"

Tom's voice emerged caught between disbelief and barely suppressed excitement.

"Can we actually defeat the evil dragon with our current forces?"

He had witnessed firsthand how Medea wielded cunning like a blade, maneuvering her enemies into corners one by one until they had nowhere left to run.

But this opponent was unlike any other.

The evil dragon. King of demons. An impassable wall that no mortal had ever breached. An ancient terror that had held dominion over the continent since time immemorial.

"Perhaps if we informed His Majesty and requested his aid—"

"Do you truly believe Peleus would permit me to walk into the Valley of Death?"

The question silenced them all.

Peleus was a brother whose heart ached merely from watching Medea sit in contemplative silence. He could scarcely bear to see her gaze drift toward the horizon without rushing to her side.

There was no conceivable world in which he would allow his beloved sister to march willingly into a swamp of death.

"Peleus would never sanction my plan to slay the dragon. He would sooner lock me in a tower than let me attempt it."

The three retainers held their tongues, unwilling to speak ill of their Emperor—yet unable to dispute Medea's assessment.

"Then what of Captain D'Angel? Is he not the most formidable warrior among the Agema?"

Tom's face brightened with sudden hope.

"His Majesty would notice D'Angel's absence immediately. We would be discovered before we cleared the harbor."

Neril's reply was swift and unsparing.

Medea's plan, it seemed, would inevitably be crushed beneath Peleus's protective instincts.

'If we had just two more ten-star knights, we might stand a chance.'

Medea's delicate brow furrowed almost imperceptibly.

In her previous life, she had slain the evil dragon even after Jared's death had scattered the expedition. She had done it alone—trapping the beast within its own shadow while it choked on its own venom.

But that victory had only been possible because she had spent dozens of days studying the dragon's patterns, learning its weaknesses, devising that single, desperate stratagem.

"Neril, Tom—you each achieved eight and seven stars recently, did you not? How many ten-star knights currently serve in the Agema?"

Tom counted on his fingers.

"I heard that Vice-Captain Menil and Senior Alex both reached ten stars not long ago—"

"But Your Highness, Alex's Auror control remains unstable. And Vice-Captain Menil is still recovering from the wounds that bastard Jared inflicted during the last wave."

Neril's concerns hung heavy in the air.

Kensington released a long, weary sigh.

"Either His Majesty or Sir D'Angel—we must secure one of them. They are the two mightiest blades in all of Valdina."

"Take Sir D'Angel with you. I will do everything in my power to conceal his absence. At the very least, we should be able to evade His Majesty's notice until Your Highness has cleared the strait."

Kensington's eyes fell closed, his voice trailing into silence as he swallowed the fears he could not bring himself to voice.

"But, Your Highness... unless there exists another sword powerful enough to sever a dragon's head, I see no alternative—"

An Uninvited Guest

Shhh—!

A small stone, hurled with impossible precision, struck the flag and sent it clattering to the floor.

"Who said the Princess has only two swords remaining?"

None of them had sensed his presence until that leisurely, flat voice reached their ears.

A man lounged against the windowsill with the easy grace of a predator at rest.

"I will deal with the evil dragon."

The corners of his lips curved upward, forming a smile as beautiful as it was dangerous.

"The First Prince...!"

Kensington's eyes flew wide. His cane slipped from nerveless fingers, and he barely caught it before it clattered against the marble.

"Why are you—how did you—"

This was the private palace of the Princess of Valdina.

The most secure location in the kingdom, accessible only to her most trusted confidants.

And yet here he stood—the son of the old Emperor, the most dangerous man in all of Katzen.

"It has been some time, Kensington."

The greeting was delivered with languid ease, as though they had merely crossed paths at a garden party.

But Kensington could not bring himself to return it.

For the second time since hearing Medea's plan to hunt the evil dragon, the leader of the Red Fox—a man who rarely lost his composure—found himself utterly speechless.

"Such an unusual sight. Though I must say, you look far healthier than you did when you served beside the Emperor."

The casual observation made it clear: Cesare already knew precisely how Kensington had come to stand at Medea's side.

Ignoring the sharp wariness and unspoken questions radiating from everyone in the room, Cesare pushed himself from the windowsill and approached the table.

"Set your concerns aside. You will not require additional ten-star knights."

His outstretched hand descended upon the map, plucking up the flag that marked the evil dragon's lair.

"Nor will you need to bring along dear Captain D'Angel."

His voice was laden with arrogance—and absolute confidence.

The flag snapped in his grip.

Its broken halves dangled limply from his fingers—a small, deliberate destruction.

"Isn't that right, Medea?"

Those dangerously gleaming eyes fixed upon her alone, burning with an intensity that seemed to consume all the air in the room.
