Ambient: The Trap Closes
The Final Move

The Toad
in the Jar

"Two beasts locked jaws—neither willing to release."

The Minister's Estate

Count Etienne stared at the letter from Duke Claudio.

The culprit was a maid.
She acted alone.
She has taken her own life in penance.

The parchment crumpled in his fist.

"He thinks I am a fool!"

His gaze slid to the small coffin placed beneath the window.

A dead servant. A convenient sacrifice.

It was not compensation.

It was mockery.

"So be it, Joaquin," Etienne hissed. "If you want war, I will give you war."

He spun toward his clerk.

"Draft an order. Baron Brega—the Southern Tax Collector—is dismissed for corruption. Effective immediately."

The clerk went pale.

"M-My Lord… Baron Brega is the Regent's—"

"Replace him with my nephew," Etienne snapped. "Lord Pecs."

The clerk swallowed hard.

The southern taxes were the Regent's lifeline.

"Do it."

Ink spilled.

A blade was drawn.

The Duke's Estate

"He dismissed Brega?!"

The Regent slammed his fist into the desk. Papers flew.

"That was my man! My artery!"

The damage was instant.

Etienne's officials froze the Regent's orders.

The Regent's vassals retaliated in kind.

Documents stalled. Funds vanished. Appointments were blocked.

The state began to choke.

Two beasts locked jaws—neither willing to release.

The Prime Minister's Office

Prime Minister Siseo watched the reports pile up.

For the first time in years, he smiled.

"They're eating each other alive," he murmured. "Just as she said."

Opportunity will come soon.

His aide entered hesitantly.

"My Lord… the maid from the Princess's palace has returned. She is… hysterical."

"Bring her in."

The woman collapsed at his feet, sobbing.

"She threatened my son," the maid cried. "The Princess said she would protect him if I obeyed. I gave you the tea… but told the Regent you were drinking it."

Siseo closed his eyes briefly.

So that was Medea's angle.

She made Claudio believe the Prime Minister was losing his mind—
slowly poisoned, easy prey.

"Continue," Siseo said calmly. "Serve the tea. Replace the poison with herbs."

The maid nodded frantically.

When she left, Siseo straightened.

"The board is set," he said quietly. "Remove the Minister."

The Regent's Office

"Prime Minister Siseo has launched an audit," an aide reported.

The Regent scoffed.

"Siseo? He's already half-mad."

"Should we intervene?"

"No." The Regent smiled thinly. "Let Etienne sweat. When he panics, he'll crawl back to me."

He waved the matter away.

"Siseo won't last long."

It was a fatal miscalculation.

The Minister's Estate

Bang!

The doors burst open.

Etienne stormed into the hall.

"What is this insolence—?!"

Silver armor flooded the room.

"Lark E. Etienne,"

the Knight Commander announced, unrolling a decree.

"You are under arrest."

"On what grounds?!"

"Human trafficking. Murder. Treason."

A ledger was raised.

"The children you sold to 'Black Eyes.' Sold during wartime."

Etienne's blood ran cold.

Treason.

No bribe could save him now.

"Seize him!"

Knights forced him to his knees.

"Umberto!"

"Run! Tell Duke Claudio—!"

"Yes, Master," Umberto cried, bowing deeply.

Etienne was dragged away.

The Study

Umberto entered quietly.

He picked up a quill.

He did not write to the Duke.

Instead, he wrote:

The toad is in the jar.

The fly is free.

He set the quill down gently.

"Good luck, Master," he whispered to the empty room.

"You'll need it."

The Trap Springs

The Toad is Caught

A minister falls to hubris.
A regent ignores the danger at his door.
And a princess watches all the pieces fall into place.
