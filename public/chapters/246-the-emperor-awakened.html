Chapter 246

The Emperor
Awakened

"All his glory would have been impossible without her."

Several days had passed since Medea's disappearance. Her retainer Neril had vanished alongside her, leaving not a single trace behind.

Even the Agema—Valdina's finest—had uncovered nothing.

"I hope you've gone somewhere no one can follow... and yet, selfishly, I hope it's still close enough for me to find you."

D'Angel released a hollow laugh, aware of the contradiction in his own words.

The continent was vast beyond measure. Where in all the world had Her Highness gone?

"Sir D'Angel."

A quiet voice echoed through the empty chamber.

"Kensington?"

D'Angel's eyes widened as the spymaster emerged from a concealed passage in the wall.

"How are you here? Shouldn't you be with Her Highness?"

"I will remain in Valdina. In my current condition, I would only become a burden to Her Highness. Besides—someone must stay behind to monitor the situation and relay intelligence."

"You've been hiding in this villa all this time? Without anyone's knowledge?"

D'Angel suppressed a disbelieving laugh, but his hand shot out to grasp Kensington's arm like a man clutching at a lifeline.

"Kensington. Do you know where Her Highness has gone?"

After Medea's departure, Kensington had remained to observe Emperor Peleus.

The Emperor of Valdina was, by all appearances, a brother who loved his sister so deeply he would wage war against the Katzen Empire for her sake.

But Kensington did not place his trust entirely in appearances.

Had he not witnessed Perdiccas II attempt to murder his own children—and the loyal subjects who had served him for a lifetime?

The true hearts of emperors could never be discerned from their outward demeanor.

Kensington had genuinely expected that after Medea's flight, Peleus would feign distress before dispatching D'Angel to capture and return her.

But the actual orders had been different.

To protect Her Highness. To help her escape—forever.

It was only after watching Peleus's most trusted confidant depart with a farewell meant to last a lifetime that Kensington had finally allowed himself to breathe.

"Yes. I know where she is."

A faint smile touched Kensington's lips.

"If you depart now, you should be able to reach her before they arrive at the canyon."

Elsewhere

At that same moment, another party was racing toward the dragon's lair.

"Faster! We must reach it before anyone else. Move!"

The man who drove his mount with savage urgency was Jason.

While Medea and her companions sailed from the Strait of El Amuz, Jason and his retinue pushed inland at a brutal pace—following the exact route his expedition had taken in another lifetime.

"Your Highness, why such haste? You have only just emerged from the abyss. Surely you should rest—"

Several horses collapsed from exhaustion. Jason did not slow.

"Not this route. We cross through the Fields of the Dead and traverse the Desert of Bones."

The eyes that issued these commands belonged to a man who might have been a prophet driven to madness—or a sage who had glimpsed ultimate truth.

From the moment they crossed Katzen's borders, Jason's orders had grown increasingly inexplicable.

He commanded them to ford a lake across seemingly flat terrain. He ordered them to cover their ears for no apparent reason. He led them in circles that defied all tactical logic.

His men were baffled.

And yet—

"Demons! Demons are upon us!"

"Your Highness... how did you know a wave would spawn there?"

In every perilous situation, Jason displayed uncanny foresight—as though he had already lived through what was to come.

It was from that point forward that his men began to accept his orders without question.

"Your Highness! How did you know a swarm of iron scorpions would arrive?"

"The cave beasts—their weakness is fire!"

"I saw it in a dream."

Jason's answers were vague, as though he possessed neither the desire nor the time to explain in detail.

"Is this not divine revelation? Your Highness is truly the goddess's chosen—the rightful master of this land!"

Their lord seemed to possess every secret the continent held.

As his reputation grew—bolstered by his lineage and now his seemingly supernatural insight—so too did his followers' reverence.

"It's as though you've become an entirely different person since emerging from the abyss."

"What a dreadful place that must have been. You've been completely reborn."

His subordinates speculated that the agonizing experience of the abyss had fundamentally transformed him.

The truth was something else entirely.

The Abyss

Some time ago, Jason had awakened to memories of his past life.

It was within the abyss that the full recollection had finally crashed over him.

The abyss was every bit as terrible as its reputation suggested.

Complete darkness for days on end. Nothing to hear. Nothing to see.

Amid that cruel, endless suffering, Jason had screamed until his throat bled.

But the abyss swallowed all sound—every scream, every groan—returning only the echo of his own sobs.

As madness crept closer with each passing moment...

Everything was erased. Fell silent. As though the self had ceased to exist.

Death felt near.

And then—

Reality shattered. Colors bled together. Light blazed like the sun.

Past and present collided, merging into one.

"Ugh—!"

Jason awakened to his previous life.

He touched his face. His cheeks were soaked.

The tears that had flowed unceasingly during awakening—were they born of regret for the past, or joy for what lay ahead?

He could not say for certain.

But of this much, he was absolutely sure:

Jason Castullo. Warrior who slew the evil dragon. Future Emperor of the Katzen Empire.

His ambition had never been hollow fantasy.

It was something that had actually happened. And that was precisely why he could not let it slip away.

But the elation was short-lived.

Jason quickly realized that his past life differed drastically from his present circumstances.

His uncle Perdiccas II—who had oppressed him so mercilessly—still lived. And his eldest son, Cesare, who should have died young, was not only alive but thriving.

All of these discrepancies pointed to a single answer.

The Truth

"Medea. You were first. You already knew."

"I don't know... perhaps there was some unfortunate connection from a previous life."

Her words and actions—so incomprehensible, so inexplicably hostile—finally made perfect sense.

Medea, his first wife. The woman he had betrayed. The woman he had ultimately destroyed along with their children.

She remembered.

That was why she had been his enemy from the very beginning.

"...I must reclaim Medea."

The reason he found himself in such a wretched state—so different from his glorious past life—was because Medea was no longer at his side.

After becoming Emperor, he had tried desperately to erase this truth, to deny it.

But in the deepest recesses of his heart, he had always known:

All his glory would have been impossible without his wife, Medea.

"And everything must be restored to its proper place."

Jason murmured the words like a vow.

The frustration and depression that had plagued him for so long had vanished from his bearing.

In their place burned the energy of a man resurrected—a god returned from death.

When he emerged from the abyss, he was utterly transformed from the man who had entered.

In his previous life, whatever advantages his wife had provided, he had still led an expedition across the continent and ruled an empire for decades. That weight of experience, that accumulated wisdom—it now lived within him.

He was no longer the weak, pathetic archer he had once been.

"Your Highness! An oracle has been issued from the Holy Kingdom!"

News of the oracle reached Katzen at last.

Jason startled. For a moment, like Medea before him, he was bewildered.

'Why an oracle now?'

The content was nothing he had anticipated.

'Sacrifice Medea to the dragon? The first oracle should have commanded Rachel and me to wed...'

But his confusion did not last.

Oracle and awakening.

Amid these unexpected changes, Jason found renewed strength to push forward.

"...I must slay the evil dragon. And save Medea."

The expedition had succeeded in killing the dragon only because of Medea's ingenuity—she had devised the method that made it possible.

Jason had been the one who witnessed it most closely.

By rescuing her from death as a sacrifice, he would earn her forgiveness and wash away the sins of his past life.

"If I defeat the evil dragon, I can reclaim everything I've lost."

A warrior who conquered a beast that even the Holy Kingdom dared not challenge.

By restoring his reputation, he would finally be able to stand against the First Prince, Cesare.

"Cesare... how in the world did you survive?"

Jason could not comprehend how a man who should have died early in the previous timeline was now running rampant across the continent.

But it no longer mattered.

The Emperor's Vow

"Cesare. This world is no longer yours."

Jason felt no fear.

He was not the weak, pathetic Jason Castullo of old.

Grand Duke Jason might have found the First Prince burdensome. But Emperor Jason—who had weathered every conceivable hardship—was a different creature entirely.

To him, the variable called Cesare was nothing more than a sickly child who had died young. An inconvenience already resolved.

"...I will slay the evil dragon with my own hands."

Jason's eyes blazed with an intensity unlike anything he had shown before—a depth that could not be easily fathomed.

"For Medea and myself. For the two of us."

An obvious declaration—yet utterly, devastatingly one-sided—echoed through the darkness.

End Chapter 246
