Chapter 26

The Distance
Between Hearts

"I wanted to give you everything—except the one thing you truly needed."

Was this truly Cesare's nature?

He was a man of fierce pride—one who had never been, nor ever intended to be, anyone's pawn. She had always known he would recoil at being moved like a piece on a game board, even if the outcome served his interests.

His reaction should not have surprised her.

And yet... I never expected him to sever himself so completely. So without hesitation.

A hollow ache settled in her chest.

Perhaps, somewhere in the depths of her heart, she had believed he would understand. That he, who always seemed to perceive her thoughts before she voiced them, would see through to her true intentions this time as well.

She had been waiting. Hoping.

"...You said you only needed to be by my side."

Her voice drifted through the empty bedroom—aimless words spoken to no one, too soft to penetrate the door that separated them.

"You don't have to love me."

"Dea. I'm simply here. Beside you."

"I only wanted to give something back to you..."

That had been her reasoning. Her justification.

Since she could not offer him affection, she would offer him a crown instead. A throne seemed a fair exchange—something to assuage the guilt of accepting a heart she could never return.

But if you pull away from me like this...

What am I supposed to do now?

Medea's hands curled into fists at her sides.

She felt the sting of heat behind her eyes and laughed bitterly at herself.

"When did I become so weak?"

What right did she have to feel disappointed?

"You were the one who gave me something I never asked for."

She wiped the dampness from her cheeks with the back of her hand. Lifted her chin. Fixed her gaze upon that unyielding door once more.

The turbulent waves in her green eyes gradually stilled, forced into submission by sheer will.

She continued to stare at the door until exhaustion finally dragged her into restless sleep.

"Ah—!"

Medea jolted awake, fingers clutching the sheets in a white-knuckled grip.

She had dreamed of a golden tower consumed by flames—its spires crumbling, collapsing upon the figure trapped beneath.

The air was cold. Beyond the window, the sky remained shrouded in predawn darkness.

"Neeiighhh—!"

The distant cry of a horse pierced the silence.

As if drawn by an invisible thread, Medea rose and moved toward the window.

Torches blazed throughout the palace grounds.

Below, she could make out Gallo's mercenaries assembling alongside the First Prince's elite forces—their black armor gleaming in the firelight like scales of some great serpent.

"...Everything is prepared..."

"...We depart immediately..."

Fragments of conversation drifted up to her window. The horses were laden with weapons and supplies—clearly outfitted for a lengthy campaign.

"...Cesare."

She spotted his black stallion at the head of the formation.

Without conscious thought, Medea seized her robe and flew down the stairs.

"Cesare!"

He turned at the sound of her voice, his expression flickering with something unreadable before settling into careful neutrality.

He dismounted and strode toward her, a frown creasing his brow.

"I gave explicit orders not to wake you. Who—"

"You're leaving?"

She cut through his words without ceremony.

"Where are you going?"

Cesare hesitated. Then, as if reading the exhaustion carved into her features, he answered.

"...The Golden Tower. The warlocks' stronghold. Their location shifts constantly—we must strike before the coordinates change. There is no time to delay."

The Golden Tower.

A chill ran through Medea's body.

The dream—the tower wreathed in flames, collapsing upon itself—flashed through her mind with terrible clarity.

"Where? Where is this Golden Tower?"

Silence.

She understood his refusal to answer.

"You fear I will follow you again."

"I will return as soon as the main encampment is neutralized. It should not take long."

His words were meant to reassure, but his tone made clear that this was not a negotiation. He would not permit her to accompany him.

Behind them, Terrence's voice carried across the courtyard, urging that the transfer circle was prepared.

Cesare gazed down at Medea for a long moment.

His eyes traced the pallor of her cheeks, the slight tremor of her lips in the cold night air—a caress that never quite became touch.

"...Forgive me for acting on my own, Dea."

He leaned toward her—instinct driving him to press a kiss to her cheek, as he had done countless times before.

Then he stopped.

Drew back.

As though realizing that even such a small gesture would be forcing his unwanted heart upon her once more.

Instead, he draped his coat across her shoulders and turned away.

A violet sigil blazed to life beneath their feet—the activation of powerful teleportation magic.

In the span of a heartbeat, every figure in the courtyard vanished without a trace.

Medea clutched the coat he had left behind, her fingers curling into the fabric.

I should have apologized. Should have said I was sorry.

I should have held onto him. Admitted that I wanted this too.

Or at the very least...

"I should have said goodbye."

Regret settled around her like morning mist—too late, too faint to change anything at all.

The Northern Border of Katzen

"Commander, why didn't you inform Her Highness about the warlocks beforehand?"

Gallo stumbled slightly as the warp deposited them onto solid ground, still disoriented from the magical transit.

"Knowing her, she would have insisted on accompanying us. It struck me as strange that you kept it from her."

Terrence opened his mouth to add something, then promptly closed it when he noticed the dagger spinning lazily between Cesare's fingers.

"This mission is too dangerous. I know precisely what would happen if I brought Dea into this."

Cesare lifted his gaze to the snow-capped mountains looming before them, their peaks swallowed by darkness.

The turmoil of recent days churned within him—the refused crown, the question of independence, Medea's machinations, the dream of a future that seemed to slip further away with each passing moment.

The blueprint he had carried his entire life—that unwavering certainty of purpose—now trembled beneath the weight of her existence.

When news of the warlocks' location had arrived, it had offered a moment of clarity amidst the chaos.

"Dea's safety is paramount. Nothing else matters."

She had not changed. He would not attempt to change her.

Even if a small, wounded part of him ached at the knowledge that her heart might never return to him.

Whether in Katzen or anywhere else—I need only eliminate every threat that exists.

So that she never again has reason to throw herself into danger.

"Where exactly are these bastards hiding?"

Gallo peered through his telescope at the forbidding mountain range stretching before them.

The mana reserves they had prepared only permitted teleportation to Katzen's border—no further.

"From this point forward, detection wards will trigger an alarm the moment anyone employs magic."

Terrence picked his way through the underbrush, using his rapier to disarm ancient trap mechanisms hidden among the rocks.

"These enchantments are extraordinarily old. Layers upon layers of concealment. Wherever the entrance to this mountain stronghold lies, it has been hidden with painstaking care..."

He swallowed a weary sigh.

The peaks rose before them—dense, treacherous, seemingly endless. Finding the Golden Tower within this labyrinth would prove no simple task.

And whether they could locate the darkness that had been steadily amassing its strength beyond the ice wall remained entirely uncertain.

The Tower of the Western Empress — Imperial Palace

"Those wretched fiends...!"

The Western Empress's fury erupted like a breaking dam.

Before her lay a napkin covered in minuscule handwriting—a covert message from her father, the Count of Montfort, smuggled in under the pretense of delivering food.

The cramped letters detailed everything: how her beloved Juan had been hunted by a mutant demon during the competition, how he had fallen utterly from Perdiccas II's favor.

"These demonic creatures reduced me to this state, and now they target my son!"

She trembled with impotent rage.

Trapped alone in this tower, stripped of allies and resources, she could do nothing but watch as her world crumbled.

Word had reached her that Juan—banished to the dungeons for days on end—had collapsed from illness almost immediately upon his release.

The news carved through her heart like a blade.

"My poor boy... How terrified he must have been..."

When she thought of the architect of their destruction, her teeth ground together until her jaw ached.

Her informants within the Emperor's palace had relayed something else: Perdiccas II had offered the Crown Prince title to Cesare.

And Cesare had refused.

"Why?! Why must everything flow back to him?!"

She clutched her skull, fingers digging into her scalp.

Fury toward the First Prince—the man who had dragged her and her son through the mud while ascending step by calculated step—blazed white-hot within her.

"I should have killed him when I had the chance. I should have moved sooner..."

Footsteps echoed through the empty chamber.

Click. Click. Click.

Half-worn shoes came to rest before her, their owner materializing from the shadows.

"What are you doing here...?"

"A common enemy has a way of forging the most unlikely alliances."

The Western Empress looked up, her eyes narrowing as recognition dawned.

"I have learned that the First Prince departed Katzen with his forces just last night. A secret expedition, it seems."

The smooth, cultured voice resonated through the prison's cold stone walls.

"Your Majesty, Western Empress... If I were to tell you I possess a plan to eliminate the First Prince once and for all—would you be willing to cooperate?"
