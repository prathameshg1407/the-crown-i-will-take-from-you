Chapter 294

Seeds of
Doubt

"Suspicion is a poison that requires no vial—only a single, well-placed word."

"Truly, the Emperor's foresight illuminates the path before us."

Cesare's voice dripped with honeyed mockery as the Emperor's hands trembled against his staff.

"That the sovereign of this empire himself serves as my wife's protector—what greater wedding gift could we possibly receive? This unworthy son failed to recognize the depth of Your Majesty's affection and showed such disrespect."

The words landed like poisoned darts, striking both Emperor and Empress with surgical precision.

Perdiccas II seethed with impotent fury. He could not reclaim the guardianship and its privileges without appearing to contradict his own imperial decree.

The Western Empress fared no better. That same privilege would serve as an impenetrable shield, protecting the Crown Princess from any scheme she might devise.

A firm hand closed around Medea's, drawing her gently toward the exit.

"With your permission, my wife and I shall take our leave. Your Majesty, please guard your health carefully."

Cesare paused, his smile sharpening.

"After all, you must remain strong if you wish to continue protecting my wife... and this realm."

The words fell like bitter medicine forced down an unwilling throat.

"You arrogant wretch! Blinded by your wife's skirts, you dare cast covetous eyes upon my throne! Get out of my sight—now!"

Perdiccas II's roar echoed through the garden. Then his gaze turned to Medea, cold as winter frost.

"I granted you a privilege, girl, but do not presume to overstep your station. Make no mistake—not everyone who stands beside the Prince becomes Empress."

The threat was unmistakable: there would be no coronation for Cesare. No crown for his bride.

Behind the Emperor, the faces of the Western Empress and Juan bloomed with barely concealed triumph.

"I would never dare aspire to such heights, Your Majesty."

Medea bowed her head with perfect grace.

"Such ambitions are merely the delusions of fools."

The Emperor's eyes narrowed. She who had stood so rigidly upon her privileges now bent like a willow—and called someone a fool in the same breath.

Is she mocking me?

"Though the order of precedence is clear to all, there are always those who place personal ambition above the prestige of the throne."

Medea's voice remained perfectly measured, as though she noticed nothing of the Emperor's reddening face.

"Surely the master of mighty Katzen sees through such intentions with ease."

Perdiccas II went still.

It was as though someone had slipped a cold blade directly into his skull, cutting through the fog of rage.

Nothing is more dangerous than anger... except suspicion.

Medea suppressed the smile that threatened to surface.

Did the Empress—mistress of the Inner Palace—truly forget the privileges her husband had granted to the Princess of Valdina?

Or did she remember perfectly well... and still dare to test the Emperor's authority?

A single drop of doubt. That was all it took.

Medea knew the Emperor's paranoia would do the rest—spreading that drop until it stained everything black.

Perdiccas II despised both Medea and Cesare with a hatred that bordered on obsession.

But that very hatred had become the perfect smokescreen. While the Emperor focused all his fury upon them, he would never notice the seeds of suspicion now taking root in his mind.

Seeds that would grow toward the Western Empress.

The Corridors Beyond

✦ ✦ ✦

Led from the imperial presence by Cesare's hand, Medea's face remained carefully blank.

The Emperor harbors deep hatred for Cesare—but also an inferiority complex.

If his son had died, that death would have become a thorn lodged permanently in his heart. But Cesare had survived. Returned. Grown stronger.

And that survival only sharpened the Emperor's sense of inadequacy.

The crown won't come easily to Cesare's hands.

The path forward crystallized in her mind.

The Third Prince holds the strongest position. He must be the first to fall.

She was deep in calculation when Cesare stopped abruptly.

His grip on her hand had tightened without warning.

"What is it?"

She looked up at his face, but he wouldn't meet her eyes. His gaze drifted somewhere beyond her—avoiding, evading.

"I made you listen to filth."

His voice was quiet. Rough. As though the words scraped against something raw inside him.

"This was your first time setting foot in Katzen's Imperial Palace. And the first things you witnessed were the ugliest it has to offer."

His hand rose to brush along her silver hair.

His fingers traced the curve of her ear, then smoothed gently over the delicate lobe—as though he could wipe away the Emperor's venomous words that had assaulted it.

The touch was impossibly tender. An apology made flesh.

"It won't happen again."

Medea felt the raw emotion buried beneath his carefully controlled voice. She shook her head.

"You know it's still too early for this."

She was grateful that he had defended her before the Emperor. But this—baring his fangs the moment she was touched—was not the Cesare who had survived this long.

Cesare. You must remain yourself.

Detached from emotion. Unbound by sentiment. That cold superiority had kept him alive in this viper's nest.

A sound escaped him—something between a laugh and a sigh, heavy with self-mockery.

"I can't do that."

The hand that had been tracing her ear fell away. His arms came to rest upon her shoulders, drawing her into a loose cage of warmth.

"When it comes to you, Dea... I find I cannot."

Before she could stiffen, Cesare buried his face against her shoulder.

His dark hair tickled her throat. She could feel the tension coiled through every line of his body.

"I have always mocked those who let emotion override reason. And now I find myself doing that very contemptible thing."

He swallowed the rest of his words, as though afraid of losing himself entirely.

Then she felt his spine straighten—because a small hand had come to rest against his back.

"None of this was unexpected. And none of it is your fault."

Having endured such hatred, such unprovoked cruelty—it was more than enough.

And experiencing the Emperor's venom firsthand, it was not difficult to imagine the childhood of a boy who had grown up drowning in it.

I know what it means to be unwelcome.

She understood the particular loneliness of wandering through magnificent halls with no one to turn to. The misery of splendor that offered no warmth.

"Cesare. No one chooses their bloodline."

The world had condemned him. Every pointed finger, every whispered accusation—and still he had fought to survive.

"If surviving is a sin, then one might as well be wicked with pride."

He lifted his head.

For the first time, something in his expression made him look like a lost child—uncertain, vulnerable, desperately searching.

Before she realized what she was doing, Medea's hands rose to cup his face.

She was not tall enough to reach him. But before she could rise onto her toes, he bent toward her, lowering himself to meet her touch.

She traced the shell of his ear, just as he had done to hers.

His skin was cold. The curve of his ear, unexpectedly soft.

I don't want you to be hurt either. I want to erase it—all of it—just like this.

The wish rose unbidden.

And before she could think better of it, she pressed her lips to his cheek.

A kiss of comfort.
Soft as a breath. Warm as a promise.

The realization of what she had done crashed over her an instant later.

Heat flooded her cheeks—but it was too late to stop.

In the next heartbeat, an arm circled her waist and pulled her sharply forward.

She found herself pressed against his chest, held as though she might disappear if he loosened his grip.

Through the layers of silk and wool, she felt the frantic pounding of his heart.
