"A river does not break stone by force. Only patience."

"The only Valdina I want to protect belongs to Peleus."

Medea's voice was calm. Absolute.

Siseo looked at her properly for the first time.

She was exhausted—dark circles faint beneath her eyes, tension coiled tight in her jaw—but beneath the strain burned something unmistakable.

Conviction.

Not ambition.

Not hunger.

Loyalty.

She means it, he realized.

Whatever had reshaped her, whatever furnace she had crawled out of, it had burned away hesitation. What remained was purpose.

"It is fine if you don't trust me yet. You will."

She didn't say soon.

She didn't need to.

"I understand your intent, Your Highness. But Etienne is not Quiggin. He is entrenched—hereditary power, generations of allies. He controls the Inner Palace."

Siseo replied carefully.

"And the Regent shields him."

Medea said.

"Yes. If Etienne falls, Joaquin will bleed. He will protect him with everything he has."

Siseo had tried for years. He had failed every time.

"What if we don't fight them together? What if we isolate him?"

Siseo shook his head.

"Their bond is survival. You won't break it."

Medea smiled faintly.

" A river does not break stone by force.
Only patience. "

She turned to the window.

"Don't worry about how to separate them. Prepare for what happens after."

There was no hesitation in her voice when she spoke again.

"Those who harbor disloyal thoughts must be removed. Even if they are family."

No mercy.

Only necessity.

She handed him a folded parchment.

"Meet me here next time. It's quieter."

Siseo unfolded it—a concealed route, blind to palace watchers.

This is real, he thought.

She's already moving.

A maid entered quietly with fresh water.

The kettle steamed as she poured.

The scent filled the room—bitter, floral, heavy.

"This blend helps my headaches."

Siseo said absently.

Medea inhaled once.

Then again.

Her eyes flicked—just once—to the maid.

"I want her."

Medea said calmly.

Siseo frowned.

"There are others—"

"I want her."

The order settled like frost.

"She is important to me."

"It is only tea. Unless you believe otherwise."

Siseo exhaled.

"…Take her."

Days Later — The Princess's Palace

Sunlight filtered through silk curtains.

Medea sat at a small table. Across from her, the tea was being prepared with meticulous care.

"Is this amount sufficient?"

Medea asked, holding up a measured pinch of leaves.

The maid did not answer.

Maria—silent, obedient—poured the water.

The scent bloomed thickly.

"Drink."

The maid stiffened.

"Your Highness—"

"Drink."

The word cut clean.

"This is an order."

A flicker of fear surfaced before discipline crushed it.

"I don't understand why you are treating me this way. I have served the Prime Minister faithfully for years."

The maid said tightly.

Medea regarded her without emotion.

"So did many traitors."

She knocked the tea canister from the table.

It shattered.

Leaves scattered across the floor, their odor overpowering.

Medea stood.

In her past life, she had heard the report delivered like an inconvenience.

Prime Minister Siseo has taken his own life. The strain broke him.

No history.

No warning.

No note.

Only madness.

She looked at the maid now—the hands, the precision, the calm.

"Hallucinogenic compounds. Slow accumulation. Indistinguishable from stress."

Medea said softly.

The maid's breath hitched.

"You didn't cure his headaches. You hollowed him out."

Silence.

Medea stepped closer.

"It is fortunate that you came to me before you finished the work."

The maid finally broke.

"Who… who sent you?"

Medea smiled—not kindly.

"That is what we're about to discuss."

The door closed.

And the tea cooled, untouched.
