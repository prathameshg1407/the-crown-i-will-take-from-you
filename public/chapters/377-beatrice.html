Chapter 42

Beatrice

"In new life, the wounds of the past find their healing."

"Your Highness! Push harder!"

A midwife's urgent cry echoed from the maternity ward atop the central temple.

Medea squeezed her eyes shut. Cold sweat traced down her temples.

Having endured this agony in her past life, she felt no fear or dread now.

No—compared to the past, when she'd suffered alone in extreme isolation during her husband's remarriage, or during the expedition to slay the dragon—this moment felt like an undeserved blessing.

"If you can't bear it, scream! Just a little longer! Hold on just a bit more!"

Despite the midwife's encouragement, Medea couldn't comply.

And for good reason.

"Damn it, do something—she's in pain!"

The man standing beside her, clutching her hand, was on the verge of losing his mind entirely.

"Cesare, I'm fine..."

"No. I'm sorry, Dea. Don't—don't say that."

Cesare's expression twisted with anguish before he forcibly smoothed it, attempting to reassure her.

"It's all right. I'll be by your side. Don't be afraid."

He gripped her hand tightly with one of his own while kissing her sweat-dampened temple again and again, murmuring comfort.

But Medea knew it was he, not she, who was truly terrified.

"I've already administered holy water and healing potions. Anything more could endanger both mother and child."

The Druid-born healer finally laid eyes on the infamous "Black Devil" of the continent this evening.

But even he turned out to be human after all.

The man looked ready to collapse—eyes bloodshot, trembling with barely suppressed panic, as though on the verge of a complete breakdown.

"You're doing beautifully! Just one more push!"

"Ngh..."

Sweat poured down Medea's face.

The man beside her was hypersensitive as a cornered animal—his expression turning savage every time Medea swallowed a moan, as though his own life were being carved away.

"Cesare, please—get a hold of yourself. You're being too aggressive..."

Get out.

Only when Medea issued that banishment threat did Cesare—terrified of being driven from her presence—forcibly suppress his hysteria.

WAAAAAH!

At last, the child cried out—loud and strong.

"Your Highness, it's a princess! She's very brave and absolutely beautiful!"

The midwife smiled warmly and placed the precious bundle in Medea's arms.

Medea lifted her gaze. A small, warm weight settled against her chest, pressing directly on her heart.

She drew a deep, shuddering breath.

"Oh... my baby..."

For ten months, she'd carried this child. For ten months, she'd wondered countless times: What will your face look like?

If I see traces of the two children I lost in your features, will I smile with joy—or will I be consumed by guilt?

"Hello, my baby."

Without realizing it, tears streamed down Medea's face.

"Cesare, look..."

She felt the warmth of his tears reaching her own.

"Do you see? The baby is watching us."

It was hard to believe the child had just entered the world mere moments ago—her eyes were so bright and clear.

Black hair, exactly like Cesare's. Brilliant green eyes, exactly like Medea's.

The sight of her gazing at the world while wringing her tiny hands made them both fall in love instantly.

Cesare couldn't speak.

Countless words rose to his mind, but none could fully express what he felt in this moment.

The child of himself and Medea.

A piece carved from both their hearts.

His first complete family—where he, who had belonged nowhere, could finally take root.

The Name

"Your Majesty, please choose the baby's name!"

Madame Tolin urged excitedly.

Medea looked up at Cesare. She understood perfectly why he seemed at a loss for words.

"Cheche, you decide."

Medea's whispered nickname—reserved only for her deepest affection—tickled his ear.

Cesare blinked as though waking from a dream, then carefully placed his hand near the child's tiny fingers.

"Beatrice."

His low voice trembled at the end, then fell clear and certain.

"Beatrice would be perfect."

"Blessing... what a wonderful name. It's befitting of Your Majesties' child."

Madame Tolin immediately grasped the meaning and gasped in delight.

"Beatrice."

Medea met her daughter's clear green eyes.

"Welcome to the world, little one. Welcome to Mom and Dad."

Meanwhile, in Valdina

Valdina Imperial Palace

A bright, crisp wind—characteristic of the north—swept through the palace corridors.

"Good morning, Commander!"

As D'Angel entered the palace halls, muscular guardsmen were scattered here and there, grimacing and nursing sore backs.

D'Angel waved cheerfully in greeting before turning the corner.

After navigating the winding, maze-like corridors for some time, he pushed open a door without hesitation.

"Oy, Sissair! Come out before you're crushed to death by paperwork!"

The spacious office D'Angel entered was buried under mountains of documents.

As expected of the Prime Minister's quarters—notoriously the busiest office in Valdina Palace—there seemed no room to breathe.

"What brings you all the way to this remote administrative wing, Commander?"

A young man surrounded by paper stacks raised his head.

He was strikingly handsome, even with spectacles catching the lamplight and obscuring part of his face.

The sharp acuity revealed through his slightly furrowed brow was actually rather captivating.

"I heard from your aide you haven't gone home all week—working overtime again. Take it easy! What's the point of living if you're obsessed with work?"

You need fresh air, flowers, maybe meet some women, right?

D'Angel was genuinely worried.

How many women in Valdina have their eyes on this one man? Even D'Angel, who knew him well, was constantly receiving requests to arrange introductions.

'What are you going to do if you waste your prime years hunched over a desk with that stubborn workaholic attitude?'

But D'Angel also understood, on some level, why Sissair was so consumed by state affairs—why he had no time for socializing.

'It's because of Her Majesty Medea.'

Ever since returning from Katzen a year ago, that damned workaholic tendency had only intensified.

"People tend to become more extreme when they taste a brief moment of hope—only to have it snatched away."

At D'Angel's observation, Sissair immediately lowered his gaze back to the documents.

"I'll manage myself, Commander. You do whatever you like—you're already doing that well enough."

Despite the cold response, D'Angel ignored it and shoved aside the pile of documents in front of Sissair.

Papers fluttered through the air. Sissair's brow furrowed deeply.

But D'Angel was well accustomed to that expression.

"Hah! If you're not going to meet women anyway, why don't you train with me instead?"

"D'Angel, don't tease him so much."

Peleus, passing by the office, offered Sissair a lifeline.

"Fate doesn't happen just because you want it to. It only comes when the person involved desires it."

The young emperor—who seemed as cold as the northern wind—actually felt quite sorry for Sissair.

Perhaps he'd stirred up someone who'd been living contentedly and sent him wandering after something unattainable.

But the commander of the Agemas, Valdina's strongest steel knight, pretended not to hear even this kindness.

"What would Your Majesty know? Sissair isn't even the real problem right now!"

D'Angel's target shifted.

He set down the mountain of documents and placed both hands on his hips.

"Even if Sissair has a father managing his household, Your Majesty must find a consort! Are you going to leave the position of this empire's mistress vacant forever?!"

Silence.

"I'm not the only one worried! Marquis Gillifort can't sleep at night, terrified his funeral will come before Your Majesty's wedding!"

Peleus took a step backward.

He realized too late that he'd accidentally lit a fuse while trying to help his beloved subject.

But that didn't mean he could escape D'Angel's bombardment.

"Your Majesty should have children like rabbits by now—but you don't have any!"

D'Angel couldn't find even a shadow of romance near his lord.

He snorted like an indignant rhinoceros.

"Aren't you going to marry?! Who else is there in this royal family besides Your Majesty? If something happens to you, who will lead this great nation?! This is an emergency!"

"Why single me out? We have Via."

Peleus protested quietly.

Medea's one-year-old daughter, Beatrice, was greatly beloved by her uncle Peleus.

Where had he heard the superstition that accumulating good deeds would earn the goddess's blessings upon a child?

It had all started during the month Princess Beatrice was born—a joint operation between three kingdoms: the Facade, the Knights Templar, and the Agemas.

The allied forces annihilated a monster wave in the eastern continent and eradicated entire species of beasts.

Of course, the child's father had also firmly believed in the superstition and cooperated with utmost dedication.

It was the first time the normally standoffish brothers-in-law had truly united as one.
