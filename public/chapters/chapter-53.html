"Fighting alone doesn't make you innocent."

The Observer

"It's funny," Cesare said quietly, gazing at the wreckage of the hall beyond the open doors.

Gallo sheathed his dagger with a click.

"What is?"

"Out of everyone in there," Cesare replied, "not a single one is truly protecting that girl."

He replayed the moment Etienne charged.

Medea hadn't screamed.

Hadn't searched the room for a savior.

She had simply tightened her grip on the fan, calculating the angle of a killing blow.

Like a fledgling bird

standing against a storm,

wings broken but eyes clear.

She knows there's no one coming for her, Cesare thought. Just like I did.

The Imperial Palace rose in his memory—corridors full of smiling knives, tutors who watched him like prey.

Powerless. Isolated. Forced to survive with nothing but instinct and brutality.

That same awareness lived behind Medea's calm gaze.

The Objection

Gallo scoffed.

"Boss, your sense of pity is twisted. You do realize she engineered that entire disaster, right?"

"I do."

"The Minister losing his mind. The Claudio brats getting crushed. That was all her. She didn't stumble into chaos—she authored it."

Gallo shook his head.

"She's not a victim. She's the villain. If you said you were interested because she's pretty, I'd get it. But pity? She doesn't need it."

Cesare's voice dropped.

"Fighting alone doesn't make you innocent."

"It just makes you lonely."

Gallo clicked his tongue.

"Next time, keep your loneliness to yourself. I don't like being called a wild dog. My pride took damage."

Cesare flexed his fingers.

"Should I kill you to restore it?"

"My lord!"

The Shadow Reports

Zeta dropped from the tree above them, landing soundlessly.

"Report."

"The Book of the Sage isn't in the Royal Library. Intelligence suggests Regent Claudio took it when he left the palace."

Cesare's eyes sharpened.

"The Regent has it."

"Yes. We couldn't confirm more. The knights withdrew early—apparently the banquet ended in… catastrophe."

Cesare smiled faintly.

"Yes. A catastrophe."

He glanced at Gallo.

"You were saying something about being treated like a mutt?"

Gallo snapped to attention.

"I was mistaken! I love being a mutt! Woof!"

A silver dagger thudded into the trunk inches from his head.

"Move," Cesare said. "We're stealing a book."

The Next Day

A Salon in District One

Rain battered the windows, but inside, the atmosphere burned with gossip.

"Did you see the banquet?"

"I heard Etienne was naked!"

"I saw it. With my own eyes."

Fans snapped open and closed.

"And what he said to Samon—'You're pretty too.' The rumors were true, then. The Minister prefers men."

"Disgusting."

"The Regent tried to silence it this morning."

"Too late. You can't smother a fire once the city smells smoke."

The focus shifted—inevitably—to the Claudios.

"Birna looked like a chandelier."

"Diamonds everywhere. No restraint."

"And she took the Princess's dress. Demanded it, they say."

"The nerve. Her father is Regent, not King."

"And then she was crushed by a naked man. Honestly? Poetic."

"I heard she screamed at the servants helping her."

"Tacky to the end."

The verdict was unanimous.

For years, Medea had been the kingdom's convenient fool.

Now she was the dignified victim—

and the Claudios, the grasping pretenders.

"She wore black for the soldiers," a young noblewoman sighed. "That is a Royal heart."

The Duke's Estate

"Why is this happening?!"

Joaquin Claudio paced his study like a caged beast.

"They're mocking us! My children were attacked, and the city laughs!"

"Your Excellency," an aide murmured, "it may be wise to remain inside for a time."

Joaquin slammed the door.

The walls felt closer every hour.

Birna's Bedroom

"I can't go out!"

"I can't ever go out again!"

Birna screamed into her pillow, sobs wracking her body.

The dress tearing.

The wine soaking her skin.

Etienne's hands—

The wine.

Her breath hitched.

"He drank it,"

she whispered.

"The wine I drugged."

The realization struck like ice.

I did this.

"My reputation… no one will marry me," she wailed. "I'm ruined!"

The Mother's Venom

Catherine stroked her hair, face carved from frost.

"It isn't your fault," she said softly.

Birna looked up.

"Then whose?"

Catherine's eyes hardened.

"Hers."

"Medea?"

"If she had worn the dress," Catherine hissed, "if she had been obedient—none of this would have happened."

She did not acknowledge Birna's disobedience.

Nor the poison.

In her mind, guilt had already chosen its vessel.

"She used you to elevate herself. She made us look monstrous so she could look noble."

Catherine stood, resolve crystallizing into something cold and dangerous.

"Don't worry, Birna. Mother will fix this."

Her lips curved—not in comfort, but in promise.

"That girl will pay

for every tear you shed."

Threads Tighten

Conspiracy Blooms in Darkness

A princess walks alone.
A mercenary sees himself in her eyes.
And vengeance sharpens its blade in the shadows.
