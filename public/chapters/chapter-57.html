The Minister's Estate

"Umberto! Send the letter! Tell Duke Claudio!"

Etienne's screams echoed through the foyer as the Royal Knights dragged him away.

Umberto stood amid the chaos, clutching the sealed parchment in his hand.

The crest stared back at him—the bloated toad of House Etienne.

Slowly, calmly, he walked to the fireplace.

The parchment touched flame.

The wax seal melted first, dripping like fat.

The paper curled, blackened,

and collapsed into ash.

"Good luck, Master," Umberto murmured.

A knight barked, "You! Stop moving! Everyone to the courtyard!"

Umberto raised his hands obediently.

As the servants were herded together, fear and confusion swallowing the hall, he blended into the crowd.

By the time the names were counted, he was gone.

The Duke's Estate

Because the letter never arrived, the truth reached the Regent far too late.

"Arrested?!"

Claudio hurled a porcelain vase across the room.

Crash!

"How did Siseo move so fast?" he snarled. "Why wasn't I informed?"

Samon stood rigid.

"The Royal Knights acted under military authority. Not corruption charges—treason."

"Selling children to foreign powers during wartime," Samon said grimly. "If proven, it's a death sentence."

Claudio's knees weakened. He collapsed into his chair.

He could have stopped this.

If he hadn't mocked Siseo's 'madness.'

If he hadn't let pride blind him.

If he hadn't wanted Etienne frightened, humiliated, dependent.

"I told myself I was letting Siseo dig his own grave,"

"…Instead, he dug mine."

Samon leaned forward.

"We have to get Etienne out. If he breaks, Father, he will name us. The rebel funding, the shadow accounts—everything."

"The palace is sealed," Claudio muttered. "Pinatelli purged our judges. Our messengers are gone."

Samon's eyes hardened.

"There is one path left," he said quietly.

"We still have Medea."

The Princess's Palace

"Medea! Disaster has struck!"

Claudio burst into her study, clutching a handkerchief to reddened eyes.

"Minister Etienne has fallen into a vile trap! Enemies of the Crown seek to destroy a loyal servant!"

He slammed a document onto her desk.

"A full pardon. Your Royal Seal can end this injustice at once."

Medea looked at the parchment.

Once, she would have signed it without reading.

Once, she would have believed him.

How small I was,

she thought.

She slid the document back.

"Uncle," she said gently, "I don't understand slush funds or military law."

Claudio relaxed—just slightly.

"But I've learned something else."

She twisted her signet ring slowly.

"Madame Quiggin. Minister Etienne. Even my maids. Everyone stole from me while I lay broken."

Her green eyes lifted, clear and cold.

"You once said the Royal Seal can do anything."

"If that's true… why should I give it away for free?"

Claudio blinked.

"Free? Medea, this is mercy. Justice."

"Mercy doesn't buy dresses," she replied flatly. "Go back, Uncle."

She turned her chair away.

"Medea—wait."

Claudio stared at her back.

Is this greed?

Or awakening?

"How much?" he asked finally, voice tight.

Medea smiled.

"Well… I hear gold bars are stored in chests now. Standard size."

She scribbled a number and slid it across the desk.

Claudio's breath caught.

"Thirty chests?!"

"That's extortion!"

"Is it?" Medea shrugged. "Etienne stole millions. If my brother returns, he loses his head and his fortune. Thirty chests seems merciful."

"But thirty—"

"Take it or leave it."

Silence stretched.

The girl before him was no longer gentle prey.

She was a creditor.

"…Fine," Claudio said hoarsely. "I'll speak to his family."

"Oh, I expect exactly thirty," Medea chirped. "I know the numbers. Don't try to short me."

She stood and patted his arm, affectionate as ever.

"Uncle, squeeze him dry. You're on my side, aren't you?"

Claudio left the room feeling as though

a kitten had sunk claws into his throat.

Medea's smile faded the moment the door closed.

"Thirty chests," she murmured to Neril.

"That should restore the Royal Treasury nicely."

Her fan snapped shut.

"And Etienne,"

she added softly,

"will still hang."
