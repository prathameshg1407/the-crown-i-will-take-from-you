Chapter 276

The Weight of
Broken Vows

"Some bonds cannot be severed—and some sins can never be undone."

Then he saw her emerge from the Chamber of Judgment.

Medea.

Perhaps she caught the faint whisper of her name escaping his lips, for Medea looked up.

Jason stepped toward her without conscious thought.

But the moment her green eyes locked onto his, he froze mid-stride.

There was fire in those impassive eyes—cold flame, directed squarely at him.

She spoke not a single word, yet Jason felt the lash of shame crack across his soul as surely as any whip.

No excuse could save him. He knew that with terrible clarity. There was no escape from his own transgressions.

Thud.

His knees struck the stone floor as though his body had simply given up the pretense of standing.

Where did it all go wrong? At what point could I have turned back?

No matter how desperately he tried to rationalize, to deny—none of it mattered now. The water had been spilled. There was no retrieving it.

"Medea... I... I too..."

And yet he had to explain. Had to make her understand.

Why did I do what I did? Why did I have to?

His hand reached toward her.

In what felt like an eternity, Medea's eyes widened.

Then her gaze shifted—slid away from him. Those trembling green eyes were pointed in his direction, yet they focused on something beyond, something he could not see.

And then, as though all strength had drained from her body, she swayed and collapsed.

"Your Highness!"

"Medea!"

Before Jason could react, a powerful arm swept in and caught her.

Cesare.

He gathered Medea's crumpling form into his arms with fluid grace, moving with the lethal precision of a hunting leopard. In a single motion, he drew her away—creating distance so absolute that not even Jason's fingertips or shadow could reach her.

Shielded completely by his broad frame, only a cascade of silver hair remained visible.

Jason stood transfixed.

The sight of Cesare holding Medea without a single gap between them—so completely, so protectively—struck him harder than watching their wedding ceremony had.

Face contorted with fury and helplessness, he raised bloodshot eyes.

"Step aside! Shouldn't someone examine her condition first? You're putting her in danger—"

"Remove him."

Cesare's voice emerged flat, emotionless. He didn't even turn to look at Jason as he issued the command to his men.

Cradling Medea gently, he began to walk away.

Fire blazed in his golden eyes.

"Release me! Let go! I must see Medea! This is my responsibility—this happened because of me!"

Jason struggled against the hands restraining him, desperation bleeding into his voice.

"If Medea collapsed at the sight of me, it means her feelings for me haven't completely vanished!"

A glimmer of hope flickered in his eyes, tangled with shame and anguish.

The opposite of love is not hatred—it's indifference. Dea, you still love me.

The moment the truth of his past life had been revealed, Medea had lost consciousness before his eyes.

That violent reaction—surely it proved she was still moved by him?

"You understand nothing!"

At Jason's shout, Cesare halted mid-stride.

"You don't know—you cannot possibly sever the bond between us!"

"Gallo."

Cesare glanced back over his shoulder, his tone as languid as ever.

"Are you going to let him keep barking?"

Though his words were casual, Gallo recognized instantly that they had reached the edge of something dangerous.

"What? Oh—yes, of course! Leave him to me!"

"Unhand me! Medea! I need to be with her! I will make her—urgh!"

Jason lunged after Cesare but was caught fast by his men.

"What's gotten into you lately, Grand Duke? Have you grown tired of living?"

Gallo's cheerful tone belied the threat beneath.

"You seem to have forgotten all those days you spent gasping for air underwater. And now you don't even recognize when you're being spared."

"Ngh—!"

"If you're going to bark, at least clean your neck first."

Gallo stuffed a handkerchief into Jason's mouth as he writhed.

A blade slid from his sleeve, catching the dim light with a cold gleam.

The chill in Gallo's smiling eyes made Jason go still.

"Shh. We have quite a bit of history between us, don't we? Let's not make a scene. It wouldn't do to let the squealing of pigs echo through the sacred temple."

Silenced and shamed, Jason could only watch as his wife was carried away in another man's arms.

The Katzen Prince's Residence

Terence straightened from beside the bed, vials and cloths still in hand.

"There's some form of energy in conflict within her body. The Princess isn't ill, per se—rather, this appears to be a side effect of her system struggling to adapt to an unfamiliar power."

On the bed before him, Medea lay motionless, eyes closed.

"It's not sorcery. As far as I can determine, it's divine power—though I cannot say for certain."

Terence had been present in the Chamber of Judgment when the sacred relics revealed Medea as the true hidden Saintess of the Holy Kingdom.

And yet, this wasn't quite the power of a saint...

"There's a strange energy interwoven with the holy power. It doesn't follow the patterns I'm familiar with... But I lack the expertise to identify it precisely. Perhaps my master could..."

Cesare's brow furrowed slightly.

Terence's teacher—the Magic Tower Lord—had departed for the Tower immediately after the wedding.

"He rarely ventures beyond the Tower walls. Having walked abroad for the first time in ages, he'll likely remain sequestered for quite some time."

A commotion erupted beyond the door.

Moments later, Peleus swept in at the head of the Valdina delegation.

The instant he caught sight of his sister lying pale upon the bed, he strode forward.

"What happened to Dea?"

The faint scent of blood clung to him—he had just finished capturing the warlocks who had attempted to flee the Central Temple.

"We exposed the false Saintess, so why has this happened? What does it mean that the sacred relic reacted to Dea?"

"Please remain calm, Your Majesty. It appears the power dormant within Her Highness has temporarily surged beyond her control."

Terence provided a measured account of the sequence of events and the conflicting energies now warring inside Medea's body.

"The symptoms resemble the mana runaway a knight experiences during aura awakening. Fortunately, judging by the nature and flow of this energy, it doesn't appear to be malevolent toward Her Highness."

"Haah..."

Peleus pressed his hand to his forehead and squeezed his eyes shut.

Why can't this child ever know peace?

No sooner had she unmasked the false Saintess than some mysterious power had overwhelmed her.

Gazing at his sister's ashen face, he felt only sorrow.

"...Why did the First Prince allow matters to deteriorate to this point?"

A voice cut through from behind.

"We entrusted Her Highness to you in the belief that the First Prince would keep her safe. Yet barely half a day has passed, and here she lies."

Lamplight glinted off the speaker's spectacles, masking his expression entirely.

The criticism that followed was ice-cold and merciless.

"The bold promises you made have already been shattered here in the Holy Kingdom. Can Her Highness truly expect safety in Katzen?"

"Lord Sissair."

"If you had revealed your true nature earlier, this marriage would never have taken place. And yet you concealed it until the very end."

Even Peleus turned to stare at Sissair in surprise.

Whatever the circumstances, he hadn't expected the Prime Minister—a man who prided himself on cold rationality—to attack a foreign power, let alone the imperial family, with such venom.

"Were you hiding your true intentions all along? Or do you mean to cast off your mask of civility now that the ceremony is complete?"

"Sir, isn't this rather excessive? Do you imagine His Highness the First Prince to be omniscient? Could anyone have predicted Her Highness's awakening?"

Sissair's relentless accusations drew anger even from the normally composed Terence.

"Or shall we blame His Highness's notorious fate? Since all those close to him have perished, perhaps that ominous aura has now transferred itself to Her Highness?"

The air in the chamber crystallized with tension. Terence's glare could have cut steel.

"Prime Minister Sissair. The alliance and solidarity we sought with Valdina did not include tolerating insults to my lord."

But Sissair's eyes weren't on Terence.

They were fixed on Cesare.

"I wonder. One person's sincerity doesn't always receive equal measure in return."

His voice was calm, but the sharpened hostility beneath was unmistakable.

"Isn't that so, Your Highness the First Prince?"

"...You seem to have much you wish to say."

The First Prince's golden eye met Sissair's gaze, then flickered briefly toward Medea's still form.

"Let us speak outside."

He silenced Terence's attempt to follow with a subtle shake of his head, then turned and walked from the room.

The Reckoning

The corridor's chill air hung heavy around them.

The space where the two men now faced each other felt colder still.

"Is there something particular you wish to discuss with me, Your Highness the First Prince?"

A vivid sneer threaded through Sissair's measured tone, spreading like poison through the darkened air.
