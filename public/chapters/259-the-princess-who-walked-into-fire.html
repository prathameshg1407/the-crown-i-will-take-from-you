Chapter 259

The Princess Who
Walked Into Fire

"In the temple of divine judgment, a woman appears like light through darkness—and changes everything."

The King of Mycenae was the eldest ruler present in the Great Temple, a man whose experience stretched across decades of continental politics. His white beard and weathered face commanded respect from every monarch and ambassador gathered at the round table.

"Emperor of Valdina, if you continue to reject the revelation given by the Goddess herself, this situation will only deteriorate further."

His voice carried the weight of authority earned through long years of leadership.

"Are you truly prepared to watch the entire continent be sacrificed—all because of blood ties?"

'A fine young man, caught in winds far too fierce for his years.'

The King of Mycenae actually felt genuine pity for Peleus. Of all the rulers demanding Medea's surrender, he was among the few who still wished to save the young Emperor if possible.

"We have no desire to put you on trial, Emperor. We do not wish to bring Valdina down and sow further chaos across these already troubled lands."

He extended his hand across the table—a gesture both literal and symbolic.

"All we seek is the safety and peace of the continent. If you would simply give us the Princess—"

"No matter how many times you ask, my answer remains unchanged."

Peleus sat perfectly straight, his gaze fixed on a point beyond the assembled rulers. Even under the crushing pressure of their collective judgment, his noble bearing remained utterly intact.

"My sister is innocent. She cannot possibly be the cause of this disaster. Therefore, I formally request that the oracle's true meaning be examined and confirmed once more."

His voice resonated with unshakeable conviction.

"You shameless fool!"

"This is madness! Are you saying you'll simply stand by and watch hundreds of thousands of people across the continent be slaughtered?!"

The assembled rulers' anger erupted like a dam breaking. Voices overlapped in furious denunciation.

"Emperor of Valdina—do you truly wish for us to march upon your lands?"

The King of Mycenae's patience, stretched thin by the young Emperor's stubborn refusal, finally snapped.

"Are you now prepared to turn the entire continent into your enemy? Should I understand this to mean you're confident Valdina can withstand the combined might of every kingdom here assembled?"

The atmosphere in the chamber reached a fever pitch—excitement and terror mixing into something combustible.

Discussions erupted about putting Peleus himself on trial. About declaring war on Valdina and forcing compliance with the oracle.

The Agemas—Valdina's elite royal guard—sensed the murderous intent filling the temple. Their hands never left their sword hilts. The air itself seemed to crackle with the promise of violence.

A bloody standoff materialized: Valdina and its handful of guards on one side, the combined kingdoms of the continent on the other.

It wouldn't have been surprising if someone had drawn steel and turned the sacred temple into a battlefield.

Then Peleus spoke, and his voice cut through the chaos like a blade through silk.

"The problems that human beings can solve are never truly disasters or divine wrath."

Cool. Calm. Utterly composed.

"You need not worry. I swear on my honor and my crown that the demons will not ravage your lands."

"Are you claiming that Valdina will take full responsibility for suppressing the monster waves?"

"Valdina's soldiers have already been dispatched to multiple kingdoms—including Molossia and Mycenae. And I myself will take to the front lines to stop the demons."

His voice left no room for doubt or negotiation.

"If the only way to prevent disaster according to the oracle's will is through my sister's life—"

Silence fell across the temple like snow.

"Then I will gladly offer my own body to prevent that disaster instead."

The Sacred Vow

Sunlight streaming through the temple's high windows brushed against the Emperor's silver hair, illuminating him as though with a divine halo. A noble and sacred aura—reminiscent of the paladins of old—radiated from his very presence.

Rumors of Peleus had spread across the continent long before this moment. They called him the"Holy Slayer"—the young Emperor who had personally subdued the fierce nomadic tribes of the northern plains through both martial prowess and unwavering conviction.

Now this same Emperor offered himself as collateral to free his sister from the oracle's judgment.

His words carried the weight of an absolute decree. He did not evade his responsibility as a monarch or as a brother. In the face of endless demonic hordes threatening the continent, he spoke not a declaration but a sacred will.

Even those who had been shouting the harshest accusations fell silent.

'What is this? You must be desperate, scrambling to find a way to avoid a clean death.'

Saint Rachel forced the trembling corners of her lips upward into a smile.

Peleus absolutely could not be allowed to take Medea's place. The false oracle she'd created demanded the Princess specifically—substitution would unravel everything.

"Everyone, please—calm yourselves. Now that I consider it more carefully, this situation doesn't seem to reflect His Majesty's will at all."

Rachel's voice rang out sweet and clear, drawing every eye.

"The Princess of Valdina fled on her own to escape the oracle and preserve her life. His Majesty is merely trying to protect his sister's location through this... desperate sacrifice."

She widened her beautiful eyes as though the realization had only just struck her.

"But how could such a brilliant woman not know the trouble her actions would bring to her only brother and her beloved country?"

Rachel let out a delicate sigh of regret.

"Ah... why would someone of such noble imperial blood make such a selfish choice?"

The Saint's single observation reignited the dying embers in the hearts of the assembled rulers.

"So while we die by the thousands every day, she hides herself away to save her own skin?!"

"We need to track her down immediately! Search every corner of the continent!"

"Who claimed that Valdina's bloodline was righteous and upright? Nothing but cowardly fugitives!"

The tension that had briefly dissipated flared back to life with renewed intensity.

Peleus's eyes, watching the atmosphere turn increasingly hostile toward his sister, went cold as winter steel.

"Who—"

That single word hung in the air.

And then:

"Did you say she ran away?"

A clear voice cut through the marble and stone of the temple like light through darkness.

"Valdina has never backed down from adversity. Neither has Valdina's daughter."

Every head turned as one. Every gaze focused on a single point.

Bam.

A woman walked with regal bearing between the massive white marble pillars that supported the main shrine.

One step. One step.

The sound of her footsteps echoed through the sacred space—quiet, measured, yet utterly impossible to ignore.

With every step, her lustrous silver hair cascaded down her shoulders, catching the sunlight and transforming it into a mysterious, almost otherworldly halo.

Her green eyes sparkled like spring meadows touched by morning dew.

"The Princess of Valdina...?"

Someone whispered in disbelief. The tremor spread through the assembly as though even the air itself had stopped flowing.

Sunlight poured between the pure white pillars, enveloping Medea in radiance so complete it seemed staged by divine hands.

As if by the Goddess's own blessing, this great temple appeared to have been prepared specifically for her arrival.

Questions too blasphemous to speak aloud crossed the minds of those gathered:

Is this woman truly the one the Goddess has appointed as the bride of an evil dragon?

Could the oracle have been mistaken? Could the Goddess have chosen the wrong person?

If she is destined to be a sacrifice to darkness... why, at this very moment, does she seem closer to divinity than any of us?

"I am Medea de Valdina."

Her voice rang clear as a bell.

"I would rather cast off the chains the Goddess has placed upon me with my own hands than flee from my fate like a coward."

"..."

The chamber erupted in shocked murmurs.

High Priest Paul rose abruptly from his seat, his face flushed with indignation.

"Truly bold words, Your Highness! But you failed to show your face until the continent descended into chaos. Isn't that exactly how this catastrophe came about?"

'That witch dazzles people's eyes even at the very end!'

Paul thought her radiant brilliance was nothing more than a dirty trick—manipulation dressed as divine favor. He turned sharply toward Pope Benedict.

"In any case, the matter is settled. Since the subject of the oracle has arrived, Your Holiness, please proceed immediately to fulfill the Goddess's command."

He wouldn't be able to rest until the Princess of Valdina was delivered into the dragon's lair—her fate sealed, his conspiracy secured.

But before he could press the point further:

"High Priest Paul."

The special envoy from Katzen stepped forward with measured deliberation.

"No matter how urgent this situation may be, is it truly appropriate for a priest to display such a harsh attitude toward someone who has accepted the oracle without protest or evasion?"

The speaker was Count de Montfort—father of Katzen's Empress Dowager and one of the most powerful nobles on the continent.

Standing beside him was Marquis Eratosthenes, who like Pope Benedict had remained silent throughout the proceedings until this moment.

All of Emperor Perdiccas II's most trusted advisors had assembled as special envoys to this council.

"At this point, High Priest, I'm beginning to wonder whether you're genuinely concerned with the oracle—or if you're simply trying to corner Her Highness the Princess for your own purposes."

"Mind your words, Count! Do you dare insult the priesthood in the very presence of the Goddess?!"

Even facing High Priest Paul's fury, Count de Montfort—a nobleman from the powerful empire of Katzen—showed not the slightest sign of intimidation.

But while outwardly he appeared to be defending Medea, his eyes gleamed with the calculating brightness of a predator sizing up prey.

"As a loyal subject of Katzen, I am merely conveying His Imperial Majesty's message."

Count de Montfort had received specific instructions from Emperor Perdiccas II before this council.

"His Majesty does not consent to this oracle."

Gasps echoed through the chamber.

"Furthermore, His Majesty is the guardian who made a sacred promise to protect Her Highness the Princess. He cannot—will not—break that oath by sending her to her death."

The implications hit like a thunderclap.

If Katzen and Valdina—the two greatest empires on the continent—were to unite in opposition, even the combined pressure of every other kingdom would be insufficient to enforce the oracle.

"However..."

Count de Montfort paused, letting the weight of that single word settle over the assembly.

"We cannot protect Her Highness blindly. While considering this dilemma, a solution occurred to us that might allow us to overcome this difficulty."

The moment those words left his mouth, Medea's hands clenched into fists. Her green eyes went cold as winter frost.

"I wonder if there might be another reason why Valdina remains a protected land. The Philosopher's Stone, for instance."

He let the name hang in the air like bait on a hook.

"If we could locate this legendary artifact and share its protective power with the entire continent, I'm certain we could overcome this crisis without needing to fulfill the oracle at all."

Rachel ground her teeth together, completely blindsided by this development.

She'd had no idea about Emperor Perdiccas II's obsession with the Philosopher's Stone.

'Why is Katzen interfering now?!'
