Ambient: Divinity
Three Days of Suffering

The
Vigil

"Secrets in a palace are like smoke"

The Queen Mother silenced the palace that day.

The accusations.

The confessions.

The blood dragged across marble floors.

All buried under threat of treason.

But secrets in a palace were like smoke—no matter how tightly sealed, they always found a crack.

By sunset, whispers had already begun.

"She's still kneeling."

"They say the Queen Mother ordered her to remain until the Goddess speaks."

"The Goddess? She'll grow old on that stone."

"This isn't penance. It's humiliation."

Medea heard them.

She felt their gazes pressing into her back like needles. A princess—undebuted, unwed—forced to kneel in public, watched by soldiers and servants alike.

It was shameful.

And that was exactly why it mattered.

If I bow now, she will forgive me… and forget everything else.

eyes fixed on the Goddess's stone face

The injustice.

The cruelty.

The Claudio family's poison.

Forgiveness would close the wound too cleanly.

She needed it to fester.

She needed the Queen Mother to remember.

— Midnight —

The sun sank.

Cold crept in like a living thing.

Autumn wind scoured the courtyard, biting through Medea's thin indoor dress. Her fingers lost feeling. Her knees burned, then numbed.

Neril

Neril knelt before the guards, clutching a wool blanket.

"Please. Just this. She's been here all day."

"The Queen Mother forbade it," a guard replied, unmoved.

"If the Princess wants comfort, she must ask forgiveness."

Neril's hand trembled at her sword.

Then Medea lifted her eyes.

Trust me.

The look was calm. Absolute.

Neril swallowed her fury and stepped back.

Goddess, she prayed silently, staring at the statue. If you have any mercy at all—look at her.

Eventually, the guards dozed.

The courtyard emptied.

Only Medea remained awake.

Her body was numb. Her thoughts drifted in and out like mist. But beneath the exhaustion, her mind stayed razor-clear.

I have turned the Queen Mother against the Regent's servant.

I have exposed their rot.

This pain is not meaningless.

It is payment.

As her vision blurred, moonlight brushed the statue's fingertips.

The stone caught the light—refracting, bending—

And something soft seemed to descend.

Not a voice.

Not warmth.

Just light, faint as breath,

folding around Medea's bowed form like wings.

— Day Three. Noon. —

The sun was merciless.

Heat crashed down like a hammer.

Medea still knelt.

Her lips were cracked.

Her skin pale as parchment.

Her body swayed slightly—

but she did not fall.

Across the palace, unease spread like rot beneath silk.

— The Queen Mother's Chambers —
Queen Mother

"She's still there?"

The Queen Mother paced, nails biting into her palm.

"She hasn't begged. She hasn't cried. She hasn't said a word."

She had expected Medea to break within hours—tearful, desperate, repentant.

Three days had passed.

Baroness Pinatelli

"Your Majesty, the rumors are shifting. People are asking what crime a child could commit to deserve this."

"She's trying to shame me. She wants me to yield."

Her steps faltered.

"Why does everyone look at me like I'm a monster?"

voice cracking

Pinatelli lowered her gaze.

Because you are watching a child suffer… and refusing to stop.

— The Prime Minister's Office —
Count Cesare

Cesare paused at the doorway.

"You changed rooms."

This office faced the courtyard. From the window, the Royal Chapel was clearly visible—and the small figure kneeling before it.

"The light is better here."

Siseo said flatly

Cesare smiled behind his mask.

"Are you worried about the Princess?"

"Unnecessary."

Siseo muttered—then glanced toward the window.

The negotiations were finished. Fasade's mercenaries would remain in Valdina, a silent blade against Imperial interference.

A good deal.

Yet the air felt heavy.

Marquis Gallo

"It's been three days. Even Kazen royals aren't this cruel. Does she want the girl dead?"

"The Princess has grit. More than I expected."

"It's foolish. The Queen Mother left her an exit. If she apologized, this would end. Instead, she's making herself hated."

Cesare laughed softly.

"Is she?"

Siseo looked up.

"When punishment goes too far, hatred rots into guilt."

eyes fixed on the courtyard

"And guilt always seeks absolution."

"She isn't being stubborn. She's waiting."

"For what?"

"For the Queen Mother to break."

A sudden shout echoed outside.

Light erupted in the courtyard—

blinding, unnatural.

"What in the hell—?"

Gallo gasped.

Cesare's smile vanished.

Below them—

The statue of the Goddess

was glowing.

Not blazing.

Not burning.

But shining.

And at its feet, a lone girl knelt—

small, unmoving, wrapped in light.

The palace
held its breath.
