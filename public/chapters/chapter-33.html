The Duke's Estate — Birna's Room

"It isn't fair!"

Birna sat on her bed, fists clenched in the silk sheets, tears streaking her flushed face.

"She took it right out of my hands! That half-blood girl! How could Grandmother humiliate me like that?!"

Catherine pinched the bridge of her nose. Managing the palace was simple. Managing her daughter was exhausting.

"She wants me to call her Princess Medea now. Not 'sister.' She wants me to bow to her!"

"It is a performance. Nothing more."

Catherine said sharply.

"But I don't want to! It should be mine! When Father becomes King, she won't even dare look at me!"

Catherine grabbed her shoulders.

"Do not ever say that again."

Birna froze.

Catherine's eyes were no longer maternal. They were calculating. Cornered.

"Have you said this to anyone else? Friends? Ladies-in-waiting?"

"No! I swear!"

"One word like that, and we are dead."

Catherine hissed, shaking her once, hard.

Birna's breath hitched.

"Smile. Bow. Apologize. And when this is over, I will give you everything."

Birna nodded frantically.

"Good."

The door slammed shut behind Catherine.

The Duke's Study

Joaquin Claudio stood rigid, a crumpled summons burning in the fireplace.

"She called Montega."

Catherine's blood chilled.

"Count Montega? The King's man?"

She whispered.

"He never liked me. If he sides with Medea, she won't be isolated anymore."

"And public opinion is shifting. They pity her now."

Catherine added grimly.

"She was supposed to be the witch. The excuse."

"If they see her as a victim, the rebellion loses its justification."

Catherine said quietly.

Silence.

Then Catherine smiled.

"Then let us remind them who she really is."

"A martyr?"

"No."

She leaned closer.

"A glutton."

"A banquet. Lavish. Extravagant. For Medea's recovery."

Joaquin's eyes gleamed.

"Gold, wine, imported delicacies. Invite Montega. Invite the hungry."

She kissed his cheek.

"Let them watch the Princess feast."

The Minister's Estate

Crash.

Glass shattered against stone.

Etienne's screams echoed through the corridors.

Umberto wiped red wine from his sleeve, exhausted.

"He's worse. Ever since Quiggin died."

A servant whispered.

That night, Umberto found a note.

[ I know your secret. ]

He laughed it off. Burned it.

The next day—

[ Red Fox. ]

His hands shook.

No one knew that name.

On the third day, the note waited for him inside the Minister's office.

Centered. Deliberate.

A cipher.

His cipher.

Umberto staggered back, bile rising.

This wasn't a threat.

It was proof.

Someone wasn't watching.

They were inside.

And whoever they were…

they knew exactly who to bleed next.
