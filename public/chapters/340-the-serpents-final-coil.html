Chapter 340

The Serpent's
Final Coil

"We were only ever using each other. Don't pretend otherwise."

The moment she learned of Medea's pregnancy—the moment she realized the Third Prince had been supplanted—the Western Empress remembered.

She remembered the contemptuous gaze of that man. The way he had turned his back on her like discarding a worn glove, the instant he found a more useful ally.

Eleanor... if it had been that woman, would you have done this to her too?

He should have known. A man so obsessed with the woman he once loved would be infinitely more cruel to one he had never cared for at all.

That belated realization shattered whatever remained of the Western Empress's pride.

Eleanor—foolish, useless Eleanor—was still wiser than I ever was. At least she never trusted him.

The Western Empress bit down on her lip until flesh tore and blood welled dark against her teeth.

No. I will not crumble like this.

"I never imagined I would die here."

The words emerged hollow, spoken to no one.

Footsteps echoed from the darkness.

The Western Empress's head snapped up.

"I thought you might be lonely."

Jason Castulo emerged from the shadows, his smile as soft as candlelight.

"You—! How are you here?!"

All access to the West Palace had been sealed by Perdiccas II's explicit command. The guards were under strict orders—not even a rat should pass through.

Yet here he stood.

"That's..."

Jason paused mid-step and placed one hand against the wall. His fingertips traced the cracks in the ancient stonework, and fine dust motes rose into the air.

"...a secret I've kept for a long time."

A faint smile played across his lips.

"As a child, I used to play hide-and-seek in this very palace. These corridors... the Emperor himself would chase me through them, back when he was still healthy enough to hold a child in his arms."

The Western Empress's shoulders trembled. Even knowing about the secret passages, the sheer cunning of this man—the patience with which he had concealed such knowledge—sent ice crawling down her spine.

The apple offered by such a serpent was always poisoned. And I devoured it without question.

"This is all your doing, isn't it? You—you provoked me into this! You despicable devil!"

Fear and fury tangled in her voice. Her eyes blazed with hatred.

"And now you come crawling back, expecting me to shoulder all the blame? Do you think that will erase what you've done?"

Jason's expression did not waver. The corner of his mouth merely twitched upward.

The Western Empress faltered.

Why doesn't he show any fear? Castulo was always cautious—always calculating. Isn't he afraid of Cesare's wrath?

Does he not know what fate befalls those who dare cross that man?

"What are you—"

The words died in her throat.

Her fingers refused to move. Her limbs had turned to wood.

In a flash of terrible clarity, she remembered the dust rising from the wall—the dust Jason had so casually disturbed with his fingertips.

"You—! Paralysis powder! You cunning—"

But it was already too late.

She could only roll her eyes, watching in frozen horror as Jason withdrew a small vial from his coat and uncorked it with deliberate slowness.

He approached. One step. Two.

The Western Empress's eyes widened, terror flooding through paralyzed veins.

Black liquid spilled past her lips.

"Ngh—! Ja... Jason...!"

"Hush now. This will all be over soon."

His voice was gentle—almost tender—as he produced a folded sheet of paper from within his coat.

He opened it slowly and held it before her dimming eyes.

A suicide note. Written in her own handwriting.

"You'll apologize for your crimes with your death. And what can that bastard Cesare do about it?"

Jason's smile was serene.

"Nothing at all."

"Ugh... ghh..."

Her tongue hardened. Her body crumpled where she stood.

As the paralysis began to fade—too late, far too late—the Western Empress thrashed her arms in one final, desperate motion. Her fingernails raked across the back of Jason's hand, tearing flesh in a deep, ugly gash.

He did not flinch.

He merely waited, patient as stone, until her struggles ceased.

And then—silence.

Her eyes remained open. Fixed. Glassy with disbelief.

Not Cesare. It wasn't Cesare who killed me.

It was Jason Castulo—the man I always dismissed as beneath my notice.

"Don't blame me."

Jason leaned close, his lips brushing against her ear.

"We were only ever using each other. You knew that from the start."

His hands moved with practiced efficiency. A rope wound around her neck, staging the scene he had envisioned.

Then he erased every trace of his presence and melted back into the darkness.

Outside the West Palace, Jason pressed a hand to his face—rough, dry, drained of all warmth.

The scent of the Western Empress's perfume still clung to his fingertips. What had once been sensual now reeked of decay, as foul as a corpse left too long in the sun.

Yet beneath the disgust, something else burned within him. A fury he could not name. A love turned to poison.

It wasn't enough. The plan I prepared for Cesare's return—it wasn't enough.

And now... the pregnancy of the First Princess. The news that has set the entire empire ablaze.

"What will you do now, Medea?"

His crimson eyes gleamed between his fingers like embers refusing to die.

"So this is the man you chose."

Cesare must be extraordinary indeed—for you to abandon me, for you to carry his child.

Very well. If that's how it must be... then I will shatter your faith in him. I will force you to see reality.

He turned on his heel. When he spoke again, his voice was low and inexorable.

"Contact the Temple. Set things in motion."

"Yes, Your Highness."

The palace fell silent once more, as if nothing had ever disturbed its slumber.

The Next Dawn

When the sun rose the following morning—

"Your Majesty? Your Majesty—! AAAAAH—!"

A scream tore through the West Palace, echoing off the empty walls like a death knell.

The First Prince's Palace

The palace was alive with activity for the first time in months.

Knights, mercenaries, maids, attendants, cooks from the kitchens—everyone had gathered in the courtyard to welcome their lord and lady home.

"Your Highnesses! Your Highnesses!"

The First Prince and his wife descended from their carriage. Behind them, servants hastened to unload the mountain of gifts bestowed by the Emperor himself.

"We offer our prayers for your safe return! And our congratulations on the blessing of a royal heir!"

News from the imperial palace had spread like wildfire. The joy among the First Prince's faction was palpable—an almost giddy relief that seemed impossible just months ago, when word of Cesare's disappearance in the frozen north had sent them all into despair.

The couple made their way inside, accepting congratulations at every step.

Medea, in particular, was treated as if she were made of glass. The staff had always been courteous, but now their attentiveness bordered on absurdity—as though she might shatter at the slightest breeze.

"We shall devote ourselves entirely to your care, Your Highness. Please, do not worry about a single thing."

Beneath their earnest smiles, Medea glimpsed something else. A flicker of vindication. Of pride.

They see this child as their victory.

"Welcome home."

Old Mrs. Tolin stood at the entrance, her wrinkled face already streaked with tears.

"Thank you. Thank you, Your Highness. Even if this old woman were to die tomorrow, I would carry this gratitude into the next life."

She clutched Medea's hands, pouring out her joy in an uncharacteristic flood of emotion.

"His Highness has finally found a true family. For so long, he had nowhere to rest his heart—like a reed adrift on the water..."

Mrs. Tolin—always so composed, never a hair out of place—was practically glowing.

"How happy the late Lady Eleanor must be, watching from above... Forgive me, I speak out of turn. You must be exhausted. I'll prepare your bath at once."

At last, Medea found herself alone.

Her reflection in the mirror looked haggard, shadows bruising the hollows beneath her eyes.

Small wonder. The Emperor kept me at the palace far longer than anticipated.

Perdiccas II's sudden interest had been... overwhelming.

"When is the child due?"

"Have you determined the sex?"

"How is your health? The morning sickness isn't too severe, I hope?"

"And what of prenatal education? You must begin at once."

The Emperor's questions had been relentless.

"Excellent. You are no longer merely a daughter of Valdina—you are now a daughter of Katzen."

His tone had dripped with self-satisfaction, as though bestowing some great honor.

"I confess I have been... less than warm toward you in the past. But that will change. How could I be cold to the mother of my only grandchild?"

Unable to win over his son—who had long since grown immune to every manipulation—Perdiccas II had shifted his focus to his more tractable daughter-in-law.

"My dear girl isn't eating? What would you like? Name it—I shall have it found, no matter the cost."

Medea had barely been able to think straight, bombarded on all sides by his suffocating attention.

When his forms of address grew intimate—my dear girl, daughter—her skin had crawled.

I saw it in his eyes. The anticipation. He intends to take this child from me.

It had taken every ounce of her composure not to flinch.

Now, alone at last, Medea pressed a weary hand to her still-flat stomach.

The door opened softly.

Cesare.

Without a word, he crossed the room and wrapped his arms around her from behind.

Then—as if handling something infinitely precious—he pressed a rain of kisses to the crown of her head, her temple, the delicate curve of her neck.

"Medea. You stubborn, impossible woman."

His voice was stern, but his hands told a different story. Long fingers swept gently across her abdomen, tentative. Almost reverent.

"How could you hide this from me?"
