Chapter 291

The Emperor's
Contempt

"A father's cruelty cuts deeper than any blade... especially when wielded against his own blood."

Perdiccas II lounged upon the wide terrace overlooking the Imperial Palace's first-floor gardens, his posture deliberately careless as he sprawled across a gilded chair.

The sweet fragrance of blossoming flowers drifted up from below, carrying with it the warmth of late afternoon sun. It was the sort of day that softened even the harshest temperaments—and today, the Emperor's mood was uncommonly generous.

Thwack!

An arrow embedded itself in a makeshift target at the far end of the manicured lawn.

"A perfect strike! Congratulations, Your Highness!"

The attendant's praise echoed across the garden, though the Emperor's satisfaction stemmed from something far more personal than his son's marksmanship.

"Did you see that, Father?"

Third Prince Juan's voice rang with youthful excitement as he lowered his bow, golden hair catching the sunlight like spun thread.

"Juan grows more like Your Majesty every day."

The Western Empress spoke from her position beside the Emperor, her typically cold features softened by a rare maternal warmth.

"His bearing, his physique—when I look at him now, I cannot help but recall Your Majesty in your youth. That same dignified presence."

It was a masterful stroke of flattery—praising the Emperor while elevating her son in a single breath.

In truth, Juan bore far greater resemblance to his mother's side. He possessed her refined, slender build rather than his father's imposing frame. But dressed in a uniform that mirrored the Emperor's own colors and cut, the illusion of similarity was convincing enough.

He chose those clothes deliberately, of course.

"Nonsense."

Perdiccas II waved a dismissive hand, though his eyes remained fixed on his son with unmistakable pride.

"At his age, I was already a force beyond compare. The boy hasn't even faced a proper pack of lynxes yet."

Something flickered beneath his bluster—a subtle ache at watching his son's vibrant youth, knowing he could never reclaim his own.

"Of course, Father."

Unlike his mother, whose smile had dimmed at the slight, Juan only beamed brighter.

"I am merely a sapling struggling to grow beneath Your Majesty's towering shadow. The great deeds you have accomplished—I would never presume to match them."

Twang!

He drew and released another arrow. This one fell short, dropping limply to the grass before reaching its target.

"You see? Without Your Majesty's guidance, I am hopeless."

This was the crucial difference between the Third Prince and the First.

Where Cesare challenged and defied, Juan knelt and submitted. Where the elder son met his father's gaze with barely concealed rebellion, the younger bowed his head like a loyal hound awaiting its master's approval.

"With a bit more practice, you may yet stop merely imitating me."

The Emperor patted the Western Empress's hand absently, his gruff words at odds with the satisfied smile playing at his lips.

The attendant standing nearby swallowed a quiet sigh.

You were once the wisest of men, Your Majesty. Shrewd beyond measure. Yet now you crave nothing but honeyed words and empty flattery.

It was no secret that the Emperor had been seeking the Western Empress's company with increasing frequency these days.

"Your Majesty."

A subordinate approached, and the chamberlain gave a subtle nod before turning to address the Emperor.

"What is it?"

"His Royal Highness the First Prince and the Crown Princess have arrived."

At the mention of Cesare's name, Perdiccas II's expression soured instantly.

"Tell them to wait. Juan's practice is not yet finished."

"Your Majesty, since the First Prince and his wife have arrived, we shall take our leave."

The Western Empress's smile had vanished, replaced by cold calculation.

So that is why we were summoned to the Imperial Palace today.

The Emperor's games—pitting his sons against each other like fighting cocks—were nothing new. But this particular arrangement presented an opportunity.

Let the Princess witness firsthand how her husband is treated. Let her experience the Emperor's disdain. Perhaps then she will understand her true position in this court.

The humiliation of the coronation ceremony still burned. This was a chance to restore what had been lost.

"But surely this is a momentous occasion—the Crown Princess's first formal audience with Your Majesty."

The Western Empress bowed her head with exaggerated deference.

"The people speak of her as Katzen's great hope. Our humble presence should not obstruct such an important figure."

The barb was subtle but precise—a needle slipped beneath the Emperor's pride.

Juan caught his mother's meaning immediately. His cheerful expression faded as he carefully set down his bow.

"Brother Cesare has arrived. How could I possibly keep him waiting?"

Perdiccas II's face darkened.

"You will stay! Did I not say there was more to teach you?"

The Third Prince retrieved his bow immediately, eyes wide with carefully performed fear—as though his father's word were absolute law, superseding even filial duty to his elder brother.

Yes. Juan is different.

The Emperor studied his younger son's submissive posture, his eager-to-please expression.

Look at those frightened eyes, gazing up at me with such reverence. He fears Cesare, perhaps—but he places his father's commands above all else.

A drop of dark satisfaction seeped into the Emperor's heart.

Perdiccas II turned to the chamberlain, his voice cold.

"Inform the First Prince that I am occupied. What manner of elder brother cannot spare a moment's patience while his younger sibling labors to lighten the burdens of the realm?"

The old chamberlain hesitated.

"Your Majesty, perhaps—"

"Are your ears failing you in your old age? Go. Now."

The chamberlain bowed and withdrew, his footsteps heavy with reluctance.

The Corridors of the Imperial Palace

✦ ✦ ✦

"I must offer my sincerest apologies, Your Highnesses. His Majesty is... indisposed."

The imperial chamberlain bowed deeply before Cesare and Medea, his aged face creased with genuine shame.

He could not bring himself to admit the truth—that the Emperor was too engrossed in watching the Third Prince's archery practice to receive his own heir.

According to imperial protocol, the coronation ceremony remained incomplete until the Crown Princess received the Emperor's formal acknowledgment. Until then, Medea's position as a member of the Katzen royal family was... imperfect.

And Perdiccas II knew this perfectly well.

Medea's ceremonial veil hung motionless beneath her diadem.

The chill of the corridor seeped through her gown as the minutes stretched into hours. Two hours of standing before closed doors, waiting for an audience that would never come.

"I regret to inform Your Highnesses that His Majesty will be unable to receive you today. Please return on a more... auspicious occasion."

The chamberlain's voice was barely above a whisper, thick with mortification.

You planned this from the beginning, didn't you, Father?

Cesare's eyes turned to ice as he saw through the Emperor's intentions with crystalline clarity.

A Crown Princess who had not received imperial approval would forever remain incomplete in the eyes of the court. Her authority would be questioned. Her legitimacy, doubted.

"If the Emperor's condition is truly so grave, I must see him personally. As his heir, it is my duty."

"I... Your Highness, His Majesty has forbidden all visitors except those in imperial dress."

Cesare's lips curved into something cold and dangerous—not quite a smile.

"I see."

"Forgive me, Your Highness."

The old chamberlain lowered his head, unable to meet either of their gazes. The excuse was pathetically transparent—and delivering it before the clear-eyed Crown Princess made his shame all the more acute.

A vein stood out sharply on the back of Cesare's hand.

For himself, he cared nothing about his father's petty cruelties. After years of such treatment, the sting had long since dulled.

But Medea...

She did not leave Valdina to be treated like this.

Not on her very first day within the Imperial Palace.

Word traveled faster than wind in these halls. By nightfall, every servant, every noble, every whispering courtier would know that the Emperor had refused to receive the Crown Princess.

And from such humiliation, authority could not easily recover.

"It seems my absence was longer than I realized."

Cesare's voice dropped to something languid and lethal—the calm before violence.

"Long enough for the Emperor to forget me entirely."

His hand drifted to the hilt of his sword, fingers curling around the scabbard with unconscious intent.

The air in the corridor seemed to thicken, pressing down with the weight of barely restrained fury.
