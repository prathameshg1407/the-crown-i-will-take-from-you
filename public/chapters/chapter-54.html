The Mother's Venom

"It doesn't matter," Catherine murmured, stroking Birna's hair with slow, hypnotic motions.

"There is no man worthy of you in Valdina anyway."

Her voice softened, turning honeyed.

"You deserve someone who holds empires beneath his feet."

She pressed a kiss to Birna's forehead.

"Don't worry, my love.

Mother will make Medea pay for every tear you shed."

The Guilty Silence

Birna went still.

Should I tell her?

The thought flickered—then died.

If Mother knew I caused this…

she would never forgive me.

And besides, Birna told herself desperately,

how do I know it was my powder?

That pig could have eaten anything.

Later that night, Birna grabbed her maid's wrist with shaking fingers.

"Sheila," she whispered, her eyes wild. "Erase this from your mind. We know nothing. Do you understand?"

Sheila swallowed and nodded, pale as a corpse.

The Minister's Estate

Count Etienne writhed in his bed, drenched in sweat.

"Why—why does it hurt?!"

The pain in his lower abdomen was unbearable.

It felt as if fire had nested inside him, chewing through flesh and pride alike.

The physician knelt beside the bed, trembling.

"It appears to be a severe reaction to a hallucinogen, my lord. The toxicity… concentrated in your reproductive organs."

"Fix it!" Etienne screamed. "Give me an antidote!"

The doctor hesitated, then lowered his head.

"I'm afraid the damage is irreversible."

Silence.

"…What?"

"You are likely infertile,"

the doctor said carefully.

"And impotent."

The word echoed like a death sentence.

"Impotent?" Etienne whispered. "Me? Larque Etienne? A man among men?"

He seized a porcelain vase and hurled it across the room.

Crash!

"Get out! You're lying!"

But every physician said the same thing.

The poison had destroyed him.

When the room emptied, Etienne lay in darkness, rage congealing into something cold and absolute.

He had never cared for women.

But he had cared for legacy.

A noble line.

Proof that he mattered.

Now that future was gone.

"Who did this?" he rasped. "Who stole my manhood?"

The Investigation

Umberto stepped forward from the shadows.

"We traced the substance, Master. It was introduced by Lady Birna's maid."

"Birna?"

"It appears she intended to drug the Princess… but you drank the wine instead."

Etienne laughed—a dry, broken sound.

"You fool," he snarled. "Do you truly believe that?"

Umberto stiffened.

"Master?"

"Think,"

Etienne hissed.

"Drugging the Princess is the perfect excuse.

What if the Regent ordered it?"

"But… Duke Claudio is your ally."

"Allies betray each other," Etienne growled. "I betrayed him with Quiggin. He knows it. This is revenge."

He clenched the bedsheet until his knuckles went white.

"He crippled me so I can't challenge him."

Etienne's eyes burned.

"Send a message to the Duke. Tell him I know."

The Duke's Estate

The scandal had become an inferno.

The Regent barred his doors, refused visitors, and drank alone in his study.

Then the letter arrived.

His aide's hands shook as he spoke.

"The Minister… has written, Your Excellency."

Claudio read it once.

Twice.

His blood ran cold.

Evidence confirms the substance found in my system matches that used by Lady Birna.

"He knows,"

Claudio whispered.

"He knows Birna did it."

"The Minister asks if this was your will," the aide continued. "He demands… responsibility."

"Responsibility?!" Claudio roared, throwing the letter into the fire. "He attacked my daughter naked!"

But fury didn't erase fear.

Etienne controlled the Inner Palace.

If he turned now, everything Claudio had built would collapse.

"Prepare the carriage," Claudio said hoarsely. "Bring the heavy chest."

The Confrontation

Etienne refused to see him for hours.

When Claudio was finally allowed inside, the bedroom was dark and stank of medicine and decay.

"What do you want, Joaquin?" Etienne rasped.

"I brought gifts," Claudio said, forcing composure. "Rare medicines. Artifacts. And—"

He gestured.

Servants placed a velvet-wrapped chest on the table.

"The Book of the Sage."

Etienne's eyes flickered.

The inheritance of the late King.

Priceless.

"Take it," Claudio said. "As compensation for my daughter's foolishness."

Etienne looked at the book—then pushed it away.

"A book cannot fix what is broken."

Claudio stiffened.

"What do you mean?"

"The doctors say I am barren," Etienne said flatly. "My line ends with me."

"I… regret that," Claudio said carefully. "But surely there are alternatives—"

"There is one," Etienne interrupted.

He leaned forward, eyes gleaming like wet stones.

"Since your daughter destroyed my future,

she will restore it."

The world tilted.

"What?" Claudio breathed. "You want to marry my daughter? After you attacked her? After—after this?"

"It is fair," Etienne replied calmly. "She ruined me. Now she belongs to me."

"You're insane!" Claudio shouted. "I will never allow it!"

"Then I release everything,"

Etienne shrugged.

"The embezzlement. The bribes. The rebel funds.

I will burn your house to ash."

Claudio went still.

He looked at the broken man in the bed.

At the rejected Book of the Sage.

And he understood.

"…Give me time,"

he whispered.

"You have until week's end," Etienne said, closing his eyes. "Now get out."

A minister castrated by conspiracy.

And a girl who poisoned the wrong cup—
now offered as payment.
