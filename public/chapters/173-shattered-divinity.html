Chapter 173

Shattered
Divinity

"When gods fail to speak, mortals must break their idols."

Medea's emerald gaze moved between Rachel and the weeping statue, perceiving what others could not.

Concealed within the Goddess's sculpted eyes were spherical pellets of wax infused with crimson dye.

If you blend honey with beeswax, the melting rate slows considerably...

She had calculated precisely how long the mixture would take to liquify at room temperature. Once all the guests had assembled, the scarlet liquid would seep forth, creating the illusion that the divine statue wept tears of blood.

Those who witnessed today's coronation would carry this tale across the continent—that the Goddess Herself refused to acknowledge Rachel as Her chosen representative.

Rachel's unexpected arrival—her premature intervention on Claudio's behalf—had created even more dramatic timing than Medea had orchestrated.

A small voice whispered that she might incur divine wrath for employing such manipulative tactics against the Saintess.

But Medea felt no fear.

She had reconciled herself to damnation long ago.

◆

Rachel's composure shattered completely.

When Claudio had attempted to introduce gunpowder into Valdina's political landscape, Medea had simply detonated the entire arsenal.

That wretched girl is destroying my carefully rebuilt bridges with the priesthood!

How many sacrifices she'd made to regain the temple's trust! Enduring unspeakable torments from dark sorcerers, all to demonstrate her absolute divine power—those countless agonies flashed through her memory.

"I'll prove it myself!"

There must be evidence of tampering in that statue. I'll simply expose the mechanism. That will settle everything.

Rachel strode toward the Goddess's effigy with determined purpose.

The moment her fingers reached toward the statue—

CRASH!

The circular mirror mounted behind the statue shattered.

Without cause. Without contact. As though refusing to permit Rachel's touch upon the sacred image.

Rachel, I knew if you took my bait, you'd attempt to expose the manipulation with your own hands.

So Medea had positioned a thin wooden board beneath the floor where the mirror stood, installing a delicate, unstable pedestal.

The instant Rachel's weight pressed down directly before the statue, vibrations would travel through the mechanism—shattering the mirror.

"Wh-what..."

Rachel's bewildered expression reflected in a thousand fractured shards as they cascaded to the floor.

"The mirror shattered the moment the Saintess approached!"

"When she reached for the statue, the Goddess expressed Her fury again!"

"Merciful heavens, what does this portend?"

The cascade of inexplicable phenomena—far from revealing manipulation—only lent greater credence to Medea's implication that Rachel had incurred divine displeasure.

Rachel's expression hardened. She had to stem the wildfire of suspicion before it consumed her completely.

"Someone orchestrated this deliberately! To ensnare me, to undermine the Holy Kingdom—"

"A trap, you say?"

A sharp voice cut through the murmurs—one of the Valdinian nobles.

"Saintess, choose your accusations carefully. Are you suggesting that Valdina deliberately desecrated a divine statue to sabotage our own once-in-a-generation celebration?"

Months ago, this same Saintess had shamelessly attempted to frame their Princess. Now she dared speak of conspiracies?

"Saintess, compose yourself. We shall protect you and uncover the truth."

The Templar Commander intervened smoothly, steadying Rachel with quiet authority.

Beyond the immediate confusion, he inwardly cursed her lack of subtlety—to speak so openly of traps at an official state function showed dangerous recklessness.

The banquet hall erupted into heated debate: were these phenomena genuine divine manifestation, or merely human deception?

When High Priest Paul joined the fray defending the Saintess, the noise escalated to near-chaos.

Unnoticed amid the tumult, a figure rose from the shadows.

A small pebble that had been rolling between the man's large, elegant fingers found its way silently back to the windowsill planter.

"Everyone! Do not succumb to these petty schemes aimed at our Saintess! You insignificant—ugh!"

Paul's impassioned defense cut off abruptly. Something hard and precise struck his calf with devastating accuracy.

"Your Eminence! Are you injured?"

Paul collapsed to the floor as though genuflecting, so disoriented he failed to notice the figure that glided past him with predatory grace.

CRACK

A sound like thunder splitting stone...

"Something just struck me..."

As Paul groped at the floor, a sharp, splintering crack sliced through the air.

Those who turned toward the sound stood paralyzed with shock.

"The Goddess's statue...!"

The divine effigy that had wept blood at Valdina's coronation lay shattered beyond recognition.

Marble dust rose in pale clouds above pristine white fragments.

And through that distant haze emerged a figure.

Sword in hand, he made no attempt whatsoever to conceal his role in the statue's destruction.

"My blade...!"

A knight near Paul gasped, face draining of color as he registered his empty scabbard and his own sword gleaming in the stranger's grip.

He hadn't even felt the weapon leave his side.

"You DARE desecrate the sacred image of the Goddess?! What manner of heretical monstrosity commits such blasphemy?!"

High Priest Paul's voice trembled with genuine horror.

The statue symbolized the divine presence itself—physical evidence of the Goddess's connection to Her faithful, a manifestation of belief transcending all national boundaries.

To shatter it openly meant defying the deity Herself.

In six decades of life, Paul had never encountered such brazen sacrilege.

"Vile heretic! Do you not fear divine retribution? Only when you burn at the stake will you comprehend your sin!"

Paul's tirade continued, but both the Knights Templar and the Agemas straightened with instinctive wariness.

None of them had sensed the man's presence until he'd already reached the statue.

The figure began to move.

With each measured step, the dark silk cape draped across his shoulders billowed like shadow given form.

Chandelier light played across the subtle shift of muscle beneath his tailored coat—revealing a frame of coiled steel and lethal precision.

Long, powerful legs carried him forward with fluid economy of motion. Perfect musculature visible beneath fine fabric suggested explosive strength held in perfect restraint.

Each slow, graceful step commanded silence—the footfalls of an apex predator stalking prey.

Though his hands remained empty, the very air around him thrummed with barely leashed violence.

Standing amid the shattered remnants of divinity, the man fixed his gaze upon Rachel.

As though having destroyed the Goddess's image, he now turned his attention to Her supposed vessel.

Rachel froze involuntarily under that golden stare.

"Protect the Saintess!"

Knights Templar moved with practiced efficiency, forming a defensive circle around Rachel with drawn steel.

"How absolutely unconscionable—drawing weapons at a diplomatic banquet!"

D'Angel's expression twisted with outrage at the Templars' battle-ready stance, as though assassins had materialized in their midst.

Are these fanatics genuinely attempting to sabotage Valdina's coronation?!

Setting aside the mysterious stranger entirely—who would bear responsibility if these zealous knights started swinging blind steel to "protect" their Saintess?

But the man seemed utterly oblivious to the razor-sharp hostility radiating from the Templar formation.

He glanced casually across Paul, Rachel, and the surrounding knights before allowing a cold smile to curve his lips.

"If all who bear the Goddess's name possess such pitiful intellect, the Holy Kingdom's restoration to former glory will prove... exceptionally distant."

His rich, mocking voice struck directly at the Holy Kingdom's most sensitive wound.

Even now, their influence spans the continent...

But "former glory" meant something else entirely—the golden age when the faith held absolute dominion over all lands.

The stranger displayed complete indifference as the Holy delegation's fury ignited.

"You insolent bast—"

"High Priest."

The man's voice cut through Paul's explosion with surgical precision.

"*This* is what genuine manipulation looks like. Breaking. Destroying—"

The heel of his polished black boot ground deliberately against the statue's shattered fragments.

"It is foolish human nature to derive satisfaction only through elimination."

*Crunch.*

The sound of marble pulverizing beneath his weight sent involuntary shivers through every witness.

"Surely you comprehend better than anyone that tears—such trivial messages that neither kill nor break—suit only that gentle soul?"

His extended finger pointed skyward as he addressed the elderly High Priest with startling naturalness.

Like a sovereign born and raised with all creation beneath his feet from the very beginning.

Among the assembly's shocked stares, only the man's eyes gleamed with clarity.

Golden. Luminous. Absolute.

His gaze drifted briefly across the statue's demolished remains, taking in the scattered debris and exposed mechanisms.

The corners of his mouth lifted almost imperceptibly—as though he understood precisely what had transpired.

The Templar Commander, who had been tensely monitoring his subordinates, suddenly froze.

Belatedly, recognition dawned across his features.

"First Prince...?"

"When gods prove silent,
princes shatter their idols with steel."
