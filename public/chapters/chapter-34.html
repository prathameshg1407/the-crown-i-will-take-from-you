The Minister's Estate

Umberto stared at the cipher.

At first, it was just noise—broken lines, misaligned characters, meaningless symbols. Then the structure revealed itself, like bones beneath rotting flesh.

[ Do not search for me again,
Lord of Kensington. ]

[ What is revealed will not be me.
It will be you. ]

The ink seemed to bleed into the paper.

Lord of Kensington.

The name clawed at his throat.

Umberto was not a servant. He was one of the last remnants of the Red Foxes, the clandestine intelligence network once commanded by Count Kensington before the Imperial purge. He had fled Kazen, buried his name, erased his past, and hidden himself behind the perfect anonymity of a minister's valet.

If this identity surfaced, death would not be swift. The Empire did not kill traitors.

It made examples.

His fingers trembled as he wrote his reply in cipher.

[ What do you want? ]

He placed the note in the window frame.

Hours passed.

By dusk, the parchment was gone.

In its place sat a small pouch.

And beneath it, a final message.

[ Give this to your master. ]

Umberto opened the pouch.

The scent struck first—sweet, heavy, almost narcotic.

A candle.

It was sculpted in the shape of a coiled snake, the wax pale and unnervingly smooth.

His stomach twisted.

Etienne adored scented candles. Ever since Quiggin's death severed his… other comforts, the Minister had relied on them to calm his urges.

Is it poison?

Umberto hesitated, then wrote another message.

[ Impossible. Too dangerous. ]

The response came swiftly, brutally.

[ Is it more dangerous than Ossoff? ]

Umberto went cold.

Ossoff.

The hidden sanctuary of the surviving Red Foxes.

The last place they gathered.

Whoever held this leash did not threaten him alone. They held every Fox still breathing.

Umberto closed his eyes.

One man.

One repulsive, disposable man…

…or the annihilation of his entire network.

When he opened his eyes, the decision was already made.

The Next Morning

Sunlight forced its way through the thick velvet curtains.

Umberto stood beside the bed, posture immaculate.

"Master. It is time to wake."

He said gently.

"Shut up. You buzz like a fly."

Etienne groaned, rolling over.

Then his nose twitched.

"…What is that smell?"

"A new candle, sir. A gift from Baron Girion."

A prepared lie. If the Minister died, Girion would take the fall.

Etienne squinted at the flickering flame.

"Hm. At last. Someone with taste."

The scent unfurled through the room—musky, warm, soothing. Etienne sighed, visibly relaxing.

"You useless swine. Why wasn't this lit earlier?"

The metal struck Umberto's chest.

He did not react.

"I wished to save it for a special occasion."

"Every moment I'm awake is special. Get out."

Etienne sneered, sinking back into the pillows.

Umberto bowed.

"With every step I take, I pray for your eternal peace."

The door closed.

The smoke thickened.

Umberto did not look back.

District Three – The Vegetable Market

An ox cart creaked through the early fog.

Tom whistled, straw hat tilted low.

"Delivery complete?"

A voice asked.

Neril stepped from the shadows.

Tom grinned and passed her a folded slip of parchment.

"But seriously, that valet? He's terrified. Whoever your mistress is—she's got him gutted."

"You are paid not to understand."

Neril replied, tossing a heavy pouch onto the cart.

Coins clinked.

Tom caught it easily.

"Fair enough."

As she walked away, Tom glanced toward the veterans' housing near the castle wall. Smoke rose from warm chimneys. The food lines were full. Children laughed.

"Huh. Guess the Stupid Princess was smarter than all of us."

He muttered.

The Princess's Palace

Neril knelt, presenting the note.

[ Initiation completed. ]

Medea read it once.

Then smiled.

"Well done. Your stealth is improving."

Neril hesitated.

"Your Highness… how did you know? About the servant?"

Medea leaned back, fingers folded.

"I saw his badge."

During the chaos in the chapel courtyard—when Etienne had forced his way forward—a pin had fallen from Umberto's coat.

A Red Fox.

In her previous life, Jason had used the Red Foxes to seize Kazen's throne.

And she had been the one who found them. United them. Handed them to him like a gift.

I built your empire, she thought coldly.

So I will dismantle it.

"I know their symbols. Their codes. Their fear."

She stood.

"The trap is complete."

Outside, Imperial banners snapped in the wind.

"Prepare the carriage, Neril. Our guest has arrived."

Her smile was thin.

"Now we hunt."
