Chapter 163

The Serpent
& the Saint

"Holy words flow sweetest from serpent tongues."

Rachel could not suppress the melody that escaped her lips—a soft, triumphant hum as she envisioned Medea's inevitable downfall.

Soon, the beloved princess would be abandoned by every soul in the kingdom. Soon, she would kneel before Rachel in abject surrender.

With a languid grace, Rachel strolled through the cottage house, pausing occasionally to verify that the warlocks' preparations remained intact.

Satisfied, she reclined upon her luxurious bed, silk cushions cradling her as she gazed at the ornate ceiling.

The moment draws near. The instant the Knights Templar arrive, my scheme to drag Medea through the mud will commence.

A delicate laugh bubbled from her throat.

Heh heh.

The melody resumed, soft and sinister.

The Gates of Valdina's Royal Palace

"Make way! Make way!"

A procession of knights astride pristine white destriers thundered toward the palace gates, their passage raising clouds of golden dust that hung in the afternoon light.

"Your Eminence, we have arrived."

"At last. An interminable journey to such a remote corner of the world."

An elderly priest descended from an immaculate white carriage, his movements stiff with age and irritation. His courteous words dripped with barely concealed condescension.

"A servant of the Goddess goes where commanded, of course. But one must wonder why divine providence chose such an... insignificant realm."

"On behalf of His Majesty King Peleus, I extend Valdina's warmest welcome to High Priest Paul and the esteemed Knights Templar."

A slender, handsome figure awaited them at the gate—impeccably dressed, a knowing smile playing at his lips. Sunlight glinted off his spectacles, rendering his eyes unreadable.

It was Sissair.

"You must be Sir Sissair. We appreciate the hospitality."

The Templar Commander offered a respectful nod, but High Priest Paul's frown only deepened.

The young Prime Minister of Valdina.

Under this man's guidance, Valdina had absorbed the nomadic tribes of the plains and the seafaring peoples of the coast with remarkable ease.

During his journey through the capital, Paul had been reluctantly impressed by the bustling streets, the prosperous merchants, the evident wealth. It had embarrassed him more than he cared to admit.

Even so, it remains a minor kingdom. These upstarts fancy themselves sharks simply because they've ridden a fortunate current.

He straightened his aching spine—the carriage ride had been brutal—and cast a warning glance at his knights.

No sign of weakness. We must demonstrate the majesty of the Holy Nation to these provincial Valdinians.

"Where is the Saintess?"

High Priest Paul dispensed with pleasantries entirely, his demand cutting through Sissair's greeting like a blade.

The dismissive words, the imperious tone—he made no effort to conceal his contempt for Valdina.

Sissair's smile never wavered.

"Tell me, High Priest—do you come as an official representative of the Holy Nation, or merely as a common priest of the temple?"

His voice remained pleasant, almost musical.

"Once you clarify that distinction, I shall be delighted to address your inquiry."

Behind those sun-reflecting lenses, the Prime Minister's true expression remained an enigma.

"This is—"

"Surely our meeting will prove more fruitful if both parties' intentions are transparent from the outset, don't you agree?"

Paul hesitated, swallowing his indignation. The Saintess's return was paramount. Valdina had discovered her first; the advantage was theirs.

"Ah, forgive me—this old mind wanders. Pray tell, where might I find His Majesty the King of Valdina?"

Sissair offered no reply.

He simply regarded the High Priest and his retinue, that enigmatic smile fixed upon his features, radiating an unshakeable confidence.

The silence stretched. Grew uncomfortable. Became unbearable.

"Now see here—"

Anxiety gnawed at Paul's composure. What if Valdina seized upon his discourtesy as justification to withhold the Saintess?

How could I possibly face His Holiness? I cannot return without her!

He forced his features into something approaching warmth.

"You must understand our eagerness. Surely you appreciate how long the Holy Nation has awaited this miracle?"

"Your Eminence, please forgive our haste. We rode without rest from the Holy Land itself."

The Templar Commander added his voice to the appeal.

"Indeed. We too experienced delays—the demonic incursions following the Wave demanded attention during our return journey. Otherwise, we might have established contact much sooner."

Sissair clicked his tongue sympathetically, then shrugged with elaborate nonchalance.

"Though I confess, who could have predicted the Saintess would receive her revelations here, in the territory of Valdina?"

The implication hung in the air like incense smoke.

Paul inclined his head, acknowledging the unspoken message.

"Naturally. Upon my return to the Holy Land, I shall personally inform the continent that the Saintess's presence has been confirmed. The Goddess's chosen representative has descended upon the sacred soil of Valdina."

"..."

"A vow sworn before the Goddess herself. How could anyone doubt such a promise?"

"Perhaps. Nevertheless, as representatives of our respective nations, should we not first establish the precise terms of any agreement?"

"Then permit me to meet the Saintess first—"

These northerners are insufferably stubborn.

Paul attempted to press forward, but Sissair's silver tongue redirected him with practiced ease.

"The High Priest clearly grasps the urgency of these matters. Please, follow me."

Before Paul could register what had happened, he found himself within the administrative palace.

Several Hours Later

"Your Eminence, did the negotiations conclude favorably?"

Only after reaching an agreement regarding the Saintess was Paul finally released from the Prime Minister's office.

"Shall we proceed to meet the Saintess now? Your Eminence...?"

The Templar Commander's words died in his throat.

Paul emerged looking a decade older than when he had entered. His wrinkled face had drained to the pallor of parchment, as though some vampiric creature had siphoned his very essence.

"End well? End well?!"

His voice cracked with impotent fury.

"Those damnable serpents! Their bellies must be writhing with vipers!"

Having been thoroughly outmaneuvered by Sissair, the High Priest erupted in decidedly unpriestly rage.

The Cottage House

At least these Valdinians possess some discernment.

Paul surveyed the cottage house with grudging approval. The splendid accommodations rivaled any palace—a clear indication that even Valdina recognized the Saintess's significance.

Though the atmosphere is peculiar. Something almost... oppressive.

The elegant architecture harmonized beautifully with the surrounding gardens, yet an inexplicable gloom permeated the air, grating against his senses.

Such murky energy. No wonder Valdina has remained a provincial backwater until now.

Attributing the unease to some deficiency in the Valdinian royal character, he proceeded inside.

"O Holy One. I, Paul, am blessed to behold your radiant presence at last."

The humiliation suffered at Sissair's hands evaporated the moment he beheld the Saintess.

She was everything he had imagined and more. Her gentle smile seemed to warm the very air around her. Her crystalline eyes shimmered with pure, untainted virtue.

"I... I remain uncertain. Am I truly deserving of such reverence?"

Every gesture radiated humility. Every word dripped with gracious modesty. And the holy power that bloomed from her delicate fingertips—pure, brilliant white—left nothing to doubt.

If her power at first awakening already reaches such heights, imagine what she will become after proper doctrinal training!

The Holy Nation could reclaim its supremacy over the continent at last.

As the Pope's strength had waned over the years, the temple's influence had dwindled accordingly. They barely maintained their position as the second power on the continent, locked in constant competition with Katzen—and now Valdina was rising to challenge them as well.

Rachel's emergence was nothing short of providential—an opportunity to reassert the Holy Nation's dominance.

"You speak nonsense, Holy One. You vanquished a ghoul horde. You purified demonic corruption. Such feats belong solely to the Goddess's chosen representative."

Paul shook his head vehemently, as though she had suggested the sun might rise in the west.

His heart swelled with fervent joy.

"We shall escort you to the Holy Land with all honor. His Holiness the Pope and a million faithful priests await your arrival with breathless anticipation."

"A moment, please. If I am to depart, I wish first to bid farewell to those who showed me such kindness."

Her voice softened with apparent emotion.

"His Majesty the King of Valdina... and the others who welcomed me so warmly... I should—"

Her words ceased abruptly.

Her beautiful eyes widened to impossible dimensions, as though witnessing something beyond mortal comprehension.

Then she collapsed.

"Saintess! Someone—anyone! The Saintess has fallen!"

Paul caught her limp form, his aged arms trembling with the effort.

It was then that he noticed the bandages wrapped around her slender arms.

They were turning crimson.

Blood seeped through the white fabric, spreading in dark, accusatory blooms.

" The stage was set.
The audience assembled.
And the performance had only just begun. "
