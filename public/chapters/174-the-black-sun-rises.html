Chapter 174

The Black Sun
Rises

"When darkness walks in daylight, even the faithful tremble."

Despite the passage of many years, the black demon he'd encountered during countless Demon Wave suppressions remained indelibly seared into his memory.

"First Prince? You're speaking of Katzen's First Prince?"

"That man is the First Prince? The Demon of War himself?"

The hall erupted into chaos.

Who? Who is this brazen heretic who just shattered the Goddess's statue and confronted the Knights Templar?

They could scarcely trust their ears.

News of Katzen's First Prince remained the continent's most incendiary topic—from his terminal illness to his miraculous resurrection and triumphant return to the empire.

And now, astonishingly, such a figure had materialized at Valdina's imperial coronation. In the most unexpected manner. With the most shocking actions.

The First Prince's lips curved upward, as if to confirm: *Yes, that's correct.*

Meanwhile, Paul's eyes bulged with disbelief.

"That lunatic is Katzen's *First* Prince?!"

Yet since the identification came from the Templar Commander himself—who had once allied with Katzen in continental warfare—it must be truth.

Paul ground his teeth in impotent fury.

He said he feared no consequences...

Who else but Katzen's Black Devil would dare such presumption before this assembled company and the Saintess herself?

"First Prince... Your Highness, the Saintess nearly suffered injury. What justification can you offer for damaging the sacred statue of the Goddess?"

Paul struggled to contain his rage.

"You must provide proper explanation. Surely you comprehend that destroying a divine effigy constitutes grave heresy."

"Oh?"

The First Prince raised an eyebrow with apparent surprise.

"One cannot destroy a statue while blood flows from its eyes? The moment you ignore the Goddess's tears, you deny that statue's very existence. Is disregarding divine will not the true heresy?"

"First Prince—!"

"How peculiar. Whom exactly does the Holy Kingdom represent? God? Or mortals?"

Paul found himself momentarily speechless, struck by the precision of that cutting question.

"That... that is..."

"Indeed. The answer has already been provided."

He stepped forward without hesitation, as though he'd never intended to hear a response in the first place.

Those listening to the exchange began to harbor doubts of their own.

"The First Prince speaks truth. The High Priest's behavior grows stranger by the moment. The Goddess grants us divine revelation after centuries of silence, yet instead of rejoicing, he attempts to deny it unconditionally."

"Why would that be? There's only one plausible reason—don't even glance in that direction!"

The murmuring swelled like a rising tide.

The First Prince's overwhelming presence instilled strange confidence in the assembly. Having successfully guided Rachel into Medea's carefully prepared trap, he now sealed it completely.

"Wait. This cannot simply be dismissed."

Rachel bit the inside of her cheek in silent fury. She hadn't surrendered yet.

"As the Saintess, I cannot permit Valdina to impersonate the Goddess and sow chaos across the continent."

"Saintess."

Medea responded with measured calm, leaving no avenue for retreat.

"You are no longer some peasant girl wandering the plains. As you yourself declared, you represent the temple—the Goddess Herself, and the Holy Kingdom. Therefore, you must exercise extreme caution with your words."

Medea addressed not Rachel, but the assembled priests.

"When this moment is recorded and transmitted to future generations, will the Holy Kingdom bear that shame with dignity?"

A reminder: in the distant future, responsibility for this fleeting instant's consequences would rest with them—not the Saintess.

"..."

Rachel's face flushed at the pointed rebuke.

Medea smoothed her white gloves with deliberate precision.

Observers recalled another occasion—when this same Princess had boldly challenged Katzen's Fourth Princess to single combat.

What had been the outcome?

The Fourth Princess of that mighty nation had suffered two crushing defeats. Katzen was forced to pay double tribute as penalty for their wager.

"Valdina will not tolerate a second instance of such discourtesy."

Rachel retreated a step at the quiet warning.

Then, realizing she'd backed away involuntarily, she bit her inner cheek harder.

Damn it all... this cannot continue...

After Medea had contradicted Rachel, her carefully orchestrated momentum shattered against the weeping statue.

Then Katzen's First Prince branded the divine manifestation as evidence the Goddess rejected the Saintess's claims.

I am the Saintess. The one and only Saintess to emerge on this continent in hundreds of years.

Rachel repeated the mantra silently, but her mouth had gone completely dry.

She detected a gleam of suspicion in eyes that had previously held her up as the Goddess's unquestionable representative.

Rachel could not tolerate even hairline cracks in what should have been absolute, unwavering faith.

"Your Highness, First Prince."

She addressed him with forced courage.

Having grown among black magicians, she had yet to viscerally comprehend why these gathered nobility feared the First Prince so profoundly.

"The Goddess does not forgive those who distort Her will. Do you not fear divine retribution, Your Highness?"

The First Prince turned his head slowly.

"The Saintess proves diligent in her doctrinal studies."

He possessed neither intention nor need to engage seriously with the Saintess.

Regardless of her title or purported powers, such things held no significance for him. After all, only one being commanded his true devotion.

For one brief moment...

...those golden eyes, burning with impossible heat, brushed past Medea.

Rachel, oblivious to why the Prince's demeanor had momentarily softened, persisted stubbornly and reached toward him again.

"I am the Saintess! You cannot treat me with such—!"

In that instant, golden eyes blazing with lethal intent fixed upon Rachel with glacial precision.

She froze as though ensnared in a serpent's coils, unable to move.

"S-Saintess... this way, please..."

"Your Eminence, it appears there remains much to teach her."

The High Priest found himself utterly unable to refute the arrogant admonishment—as though the Saintess were merely a clumsy child requiring discipline.

Was it Katzen's institutional might? Or the First Prince's personal power?

It burns... it burns...

Onlookers stood transfixed with shock.

One truth crystallized with absolute clarity: the High Priest—who should have been invoking the Holy Kingdom's majesty with righteous fury—had backed down completely.

The First Prince turned away, leaving both High Priest and Saintess speechless in his wake.

Step. Step.

The man crossed the banquet hall, now wrapped in suffocating silence. Countless eyes tracked his passage with singular focus.

They could not help but feel both fear and awe toward this figure who had so suddenly and completely dominated the assembly.

The Demon of War. The Black Beast.

His notorious reputation hadn't prepared them for such devastating handsomeness.

Black hair gleamed like polished ebony. A strong, sculpted jawline possessed the perfection of divine craftsmanship.

Yet what truly captured their attention transcended his outstanding appearance—which seemed Apollo reborn—his broad shoulders, or his lithe, panther-like physique.

A strange, intoxicating atmosphere emanated from him.

Leisurely gait. Indolent bearing.

The relaxed aura surrounding the First Prince seemed designed to lower one's guard. But beneath that composure lurked terrible danger—that tranquility might flash like lightning and crush your throat before you realized.

Like the Goddess's statue, shattered to fragments beneath his hands.

If Emperor Peleus resembled frigid ice, First Prince Cesare embodied the blazing sun itself.

But the true danger lay in how that solar heat could cook flesh and blind eyes without the victim ever noticing until too late.

He reached the Valdina royal family in one continuous motion—his pace neither hurried nor slow, perfectly calibrated.

The corners of his lips, held in casual repose, curved with the faintest suggestion of a smile. Or perhaps not.

Supremely arrogant, as though the entire world existed beneath his feet—yet no one could deny that even such hubris suited him with suffocating perfection.

"Cesare?! How—how are you here? My uncle appointed *me* as this delegation's representative!"

No matter how stunned the assembled guests felt, none matched Jason's reaction. His crimson eyes bulged as though they might burst from their sockets.

"When the black sun rises,
even the chosen of heaven must bow."
