Chapter 195

The Bitter
Honey

"The cruelest revenge is the one served with a smile."

The elderly servant straightened, unable to believe what he had heard.

"Leave. Do not return until I summon you."

"My lord?"

"Are your ears stuffed with wool?"

The twisted expression, the venom in each syllable—these remained unchanged.

The servant bowed hastily, fearing he might become the next target of his master's wrath.

"Yes, yes, of course. Please call whenever you have need of me."

Click.

The door closed softly behind him.

❧

A new guest had taken up residence in the Claudio mansion.

Marieu attended to Samon with a devotion that bordered on the sacred. Her care was so thorough, so unfailingly gentle, that even Catherine found herself moved.

"You were Medea's handmaiden, weren't you? Since when have you and Samon...?"

"I suppose you would have to ask him, my lady. At present, your son will tolerate no one else at his side."

Catherine's lips pressed into a thin line. She had little fondness for this woman—a disgraced former servant expelled from the palace in scandal.

But one by one, the caregivers had fled, driven away by Samon's relentless hysteria. Even his own mother could no longer manage him.

Marieu alone possessed the ability to soothe her son's rages without flinching.

"Please, do not trouble yourself on my account. It is my greatest joy to be of service to him."

Marieu's expression remained serene—more tranquil than a saint's, stripped clean of the bitterness and longing that had once defined her.

She helped Samon to the washroom when his damaged body made movement difficult. She wiped the perspiration from his brow without so much as a flicker of resentment crossing her lovely features.

Her only concern, it seemed, was his comfort.

Even a man as callous as Samon found himself affected by her boundless, angelic ministrations.

Mother and the servants grow more reluctant to approach me by the day.

Samon had noticed the shift. The terror he inspired through his violent outbursts was temporary at best; the household staff had grown increasingly careless, their fear fading into weary resignation.

The Fourth Princess has abandoned me. Katzen has abandoned me. They all believe I am finished.

Yet Marieu—the former lover he had cast aside so cruelly—remained devoted even in the face of his utter degradation.

This woman will accept anything.

For the first time in weeks, a cunning gleam flickered in Samon's eyes.

Recovery must come first. Above all else.

He needed to regain his strength as quickly as possible. Only then could he exact his vengeance upon that accursed Medea—the one responsible for reducing him to this wretched state.

She orchestrated this. Just as she deceived us three years ago, she lured me into that trap...

If he hadn't been locked in the basement. If he hadn't lost consciousness at the critical moment.

His right leg might still be intact.

At the thought, Samon's teeth ground together, his jaw aching with the force of suppressed fury.

I will make you suffer a hundredfold for what you've done to me.

"What is it?"

A soft touch against his forehead startled him from his dark reverie. Marieu was gazing at him with gentle concern.

"Your expression turned so troubled. I wish only for you to find peace."

Her naïveté—so complete it verged on foolishness—was not without its uses.

Samon gave a grudging nod.

He understood her nature now.

She tended to him day and night without expectation of reward, never once pressing him to make her his wife.

What a fool I was not to recognize such a useful asset. I discarded her—and murdered our child—for nothing.

Her lineage was common, her status beneath him. But her beauty remained exquisite, and her devotion was absolute.

Where else would I find a woman so lovely and so completely mine?

His thoughts drifted to the past.

Perhaps there was no need to kill the child. I could not have predicted the Fourth Princess would behave this way. Then again—if I had spared the infant, Peleus would have run me through with his sword.

Regret coiled through him, cold and unsettling.

If given the chance to do it over again, he would choose differently.

Why hadn't he been clever enough to see that he could have kept Marieu without sacrificing the princess's favor?

"Marieu... about the child. I know this comes late, but... I am sorry."

The words emerged stilted and awkward. Apologies did not come naturally to him.

But instinct told him that if he wished to keep Marieu at his side through his recovery, he would need to address the past.

"What I did to you—to both of you—was inexcusable. I was too harsh—"

He could not finish.

Marieu's hand pressed gently against his lips, silencing him. Her eyes were wide with surprise.

"Please. Don't speak of it. That belongs to another time. Let us leave the past where it lies."

Her smile was soft, almost luminous.

"Marieu..."

"I still love you, Samon."

Her quiet confession swelled his pride. He nodded magnanimously.

"Yes. We are still young. We shall have another child."

"Surely there must be something you wish to gain from caring for me so devotedly."

For the briefest instant, something flickered across Marieu's face—a ripple disturbing still water.

"...Of course."

When she spoke again, her expression had smoothed back to its gentle mask. But her voice emerged strangely tight, as though invisible fingers were wrapped around her throat.

"Our firstborn shall bear my family name. Even if my mother objects, I will ensure the child is added to the register."

Samon spoke with the haughty assurance of one granting an immense favor. And for him, it was.

Three years ago, at the height of their passion, he had never once made such a promise—had drawn careful lines around any commitment he could not easily escape.

The concession spoke volumes about how deeply Marieu had embedded herself in his heart.

"I am so happy, darling."

Marieu pressed her cheek against his chest, her expression one of rapturous gratitude.

"Thank you for opening your heart to me. I will remain at your side. Always."

Samon stroked her shoulder contentedly as honeyed words dripped from her lips.

❧

After a time, Marieu glanced at the clock and rose gracefully to her feet.

"Ah—it's already time for your meal. I shall fetch it."

"There's no need. Have one of the maids bring it."

"No, darling. I wish to serve you myself."

She slipped through the door and made her way down the corridor. Near the kitchen, she encountered a maid pushing a cart laden with covered dishes.

"Are you taking that to the master?"

"Yes, my lady. His meal and medicine, as ordered."

"Give it to me."

Marieu lifted one of the lids and frowned.

"The stew has already gone cold. Would you serve lukewarm food to an invalid? Return to the kitchen and reheat it properly."

The maid bristled inwardly—this woman had certainly grown bold, giving orders as though she were already mistress of the house.

But the master tolerated no one else these days. The maid had no choice but to obey.

Cold, she says. It's perfectly warm enough to eat.

"...Very well."

The maid retreated toward the kitchen.

The moment she was alone, Marieu withdrew a small pouch from the folds of her bodice.

"What purpose requires such a potent compound, young lady?"

The old herbalist's suspicious gaze remained vivid in her memory.

"Heed my warning well: use only an amount the size of a fingernail. No more. Exceed that dose by even a hair's breadth, and you will render the patient permanently barren. Not even the goddess Hella herself could restore what would be destroyed."

The colorless, odorless powder cascaded over the food awaiting Samon's consumption.

The amount she sprinkled was far, far greater than the size of a fingernail.

Marieu paused before a mirror in the hallway.

The reflection gazing back at her was soft and beautiful—still alluring, still capable of inspiring devotion.

"Another child, you say?"

Her smile never wavered.

"There will be no such thing, darling. Not ever. I will not permit it."

"Here we are—piping hot, just as you requested."

The maid returned, placing the reheated stew upon the cart.

"Excellent. Thank you for your trouble."

As Marieu pushed the cart down the corridor toward Samon's bedchamber, a soft melody escaped her lips.

Hmm, hmm, hmm...

The sound drifted through the empty hallway—gentle, almost sweet.

Like a lullaby sung to the dying.
