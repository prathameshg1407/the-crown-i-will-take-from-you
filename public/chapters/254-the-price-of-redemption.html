Chapter 4

The Price of Redemption

Some debts cannot be repaid with blood alone.

Medea detonated the white light bomb.

BOOM! BANG!

Silver streaks of light burst forth in rapid succession, blazing like lightning strikes across the sky, searing the evil dragon's vision with merciless intensity.

Its crimson pupils contracted to slits, as if torn by the brightness. The beast's ancient gaze locked onto Medea with murderous focus.

The evil dragon reflexively altered its flight path, banking hard despite its enormous size.

Then it began to plummet—descending at a speed that defied its massive frame, swift as a hurricane wind.

Its trajectory led directly toward Medea.

Each powerful flap of the dragon's wings generated fierce gusts that sent dust and gravel flying in all directions like shrapnel.

Thud! Thud! Thud!

Silva, proving himself a mount of legend, flew through the canyon without a moment's hesitation—leaping over scattered rocks and treacherous pebbles with preternatural grace.

Medea pressed her body low against his neck as they passed precariously through a narrow crevice in the rock face.

SCREEEECH—

The dragon's razor-sharp talons scraped against stone as it tried to snatch both Medea and Silva simultaneously.

Sparks erupted along with the sharp, grinding sound of metal against rock.

The massive shadow grew closer and closer, threatening to swallow her whole.

"Now!"

The instant the dragon's shadow reached its intended target, Medea's voice rang out across the canyon.

At her command, dozens of fire arrows shot upward as if tearing through the very fabric of the sky.

Terence's magical formulae—inscribed meticulously on each arrowhead—added explosive force to their flight. The trajectories of flame stretched out with sharp, unwavering precision.

CRACKLE! WHOOSH!

BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!

The arrows crossed paths mid-air, weaving together to form an enormous semicircular prison of fire that enveloped the dragon completely.

It was a formation calculated with perfect accuracy—predicting the flight patterns and instinctive behaviors of the evil dragon based on hard-won knowledge.

KYAAAAAAAH!

The evil dragon roared—a sound that shook the canyon walls—enraged at the cage binding its freedom.

It exhaled torrents of blue flame and beat its wings with furious desperation. Sharp talons flailed uselessly against the burning barriers.

KRRRRRRRR!

And then, as the trap tightened, the imprisoned dragon became covered by its own shadow.

Its own venom—the deadly poison it secreted to paralyze prey—turned against its creator.

The evil dragon writhed and roared in agony.

Its predatory eyes began to shift color—from crimson to sickly yellow—while its scales distorted and split along invisible seams.

"Ugh..."

"Just hold on a little longer!"

The dragon's struggles were so violent that Terence—who was maintaining the fire prison through sheer magical willpower—turned visibly pale, sweat streaming down his face.

At that critical moment, Cesare kicked off from the ground with explosive force.

The long, black aura he channeled extended like a colossal blade, slashing down toward the evil dragon's exposed neck.

It was the moment when the killing blow should have connected—

"KYAAAAH!"

"Impossible!"

The evil dragon, pushed to its absolute limit, unleashed a desperate countermeasure.

And in that same instant, Jason leaped from a high cliff and brought his sword down directly toward Cesare's head.

KRRRRRRRRR—!

The two blades met with a screech—sliding against each other for an agonizingly long moment.

There was an eerie, grinding sound, like rough iron chains being dragged across stone.

"My dear brother."

Cesare's lips curved upward in a smile that didn't reach his eyes.

"What brought you to this place?"

But his golden eyes radiated fierce anger at having his grand undertaking interrupted at the crucial moment.

"Cesare, I cannot allow you to steal what is rightfully mine."

Jason harbored the same fury toward his opponent—perhaps even deeper, more poisonous.

If hatred could be forged into a weapon, Jason's blade would have severed Cesare's head a dozen times over by now.

Yet despite attacking Cesare from a blind angle while he was focused on the dragon, Jason had failed to inflict even the slightest wound.

Ssssch—!

Jason swung his sword with savage intensity.

The dull orange aura surrounding his blade flickered across Cesare's cheek, drawing blood.

A single crimson drop slid down between the shallow cut.

"Oh?"

This was the first time Jason had successfully wielded aura—and his swordsmanship had improved enough to actually wound Cesare, however superficially.

"Did you practice in the abyss, brother? Your skills have certainly improved."

Cesare raised an eyebrow with aristocratic disdain, not even bothering to hide the thorny mockery hanging between his lightly curved lips.

"Do not think of me as the Jason of old, you fool! How dare you covet my glory!"

Jason glared at Cesare with eyes burning with resentment.

"You stole everything! My crown, my throne, my woman! You took everything! If not for you—if only it weren't for you—all of it would have fallen into my hands!"

Deep within Jason's crimson eyes, flames of jealousy and inferiority complex flickered like hellfire.

'What's different in this life? What has changed?'

Jason's subconscious whispered that perhaps the answer lay with Cesare himself.

'Because you're alive. That must be why she won't come to me!'

If you weren't here... if you had never existed...

Medea would have seen him. She would have chosen him over everyone else.

Jason's sword surged forward again like a tempest wind.

A murky orange aura cut through the air, drawing vicious lines as it approached its target.

"Your brain must have melted as well."

CLANG!

Cesare's blade shot out like black lightning, striking Jason's sword squarely.

"Or perhaps this delusion of yours is worsening?"

Ssssssch—

In an instant, the two swords clashed again with a sharp, grinding screech.

The golden eyes visible between the crossed blades gleamed with dangerous beauty.

"You should guard your tongue more carefully, brother."

I know you have a talent for making vulgar statements about my woman.

"What if that tongue of yours gets severed while you're running your mouth?"

As though he genuinely intended to do so, Cesare's razor-sharp blade rushed toward Jason's face.

Contrary to Jason's desperate hopes, even the accumulated achievements and combat experience of Emperor Jason—who had awakened with all his memories from a previous life intact—seemed unable to exert any meaningful pressure on Cesare.

"Your lifeline must be remarkably long, judging by how you've managed to survive this far."

These words were nothing less than confirmation that the attacks by mutated beasts that had plagued Jason's expedition in recent days were no accident.

Veins bulged visibly along Jason's neck.

"How dare you, you bastard—!"

KRAAAAAAH!

Meanwhile, the evil dragon—which had barely escaped the brink of death thanks to Jason's timely intervention—pulled out a final, desperate technique.

Shadows fractured and split into countless branches, bursting through the gaps in the crimson fire prison like dark tentacles.

From the fragmented, pitch-black shadows that resembled liquid darkness itself, multiple evil dragons emerged.

"What the—the dragon just multiplied!"

Medea could hear Gallo's panicked scream echoing across the canyon.

'An illusion?'

Medea's mind raced as she observed the phenomenon.

She had never heard of—nor experienced—an evil dragon employing illusionary magic of this caliber.

The clones, now numbering five in total, were so meticulously detailed it was nearly impossible to distinguish them from the original dragon.

The duplicates immediately flapped their wings and scattered in different directions.

WHOOSH! WHOOSH!

"Everyone, take cover!"

Shattered rock fragments and choking dust rained down from above as the dragons attacked simultaneously in all directions.

The multiple evil dragons—freed from their fire prison—immediately began hunting down the individuals who had driven them to near-death.

KRAAAAAAAH!

The dragons exhaled searing flames and wielded their sharp, thorn-covered tails like weapons, shattering boulders where the expedition members might be hiding.

Their primary target was obvious: Medea, who had captured the original dragon's attention and lured it into the trap.

KYAAAAAAAH!

All five clones simultaneously began pursuing Medea with single-minded fury.

Medea yanked the reins sharply, and Silva slid down the treacherous rocks of the ravine at breakneck speed.

However, the violently curved trajectory combined with the unstable terrain caused Silva's body to sway dangerously.

The accelerating centrifugal force upset Medea's precarious balance.

The moment her hands could no longer maintain their grip, she released the reins—

—and was thrown clear from Silva's back.

The shadow of the nearest evil dragon loomed darkly over her rising form, visible even through the billowing dust.

"MEDEA!"

Jason—who had been barely holding his own against Cesare's relentless assault—spotted the evil dragon bearing down on Medea's fallen form.

Impossibly, Jason moved faster than Cesare this time. Faster than anyone.

Perhaps it was born of sheer desperation.

Because he wanted to turn back time. Because he wanted to return to the beginning. Because he craved forgiveness above all else.

'No! Not again!'

Still, the distance was too great.

The evil dragon opened its maw wide, as if to devour Medea whole.

Even though it was merely a clone—having divided the original's power—it radiated a ferocious aura that was in no way diminished.

In that split second, Jason threw himself forward with reckless abandon.

He turned his back on Medea and positioned himself directly in the dragon's path.

The clone's razor-sharp fangs sank brutally into Jason's exposed back.

"Ungh!"

The teeth that should have torn through Medea's flesh instead buried themselves deep in Jason's body.

They ripped through muscle and shattered bone with sickening efficiency.

"Your Highness!"

At that precise moment, D'Angel's blade—swung with the full force of a ten-star knight—cleaved the dragon's clone cleanly in two.

The bisected creature fell to the canyon floor in smoking pieces.

"Gaaah—!"

Jason collapsed, blood streaming from multiple wounds.

He looked up at Medea—his former wife—who was staring down at him with wide, startled eyes.

Despite being drenched in his own blood, he forced his lips to curve upward in a smile.

"Medea... my wife. My companion..."

Shock, resentment, and hatred filled her large, luminous green eyes.

A kaleidoscope of complex emotions played across her features.

He wished desperately that she could read the words he hadn't yet managed to speak—that she might see them reflected in his eyes.

'Trust me, Medea. I am not the man I was before.'

Now that he had recalled all his memories from that terrible future, he wanted to tell her everything.

'I will restore all the glory you were denied.'

Their children would be raised as legitimate Prince and Princess—not bastards hidden in shame. The empire would be passed to their son Lian, as it should have been.

He would protect them without being swayed by gods, oracles, or anyone else's machinations.

The man who had been a foolish husband and cruel father was trying to atone for his brutal past—as if he truly believed that doing so would erase the timeline in which he'd driven his wife and children to their deaths.

'This time—this time, I swear—I won't betray you again.'

So please, give me one chance to prove it.

"This time... it will definitely... ugh... be different..."

In his increasingly blurred vision, he searched her face desperately.

Have I ever seen those green eyes so completely filled with me—focused solely on me—in this new life?

In that moment, he realized something with startling clarity.

He had missed those eyes—not just a little, but so much it made him feel faint.

Jason's consciousness slipped away into darkness.

Medea stood frozen, staring down at Jason's bloodied form.

Her expression was unreadable—shock and something darker warring behind her eyes.

D'Angel approached cautiously.

"Your Highness, are you injured?"

She didn't respond immediately. Her gaze remained fixed on Jason's unconscious face—the man who had murdered her children, who had poisoned her with his own hands, who had smiled as her world burned.

The man who now lay bleeding because he'd thrown himself between her and death.

'Does he think one act of sacrifice erases everything? Does he believe this changes what he did?'

Finally, Medea spoke, her voice eerily calm.

"I'm unharmed. See to the others."

She turned away from Jason without another glance, her movements precise and controlled.

Behind her, Cesare appeared—his golden eyes studying the scene with calculated interest.

Their gazes met briefly over Jason's fallen body.

No words were exchanged, but understanding passed between them like a current.

Some debts cannot be repaid. Some sins cannot be forgiven. Not with blood, not with sacrifice, not with all the desperate redemption in the world.

End Chapter 4
