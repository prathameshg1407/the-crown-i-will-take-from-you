Chapter 183
Continental Festival

Blood-Red Reflections

When the past refuses to remain buried, and ghosts walk among the living.

The assembled dignitaries shifted uneasily, unsettled by the Emperor of Valdina's pointed words that hung in the air like an unsheathed blade.

High Priest Paul struggled to contain his fury, his jaw clenching with barely suppressed rage. Beside him, Rachel's porcelain complexion had drained to an ashen pallor.

She saved the Emperor's life. Is it truly necessary to humiliate her so publicly, before such an audience?

Despite her inner turmoil, Rachel summoned a smile touched with gentle melancholy.

"Forgive me, Your Majesty. The High Priest desires only peace and harmony between our two nations."

"I understand perfectly. Naturally, I have complete confidence that the Saintess shares that sentiment."

Peleus's tone carried a sardonic edge that made several guests wince.

"Dea, let us depart."

With those final words, Peleus wrapped a protective arm around his sister's shoulders and guided her away, leaving behind an atmosphere thick with tension—as though the Holy Nation alone bore responsibility for the diplomatic chill that had descended upon the gathering.

"Saintess, my deepest apologies. I fear my intervention only exacerbated the situation."

"No, Your Eminence. You did everything within your power."

Rachel's smile remained soft, beatific—the perfect mask.

"The Goddess herself offered her blood and flesh countless times to humanity, even when they rejected her divine will. What consequence is a moment's misunderstanding to me?"

"Your wisdom and magnanimity are truly remarkable! Yet they remain foolish and utterly unworthy of your benevolence!"

Paul's voice trembled with genuine admiration and wounded pride—though he remained oblivious to the fury simmering beneath Rachel's serene facade.

The warlocks had prophesied that the Emperor of Valdina would suffer grievous injury this very evening.

Rachel had planned to rescue Peleus once more—to cement her position as his savior, to bind him to her with chains of gratitude and debt.

That carefully orchestrated plan now lay in ruins.

She bit down on her lower lip, hard enough to reopen the barely healed wound. The sharp, metallic taste of blood flooded her mouth.

Very well. Do as you please.

Rachel's gaze fixed upon Peleus's retreating back—and upon Medea, sheltered protectively beneath his arm.

Family. That girl's only family.

If it cannot be claimed, if it cannot be possessed...

Then it would be far better to simply destroy it.

"Dea, I must attend to a matter briefly. D'Angel will remain at your side."

Peleus spoke with the grave concern of a man abandoning his sister to a den of wolves.

"Have you forgotten where we stand? This is Valdina, brother. Neril and the Agema are here as well."

Medea shook her head with gentle exasperation. What commander of the guard abandons his Emperor's side?

D'Angel must remain with Peleus. There's no predicting how events might deviate from the plan.

Despite her meticulous preparations, Medea refused to dismiss even the remotest possibility of interference.

Moreover, D'Angel's presence might complicate her own carefully laid schemes.

As Captain of the Valdina Imperial Guard, D'Angel operated with considerable autonomy—unlike Neril, who invariably prioritized Medea's commands above all else.

"Go quickly, brother. You'll be late."

Medea gestured toward the anxious attendant shifting his weight impatiently in the distance.

"Yes, Your Majesty. We shall attend Her Highness with utmost vigilance."

Despite the Agema's firm assurance, Peleus's expression remained troubled, unconvinced.

"Very well. I shall return shortly."

He nodded, then suddenly lifted his gaze, sweeping the assembled guests with a penetrating stare that left no room for misinterpretation.

The young nobles flinched visibly beneath the Emperor's silent but unmistakable warning.

Once Peleus had departed, Medea addressed the Agema guards whose eyes burned with protective fervor.

"Maintain your distance. Don't make your presence too conspicuous. Tonight celebrates Saint Augustine and continental unity—not Valdina's martial superiority."

Creating an atmosphere of intimidation would only multiply Valdina's enemies unnecessarily.

Now was the moment to lower their guard, to extend the hand of friendship—however calculated that gesture might be.

"Your Highness, I wonder if you remember me? We were introduced at the banquet several days past. I am Lord Norfolk of the Kingdom of Sonia."

Despite Peleus's explicit warning, the moment he vanished from sight, young nobles began approaching Medea one by one—hesitant, yet inexorably drawn.

"Of course, Lord Norfolk. I understand Sonia's renowned vodka represents one of your kingdom's finest exports. Perhaps we might feature it prominently during the Continental Festival?"

Medea offered a smile—warm, genuine, utterly unlike her typical reserved demeanor.

When the notoriously aloof Princess responded with such gentle warmth, the young lords found themselves completely disarmed. Their faces flushed crimson as they stammered, unable to maintain eye contact.

"Oh, naturally! If this could facilitate trade relations with Valdina, we would desire nothing more—no, it would be absolutely ideal, I mean to say..."

Medea's smile deepened slightly.

Vodka, distilled primarily from grain, thrived in colder climates. Through strategic trade agreements, Valdina could finally unlock the economic potential of its harsh northern territories.

"I believe Sir Sissair would be better equipped to discuss the particulars. Neril, please escort the Duke."

"Your Highness, I would much prefer if you—"

"I shall ensure you're well attended."

With characteristic efficiency, Norfolk was smoothly redirected under Neril's capable guidance.

Medea found herself mildly bemused by the envoys' surprising naïveté—they seemed far less politically sophisticated than she'd anticipated. Nevertheless, she methodically connected each visitor with appropriate contacts.

"Sir Gilliforth possesses unparalleled expertise in armaments. Ah, there's Martin now. Please introduce Sir Gilliforth to the Marquis."

"Yes, of course! My gratitude, Your Highness. Though if it wouldn't impose too greatly, perhaps we might converse briefly before—"

"Your Grace, surely you notice the Marquis gesturing rather urgently? He appears quite eager for your company."

"He does? Oh, yes, I suppose..."

The pattern repeated endlessly—nobles politely but firmly redirected by the Agema, departing with poorly concealed disappointment.

Weren't their approaches motivated by national interests and trade opportunities?

Then Medea noticed the telling details she'd been too focused to recognize.

Eyes that lingered too long. Cheeks that flushed at her slightest attention. Fingers that fidgeted nervously, uncertain where to rest.

Tonight was Saint Augustine's Night—the evening when young men and women sought the partners destined to share their lives.

She realized, belatedly, that she too numbered among those bright-eyed, eligible young women the festival celebrated.

Medea released a soft, bitter laugh.

Unfortunately, such tender emotions had long ago withered and crumbled to dust within her.

Perhaps I once appeared exactly like them...

In their eager expressions, Medea glimpsed fragments of her former self—the naive girl she'd been before betrayal had hollowed her out.

Was my vulnerability so transparent? Was I truly that easy to manipulate?

"...Your Highness? Are you quite well?"

"Forgive me, but I find myself somewhat overwhelmed. We shall have to continue this conversation another time. Neril, assist me."

Medea forcibly dismissed the dark memories threatening to surface.

"Of course... please, do rest comfortably. My apologies for imposing."

The assembled suitors recognized the polite but definitive dismissal. They withdrew reluctantly, disappointment etched clearly across their features despite their best efforts at composure.

"Your Highness, are you certain you're all right?"

Neril settled Medea into an ornate chair positioned at the temple's periphery, concern evident in her attentive gaze.

"I'm fine. I merely required a plausible excuse to conclude the conversations."

Yet Neril had glimpsed the shadow that had momentarily crossed her mistress's carefully controlled expression.

"Don't concern yourself with me. More importantly, I haven't seen our 'fox' recently. It's well past time he should have secured the 'rabbit' and returned."

Even while seated, Medea's sharp gaze swept the temple's interior, cataloging movements and absences with practiced precision.

"I shall investigate immediately, Your Highness."

Neril inclined her head.

"Yes. You understand? There must be no unforeseen complications tonight."

Left momentarily alone, Medea's attention drifted upward toward the grand altar where Peleus would soon deliver his address.

Magnificent stained glass adorned the soaring ceiling, fracturing the fading daylight into kaleidoscopic patterns that painted the sacred space in jeweled hues.

Behind the central altar, an imposing stone figure commanded her focus.

The gray statue of the Goddess stood resolute and unyielding, seeming to absorb all the chromatic brilliance streaming through the stained glass—transforming that radiant light into something more solemn, more profound.

The carved figure emanated an aura both nurturing and formidable, benevolent yet implacable—divine authority made manifest in stone.

Medea found herself staring at the statue, transfixed by emotions she couldn't quite identify.

A sound from nearby broke her reverie—footsteps approaching, fabric rustling.

Presumably the guard who'd departed earlier to fetch refreshments had returned.

"Leave it there. I'm not thirsty at present."

Silence.

No acknowledgment. Yet the presence lingered, refusing to withdraw—an irritating persistence that demanded attention.

Medea lifted her head with mounting annoyance.

Then she froze completely.

Brown hair caught the multicolored light streaming through the stained glass—and in that chromatic illumination, the strands appeared blood-red, as though dipped in crimson.

"...Mede—no. Princess."

The man had begun to speak her name with familiar intimacy before catching himself, hastily amending to proper formality.

That voice—dry, constricted, weighted with suppressed emotion she couldn't decipher.

The owner of that haunted voice was Jason.

The past she had murdered and buried now stood before her—breathing, speaking, real. Some ghosts refuse to remain in their graves.
