Chapter 249

Children of
Prophecy

"The child of prophecy has survived the fog of disaster."

The old man's words tumbled out like prayers too long held silent.

"The child of prophecy has survived the fog of disaster. The goddess has forgiven our folly and has not yet abandoned this land."

"At last—the day of atonement has arrived!"

"The power of Shadeia passes only through the maternal line. Have you never experienced divine grace? Have you never glimpsed places that no other can see or reach?"

Neril's eyes sharpened dangerously as she glared at the stranger prostrating himself in gratitude and the strange old herbalist.

"Why are you blocking our path with this incomprehensible babble?"

"Wait."

Medea raised her hand, halting Neril's advance.

A second life had been granted to her—a miracle that no one in recorded history had ever achieved or experienced.

"Three years ago... were you the one who spoke to me of the Dawn's Goblet?"

The old man before her reminded Medea of an elderly woman she had once encountered in a shabby alley in Valdina. They shared something ineffable—a quality difficult to articulate.

"Ah. The priestess has walked that path."

The old man sighed as though he comprehended everything, then stepped forward and took Medea's hand in his own.

She was about to speak when—

KAAAAAAK—!

"Wyverns! It's a wyvern swarm!"

At the village entrance, a terrible cacophony erupted—human screams intertwined with demonic shrieks.

Medea lifted her gaze.

A flock of wyverns circled overhead, their wings spread wide against the gray sky. Intermediate-level beasts with the wings of bats and the heads of lizards.

They blotted out the light until day became night in an instant.

The Prophecy

"Already... so soon..."

The old man squeezed his eyes shut and spoke with desperate urgency.

"You are the child of prophecy—heir to the lineage of Shadeia, successor to the Priestess. Because our tribe was deceived and your mother was unjustly cast out, your generation's power will be even greater than hers."

As he spoke, tears flowed ceaselessly from his weathered eyes.

The weight of decades of regret and remorse seemed to press down upon his shoulders.

"But remember—the greater the power, the greater the trials that follow. You must constantly seek the light and drive back the darkness that presses in upon you."

"I don't understand a single word you're saying."

Rather than attempt an explanation, the old man pressed something into her palm—a necklace drawn from within his robes.

A wing-shaped pendant cradled a transparent crystal beneath a golden ring. Multiple layers of delicate gold filigree overlapped the stone, catching the dim light.

"I am no priest—I cannot properly discern your fate. But you were born under the orbit of Venus, and that strength should carry you through any ordeal. There is a place upon Venus that determines your destiny—"

KAAAAAAK—!

A wyvern spotted Medea's party and dove toward them.

Neril leapt skyward and severed its head in a single stroke. The beast's wings beat uselessly as it plummeted toward the chapel.

The ancient roof collapsed in a shower of rotted timber and dust.

KIAAAAGH—!

Another wyvern descended like a hurled spear, talons extended toward Medea.

A flash of silver—and its clawed foot tumbled away, severed cleanly.

"Are you unharmed?"

Cesare materialized atop the falling wyvern's corpse.

Mounted on his black stallion, sword still in motion, he appeared utterly unruffled—his composure all the more striking against the chaos erupting around him.

"Stop them! They're converging on Her Highness!"

Yet the wyverns continued to swarm toward Medea as though drawn by an invisible force.

"Why do they keep gathering here?!"

Gallo gritted his teeth as he cut down beast after beast.

Wyverns were creatures of pure instinct—yet these showed no hesitation even as their kin were slaughtered around them.

"Medea. We must leave. Now."

Cesare had closed the distance between them. He held Silva's reins in his right hand, the great black horse stamping impatiently.

"...You should seek shelter as well."

The old man shook his head at Medea's words.

"This is punishment visited upon our tribe for failing to recognize the true Priestess. Do not concern yourself with us—we have learned to survive such trials over the years."

WHOOSH!

As if to prove his point, a wyvern impaled on a massive harpoon crashed to the ground beside his companion.

The old man's gentle eyes hardened with sudden battle-fire. Medea recalled that this was a herbalist who climbed the treacherous Moonlit Cliffs to harvest rare plants.

"Do not worry for us. When what was twisted is finally set right, this cruel separation shall end."

"Child of Selene—go swiftly. Walk your path without looking back or hesitating."

His gaze lingered on Medea, then dropped to the necklace clutched in her hand.

"I never imagined I would live long enough to fulfill my mission—to deliver the Priestess's mark with my own hands."

Then he turned and plunged into the swarm of wyverns.

Medea felt a strange, inexplicable pull—an urge to follow the old man and his people. To pursue these strangers who had spoken her mother's name and poured forth words she could not comprehend.

"Medea."

But Cesare's voice called her back.

She turned her mount and followed him. Silva galloped powerfully toward the anchored ship, utterly unintimidated by the ferocious beasts filling the sky.

Medea's fingers tightened around the necklace.

Venus. Shadeia. The Priestess.

A feast of unfamiliar words—yet her heart would not stop pounding.

At Sea

The ship carrying Medea and her companions pulled swiftly away from shore.

By the time the wyvern swarm appeared as distant specks against the horizon—small enough to cover with an outstretched palm—they had already begun dispersing in other directions.

"Remarkable that you managed to keep your composure through all that."

Gallo shook his head in disbelief.

"The beasts in this region are influenced by the evil dragon's movements. The fact that the wyverns were driven this far from their territory means the dragon itself must be stirring."

"Don't take it too seriously—we haven't even started the real journey yet."

Gallo clapped Terence on the shoulder.

"What's the point of worrying? Just think of it as a very large wyvern, yes?"

Terence released a long-suffering sigh.

After another full day at sea, the ship bearing Medea and her party reached the coast of the Forest of Eternal Darkness.

Cesare issued orders to a portion of his men.

"Zeta, Kappa—return to the base and secure the rear. Gather intelligence from across the continent and relay it to me."

"Yes, Your Highness."

"I've already marked the falcons with your scent. Simply hand your reports to them."

Gallo gestured toward the birds circling overhead, their talons glinting.

The party disembarked with their supplies and horses, then paused at the edge of the black sand beach.

The sea breeze was cold and damp. Waves rolled slowly over the dark shore, wetting the horses' hooves.

The path from the coastline to the forest entrance resembled a natural tunnel—a tangled arch of ancient roots and weathered stone.

At last, they stood before the true entrance to the forest.

The canopy was so dense that even in broad daylight, the interior appeared pitch black. Beyond the threshold lay absolute darkness.

"The atmosphere alone is enough to unsettle a man."

Tom grumbled under his breath.

Neril's response was immediate.

"Then stay behind me."

"I possess the heart of a warrior. Retreat is not in my vocabulary."

She kicked her horse forward.

"Brother Gallo, shall I take the lead?"

The sly provocation as she passed was not lost on him.

"Oh? Look at this one."

Gallo spurred his mount after her, followed by Terence and Cesare's men. Medea and Cesare brought up the rear.

A faint fog rose from the forest floor as they entered, darkness enveloping the expedition like a shroud—as though fate itself were testing their resolve.

Meanwhile — The Holy Kingdom

Sacramontis — Headquarters of the Knights Templar

"Captain... must we truly do this?"

In the normally silent quarters of the Templar Knights, bewildered voices echoed off stone walls.

"I struggle to understand how it serves God's will for us to hunt down and kill a woman who fled for her life—when the Princess of Valdina is neither heretic nor sinner."

Leonor swallowed a sigh at the obvious skepticism in their voices.

"This matter was decided by the Holy Council. We act as instruments of the Central Temple—nothing more."

The Council's Debate

Leonor recalled the contentious assembly that had preceded this order.

"Valdina's Princess has disappeared!"

"Peleus obviously concealed his sister. A man who would declare war on the Katzen Empire for her sake would never accept the oracle at face value!"

When news of Valdina's situation—which Sissair had labored to delay by every means possible—finally reached the central temple, the most furious voice had been that of High Priest Paul.

"The oracle has not yet been formally proclaimed across the continent. As priests, is it not dangerous to brand her with preemptive condemnation?"

"Indeed. Moreover, the Emperor of Valdina has expressed his understanding of the gravity of the situation and continues diplomatic overtures—"

"Ha! 'Understanding the gravity'? This is clearly Valdina's cunning stratagem to buy time. Can you not see it?!"

Paul had refused to yield even before the other High Priests who counseled neutrality.

"The Goddess has bestowed her merciful grace upon our holy nation for the first time in an age. If we fail to uphold her noble will through negligence, the most terrible misfortune shall befall us. This is no time to sit idle!"

After much debate, the majority vote had authorized the order to the Knights Templar.

"Leonor! You must dispatch the Knights immediately and track down Valdina's Princess! She should have been seized the moment we received word. Where has that cunning woman fled by now?!"

Leonor had sensed that High Priest Paul was fixated less upon the oracle itself than upon its designated subject—the Princess of Valdina.

The position of High Priest ranked immediately below the Pope. Only the most devout and noble among the Central Temple's clergy could attain such standing.

Yet in that moment, Paul had seemed merely... human. A man determined to bring down the princess who had humiliated him and ensure her miserable end.

"How can you remain silent?! You witnessed it yourself—what a cunning woman she is!"

"...We shall depart at first light."

Leonor had struggled to mask his reluctance.

"Captain, surely..."

The Temple Knights still could not conceal their misgivings.

In truth, Leonor shared their reservations.

"There is nothing to be done. Once an oracle has been pronounced, to defy its will is heresy. It is sin."

But as Captain, he could not permit division within his order. He disciplined his knights with stern words.

"...Captain. I wish to volunteer for this mission."

Theo was the sole Templar to step forward willingly.

"Ah. Thank you, Theo. I am relieved that at least one among you comprehends the gravity of our duty."

Leonor, who had been forced to play the villain alone, was secretly moved. He clapped the handsome young knight on the shoulder.

"...Yes, Captain."

The piety befitting a knight of God was evident in his firm grip upon his sword, in the clean lines of his features.

Leonor felt vindicated in his choice of Theo as successor—a man capable of separating personal feelings from sacred duty.

The True Reason

But Leonor did not know.

Theo's fingertips trembled as they gripped his sword.

'...If she is captured, I will be the only one capable of creating an opening—a chance for her to escape the Templars' watch.'

The true reason his trusted successor had volunteered remained hidden.

Theo lowered his eyes.

The fear of betraying the goddess he so revered still coursed through him like fever.

Yet from somewhere deep within rose a strange, inexplicable certainty—almost a calling, a sense of mission.

Medea Valdina's death must be prevented.

"Yes. I must go."

Theo clenched his fists and renewed his silent vow.

The following dawn, the elite of the Knights Templar rode forth from the Holy Kingdom.
