Chapter 313

The Tower's
Cold Embrace

"Pride is the last armor of the fallen."

Neither the golden throne nor the vast power that commanded an empire could salvage the Emperor's pride at this moment.

The banquet's elegant melody had long since faded from his ears. The intoxicating satisfaction that had filled him—as though the entire world existed to celebrate his magnificence—lay shattered at his feet.

That man still stands so tall. So vital. While I...

The thought burned through him like venom.

"So the Western Empress still harbors such vigor that she would betray me behind my back?"

Fire blazed within Perdiccas II. His grip on the golden staff trembled with barely contained fury.

"Western Empress."

He descended from the platform, each step a supreme effort of will. Every fiber of his being screamed to strike her down where she stood—to beat her until nothing remained of that dignified composure.

But he was Emperor. And emperors did not lose control in public.

"Since you possess such remarkable strength of will—enough to make my imperial authority seem laughable—perhaps confinement would serve you well."

He swallowed the trembling rage in his breath and scratched his chin, forcing a cruel smile.

"The abyss might be fitting... but no. You remain my wife, after all. That would be excessive."

His eyes never once glanced toward the man standing beside her.

"A tower will suffice. The cold winds there should help clear your head."

Silence hung heavy in the air.

"Your Majesty, I beg you—look upon me with mercy once more..."

"Western Empress."

The Emperor's voice cut like a blade.

"You will 'cool your head' and reflect upon your actions. Now go."

His tone sharpened further.

"To cast me aside so readily—twenty years of devotion, wasted in a single evening."

The Western Empress swallowed the silent sigh that threatened to escape her throat.

Her gaze found her son Juan, whose face was a mask of bewildered anguish. With a subtle shake of her head, she warned him: Do not interfere. Do not make this worse.

"As Your Majesty commands."

She bowed her head briefly, accepting the sentence with what remained of her composure.

"But I ask one thing, Your Majesty. Remember this: I swear before the goddess herself—if I have acted dishonorably, I will accept any punishment. Even eternal damnation. Even endless cycles of rebirth in suffering."

Perdiccas II waved his hand dismissively, as though swatting away an insect.

"Take the Western Empress away."

"Release me. I know the path to the tower."

The Western Empress rose. With deliberate grace, she smoothed her disheveled hair and pressed the wrinkles from her stained gown. Then she lifted her chin.

The gestures could not erase her humiliation—but they projected an image of a woman who would not crumble, no matter what indignities life demanded she endure.

"I take my leave, Your Majesty."

The Empress's gaze swept across the hall one final time.

She saw the bloodied knight from Valdina. Her son Juan, still frozen in contemplation. Alfred—the man whose presence had unraveled everything—watching her with eyes full of ancient pain.

And finally, she locked gazes with the First Prince and his wife, who regarded her with expressions of carefully measured neutrality.

To construct such an elaborate trap using old affections as bait... that barren woman lacks the cunning.

Which left only one possibility.

"Princess."

Her voice carried the quiet venom of recognition.

"I confess I underestimated you."

How did she discover Alfred's existence? How did she know to use him against me?

The answer crystallized with painful clarity: the First Princess had aimed at her with surgical precision.

"Forgive me, Your Majesty. I merely wished to protect Your Majesty—the same Your Majesty who showed me such... kindness... when I first arrived at this court."

The First Princess bowed her head, her expression the very picture of innocent dismay.

"I never imagined matters would escalate so dramatically."

But the Western Empress understood the message hidden beneath those honeyed words: You aimed at me first. This is merely my response.

The Western Empress's teeth ground together behind her serene smile. She clutched her ruined dress until her knuckles whitened.

"Your concern is... noted, Princess."

Her benevolent expression never wavered.

"I shall remember your thoughtfulness most fondly. And when I return—"

A pause, deliberate and weighted.

"—I will be certain to repay it in full."

She spoke as though the tower were merely a temporary inconvenience—as though her return to power were an inevitability rather than a distant hope.

The Emperor watched the Western Empress's retreating figure with cold, calculating eyes. Only when she had vanished from sight did he turn back to address the silent hall.

He scratched his chin thoughtfully.

"The Inner Palace cannot remain without leadership. Eastern Empress—you shall assume full authority during this... transition."

Imprisonment in the tower was humiliation enough. But to strip the Western Empress of her actual power—to hand it wholesale to her rival?

The pronouncement fell with deliberate cruelty, delivered in a tone so frigid it froze even the Third Prince in place. The son who had always tacitly supported his father now found himself invisible, irrelevant to the proceedings.

"Eastern Empress. Have you lost your tongue?"

The woman in question started, clearly overwhelmed by the sudden reversal of fortune.

"Y-yes! That is—I will serve with utmost diligence, Your Majesty!"

The Emperor nodded curtly, already moving on to the next target of his wrath.

"Templar Commander."

"Yes... Your Majesty."

"You are an imbecile."

The public rebuke—delivered without hesitation or softening—demonstrated the full depth of Perdiccas II's fury.

"I entrusted you with the protection of my family. Instead, you fumble about without proper investigation, creating diplomatic incidents with Valdina. You are hereby relieved of command. Await my decision regarding your punishment."

"I beg Your Majesty's mercy."

The Templar Commander—who had watched the evening spiral far from his original designs—prostrated himself, careful to avoid the Emperor's gaze.

"First Princess."

Medea remained perfectly still, her expression unreadable.

"Your intentions were admirable, but you shall face consequences for the disruption you have caused."

Perdiccas II's emotions warred within him. Fury at his wife's betrayal. Grudging acknowledgment of his daughter-in-law's role in exposing it. Wounded pride that she—of all people—had witnessed the shameful spectacle.

Yet he remained bound by appearances. He could not mistreat a Princess of Valdina without risking diplomatic catastrophe.

"Words alone will not compensate your nation—or your injured escort—for the indignities they have suffered."

His piercing gaze settled upon Medea.

"Therefore, I shall grant your escort the position that fool has forfeited. She shall lead the Templars."

Medea bowed with practiced elegance, betraying no emotion.

"Your Majesty's generosity overwhelms me. However, I must respectfully decline."

"Compared to the countless privileges and honors Your Majesty has already bestowed upon me, my actions tonight were insignificant. I could not accept such a reward in good conscience."

It was the perfect response—the embodiment of Valdinian character, where debts of honor were repaid rather than exploited.

She turned to face the Valdinian minister.

"Lord Sissair, I trust you understand the necessity of this outcome?"

"Your Highness, I fear I cannot comply. Valdina's honor demands otherwise."

Sissair adjusted his spectacles, his expression glacial.

"How long have you dwelt in Katzen, that you would so readily overlook insults to your homeland?"

His tone sharpened with each word.

"Did the Empress's tender 'affection' for her family injure your limbs as well as your memory? Tell me, Your Highness—do these people deserve such devotion? A nation that ceased treating you as human the moment you crossed its borders?"

"If you intend to discard one of Agema's finest knights—treating her as a servant to be sacrificed for the sake of these people—"

He met Medea's eyes with unflinching directness.

"Then I shall take Sir Menil with me. If she remains here any longer, even her bones will be ground to dust."

It was a devastating rebuke—aimed squarely at Perdiccas II and his Imperial Knights.

The veins at the Emperor's temples pulsed visibly.

"Lord Sissair!"

Medea's voice rose with apparent indignation.

"I am the wife of the First Prince. When I took His Highness's hand and entered this land, I swore before the goddess to live as a citizen of Katzen. Is it not natural that I should prioritize this nation's interests? Why do you condemn me for honoring that vow?"

Her voice remained calm, but cold fury threaded through every syllable.

"If you insist upon this course, then do as you will. But remember—I have written to my brother directly regarding these matters."

A pause, deliberate and dangerous.

"I wonder how the King will view his minister trampling upon his only sister's dignity to salvage his own pride."

Sissair and Medea faced each other in tense silence.

The intensity of their confrontation made the assembled guests momentarily forget they shared a homeland. Old rumors resurfaced in whispered exchanges: that during the King's frequent absences for war, the young minister and the Princess had been... close.

"...Very well, Your Highness. I shall defer to your judgment."

In the end, it was Sissair who yielded first.

"But I will take Sir Menil with me when I depart."

The hard-liner minister—renowned throughout the realm for his uncompromising nature—had bowed his head the instant the Princess invoked her brother.

The rumors of how deeply the King of Valdina cherished his sister, it seemed, were no exaggeration.

Perdiccas II observed the exchange with a complicated expression. He had not expected his daughter-in-law to resolve his diplomatic dilemma so elegantly.

Perhaps the Princess of Valdina was not quite the nuisance he had assumed.

"Minister, if you take her in that manner, what becomes of my face?"

He turned to his attendant.

"Chamberlain—fetch my personal sword. Present it to that escort as a gift."

"At once, Your Majesty."

A blade was brought forth—infinitely superior to the heavy ceremonial sword that had concealed the damning letters.

It was placed before Menil with reverent care.

"Is that... the Heart of the West Wind?"

Whispers rippled through the crowd.

The legendary blade—forged from mithril and consecrated by the Central Temple itself. According to legend, its wielder could strike with the speed of the wind itself, their blade singing through the air twice before an enemy could react.
