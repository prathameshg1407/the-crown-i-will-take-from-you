Chapter 375
Epilogue

Golden Stars
Descending

"When darkness finally closes, light reveals the path forward."

Boom.

The rift that had swallowed Jason and Rachel sealed itself completely.

At that same moment, the bodies of Neriel, Saya, Cesare, and Peleus—blurred and flickering only moments before—solidified once more into clarity.

"What in the world just happened...?"

"It's divine punishment! The goddess has judged Castullo!"

Someone shouted through gritted teeth.

Relief at having survived transformed rapidly into fury toward the villain and exultation at witnessing his end.

"He tried to take us all as sacrifices—with that monstrous face!"

"More vicious than the devil himself! More wicked than the warlocks! What a Prince—what a waste of a title! You bastard! May you rot in hell for eternity!"

"The Grand Duke and that fake Saintess both deserved to die! That madman got what he earned!"

They spat their contempt into the ruins.

Rachel and Jason, who had joined hands in mutual greed, ultimately drove each other into the abyss and perished together.

Whether Jason's demise came from the goddess statue crushing his skull, the price of shattering the Golden Rule, or divine retribution for threatening the goddess's chosen champion—none could say for certain.

But one truth remained absolute:

Medea would never see Jason or Rachel again in this life.

They were sealed in a void from which they could never escape.

Medea picked up a broken fragment of the crown.

Though everything else summoned from the distorted timeline had lost form and vanished, this tarnished piece remained.

Was it Jason's obsession—his refusal to release her even at the very end?

Or evidence of his original sin?

At that moment, warmth enveloped Medea's trembling form.

"...Dea. God, Dea."

She looked up to find Cesare holding her tightly.

His breath against her shoulder came ragged and uneven. Medea could feel the terror he'd endured.

It was both touching and heartbreaking—this man who hadn't flinched when swearing an eternal vow that would burn his very soul now breathed with such desperate anxiety.

"Dea, whenever I'm near you, my heart feels like it might shatter."

His voice dropped low, rough with emotion.

Cesare would never forget that moment—when he'd become transparent, when the boundary between past and present had tried to separate him from Medea.

"Me too."

He stiffened slightly at her response.

"While you slept, I worried constantly. What if something went wrong? What if I lost you?"

Her steady voice trembled at the end.

Even after awakening to the divine power flowing through her veins, even in the moments when she'd prepared to end Rachel and Jason, one fear had persisted above all others.

Cesare Katzen. I'm afraid of losing this person.

Medea embraced him fiercely.

His lips descended on her forehead, her eyelids, spreading warmth as though releasing all the pent-up tension.

"Your Majesty! The coronation must be concluded at once!"

Marquis Leniard's urgent shout echoed from below the altar.

The imperial crown sat upon the velvet cushion he clutched protectively.

Even when Jason's dark magic had warped reality itself, when primordial horrors poured from the void—this old man had risked his life to safeguard that crown.

Technically, Cesare's coronation as Emperor remained unfinished.

"Your Majesty."

At the Marquis's plea, the tension in Medea's body eased slightly.

"They're calling you."

She smiled gently and nudged him toward the altar. The old man looked ready to collapse.

But Cesare didn't budge.

"It doesn't matter. I won't become Emperor."

"What?"

Medea lifted her head from his embrace.

"I will not accept the crown."

"You're saying you'll give up the throne—when it's right there within your grasp?"

"Dea, I promised you I would leave this place and establish independence. Even if circumstances have changed, that promise hasn't."

Cesare gripped her hand tightly.

"You are the only thing that matters to me. Without you, nothing else holds any meaning."

His golden eyes darkened with intensity.

The grip on her hand tightened.

This man, who rarely revealed his innermost feelings, couldn't hide his anxiety now. He feared Medea would be lost to him forever because of this turn of events.

"I will establish our independence. Katzen was a land of pain for you. So I want to change that. I want you to live somewhere safe and warm. I'll be there with you."

On the glacier, Cesare hadn't forgotten the promise made that day.

Even the throne awaiting him—he would turn his back on it without a shred of regret.

All for her.

"If you're going to leave..."

Medea kissed him mid-sentence.

He froze.

His eyes struggled to comprehend the meaning of the kiss even as the corners of his lips softened involuntarily.

"What if I said I'd stay in Katzen?"

"...Are you serious?"

"Cesare, you've proven to me that this place is no longer just the land I once knew."

New happy memories had been layered over her suffering, one by one, as though to compensate for all she'd endured.

Now, recalling the past no longer brought only pain.

There were more days when she woke in a warm embrace than days haunted by nightmares.

More days when she felt the gentle coolness of wind on her cheeks than days when cold seemed to slice through her heart.

Medea took Cesare's hand and placed it on her stomach.

"I don't mind drawing a future with you and this child in this empire."

"That's enough."

Cesare kissed her. Joy surged through his lips like a tide.

"Dea, if you're in my tomorrow—if our child is in my tomorrow—I can do anything."

The black star cried out in joy, having finally found its true owner.

Medea embraced her beloved, loyal star by the neck.

✦ ✦ ✦

People witnessed a cascade of golden stars descending upon the two embracing figures

Pure white radiance expanded outward in widening circles, illuminating Medea with brilliant light—as though commanding her never to forget.

The darkness was expelled by boundless luminescence.

The hidden blue sky was finally revealed.

The remaining corpses of those who had fallen, touched by the light, crumbled to ash and were carried away without trace by the gentle wind.

"Saintess..."

Those who witnessed the miracle knelt and pressed their foreheads to the ground.

It was an act of reverence for the true representative of the goddess.

Everyone present had become a witness.

The story of the false Saintess's downfall and the moment the true Saintess appeared on earth would soon spread across the entire continent through their testimony.

The People's Cry

"SAINTESS MEDEA! PROTECT THIS LAND!"

The survivors' cries of relief and gratitude shook the roofless cathedral to its foundations.

Several Days Later

Katzen Capital, Persepolis

Several days had passed since the goddess's champion descended and the unfinished coronation of the Emperor of Katzen.

"Well, there's nothing left intact."

Gallo clicked his tongue as he surveyed the cathedral—ceiling blown away, pillars shattered halfway.

"Boss, I think it'd be better to tear everything down and rebuild from scratch."

"Not a bad idea."

Cesare nodded absently.

The cathedral's basement was already contaminated with dark magic. It would take considerable time to cleanse it completely.

"Your Majesty—no, Your Highness."

Marquis Leniard hastily corrected himself at Cesare's sharp glance.

Because the coronation remained incomplete, Cesare had not accepted the title of Emperor and maintained his status as First Prince.

"Then please, hold the coronation ceremony in the palace. We cannot leave the throne vacant."

But Cesare was already striding far ahead, paying no heed.

Medea felt a presence behind her as she gathered remnants from the ceremony.

"Saintess."

It was Theo.

She noticed he wore the armor of a Knight of the Holy Nation.

"You're awake?"

"...There was no rest."

He bowed his head.

"You did well. You led the Facade admirably."

After pouring his holy power into countering Jason's dark magic, Theo had collapsed unconscious. He'd only just recovered.

Instead of showing pleasure at her praise, he knelt immediately.

Thud.

At the sound, Gallo and Terence turned in surprise.

Gallo moved to intervene, but Terence raised a hand to stop him.

"I apologize, Your Highness."

Theo's voice trembled.

"If I had kept closer watch on Rachel from the beginning, none of this would have happened."

He lowered his head further.

Ultimately, all this tragedy was his fault. He felt ashamed to even breathe.

Yet he still had a reason to live.

"I know this is shameless, but..."

Theo bowed repeatedly, forehead striking the cold stone floor.

"Saintess... please save the Holy Nation."

Then the High Priest of Katzen knelt beside Theo and added his plea.

"Please help us. Dark forces have seized the temple and murdered His Holiness. We have no way of knowing the fate of the survivors."

The vassals of the First Prince's faction weren't surprised by the request.

They had already anticipated that after defeating Rachel and the warlocks, Medea would need to restore the Holy Nation.

The problem was that Medea's position was no longer just that of a Saintess.

"Absolutely not! What are you suggesting?!"

Marquis Leniard blocked Theo and the High Priest's path.

"There's no need for Her Highness to go personally. We can send aid from Katzen and provide active support."

"No. Only the Saintess can restore order. Now that dark magic may have penetrated the heart of the central temple itself, only the Saintess can set this chaos right."

Theo raised his head and met Medea's eyes.

"So please. I beg you."

He pleaded once more.

Even now, he had no peace thinking of the Temple Knights' remains, abandoned somewhere in the cold.

Medea met Cesare's eyes and nodded slowly.

"I'm going to the Holy Nation."

"Your Highness...!"

The suppressed cries of the First Prince's followers seemed to gather into one anguished sound.

"I'll go with you."

A voice spoke suddenly from beside her.

To Be Continued

Medea turned toward the voice, and their eyes met.

Together, they would face whatever darkness remained.
