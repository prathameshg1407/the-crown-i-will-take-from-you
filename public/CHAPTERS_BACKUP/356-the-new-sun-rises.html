Chapter 356

The New Sun Rises

"Katzen welcomes its new emperor—even if the light burns flesh from bone."

The first thing Terence saw upon entering the blood-soaked chamber was Cesare cradling someone in his arms.

"Cesare, you..."

"Your Highness, the First Prince..."

The Chamberlain's words died mid-sentence.

He'd realized the person the First Prince held wasn't the Emperor—but Eratosthenes.

Cesare quietly wiped the Marquis's face with his sleeve.

Until the dark crimson stains disappeared and the familiar, dignified old man emerged once more, Cesare's movements remained as careful and reverent as if performing a sacred rite.

He covered Eratosthenes with the monk's robes that had been saturated with spilled blood.

What in God's name... How should I interpret this?

The Chamberlain struggled to comprehend a son treating his father's murderer with such tenderness.

"Chamberlain."

Cesare's voice cut through the silence.

"The Emperor was not murdered."

The Chamberlain froze.

"Lord Eratosthenes died from wounds inflicted by the Emperor himself. His Majesty's paranoia wasn't limited to recent days—he mistakenly believed the Marquis had poisoned him."

Only Cesare's measured voice echoed through the Imperial chambers.

"Your Highness, but—"

"There was no poison. Unfortunately, the Emperor misunderstood the medicine he was given."

Silence.

"Chamberlain, you've always championed stability through chaos. You've worked tirelessly to keep this nation from fracturing. I trust that commitment extends to today."

It was both warning and command—the truth of the Emperor's death must not spread beyond these walls.

Understanding dawned on the Chamberlain.

Cesare had no intention of allowing Eratosthenes' name to be tarnished.

The biological son of Perdiccas II was concealing his father's murder to protect the one who shared no blood with him.

Moreover, this narrative suggests Lord Eratosthenes died a noble death, innocently sacrificed. A grand state funeral alongside the Emperor would be appropriate.

A funeral shared with his enemy—it wasn't merely inadequate. It was an insult.

If Perdiccas II possessed the temperament the Chamberlain knew, he'd be furious enough to claw his way back from the underworld.

Yet simultaneously...

Ah.

The Chamberlain understood.

This meant Perdiccas II would never receive retribution for the wounds he'd inflicted upon countless souls.

"I understand, Your Majesty."

The Chamberlain bowed deeply.

The respectfully altered title needed no explanation—he'd just acknowledged the empire's new master.

Cesare gave no response.

The attendant watched the First Prince's retreating back.

In this place drenched with blood, after witnessing the deaths of both biological father and surrogate grandfather, the man hadn't wavered in the slightest.

Even with traces of divine punishment still marking his skin.

The Chamberlain shuddered.

No oracle, no god could halt the path such an unyielding being had chosen.

"Katzen... you're welcoming a new sun."

Even if flesh burned in its heat, the pain would be forgotten in the brilliance of that light.

Cesare emerged from the Emperor's chambers with steady steps.

Palace attendants who recognized him bowed low.

News of Perdiccas II's death was already spreading.

Even those who hadn't yet heard the announcement paid tribute—recognizing that Katzen had already fallen into the First Prince's hands.

Some sought to ingratiate themselves early. Others, desperate opportunists, hoped to claim belated rewards.

Yet none dared approach given the First Prince's forbidding demeanor. They could only watch his back as he passed.

First Prince's Palace

Medea only learned that Cesare had awakened after arriving at the palace.

"If something this significant happened, you should have informed me immediately."

"Forgive me, Your Highness."

Menil scratched her head sheepishly.

"The moment the First Prince opened his eyes—despite Lord Terence's protests—he heard news of Your Highness and rushed to the Imperial Palace. I assumed you'd cross paths..."

Medea bit her lip silently.

Her heart sank at the realization they'd missed each other despite both searching so desperately.

"Still, how fortunate that the First Prince has awakened! The palace was in a celebratory mood just hours ago."

Despite Medea's scolding, joy filled Menil's voice.

"At least there's no longer any need to evacuate Your Highness from Katzen."

Though she hadn't shown it, Menil had been receiving increasingly urgent summons to bring Medea home.

Letters from her superior, D'Angel, now contained more insults than actual orders.

Yet since arriving in Katzen, Menil's perspective on the First Prince had shifted considerably while working at the villa.

Having stood with the First Prince's faction at the crossroads of life and death multiple times, a sense of comradeship had taken root. She found herself hesitating to follow those distant commands.

Above all, unless her true master—Medea—ordered it, Menil intended to remain.

"The priests and wizards all departed without helping, yet somehow he recovered... At least he's conscious."

Medea paused.

At a time when neither holy power nor mana could touch him, what did Cesare do to awaken?

Did my blood actually work?

To know for certain whether that truly revived him, she needed to see him with her own eyes.

Only when she witnessed those calm golden eyes—not closed in unconsciousness—would she feel relief.

Medea curled her fingertips inward, concealing her silent anxiety.

"Prepare the carriage. I must go to the Imperial Palace."

"What if your paths cross again? Gallo sent word to Sir Terence. You should wait here."

Mrs. Tolin positioned herself beside Medea protectively.

Urgent footsteps echoed through the hall.

"Your Highness!"

Kensington and Gallo burst in simultaneously, their expressions grim.

"What are you doing pushing yourself like this? Menil, give Kensington a handkerchief—"

Kensington, who normally struggled with his injured leg, had abandoned his cane entirely to run. Sweat beaded on his forehead.

"The Marquis of Eratosthenes poisoned the Emperor."

"...What?"

Medea stood frozen, speechless, handkerchief forgotten in her hand.

Menil and the other attendants shared her shock. Mrs. Tolin drew a sharp breath.

"And the Marquis took his own life on the spot."

Gallo's addition struck like a physical blow.

"Your Highness, are you all right?"

Even for Medea, the Emperor's death came as a shock.

Even in her previous life, Perdiccas II had lived an exceptionally long time.

He'd fought Jason in brutal power struggles to avoid ceding the throne, ultimately meeting his end caught in Medea's machinations.

But how could the Marquis of Eratosthenes eliminate Perdiccas II...

The reason that old man had become like dried kindling was because he'd burned everything for revenge.

Medea's gaze drifted to the window.

In the courtyard below, a small monk played with young attendants.

The child who'd initially been hesitant and timid now seemed to be gradually warming to others his age.

I should have realized when the Marquis sent that child to me.

Medea bit her lip.

She didn't fully understand why, but news of Eratosthenes affected her more deeply than the Emperor's death.

An old man who'd always appeared withered, yet who'd never turned her away.

Perhaps, if she'd acted differently, she could have prevented his death.

"The head of the imperial household requests Your Highness's presence at the palace immediately."

"...What about Cesare?"

He must be wounded by this as well... Could I have prevented it?

"His Highness the First Prince heard the news just as he was leaving the palace and returned inside immediately."

Kensington added this detail carefully.

"The palace gates have been sealed. It seems the general public hasn't been informed yet—by the First Prince's orders."

That the truth of the murder hasn't spread means Cesare doesn't want it known.

Medea nodded slowly.

"Prepare the carriage."

Coincidentally, there was no longer any conflict about where she needed to be.

The empty throne would not release Cesare.

Before departing for the Imperial Palace, Neril appeared at Medea's side.

"Your Highness."

Neril glanced around cautiously.

Seeing Gallo occupied with inspecting the carriage in the distance, she lowered her voice.

"Valdina's army has reached the border."

Medea's movements stilled.

"His Majesty accompanied them personally."

Peleus—dear God, why would he come here?

Her brother cared for her so fiercely that she'd never expected him to remain idle after hearing about the oracle.

But now Perdiccas II was dead.

It was dangerous timing—the resulting chaos could easily spread to Valdina.

"You know well that no one can stop His Majesty. Sir Sissair accompanied him."

If both of them abandoned their duties, what will happen to state affairs?

"Neither of them would abandon their responsibilities without making arrangements. This journey to Katzen is highly classified—they've asked me to keep it quiet."

As Neril spoke, she leaned closer, voice dropping to barely a whisper.

"Lord Sissair instructed me to tell Your Highness: The only opportunity to leave is now."

Silence.

"Your Highness, what the lord means is—"

"I understand his meaning perfectly."

With Perdiccas II's death creating chaos over succession, this was indeed the only window when Medea could easily depart Katzen.

Medea nodded slowly.

"Tell them to hold their position for now."

After a moment's hesitation, she drew a deep breath and climbed into the carriage.

Medea gazed through the window.

The Imperial Palace drew steadily closer.

With each turn of the wheels, with each passing moment, the path before her became clearer—even as the choices grew more impossible.
