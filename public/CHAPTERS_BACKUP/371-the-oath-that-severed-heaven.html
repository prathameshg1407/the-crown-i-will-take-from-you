Chapter 371

The Oath That
Severed Heaven

"When mortal will defies divine judgment, even the gods must bear witness."

The crimson beam struck Cesare with the same fury it had shown when he first refused the oracle's second revelation.

"Your Majesty, stop!"

The Marquis de Leniard, his face drained of color, leaped forward as though he could shield Cesare with his own body. It seemed he would witness the prince's corpse before the crown ever touched his head.

"No, no, please—if this continues...!"

But the Marquis was repelled by Cesare's black aura, thrown back like a leaf against a storm.

Crack! Crack!

The red light writhed and coiled like serpents around his black aura. Where they touched, Cesare's skin split open. Crimson scars bloomed across his flesh, spreading like bloody flowers.

Still, he did not stop.

"The flesh of those who break this covenant will be reduced to ash. Their souls will be torn apart in the primordial darkness, never to be known again in any life."

His voice rang through the cathedral without a single tremor, unwavering as stone.

But when the scars reached his beautiful face, splitting the skin across his cheek, Medea cried out before she could stop herself.

"Cesare!"

He raised his head.

Amidst the countless shouts and gasps filling the cathedral, Cesare responded only to her voice.

Meeting her green eyes, his thumb slowly traced her name where it was engraved beside his own on the oath stone.

All the truth he couldn't speak aloud would be sealed beneath this stone forever.

"How dare you try to take her from me?"

His will was absolute, unyielding.

He smiled—arrogant despite the pallor of pain on his face—and placed his bloodied hand firmly on the stone tablet.

"This oath will remain unchangeable even when the continent crumbles to dust and the gods themselves fade into oblivion."

"That's—that's madness. How could anyone make such an oath...?"

The color drained from every face in the cathedral.

In this uncertain world where even tomorrow was a fragile hope, who could promise eternity?

And not just eternity—but beyond death, beyond the destruction of body and soul?

"Why...?"

Amid the astonishment, Medea's voice broke.

Then she understood.

That arrogant man hadn't changed one bit since the day he first saw the primordial curse three years ago.

He was declaring—to Rachel, to Jason, to the goddess herself, to everyone in this world—

That they were wrong.

Nothing could separate Cesare and Medea.

'He knows what this means to me, Cesare.'

A man who had grasped the world with the singular desire to survive was now sacrificing his own life to become her shield.

So that not a single stain would touch her. So that Medea's victory would be complete.

She realized, in that moment, that joy and terror so intense it blanked her vision could exist side by side.

"NOOO! Stop! I said STOP!"

A sharp, hysterical scream tore through the air.

The beautiful mask of the gentle Saintess shattered. Rachel's face twisted with wild rage.

She could not endure the humiliation of being dragged into the abyss at the very moment she had been certain of perfect victory.

"Even now—even at the very end—I won't be able to take anything from you!"

Time and time again, she had tried to steal Medea's fate. And time and time again, she had been crushed.

She had been so certain this time would be different.

But even after all her scheming, the bitter taste of failure—of being unable to take even one thing from Medea—was unbearable.

"AHHHHHHH!"

Bloodshot veins burst across her violet eyes.

"What—what is that?!"

Someone pointed a trembling finger toward the sky.

Ssssshhhhh...

Stone dust scattered across the slab as Cesare's oath was engraved into the ancient tablet.

Dooooooooo—

A golden sphere materialized above the Stone of Oath.

The entire cathedral shuddered with a deep, resonant hum.

The domed ceiling, the marble floor, even the stone statues lining the walls—including the towering statue of the goddess at the center—began to vibrate with a low, sacred tone.

Divine Manifestation

"That... that's Venus...!"

A black star rose above the brilliant golden sphere and began to orbit it.

"Oh my God! Look—look at His Majesty's wounds!"

Under the glow of the golden sphere, Cesare's scars were healing.

Blood clotted. Split skin knitted back together, sealing itself as though time were reversing.

"It's healing!"

The black star cluster circled the golden sphere faster and faster.

It looked like a dark ring encircling Venus itself—a cosmic ballet of light and shadow.

The sight was breathtaking. Vast. Beautiful.

Even if the goddess herself descended to this mortal realm again, could anyone truly feel such awe? As if witnessing a cross-section of the universe itself?

Everyone in the cathedral stood with mouths agape, utterly spellbound.

This was a spectacle they might never witness again in a hundred lifetimes.

The black star cluster and the golden planet seemed to merge, two lights becoming one. Then they sharpened—transforming into a blade of pure celestial power.

SHHHINNNG!

The divine blade severed the crimson beam striking Cesare.

Kiiiiaaaaaaa—!

An inhuman scream echoed through the cathedral's vast hall.

The red beam writhed violently in midair like a snake with its spine cut, then crumbled into ash and vanished.

Awe swept through the cathedral like a wave.

"The divine punishment—it's gone!"

Someone shouted.

"The goddess has accepted the oath! She's blessed the First Prince!"

A golden power—unmistakably divine—had saved Cesare. A miracle had occurred, healing every scar inflicted by the goddess's punishment in an instant.

This was unprecedented in the entire history of the continent.

The audience erupted into chaos, thrown into turmoil by the shocking miracle.

Cesare's face bore no trace of scars. No pain remained.

He stood once more as the conqueror who had brought even storms to their knees.

He was the man who had subdued the oracle itself.

The crowd stared in awe at his overwhelming presence.

And then—

"HURRAH! HURRAH!"

Cheers erupted from the First Prince's faction. They embraced each other, shouting in triumph.

Joy rippled outward, spreading through the crowd like wildfire.

"It's a miracle! A true miracle!"

"The love between the First Prince and the First Princess has moved the goddess herself!"

The hall filled with celebration and praise.

But amidst the jubilation, one person felt no joy at all.

"This... this can't be happening. How? How did it come to this...?"

Rachel's face twisted with horror, rage, and something darker—fear.

And at that very moment, as if the universe responded to her terror—

"AAAAHHHHH!"

Rachel couldn't suppress the scream that tore from her throat.

"Saintess!"

People turned toward her in shock.

"It hurts! It hurts so much! Help me!"

Rachel screamed.

Her vision went white. She knew this pain—the sensation of her entire body being consumed by fire.

It was the price demanded of those who broke the Golden Rule.

"Someone help me! Please—I'm going to die!"

Unable to endure the agony, she flailed wildly.

But the crowd slowly backed away, eyes wide with shock.

Why? Why are they staring at me like that...?

"Look—on the Saintess's back...!"

Letters appeared across her skin, seared like scars into her flesh.

[ THOSE WHO CLAIM TO BE GODS ]

Rachel shouted frantically at the Holy Nation delegation.

"Paul! Help me! What are the priests doing?! Can't you see what's happening to me?!"

But Paul could not answer.

He was no exception to the goddess's wrath against those who had broken the sacred taboo and twisted her divine will.

"High Priest Paul!"

"W-what in the world is going on?!"

The scars of imposture were being branded onto the bodies of every priest from the Holy Nation—including the Pope's own Vicar.

Medea watched it all unfold, and finally understood.

The goddess had been waiting for this moment.

The moment when the miracle toward her chosen agent and the condemnation toward the impostors would be realized simultaneously through Medea.

"With great power comes great trials. You will constantly seek the light, driving away the encroaching darkness."

Only now did she fully grasp the meaning of Chief Shadeia's words in the Swamp of Gluttony.

The Golden Rule of this world was an inseparable law—one that bound even the gods themselves.

To sweep away the darkness through her divine agent once more, a corresponding accumulation of evil had been absolutely necessary.

Like a balanced scale.

And today, the moment the oath stone collided with the power of darkness, the scales tipped.

Rachel and the black magicians had reached the critical threshold first.

And so the goddess unleashed the torrent of her pent-up wrath upon them.

"Ghhh—khh..."

The moment the acrid smell of burning flesh reached his nose, a choked scream burst from Paul's lips.

In punishment for defying the Golden Rule, his limbs began to wither and decay.

Crackle...

The disguise he wore could not withstand divine judgment. It crumbled and tore apart.

The crowd gasped in horror when a disfigured face appeared beneath Paul's mask of stolen flesh.

"It's fake! That man—the oracle was fake too!"

"Those evil creatures who claim to be gods have deceived us all!"

The High Priest of Katzen, recognizing the telltale traces of black magic on Paul's collapsing mask, shouted at the top of his lungs.

"It's black magic! These damned sorcerers murdered our priests and wore their skins like costumes!"

Only then did the people understand the full scope of the deception.

The reason the First Prince had refused the oracle without the slightest hesitation.

The reason he had staked his life on an unbreakable oath.

He had known the truth all along.
