Chapter 272

The Serpent in the
Sanctuary

"Behind every blessing lies a curse waiting to be spoken."

Rachel stood in her cream-colored cassock, a vision of purity that mirrored the pristine white of a bridal gown. Every inch of her appeared immaculate—her features as lovely as ever, her hands spotless and unblemished.

But beneath that serene exterior, something far darker churned.

Each time Medea moved, each time that accursed god responded with yet another display of divine favor, Rachel found herself compulsively clenching and unclenching her fist—the one that still bore the fading mark of black magic.

It's fine. Everything is fine.

She repeated the words like a prayer, but they did nothing to quell the fury rising within her.

This magnificent temple. The family that treated Medea as though she were a precious jewel. The adoring subjects. And worst of all—the First Prince, that merciless creature who treated human lives like insects, standing before everyone as her devoted protector.

It was all supposed to be mine.

Rachel lowered her gaze, concealing the bloodshot rage in her eyes as she fought to suppress the inferno within.

It's fine. It's fine. Everything is fine...

She repeated it again, a mantra of self-deception.

Because all that pretense—that illusion of having the world at her feet—will crumble to nothing in a few short hours.

She could afford to let the doomed princess enjoy her final moments of fleeting pleasure. After all, what harm was there in granting a brief reprieve to someone destined for eternal slumber?

Yet even as she consoled herself with these thoughts, Rachel's fingernails dug into her palm with savage intensity, drawing crescents of blood.

"Are you quite well, my lady?"

Leonor's voice cut through her reverie. The knight rarely concerned himself with the Saintess's affairs, but something in her appearance had arrested his attention.

Her complexion had gone ashen—nearly gray, like weathered bone stretched over prominent cheekbones. She looked less like a holy woman and more like something excavated from a crypt.

"I'm quite well. Merely a moment's dizziness. I wouldn't dream of causing a disturbance during such a sacred occasion."

Given the relentless criticism the Saintess had endured—both within the temple and beyond—regarding matters of faith and trust, her haggard appearance seemed understandable enough.

She must be under considerable strain.

Leonor accepted this explanation and turned away.

"Should you feel unwell, please inform me at once. I'll summon a healing priest."

"Once this moment passes, I shall be perfectly fine."

Rachel's murmur carried an edge that might have been prayer—or might have been something far more sinister.

"Once this moment passes..."

Her gaze dropped once more, veiled and venomous.

Following the ceremony, the reception celebrating the sacred union commenced in the central corridor adjoining the main sanctuary.

Sacred paintings depicting the Holy Land's storied history adorned each wall. Above, an endless procession of crystal windows channeled the dying light through the vaulted ceiling, painting everything in shades of amber and rose.

Beyond the glass, the sun descended toward the horizon, setting the sky ablaze with crimson.

Gentle music drifted through the corridor, mingling with the laughter and murmured admiration of the assembled guests.

"Heavens above—is that truly essence of the World Tree?"

"Indeed. They say the First Prince's faction journeyed to the Elven Forest themselves to procure it."

Crystal glasses lined the tables, each filled with cocktails infused with the legendary essence. A subtle, intoxicating sweetness—unique to the World Tree—perfumed the entire hall.

"What an exquisite ceremony. I wonder when we shall witness anything so magnificent again."

The Pope delivered a brief congratulatory address, followed by a blessing. So moved was he by the sacred significance of the union that he had elected to preside over the reception personally.

At the head of the grand table, Medea and Cesare rose from their seats.

Both had changed into lighter attire befitting the celebration.

Cesare's military-style uniform, cut from charcoal fabric, traced the elegant lines of his powerful frame—broad shoulders, solid chest, a silhouette both refined and imposing.

Medea wore a silver gown of Sylvarend silk, crafted by elven artisans. The fabric held the quiet luminescence of moonlight—a soft gray-white that seemed to shimmer with subtle green undertones as the corridor's light played across it.

A delicate crown of silver bellflowers adorned her hair, lending her the ethereal quality of a forest sprite made flesh.

"The couple shall now exchange the Cup of the Covenant."

At the Pope's pronouncement, a knight of the Temple approached bearing the sacred vessel.

It was Theo.

As the designated successor to the Chief Priest, he had been entrusted with this honor. Setting down the cup with careful reverence, he stepped back—and for the briefest instant, his eyes met Medea's.

Theo inclined his head, the movement almost imperceptible.

Only one person noticed the subtle exchange: Cesare, who stood close enough to catch it.

The First Prince's lips maintained their gentle curve, but his golden eyes tracked Theo with predatory focus until the knight had retreated entirely from the ceremonial space.

The Pope raised his hand in blessing, and the Cup of the Covenant filled with holy water.

He presented a cup to each of them—first to Cesare, then to Medea.

Rachel's fists tightened as she watched Medea accept the silver chalice.

The moment Medea raised it to her lips, Rachel's violet eyes flashed with barely concealed anticipation—the hungry gaze of a predator watching its prey step into a trap.

She followed every movement, every swallow, until the last drop of holy water had disappeared past Medea's lips.

So consumed was she by her focus on Medea that she failed to notice something peculiar: the sacred relic—the Cup of the Covenant itself—glowed with slightly diminished radiance, as though some dark presence had dulled its light.

Cesare lowered his emptied glass with characteristic ease.

"To the union blessed by heaven!"

Cheers erupted throughout the hall.

At the Pope's signal, soft strains of music rose from the far end of the corridor—harp and strings weaving together in delicate harmony.

Cesare extended his hand.

As Medea placed her fingers in his palm, the gathered guests fell silent, collectively holding their breath.

The First Dance

The two of them glided past the parted crowd and stepped onto the circular dance floor.

As the melody enveloped them, Cesare leaned close, his voice dropping to a register meant for her ears alone.

"Are you prepared for tonight?"

The words brushed against her ear like silk, laden with deliberate ambiguity. Whether he referred to their wedding night or the plans they had set in motion, he left tantalizingly unclear.

A lesser woman might have blushed. Medea was not a lesser woman.

"As long as you perform adequately, I shall endeavor not to be disappointed."

He paused for the space of a heartbeat, as though her indifference had genuinely caught him off guard. Then laughter rumbled low in his chest, and his arm drew her waist closer.

"Naturally. How could I possibly disappoint my lady's expectations?"

Everything. He would lay it all before her.

Cesare whispered the promise against her hair, his thumb tracing a gentle path along her cheek—a touch impossibly tender, as though she were something infinitely precious.

What does he think he's doing?

Medea's eyes narrowed in wordless warning.

"Smile, Medea."

His voice was calm, almost conversational.

"We must look the part of a blissfully wedded couple. After all—wouldn't our prey feel safest crawling into a trap they believe to be harmless?"

Medea's gaze flickered over his shoulder, finding without difficulty the eyes that watched her with undisguised hunger—eyes that seemed ready to devour her whole.

She exhaled softly. Then, in a gesture that drew gasps from the crowd, she lowered her head and buried her face against Cesare's chest.

Delighted murmurs rippled through the hall.

"Can that truly be the First Prince of Katzen? I've never seen him laugh so freely!"

"And the Princess of Valdina! So bold before an evil dragon, yet now so bashful. How utterly charming."

"A match blessed by heaven itself. They complement each other perfectly."

The couple moved across the floor like water finding its natural course, their steps perfectly synchronized until the final note of the harp faded into silence.

To the watching guests, the scene seemed plucked from an illuminated storybook—a tale that had begun in tragedy only to conclude in radiant joy.

In that moment of collective enchantment, nearly everyone present succumbed to the romance of it all.

Nearly everyone.

Two figures remained unmoved by the spectacle.

Rachel. And Jason.

Both stood rigid as stone, fists clenched so tightly the blood seemed ready to burst from their palms. Their eyes remained fixed on Medea alone—Medea, wrapped in Cesare's arms, bathed in the sanctuary's glorious light, seemingly drunk on love and happiness.

It should have been mine.

Perhaps it was the strange alchemy of having been bound to the same soul in a previous life. Whatever the cause, the thoughts coursing through Rachel's and Jason's minds ran along eerily parallel tracks.

Rachel burned to possess everything Medea had claimed.

Jason seethed with the desire to destroy the man who had taken his place at Medea's side.

Both pairs of eyes were veined with red. Both faces wore expressions of barely leashed fury, as though they were branding this moment into their memories—every detail of Medea's expression, every breath she drew, seared into their consciousness forever.

But the true irony lay in what neither of them recognized.

Despite the mirror-like symmetry of their obsessions, they felt nothing for each other.

The singular devotion Jason had once harbored for his second wife—so fierce it had driven him to abandon Medea and even his own children—had withered to nothing.

He looked at the Saintess now and felt only indifference.

Rachel's feelings had evolved in parallel. What purpose did it serve to waste emotion on a disgraced prince who could no longer even compete for Katzen's throne?

The bond that had once made them husband and wife had dissolved the moment they ceased to be useful to each other.

The banquet had reached its zenith when Medea rose from her seat, one hand pressed to her temple.

"Please excuse me for a moment."

Her voice carried a note of genuine discomfort.

"I believe I may have overindulged in the World Tree essence."

"Of course, Your Highness. Allow me to escort you somewhere you might rest."

At last. The medicine is taking effect.

Behind her mask of concern, Rachel smiled—a small, secret expression of dark satisfaction.

Sleep well, Princess. Sleep forever.
