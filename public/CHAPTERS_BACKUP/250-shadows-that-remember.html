Chapter 250

Shadows That
Remember

"The older the trap, the more its structure remains unchanged."

The Forest of Eternal Darkness swallowed them whole.

The expedition found themselves dwarfed by trees that rose endlessly toward an invisible sky—ancient sentinels whose canopy blotted out every trace of daylight.

It was dark as midnight even at noon. With every exhale from horse and rider alike, unidentifiable sounds stirred in the shadows just beyond sight.

Medea remembered the first time she had set foot in this forest, long ago.

There had been a time when she walked these paths with her young son cradled in her arms, forcing herself to move as though nothing were wrong.

Yet even though the terrain now seemed more treacherous and unforgiving than it had been then, she felt no fear.

Perhaps because this time, she did not feel so utterly alone.

"Did you see that?! Did you witness my incredible reflexes—striking true even from the corner of my eye?!"

Tom shouted triumphantly over the fallen corpse of a hobgoblin.

The Forest of Eternal Darkness did not welcome the strangers who had invaded its domain. Moments earlier, they had encountered a hunting party of hobgoblins—creatures far larger and stronger than their lesser goblin kin.

"On the continent, you'd only encounter beasts like these during a wave. But here? Hobgoblins are practically the welcoming committee!"

No one appeared particularly surprised—those who traveled through this forest had already endured countless hardships.

Tom's voice remained bright despite everything.

"Perhaps I was suited for this all along? Isn't it far more exhilarating than merely executing precise, elegant swordwork?"

There was genuine joy in swinging his blade freely, carving whatever trajectory pleased him.

"Tom, keep your distance. If you lose focus and splash blood on Her Highness..."

Die.

Neril swallowed the final word.

Rustle.

A silver arc sliced cleanly through another hobgoblin. Long, graceful legs kicked the spurting corpse aside before a single drop could reach Medea.

Neril remained at the Princess's side like a hunting hound guarding a lamb—vigilant, tireless, lethal.

"Ha! Now I understand why they call it the Pitch-Black Forest. It must be my imagination that every creature in existence keeps flinging itself at me to obstruct my view."

Meanwhile, Gallo maintained his position at the vanguard, grumbling without pause.

Yet his twin axes never missed a single winged bat-demon that swooped toward him from the darkness.

Crunch. Rustle.

Demons cleaved in two fell around him as he walked. Amidst the carnage, still complaining, Gallo resembled nothing so much as a massive iron ball forged from human flesh.

Tom let out an admiring whistle.

"The fake Facade head earned his position through more than just nepotism, I see..."

"What did you just call me, you little runt?"

"Nothing! I merely meant you're obviously just a pretty face for public appearances. A regular layabout."

"Well, I won't deny that I possess exceptional good looks."

"...Where do you find such confidence after laying eyes upon your Boss's face?"

"What the hell is that supposed to mean, brat?!"

Gallo and Tom bickered ceaselessly.

Perhaps owing to their similar temperaments, they had become fast friends more quickly than anyone else among Medea and Cesare's combined retinues.

Tom was the only one who responded to Gallo's constant nonsense—and the reverse proved equally true.

Terence, by contrast, wore an expression of perpetual exhaustion beneath his low-drawn hood.

As a wizard trained in the Magic Tower, he moved with surprising agility through the forest, though he rarely deigned to engage in combat personally—leaving such matters to Gallo's or Cesare's subordinates.

When his turn did come, he wielded a long, elegant blade with practiced precision—seemingly more concerned with preventing demon blood and forest filth from staining his robes than with the kill itself.

Terence's standards for cleanliness bordered on the obsessive. He cast purification spells upon his garments every night before sleep.

"Brother, what purpose does endless cleansing magic serve in a forest teeming with demons? That's why the shadows beneath your eyes grow darker by the hour."

Gallo shook his head, gesturing toward the bruised crescents beneath Terence's eyes.

"Silence. I am entirely self-sufficient. And these circles have been here since birth."

Terence's pockets bulged with small magic stones extracted from hobgoblin corpses. Purification magic was hardly expensive—he would likely remain immaculate until the expedition concluded.

"How on earth did you survive continental warfare if you couldn't even procure magic stones?"

The Hidden Danger

"Everyone—halt."

The party stopped at Medea's command.

No demons had emerged. The path ahead appeared perfectly ordinary.

"Your Highness?"

"What's the matter? There's nothing—"

Gallo fell silent when he met Medea's gaze. Her green eyes were fixed upon something only she could perceive.

Thump.

Medea dismounted and stamped her foot against the earth.

Thump. Thump.

A slow, rhythmic tremor shook the ground—faint, but unmistakable. She withdrew something from her supplies and scattered it across the trail.

Neril recognized the grey lime powder from the village, though the rest of the party watched in bewilderment.

"Why is Her Highness—"

"Quiet."

In that instant, the ground collapsed.

Simultaneously, several massive creatures—thin and impossibly long—burst onto the road.

SHRIEEEEK—!

The horses reared in terror.

"Sand squirrels!"

The expedition surged forward to engage the hidden predators.

Sand squirrels were beasts that lurked within quicksand, lying in wait to ambush their prey. Each creature was enormous—long enough to carry two carriages with ease.

Hundreds of razor-sharp legs moved in horrifying unison, producing a skin-crawling screech with every motion.

"Gallo—the shells are iron-hard. Don't strike the carapace; target the joints."

"What? Yes—understood!"

How does the Princess know this?

The thought flickered through Gallo's mind, but his body was already moving.

Moments later, the expedition stood beside a mound of sand squirrel corpses.

"Her Highness was correct. These shells are harder than iron plate."

When Alpha struck a carapace experimentally, the impact produced a dull, resonant thud.

Every slain sand squirrel bore wounds to its delicate joints—cuts and punctures in the only vulnerable spots on their armored bodies.

Had they continued along this path, they would have plunged into quicksand—trapped, helpless against those iron shells, devoured alive.

'Yet I sensed nothing.'

Gallo had survived countless battlefields. He prided himself on possessing instincts for danger that were second to none.

How had he remained utterly oblivious?

Even Tom's face had grown pale, his usual humor extinguished. This place was genuinely perilous.

Its reputation as the Forest of Death was no exaggeration.

"The Forest of Eternal Darkness poses dangers beyond mere demons."

Medea's calm voice cut through the lingering tension.

"With humanity's presence long vanished, the forest itself has become a hunting ground. It functions as one vast, interconnected trap."

She paused.

"But the older a trap grows, the more its structure remains unchanged. Once you understand its patterns, avoidance becomes simple. There is no need for undue concern."

Though Gallo recognized her words as reassurance, his confusion only deepened.

'Then how does Her Highness know these patterns?'

Suddenly, he became aware of something that had escaped his notice.

It had been several days since they entered this forest.

Her Guidance

"You've been wounded. Apply this crushed herb to neutralize the poison, or your flesh will rot."

She handed the medicine to Beta, scratched by a shadow rat's claws.

"This path is wrong. We must bear further north."

"Ensure the horses eat only grass bearing this pattern."

"Submerge a stick before drawing water. If nothing occurs, it is safe to drink."

While the rest of them scrambled to adapt to this hostile environment, the true leader of the expedition had been Princess Medea all along.

Isn't this strange?

How could a young princess who had never left Valdina possess such intimate knowledge of the Forest of Eternal Darkness?

This land of death bore no resemblance to her delicate, beautiful appearance.

She seemed to know precisely where danger lurked and what form it would take.

As though she had experienced it all before.

Neril's mind turned to an earlier memory.

"The shells are thick—target the joints, not the carapace."

"Strike beneath the chest—that is where they are weakest!"

It had been Medea who revealed the bat-demons' vulnerability during the battle at the Strait of Valdina.

"...Your Highness."

But Medea was already glancing toward the quicksand, seemingly oblivious to the unspoken questions and confused stares directed at her.

"We must reach higher ground. Remaining here any longer is dangerous."

She bent to examine the vein patterns on a nearby shrub's leaves, then straightened and turned away.

"There is no time. The sun will set soon."

There was no hesitation in her steps as she walked the unfamiliar road.

As though she knew every shortcut by heart.

That Evening

The stew bubbled quietly in its pot.

Crackle. Crackle.

The campfire's flames cast dancing shadows across weary faces.

Tonight's cooking duty had fallen to Neril and Gamma. But Neril remained lost in thought, nearly tossing an unwashed, dirt-covered carrot into the pot before Gamma caught her hand.

"Eat. Please."

Among the expedition members gathered for their meal, the only sounds were the quiet slurping of stew—uncharacteristically subdued for this usually boisterous group.

Medea knew she was the cause of the heavy silence. But she pretended not to notice, idly stirring her spoon through the broth.

What was she supposed to say?

She could not tell them the truth. And even if she did—who would believe her?

That she had already traveled this entire expedition in a previous life? That she had died miserably and been resurrected?

Silent Mockery

"Have you ever experienced the power of God? Have you ever glimpsed places that no one else can see or reach?"

Medea let out a hollow, silent laugh as she recalled the old man's words from the Swamp of Gluttony village.

'Yes. It must have been divine power that granted me a second life.'

A miracle bestowed by the goddess who had taken pity upon her suffering.

But the goddess's favor extended no further. She would not forgive anyone who dared threaten her precious Saintess.

Viewed in that light, the oracle demanding Medea as a sacrifice was simply divine punishment for targeting Rachel.

Medea swallowed another bitter laugh.

She did not notice the golden eyes watching her from across the fire—observing her silent self-mockery with an intensity that burned brighter than the flames.
