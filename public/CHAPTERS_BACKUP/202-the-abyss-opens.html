Chapter 202

The Abyss
Opens

"From the depths came creatures of nightmare—
and the man who had made himself a monster."

Screeching.

From the black sphere—that void so deep it seemed bottomless—a swarm of creatures exploded forth into the light of day.

Man-eating bats. Dozens of them. Hundreds.

Their leathery wings beat the air with sickening rhythm. Razor-sharp teeth glinted in wide, gaping mouths. Eyes burned crimson with feral hunger. Talons, curved and cruel, flexed in anticipation of tearing through flesh.

"Your Highness!"

"Angemas?"

Medea's eyes narrowed sharply.

Angemas—mid-to-high-tier demonic beasts that dwell in mountain caverns, far from the coast.

The Strait of El Amuz had seen monster waves before, but they had always emerged from the sea. Land-dwelling demons had never manifested here. The geography itself made it impossible.

Until now.

"Vice-Captain, what in the hells—why aren't these sea demons?"

The Agema's voice cracked with confusion.

Before anyone could answer, Medea's voice cut through the chaos like a blade.

"Strike the abdomen—just below the chest! That's their weakness!"

The Agema guards had no time to wonder how their Princess knew the anatomy of a creature none of them had ever seen before.

Instinct took over. Blades flashed silver in the dim light.

Shing! Slash!

Crimson blood sprayed across the dock as steel found soft underbellies.

"KAAAAH!"

The bats shrieked—a sound like metal scraping bone—and recoiled. They had not expected such fierce resistance from mere humans.

But Medea's expression remained grim.

There's nowhere near the docks suitable for a defensive formation. If their numbers grow, the Agema won't be able to hold.

Though her guards were elite—among the finest warriors on the continent—they had limited experience fighting demons. Their training had focused on human opponents: soldiers, assassins, knights.

They had defeated waves of ghouls and trolls on the plains months earlier, true. But Valdina itself, protected by the sacred Philosopher's Stone, had been free of demonic incursions for generations. The Agema were skilled, but unfamiliar with the unnatural speed and ferocity of hellspawn.

And there was something else gnawing at the edge of Medea's mind—something far more dangerous than the bats.

Jared will be here soon.

That was the real reason Medea had traveled so far west to see off the Duke of Seres.

"Your Highness, Jared has vanished from Katzen Castle. We are attempting to track his movements."

"Message from the Facade Master—Jared has been sighted in the Strait."

Intelligence from both the Red Fox operatives embedded in the Katzen court and from Zeta's spy network had confirmed it.

Medea had been waiting for him.

He wouldn't pass up an opportunity like this—catching me isolated, away from the villa's defenses.

But the sudden eruption of Angemas complicated everything.

Fighting Jared and a demon swarm simultaneously—this will be difficult.

As if summoned by her very thoughts, shadows moved.

Black-clad figures materialized between the weathered pillars of the dock—assassins, cloaked in darkness, cutting off the Agema's retreat.

Clang! Screech! Clang!

The harsh clash of steel against steel mingled with the inhuman cries of the Angemas.

Chaos erupted.

"Damn it—how long has it been since we faced odds like this?"

An Agema guard cursed through gritted teeth as he parried a blade meant for his throat.

"Ugh—!"

Deputy Commander Menil staggered backward, eyes wide with shock.

"Hah... hah..."

A shadow lunged. A massive hand—grotesque, misshapen—seized Menil by the throat and hurled him across the dock like a ragdoll.

Crash!

He hit the ground hard. Blood pooled beneath him, spreading from a wound in his side—deep, ragged, as though pierced by something far stronger than any normal blade.

It was difficult to believe a human hand could inflict such damage.

Thud. Thud.

Heavy footsteps echoed across the blood-slicked wood.

A man emerged from the carnage, his smile twisted and venomous. His eyes glowed faintly crimson in the shadow.

Jared.

"Kekeke. So the mighty Agema aren't so fearsome after all. All those tales of being the continent's strongest—nothing but lies, it seems."

He raised his right hand, brandishing a wickedly curved sword.

It was the hand that had lost a finger three years ago—severed by a Facade mercenary's blade during a skirmish at the border.

But now, impossibly, a new finger jutted from that empty space.

Except it was no ordinary finger.

It was grotesque. Bloated. Thick as three or four fingers fused together, covered in sickly green skin that glistened wetly under the pale sky. Sharp, yellowed talons protruded from beneath the nail bed.

The sight was so revolting that even Jared's own assassins turned their heads, fighting the urge to retch.

But Jared himself seemed pleased.

Such power. Can they truly be gods, these warlocks?

"There is no need to concern yourself with the General's hand."

"You will regain your former prowess—perhaps even surpass it."

Not long after Raju's confident departure, the black-robed warlocks had come for Jared.

They brought with them a severed troll's finger—still twitching with residual life—and grafted it onto his mutilated hand.

The dark magic required to fuse demonic flesh with human bone was astonishing enough. But what truly astounded Jared was the power the transplant granted him.

His right hand could now pulverize stone as easily as a troll could. He could hurl grown men across rooms with a flick of his wrist.

True, his swordwork had become clumsier—the finger's unnatural bulk threw off his balance. But the raw strength more than compensated.

Aside from its hideous appearance, there are only advantages.

Add to that the coordinated assault from his assassins—and the Angemas, which mysteriously ignored Jared's forces entirely—and the tide of battle turned decisively against Valdina.

"Attack! Kill them—kill them all!"

Jared roared, venting years of pent-up rage and humiliation.

"Agh—!"

The Agema fought valiantly, but they were caught between two fronts. Demons tore at them from one side, assassins from the other.

"Hah... hah..."

One by one, guards fell—bitten, slashed, overwhelmed.

Jared's grin widened as victory seemed assured.

"Oh, our beautiful Princess seems to be in quite the predicament."

Medea said nothing.

"Ah, forgive me—I should address you properly now. Your Highness, the Noble Princess of Valdina."

His voice dripped with mockery.

With theatrical flair, Jared drove his sword into the wooden planks of the dock.

Thunk.

At that signal, the shrieking Angemas abruptly ceased their assault. As one, they collapsed mid-flight, bodies disintegrating into ash that drifted lazily toward the sea.

Jared controls them.

Medea's gaze darkened with understanding.

Which means this entire attack—the rift, the Angemas—was orchestrated by him.

Her green eyes swept over Jared, coldly analytical, cataloging every detail of his transformed appearance.

"Now then, Princess—do you truly believe you can survive this?"

The assassins moved with practiced precision. Blades kissed the throats of the fallen Agema guards—still alive, but helpless.

"Jared."

Medea's voice was calm. Too calm.

"You've allied yourself with dangerous company."

"What will you do, Your Highness? You've already lost one of your precious guards. Do you intend to lose the rest as well?"

Silence.

"Come with me. Surely the Noble Princess of Valdina would not abandon her beloved protectors."

He gestured toward a black carriage waiting at the edge of the dock—ornate, ominous, clearly prepared in advance.

"Your Highness, no! Jared, you bastard—!"

Neril's scream tore through the air.

Slash!

An assassin's dagger bit deep into her shoulder. Blood bloomed across her uniform.

"The neck is next, Your Highness."

The assassin's voice was flat. Emotionless.

Medea's emerald eyes swept across the scene—her wounded guards, the waiting carriage, Jared's triumphant sneer.

She drew a slow breath.

"Very well. Stop this."

Her voice rang clear and cold.

"I have business to attend to."

Without hesitation, she stepped forward—spine straight, head high, every inch the Princess she was born to be.

She stopped before Jared, meeting his gaze without flinching.

"Open the door."

"Excellent choice, Your Highness."

Jared's grin widened, feral and pleased.

"Once before, you risked your life for your people. Now you do so for your guards. The Princess of Valdina truly is selfless."

He chuckled darkly as he gestured her toward the carriage.

Medea climbed inside without a word.

Jared followed her in—and the moment the door closed behind him, he moved.

Shing!

His sword flashed outward in a wide arc—not at Medea, but at his own men.

"What the hell—?!"

The assassins barely had time to scream as the blade severed limbs and opened throats.

"My apologies, gentlemen. But this prize is mine alone."

Jared's voice was almost cheerful as he slammed the carriage door shut.

Arcane Transport

Inside the carriage, inscribed across the floor in fresh blood, glowed an intricate magic circle.

Symbols writhed. Glyphs pulsed with crimson light.

"He changed the coordinates!"

One of the assassins—still alive, clutching his severed arm—screamed in realization.

Fwoooosh—!

Red light erupted from the circle, consuming the carriage whole.

The bloody radiance swallowed it—and Medea with it—leaving nothing but scorched planks and the lingering scent of sulfur.

The dock fell silent.

Bodies lay strewn across the bloodied wood. Wounded Agema guards struggled to rise. The assassins who remained alive clutched their wounds, eyes wide with shock and fury.

And where the black carriage had stood moments before—

Nothing.

Only empty air and the faint echo of Medea's calm, unwavering voice.
