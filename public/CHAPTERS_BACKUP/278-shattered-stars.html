Chapter 278

Shattered
Stars

"Some truths are witnessed across the boundaries of time itself."

The forged letter hovered before her eyes, damning in its terrible clarity.

"I don't understand... Your Majesty, have I ever given you false counsel?"

"No..."

As Medea's anguished moan escaped her lips, the next fragment of the mirror flared to life.

The Third Fragment

"It's a trap, Your Majesty. The Princess cannot possibly journey to Valdina at this time. You must not go. The border is overrun with demonic beasts."

"I know."

Peleus's voice was quiet, resolute.

"But if there is even the smallest chance that Dea is coming—she'll be consumed by those creatures. I cannot allow that."

And so Peleus rode forth.

Her brother was no fool. He had known with certainty that it was a trap.

Yet he could not abandon the bonds of blood and flesh—not until the very end.

Oh, Peleus...

Medea pressed both hands over her mouth. Tears streamed unchecked down her face, born of shock so profound it felt like drowning.

The Fourth Fragment

The vision shifted once more.

In the next shard, she saw him.

Her brother, surrounded by a swarm of demonic beasts pouring across the border.

Half his body had been torn away. His clear blue eyes—once so full of life—had long since dimmed to nothing.

Frozen. Staring into the void.

"Goddess, please... protect him."

And clutched in his fallen hand, just beyond his lifeless fingers—

The forged parchment.

"NO!"

Medea screamed and lunged toward Peleus.

But her hand passed through him as though through mist. She could not touch him. Could not close his eyes.

Her brother had died like this—because of her. Because he could not surrender the foolish devotion of family until the very end.

This was different from hearing the news spoken aloud. The shock of witnessing his final moments with her own eyes was devastating beyond measure.

"AAAAHHHH!!!"

She doubled over, howling. The pain felt as though her heart was being torn asunder.

Meanwhile, the border landscape—swarming with beasts like carrion birds circling prey—began to recede.

As if this was not the end. As if there was still more to show.

Her vision blurred and twisted.

Countless stars streaked past in reverse. Time itself seemed to unspool, flowing backward.

Until at last, she arrived at the beginning.

The point where everything had started.

Echoes of a Past Life

"Truly, Uncle? Did he really say that?"

"Jason—here. I've brought you the Philosopher's Stone."

"Yes. The evil dragon fell by your blade."

And there, before her eyes, the past she had never wished to face again unfolded in merciless detail.

"Peleus, help me. The Emperor watches our every move. If I can protect my children, even for a moment..."

"Lian, go to the imperial palace. You should be by your father's side."

"Birna—take Leah and run! Hurry!"

Her past life—weak, foolish, blind—was laid bare under the cold, unforgiving light.

Not a single fragment of oblivion was permitted. Every mistake, every betrayal, every moment of naive trust passed before Medea's eyes.

It was suffocating agony, but she did not look away.

Her eyes remained wide despite the tears blurring her vision. Beneath her sleeves, her fingernails carved crescents into her own flesh.

Then—

Medea sensed that someone was watching her.

Beyond the curtain of flowing memories, a figure stood.

Jet-black hair. Golden eyes that burned like twin suns.

A man who could never be buried among countless stars.

"Cesare..."

The sound that escaped her lips was equal parts sigh and relief.

Two pairs of eyes met across the fragments of her shattered past.

Through Cesare's Eyes

"Truly, Uncle? Did he really say that?"

Cesare saw it too.

The moment when a little girl with innocent, trusting eyes began to wither—like an ancient tree slowly dying from within.

"Princess of Valdina, please—call me Jason."

"I love you, Medea."

The moment when Jason's shallow affection became the girl's only lifeline in a world of vipers.

From the day she stole her nation's sacred treasure and fled. Through Jason's ascension to the throne. To the moment when every ounce of love and sacrifice she had offered was trampled beneath his boots.

"...So that's why."

Beneath his black sleeve, Cesare's nails buried themselves into his clenched fist.

In this lifetime, the gears that had never quite meshed finally clicked into place.

Why she fought so desperately to protect her country. Why she burned with such hatred for Jason.

Now he understood why she had cast herself aside without hesitation in pursuit of her goals.

"Didn't the oracle come again? The revelation that mother and daughter would bring destruction upon this empire!"

Within Cesare's starlit memories, Jason and Rachel had conspired to take Medea's life.

"You cannot possibly sever the bond between us!"

Remembering Jason's pathetic wailing in the Chamber of Judgment, Cesare ground his teeth.

Medea, standing in that prison cell, had looked like a tree whose tears had long since run dry.

Like a branch snapped clean—no hope remaining, not even the memory of hope.

Why... why didn't you seek me out?

Cesare could not contain the inferno that erupted within his chest.

The Fury Within

He wanted to slaughter everyone who had reduced her to that state.

He wanted to crush the throats of all who had dared to laugh at her suffering—to make them pay a hundredfold for every betrayal.

Where was I? Medea, why didn't you come to me?

I would never have abandoned you like this. If I had been there...

"Ah."

Then, with sudden, terrible clarity—he understood.

A sharp breath escaped from between his parched lips.

"I..."

In his past life, he had been unable to overcome the curse that the Primordial Darkness had placed upon him.

He could not have recognized its existence, let alone found the Dawn Drop that would break it.

In this lifetime, the antidote had come to him only because of Medea's changed choice.

Cesare looked up.

The moment he glimpsed those green eyes watching him through the fractured memories—

He moved without hesitation, striding from star to star.

"Cesare..."

The instant her startled voice called his name, soft as a prayer—

Cesare pulled her into his arms.

Like a constellation of black stars enveloping Venus, darkness drifted down in silence and swallowed them both.

Awakening

Medea opened her eyes.

A man's silhouette wavered before her blurred vision. The lamplight caught his hair, turning it to silver.

"Peleus."

She threw her arms around his neck with all her strength. The cry she had been suppressing finally broke free.

"You can't go... Promise me you won't go..."

As she gasped for breath between sobs, a warm hand stroked her back with gentle understanding.

But pressed against his chest, a faint scent of musk reached her—not her brother's familiar fragrance.

The realization struck her unconsciously.

She jerked back.

Looking up with wary eyes, she found Cesare gazing down at her.

Only then did she understand: what she had witnessed in the vision was no illusion.

"Did you see it?"

Her voice—always so composed, so indifferent—trembled now.

"You too... did you see everything?"

Cesare held her gaze.

"...Yes."

Exposed

It felt as though she had been caught in her deepest shame.

His gaze seemed to cut straight through her, leaving fresh wounds upon her heart.
