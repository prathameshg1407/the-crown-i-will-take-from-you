The Beast in
the Shadows

"In the hunt for glory, one must beware of becoming the prey."

A ruthless conqueror who had shattered his enemies upon the edge of his blade and carved his path through rivers of blood.

No matter how much the oracle had bound them together, Medea would have to spend the rest of her days beside such a dangerous man.

Despite herself, Angelique felt a flicker of sympathy.

"Angelique! Mind your manners."

The Eastern Empress appeared at her daughter's side, her voice a soft but unmistakable reprimand.

As she spoke, she cast a sidelong glance at Medea, gauging whether her daughter's casual disregard for proper titles had caused offense.

With a final murmured reminder that Angelique must not revert to her former unruly ways, the Empress withdrew.

Angelique pursed her lips in silent rebellion.

"Does it displease the Fourth Princess to find me here again?"

Medea's lips curved into a knowing smile.

The expression was eerily reminiscent of three years past—when she had claimed the island of Shadeia with that same serene, unshakeable composure.

Angelique's words died in her throat.

"Very well... I concede it now. You stand above me."

She cleared her throat awkwardly, feeling an inexplicable chill trace down her spine.

"Such gracious praise from Your Highness—I hardly know how to respond. It gladdens me to see you again in this unfamiliar land of Katzen, after all we have shared."

Medea's tone was warm, courteous, utterly disarming.

"Yes, well... Let us put the past behind us and forge a better path forward."

Angelique extended her hand in a gesture of truce.

"For what it's worth, I intend to support my elder brother. Certainly more than that brooding Juan—"

"How long have you fancied yourself a saint?"

A sharp voice cut through the air.

It was the Third Prince, Juan, striding toward them in his hunting attire, every step radiating barely contained hostility.

"Did you truly believe His Majesty would welcome you back after you thrust your nose into affairs that don't concern you?"

Though his words were framed as mere rebuke, the venom beneath them was unmistakable.

This was not the courteous, measured Third Prince the court had come to know.

Angelique snorted derisively.

"Perhaps His Majesty should send me to a tower this time, then. If you have grievances for the Emperor, do share them with me—I shall be delighted to relay every word."

Her sneer deepened.

"The Emperor won't linger there long, so I'll stay overnight and attend to Her Highness the Empress Dowager. After all, I doubt I'm the only child concerned about our parents."

She leaned in, her voice dropping to a whisper meant only for Juan's ears.

"You would do well to conduct yourself carefully hereafter, brother. The Western Empress's romantic entanglements are positively riveting—but it seems she gave no thought to her only son, did she?"

The corners of her mouth twisted with mockery.

"The scandal was so enthusiastic I heard whispers of it the moment I set foot in the capital."

She laughed, a sound like breaking glass.

"How dare you speak of my mother—!"

Juan's hand flew upward, poised to strike.

"My, my, Juan—thinking of the consequences? How unlike you. I only mention it out of concern, naturally. I would hate to see you cast out as I was."

Angelique delivered her poison without so much as a blink, her eyes wide with exaggerated innocence.

Juan's hand froze mid-air, trembling with suppressed fury.

I was barely permitted to accompany Father today.

He had intended to assist the Emperor throughout the hunt, to reclaim even a fraction of lost favor. But if this venomous creature continued provoking him about his mother, he would forfeit even this precarious opportunity.

"A word of advice, brother—rather than chasing some impossible throne, why not simply accept your place beside the next Emperor? Spare yourself the hardship."

"...What did you say?"

Juan's glare could have scorched stone.

"Oh, you didn't expect such wisdom from me? Well, I've seen a bit of the world now—I've matured considerably. Consider it sisterly guidance, dear Juan. Do think on it."

Her laughter rang out, bright and merciless.

Three years of exile had clearly taught the Fourth Princess the art of dismantling people with precision.

Juan's hands shook. He was moments from lunging at her.

Raised in the Western Empress's sheltered garden, and still so easily baited by such transparent provocation.

Medea clicked her tongue inwardly and took several quiet steps backward, distancing herself from the brewing tempest.

The Fourth Princess and Third Prince were so consumed by mutual hatred that neither noticed her subtle movements.

"That will be quite enough, both of you. The hunt is about to commence."

Medea extended a hand as if to calm them.

In the same motion, she flicked a small stone concealed within her sleeve.

Snap—

A thin blade affixed to the stone's edge sliced cleanly through the cord securing Juan's quiver.

"Angelique, that insolent mouth of yours needs to be—"

Juan surged forward, murderous intent radiating from every pore.

Clatter!

The quiver's strap gave way, arrows cascading to the ground in a noisy avalanche.

The sudden commotion shattered the tension, forcing both parties to pause.

Juan blinked, the murderous haze lifting from his eyes.

"Haah... haah..."

His breath came in ragged bursts as he knelt to retrieve the scattered arrows, each exhale thick with barely swallowed rage.

"Here, allow me—"

"I don't require your assistance."

The Third Prince's voice was ice as he cut off Medea's offer.

The First Princess. The one who dared scheme against my mother.

Revulsion twisted his features as memories surfaced—a time when he had foolishly played the gallant gentleman before this woman.

Juan snatched up his quiver with clenched teeth and rose, entirely failing to notice that Medea had slipped a small, folded paper into it along with one of the arrows.

"What exactly did you pick up? Do you imagine that cold-blooded husband of yours will soften just because you play the gracious lady?"

Angelique, still bristling, snapped at Medea.

Medea's smile remained serene, utterly unperturbed.

"Who can say? Goodwill has a way of returning to those who offer it. Wouldn't you agree, Angelique?"

Just as I am now returning the 'goodwill' you showed me three years ago.

The Fourth Princess's face drained of color as she glimpsed the meaning lurking beneath that pleasant smile.

...And they said she didn't suit Cesare?

In the art of reading hearts and turning them to one's purpose, this woman was perhaps the First Prince's perfect counterpart.

"Hear me! The first to fell the white stag shall be declared victor of today's hunt!"

BOOOOOO—

The hunting horn's call reverberated through the forest like a thunderous heartbeat.

"Forward! Onward!"

Perdiccas II's mount surged into the treeline with explosive force.

Gone was the anxious, manic pallor that had haunted him of late. Now his face blazed with the vigor and enthusiasm of a sovereign reveling in the wild splendor of his domain.

Serwood Forest had long been reserved exclusively for the Imperial Family's pleasure.

A sanctuary of controlled wilderness—incomparable to the chaos of monster waves or military campaigns. Every dangerous predator had been systematically eliminated.

Within such carefully managed grounds, even Perdiccas II could ride with the boldness of a warrior king.

"Father, I shall drive the white stag directly to you. The glory of the kill shall be yours alone."

"You won't claim it for yourself?"

"How could I presume to steal such honor from my Emperor? I have been practicing the archery techniques you taught me—it would be my privilege simply to serve as your beater."

Juan's voice dripped honey.

"See, Father—this is the very bow you bestowed upon me. With it, I shall safeguard the entire imperial household."

"Hmph. What am I to do with a son so utterly lacking in ambition? Pathetic creature."

The Emperor's grumble held no real sting; indeed, a note of warmth softened his words.

He recognized the flattery for what it was—a desperate bid to escape his current disgrace. Yet Juan's honeyed phrases stirred memories of simpler times.

Of a small boy's hand wrapped around a bow. Of patient lessons given by a father who had not yet learned to see threats in every shadow.

"Very well. Let us proceed. And stay alert."

The hunting grounds stretched endlessly before them, a labyrinth of ancient oaks and dappled sunlight.

Despite their prolonged search, no sign of the white stag materialized.

"Are there even any white deer in this forest?"

Perdiccas II's irritation grew palpable.

The constant jostling of horseback riding was sapping his strength. Though the Katzen Empire had flourished under his reign, the truth was that most of those achievements belonged to Cesare.

An emperor consumed by the pursuit of power had little time for maintaining physical prowess. The extended hunt was rapidly becoming a form of torture.

"Father—there!"

Juan's voice rang out as he gestured urgently toward a distant thicket.

Through the foliage, a pale shape flickered—something white, something moving.

The hounds erupted in frenzied barks and bolted forward.

"It's mine! That beast is mine! No one touches it!"

The Emperor's earlier fatigue vanished in an instant. He spurred his horse ahead of the hunting party, consumed by sudden, fierce exhilaration.

If I capture the white stag before anyone else today...

...then all those young fools—Cesare included—would witness irrefutable proof that their Emperor still commanded strength and vitality.

The white creature fled deeper into the forest.

"After it! Don't let it escape!"

Perdiccas II drove his horse forward with reckless abandon, heedless of the branches that clawed at his face.

His blood sang. For one glorious moment, he felt young again—a prince on the hunt, master of all he surveyed.

How far had they ridden?

"It's mine! Mine! No one else dare touch it! All this auspicious fortune belongs to the throne!"

The Emperor thundered onward, sword raised instead of bow, caught up in the fever of pursuit.

And then he stopped.

Grrrrrrrr...

What stood before him was not a white stag.

It was a black bear—massive, monstrous, its yellow eyes blazing with unnatural light as it fixed its gaze upon the Emperor.

NEEEIGGHHH—!

"Your Majesty!"

The Emperor's horse screamed and reared backward in terror.

The guards rushed to close the distance, spurring their mounts desperately.

But the bear was faster.

A paw the size of a pot lid crashed into the horse's flank with devastating force.

"—!"

The horse buckled from the impact. Perdiccas II was hurled from the saddle, his body bouncing across the forest floor like a discarded doll.

"Father!"

"Protect His Majesty!"

The knights converged—but even as they drew their weapons, unease flickered across their faces.

"A bear? How is it so immense... so powerful...?"

The creature before them defied nature. Its bloodshot eyes bulged with madness. Foam dripped from its maw, thick ropes of saliva pooling on the ground.

Yet its ferocity showed no sign of abating—if anything, the beast seemed to grow more aggressive with each passing second.

The Captain of the Imperial Knights felt his face drain of color.

"M-Monster! It's a Giant Bear! All knights—battle formation! Protect His Majesty!"
