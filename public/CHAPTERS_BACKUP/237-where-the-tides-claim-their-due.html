Chapter 237

Where the
Tides Claim Their Due

"The sea keeps no secrets—only bones."

"I am untouchable," Birna muttered into the howling wind, her voice carrying the frayed edges of madness."If anyone dares lay a hand on me, no one—no one—will forgive them."

The words dissolved into the storm. Pride crumbled into something rawer.

She began to weep.

"Mother... Father..."

Her voice broke apart like driftwood against rocks.

"I was wrong. Birna didn't know... I couldn't help it. I couldn't..."

She buried her face in a mud-stained rag and sobbed—deep, shuddering sobs that seemed to tear themselves from somewhere primal.

Then, slowly, she raised her head.

Her eyes, fixed upon the roiling sky, had gone strangely vacant. Empty as a house abandoned in haste.

CRACK—!

Lightning cleaved the sky in two.

The bolt struck mere inches from where she stood, and in that blinding instant, a vast and terrible light engulfed her completely.

Her vacant eyes went wild—darting, unseeing—as though witnessing something that transcended time and space itself. Visions unspooling before her that no mortal was meant to see.

"AAAAH! Sister! I'm sorry! It was my fault!"

She doubled over, clutching something to her chest. When she straightened, her hands trembled around a filthy cloth pouch—sodden with mud and rain, barely recognizable.

"Please... I will never betray my sister again..."

She sat hunched on the cliff's edge, shaking like a cornered animal. Terror had stripped away every pretense, every carefully constructed mask.

"Sister Medea... forgive me! Forgive me!"

Her bare feet—blackened with grime—slipped on the rain-slicked stone.

A soft sound. Almost gentle.

The splash was swallowed instantly by the incoming tide, erased as though it had never been.

Soon, there was nothing left on the cliff at all.

The Strait of Valdina — Days Later

Anyone who ventured into the city could scarcely avoid hearing about it.

"Did you see it? I witnessed the whole thing with my own eyes!"

The incident at the memorial service had become the talk of every tavern, every market stall, every whispered conversation in shadowed doorways.

"That dead woman was his longtime lover. He murdered his own child to save his miserable skin, then took up with another woman. Small wonder she went mad and sharpened her blade for vengeance."

"And crawling into the Fourth Princess's bed! Is that any way for proud royalty to behave? It's no surprise that Etienne—depraved as he was—only ever tormented that particular man. He must have sensed the rot beneath the surface."

For months now, the name Claudio had echoed through every corner of the realm.

And the long-awaited finale had been staged with spectacular magnificence—courtesy of Samon himself.

"What kind of family breeds such endless scandal? Father, mother, and now the son! They've manufactured more gossip than I'll encounter in a lifetime!"

"Naturally. Those who coveted the throne without understanding their own limitations—how could their private affairs be anything but sordid?"

"My word, what tenacious creatures they are. Clinging to life with such stubborn persistence."

Medea had wanted more than mere ruin for her uncle's family. More than the simple loss of power.

She had wanted to see whatever shreds of dignity they possessed ground into the mud.

And she had succeeded.

The name Claudio would endure in this nation's memory—in its histories and its whispered tales—for generations to come. A synonym for foolish ambition. For hollow vanity. For the bitter fruit of overreaching pride.

"But did you hear? Her Highness Princess Medea gathered the Claudio dead and built them a proper tomb."

"All except the wayward daughter who vanished. She recovered the remains of her aunt—flogged to death, that one—and her cousin who was stabbed at the memorial. Even her uncle, whose ashes were hastily disposed of to conceal his wife's affair."

"If it were me, I'd have let them rot wherever they fell! But Her Highness still honored their final journey—because they were family. Truly, she is a woman of extraordinary compassion."

Throughout the realm, people marveled at the Princess's mercy. Her grace in extending familial duty even to those who had betrayed her most grievously.

But they were wrong.

"Your Highness. This was recovered from the cliff where Birna Claudio disappeared."

Kensington extended his hand. Medea did not speak.

"It was a night of fierce tides. The body has yet to surface. If you wish, I can dispatch additional search parties."

"...No. This will suffice."

Medea accepted the cloth pouch, worn and dirty beyond recognition.

Thud. Thud.

The gentle rhythm of waves breaking against distant rocks reached her ears.

At the terminus of a gently sloping hill overlooking the sea, three small graves stood in a silent row. The shadows of passing clouds drifted slowly across the headstones, swallowing the fading light of the setting sun.

Medea stood before the three markers—each a different size.

She let something fall from her fingers.

"The whole family is finally here."

It was the filthy rag pouch that Kensington had delivered.

The world believed Medea had built this tomb to honor her uncle's memory—a final act of familial piety.

"Goodbye, Leah."

But they were mistaken.

"Your mother has avenged you."

This was Medea's offering to her daughter. The broken bodies of those who had taken everything from her, laid at her child's feet.

" Mama, don't cry.
Leah will hold you. "

Now, at last, it was time to let her go.

The tears came unbidden at the thought—her precious girl, lost forever.

Medea reached out, her pale fingertips tracing the empty air as though sketching the contours of a face she would never touch again.

But there was still work to be done.

She turned her gaze toward the horizon. In the distance, the temple's cream-colored bell tower hung suspended in the dying light of sunset.

The Palace of Valdina

"Did Medea go to visit them?"

Peleus's brow furrowed when he learned where his sister had gone upon her return. He found her sense of duty almost exasperating in its thoroughness.

One must repay what one has received—but even vengeance should know its proper limits.

Of course, the Claudios who had been on the receiving end of that vengeance would hardly agree with such a sentiment.

Still... if there are those who would seek to exploit Dea's sense of fairness—her inclination to return precisely what she is given...

Peleus paused mid-thought, his expression sharpening.

Because he realized there was already a man who had bestowed considerable"favors" upon his sister. His striking blue eyes narrowed dangerously.

"Dea. I will handle the matter of repaying the First Prince's assistance. You needn't concern yourself with it."

"Where on earth did that come from?"

Medea found herself caught between bewilderment and amusement at her brother's suddenly grave tone.

"If he ever attempts to approach you under the pretext of collecting on that debt, you must inform me immediately. Do you understand?"

Medea noticed her brother's hand drift unconsciously toward his sword hilt.

If Peleus discovered that Cesare had been climbing through her bedroom window uninvited...

Trouble would certainly follow.

She really ought to tell him to stop spouting such nonsense.

The thought had barely formed before she caught herself—and froze.

Because she realized she had been taking it for granted that Cesare would come seeking her out. As certain of his return to Valdina as she was of the sunrise.

"Peleus, this is hardly the time to worry about me."

Medea adopted a playfully scolding tone.

"Do you have any idea how many portraits of eligible noblewomen D'Angel has been thrusting at me? You need to get married, brother. Now that even Samon Claudio is dead, ambitious families will target you directly."

An unmarried Emperor without heirs was, after all, a prize worth pursuing at any cost.

"Well, as for you—"

"I have made my decision."

When Medea paused, Peleus continued.

"I contemplated this matter at length while crossing Katzen's border. Valdina has grown strong enough to stand alone—we no longer require alliances sealed through marriage. So tell me: why should I send you away? No place in this world will love and cherish you more than this nation. More than your homeland."

"Peleus..."

"I have no desire for marriage. I lack the confidence to find someone who would care for me as our mother and father cared for each other—and truthfully, I have no wish to try."

"Making my nephew or niece heir to the throne is a perfectly acceptable alternative."

Peleus had witnessed, throughout his entire life, how those who lost a beloved were shattered by grief.

He could not afford such weakness. He was the Emperor of this realm—and Medea's only remaining blood.

"There are plenty of worthy men in Valdina. I can rest easy knowing you'll find happiness here."

In truth, he had already identified several candidates he deemed acceptable matches for his sister.

"Peleus. Do not make such weighty matters sound so simple."

Medea blinked rapidly, her eyes stinging.

"Did you learn nothing from our uncle? Have you forgotten what happens when you leave direct siblings behind in the line of succession?"

The thought of her brother caring for her so deeply—sacrificing so much—made her eyes burn with unshed tears.

"Valdina and I will protect you to the very end, no matter what may come."

Peleus reached out and stroked her hair with gentle fingers.

"So please... allow yourself to rest now. Let your heart find some measure of peace."

A Brother's Prayer

Pale skin and soft silver hair. His sister's face was small enough to vanish behind his hands—a reminder of how fragile she remained, despite everything she had endured.

God, I dare to ask you this one thing:

Protect this child.

In the moment when he should have felt the relief of victory—having eliminated a threat that had loomed over them for so long—Peleus instead felt a cold fear coiling in his chest.

He repeated the prayer to himself, again and again.
