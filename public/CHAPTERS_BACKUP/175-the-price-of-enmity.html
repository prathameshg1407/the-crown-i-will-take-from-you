Chapter 175

The Price of
Enmity

"Fear proves more effective than forgiveness when dealing with enemies."

Cesare turned toward Peleus with the faintest suggestion of a smile.

"I trust you weren't too startled by this uninvited guest."

His soft voice resonated through the chamber with an almost musical cadence.

"I convey Katzen's most sincere congratulations to Valdina's Emperor. May the alliance between our nations strengthen further under Your Majesty's enlightened rule."

The audacity was breathtaking—to speak on Katzen's behalf without imperial edict or official mandate.

Yet no one questioned his authority.

Imperial edicts are merely ink on parchment. Everyone understood implicitly that Katzen's next Emperor would be First Prince Cesare.

Jason's fists clenched in impotent fury.

Despite being Grand Duke and the Prince's own cousin, Cesare hadn't granted him even the courtesy of forewarning.

It was a calculated action that utterly demolished his standing as this delegation's appointed leader.

The First Prince had always been willful, but Jason found himself appalled by such brazen disregard displayed before foreign powers.

If *she's* watching this humiliation...

"Your Highness honors us by attending personally. Valdina will not forget Katzen's sincerity."

Emperor Peleus remained wary of the First Prince, yet treated him with measured respect. He stood among the few present today who weren't overwhelmed by Cesare's commanding presence.

Peleus responded with a cold, penetrating gaze—as though attempting to decipher the Prince's true intentions.

But the undeniable truth remained: Cesare proved vastly preferable to Jason, who had coveted both his sister and his kingdom.

Even if the opponent stood poised to become Crown Prince—even if he possessed the audacity to metaphorically slap royalty across the face upon arrival—there existed sufficient reason not to reject an extended hand.

He is Katzen's First Prince...

Medea studied him with quiet intensity.

This marked her first encounter with him alive—in either timeline.

He'd seized everyone's attention the instant he materialized. He'd even shattered the Goddess's statue before the Holy Kingdom's entire delegation, though his motivations remained opaque.

Regardless of Rachel's formidable holy powers, proving manipulation from pulverized marble dust would prove impossible.

A far more definitive outcome than my original stratagem.

"Thank you for your gracious hospitality. As expected, Valdina proves both kind and welcoming."

The First Prince's eyes—pronouncing the word *kind*—shifted almost imperceptibly as they found Medea.

"May the Goddess's grace rest always upon Valdina's Princess."

He greeted her with surprising courtesy.

"It is an honor to see Your Highness in such robust health."

Medea spoke with deliberate obliqueness. The Prince's smile deepened perceptibly.

He knows I provided the antidote.

Medea attributed the Prince's amiable demeanor to simple gratitude for her assistance.

Had she observed more carefully, she might have detected the flash of pure delight in those golden eyes—as though he'd encountered something precious beyond measure.

◆

The Holy Kingdom delegation could no longer endure the chaotic atmosphere generated by the weeping statue and shattered mirror.

They recognized that if this environment persisted, even continental faith in the current ecclesiastical authority might begin to fracture.

Medea observed their departure with satisfaction—the Holy delegation fabricating excuses for their premature exit.

"Your Highness! It's been far too long."

A cheerful voice cut through her thoughts.

Medea turned to find a young man with straw-blond hair and an impish grin.

"Sir Gallo."

"Ah, forgive me—I should demonstrate proper etiquette now that you've ascended to Imperial Princess."

Gallo executed an exaggerated bow, touching forehead to chest in theatrical deference.

When their eyes met, he winked conspiratorially.

Delight animated his sun-bronzed features.

"Sir, you remain unchanged."

"Though we've exchanged only correspondence recently without meeting face-to-face, Your Highness retains her characteristic kindness. Thank you for remembering us and extending invitations to such an august occasion."

"I wished to address Valdina's trusted friend properly."

The Facade mercantile house had departed Valdina three years prior, carrying the Lantern of Dawn that Medea had gifted them.

Yet Gallo had continued serving as liaison between the First Prince and Medea throughout that time.

"Truthfully, I worried that we arms merchants might diminish the banquet's dignity by attending. Instead, I witnessed scenes of such rarity they'll never be replicated in my lifetime. Thank you once again for including me."

He laughed heartily.

Where else would one observe a Prince commandeering a holy knight's sword to demolish the Goddess's statue—then watch the Holy Kingdom's priests retreat in utter disarray?

It would have been tragic to miss such a spectacle from any perspective.

"Indeed. I trust you'll prove a reliable conduit for those unable to attend today."

Gallo grasped her meaning immediately, his grin turning mischievous.

"Naturally. The Goddess manifested Her divine will after centuries of silence. Though I profess atheism, I cannot help but spread word of this miraculous revelation."

Despite being merely the public face of Facade, he commanded that vast mercantile empire's external operations. Perhaps such responsibility explained his exceptional perceptiveness.

Medea nodded, her composure unwavering.

"But is this truly wise? Making enemies of even the Holy Lands carries considerable danger."

Gallo glanced around, confirming their privacy before lowering his voice.

"There's an old saying: 'One steep slope brings ten misfortunes.' Now that Valdina has ascended to empire, you'll accumulate more enemies than allies."

His expression remained cheerful, but the warning carried weight.

"Should you wish it, we can deploy our operatives. Facade excels at... managing unsavory characters who operate in shadows."

Another wink.

"I suspect you already comprehend our capabilities."

Subtle confidence threaded through his tone.

Facade operated as an independent arms dealer sworn to no nation. How many would have targeted this unprecedented continental phenomenon?

"I'll accept only your sincere well-wishes."

"Your Highness, the reality of accumulating more enemies than allies applies not solely to Valdina—but to you personally. The Princess herself grows increasingly vulnerable."

Gallo's eyebrows rose with genuine concern.

Regardless of his lord's directives, he truly worried for the remarkable Princess before him.

She now stood as the Emperor's sole sibling—the Valdina Empire's spiritual cornerstone.

Moreover, the Princess herself displayed alarming disregard for personal safety when pursuing her objectives.

He recalled Valdina's Princess arriving at the minister's estate in dead of night, seeking evidence of treason.

She slew the legendary Hydra. Evaded the Western Empress's assassins. Escaped while carrying my unconscious master to safety...

If she served as his superior, he'd have asked the impertinent question of whether she possessed multiple lives.

Retrospection made Gallo dizzy despite the events not being his own burden.

"I know."

Medea's tranquil response cut through his concerns.

"But you must acknowledge that Valdina has always possessed more enemies than allies."

"..."

Duke Claudio had dreamed of rebellion.

Powerful nobles had exploited citizens and eroded the kingdom throughout a decade of war.

The Katzen royal family had sought to exploit Valdina during those vulnerable gaps.

Yes... the Princess speaks truth.

Gallo conceded internally. Valdina's enemies had always existed—and the worst period hadn't been now, but then.

"Defense alone cannot adequately counter enemies. Regardless of defensive strength, they'll inevitably discover vulnerabilities."

Her calm voice pierced Gallo's contemplation.

"Fear proves far more effective. If they understand that opposing me means forfeiting everything—status, position, and..."

"..."

"...even their lives."

Medea's voice remained so composed it actually conveyed anticipation.

"..."

"I believe you already understand this truth."

Gallo found himself speechless, confronted by those emerald eyes that curved with a slight smile—utterly incongruous with the chilling words they accompanied.

"The wisest rulers understand:
mercy invites betrayal,
but fear commands loyalty."
