Chapter 264

The Signet
& the Vow

"I do not betray my wife."

The moment her conversation with the Pope concluded, a figure stepped into Medea's path.

"Dea... How could you..."

It was Peleus.

Had the Pope not been the one occupying Medea's attention, her brother would surely have shouldered his way through and interrupted mid-sentence.

"What were you thinking, surrendering to his whims like this?"

His voice emerged low and strained, each word forced through clenched teeth. The young Emperor's handsome face had gone as cold and forbidding as a northern gale.

"Peleus."

"It's not too late to withdraw. Even if we must reconvene the Continental Congress to annul this—"

Medea caught her brother's arm before he could storm after the departing Pope.

"Those who sit upon thrones should not love. The imperial seat is a merciless place. I don't wish to see you condemned to such loneliness."

"I know."

Better than anyone else in this world, I know.

The words echoed silently in Medea's heart.

"Peleus... I chose him."

Fury flickered in those sapphire eyes. Medea tightened her grip on his arm, willing him to understand.

"He cut down Jared for my sake. He risked his life to slay the evil dragon and halt the oracle's demand for my sacrifice. How could I not care for a man who has done all that?"

"..."

"I want him, Peleus."

Yet the more she explained, the darker her brother's expression became—not with anger now, but with something far more painful.

Self-reproach.

"This is your brother's failing."

He pressed a hand to his gaunt face.

"I left you alone for far too long. And now..."

Now he was paying the price for abandoning his little sister when she had needed him most.

What use is it to be praised as a wise Emperor? I couldn't reach the heart of a young girl starving for affection.

"You're right... Hah. Dea, men will do anything to achieve their ends."

In his eyes, she had fallen for calculated kindness—favors offered with ulterior purpose. He didn't know who Cesare truly was, couldn't fathom what dark designs lurked behind that charming facade.

Rather than being reassured, his face grew grimmer still.

Medea found herself at a loss.

'This is becoming more complicated than I anticipated...'

She took a step backward—and collided with something solid as a stone wall.

The impact sent her stumbling.

Peleus moved instinctively to catch her.

But before he could reach her, a hand extended from behind, catching Medea's shoulders with effortless grace and steadying her balance.

"Careful."

Cesare.

Peleus's gaze dropped to where those fingers rested on his sister's shoulder. The temperature in the air plummeted.

"First Prince."

"Your Majesty."

Before Cesare could complete even a cursory bow, Peleus's voice cut through the air like a blade of ice.

"I will confess that I once held you in high regard, First Prince. However, it seems the blood of Katzen flowing through your veins cannot be denied. Do you think me blind to your intentions? You mean to use our Dea—to leverage Valdina itself—in your quest for the imperial throne."

Peleus could restrain himself no longer. His accusations came sharp and direct, bristling with open hostility.

"Every time Dea found herself in danger, you appeared as if by providence. It was all calculated for this moment, wasn't it? You even brought that black beast into Valdina, fully aware of the chaos it would unleash!"

"Peleus!"

Medea's face drained of color.

Never before had she witnessed her reserved, solitary brother attack someone so primitively, so directly.

It was proof of how deeply his anger ran. Yet Cesare had no obligation to endure such accusations.

This had been her plan from the very beginning. Cesare had been the first—and most capable—ally she had sought for the expedition. Moreover—

"Peleus, this marriage was my decision—"

But before she could finish, Cesare stepped forward, positioning himself directly in her path.

As though shielding her from her brother's fury.

"I understand your concerns, Your Majesty. Indeed, no one on this continent has experienced the cruelty of the throne more intimately than I have."

A ripple of shock passed through the assembled onlookers.

Everyone present understood his meaning—the veiled reference to two people: his father, Perdiccas II, and his adoptive mother, Empress Eleanor.

The First Prince himself was living proof of Perdiccas II's betrayal of his own wife.

Yet Cesare, having laid bare the shame no one dared speak aloud, appeared utterly untroubled.

"That is precisely why I swore an oath to my mother only moments ago. I will hold this engagement as sacred, and I will honor it with everything I have."

He took Medea's hand in his own.

Then, with deliberate grace, he drew a ring from his finger.

"Even if I were to refuse all aid from Valdina, you would not believe my sincerity. Very well—I shall offer the Princess something of equal measure."

Slowly, as though oblivious to the flames blazing in Peleus's eyes, he slid the ring onto Medea's slender finger.

Her small, pale hand disappeared entirely within his grasp.

"Medea."

When he withdrew his hand, the signet of the First Prince gleamed upon the ring—unmistakable and absolute.

"This ring represents my power—and now, yours as well. From this moment forward, you possess the authority to decree laws within my territories, to command my armies, and to rule my subjects."

Cesare spoke with perfect calm, as though he couldn't feel Medea's astonished gaze fixed upon him.

"None may defy your will. And whoever dares threaten you shall become my enemy."

Cesare turned to face Peleus directly.

Golden eyes met sapphire in a clash of wills.

"..."

For the first time, Peleus found himself without words.

Cesare's response to the accusation that he sought to exploit Medea was almost embarrassingly decisive.

Yet Peleus could no longer argue.

To surrender the signet ring—the very symbol of the First Prince's sovereign authority—was to relinquish power itself.

The onlookers could scarcely believe their eyes.

"Even if Valdina provides support to the First Prince through this marriage, the Princess is not Valdina's sovereign—there are limits to what she can offer him."

"But with that signet ring, she can wield her husband's actual power—not merely the ceremonial authority of a consort."

"No matter how you calculate it, this arrangement overwhelmingly favors the Princess."

'Has the First Prince lost his mind after slaying the dragon?'

"The man with the strongest claim to Katzen's throne—surrendering his seal to prove his devotion?"

At that moment, Cesare turned slightly toward Medea.

"Dea. I shall place the wedding band upon your finger at the ceremony."

The assembled guests could not help but notice how the First Prince's voice softened when he addressed the Princess—lower, warmer, far more intimate.

And he had used her familiar name.

'Ah. Now I understand. When a man is blinded by love, the world can turn upside down overnight.'

It seemed even the Blood Conqueror was not immune to love's transformative power.

As if to cement their conviction, Cesare spoke once more.

"I will not forsake Dea."

His golden gaze swept across the assembly—passing over Medea, then Peleus, and finally coming to rest upon an elderly figure standing amid the crowd.

Eratosthenes. His maternal grandfather.

There was no longing in Cesare's eyes. No joy at their reunion.

Eratosthenes returned the look with equal detachment. Unlike the excited murmuring of the crowd, his weathered face remained utterly impassive—as though he placed no faith in the sincerity of a grandson who would surrender even his seal.

Though years had passed since their last meeting, the two acknowledged each other with all the warmth of strangers passing on a winter road.

Cesare spoke again, his voice carrying through the hall.

"I do not betray my wife."

"That wasn't necessary, you know."

When they were finally alone, Medea spoke in a low voice, lifting her hand to examine the signet ring adorning her finger.

"Peleus would have objected regardless, but the ceremony would have proceeded on schedule. Yet you gave me this—your seal, of all things. How do you intend to reclaim it later?"

Cesare merely shrugged.

"If I hadn't, your brother was fully prepared to challenge me to single combat—to the death, most likely. I'd rather not cross swords with my new family on my wedding day."

"Surely not..."

Medea paused, reconsidering.

Given the ferocity of Peleus's earlier demeanor, Cesare's assessment was probably accurate.

Rather than pressing the point, she shifted the subject.

"Hah... There were members of the Emperor's faction among the Katzen delegation earlier, weren't there? You wanted him to see."

A message meant for Perdiccas II.

'I wanted to show you.'

Though the intended recipient was not her father.

Cesare swallowed a bitter laugh. Medea was perceptive in the strangest ways.

In that brief moment when his eyes had met his grandfather's cold, dismissive gaze—how had she noticed the flicker of emotion he hadn't even acknowledged to himself?

'I suppose I wanted to prove that I am nothing like your son-in-law.'

A fleeting shadow passed across his beautiful features.

"I gave my absolute best, and instead of praise, I received only reproach. The injustice of it wounds me deeply."

Cesare pressed a hand to his chest with theatrical despair.

But his voice rippled with lazy amusement—he sounded anything but discouraged.

"That was rather excessive of you..."

"Everything seems excessive compared to your marriage proposal, which contained not a single shred of romance."

"..."

Medea fell silent.

During their flight from Death Valley to Sacramentis, a realization had crystallized within her.

'This will not be the end. This life has diverged too far from the path I once knew.'

The changes she had set in motion would continue to generate unpredictable variables—and fate would keep finding ways to target her.

Jason had awakened to memories of his past life.

The knowledge and experience that had once been hers alone could no longer be monopolized.

"As long as the oracle continues to hunt me, I will never truly be free."

She had understood this instinctively. To avoid repeating the tragedies of her previous existence, she had to seize the initiative.

The Proposal

"Cesare, take me as your bride and inherit the oracle. In three years, I will make you Emperor of Katzen."

Within that span, she had planned to eliminate both Jason and Rachel. Fierce determination had blazed in her rust-colored eyes.

That was how this elaborate performance had begun.

A play conceived by Medea—and brought to completion by Cesare.

"...Since when did the indomitable First Prince become so sentimental?"

Medea's soft snort drew a smile to Cesare's lips.

"And if I had refused your proposition?"

"Then I would have sought someone else to fulfill the covenant."

In that instant, something dangerous flickered through those mesmerizing golden eyes.

"...It wouldn't have been that grub of a brother of mine. So tell me—who else did you have in mind?"
