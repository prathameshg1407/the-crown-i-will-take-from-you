Chapter 320

Poisoned Faith

"Even the holiest sanctuaries harbor serpents in their midst."

Pope Benedict removed his mitre as he entered his private office, night sweat trickling down his forehead into his white beard.

The oppressive air of the oracle chamber still clung to him like a shroud.

"The oracle room is a sealed chamber, Your Holiness. It's difficult to remain there for extended periods, yet you've been spending hours inside lately."

Chief Priest Anastasia draped a thick blanket across the Pope's shoulders, her voice laced with concern.

"Does this have something to do with why you dispatched the Templar Commander outside the castle?"

The Pope remained silent for a long moment before nodding slowly.

"There's something that continues to trouble me."

He lowered himself into his chair with the careful movements of an old man bearing too many burdens.

"In all the recorded history of our faith... has the Goddess ever delivered two separate oracles about the same matter?"

The second oracle had appeared after a significant delay following the first—the one that had declared Medea must become the bride of the evil dragon.

'And today I discovered something else. The base of the oracle stele... it's been broken. The fracture is so fine it's nearly invisible.'

That discovery had lodged itself in his mind like a splinter he couldn't remove.

He had sent Leonor, Commander of the Templars, on a covert mission—to search the ancient sanctuaries for any precedent of tampered oracles.

It was the first time in centuries that anyone had dared move the sacred stele from the oracle chamber.

"But Your Holiness, isn't it excessive to send only Sir Theodore as escort? For just the two of them, this investigation will take considerable time."

"This must remain absolutely secret."

The Pope's sigh carried the weight of his years.

"If word spreads that we're questioning the authenticity of the Goddess's words, the entire Church could be thrown into chaos."

Deep lines of exhaustion carved through the Pope's weathered face as he pulled the blanket tighter.

"Leave this matter to me. And please, no more agenda items tonight. I've spent far too long in the oracle chamber today—I'm certain my other duties have piled up considerably."

But Anastasia shook her head firmly.

"Your Holiness, it's time for rest. You cannot continue driving yourself like this—your body will fail you."

She understood better than anyone the crushing burden the Pope now carried.

After exposing Rachel as a false Saintess, he had launched sweeping reforms—purging corrupt priests and attempting to revive a temple that had grown complacent and bloated with excess.

Many positions had been filled with new blood in a remarkably short time.

Yet not everyone welcomed these winds of change sweeping through the sacred halls.

The common people rejoiced at the promise of a purified temple. But among the nobility and the priesthood, discontent festered like an infected wound.

"Perhaps... wouldn't it be better to make Rachel's execution public sooner? Then Saintess Medea might forgive us and return to the fold—"

"It's not merely anger that keeps her away."

Pope Benedict's voice carried a note of finality.

The Saintess's decision to abandon the temple hadn't been made recently. It dated back to the very beginning—to when they had sacrificed her to a false oracle, blinded by their hunger for glory and power.

"What should we do, Your Holiness? Even among the high priests, voices of anxiety grow louder. Some fear the Saintess has forsaken the Church entirely."

"I cannot answer that. Even this old man doesn't know. Now please, go rest."

Left alone in the silence, Pope Benedict found sleep impossible.

'Even if the Goddess shows us the consequences of our failings, I must continue serving Her without shame until my final breath.'

"I must do what I can, for as long as I am able."

He knelt before the small prayer alcove in his chamber, rosary beads sliding through his fingers.

The statue of the Goddess gazed down at him with stone eyes that revealed nothing.

'I don't know how much longer this old body can endure—'

Knock knock.

The Pope's eyes opened.

"High Priest Paul? What brings you here at such a late hour?"

"Forgive the intrusion, Your Holiness. I know it's improper, but—"

Paul's face was flushed, his breathing labored. The sharp scent of alcohol preceded him into the room.

The Pope's jaw tightened.

"High Priest, do not forget your sacred duties. This is the last warning I will give you."

"I understand, Your Holiness! That is precisely why I've come."

Paul's words tumbled out in a desperate rush.

"I wish to make confession. I want to confess all my transgressions and discourtesies toward Saintess Medea—to be cleansed of my sins and reborn with a pure heart."

The Pope studied him in silence, weighing the sincerity behind the words.

"Truly, Your Holiness."

"If you seek confession, the confessional will be available after dawn prayers tomorrow."

"But then... the others would see..."

Paul stammered, wringing his hands.

"I beg you. If my confession becomes public knowledge... I would lose face as a high priest..."

In other words, he wanted absolution without the embarrassment of public penance.

The man still clung to worldly vanity. What value could such a confession possibly hold?

Yet when the Pope noticed tears tracking through Paul's trembling fingers and beard, he couldn't suppress his sigh.

"...Very well. Come in."

The chamber was cluttered with the remnants of prayer—incense ash, scattered scripture, worn cushions.

As Paul entered, the Pope turned toward the altar and knelt to retrieve a fresh set of rosary beads from the drawer.

'The star-blessed beads should be here...'

A shadow fell across the back of his hand.

"High Priest Paul?"

He looked up.

Paul's mouth twisted into something grotesque, tears still glistening on his cheeks.

"Forgive me, Your Holiness. But this is for the temple. For the Goddess."

His voice cracked with fervent conviction.

"I cannot stand idly by while a blind Pope leads us to ruin."

Thud.

An empty vial rolled across the floor.

Meanwhile, in the Katzen Imperial Palace, the vassals of the First Prince's faction gathered in the long, oval-shaped conference chamber.

At the head of the table sat three figures: Prince Cesare, Princess Medea, and the First Prince himself.

"Lord Alfred, is it confirmed that Grand Duke Carlisle has returned to his homeland?"

"Yes, Your Highness. His ship departed at dawn the day after he left the tower. It appears Count Montfort—father of the Western Empress—applied considerable pressure to facilitate his departure."

"And when news of the Fourth Princess's repatriation reached her, the Eastern Empress rose from her sickbed. Though she hasn't yet recovered enough to move about freely."

The reports continued in measured succession.

"The Eastern Empress's faction is attempting to install Countess Olcane Delgano as the new head of palace maids, replacing the Western Empress's people. However, they're meeting significant internal resistance. Just recently, the husband of Countess Beaumont—a close ally of the Eastern Empress—was demoted for corruption. That was the Third Prince's doing."

The Western Empress and Eastern Empress factions were locked in vicious conflict, as were their respective children—the Third Prince and the Fourth Princess.

The Fourth Princess's faction, long suppressed, now seized this opportunity to strike back against the Third Prince with relentless ferocity.

For their part, the Third Prince's supporters had no intention of relinquishing power despite the Western Empress's fall. They defended their positions with equal intensity.

"Fortunately for us, this internecine warfare means our faction is experiencing relative peace."

With the Western Empress imprisoned in the tower, the Third Prince had temporarily abandoned his pursuit of their cause.

Instead, he focused entirely on protecting what power remained to him.

"The imperial court is in such turmoil between those two factions that this is the first time we've had breathing room in months."

The vassals completed their briefing, then added with barely suppressed excitement:

"And even within the Third Prince's faction, several individuals have expressed interest in... joining our side."

When the Emperor's life had seemed in jeopardy, those eager to abandon a sinking ship had naturally emerged.

What surprised the vassals was their chosen destination—the First Prince's faction, the very group they had feared and scorned for so long.

Medea, listening to the reports, maintained an expressionless mask.

'How remarkable that His Highness can remain so composed...'

The First Prince's vassals could barely contain their astonishment.

Not long ago, Medea had been dismissed by nearly everyone outside a handful of Emperor-faction nobles. Now her name dominated every conversation in Katzen's social circles.

"Does Your Highness the Princess have any other observations to share?"

The cautious deference in their voices, the respectful bows—all remarkably sincere.

With control of the faction's slush funds in her capable hands, Medea had demonstrated her political acumen clearly.

Her cold, analytical demeanor seemed the perfect complement to a kingmaker's role. They had every reason to show respect.

"Thanks to Her Highness's efforts, our First Prince faction's position has never been stronger!"

"Once the Third Prince falls, the Crown Prince title will finally belong to our lord!"

Excited murmurs of agreement rippled through the conference hall.

"I wouldn't celebrate prematurely."

The Marquis of Rhenard's deep voice cut through the optimistic atmosphere like a blade.

"We face one fatal obstacle in securing the Crown Prince position for His Highness."

Silence descended on the chamber.

"His Majesty the Emperor despises the First Prince with every fiber of his being. What makes you think he'll simply hand over his throne willingly?"

The words hung in the air like a death sentence.

Reality crashed down on their brief moment of hope.
