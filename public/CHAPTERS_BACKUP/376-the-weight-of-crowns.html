The Weight
of Crowns

"Some duties cannot be abandoned, even for love."

Doesn't he see the poor old man standing beside him now—eyes blazing with determination, clutching the imperial crown?

But Cesare merely shrugged.

"The coronation can be postponed as long as I wish. And how could I possibly let you go when you're not even traveling alone?"

His golden eyes caressed Medea's still-flat belly with unmistakable tenderness.

Ugh.

Marquis Leniard, having reached his absolute limit, stumbled backward. Neriel rushed forward to support him.

"Cesare, you're postponing your ascension and going to the Holy Nation? Have you lost your mind?!"

Terence forgot all courtly protocol, addressing his prince by name in front of everyone.

"We can understand Her Highness's desire to travel to the Holy Nation. She's not only Katzen's representative but the entire continent's. We wouldn't dare question her determination to fulfill that sacred responsibility."

Terence's eyes were bloodshot behind his slightly cracked spectacles.

He'd been waiting for Cesare to awaken, filling the void with his own body and will. And now, the moment his prince woke, he planned to abandon his post again.

No matter how restrained Terence tried to be, his rage couldn't help but surface.

"Since the Emperor passed, there are mountains of pending state affairs! Hyenas circle the throne, drooling over the position—and where are you going?!"

That's right!

The vassals of the First Prince's faction emphatically agreed with their eyes alone.

"After witnessing that hell, is there still a brave fool who dares challenge me?"

"Please, please be reasonable! If you wait patiently, Her Highness will return on her own!"

But Cesare didn't answer.

His gaze drifted silently toward Theo and Peleus.

In that look, Medea read traces of his worry—the fear that she might never return to Katzen.

She reached out and gently took Cesare's hand.

The unexpected contact made him flinch. She cupped his hand, squeezing it tightly.

Meeting his sun-gold eyes, she spoke.

"Cesare, it will take quite some time."

A pause.

"But I will come back to you."

Medea knew this man was the only one who would always welcome her warmly—the only arms in which she could truly rest.

"So take up your responsibility here, and I'll do the same."

The Crown's Promise

After a long silence, Cesare slowly nodded.

He glanced at Marquis Leniard.

The Marquis hesitated, then bowed his head and presented the crown to Cesare.

Cesare took it—and placed it in Medea's hand.

The weight was substantial.

"Yes. You'll have to come back and place this on my head yourself."

Cesare's large hand closed firmly over hers, enclosing the crown.

"Otherwise, your husband will remain a nameless man for the rest of his life."

There was even an arrogance in his tone—a certainty that he could rule perfectly well without the Emperor's crown.

"Dea, your brother will accompany you."

Peleus stepped forward.

"Your Majesty, just a moment—"

Sissair hurried forward, alarm written across his face.

"I'll escort you to the Holy Nation myself. Don't worry about your safety."

Cesare had watched Terence's outburst as though it were someone else's problem.

Now it was Sissair's turn to feel the same frustration.

"Your Majesty..."

Is the Emperor of Valdina now saying he, too, will abandon his post?

Valdina's Prime Minister, Sissair, lit a fire in his eyes.

But when Peleus didn't budge, Sissair opened his mouth—then swallowed his words.

"If that's the case, I must return to Valdina immediately. Someone must attend to state affairs without forgetting their duty."

It was unclear whether the remark was a plea or biting satire.

Peleus deliberately avoided his gaze, pretending not to notice.

Sissair's eyes, sharp behind his spectacles, swept past Medea and landed on Cesare.

"I came to fetch Her Highness in case she wished for freedom. But her husband has bound himself so tightly to her side that I suppose she no longer needs us."

He thought to himself:

There is no other madman on this continent who would dedicate his eternal destiny to his wife—going so far as to swear an unbreakable oath—than Acares. Or rather, Cesare Katzen.

"Yes. You, and no one else, will have to worry about that until the day you're laid in your coffin."

Cesare responded coolly.

It was a bonus that his gaze remained fixed on Theo and Kensington.

Theo swallowed a breathless sigh.

He'd revealed his true feelings for a fleeting moment without realizing it—but Medea hadn't even taken them seriously.

And that greedy man, who cannot tolerate even that single moment, is the Saintess's companion.

There would never come a time when Theo could understand her choice.

But this was enough for him.

The feelings that hadn't been reciprocated would remain unspoken—transformed into nothing more than reverence for the goddess's representative.

"Please have no fear. I will serve you with all my heart."

Not long after, a carriage was prepared before the cathedral.

The black carriage bore the emblem of the Katzen Imperial Family. The guards surrounding it were the elite Agemas of Valdina.

Peleus mounted his horse. The Agemas' insignia engraved on his silver armor gleamed in the sunlight.

Medea looked back before entering the carriage.

"I'll be back."

She raised her hand—but couldn't meet his eyes for long. She lowered her gaze, afraid he'd see her reddened eyes.

The time she'd waited for him had been too long. Their reunion, too short.

'Have a safe journey, Dea.'

Cesare nodded slowly.

Rattle, rattle.

The carriage wheels began their slow journey toward the Holy Nation.

Cesare couldn't tear his eyes away until the carriage vanished from sight.

In front of Medea, he'd pretended to understand—accepted the cause of saving the Holy Nation and let her go calmly. That was his limit.

Deep nail marks cut into his palms beneath his clenched fists.

If he loosened his grip even slightly, he felt he might chase after the moving carriage.

One Year Later

Holy Kingdom of Sacramontis

After the service, Pope Anastasia removed her mitre.

"Captain Theo, is today the day we depart for Katzen?"

"Yes, Your Holiness."

The beautiful knight beside Pope Anastasia answered.

On the right shoulder of his cream-colored armor hung a silver badge—belonging to Leonor, the former Commander of the Temple Knights.

Grand Master Theodore of the Knights Templar was known to always wear this badge in memory of his predecessors and fallen comrades.

"It's already been a year! Time truly flies."

One of the Pope's sleeves billowed gently in the wind.

A black blindfold covered the upper half of her face—a scar from torture inflicted by the black magicians who had occupied the central temple.

But Anastasia had survived.

She was one of the precious few who endured when so many priests had fallen. Eventually, she became the first mid-level priest to ascend to the papal throne.

One Year Ago

After High Priest Paul assassinated Pope Benedict, the Holy Nation engaged in fierce debate over the vacant throne.

The candidates narrowed to just two surviving priests.

Both had gained attention for facing the primordial darkness head-on.

However, the High Priest of Katzen was greatly weakened in body and spirit after that day's horrors. It was judged he couldn't lead the Holy Nation.

In the end, the position went to Anastasia.

The backlash from the faithful against the youngest Pope—who shattered centuries of tradition—was considerable.

Nevertheless, no one could deny that the election was smoothly accepted thanks to the firm support of Saintess Medea.

Pope Anastasia restored the central temple and championed sweeping reforms.

And at the center of it all stood Medea.

The Saintess's Decree

"Most high-ranking positions in the central temple have fallen vacant. Anastasia, if not now, when will there ever be another opportunity like this?"

Medea set the nation straight—radically and fiercely.

"Any evil committed in the Holy Nation in God's name will no longer be tolerated."

"Don't you realize that your stubbornness and arrogance weakened the goddess's power and ultimately created an opening for darkness to creep in?"

"If we remain obsessed with the authority before our eyes, losing sight of the truth—if we still don't recognize the work His Holiness and the priests accomplished—then there is no future for this Holy Nation."

Ultimately, the central temple abandoned the monotheistic doctrine, religious persecution, and biblical distortions the previous establishment had maintained for centuries.

A new order was established—integrating Catholic and Protestant faiths.

Medea even embraced the Shadeia tribes as part of the temple.

"After Saintess Medea awakened, the divine judgments that had been falling upon the Shadeia ceased. We could finally bring them back whole."

Well-versed in primordial forces, the Shadeia proved invaluable in restoring sacred relics destroyed by black magicians.

They also excelled in combat, having lived as a wandering tribe and faced the demons sent as divine punishment.

Thanks to this, many young Shadeia priests were selected to join the Knights Templar.

"While we're on the subject, don't overwork yourself, Knights Templar. We just finished subduing the monsters in the Swamp of Gluttony. I heard Sir Theo hasn't rested at all."

"No, Your Holiness. This is how I repay the Saintess."

Theo smiled faintly.

While subduing monsters in the abandoned lands, he never turned a blind eye to suffering people—as though repenting for his sin of failing to stop Rachel, choosing to walk through the most difficult paths.

Pope Anastasia clicked her tongue.

"By the way, I wish Saintess Medea would stay here instead of returning to Katzen."

"Your Holiness, I'd be tempted to agree—but there are no priests brave enough to take responsibility for the Emperor of Katzen anymore."

The senior priest assisting her shook his head.

"I don't know if this is a temple or the Katzen Palace. When the Saintess was in labor, he practically emptied the prayer room."

"Ah, how could I forget that day?"

Even Pope Anastasia's composure broke at the memory.

When Medea arrived in the Holy Nation, she was already well past the early stages of pregnancy.

About half a year later, she gave birth in the central temple.

"At that time, I truly thought there was darkness lurking in his blood. Afterward, I seriously considered performing an exorcism—wondering if some evil spirit had possessed him."

Anastasia shuddered at the memory of that day.
