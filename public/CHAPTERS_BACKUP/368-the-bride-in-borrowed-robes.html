Chapter 368

The Bride in
Borrowed Robes

"You certainly look happier than before."

Dea. I swore I would stand beside you.

But then the Emperor had died.

Circumstance had left Cesare no choice but to inherit Katzen's throne. The chaos that followed—the enemies circling at every turn—and through it all, Medea had been forced to bear the burden alone.

The knowledge was unbearable.

I promised I would protect her.

Had he not dared to claim her side, believing himself the only one capable of shielding her completely?

Yet that oracle had betrayed them so cruelly. Cesare could no longer contain his fury—at the world, at fate, at himself.

If this is how it ends... then nothing has changed.

Memory overlaid the present—the image of a young Valdinian princess enduring the Queen Mother's punishment alone in the rain, abandoned by all who should have protected her.

"Damn it... for something as pathetic as divine punishment..."

Why had he not endured it? Even if it had cost him his life on the spot, he should never have succumbed.

The north wind cut across his sculpted features.

That he had lost consciousness while fighting the punishment to the very end—that he had refused the oracle with his last breath—offered him no solace now. It only proved his own weakness.

His grip on the reins tightened until the leather groaned in protest.

Why does the Goddess...?

The flames of his rage spread to the source of the divine punishment itself.

Why do You push her to such extremes?

Even when he had been dying from the Curse of the Beginning, Cesare had never blamed the divine. He neither believed in nor relied upon any god.

When the Holy Kingdom had spoken of Medea as a Saintess—as the Goddess's chosen agent—he had simply thought it fitting. Compensation, perhaps, for the wounds she had suffered in her previous life.

But now, for the first time, fury toward the heavens welled up from the depths of his heart.

You called her Your child.

If she is truly the daughter of Venus, Your representative on earth—

Then why do You permit frauds and falsehoods to flourish? Why do You use Your own name to drive us apart?

An oracle commanding her husband to take another wife.

Forcing Medea to relive the most agonizing nightmare of her past life—pushing her once more to the precipice.

Cesare's teeth ground together. Perhaps his blasphemous thoughts had drawn divine attention, for a crimson glow flickered across the back of his hand where it gripped the reins, leaving a fresh scar.

"Hah!"

A cloud of white dust erupted behind the black horse as he drove his heels into its flanks once more.

✦ ✦ ✦

Dong... Dong...

At that same hour, within Katzen's cathedral, the ceremonial bells tolled to herald the coronation.

The nobles of Katzen claimed their seats first. The royal family—the Fourth Princess among them—stood at the fore, with the aristocracy arrayed behind. Each face was a mask of suppressed anxiety, tension, and veiled anticipation as they studied one another's expressions.

Shortly thereafter, the foreign delegations made their entrance.

The cathedral's somber grandeur was enriched by costumes representing the patterns and origins of a dozen nations.

"How long has it been since we last met in such circumstances? The Imperial Council at the Central Temple, was it not?"

"Ah, I was actually in Katzen quite recently—the late Emperor's birthday banquet. I never imagined it would all end so... abruptly."

The envoy from a minor kingdom clicked his tongue.

"But setting aside the coronation itself—is it not strange that another oracle has emerged so soon?"

A cautious question, voiced as the conversation deepened.

"It has been scarcely a year since the first prophecy was delivered. Has there ever been a time in continental history when two oracles followed so closely upon one another?"

"Perhaps because they are connected? The first oracle named the Princess of Valdina as the bride of evil incarnate. The second names her husband as the Saintess's destined consort."

"Then does the second not contradict the first?"

Another envoy tilted his head, troubled.

As a devout believer, the inconsistency was difficult to accept.

"Two oracles, when obtaining even one is nearly impossible? How can such a thing be?"

The speaker noticed attention shifting toward their conversation and hastily lowered his voice.

"If it is the will of the Goddess, what choice do we have? We can only observe for now."

Eyes drifted toward the Valdinian delegation.

The young Emperor of Valdina—known until now only through fearsome rumors—sat flanked by knights whose savage reputations preceded them. The tension in the cathedral thickened palpably.

"Still... I cannot shake this unease. If this oracle is truly as the scriptures describe, why does it fill me with such dread?"

"Hush! Do you wish to be excommunicated for such blasphemy reaching the Holy Kingdom's ears? What power does a minor nation like ours possess? We must follow the current, not swim against it."

A sudden intake of breath.

"Ah—there! Look!"

A cream-colored carriage drew to a halt before the cathedral's great doors.

"The Holy Kingdom has arrived! The Lady Rachel has come!"

Conversation ceased.

The first to descend was Pope Paul, resplendent in priestly vestments far more ornate than customary. He drew every eye before turning to extend his hand toward the carriage.

First came the hem of a soft ivory gown. Then, emerging into the light, a golden-haired woman.

"Your Eminence Paul. Lady Rachel."

The one who greeted them was Marquis de Leniard himself.

That he should personally attend to their protocol at a coronation witnessed by dignitaries from across the continent was a clear signal: he recognized Rachel's status absolutely.

More than that—it was an open declaration that he, the pillar of the First Prince's faction, had abandoned the First Prince's wife and aligned himself with this new power.

The faces of the Holy Kingdom delegation brightened visibly.

"Please, allow me to escort you inside. As I mentioned, I must apologize once more that His Highness the First Prince was unable to attend the ceremony."

"Your Excellency is too gracious. We shall soon be family, after all. If we cannot extend such consideration to one another, even the Goddess would reproach us."

Paul—or rather, the warlock masquerading as Paul—was delighted.

If I had been forced to face that infamous conqueror who annihilated my brethren, concealing my anxiety through today's grand undertaking would have been... challenging.

Far better to conduct this ceremony with the groom's seat empty than to confront someone possessed of such beastly instincts.

Once the oracle is fulfilled, none of it will matter.

"If our Saintess's ascension to the throne is assured, what difficulties could possibly remain?"

Let the First Prince wake in fury—by then, everything would be irreversibly decided.

"Indeed. That much is certain, Your Eminence. You may rest assured."

"Ha! I only wish I had a chisel."

The implication was unmistakable: he wished to scrape away Medea's name, already carved into the Oath Stone.

Marquis Leniard's expression hardened—but only for an instant. The Holy Kingdom's delegation noticed nothing.

"Worry not. Once the Saintess's name is inscribed upon the Oath Stone, all will know who the true consort is."

"As expected from a great patriot! You speak with perfect understanding. Please—this way!"

Paul strode briskly toward the cathedral's interior.

The moment Rachel appeared, she commanded universal attention.

"My lady, I am the Duke of Nova from Suses. We met once before, at Valdina's Imperial Proclamation Ceremony."

Nobles rushed to greet her, vying to catch her eye. After her prolonged absence, she had returned as the sole representative of the Holy Kingdom—and as the woman who would soon become Katzen's next Empress.

"Of course, Your Grace. Thank you for attending. May the Goddess grant peace to you and your house."

Rachel responded to each supplicant with a voice sweet as honeyed wine.

Her bearing was a peculiar blend of arrogance and serenity—as though she were already the mistress of this place.

Then her gaze fell upon a figure ahead, and her smile deepened.

The handsome man with the pleasant expression wore a crimson tunic. Upon his breast, a metal brooch bore the pattern of the Katzen imperial family.

"Grand Duke Castullo. It has been too long."

"I am relieved to see the Saintess has arrived safely."

Jason inclined his head.

For a moment, the two exchanged a glance weighted with unspoken understanding.

"It is thanks to the Grand Duke's kindness. The Goddess teaches that we must always repay those who show us favor."

Rachel's smile turned meaningful.

She knew of Jason's obsession with Medea. These words were her covenant—a promise to the co-conspirator who had joined her in this grand design.

"As the Goddess's representative, I can assure you: the day approaches when Your Highness will achieve your heart's desire."

A promise to deliver Medea into his hands.

"I shall do everything in my power to assist. It will be simpler once you are acknowledged as a member of the royal family."

Pride and arrogance dripped from every syllable.

"...Saintess."

His crimson eyes studied her with unsettling intensity.

They traced the Katzen imperial crest embroidered upon her beige robe. The elaborate tiara crowning her golden hair—set with jewels of five colors, far too ostentatious for any religious ceremony. The diamond-studded veil draped over her shoulders, its opulence verging on vulgarity.

From head to toe, she was dressed as a bride.

A bride who seemed, somehow, slightly... frivolous.

"Grand Duke? Why do you stare so? Is something amiss with my appearance?"

Rachel, sensing the scattered glances around her, asked with a note of irritation.

The corners of Jason's mouth curved—something that might have been a smile, though it reached nowhere near his eyes.

Contempt flickered through his crimson gaze. And beneath it, something almost like regret.

"It seems that with a change of circumstances comes a change of countenance. Perhaps... this is the Saintess's true face."

"What?"

"You certainly look happier than before."

"Before? What time are you referring to?"

Rachel's confusion was evident, but Jason had already withdrawn into his own thoughts, his attention drifting elsewhere.

"Saintess, please proceed to your seat."

Paul approached and extended his hand. Rachel accepted it, allowing herself to be led forward once more.

Hmph. That brooding fool came all this way just to spout nonsense.

A faint smile lingered in her eyes as she glanced toward where Jason sat.

"Ah—! Over there—!"

And then, at last, the First Princess made her entrance.

The cathedral fell silent.

"..."

Every voice stilled. Every eye turned.
