Chapter 152

The Sword That
Kneels

"Your enemy is my enemy. Your dream is my desire."

A faint smile curved across the mercenary's lips at Medea's words.

What could possibly be amusing? A flicker of irritation stirred within her. The man before her seemed to be mocking her—though she couldn't fathom why.

"I hope you remain exactly as you are, Princess of Valdina."

The mercenary inclined his head in a bow.

As he leaned closer, his shadow fell across her—vast and encompassing, as though it might swallow her whole and hide her from the world.

Their eyes met. Within the gold of his gaze, deep and nameless emotions swirled like currents beneath still water, then gradually subsided.

He reached out.

A strand of pale silver hair caught between his fingers, winding around them like silk thread.

"It has been... entertaining."

Her lips, red as winter berries, curved with amusement. When she spoke, her voice was soft as velvet.

"That sounds rather like a grand farewell. Are you departing for Katzen?"

The mercenary merely lifted the corner of his mouth. He offered no proper answer.

Medea gave a slight nod, as though she understood regardless.

Whatever his reasons, it is no bad thing that Facade is leaving Valdina.

The time has come to send them away. If not now, it will only grow more difficult.

Claudio, who had threatened the throne, had departed. Her brother, the King, had returned. If two predators were left in the same cage, they would inevitably turn on one another.

"Acares, if you intend to involve yourself in the struggle for the throne, choose your patron wisely."

Cesare paused at her words.

"If it were my decision, I would take the hand of the First Prince. Rather than someone like Grand Duke Castullo."

She recalled their conversation from that day. But time had passed, and circumstances had shifted.

"You still speak of the First Prince?"

There was a sardonic edge to his words, though something else lurked beneath—something more poignant than mockery.

"Princess, I regret to inform you, but he will die soon. He will perish before any plan can come to fruition. Therefore—"

"And if he survives?"

Medea's voice cut through his.

Cesare turned his head, fixing her with a stare. The languid ease that usually softened his features had vanished. In its place, a cold intensity rose in those settled golden eyes.

"That will not happen."

He spoke slowly, each word deliberate, leaving no room for doubt.

"Some beliefs must be shattered before they reveal their true worth. The First Prince of Katzen is dead."

Cesare Dwisler-Katzen dies.

He had finally admitted it—that he saw no path forward.

"There is no other hope. This is the end."

Gone was the man who had sworn to struggle until his final breath. In his place stood someone prepared to accept death with quiet resignation.

And it was here, before Medea, that he made this admission. The irony was not lost on him—bitter and sad in equal measure.

Of all people, I confess my end to the one person I least wished to show it to.

Medea, who could not have known the depths of Cesare's despair, regarded him with luminous green eyes.

Her voice rang clear, utterly unmoved by his resignation.

"Well. That remains to be seen."

With a soft creak, she opened the drawer beside her.

It was almost absurd—the casual way she spoke, as though she had only just remembered, when in truth she had prepared this from the very beginning.

A small velvet box appeared before Cesare's eyes.

"...What is this?"

Green eyes answered him silently: Open it.

Click.

The lid lifted.

His fingers froze. A violent tremor passed through his golden pupils.

Silence descended upon the chamber.

"...This is..."

"You recognize it, don't you, Acares?"

Nestled within the velvet lay a cylinder of pale blue crystal, radiant as captured starlight.

"The Dawn's Goblet. What your Facade has been seeking."

Cesare said nothing.

"It belongs neither to you nor to the Tower. It is ours—Valdina's."

Medea's voice was calm, matter-of-fact.

Cesare lifted the vial with trembling hands. Even as the cool glass touched his skin, it felt unreal.

The Dawn's Goblet... truly before my eyes. Truly within my grasp.

As if mocking him, the silver liquid within shifted and swirled.

The light it cast was dazzling—like stars suspended in liquid form.

The counterfeit he had discovered in the King's Tower was nothing compared to this.

Understanding dawned. Slowly, he turned to face Medea.

"So it was you, Princess. You were the one who planted the fake."

"Surely you don't intend to be angry? I've been remarkably tolerant, allowing you to ransack a foreign nation's palace. Consider it a courtesy born of... affection."

Her expression remained noble, her bearing unshaken—a silent rebuke to Facade's shamelessness.

"How could I possibly be angry?"

Cesare forced the words out, his throat so tight he feared some strange sound might escape.

"Acares, deliver this to the First Prince. Consider it a gift from Valdina."

A pause.

"Furthermore, let this serve as my gesture of favor toward your Facade. Do not forget it."

The messenger she had chosen was not the Tower, but Facade.

Her calm voice, carrying an unspoken command to remember, pulled Cesare back from the distant reaches of his thoughts.

"Princess, do you truly intend to save the First Prince?"

"Yes."

"Why?"

Medea turned to face him fully. A cool, impassive air settled over her delicate features.

"I want him to survive."

I want him to shake the empire while I still draw breath. I want the Emperor and Jason to be swept away by that upheaval—without any aid from me.

The First Prince is a predator. A dominant force that disrupts the natural order.

If he, who perished early in my previous life, does not die this time—how will the empire change? How will the very continent be transformed?

Cesare's eyes gleamed at her words.

"Very well. I will tell him."

Medea did not notice how hoarse his voice had become, how it cracked at the edges.

"Good, Acares. I trust you will not betray my faith."

Entrusting the Dawn's Goblet to Facade was a tremendous gamble.

At her words, the mercenary smiled once more—and then, to her astonishment, he knelt before her.

A cool, steady hand reached out and took hers.

As his dark hair brushed against her skin, Medea felt a searing heat—like a brand being pressed into flesh.

Cesare bent low and pressed his lips to the back of her hand.

"In the name of Facade, I vow to declare that Valdina's Star saved the First Prince."

Medea found herself momentarily speechless.

The reverence that radiated from his composed posture, the self-imposed submission in his bearing—it was unlike anything she had witnessed from him.

He kneels. He bows his head.

Since her first encounter with Acares, the leader of Facade, she had never imagined such a sight.

Was this not the same man whose arrogance bordered on shamelessness, regardless of rank or station?

"And I..."

A rich, resonant voice filled her ears, as though the oath was not yet complete.

"Medea, I will become your greatest sword."

Silent Vow

The Princess could not possibly understand the joy that now flooded through Cesare.

It had not been his choice—this man who had fought so fiercely against the curse of his blood, who had defied fate itself—to finally accept everything.

Harsh reality had forced his hand.

Cesare, too, was merely human. He could not escape the shackles of destiny.

But the Princess before him had shattered those chains.

Like the sun rising beyond the horizon, she had driven back the darkness at the edge of death and pulled him from the abyss.

Medea de Valdina.

Just as a newly hatched bird imprints upon the first creature it sees, so too did Cesare's golden eyes fix upon her.

From the fragile silver of her hair—so delicate it seemed it might break—to the unwavering resolve in her gaze, everything was carved into him.

I will likely spend the rest of my life devoted to this woman.

The certainty of it settled into his mind like prophecy.

"There is a falling star—a great one, shrouded in darkness. That star will cut down the chaos blocking your path."

The prophecy of the blind old woman—words he had once dismissed without understanding—had come true at last.

In that moment, joy and rapture surged through Cesare's heart.

Instinct guided him more than reason.

He took her hands and drew them around his own neck—as though she were fastening an invisible collar, a leash only she could hold.

"As the Princess wishes."

Your enemy is my enemy. Your dream is my desire. I will be your only and eternal blade.

With that silent oath resonating through every fiber of his being, Cesare lowered his head.

He pressed his lips to her palm—a seal, a brand, a promise.

The force of it, the searing heat, traveled through Medea's skin and into her very bones.

Her green eyes—eyes that rarely wavered—shook violently.

"What manner of nonsense is this—"

She tried to wrench her hand free, but his grip held firm, unyielding.

She could feel his pulse through their joined skin—steady, insistent, alive.

Just as she prepared to kick him squarely in the chest, the pressure vanished.

"Acares!"

"Until we meet again, Medea."

His voice was slow, deep, resonant—lingering in her ears like the final note of a requiem.

He had already seized the vial and leapt through the window.

The curtains billowed in his wake—the only trace of his fleeting presence.

"Insufferable... Selfish to the very end."

Medea pressed a hand to her chest with a frown.

Her heart was pounding so fiercely she feared it might be audible from outside the room.

Her fingers, which had risen unconsciously to her flushed cheek, trembled. His warmth seemed to linger still—phantom heat that would not fade.

The weight of his grip on my wrist. The brush of his hair against my hand. Those clear golden eyes gazing up at me as he knelt.

The image of a beast baring its fangs—and yet submitting, meekly, to my grasp.

The sensation was strange. Bewildering. Entirely new.

And for a moment, Medea found herself caught in it, helpless.

She exhaled slowly, as though a storm had swept through her and finally passed.

Haa.

She drew a deep breath and forced composure back into place.

The heat in her eyes cooled. A familiar chill settled over her once more.

With this, the First Prince of Katzen will be saved.

Regardless of their leader's theatrical antics, Facade would carry out its mission faithfully.

A future in which the First Prince—that ill-fated prince who had been nothing but a casualty in her previous life—survived... it was a future that had never existed before.

Even Medea herself could not say with certainty whether this choice would prove beneficial or disastrous.

Perhaps I have released something even I cannot control.

"It matters not."

What Medea desired in this life was neither an empire nor victory.

She wanted only the complete and utter destruction of her enemies.

She did not care whom her mad dog tore apart—so long as it could shake the empire to its foundations.

Medea lifted her gaze to the sky.

Darkness had fallen, spreading across the world like spilled ink.

Against that void, the silver stars burned all the brighter.

"Jason. Rachel."

In the darkness of night, the names of her enemies were carried away on the cold wind.

"Be patient. Your turn comes soon."

Medea's revenge had only just begun.

End of Part One
