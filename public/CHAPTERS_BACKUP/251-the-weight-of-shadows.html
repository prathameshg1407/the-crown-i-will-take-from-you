Chapter 251

The Weight of Shadows

In the depths of the forest, even the simplest meal can crack a carefully maintained facade.

"Sir Neril, do you happen to have any pepper?"

Gallo's voice broke the oppressive silence that had settled over dinner like a shroud. He was the first to speak all evening, his words cutting through the damp stillness of their forest retreat with an almost violent casualness.

The heavy atmosphere seemed too thick even for the moisture-laden air to disperse.

"Just eat what you're given."

Neril's tone was clipped, final.

"Brother, I'd love to, truly—but by all the gods, this stew tastes like I'm eating mud straight from the riverbed."

Or perhaps it wasn't the atmosphere at all. Perhaps they simply couldn't endure the stew's punishing blandness any longer.

"Actually, I was wondering..."

"Could we perhaps add just a pinch of salt...?"

Like a dam finally bursting, the others began to voice their complaints. One after another, increasingly harsh critiques of the stew poured forth, and at last the pressure lifted.

"Ask Medea."

The group froze once more at Cesare's casual pronouncement.

"Our precious Princess always thinks to bring the essentials."

He was referring to the time she'd hidden alone in the mountains, carefully preparing to overturn Samon Claudio's elaborate trap. His eyes glinted with playful mischief as he looked up, his face half-shadowed by the dying light. The dangerous handsomeness of his features was only accentuated by the dimness.

It was his usual pattern—deflecting with charm and barbed compliments.

He knew full well that Medea understood these woods inside and out. Yet she didn't respond to his provocation.

Even Neril, who practically worshipped her like a deity, looked shaken by the tension. But Cesare alone remained utterly at ease.

After a long, weighted silence, Medea's expression turned grave. Without a word, she reached into her coat and produced a small shaker of salt and another of pepper.

"Your Highness, if you could just pass me—"

"No."

I'll need to reconsider this arrangement.

The icy atmosphere shattered instantly when Medea intercepted Gallo's outstretched hand and snatched the pepper shaker away from his reach.

"Wait—do you truly despise me that much?"

Gallo's shocked expression drew reluctant laughter from everyone present, breaking the unbearable tension at last.

"Thank you for today, Your Highness. If not for you, I'd have been turned into a pincushion by that centipede."

The day's awkwardness and confusion were thus relegated to memory.

After all, they were members of the same expedition, bound together until its conclusion.

Whatever her reasons for being here, it was undeniable that Medea had held her own in this forest without a single misstep or complaint.

Understanding passed between them with subtle nods. Medea's gaze shifted to Cesare, who merely lifted the corners of his mouth in that infuriatingly subtle smile.

"Neril, this is... humanly speaking, this is far too bland. I should've wrestled that ladle out of your stubborn hands."

Tom, who had barely managed to finish his portion, grumbled openly at their stoic companion.

"Wait—this was made by Sir Neril?"

Gallo, who had been gleefully sprinkling pepper over his bowl, suddenly froze. Then he began shoveling the stew into his mouth with exaggerated enthusiasm, speaking not another word.

"Somehow... I sensed a kind of pure-hearted simplicity in the plainness. A certain innocent quality."

"Gallo, what happened to your earlier declaration that whoever made this clearly had no sense of taste whatsoever?"

"I... I thought Tom made it..."

Gallo's voice was small, almost pitiful.

"Just eat what you're given. Before I decide to shove actual grass down your throat."

Despite the threat, Neril paused thoughtfully before tossing something toward Gallo.

"Beef jerky?"

"What?! You had meat this whole time and didn't share?! Neril, isn't this going a bit too far, even for someone with your bloodless, ascetic disposition?!"

Gallo's face lit up regardless, and he began attacking his stew with renewed vigor.

After the meal, a rare moment of leisure settled over them—time to shake off the lingering remnants of the day's trials.

"Time for a drink! Did you think I'd embark on an expedition like this without bringing along a five-hundred-year-old honey liquor?"

Gallo triumphantly produced a small wooden barrel from within his pack.

"Tsk. So that's why you were moving so gingerly ever since we disembarked..."

"So—does that mean you won't be drinking, Tom?"

"Absolutely not. I have nothing but admiration for Gallo's foresight. Gods, just smell that oak aging!"

"The aroma is magnificent, isn't it? Come on, Your Highness—you should have some too!"

Gallo, emboldened by Tom's enthusiastic response, offered Medea a glass as well.

Medea hesitated. The liquid in the glass was a peculiar baby-blue color, thick and almost syrupy in appearance.

"Gallo. Use your head."

Before she could accept, a low voice cut through the moment. A hand—swift and decisive—intercepted the glass, snatching it away from Medea's reach.

Cesare handed her a different vessel: a small water bottle.

"Drink this instead."

The liquid inside was as clear as spring water.

"It's sap from the World Tree. It's an adaptogen—completely safe. Don't worry, just drink it."

World Tree sap?

Medea had heard of its legendary rarity, but she'd never actually consumed it before. Without hesitation, she took a sip.

The scent was oddly familiar. Slightly sweet, subtle, with a peculiar undertone...

"This... Neril, doesn't this smell exactly like that juice you've been making?"

Neril glanced at Cesare for a brief moment before nodding reluctantly.

"Yes, Your Highness. Cesare has been adding it to every meal you've taken. He was concerned that if you departed on an expedition so suddenly, your body wouldn't be able to withstand the strain."

So that was it.

She hadn't been nearly as well-prepared as she'd been in her previous life, yet somehow her body had managed the journey with surprising ease. She'd wondered if perhaps her changed circumstances had improved her constitution, but it had been the World Tree sap all along.

"You don't need to thank me. I'm simply ensuring you do what I require of you."

Terence interjected before she could speak.

Medea looked at Cesare, who merely shrugged with that infuriating nonchalance. She nodded once and took another sip.

The sap in Cesare's bottle was noticeably sweeter than what Neril had been mixing into her meals.

Somehow, inexplicably, that small detail brought her comfort.

The warmth of the campfire, the gentle murmur of her companions' voices washing over her like a familiar tide—it was all strangely pleasant.

She'd been pretending to be fine, but perhaps she'd been more on edge than she'd realized.

The way people always are when they're harboring secrets they can't afford to have exposed.

In truth, she wanted to laugh at the absurdity of it all.

This was her second life, and yet she'd never expected it would unfold like this.

Advancing through the forest together, united in purpose, marching toward the lair of an evil dragon.

"I... never thought it would be enjoyable..."

Her head drooped forward as the words slipped out, barely audible.

"Your Highness!"

"Is she all right?"

Before Neril could even reach her, Cesare was already there. His hands moved with practiced efficiency, examining Medea with focused concern.

Her eyes had closed. Her cheeks were flushed a delicate, rosy red.

"She's not injured. I believe she's simply intoxicated."

Terence completed his examination, and Gallo's eyes suddenly widened in realization as he stared at his commander.

"Boss... you didn't give it to her straight, did you?"

"I did."

"Did you not know? World Tree sap—if you consume it undiluted like that, it has the same effect as strong spirits!"

Cesare's brow furrowed slightly.

"I wasn't aware."

"Of course you weren't. You're practically immune to everything. If it's not a curse, it barely registers, right? You can't measure ordinary people by your own monstrous standards, Boss."

"I'll take Her Highness to her tent."

As though he couldn't hear Neril's immediate protest, Cesare reached out and gently squeezed Medea's cheek. It was so small, so delicate—it fit entirely within his palm.

'Succumbing to mere World Tree sap...'

His golden eyes held an odd mixture of disapproval and something that might have been fondness.

"Lead the way."

Cesare wrapped his outer cloak around Medea's small frame, enveloping her completely. With movements both careful and respectful, he lifted her into his arms and stood.

Neril moved as if to intervene, but Cesare's bearing was so cautious, his handling so gentle and proper, that she could find no legitimate reason to object.

"This is all she has to sleep on?"

Cesare's frown deepened as he surveyed Medea's sleeping arrangements—a simple field cot with a single rust-colored blanket.

He understood better than anyone that luxury was impossible during constant travel and movement through hostile terrain.

But when it came to Medea, both the threadbare cot and the thin, worn blanket seemed unacceptably inadequate.

"Bring the Sylvarand from my personal pack. You know where Alpha is stored."

Neril froze mid-step.

The Sylvarand? The enchanted blanket woven from thread spun by the elves themselves?

The elves' mysterious magic imbued it with warmth and soothing properties. It was said to grant the user clear, peaceful dreams—something even Kensington's wealthiest nobles coveted but could rarely obtain.

"The difference in resources is staggering..."

Valdina had made considerable progress in recent years, but they were still nowhere near matching Katzen's accumulated wealth.

"...Yes, my lord."

Neril departed to retrieve the blanket, and Cesare began to rise—but at that precise moment, a single tear traced its way down Medea's cheek and caught his attention completely.

"I never thought... it would be enjoyable..."

The words were barely audible, mumbled in her intoxicated sleep.

"What burdens are you carrying that weigh so heavily?"

Instead of his usual rough, calloused touch, Cesare used the soft back of his hand to wipe away the moisture trailing down her face.

Pale moonlight filtered through the open tent flap, casting everything in shades of silver and shadow.

For a long moment, he simply watched her—this Princess who held herself together so carefully during the day, only to fracture in the vulnerability of sleep.

'What secrets are you hiding, Medea of Valdina?'

The question hung in the air, unanswered, as the night deepened around them.
