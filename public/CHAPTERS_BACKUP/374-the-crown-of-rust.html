Chapter 374

The Crown
of Rust

"All crowns fade when the hands that forged them withdraw their grace."

"Di—divinity..."

The High Priest of Katzen, who had witnessed everything from a corner of the altar, suddenly rose to his feet.

It was a scene pulled directly from the sacred texts—when the goddess herself had driven back the primordial darkness at the dawn of creation.

"It is the power of the Goddess! The true Saintess defeats the darkness on behalf of the divine!"

He pressed his forehead to the shattered ground and prostrated himself.

Even without the High Priest's proclamation, everyone understood.

A true Saintess had finally appeared on this earth.

Every soul present had witnessed the goddess's power flowing through Medea.

The pure white light cascading like divine rainfall—the black vortex consuming the sky—torn to shreds by radiant spears of judgment.

From Rachel's downfall to the manifestation of forbidden magic, and now the Saintess fully revealed—Jason suddenly grasped the entire tapestry of events.

"There is something in this world that can never be turned back: time."

"So, Jason, now you must live your time as well."

"Did you know what I was going to do? Or did you deliberately drag me into this trap?"

This grand scheme he'd prepared by sacrificing everything—it had been playing into Medea's hands from the very beginning.

Is that why you stopped me with such utter contempt?

Instead of answering, Medea raised her sword.

Step by step, she advanced toward him.

"Jason, have you ever thought about the last moments of Lian and Leah?"

Silence.

Jason froze as though struck by lightning.

Medea smiled coldly at the sight.

"You—who can't even remember their final moments—dare to speak of turning back time?"

Medea stared directly into his crimson eyes.

Jason unconsciously retreated a step at the murderous intent blazing in her emerald gaze.

"Even if you turned back time a hundred, a thousand times, your original sin cannot be erased, Jason."

Her voice was dangerously low.

"How could I ever coexist with the enemy who murdered my children and destroyed everything I held dear?"

In the same breath, Medea raised her sword and drove it through Jason's chest.

The motion carried no hesitation—as though she had been waiting for this moment across lifetimes.

Jason's eyes widened in shock and despair.

He stared at her with an expression of utter disbelief.

Judgment

"Even if I were given dozens or hundreds of lives—"

Thrust.

"—the answer would be the same."

"M-Medea... How... how could you do this?"

Profound betrayal flashed across Jason's eyes.

"I was once your lover, your husband—the one you loved with all your heart!"

Betrayal. How ironic.

Despite everything he had done, Jason still believed with absolute certainty that she would never take his life.

"Don't you have any memory of the countless hardships we endured together? Not even a shred of gratitude for those long years? How can you be so cruel to me?!"

Jason gripped the blade with trembling hands.

Drip. Drip.

Hot blood flowed between his fingers.

But despite his desperate words, Medea simply looked down at him with cold calm.

"I'm glad you finally get to experience the same emotions I felt."

A frigid smile curved her lips.

"Jason, let us put an end to this wretched cycle."

"Ah...!"

His legs gave out. His body staggered.

At that same moment—

BOOM!

The black magic circle erupted with a thunderous roar.

Having lost its caster, the ritual spun out of control and turned feral.

CRACK—!

The floor split apart. Fissures yawned wide.

The rifts greedily devoured the monsters. Ear-splitting shrieks erupted from every direction.

People watched in stunned silence, overwhelmed by the deafening cacophony.

Amid the chaos, they were slow to recognize one particular scream—a man's voice, echoing like a death knell.

"AHHHHHHHHH!"

It was Jason.

"It burns—it burns!"

He writhed in agony as though red-hot iron were carving into his flesh.

Like Rachel before him, Jason's limbs were being branded. Even his forehead.

[ ONE WHO ABANDONS WIFE AND CHILDREN ]

People were momentarily confused.

When had Castullo ever married?

"AAAHHHHH!"

A desperate, guttural scream tore from deep within Jason's throat.

The divine punishment for attempting to shatter the Golden Rule was more brutal than any inflicted before.

Not a single drop of blood flowed where the brands seared his flesh, yet the pain—as though his very bones and sinews were being torn asunder—ravaged his body.

"Me-Medea...!"

Even amid torment that turned his vision white, Jason searched for only one person.

He no longer cared whether he survived this ordeal.

Now, all that mattered to Jason was—

"Look—this is the crown you've been longing for. Here..."

He wrenched the crown from his head with trembling hands.

So intense was Jason's obsession that even after the black magic shattered, the crown remained tangible in his grasp.

"Look. It's still here. It hasn't vanished. So there's still hope... This won't all come to nothing..."

The hand bearing the divine brand shook violently as he held out the crown to Medea.

"You wanted it. You wanted this."

A pathetic mixture of tears and pleading.

"I'll give you everything, so please..."

Silence.

"Dea. I only need you. The crown, the power—this time I'll give it all to you."

Still not enough?

Still... unforgivable?

Jason's lips trembled.

Medea shook her head slowly.

"I've already taken your crown."

"What... what are you talking about...?"

She raised her hand, palm open.

"It's in my hand. Like this. Right here."

The pain digging into his very marrow had already given him the answer, but Jason tried desperately to ignore it.

The Crown Decays

"Look carefully, Jason."

A cold, unwavering voice dragged him back to reality.

Jason lowered his head.

Then he looked at the crown clutched in his trembling hand.

The shining crown was slowly fading away.

"This is..."

The luster had long since vanished. Rust had begun to creep across its surface. Paint peeled away in ugly patches, revealing the corroded metal beneath.

"Ah..."

A self-deprecating smile twisted his lips.

"Ah... I see. You were right."

Jason bowed his head.

"It was never mine to begin with."

He understood at last.

All he'd ever held was faded glory—a crown he could never have grasped without Medea's hand lifting it for him.

His hand, clutching the rusted iron, trembled violently.

"But still... if only—"

Jason foolishly thrust the crown forward once more.

But at that very moment—

"Like HELL you will!"

A vicious arm shot out from behind him.

It was Rachel.

The magic circle had been forcibly shattered, and she—swallowed as the first sacrifice—had been violently expelled.

But it was a horrific sight. A vast portion of her life force had been drained by the ritual.

Darkness had already encroached upon her face. If not for her distinctive purple eyes and golden hair, it would have been impossible to recognize her as Rachel.

"You think you'll stab me in the back and live in comfort?! I won't allow it!"

Snap!

She lunged at Jason with bloodshot eyes and sank her teeth into his neck.

"Get—get off!"

"This crown is mine! No one—no one—can take anything from me!"

She tried to wrench the crown from his grasp.

"Even if I die, I'll never die alone! AAAHHH!"

Jason shoved her away with all his remaining strength.

Crash!

The recoil from the struggle sent his body careening into the pillar of the goddess statue.

"Hah... hah... hah..."

Jason clutched his bleeding neck and watched as the torn rift greedily swallowed Rachel whole.

At the same moment, he saw them.

Two small figures swaying beyond the distant horizon.

Walking toward him, their hands clasped tightly together.

"Li... Lian? Leah?"

His original sin.

"Ah...!"

Jason's hands began to tremble in the air. Primal terror flooded his crimson eyes.

"No—don't come closer... I was wrong..."

His face aged in an instant, as though decades had passed in mere moments.

The light vanished from his eyes. He repeated the same words like a broken prayer.

"Forgive me... please... forgive me..."

At that moment—

CRASH!

The goddess statue's head, broken by the earlier impact, fell and struck him.

Final Judgment

With its neck severed, Jason's broken body tumbled through the crack.

That was Jason Castullo's last moment in this life.
