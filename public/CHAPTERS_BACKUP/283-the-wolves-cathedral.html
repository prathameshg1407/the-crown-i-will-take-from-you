Chapter 283

The Wolves'
Cathedral

"In the halls of power, every smile conceals a blade—and every bow, a calculation."

"Why me?! Why am I being dragged there?! No! I've done nothing wrong! I was deceived! You were all deceived!"

A long, rending scream echoed through the dark prison corridors, swallowed by stone walls that had heard a thousand such cries before.

The Capital of Katzen — Persepolis

The crystalline sky soared high above the cathedral spire, brilliant and merciless in its clarity.

Below, a vast crowd surged through the streets leading to the cathedral, whose doors stood uncommonly wide—thrown open to receive the masses who had gathered to witness the coronation of the First Prince.

The nobility fared no differently. Every seat within the grand nave was occupied, the pews packed so tightly that latecomers were forced to stand along the walls.

One could only imagine what favors had been called in, what connections leveraged, simply to secure a place at this ceremony.

"Today, at last, we shall behold the famous Princess of Valdina."

A magnificently dressed noblewoman snapped open her peacock-feather fan with theatrical flourish.

"The oracle named her bride to the Evil Dragon, did it not? One can scarcely imagine how fearsome she must be, to have brought such a creature to ruin."

"And how insufferably arrogant she must have grown, with an Emperor for a brother who dotes upon her so shamelessly."

"Even so, Valdina has only recently begun to grow. It cannot possibly compare to our great Katzen. She will find herself quite out of her depth here."

The woman worked her fan with increasing vigor, the opalescent feathers swaying dramatically with each emphatic gesture.

"Besides, the Princess received no proper education in her youth. What does it matter if her brother adores her? If she cannot match the refinement of Katzen's court, she is nothing more than a well-dressed failure."

The noblewoman sniffed with evident satisfaction. This was Countess Delgano, sister-in-law to the Eastern Empress Dowager herself.

"With the tainted blood of the Claudios running through her veins, how could she be anything but common? Base nature cannot be hidden forever. Is that not so, Your Majesty?"

The assembled ladies turned their attention to the beautiful woman seated beside Countess Delgano.

The Eastern Empress Dowager of Katzen sat with impeccable posture, her bearing as regal as her title. Though she and the Countess were of similar age, the Empress Dowager appeared young enough to be her daughter.

"It remains my eternal regret that I allowed those vermin to live."

The low, venomous words seemed ill-suited to such an exquisite face.

"Naturally, Your Majesty. Even after you bestowed such generous favor upon that mother and daughter, did they not prove themselves utterly ungrateful? They fled to Valdina without so much as a word of thanks."

"Your Majesty's heart is too kind—soft as silk. Were I in your position, I would not rest content until their heads decorated the city gates."

Another noblewoman made a show of disgust.

The reference was clear to all present. Samon Claudio—a minor member of Valdina's extended royal family—had fled to Katzen years ago after wedding the Fourth Princess Angelique. In his desperate attempt to establish himself at court, he had adopted a strategy of craven submission to every Katzen noble who would tolerate him.

The Katzen aristocracy naturally assumed that the Princess of Valdina would prove no different.

At that moment, the Western Empress Dowager made her entrance.

"Your Majesty! What an honor!"

The noblewomen who had been clustered around the Eastern Empress Dowager scattered like startled birds, reforming in an eager swarm before the newcomer.

"On such an auspicious occasion, Your Majesty's presence elevates the grandeur of the entire coronation."

"It is no wonder His Highness the Third Prince carries himself with such dignity—he learned it from you, Your Majesty. To witness mother and son together is truly an honor beyond measure."

"Today's ceremony is for the First Prince and his bride. It would be unseemly to discuss my son."

The Western Empress Dowager's response was cold, clipped—and utterly indifferent to the sycophants surrounding her.

Without sparing them another glance, she proceeded to her seat.

The Eastern Empress Dowager, suddenly bereft of her audience, clutched her handkerchief in white-knuckled silence.

She had always been the more beautiful of the two. She had monopolized the Emperor's favor for years.

And yet these people could not scramble fast enough to ingratiate themselves with her rival.

The Eastern Empress Dowager understood the reason all too well.

Years have passed since Angelique was sent into exile, and still His Majesty refuses to recall her. The court has taken it as a sign.

In His Majesty's heart, the Fourth Princess has long since fallen from grace. And the First Prince...

The First Prince's reputation preceded him: cold-hearted, merciless, utterly without sentiment.

He did not hesitate to eliminate the very servants who raised him. Why would we be any different?

He was known to repay old grudges with interest, to eradicate his enemies root and branch.

He was not a man who could be won over by flattery. He would smile at their compliments—and then sever their hands for presuming to touch him.

His Majesty despises him too thoroughly to ever name him Crown Prince.

Which meant that among the Emperor's remaining children, the one most likely to inherit the throne was—

The Third Prince. The son of the Western Empress.

Moreover, rather than attaching themselves to the First Prince's faction—already formidable and in no need of additional allies—these nobles calculated that supporting the Third Prince's still-rising cause would earn them far greater returns.

It was the eternal instinct of those who pursued power: maximize advantage while minimizing risk.

And so the Katzen nobility had begun shifting their attention—and their loyalty—toward the Third Prince and his mother.

Why should we concern ourselves with an Empress Dowager who has no viable heir to place upon the throne?

No matter how deeply the Emperor favored the Eastern Empress, the crown would ultimately pass to a son of the Western Empress.

Given the Emperor's increasingly erratic behavior—his growing madness whispered about in every corridor—that day did not seem far off at all.

"Indeed, Your Majesty. One can see precisely where the Third Prince inherited his fair and just nature. Truly, you speak as befits the mother of a nation."

The flattery was shameless—all but declaring the Western Empress Dowager the true mistress of the imperial household, despite the Empress's seat standing empty.

A spark of fury flashed through the Eastern Empress's eyes, but none of the fawning ladies noticed.

Even if they had, they would not have cared. What was the displeasure of a declining power compared to the favor of a rising one?

They have no shame whatsoever.

The Eastern Empress Dowager's expression hardened as she noted, once again, that the Western Empress's seat was positioned one tier higher than her own—directly beside the empty throne reserved for the Empress.

She gripped the hem of her gown until her fingers ached.

"Your Majesty, if the Fourth Princess were still here, would they dare treat you this way?"

Countess Delgano dabbed at her eyes. The magnificent peacock fan that had been flourished so proudly now hung limp and forgotten in her lap.

"If not for that woman, our poor Fourth Princess would never have been cast out."

The Eastern Empress Dowager's face flushed crimson.

She had caught the Western Empress watching her—and the cold amusement in that gaze was unmistakable.

As their eyes met, the Western Empress's lips curved upward. Just slightly. Without a single word.

She's laughing at me. She dares to laugh at me.

How long does she intend to act so arrogantly over a throne she hasn't even claimed yet?

The current ruler of this empire is not the son of that lowborn woman!

The more she dwelt upon it, the more violently her teeth ground together.

Poor Angelique! Those parasites clinging to her husband's name...

When she thought of her daughter struggling alone in exile, a burning sensation rose in her chest.

Even the vicious reports about the Claudio family's behavior could not fully satisfy her wounded pride.

She was consumed by rage and humiliation—she, who had once dominated both the imperial household and society itself, reduced to this.

"Your Majesty, you're perspiring terribly. Are you quite well?"

Countess Delgano hastened to press a handkerchief to the Empress Dowager's brow.

***

"She's here! She's here! Valdina has arrived!"

The clamor from the streets penetrated even the cathedral's thick walls. Several nobles, forgetting their dignity entirely, craned their necks toward the windows.

A magnificent carriage rolled into view.

Twin flags—Valdina and Katzen—flew from the poles mounted on either side. Behind it followed the formal procession of the Princess's retinue.

"The Agema!"

Dadadak. Dadadak. Dadadak.

The thunder of hooves striking cobblestones rolled through the streets like war drums.

The symbol of the Agema—a horse at full gallop—gleamed upon the lead knight's armor. The elite cavalry that followed wore deep blue cloaks that rippled like water in the wind.

They say the Agema brought the legendary plains tribes to their knees. Their presence is truly formidable.

The carriage they escorted was enormous—easily twice the size of any standard conveyance.

Its exterior had been gilded until it seemed carved from solid gold, adorned with inlays of lustrous white mother-of-pearl. The sweeping canopy was embroidered with pure gold thread, and the royal coat of arms of House Valdina sparkled like captured starlight whenever the breeze stirred the fabric.

The carriage was so extravagant, so breathtakingly refined, that it bordered on the majestic.

Every observer arrived at the same thought:

When did Valdina become so wealthy?

They say they've opened the straits and are now collecting tolls from every merchant fleet on the continent...

A small nation that had depended on Katzen's patronage for decades.

The citizens of Katzen stirred uneasily as they witnessed firsthand the transformation they had only heard rumors of until now.

"Can you see who's inside?"

"Not a glimpse. The windows are too dark. But we'll know soon enough."

The gathered crowds began raising their arms, straining on tiptoe for a better view.

The opaque windows, revealing nothing of the carriage's occupants, only heightened their curiosity.

The union of these two young royals—bound together by an oracle's decree—had already become the subject of endless speculation.

"Does the Princess of Valdina truly understand what she's gotten herself into? Does she know what kind of man the First Prince is?"

"How could she not? His reputation precedes him across the entire continent. The goddess must be cruel indeed. No matter how much power he commands, how could any woman bear to live with such a terrifying man? I certainly couldn't. Not for all the gold in Katzen."

"Hush! Watch your tongues—both of you."

At last, the carriage rolled to a halt before the cathedral steps.

Contrary to expectation, it was the First Prince himself who emerged first, pushing open the carriage door from within.

His black cloak swept around broad shoulders as he stepped into the light.

The citizens who had been watching felt their spines straighten involuntarily. An instinctive chill passed through them—the same sensation one might feel standing on a battlefield rather than before a house of worship.

Dear gods. Just as the rumors said.

People held their breath and lowered their gazes without conscious thought.

Inside the cathedral, the nobility reacted no differently.

Even those who had been eagerly flattering the Third Prince moments before now shrank back as the First Prince's presence filled the space.

They held their breath. They avoided his eyes. They looked for all the world like children caught in some forbidden act.

When his indifferent gaze swept across them, it felt as though invisible hands had closed around their throats.

And then—

"It's the Princess!"

A woman in a velvet ceremonial gown emerged from the carriage, the imperial emblem pinned at her shoulder.

Porcelain skin that seemed to glow translucent beneath the sunlight. Silver hair that flowed like liquid moonlight.

Even the magnificent diamond tiara crowning her head seemed to dim in comparison to its wearer.

Has there ever been a woman this beautiful?

Even those who had steeled themselves against being impressed found their mouths falling open in astonishment.

In that moment—as hundreds of eyes fixed upon Medea like sharpened blades—Cesare extended his hand.

His fingers moved with natural grace toward her shoulder.

With a gentle, practiced motion, he adjusted the brooch fastening her mantle, ensuring it lay perfectly in place.

The onlookers blinked.

Then blinked again.

Is the First Prince... attending to her?
