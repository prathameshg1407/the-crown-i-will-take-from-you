Chapter 228

The Untamed
Heart

"True nobility cannot be commanded—only recognized."

Every gaze in the Princess's palace turned toward the creature that had appeared in the courtyard.

"Neeeigh—!"

A white horse of breathtaking beauty stood before them, its glossy mane catching the sunlight like spun silver, rippling with each imperious toss of its head.

"...Arion?"

Medea's eyes fixed upon the diamond-shaped marking adorning the horse's forehead—a distinctive blaze she recognized from ancient illustrations.

"Your Highness recognizes him. Yes—he carries Arion's blood. A purebred descendant."

Beside Medea, Neril drew a sharp breath.

Arion. The legendary steed of the First Emperor of Katzen—said to have been born where the sea met the shore, blessed with supernatural endurance and speed. The horse that had carried its master tirelessly from one end of the vast empire to the other without rest.

"Oh, how beautiful..."

Saya reached toward the magnificent creature without thinking.

"Stop!"

Zeta's hand caught her wrist and yanked her backward.

"NEEEIGH—!"

Hooves slammed into the earth where Saya had stood a heartbeat before. The grass was crushed flat, deep crescents carved into the soil.

Had Zeta been a moment slower, she would have lost far more than her sleeve.

"He's far more dangerous than he appears. A truly vicious temperament."

Zeta scratched his temple, clearly uncomfortable.

"Plenty of handlers have tried to tame him—most gave up after being bitten or kicked. He's too sensitive, too volatile."

He glanced at the magnificent beast, which was currently pawing the ground and snorting with naked hostility.

"Several of our men were injured just transporting him from the Facade. I brought him on my lord's orders, but if Your Highness wishes... we can return him."

Even as he spoke, Zeta felt the absurdity of the situation pressing upon him.

Having observed Medea closely, he knew she would not be frightened by a mere horse—no matter how fierce. And yet...

What manner of gift is this? A beast no one can control?

He recalled the mercenary Gallo shaking his head in disbelief: "Is the boss truly trying to court the Princess? Who sends a mad horse as a present? What if she's trampled to death?"

"Did he leave no other message?"

"...He said only that Your Highness would understand when you saw it."

Zeta's bewildered expression was almost comical.

A faint smile curved Medea's lips as she studied the white horse.

Its mane blazed like captured starlight. Its bearing radiated arrogance and power. And she understood perfectly what Cesare had meant to convey.

Arion's bloodline—legendary, difficult to master, but once bonded, more powerful than any other mount in existence.

Much like the superficial honor Perdiccas II has bestowed upon me.

The message was clear: Use it as you see fit.

Moreover, by sending the descendant of the First Emperor's own steed, Cesare—a central figure in the imperial family—had publicly endorsed Medea's new status. Should his capricious father later attempt to revoke his offer, this gift would make such a reversal far more... complicated.

Though knowing Cesare, I suspect irritating his father was at least half the motivation.

Based on everything she knew of his personality, that assessment was probably generous.

"KRRRRR—!"

The white horse reared onto its hind legs, hooves slashing at the air, trying to strike the mercenary who held its reins.

Every unfamiliar sound—every voice, every movement—drew a warning snort from flared nostrils.

Such sensitivity to noise. What others read as aggression, I recognize as vigilance.

The ferocity Medea observed was not malice. It was caution—the wariness of a creature that had learned to trust nothing.

"Release the reins. Step away."

The mercenary hesitated, casting an uncertain glance toward Zeta.

But something in Medea's voice brooked no argument. Reluctantly, he obeyed.

Not all creatures Medea had encountered during her expeditions had been savage. Certain rare beings—solitary, skittish, magnificent—required a different approach entirely.

She thought of the unicorn she had glimpsed once, its silver mane gleaming in distant moonlight.

That one was curious. Perhaps you are too.

Slowly, Medea removed the silver bracelet from her wrist.

A small bell dangled from its end.

Chime—!

A crystalline note rang through the courtyard—pure, clean, achingly sweet.

The white horse froze mid-stride. Its ears, which had been pinned flat against its skull, suddenly pricked upward.

Chime—

The second ring seemed to invite response. The horse's restless hooves stilled.

"Your Highness—"

"Shhhh."

Medea raised one finger to her lips.

The onlookers fell silent, tension coiling through the air. Neril's face had gone pale—she remembered all too well the incident three years ago, when Birna's machinations had sent Medea tumbling from horseback.

The mercenary who had released the reins positioned himself to intervene, certain the beast would charge at any moment.

Medea shook the bell once more.

The white horse took a single step toward her. Its ears remained alert, focused entirely on the source of that entrancing sound.

Another step.

The distance between them shrank.

At last, the magnificent creature stood directly before Medea.

Great dark eyes gazed down at the silver-haired woman who seemed, to the horse's perception, to be fashioned from moonlight itself.

To this horse, humans had always been tiresome creatures—loud, demanding, utterly tedious.

But this one was different.

She did not grasp at him. She did not shout commands. She simply stood there, patient as starlight, offering something that sparkled and sang.

Curious in spite of himself, the white horse stretched its neck forward and nudged the bell with its velvet nose.

Medea reached out and stroked the horse's neck.

Her touch was gentle—not demanding, not fearful. It carried a warmth that felt almost like reunion with an old friend.

"You're curious too, aren't you?"

She whispered the words like a secret shared between equals.

Instead of biting, the horse pressed its cheek against her palm with unexpected affection.

Then it glanced at the bell and stamped one hoof impatiently, as though demanding she ring it again.

"You like this?"

Laughter—genuine, warm—escaped Medea's lips.

She unfastened the bells from her bracelet and attached them to the horse's bridle.

The horse took an experimental step. The bells chimed softly. Delighted, it pranced in a small circle, producing a cascade of silver sounds.

"Neeeigh—!"

A cry of pure joy.

The white mane caught the light like liquid silver. And now those great dark eyes were fixed not on the bells, but on Medea's face—studying her with fierce, unguarded curiosity.

Medea waited.

Silent. Still. Allowing the horse to make its own decision.

Moments stretched into an eternity—a taut, breathless standoff between two proud creatures.

Then the horse that had been gazing down at her with such haughty disdain lifted one foreleg, as though issuing a command.

Saddle me. If you think yourself worthy.

A quick-witted mercenary rushed forward with the saddle. The horse circled Medea once—examining, appraising—before slowly folding its forelegs and lowering its back.

An invitation.

But even as it knelt, its head remained held high, its breath emerging in a soft, proud huff. Those dark eyes seemed to declare: This is my choice. Not your command.

"This is... impossible."

Zeta's jaw hung slack.

"He's never accepted a saddle willingly. He bares his teeth at Lord Cesare himself! And now he kneels for the Princess?"

"But... but is that proper?"

Saya clutched her hands together nervously.

"Even while submitting, he looks so... arrogant. Is a horse supposed to act that way toward its master?"

Even to someone with no knowledge of horsemanship, the creature's attitude was unmistakably unusual.

Suppressing a smile, Medea placed her fingertips lightly upon the saddle and swung herself onto the horse's back.

The motion was effortless—natural as breathing.

"Your Highness... please be careful. If you fall, I shall catch you."

Neril's voice remained stiff, haunted by memories of that day three years past.

"NEEEIGH—!"

The white horse snorted indignantly and stamped its hooves—as though personally offended by the suggestion that it might allow its rider to fall.

Medea stroked its mane, soothing.

"Just a walk for now. I'll let you run as much as you please later."

"Neeeigh—!"

As though it understood perfectly, the horse broke into an elegant, measured stride, circling the courtyard with the pride of a king surveying his domain.

Every step seemed designed to proclaim: Look upon me. Behold my magnificence.

"Clever creature. Your name shall be... Silva."

Medea ran her fingers through the silver-white mane.

A gentle breeze stirred the garden. Silva's mane and Medea's hair lifted together, rippling in the wind like twin banners of moonlight.

The glossy white coat shimmered. Combined with the Princess's silver tresses, the effect was almost otherworldly—as though horse and rider had been fashioned from the same celestial substance.

"Neeeigh—!"

Silva stamped its forelegs with evident satisfaction.

The bells upon its bridle chimed in crystalline harmony, scattering silver notes into the sunlit air.
