Chapter 167

The Board Was
Always Mine

"You may intercept the pieces, but the board itself belongs to me."

D'Angelo's expression twisted with barely contained fury.

He lifted the carpet fully, and three or four additional sheets of paper scattered in the recoil, fluttering to the ground like condemned souls. The quantity was modest, but the content was devastating.

"This... this is the migration route the Agemas took from the plains to the royal palace."

The temperature in the cottage house plummeted.

Troop movement routes constituted intelligence of the highest sensitivity—information capable of completely reversing the tide of any conflict. The Agemas' killing intent sharpened to a razor's edge as they absorbed the implications.

Even King Peleus's personal movements had been documented.

Rachel's face drained of all color.

Why are these random maps appearing here? I never—

"I—I know nothing of this! I have never seen these documents before! I cannot fathom why they would be here—"

At that moment, Neril—who had been observing the proceedings in contemplative silence—spoke.

"Rachel's homeland is the Orpheus Mountains."

Recognition dawned across the Valdinian faces.

That region straddled the border between Valdina and Katzen—territory that had once fallen under Katzenian dominion.

The implications crystallized with damning clarity:

  • A Saintess of ambiguous origins whose background could not be verified
  • Katzen—the nation that sought to check Valdina's rising power more aggressively than any other
  • Sensitive military intelligence hidden beneath her floor

"Ah. Now everything becomes clear."

Sissair's voice dripped with sardonic satisfaction.

The assembled witnesses began drawing the obvious conclusion: the Saintess had attempted to besmirch Valdina's reputation at Katzen's instigation.

"Your Highness... did you anticipate this?"

Neril turned to Medea, understanding flooding her features.

The Princess's unusually frigid reaction upon learning of Rachel's origins now made perfect sense.

You knew from the beginning. You knew something like this would unfold.

"Your Highness, truly..."

D'Angelo swallowed his admiration. Priests of the Holy Land stood among them—allowing them to witness the Princess's near-prophetic insight would only invite unwanted attention. His master would never forgive such carelessness.

"No—please, you must believe me. I am innocent..."

Rachel's protestations grew increasingly frantic.

And Medea, who had observed the entire spectacle in perfect stillness, finally turned her gaze upon the floundering woman.

Rachel's eyes—normally so composed, so calculating—now swam with confusion and panic.

Medea's Reflection

Rachel. This is Valdina. You should have understood that from the beginning.

You may intercept the pieces—but the board itself has always been in my hands.

In her previous life, Medea had learned with brutal clarity what fate awaited a princess from a conquered nation—one who held neither the title of Empress nor any formal recognition.

But this time was different. Valdina was her homeland. Her territory.

Medea had seen through Rachel's machinations from the very beginning.

When she buried the dead rats. When she dispatched Anji to steal the dagger.

Every move had been anticipated. Every countermeasure already in place.

"Your Highness, I grow suspicious. Was this not the Holy Nation's objective from the outset?"

"Preposterous! We never requested such a thing—how could we possibly have—"

Paul's face glistened with cold sweat, flushing crimson as he struggled to process the impossible reversal.

Medea advanced toward Rachel with measured grace.

Her pace was neither hurried nor languid, the hem of her gown flowing like water across marble. Her bearing remained unchanged from moments ago, when she had stood accused as the architect of curses.

"Naturally, I do not believe you bore any malicious intent."

Her voice carried the warmth of absolution.

"However, should you wish to bear the mantle of Saintess, you must exercise far greater caution with your words and actions henceforth."

Medea's gracious forgiveness accomplished what no accusation could—it transformed Rachel from a potential threat into an utter stranger.

The Agemas' gazes turned to sharpened steel as they regarded Rachel, who stood biting the inside of her cheek in impotent fury.

Months of painstaking effort—schemes undertaken at the risk of her very life—rendered meaningless in a single afternoon.

Trust, once shattered, could not be rebuilt. Every action Rachel took from this moment forward would be viewed through a lens of suspicion.

Medea turned with unhurried elegance.

"Your Eminence, I require no apology for your discourtesy. However, I must insist that you articulate the Holy Nation's official position regarding today's events with absolute clarity."

Her voice remained serene—devoid of any trace of anger toward the man who had been savaging her reputation mere moments ago.

And that very serenity made her words devastating.

The Princess intended to create an official record of the disgrace he and the Saintess had visited upon themselves this day.

How diminished will my authority become within the Holy Land? And what of the Saintess—she who should embody the majesty of divine representation?

Paul's heart felt as though it had been set ablaze.

"Your Majesty, Your Highness... this old man spoke rashly and without thought—"

"Rest assured. I trust His Holiness the Pope will provide sufficient explanation in the High Priest's stead."

Paul's vision darkened at the continued weight of Medea's implicit threats.

And so the Saintess and the Knights Templar were forced to flee Valdina as though demons nipped at their heels.

Not a single Valdinian came to see them off.

Paul had no opportunity to vent his mounting shame upon these presumptuous northern barbarians—not when his mind churned with dread over what consequences awaited him.

"Saintess, why did you act so impetuously? If you intended to make a move, you should have awaited our arrival."

Rachel nearly erupted at the rebuke—delivered as though she were a reckless child being scolded for breaking a vase.

How could I have possibly predicted the situation would reverse so completely? That I would become the one framed?

The counterfeit dagger. The planted maps. Was it all Medea's doing?

But Rachel could not even glance in Medea's direction to confirm her suspicions.

The Agemas were now regarding her as though she were a ghoul crawled up from the accursed plains.

Peleus had positioned himself between Rachel and his sister, his back turned to her completely—as though he could not bear for her eyes to so much as touch Medea.

Six months of meticulous cultivation. Squandered.

Rachel wanted to scream until her throat tore. Medea had not merely defended herself—she had transformed the entire palace into Rachel's enemy.

Family. Land. And now every potential ally within these walls.

All stripped away.

She clenched her teeth until her jaw ached, forcing down the fury that threatened to consume her. Tears welled unbidden in her eyes.

The only one who can salvage this situation is the old High Priest before me. I must endure.

"Forgive me. I was foolish."

Her fragile appearance reminded Paul that before her awakening, she had been nothing more than a young girl wandering the unforgiving plains.

Yes, she was too young and naive to comprehend the subtle power dynamics between continental nations. From this point forward, we must guide the Saintess more carefully.

Innocent and beautiful, yet guileless enough to fall prey to the machinations of the wicked.

"We shall escort the Saintess to the temple with our lives as forfeit."

The Templar Commander knelt before Rachel and pressed his lips to her feet.

This ritual should have been performed the moment they first beheld her. Circumstance had delayed the gesture, but their formal courtesy remained unchanged.

Yet the overwhelming reverence that had filled his heart when he first crossed into Valdina's borders—that transcendent emotion upon encountering the divine emissary—had long since faded to ash.

The Streets of the Capital

As the carriage made its way toward the palace gates, voices from the street filtered through the thin walls.

"So what if she's a Saintess? Lives a hard life, climbs to the top of people's heads, and has nothing to show for it but schemes!"

"How dare she slander Her Highness the Princess! The Holy Nation is no different! Can't their priests distinguish right from wrong?!"

Word of the cottage house incident had already spread like wildfire through the Valdinian capital.

The citizens' fury could not be contained.

"Look—that's the Knights Templar—"

"So? We curse with words, not weapons! They should be grateful we're not pouring chamber pots on their precious carriage like those Claudio traitors deserve!"

When had the Knights Templar ever suffered such open mockery? They had come merely to retrieve the Saintess.

How could anyone have predicted that the naive young woman would fall for Katzen's manipulation and stage such an unconscionable spectacle?

The Templar Commander sighed heavily and fixed his gaze upon the Saintess seated across from him.

"Saintess, remember this well."

His voice carried the weight of iron.

"From the moment this carriage departs Valdina, you are neither Katzenian nor a citizen of any nation. You exist solely as a servant of the Goddess. Your nationality, your memories, your connections to any people—all of it must be abandoned."

Rachel remained silent.

"Saintess."
