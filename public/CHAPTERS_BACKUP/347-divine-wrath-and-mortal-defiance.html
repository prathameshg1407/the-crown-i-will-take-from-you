Chapter 347

Divine Wrath
and Mortal Defiance

"When gods punish, even the mightiest fall—yet some refuse to kneel."

"Wait—over there!"

Angelique's scream shattered the silence.

Every eye in the hall turned toward Cesare.

He still stood, blade raised—but something was wrong.

Along his sternum, skin had begun to split and burn. Red, scorched lines spread like cracks in glass, radiating outward from his chest.

Cesare lowered his gaze slowly.

A stream of crimson light—hot as molten metal—slithered beneath his skin like a living serpent.

It was an alien force, divine in origin, compelling him not to touch the oracle.

Cesare's jaw tightened.

His golden eyes remained steady, unwavering.

"If you think this pathetic trick will make me comply, you're mistaken."

The hall held its breath.

CRACK!

His sword slammed into the table.

The crimson light exploded upward in response.

"Move! Everyone get back!"

Chaos erupted.

A whirlwind of dark red energy spiraled through the chamber.

Cesare's black aura surged to meet the divine force attempting to subdue him.

The two powers collided—black and crimson intertwining in a violent, impossible dance.

The messenger from the Holy Kingdom stared, mouth agape.

Is he... is he trying to fight God?

He's insane. Utterly, completely mad.

It shouldn't even be possible. How much did we sacrifice for this divine intervention?

No mortal can withstand that power. That's the entire point!

Yet even as he watched, Cesare remained standing.

If this fails... our entire plan...!

A violent maelstrom consumed the hall.

The sound of tearing air. Fragments of searing heat.

The intertwined black and crimson energies looked simultaneously ominous and impossible—defying every natural law.

No one dared approach the epicenter.

"Cesare!"

Only Medea rushed forward without hesitation.

"Don't—!"

Cesare's voice, raw and commanding, cut through the chaos.

BOOM!

The light finally collapsed.

Where the vortex had raged, crimson chains of divine energy now bound Cesare's black aura—and him along with it.

The beam of light that had forced him to his knees flickered and vanished.

His body bore the marks of divine punishment.

Red welts crisscrossed his bronzed skin as though he'd been lashed with whips of fire.

Medea ran to him. Her green eyes trembled with barely suppressed panic.

"This is—"

"I'm fine."

Meeting her gaze, Cesare shook his head.

But the slight tremor at the corner of his mouth, the involuntary twitch of his arms—Medea could read the intensity of the pain coursing through him.

"Don't waver. Don't let them see. Proceed as planned."

His whisper was barely audible—meant only for her.

To everyone else, it appeared as though the First Prince had simply collapsed into his wife's arms.

Cesare's hand tightened around hers reflexively, then released—afraid of hurting her.

"Cesare—"

"The plan... follow it. Don't—"

He managed those final words before consciousness slipped away entirely.

◆◆◆

The Imperial Palace, once bright and bustling, turned cold and suffocating—as though dragged back three years to darker times.

The master of the palace, the First Prince, remained unconscious.

Katzen's High Priest shook his head gravely.

"There is no cure."

"How can you—the High Priest—say such a thing?"

Marquis Leniard erupted in fury.

"I don't know what manner of affliction this is, but it's not a disease. My healing cannot touch it. Holy power... is equally useless."

The High Priest confirmed the grim truth.

"It is divine power—the omnipotent force of the Goddess herself surrounds His Highness's body. I can sense it. But it is so overwhelming that my holy power cannot penetrate it."

The High Priest had summoned every priest in Katzen, pooling their combined holy power.

Yet even that could not break the sacred barrier imprisoning the First Prince.

He wiped sweat from his brow, shaking his head in bewilderment.

Has holy power ever been used to oppress someone like this?

"Terence, could this be a dark curse like before?"

Medea's voice was quiet but urgent.

Terence shook his head.

"There's no trace of sorcery. No residue of primordial darkness. It's pristine. And that's what makes it so disturbing."

"Ahhh..."

Marquis Leniard groaned in despair.

The First Prince's faction seemed doomed to relive the nightmare of three years past.

And now—at this critical juncture, with the coronation looming—

Day after day, Cesare hovered on the brink of death.

Fever consumed his body, refusing to break. He convulsed, lost consciousness, coughed up blood.

Medea never left his side.

Despite her pregnancy, she clung to the edge of his bed, gripping cold sheets with white-knuckled determination.

"Your Highness, please... you must care for yourself..."

Her attendants begged through tears, but her resolve would not bend.

"Behold! The Goddess's judgment!"

Hearing that the First Prince teetered between life and death, the messenger from the Holy Kingdom crowed his victory.

"The son of Katzen dared defy the Goddess's command, and She Herself has struck him down! Let this be a warning—whoever disobeys divine will shall be punished severely!"

Even those who'd initially dismissed the oracle as absurd began to believe that the priests had somehow cursed the First Prince.

The rumor spread like wildfire.

"Did you hear? A new oracle came down."

"Has the Goddess lost Her mind? Tearing apart a devoted couple?"

People shook their heads at the madness of it—but soon, different voices emerged.

"Still, we must save him first. It's the Goddess we're talking about!"

"Would you rather entrust the empire to the Fourth Princess or the Third Prince—both wrapped in the Western Empress's schemes?"

"If the First Prince doesn't wake, our empire is finished!"

Though some worried about the gravity of the situation, public sentiment gradually shifted.

"We should march to the palace! Demand answers from the First Princess!"

"But... did the Prince and Princess do anything wrong? Isn't the Goddess being unreasonable?"

"So you'd rather see all of Katzen burned by divine wrath? Where's your patriotism?"

The agitator pressed those who hesitated.

The men flanking him exchanged knowing glances.

"Exactly! She framed the true Saintess! Let's march together—see if the First Princess can ignore all of us!"

The gathered crowd, whipped into a frenzy, surged toward the palace.

The two agitators slipped away, vanishing into the mob.

The First Prince's faction did not budge.

Among the crowds that gathered before the palace gates, those at the vanguard—hurling insults and filth at the First Princess—were swiftly overpowered.

The sight of them being dragged away with broken arms was horror incarnate.

"Gods... I must have been mad to provoke the Black Devil's household."

"Before the First Prince wakes, they'll slit our throats! Turn back! Now!"

The ruthless suppression—showing no mercy despite the circumstances—reminded the populace of the First Prince faction's fearsome reputation.

The palace quelled dissent through force.

Who said the pen is mightier than the sword?

When the instigators were captured or returned wounded, the flames of outrage quickly died.

After that, the First Prince's faction sealed the palace gates.

Severing all contact with the outside world, they demonstrated absolute defiance of the oracle.

"A master who rejects divine decree. Servants who uphold his will even as he lies unconscious. The First Prince's faction must be forged from steel itself!"

People felt equal parts fear and admiration for such unwavering solidarity.

Yet beyond those towering white walls, the internal situation was far more precarious than the united front suggested.

End Chapter 347
