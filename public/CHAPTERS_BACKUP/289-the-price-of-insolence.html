The Cathedral of Saint Katzen — That Same Night

Unlike the darkness that shrouded the world outside, the cathedral's interior blazed with the light of dozens of candles, bright as midday.

"Ugh—!"

Cesare gazed down at the man crumpled on the marble floor.

The priestly vestments that had radiated such dignity at the coronation were now stained with filth and dust. The towering mitre lay discarded nearby, trampled and misshapen.

It was High Priest Norman.

Cesare had no intention of letting the man who had placed poisoned flowers in Medea's hands escape unpunished.

"Your Highness, you must understand! I had no choice—the Western Empress forced my hand!"

Like all cornered creatures, Norman scrambled to deflect blame onto someone else.

"I never intended—I couldn't help it—they threatened to strip me of my diocese if I refused to comply..."

"His Holiness did not place the high priest's crown upon your head so you could grovel at the feet of the imperial family."

Cesare's voice was almost amused.

The pathetic excuses tumbling from that desperate face made it impossible to watch any longer.

"I mean—I only accepted their proposal because—"

Norman bit his tongue, but it was already too late.

"I had no choice! The Count of Montfort approached me first, and—"

"Spare me the excuses. I should have dealt with you three years ago."

Cesare cut him off as though the words weren't worth hearing.

"You had ample time to reconsider your allegiances, did you not?"

The casual observation drained all color from Norman's face.

"I have known about your collusion with the Western Empress for three years."

Back then, Norman had been certain the First Prince would never survive the curse. He had wagered on the wrong horse without realizing his every move was being observed.

In other words: Cesare had known everything from the beginning. He had simply been waiting.

"Your Highness, the gods are merciful to those who err momentarily! Do not be deceived by vain desires—remain a servant of the divine—"

"Humans are creatures of desire. What difference does it make whether one serves God or oneself?"

Cesare's voice was almost gentle as he straightened Norman's disheveled collar with deliberate care.

"You have committed only one true error."

His hand closed around the fabric at Norman's throat. The grip was iron—close enough to crush flesh.

"You touched someone you should never have touched."

"Your Highness—"

"Did you truly believe you could act with such impunity? Without consequence?"

"Forgive me! Please, I beg your mercy! I will never act so foolishly again—I will place all the power of the church at Your Highness's disposal—every resource to support your claim to the throne—"

The crushing pressure around his throat only tightened.

When those golden eyes pierced him, Norman went utterly still—like prey caught in a predator's jaws.

"What use would I have for the power of a defrocked priest?"

"D-Defrocked...?"

Norman's eyes went wide with incomprehension. He was the highest-ranking cleric in the empire—how could he possibly—

As if to confirm his rising dread, Cesare ground his heel into the mitre lying on the floor.

"A new High Priest will be arriving from the Holy Kingdom."

By now, the Façade's report of today's events would have reached Pope Benedict's ears.

The head of Katzen's diocese would be replaced before the week was out.

"No—impossible! The Holy Kingdom would never accept Your Highness dictating church appointments—you cannot do this!"

"Oh, they will not merely accept it. They will embrace it eagerly."

Cesare's lips curved into a mocking smile.

"Consider their position. In a land where the long-awaited Saintess might at any moment abandon the old faith entirely, which would they choose to keep: the representative of the goddess herself—or an aging priest who ignored both her and the Pope's explicit instructions?"

Norman had targeted Saintess Medea.

He had disregarded the central temple. He had ignored the Pope's urgent messages.

They said ignorance bred courage. Norman had never truly grasped how completely the Holy Kingdom had pivoted toward Medea—and so he had committed an act of breathtaking stupidity.

The outcome was inevitable.

Rather than protest Cesare's expulsion of their high priest, the temple would dispatch a replacement from the pro-Saintess faction—someone eager to serve Medea's interests.

"No... no..."

Comprehension finally dawned. Norman's groan was the sound of a man watching his world collapse.

The consequences of his petty scheming had returned to him magnified beyond all imagination.

"If I had known it would come to this, I would have refused the Western Empress even if it meant my own death!"

But it was far too late for regrets.

"Ugh—!"

Norman's aged body could not withstand the shock. Clutching at his chest, he collapsed, eyes rolling back.

"He's fainted."

Alpha checked the unconscious man with clinical detachment.

"Does he presume to summon the Knights of the Crusade into Katzen? Send him to the border."

Cesare nudged the limp form with his boot, utterly indifferent.

"Yes, Your Highness."

Alpha hauled Norman onto a donkey's back and secured him there like cargo.

Cesare turned to address the priests who had been forced to watch in silence.

"Before your new High Priest arrives, I trust this will serve as a useful lesson."

THWACK!

He kicked the donkey's flank with vicious force. The corners of his mouth twisted into something cruel.

"Hee-hawww!"

The donkey brayed in alarm and bolted forward.

Norman's soiled vestments fluttered grotesquely as the beast carried him away, the former High Priest bouncing helplessly with each stride.

The assembled priests watched in frozen silence as the rattling donkey and its unconscious burden vanished through the cathedral gates.

"You may go."

Only then did dismissal come.

They stared at Cesare with exhausted, haunted eyes.

The First Prince had deliberately gathered them here at this ungodly hour for one purpose: to witness Norman's fall with their own eyes.

For the crime of touching the First Princess, the High Priest who had once reigned supreme over this cathedral had been cast down to the dirt in a single evening.

The Western Empress—whose protection Norman had boasted of so confidently—had offered no shield when the moment came.

"God preserve us..."

The priests crossed themselves, eyes squeezed shut. And in their hearts, they swore a solemn vow:

No matter how the imperial family tears itself apart in the days to come, we shall devote ourselves solely to the divine—and stay far, far away from their conflicts.

***

Norman had been expelled. The priests had dispersed.

Cesare glanced at his pocket watch. It was already well past midnight.

"Boss, heading to the Façade?"

As they exited the cathedral, Gallo stretched his lanky frame and fell into step beside Cesare.

"No."

"Huh? Why not? It's so late—just sleep at headquarters and head back tomorrow."

"..."

"If you go all the way to the palace now, I'll have to trek back there in the morning to deliver reports. What a waste of effort. And if you stayed at the Façade, we could review the messages from the Holy Kingdom together—"

"Gallo."

Terence cut him off with a flat stare.

"Have you forgotten? Cesare has a wife now. He's not so idle that he can spend every night lounging about with you."

"Oh... right."

Gallo scratched his head, glancing at Cesare. The Prince's silence confirmed Terence's words.

"Well then, I'll come to the palace too! We can relocate the command center there—"

"..."

"What? It's fine, isn't it? Terence can come along! We used to do this all the time—"

"If you want the entire world to know that the Façade answers to the First Prince, by all means."

Gallo spluttered, but Cesare's languid mockery continued unabated.

In the cathedral's lingering candlelight, shadows carved themselves across the elegant planes of his face.

"You've grown rather comfortable lately, haven't you?"

Here in Katzen—with the mad Emperor watching, with the entire imperial family scheming to expose the Façade's true allegiances—Gallo was suggesting they all parade openly to the Prince's residence?

"Fine, fine! I won't go! You don't have to be so harsh about it..."

Gallo's mouth twisted into a sulk.

You could just say you want to be alone. Did you really have to make it sting like that?

But Cesare paid no attention to his wounded feelings. With fluid grace, he mounted his horse.

The smooth motion revealed the powerful line of his thighs beneath his coat.

"Tomorrow, then."

A brief, indifferent farewell—and the black horse carrying him vanished into the darkness.

"Look at him bolt like he's afraid someone will catch him. Since when did the boss become such a devoted family man?"

Gallo raked his fingers through his already disheveled blond hair, looking for all the world like a puppy that had bounded up to greet its master only to receive a scolding.

"If I pine like this, will I end up a lonely old man with no one to come home to? What do you think, Terence?"

"..."

"Hey! At a moment when our brotherhood is crumbling before our very eyes, you have nothing to say?!"

"Go home."

Terence rubbed his weary face and waved a dismissive hand, clearly deciding the conversation wasn't worth the effort.

Fwoosh—!

A thin flame bloomed, and he vanished. Teleportation magic.

"Oh, come on! Is everyone just going to abandon me?!"

Gallo's indignant shriek scattered uselessly into the cold night air.

The Palace

Cesare's footsteps halted the moment he entered the bedchamber.

Dawn was approaching, yet the lamps still burned.

"Dea?"

Candles flickered softly throughout the room.

On the table sat a teacup, its contents long since gone cold. And beside it, slumped against the headboard in an uncomfortable position, was a woman who had fallen asleep while waiting.

The candlelight's shadow danced across her face.

The image burned itself into Cesare's memory like a painting he would never forget.

Perhaps it was because no one had ever waited for his return before.

He was a man always in motion, never pausing, never stopping. Yet the strange sensation that washed over him now defied all words.

For a long moment, he simply stood there, gazing down at Medea's sleeping form.

Then, carefully, he gathered her into his arms and laid her properly upon the bed.

"Mm..."

As the soft sheets settled against her skin, Medea stirred.

A sliver of clear green appeared between drowsy eyelids.

"...Cesare?"

"Yes."

A single syllable—yet his voice emerged strangely thick.

"...I'm home."

The soft words scattered through the quiet air.

He pressed his lips to her temple.

Medea blinked, trying to focus on his face, but exhaustion claimed her before she could manage it. Her eyes drifted closed once more.

Cesare watched her slip back into sleep, then lowered himself to lie beside her.

Every inch of his skin—chilled from the cold night ride—seemed to recognize the warmth that radiated from her.

His hand covered hers.

His fingertip traced idle, wandering patterns across the soft back of her hand, as though playing some wordless game.

"Mmm..."

Medea shifted in her sleep, a small sound of ticklish protest escaping her.

His fingers stilled.

"I was so certain I could wait as long as necessary."

A hollow smile curved his sculpted lips—the face of a god who seduced the night itself.

"How arrogant I was."
