Chapter 189

The Goddess's Judgment

"You won't stop as long as you have breath. You can't let this happen again."

The Claudio family's sins ran deeper than blood.

They had systematically eliminated Valdina's loyalists, one by one. They sowed discord between Medea and the Queen Dowager, isolating her from every ally she might have turned to.

They used her royal seal as justification to persecute innocent people, orchestrating fake rebellions that left countless bodies in their wake.

They drove Cesar—the kingdom's only remaining pillar of strength—to madness, ensuring he took his own life rather than defend what remained.

They planted a traitor among Peleus's most trusted bodyguards, annihilating them and branding him forever as the Crippled King.

But among all their treacheries, what made Medea's blood boil with unbearable rage was their manipulation.

"Medea, a monster wave has broken through Valdina's borders. You have no idea how many demons infested the road to Katzen—how many of our knights we lost along the way..."

"What about Brother Peleus? Is he safe?"

"Medea, when I heard the Saintess had become Empress, I traveled all the way to Katzen out of concern for you. I've always thought of you as my own younger sister—but all you ever do is worry about that brother of yours."

"Dea, how long are you going to leave Valdina's throne vacant? We need to decide on a successor quickly, even if it means honoring your brother's final wishes. Don't you agree?"

Their unceasing greed for the throne. And her own foolishness for never noticing the enemy standing right beside her.

"Dea!"

A familiar voice shattered her spiral of dark memories.

Peleus.

'Don't come this way.'

Medea spotted her brother sprinting toward her, his face etched with concern. With deliberate calm, she frowned and shook her head firmly.

Peleus halted mid-stride, confusion flickering across his features as he tried to decipher his sister's incomprehensible warning.

'I'm sorry I can't tell you everything, Peleus.'

This was why she had insisted on confronting them alone. This was her atonement—for her fallen homeland, for her brother, for every life destroyed by her blindness.

Even now, seeing Peleus's face—caught between past and present, between memory and reality—sent tremors through her fraying composure.

CRASH!

The stained glass windows lining the temple walls shattered with a bone-chilling crack.

The explosion's shockwave could no longer be contained.

"Medea, NO!"

In an instant, kaleidoscopic shards—as beautiful as they were deadly—erupted through the air in all directions.

Medea's body suddenly lifted as a powerful force seized her waist. Simultaneously, arms like iron walls enveloped her completely.

As she gasped in shock, glass shards struck the floor with sharp, staccato plinks—right where she'd been standing mere seconds before.

She had been a hair's breadth away from being impaled.

The embrace plunged her vision into darkness. Arms crushed her protectively, refusing to release their hold.

A razor-sharp aura surrounded them both—a shield that would permit not even the smallest fragment to reach her.

The firm chest pressed against her rose and fell violently.

"Haa... haa..."

Heavy, ragged breathing betrayed just how tense the situation had been.

A deep, musky scent filled her senses.

"You..."

A piercing voice descended into her obscured ear, cutting through the chaos.

"Honestly. I can't take my eyes off you for even a second."

Gone was his usual leisurely, unhurried manner of speaking. His words now emerged clipped and harsh, as though forced through clenched teeth.

Medea raised her head. Her eyes widened.

Blazing golden eyes glared down at her with frightening intensity.

His mouth twisted. His voice emerged sharp and ragged, barely controlled.

The relaxed facade he'd maintained until now had shattered completely, revealing something raw and unguarded beneath.

"...First Prince?"

Her voice came out small, tentative.

Why him? Why here? Why me?

She couldn't find answers to any of the questions flooding her mind.

The First Prince seemed to have no intention of providing them either. She could feel his resolve clearly through the firm arms that refused to slacken even slightly.

"Wait a moment—"

Before she could finish, he swept something over her head.

Then he strode swiftly from the temple, carrying her in his arms as though she weighed nothing.

It didn't take long for her to realize that the soft fabric brushing her cheek was the hem of his cloak.

Wrapped like a cocoon, shielded from all external threats, she experienced a strange mixture of security and unfamiliarity.

"Dea!"

Peleus strode toward them, Neril and Agemas close behind.

"Are you hurt? You could have been killed!"

Peleus rarely raised his voice at his sister, but this time was different.

The memory of those deadly glass shards falling toward Medea was utterly horrifying.

"Why didn't you move immediately? You should have gotten out the moment—"

His uncharacteristically frantic words died mid-sentence.

The First Prince was still holding Medea. Even her small face remained hidden within his collar.

"..."

Cesare seemed to collect himself. With practiced grace, he gently lowered Medea to the floor.

The smooth hem of his cloak fell away, the fabric brushing against her skin like a whisper.

"I apologize. The situation was urgent—I hope you'll both forgive my rudeness."

The First Prince stepped back as though he'd never touched her at all. Without a trace of embarrassment, he offered his apology with absolute composure.

Medea felt somewhat dazed.

That distorted expression from moments ago... was it real? Or did I imagine it?

"First Prince, thank you for saving my sister."

Peleus, who had no intention of being discourteous during such an emergency, expressed his gratitude first.

His gaze flickered to the glass fragments embedded where Medea had been standing just moments before.

'If it weren't for His Highness...'

Peleus swallowed hard, relief and anxiety churning in equal measure.

The Last Detonation

BOOM!

A terrifying, incomparable explosion tore through the air.

"This is the last one."

It was the final detonation—the one Medea had deliberately ignored, or more precisely, the one the Claudio family had meticulously prepared.

Every head turned upward toward the temple.

The interior of the goddess statue was now scorched black, blown apart by the blast.

The altar—struck by bronze fragments that had once been the goddess's wrists—lay completely shattered, broken like brittle candy.

Less than an hour ago, that very altar had hosted the Continental Ceremony performed by Peleus himself.

"If only the holy water had been sprinkled even a few minutes later..."

People shivered at the thought.

"We almost—we could have been killed!"

"What in the gods' names is happening?!"

Fortunately, everyone had managed to evacuate the temple before the explosions occurred.

"We escaped quickly because of that smoke. Otherwise, we would have been confused, completely unaware of the danger!"

The smoke rising from the pillars and the initial smaller blasts had served as warnings, alerting the guests to flee.

"Truly, the goddess is protecting us!"

"Perhaps it's because Valdina is a blessed land!"

Some speculated that this Continental Festival was especially auspicious.

However, the faces of the clergy—those who actually served the goddess—were far from bright.

'Even though this is Valdina's sacred land, it's still the goddess's temple that has collapsed! Who dares to attack not just us, but the Holy Nation itself?!'

Though attacks on the Holy Nation had occurred before, this was the first time anyone had launched such a full-scale assault on an entire temple.

Pale fury burned across High Priest Baor's weathered face at the gravity of the situation.

"Theo, watch over the Saintess."

"..."

"Theodore! Where are you staring so vacantly?"

Leonor, Commander of the Temple Knights, gripped Theo's arm firmly.

"Oh—it's nothing. My apologies, Captain."

"I know you're shaken, but we're knights. We need to be even more vigilant at times like this."

Despite the sternness in his voice, Leonor patted Theo's shoulder reassuringly before turning to Rachel.

"Saintess, are you unharmed? Your face is pale. Were you badly frightened?"

Meanwhile, Rachel was too shocked by an entirely different matter to register Leonor's concern.

'Didn't they say the explosion was only supposed to target the Emperor? Why did they make it so massive? Why were we caught in it too?!'

Even now, the cries of people in shock and pain echoed so loudly they hurt her ears.

"Anyway, it's fortunate everyone evacuated safely before the explosion. There appear to be no casualties. It doesn't seem to be a monster wave either."

Just as Leonor finished surveying the surroundings—

"AAAGH! AAAAHH!"

A blood-curdling scream rang out, as if denying his words entirely.

"W-wait—did that come from inside?!"

"My legs! Oh gods, my LEGS!"

The victim's agony was so visceral, so piercing, that everyone present could feel it through those desperate screams alone.

"Look! There's still someone trapped inside!"

Someone pointed frantically toward the ruined temple.

"There! Do you see? Over there!"

"Someone's pinned under the debris! He can't get out!"

Only the man's face was visible, half-buried beneath the shattered altar and fallen statue. Blood and dust obscured his features, making recognition nearly impossible.

Just one person.

But there exists only one person in this world who can recognize a child born from the pain of her own body, no matter how disfigured.

"SAMON—!"

A mother's scream that tore the very fabric of the world

Catherine's anguished cry ripped through the air—a scream that seemed to tear her heart from her chest.
