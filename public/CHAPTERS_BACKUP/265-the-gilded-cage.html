Chapter 265

The Gilded
Cage

"It's too late to run away."

His tone shifted in an instant, taking on the cadence of an interrogation.

"Gallo? Terence? Were you considering candidates from the expedition? Or perhaps someone from D'Angel?"

Gallo was too frivolous. Terence too timid. D'Angel far too ancient.

Medea found herself slightly taken aback by this sudden line of questioning.

"I hadn't given it much thought. If you had refused, I would have found someone."

"So I was your first choice."

"..."

The same words, yet colored by a subtle, self-assured conviction. Medea felt a flicker of defiance rise within her.

"Regardless, you accepted. The covenant has been fulfilled. Nothing further will change. The wedding shall proceed as planned."

Cesare suppressed a laugh.

He recalled Medea's utterly impassive expression as she had proposed marriage atop the flying Locke.

"How can I not love a man like that?"

One would never imagine that the princess who had confessed such ardent devotion before her brother was the same woman standing before him now.

Cesare knew she possessed a talent for performance, but witnessing himself as the subject of such acting made the contrast all the more fascinating.

'She truly harbors no expectations for this marriage. No—she has no expectations of me whatsoever.'

The boundaries Medea had drawn were crystal clear.

"When our arrangement concludes, you may take whatever Empress suits your fancy."

"Medea. Have you forgotten? You may ask anything of me. I require no contract."

"As I've said before—I trust no one. There are no exceptions."

She extended her pale hand through a narrow gap in the stone wall—a wall shrouded in thorny vines, each barb a warning.

What a merciless woman you are.

Yet despite that bitter thought, Cesare found himself reaching for her hand.

Even if the thorns drew blood, even if they tore him to shreds, he would not cease climbing that wall.

He understood: this was the only path to reach her.

And when at last he stood at the summit, he would be the only one permitted to remain.

"Very well. You were so desperate that I found myself unable to refuse."

"When was I ever desperate—"

Before she could finish, he closed the distance between them.

"I am willing to be your companion."

The words were simple enough, yet delivered with such imperious certainty that they sounded less like acceptance and more like a unilateral decree.

Those golden eyes fixed upon her with the intensity of a predator watching prey already ensnared.

Perhaps she had stepped too easily into something from which there was no escape.

A sudden premonition of danger made Medea retreat instinctively.

Her wavering green eyes held the precarious wariness of a cornered creature.

"It's too late to run away."

A firm hand caught her unsteady form, drawing her close.

Slowly, deliberately, he leaned into the space where her breath hung suspended.

"Don't worry, Medea."

"..."

"I will make you the most envied bride in all the world."

His eyes blazed with quiet ferocity.

Now that she had set foot upon his path, there would be no turning back.

The Imperial Palace of Katzen

"This is preposterous!"

The palace erupted into chaos from the early hours of morning.

Following the Continental Congress, news of the oracle fulfilled by Medea and Cesare had finally reached the imperial seat of Katzen.

"You expect me to accept a daughter of Valdina as my daughter-in-law?!"

Perdiccas II hurled his scepter to the ground.

The ornate staff shattered against the marble. Count Montfort, standing opposite the throne, flinched visibly.

'Better that he throws it than keeps it in hand. At this rate, I might be the next target.'

The Count grew increasingly anxious that the enraged Emperor might strike his own father-in-law.

"I have no excuse, Your Majesty. The situation spiraled beyond anyone's control..."

Yet even as he offered contrition, Count Montfort nursed his own grievances in silence.

"How could anyone have anticipated this, Your Majesty? The First Prince appeared without warning, cast the severed head of the evil dragon before the entire assembly, and had the audacity to claim the bride in the dragon's stead. His Holiness has already consented to officiate."

The Lord Chamberlain's reply was measured and calm. He retrieved the familiar scepter and presented it back to the Emperor with practiced deference.

"The parents gave no permission! Who authorized this farce?!"

"This marriage is not an ordinary royal union, Your Majesty. It is a ceremony to inherit a sacred oracle... His Holiness has pledged to officiate personally and has granted permission for the ceremony to be held within the Central Temple itself."

Each successive revelation only deepened the Emperor's fury. Perdiccas II sat rigid with barely contained rage.

"Furthermore, His Highness the First Prince has submitted to the Holy Kingdom a marriage covenant bearing Her Late Majesty the Empress's signature."

"That insolent wretch! How did he obtain such a document?!"

"It would appear... Her Majesty the Empress entrusted it to His Highness before her passing."

"Eleanor...!"

Perdiccas's brow furrowed deeply. His fist crashed against the armrest of the golden throne with a resounding crack.

Since Cesare was not Eleanor's natural son, she would have cared nothing for whom he chose to marry. The realization only inflamed his resentment further.

'Eleanor. Are you taking your revenge from beyond the grave?'

'You must be delighted that my son has allied himself with a foreign princess.'

The Emperor swallowed his bitterness and twisted his lips into a sneer.

"Mother and son alike—neither shows the slightest regard for my authority."

The defiance was staggering, as though mother and child had conspired across the veil of death.

His expression darkened with malice.

"Very well. Let them do as they please. After all, he's the son I already discarded. Whom he marries is none of my concern."

No matter how protected she might be by oracles, the Holy Kingdom, or her brother—once she set foot in Katzen, eliminating one young woman would be a trivial matter.

The Royal Palace of Valdina

"Have you heard the news?"

When word of Medea's betrothal spread, the entire continent trembled with the implications.

The Princess's homeland was no exception.

"The First Prince of Katzen! Our beloved Princess is far too good for him! We cannot simply hand her over!"

The union of two empires that had shaped continental history became the most fevered topic in every square and marketplace.

"That blood-soaked butcher is wholly unworthy of the First Prince's title! How can our Highness be expected to trust such a man as her husband?!"

"Unthinkable! The late Emperor would rise from his very grave!"

The reactions of the Valdinian people were sharply divided.

"How can anyone refuse a marriage ordained by sacred oracle? The Holy Kingdom was determined to chain Her Highness to a dead dragon for the rest of her days!"

"Were it not for the First Prince of Katzen, Her Highness would have been branded a cursed bride for eternity. This is the lesser evil."

Those who had witnessed the charged atmosphere of the Continental Congress—where the specter of war had loomed over every delegation—understood that this marriage had been inevitable.

"Still, I..."

At that moment, the thunderous fanfare of a military band echoed through the streets.

"What's happening?"

With a rumbling like distant thunder, a magnificent carriage procession made its way toward the royal castle.

Above the lead carriage—massive and lacquered in deepest black—the banner of the First Prince snapped proudly in the wind.

"The First Prince's gift procession! They've arrived already?"

Scarcely any time had passed since the announcement of the betrothal.

Before Valdina and Katzen could even begin coordinating the countless matters their union would entail, the First Prince's tribute had already reached the royal capital.

The haste was unmistakable—a clear signal that this marriage would proceed without delay or negotiation.

The knights escorting the carriages wore cloaks and armor of unrelieved black.

Their warhorses were coal-dark, their helmets devoid of ornamentation—severe and imposing.

"The First Prince's elite guard..."

That he had dispatched his finest soldiers—the very men who had carved through enemy lines during the Continental War—to personally escort mere gifts spoke volumes about how seriously the First Prince regarded this marriage.

The Valdinians watched the majestic procession with conflicted hearts, torn between reluctant pride and smoldering resentment.

"One... two..."

The carriages kept coming. An endless river of wagons and treasure carts that made the citizens' eyes grow wider with each passing moment.

"Let me be certain I understand."

Sissair peered down at the mountain of documents spread across the council table.

"She truly intends to reside in Katzen?"

Before him lay the comprehensive inventory of gifts dispatched by the First Prince.

Gift Inventory

He read aloud from a golden scroll of exquisite craftsmanship:

  • • Five wagons laden with gold
  • • Three wagons of pure mithril
  • • Two wagons of grimoire crystals
  • • One thousand flasks of holy water from the Sacred Springs
  • • Ten treasure chests carved by the master artisans of Alpensia
  • • Three hundred golden roses, enchanted to bloom eternally
  • • The Silver Deerskin Letter of Protection
  • • The Golden Wax Covenant Seal
  • • The Jewel of the God-King
  • • The Temple Sword, along with eighty additional weapons consecrated by the High Priests...

The list continued far beyond what could be read in a single sitting.

"This is meant to be a betrothal gift?"

The unprecedented scale of the tribute made Sissair's brow furrow deeply behind his spectacles.

His sharp gaze fixed upon Terence, attempting to divine the First Prince's true intentions.

'Hmph.'

Terence allowed the corners of his mouth to curve upward as he surveyed the stacks of scrolls still waiting to be catalogued.

'And we haven't even begun reviewing the property deeds.'
