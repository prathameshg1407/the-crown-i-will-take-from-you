Chapter 318

Serpents in
Sacred Halls

"The devil quotes scripture—and pours the drinks."

"A trap? You believe someone ensnared you—that you are innocent in all this?"

Alfred's voice was a tangle of resentment, anxiety, and barely contained fear.

"Catherine, this empire... I warned you. It was never meant for someone like you."

He pressed himself against the iron bars, his once-handsome face lined with regret.

"I should have spoken sooner. If only you hadn't left the way you did—perhaps none of this would have—"

Rather than accept his outstretched hand, the Western Empress pressed her fingers to her temples in agitation.

"And if I hadn't left? What then? I would have followed you and become the wife of some village nobody?"

"..."

"Do not misunderstand me, Alfred. If you've come here to offer pity because of my current circumstances—spare me."

"Catherine!"

"I am not so easily broken. I will escape this place. My son will ascend to the highest seat in this empire."

Her voice hardened.

"But if I had chosen you? My child would never have been granted a title. He would have been nothing."

The Western Empress exhaled sharply through her nose.

Perhaps she had missed this man, in fleeting moments. But she had never once regretted her choice to leave him behind.

His dreams and hers had always run parallel—two lines destined never to intersect.

"Go back, Alfred. If you do not wish to see me dead, do not come again."

The Empress closed her eyes.

This single night—allowing herself to be swept up in old emotions, to fall this far because of lingering sentiment—had been enough.

Perdiccas II had discarded her without mercy. And yet, even so, he remained a better match for her than the man standing before her now.

The Emperor and I understand each other perfectly. We both know that a fleeting romance can never compare to a lifetime of power.

The Central Temple of Sacramontis.

For centuries, this sacred institution had radiated an aura of noble dignity and timeless authority. But in recent days, that ancient majesty had begun to crumble.

"Saintess Medea has declined to attend this great prayer assembly as well."

"How many times does that make now? When His Holiness completed the revision of the historical texts concerning the Shadea tribe, I assumed the Saintess's anger would be appeased..."

"This disaster lies entirely at Noman's feet—that imbecile. He failed to recognize the true Saintess and committed such a vulgar offense. He forgot his duty as a priest and chased after political favor. He is a stain upon the Old Church!"

The voices of the gathered priests mingled anger with barely concealed dread.

"Surely we haven't been... abandoned? If the Saintess never returns to the Old Church..."

"Do not speak such ill omens!"

A senior high priest snapped at his colleague.

"The Saintess is merely expressing her displeasure at our past conduct. The representative of the Goddess would never forsake Her own kingdom."

Yet even as he spoke, his voice trailed off—as though he himself was not entirely convinced.

At that moment, High Priest Paul shuffled past them.

"Ah, there goes High Priest Paul..."

"Leave him be."

A fellow high priest wrinkled his nose at the faint aroma that followed in Paul's wake.

"A man of his rank, drinking in broad daylight instead of attending prayers...!"

"I cannot bring myself to condemn him. Given what he's endured, I understand his suffering all too well."

Paul had awaited the appearance of the Saintess more fervently than any other.

When Rachel emerged, he had elevated her as the first and final blessing from the Goddess, pouring out his blind devotion without reservation.

But then she was exposed as a fraud—a vessel for dark magic masquerading as holiness.

"The sins he committed against the true Saintess Medea—all in service to that false prophet—will soon be made public. He may be stripped of his high priesthood entirely. Small wonder he cannot survive without drink."

Indeed, since the false Saintess Rachel's downfall, Paul's influence had collapsed entirely.

The high priests who had once followed him had long since departed—some returning to their home provinces, others aligning themselves with the Pope's faction.

Only Paul remained, hollow and alone, drinking himself into oblivion.

"High Priest, you must stop drinking now. The temple gates will close shortly."

A small tavern tucked into the back alleys of downtown Sacramontis, not far from the Central Temple.

Paul slumped over the table, methodically emptying glass after glass.

"It's nearly curfew. And this establishment... High Priest, if the Holy Guard catches you here again, there will be severe consequences."

The intermediate priest shifted uncomfortably, pressing his case.

"Why do you even bother? Worried you won't make senior priest if you're associated with me?"

Paul sneered and hurled the contents of his glass into the younger man's face.

The intermediate priest—already exhausted by endless criticism—now found himself drenched in wine. Whatever sense of duty had kept him here evaporated in that moment.

"Very well. I shall return tomorrow."

"Leaving already? Ha! Go on then—I know you've been waiting to jump ship. Go!"

Left alone in the tavern, Paul staggered through another drink.

For thirty years he had lived in abstinence. Only now did he finally understand why ordinary men sought refuge in alcohol.

"My heart burns. Why, Goddess? Have you forsaken me?"

Months had passed since Rachel's true nature was revealed. Yet Paul still could not accept the truth.

"Was I truly mistaken? Could I have been wrong?"

He could not believe it. He refused to accept it.

That Saintess Medea was the true representative chosen by the Goddess.

That the sacred history of the Central Temple had been rewritten to accommodate what he considered filthy heresy.

"No... it cannot be. I could not have been wrong. That woman is surely..."

Before his eyes, two images flickered in competition: Rachel, delicate as a flower—and Medea, fierce as a she-wolf encountered in winter's depths.

He dropped his head into his hands in anguish.

That was when a chair scraped against the floor. Someone settled quietly into the seat across from him.

A hooded figure, face obscured in shadow.

"How sorrowful, to witness a priest of God so deeply tormented."

A cup was pushed toward Paul across the table.

Beneath the dim glow of the lantern, the stranger's crimson eyes glinted sharply.

Time passed. Darkness pooled against the tavern windows like spilled ink.

The interior had emptied. Only Paul's table remained occupied. The proprietor was nowhere to be seen.

"...I truly cannot accept it. Even now."

Empty bottles littered the table—far more than before.

"His Holiness imprisoned Rachel in the darkest cell without a word—showed no interest in investigating further. He even accepted those unclean heretics as ancestors of the Holy Kingdom!"

He slammed his glass down.

"It's absolutely wrong. Everyone has become blind to the truth. The entire Central Temple..."

"Even the martyr Barter proclaimed truths that only he could perceive—yet no one believed him."

The stranger refilled Paul's glass, his tone casual.

The hooded figure was Jason.

"Perhaps the ordeal you now endure is a divine test—the Goddess separating those who can perceive truth from those who cannot."

"Hearing your words, I feel my frustration finally easing."

Paul's bloodshot eyes gleamed with desperate gratitude.

"Truly, I cannot discuss these matters with anyone in the Central Temple. The moment I mention Saintess Medea's name, they regard me as an enemy..."

His fist struck the table. The glass trembled; wine sloshed over the rim.

"This trial... how could His Holiness fail to recognize it? How have the eyes of the Church's highest authority grown so dim?"

"The current Pope must have strayed far from the Goddess's true will."

Jason spoke exactly what Paul longed to hear.

"Yes—precisely. That is why... that is why I alone could see it."

Paul muttered to himself, satisfaction spreading across his features.

At that moment, Jason's hand moved beneath his sleeve—switching the glasses.

So delighted was Paul to finally encounter someone who shared his convictions that he noticed nothing.

"Indeed, that is why I traveled here to the Central Temple. To offer what assistance I can."

In the new glass, a faint dark smoke shimmered within the wine.

"My homeland of Katzen has been in chaos ever since the Princess of Valdina appeared. I could not simply stand by and watch. I felt compelled to act."

"I knew you would understand! She was never a true Saintess. I was right. I was right all along!"

Paul seized the switched glass and drained it in one gulp, slamming the empty vessel down triumphantly.

"That monster from Valdina seeks to engulf this entire continent in flames!"

At the word"monster," something glacial flickered behind Jason's eyes.

Yet his smile never wavered. His voice remained honeyed and smooth.

"The discerning High Priest grasps the situation perfectly."

"I've heard of your circumstances as well, Grand Duke. That black demon has been rampaging under the Princess's protection, making your position increasingly untenable."

In Paul's mind, the two were bound not by prophecy but by a cursed, ominous connection—both evil, perfectly matched as mutual enemies.

He did not realize that the words he spoke in Jason's defense drove into the other man's heart like nails.

"More important than any petty conflict between those two is finding a way to correct the Central Temple's current deterioration."

Jason maintained his composure with visible effort.

"High Priest, have you ever considered this? From the very moment the dragon's severed head fell upon the Central Temple—is it not remarkable how many extraordinary events have unfolded in such rapid succession?"

"Wait... what do you mean?"

Paul's eyes widened.

"The curse of the evil dragon has blinded everyone to reality. You suspected as much yourself, did you not?"

In the dim light of the abandoned tavern, Jason's crimson eyes gleamed like banked coals.

And High Priest Paul—drunk, bitter, desperate for validation—leaned forward to listen.
