Chapter 186

Dreams and Detonations

"In the moments before catastrophe, every mask falls away."

"I, I—you idiot! Get out of here! I'll throw you in the backyard so you can't keep whining like that! Along with those worthless, filthy bastards!"

The Fourth Princess's scream echoed through the chamber, her voice shrill with hysteria as she gestured wildly at Samon.

"Ahh! Do you really want to see me bite my tongue and die? I've had enough of being associated with those beggars—even on paper!"

"I-I... Angelique! We are your parents! How could you say such a thing? Your Majesty, please!"

After the farce involving the Empress Dowager concluded, the Fourth Princess made good on her threat. She truly departed for the East, where her maternal grandfather resided.

It was said that on her way out, she spat on the palace walls—a final act of contempt that only deepened Emperor Perdiccas II's fury.

With the Fourth Princess's exit from court, House Claudio lost even its most superficial supporter. Life in Katzen grew considerably harder for Samon's family.

But Samon paid it no mind.

His plan to drive that woman from his sight had succeeded brilliantly.

'The Emperor, the peers, the Empress Dowager—all those great and powerful people never even noticed I let that woman slip away so easily.'

Samon's gaze drifted across the hall to where the Valdinian royal siblings danced in the distance.

They sparkled beneath the radiant light streaming through stained glass windows, their joy and elegance a stark contrast to the sinister calculation burning in Samon's eyes.

'You will soon be just like that too.'

Today, Emperor Peleus would ascend the altar to conduct the sacred ceremonies of the Continental Rite as ruler of Valdina.

Buried just beneath that very altar lay a concentrated powder charge—powerful enough to topple statues and obliterate the entire interior of the temple.

Samon's eyes lingered lovingly on the towering stone statue positioned behind the altar.

A thrill coursed through his body as he envisioned the glorious moment of detonation.

Stone fragments would fly outward like shrapnel. The Emperor's body would be hurled into the air by the concussive force.

And finally, the grand statue—symbol of the goddess's mercy—would tilt slowly forward and crash down upon Peleus's broken form.

Samon vividly imagined the altar's debris tearing through flesh, the statue descending mercilessly to crush bone beneath tons of carved stone.

The sound of the Emperor's body crumbling, breaking, pulverized beneath a mountain of rock.

He could barely suppress his laughter as the gruesome tableau unfolded in his mind's eye—flesh and blood bursting from between shattered stones.

His only regret was that he would not witness, with his own eyes, the vivid terror of death carved into Peleus's face at that long-awaited moment.

'When Peleus falls, the throne of Valdina falls into my hands. The throne that should have been mine from the very beginning.'

'I will take excellent care of this country you worked so hard to develop while I was away, dear brother.'

"Master."

A servant approached from behind, his voice low and urgent.

"Why are you here?"

"The butler sent me. He said that His Majesty's protection must be your top priority."

"Hmm..."

Only then did Samon's frown ease. The servant drew closer and whispered softly.

"I need you to come with me immediately. Something urgent has arisen."

"What?"

Samon's composed mask cracked at the news his subordinate delivered.

He checked the time.

Today was the only opportunity to strike at Peleus. Once he returned to Valdina, the iron-willed Agemas would surround him like an impenetrable fortress.

Impatience flared in Samon's chest.

"Let's go."

He hurried from the hall, following his man into the shadows.

Never noticing that the water lily pinned to his collar sat slightly more crooked than usual.

The chapel where the festival continued in full swing.

"..."

Medea stared blankly at the man who had appeared before her once again.

Jason, meeting those frigid eyes, was the first to speak.

"Do you want to laugh at me? That I've come crawling before you again in this pitiful form—the Emperor's dog?"

Jason's face twisted into a deep frown, his handsome features strained and pale. It was as though the very words wounded his pride.

"..."

Medea offered no response.

Jason's fingers twitched nervously. His heart felt as though it stood on the very edge of a cliff—he couldn't tell if what he felt was the fear of falling or the terrible impulse to leap.

"I don't even understand myself anymore, Medea, but... I came here to see you."

The confession slipped out before he could stop it.

"It's because of you that I've become like this. And yet—even so—Medea, I can't forget you."

Damn those vivid green eyes. They remained seared into his mind, impossible to erase.

"Medea, have you nothing to say to me?"

His red eyes fixed on her with desperate intensity.

"..."

"Please—just say something—"

"Should I?"

When his thirst had reached a point bordering on pitiful, a single short phrase finally emerged.

A voice as cold as winter ice. An indifferent gaze that refused to even acknowledge his presence.

Jason exhaled in relief at the sight of the woman who looked exactly as she had in his memories.

In the memories he'd relived endlessly over the past three years, he'd wished those eyes would look only at him. He'd wished that even a cold voice might reach his ears.

But now, facing Medea in the flesh, he realized something.

This isn't enough.

As Jason stared at her delicate features and the elegant white fingers holding her dress, a thirst unlike anything he'd ever experienced welled up inside him.

He cleared his throat, trying desperately to suppress the urge to reach out and grasp those slender shoulders.

"I returned to Katzen and thought about it for a long time. Where did everything start to fall apart?"

"..."

"As I traced everything back, step by step... I found you, Medea. At the starting point of everything I lost."

"..."

Jason was no fool. By now, he could not help but vaguely sense the antipathy the Princess of Valdina harbored toward him.

But he still didn't know.

That what Medea felt for him was not mere resentment, but a hatred as deep and bottomless as an abyss.

"I..."

Jason paused, as though his throat had closed.

"What did I do that was so wrong to you?"

No matter how deeply he searched, he couldn't find an answer. The question haunted him endlessly.

There was resentment in his voice—resentment he couldn't hide. It sounded almost heartbroken.

He didn't know that this was the unique sorrow felt only by those who had deceived themselves completely.

"I can't understand—"

"I see you've forgotten. I'm certain I answered that question before."

Medea's response was calm, measured.

The anxious, desperate expression on the handsome man's face seemed not to touch her at all—as though it didn't even reach the hem of her dress.

"It seems we shared a terrible fate in our past lives. One that can never, ever be repeated."

Her soft voice pronounced each syllable with such precision it felt as though she were engraving the words into his skull.

"A terrible fate? That can't be. I know. I'm certain of it, because... we're connected."

He paused, then spoke in a hoarse voice that sounded like gravel dragging across stone.

"Every day, I dream of you. Every single night for the past three years—without missing one."

He saw her every night.

Even when he lost the vassals of the former Emperor. Even when he was on the verge of death, pursued by monsters.

"In my dreams, you are... mine."

Jason's expression became almost ecstatic, as though he were experiencing a hazy hallucination.

"My dear..."

In his dreams, Medea was endlessly kind. Impossibly sweet.

"Please be careful. You know I only have you."

"Jason, I love you."

Green eyes filled only with affection for him. Thin arms wrapped around his neck. White fingertips threading gently through his hair.

Medea caressing his face, confessing her love.

Could the taste of the Tree of Knowledge be this sweet?

The warmth, the texture—it was all too vivid to dismiss as mere dreams.

Jason couldn't forget. He would never forget the moment in those dreams when he held this woman in his arms.

And that made the cold reality before him all the more unbearable.

'You can be so affectionate. You can love me...'

Jason wanted to seize Medea by the shoulders and scream until his voice broke.
