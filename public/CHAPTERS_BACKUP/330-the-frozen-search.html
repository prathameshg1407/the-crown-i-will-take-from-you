Chapter 330

The Frozen
Search

"In the silence of snow, hope and despair become indistinguishable."

The Katzen northern border stretched before them—vast, merciless, and far more imposing than Medea had anticipated.

Wind tore across the frozen wasteland like invisible blades, cutting through flesh and resolve alike. The air itself seemed hostile, biting at exposed skin with teeth of frost.

Medea pulled her cloak tighter as her horse crested the final ridge.

"Here it is, Your Highness. The last traces were found at this point."

Medea dismounted and approached the cliff's edge with careful steps.

Below, a dark ice wall plunged into shadow. Beyond it lay only an endless snowfield—pristine, undisturbed, and utterly silent.

No blood.

No armor.

No trace of life whatsoever.

The relentless snowfall had buried all evidence of the catastrophic battle, smothering the truth beneath layers of white.

"Is there a way down?"

The knight beside her—Terrence—gestured toward the mountain range.

"We could take a detour along the ridge. However, at the base of the cliff, bladed ice columns have formed a forest. It's impossible to reach the point of impact directly."

Medea's gaze followed his pointing hand.

Far below, something glittered like scattered starlight across the snow—thousands of razor-sharp ice pillars, each one gleaming with lethal beauty.

To investigate the barrier, we must pass through that frozen forest. There's no other way.

Hehehe-hinnnng—

Silva, the white stallion carrying Medea, took several nervous steps backward. His breath came in visible clouds as he shook his mane violently, eyes rolling white.

The horse wanted no part of that deadly ice field.

Terrence continued, his voice heavy with frustration.

"We've attempted various methods to bypass the ice wall. None have succeeded. The formation is... unnatural. Magical, perhaps."

"Then we must deal with the ice pillars themselves."

Terrence nodded, though his face remained pale from magical exhaustion.

"Yes, Your Highness. This time, however, it's feasible. I brought sufficient magic stones to replenish my power."

Swaaaaa—

A crimson flame flickered to life in his palm, casting dancing shadows across the snow.

But the effort cost him. His hands trembled slightly—he hadn't fully recovered from warping their entire company to the northern border with movement magic.

Medea shook her head.

"Sir Terrence, melting that many ice pillars with magic alone would drain you completely. There's a better way."

"Then what do you propose, Your Highness?"

"Let's go down first. I need to examine the structure more closely."

They descended along the mountain's flank, taking the long route around treacherous slopes and hidden crevasses.

When they finally reached the base of the cliff, Medea stood at the threshold of the ice forest.

She reached out cautiously, pressing her gloved palm against the nearest pillar.

Even through the leather, bitter cold seeped into her fingertips.

The ice pillars rose like a frozen bamboo forest, some reaching heights that seemed to pierce the very sky.

Beneath the intertwined columns, the ground had fused into a massive sheet of glass-like ice, continuous and unbroken.

'These pillars aren't individual formations. They're structurally connected—a single, unified lattice.'

Medea's eyes narrowed with sudden insight.

'If I can create a crack in the right location, the entire structure might fracture along its weak points.'

"Neril, bring me the gunpowder. Sir Terrence, can you place explosive charges at precise coordinates I specify?"

Terrence blinked in surprise.

"Gunpowder? Your Highness, are you certain—"

"We'll create fracture points. Strategic cracks that propagate through the lattice. That way, we minimize your magical expenditure."

Understanding dawned in Terrence's eyes.

"Brilliant. Physics over force."

After a tense period of preparation...

BOOM!

The explosion's roar echoed through the silent mountains like a thunderclap in a cathedral.

The blast erupted from the ice field's edge, sending snow and crystalline fragments fountaining into the air.

CRACK—CRACK—CRACK!

Shattered ice shrapnel ricocheted off surrounding pillars, creating a symphony of crystalline chimes that rang through the frozen air.

Yet in this vast expanse, the initial explosion had only toppled three or four pillars—barely a dent.

"Gallo! Menil! Focus your strikes on the center of that gap. Sir Terrence, amplify the vibrations with diffusion magic!"

At Medea's command, her knights moved with practiced precision.

THOOM! THOOM!

Aura-enhanced strikes hammered the frozen ground like divine judgment.

tssss... tssss...

Terrence's diffusion magic caught the tremors, magnifying them, sending resonant waves rippling through the ice lattice and reverberating off the canyon walls.

"Look! The fractures are spreading!"

CRACK!

One pillar gave way.

Then another.

And another.

The towering ice formations, unable to withstand the cascading vibrations, collapsed in sequence like falling dominoes.

In moments, a clear path carved itself through the frozen forest, marked by the rumbling thunder of shattering ice.

"I'll be damned! I thought Her Highness had some trick up her sleeve, but this—! Forward! Let's move!"

Gallo's battle-hardened voice rang with renewed enthusiasm.

The knights spurred their horses through the newly opened corridor, hooves crunching on shattered ice.

They had expected to exhaust every magic stone in their possession. Instead, Medea had cleared the way with ingenuity and minimal magical cost.

Relief and hope flickered in their eyes—emotions that had been scarce since this expedition began.

"Terrence, does something feel... different here? The sound resonates strangely."

Medea reined in Silva, frowning.

A subtle wrongness tugged at her senses—like space itself was twisted just slightly out of true.

Terrence closed his eyes, extending his magical awareness.

"You're absolutely correct, Your Highness. Here—only at this precise location—the flow of black magic collides with natural energy. You've found the barrier's entrance."

He swallowed his amazement.

'The knights who passed through didn't notice. Even I nearly missed it. Is this truly the perception of one chosen by God?'

"Everyone, battle ready! If this barrier functions like the previous one, whatever's trapped inside will attack the moment we break through."

Terrence's warning carried the weight of bitter experience.

They'd encountered this before at the Golden Tower—the instant the magical barrier shattered, demons and experimental abominations had swarmed out in a frenzy.

"Protect Her Highness at all costs."

No verbal response came, but the Agemas—Medea's elite guard—shifted into defensive positions, eyes sharp as drawn blades.

Shhhhhhhaaaa—

Terrence channeled his magic into the invisible seam between worlds.

The barrier of black magic—woven like a dark, corrupted net—began to fray and tear.

Reality and the hidden space overlapped like transparent puzzle pieces sliding apart.

The knights held their breath, swords raised, prepared for the inevitable onslaught.

They braced for demons.

For monsters.

For anything that would come screaming through that torn veil.

"...!"

But nothing attacked.

Instead, as the barrier dissolved completely, they were greeted by the aftermath of carnage.

Demon corpses lay scattered across the snow like grotesque statues—twisted limbs frozen in death, mouths still open in silent screams.

Dark, dried bloodstains painted the white ground in terrible abstract patterns.

The beast carcasses were eerily preserved in the frigid air, appearing fresh despite the time that had passed—which somehow made them more disturbing.

"Over here!"

One of the knights called out.

Among the carnage, they found him.

The warlock.

Dead, eyes frozen wide in permanent shock.

"It's... it's Cesare's."

A knight knelt beside the corpse, pulling something from the warlock's chest.

A dagger.

The blade bore unmistakable engravings—the crest of the First Prince.

Nearby, torn fragments of armor lay half-buried in snow, each piece stamped with the same royal insignia.

"Find our lord! He must be nearby!"

Alpha's shout cut through the shocked silence.

The knights' eyes blazed with renewed determination.

The evidence was undeniable now.

Cesare was alive.

SEVERAL DAYS LATER

Where the barrier had once stood, several small military barracks now dotted the frozen landscape.

Canvas tents snapped in the wind. Campfires struggled against the cold.

"Anything?"

Alpha's voice carried exhaustion that hadn't been there days ago.

Unlike that first triumphant day when they'd broken through the barrier, the search for Cesare had stalled completely.

Days of searching had yielded nothing but frustration.

"We keep finding traces of demon activity. I can't fathom how the lord managed to handle all of them alone."

Alpha shook his head in grim admiration.

The evidence painted an impossible picture: Cesare had not only killed the warlock—stabbed from behind, no less—but had also annihilated every demon within the barrier.

Single-handedly.

All they found were the remnants of that desperate battle.

'If he was in such dire straits... can he truly still be alive?'

Alpha tried to banish the dark thoughts blooming in his mind like poisonous flowers.

But the longer the search continued, the more grim possibilities presented themselves.

The group's morale sank deeper into a cold, suffocating despair.

"We press on regardless. We must find the lord's body at minimum. We cannot leave without closure."

Gallo's voice was firm, but his eyes betrayed his worry.

"The blizzards are worsening. We should halt the search before nightfall."

Alpha's response carried increasing tension.

The snowy mountains were dark even during daylight hours, the sun barely penetrating the perpetual storm clouds.

After sunset, darkness became absolute—thick enough that you couldn't recognize a person standing an arm's length away.

Recognizing the danger, Gallo nodded reluctantly and signaled the search parties to return to camp.

"Sir Neril, did you find anything?"

Neril shook her head as she approached, snow dusting her armor.

She held out a red smoke grenade—standard emergency equipment. The fact that it remained unused was both disappointing and slightly reassuring.

Gallo exhaled slowly.

"At least no emergencies. But where—"

"Gallo, wasn't Her Highness with your group?"

Neril's question made his blood run cold.

"No, I assumed Her Highness was with your party—!"

Her Highness...

was nowhere to be found.

Neril's voice scattered like a scream into the howling wind.

The blizzard struck their faces with renewed violence, as if the mountain itself had turned hostile.

Snow fell heavier.

Visibility dropped to almost nothing.

And somewhere in that white void, the First Princess Medea had vanished without a trace.
