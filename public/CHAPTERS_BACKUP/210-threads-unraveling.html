Unraveling

"The poison we give others has a way of finding its way back to us."

She thought perhaps it was the unfamiliarity of it all—kindness, sacrifice, tenderness freely given—that left her feeling so unsettled, as though she'd been handed something precious she had no idea how to carry.

That was why his voice still echoed in her ears. Why the warmth of his touch lingered on her fingertips like a phantom sensation.

Medea's expression flickered—confusion passing across her features like clouds across a winter moon. For a moment, she looked almost lost. Like a child standing at a crossroads with no idea which path to take.

"...Your Highness? Are you quite alright?"

"It's nothing. Please continue with your report on the First Prince, Sir Neril."

Medea shook her head, banishing whatever shadows had momentarily claimed her thoughts. The vulnerability that had surfaced for that brief instant vanished, replaced by her customary mask of composed authority.

"Yes, Your Highness."

Kensington resumed his report, though his eyes lingered on her a moment longer than necessary.

"There is considerable turmoil both within and beyond the imperial borders, yet the source of this particular spark remains curiously silent."

By"source," he meant Raju.

"They haven't stirred from the mansion. Given that this operation was conducted jointly by Valdina and the Holy Land, it shouldn't be long before we're able to track down the remaining conspirators."

Medea inclined her head in acknowledgment.

"The time has come to strike back. What has been sown must now be reaped."

Joaquin's Mansion

"No! Not again—not twice!"

Just as Kensington had reported, Raju was reeling from the news of his scheme's catastrophic failure. A Ten-Star General of Katzen—dead. Killed while attempting to abduct the Princess of Valdina.

Prince Cesare had openly admitted to slaying him. Grand Duke Castullo had been recalled to the Empire in disgrace.

"How in damnation did things come to this?"

Why had the First Prince saved Medea?

And the Grand Duke—appearing out of nowhere, claiming to have rescued her, only to be hauled off to Katzen alongside the chamberlain...

Raju felt as though he were losing his grip on sanity. He clawed at the collar constricting his throat, desperate for air. He was suffocating.

"Robbie, what do I do? What in God's name do I do...?"

"Please—!"

The word tore from him like a snarl.

Catherine's eyes went wide. In all their years together, Raju had never raised his voice to her.

Noticing her expression belatedly, he struggled to rein in his fury. When he spoke again, his voice had softened to something approximating tenderness.

"Don't panic. Please, Catherine—don't panic. We need to assess the situation calmly. If we lose our heads now..."

But even as he spoke, the words rang hollow—more desperate plea than reassurance. They did nothing to soothe her.

"Damn it. What I meant to say was... Look, Cath. We'll talk later."

"Robert..."

"Forgive me, but I haven't the luxury of comforting you right now."

Raju swept from the room, leaving Catherine standing alone. His mind was in shambles—he could barely string two coherent thoughts together.

"Master! We have a crisis!"

The moment he emerged, his subordinates descended upon him with urgent news.

"The Agemas have detained the entire Katzen delegation. Word has it the Emperor himself has seized control of the central trading post!"

The color drained from Raju's face.

Has the Emperor of Valdina lost his mind entirely?

"Dear God... Dear God in heaven..."

If anyone discovered his role in this disaster, death was certain. Whether at Valdina's hands or Katzen's made no difference.

He bit his lip until he tasted copper.

If the Emperor dispatches an investigative delegation, it won't take long for them to uncover my connection to Jared.

And if the warlocks' involvement came to light?

A violent shudder ran through him. He had witnessed their rituals over Jason's shoulder—had seen what they were capable of.

Being burned alive would be a mercy. And it wouldn't end there.

His beloved Catherine. His precious daughter, whom he treasured above all else...

"Monga leaf. Prolonged exposure erodes patience, amplifies anger, and ultimately causes the victim to lose all control—to explode at the slightest provocation."

Those had been Medea's instructions to Neril:

"Send this to Umberto. Ensure Raju receives it."

A gift returned in kind—repayment for the clear malice Raju had shown toward Peleus.

Raju himself had never suspected a thing.

"No. There must be a way. Damn it all, there has to be a solution—a path forward—something..."

But the keen intellect and measured judgment he had always prided himself on had long since been poisoned, corroded by the drug seeping through his system.

Rage surged through him in waves, each crest higher than the last. He could feel it building—pressure mounting in his skull, in his chest, in his clenched fists.

And then—

CRACK!

"Master...!"

"Gods above—!"

Unable to contain the pressure any longer, Raju drove his fist into the wall with bone-shattering force.

Crimson blood seeped slowly from his torn knuckles, accompanied by a pain so sharp it momentarily cleared his vision. His hand trembled—swollen, raw, the joints already beginning to purple.

"My Lord... Master...?"

His men stared at him with undisguised shock, frozen in place as they beheld the crater his fist had left in the plaster.

Catherine, too, was drowning in panic.

Medea. Is that woman immortal? How does she always manage to slip through our fingers like water through a sieve?

What should have been a minor setback for Medea had spiraled into catastrophe. The situation had grown far beyond anything Catherine could hope to manage alone.

"I need to see Samon. Where is my son?"

Terror drove her to seek out her firstborn instinctively. Samon had always possessed a brilliant, calculating mind. Surely he would devise some solution.

As Catherine approached her son's chambers, she encountered Marieu emerging from the bedroom.

"Good day, Madam."

"Yes, yes. Where is Samon?"

Catherine made to brush past the girl, but a gentle hand caught her sleeve.

"Madam, I would advise against entering just now. His mood is... particularly volatile."

She explained in hushed tones that one of Samon's servants had been beaten so severely that morning—over some minor irritation with his injured leg—that the man had to be carried out.

"Another one of his tantrums?"

So much for that option.

Catherine exhaled heavily, disappointment settling over her like a shroud. It seemed her son's assistance was not to be had today.

But almost immediately, her thoughts pivoted to the next available resource.

"Then tell me—have you seen him?"

"Him, Madam...?"

"Joaquin. My husband."

Marieu blinked, her lashes fluttering like the wings of a startled butterfly.

"Ah. I believe I saw him in the bedroom earlier. He hasn't ventured out much these days."

"Is that so? Thank you. Do take care of Samon for me."

With that vague instruction, Catherine turned and swept away.

Marieu watched her departure, then quietly closed the door behind her.

"What was that about?"

The creak of hinges had roused Samon from his fitful sleep.

"Nothing of consequence. Go back to sleep, darling."

She settled beside him, her palm resting gently against his chest.

"Evening already? Damn... I'm so tired."

"You've been exerting yourself too much. Nothing has happened—don't fret. Just rest."

Her voice was honey-sweet as she took his hand in hers.

"I will always be by your side, my love."

Samon's breathing deepened as sleep reclaimed him. Marieu remained still, her gaze drifting toward the window and the darkening sky beyond.

"Ha... how utterly delicious."

The soft, playful laughter that escaped her lips—unbidden and knowing—dissolved into the gathering shadows.

Before Catherine could reach her husband's chambers, she encountered Raju in the corridor. He had come seeking her.

"Robert?"

"I apologize for earlier, Cath."

His voice had shed its earlier sharpness, returning to the tender warmth she knew so well. Yet his face told a different story—veins still throbbing at his temples, cold sweat beading on his forehead.

Catherine noticed the dried blood staining the back of his hand but chose not to ask. Guilt and sorrow were already rising in her chest, leaving no room for questions.

"It's alright. I understand. You must be just as shaken as I am."

She reached out, laying a comforting hand on his shoulder. Part of her felt the familiar sting of regret, but another part recognized anew how much he had done for her—how he had filled the void left by her son and husband.

"Let me speak with Joaquin. Perhaps he can suggest another course of action."

"...Very well."

As though that useless man could possibly help.

Raju harbored no illusions about Joaquin's capabilities. Still, he supposed his beloved Catherine might find some measure of stability in her husband's presence.

Joaquin Claudio is a hollow vessel—utterly devoid of solutions for this crisis.

But even hollow vessels had their uses.

If circumstances demand it, I can always transfer Samon's sins onto Joaquin's shoulders. He's the boy's father, after all. Surely he'll understand the necessity of protecting his son.

Oblivious to the calculations churning in Raju's mind, Catherine entered her husband's quarters with her lover in tow.

Joaquin's living room presented an image of studied refinement. Antique portraits adorned the walls in gilt frames. Ornate carpets covered the floors. Elegant curtains cascaded across tall windows.

Yet somehow it all felt contrived—a desperate attempt to resurrect the grandeur of his days as regent, when he had ruled the kingdom in all but name.

"Madam? What brings you here?"

The elderly maid, who had been dozing on the settee, startled awake at Catherine's entrance.

The moment Catherine crossed the threshold, unease prickled at her skin.

"Where is my husband?"

"I beg your pardon? Well, I suppose..."

"If you don't know, who would? Is he not in his room?"

"W-wait, Madam!"

Catherine had begun striding toward the bedroom when the maid suddenly blocked her path.

"I-I'm not certain, Madam. I believe I saw him in the study earlier..."

The woman couldn't meet Catherine's eyes. Perspiration gathered on her brow. She possessed absolutely no talent for deception.

At that moment, something glinted on the side table, catching Catherine's eye. She reached out and plucked it from the polished wood.

A tiny pearl earring.

Too simple for Catherine's tastes. And Birna had never cared for pearls.

A flash of intuition—sharp as lightning, cold as ice—illuminated her mind.

Before the maid could finish her stammering explanation, Catherine spun on her heel.

"Step aside."
