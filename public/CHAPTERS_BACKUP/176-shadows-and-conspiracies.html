Shadows and

"In darkness they gather—those who would trade tomorrow's peace for today's ambition."

The Princess standing before him still appeared pale and fragile.

Yet Gallo couldn't shake the uncanny sensation that he stood not before Princess Medea, but his true master.

When Gallo's thoughts drifted to a certain individual, coincidentally Medea's mind traveled to the same person.

Emerald eyes swept past Gallo's shoulder—scanning behind him, around him, throughout the banquet hall.

"...I understand Your Highness's wishes. I shall send Zeta to serve as our liaison instead. Please remember—Facade stands ready whenever you require our services."

Medea inclined her head in acknowledgment.

"Yes, I understand. But..."

"Your Highness? Do you have further questions?"

Medea hesitated.

Gallo recognized what she hadn't voiced—her gaze unconsciously searching for a familiar white mask.

"Well then, farewell, Medea."

Acares—the infamous mercenary captain who'd received the Lantern of Dawn. The true leader of Facade.

How strange. Gallo had spoken her name so familiarly, as though he remembered it perfectly—as though he'd never forgotten.

"...Nothing. Thank you, Sir, for brightening today's celebration."

"Your kind words honor me, Your Highness. Please forgive me for troubling your thoughts with unnecessary concerns."

Gallo departed with his characteristic blend of warmth and propriety.

Medea nodded slightly.

*Sigh.*

Regret she couldn't quite swallow escaped as the faintest exhalation.

Which was why she failed to notice—

—the golden gaze that hadn't left her for some time.

Even after the coronation banquet concluded, few visitors sought out Jason and his entourage.

Most who should have attended him had been drawn inexorably toward the First Prince instead.

"I am Duke Nova of Sess. I've long desired an audience with Your Highness, though I never imagined we'd meet in such distant lands—"

"Your Highness, where are you lodging? The Republic of Les would be honored to provide accommodations—"

"How dare these insignificant worms disregard the Emperor's explicit orders!"

Jason erupted with fury as he watched nobles competing for the Prince's favor.

Yet he couldn't physically seize them by their collars and drag them back as he desired.

"Your Highness, rather than indulging anger, you should contemplate your next move. The First Prince operates outside convention. Without countermeasures, by the time today's report reaches His Majesty, it will be far too late."

The Emperor's chamberlain offered this counsel with calm, detached composure—through the eyes of a thorough observer.

As though testing Jason, measuring precisely how far the Grand Duke would struggle before reporting everything back to his imperial master.

Indeed, the only moment that stone-like countenance had shown disturbance was when the First Prince materialized in the banquet hall.

"I comprehend the situation without your commentary!"

"In that case, I shall withdraw."

The chamberlain bowed and departed, utterly unperturbed by Jason's outburst.

Jason's frown deepened. The Emperor's watchdog before him, the First Prince blocking from behind—he was trapped, unable to maneuver.

His chest felt compressed, as though a boulder rested upon his ribs.

Meanwhile, the Claudio contingent stood abandoned in the banquet hall like sacks of grain left to rot.

The fact that this entire debacle originated with House Claudio had been swiftly forgotten.

Their presence commanded minimal attention, and the cascade of inexplicable phenomena had made one truth abundantly clear: the Goddess did not welcome them.

Foreign dignitaries saw no reason to protect Claudio when even their own people—Valdinians—despised them.

Samon's expression hardened with frustration.

The Saintess provided no assistance. Instead, she complicated everything further.

Now even mentioning the Claudio name had become impossible.

In the end, they departed empty-handed, having gained nothing.

No one saw House Claudio off as they left the banquet hall.

Area B, Straits of El Amuz

Samon addressed Jason, who stood beside him wearing a perpetual scowl.

"Your Highness, what course should we pursue? His Majesty commanded our return to Valdina, yet circumstances have grown... complicated."

"Why pose that question to me? Since when have you and I sailed the same vessel?"

Jason snapped irritably. But Samon refused to back down.

"Are you suggesting, Your Highness, that you can manage the First Prince single-handedly? Must I remind you that House Claudio provides the funding for a prince who has lost even his territorial holdings?"

Jason snorted derisively.

"You mean coin earned through smuggling? How proud you must be—Valdinian blood in your veins reduced to the equivalent of contaminated tavern swill."

The two men glared at one another with mutual contempt.

Jason and Claudio had reunited for their shared objective, yet both remained prepared to sever ties at any moment.

However, given their precarious circumstances, they had no choice but to cling to even the flimsiest alliances.

"Your Highness, remain steadfast. Samon merely wished to emphasize that your cooperation is essential. Samon, show proper deference to His Highness."

When Joaquin—Samon's father—failed to intervene, Count Raju stepped forward as mediator.

Having survived decades as a foreigner within the Empire, the Count possessed Samon's talent for concealing emotion.

"Nevertheless, Your Highness, Samon's observation holds merit. Since the First Prince appeared without warning, delegation members will find themselves divided between Your Highness and the Prince."

"..."

"We must proceed independently, completely separate from the First Prince. To accomplish that—"

"Yes, then let us both withdraw for today."

Jason cut Raju off mid-sentence, massaging his temples.

His head throbbed mercilessly. Once again, he found himself deployed as the Emperor's attack dog to suppress Cesare.

He simply hadn't anticipated encountering his cousin quite so soon.

After the three men departed, a subordinate who'd been eavesdropping from an adjacent chamber offered counsel.

"Your Highness should never trust them. They've harbored alternative ambitions for years. You must eliminate them at the earliest opportunity."

"I'm aware. I'm merely utilizing them temporarily—ensuring they don't realize who my *true* benefactors are."

Jason's gaze drifted to the pendant resting on the table—the medium through which he communicated with his secret allies.

"How did you acquire foreknowledge of when, where, and how the Monster Wave would manifest?"

"Must I truly voice the answer? Even Your Highness possesses suspicions regarding our identity."

"..."

"Since you've refrained from speaking it aloud, wouldn't it prove wiser to maintain that discretion?"

Those who perform God's work without divine authority.

Those who dwell in darkness, sowing continental chaos while defying the Gods themselves.

Jason understood what the continent called them.

Black magicians...

Every nation on the continent strictly prohibited black magic.

A prophecy foretold that dark sorcery—which first emerged from primordial darkness—would one day plunge the world into chaos once more.

Jason harbored the vague suspicion that perhaps the prophecy's ordained moment had arrived.

Even within the continent's mightiest empire, black magicians operated with impunity—approaching the royal family, running rampant—yet no one perceived their presence.

When the day came that they emerged from shadow, how many screams would echo across the land?

The peace they currently enjoyed would certainly cease to exist.

Jason bit his lip, silencing his wavering conscience.

But the throne will be mine. Regardless of how the world transforms, I shall reign as Katzen's Emperor.

Only they possessed the means to elevate Jason—stripped of wealth and power—to the imperial throne.

"We need only await the opportune moment."

Jason hummed softly, the sound carrying dark promise.

"In shadows they conspire,
trading tomorrow's light
for tonight's fleeting power."
